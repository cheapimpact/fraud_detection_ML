TINJAUAN UMUM DAN INDUSTRI
Pada tahun 2023, di tengah kondisi perekonomian 
global yang mengalami perlambatan, yang ditandai 
dengan menurunnya tren harga komoditas utama 
ekspor, pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia mencapai 
5,05%. 
Sesuai

selama dua tahun berturut-turut, maka pencapaian ini 
membuat Indonesia kembali menjadi menjadi negara 
upper middle-income.
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia yang stabil dan 
tingkat daya beli konsumen yang mulai pulih setelah 
pandemi, diharapkan dapat menjadi katalis positif bagi 
meningkatnya permintaan di industri properti. 
Terdapat pula beberapa kebijakan pemerintah yang 
turut mendukung penguatan sektor properti, antara 
lain pemberlakuan kebijakan relaksasi rasio Loan To 
Value (LTV) untuk kredit properti dari perbankan 
menjadi 
maksimal 
100%

pemberian Pajak Pertambahan Nilai DTP (Ditanggung 
Pemerintah) untuk harga rumah hingga Rp 2 miliar 
dengan harga jual tidak boleh melebihi Rp 5 miliar, dan 
pemberian bantuan administrasi serta penambahan 
target Bantuan Rumah Sejahtera terpadu. 
Pemerintah juga mengatur ulang peraturan terkait 
kepemilikan hunian bagi Warga Negara Asing dengan 
syarat 
dan 
ketentuan 
yang 
diatur 
sesuai 
Undang-undang dan regulasi lainnya yang diterbitkan 
terkait kepemilikan properti oleh orang asing yang 
berkedudukan di Indonesia. Dengan terbukanya 
potensi pasar properti untuk warga negara asing, 
diharapkan  dapat mendorong kinerja bisnis properti 
di Indonesia dan memberikan dampak besar pada 
perekonomian nasional. 
Selain itu di awal tahun 2023, Pemerintah Indonesia 
secara resmi mencabut Pemberlakukan Pembatasan 
Kegiatan Masyarakat, dan di pertengahan tahun 2023 
Pemerintah secara resmi mencabut status pandemi di 
Indonesia. Sehingga di tahun 2023, para ekspatriat 
atau Warga Negara Asing (WNA) mulai kembali datang 
ke Indonesia, dan secara tidak langsung memberikan 
dampak positif pada pasar properti Indonesia. Hal ini 
turut berimplikasi terhadap naiknya tingkat kunjungan 
ke wisata belanja dan hiburan di pusat perbelanjaan 
dan naiknya tingkat hunian perkantoran, apartemen 
dan hotel selama tahun 2023.  
Bisnis ritel terus menunjukkan perbaikan pada tahun 
2023, beberapa brand asing kembali percaya diri untuk 
memasuki pasar Indonesia. Di Jakarta, pembukaan 
kembali beberapa toko ritel yang tutup sementara 
selama pandemi dan komitmen para penyewa untuk 
kembali berbisnis, meningkatkan rata-rata tingkat 
ketersewaan menjadi ±74% pada tahun 2023 atau 
meningkat sekitar ±5% dibandingkan tahun 2022 
(sumber: Q4-2023-Colliers Quarterly-Jakarta-Retail). 
GENERAL REVIEW AND INDUSTRY
In the Year of 2023, in amidst of slowing global economic 
conditions which is marking by the downtrend in prices of 
main export comodities, Indonesia's economic growth 
reached 5,05%. According to the World Bank classiﬁcation 
which was updated in July 2023, and Indonesia’s economic 
growth has been above 5% for two consecutive years, so 
this achievement makes Indonesia returned to be an upper 
middle-income country.
Indonesia's stable economic growth and consumer 
purchasing power which is starting to recover after the 
pandemic are expected to be a positive catalyst for the 
increasing demand in the property industry. 
There are also several government policies that support 
strengthening 
the 
property 
sector, 
including 
the 
implementation of a policy of relaxing the Loan To Value 
(LTV) ratio for property loans from banks to a maximum of 
100% (with zero down payment), providing DTP Value 
Added tax (DTP = borne by the government) for residential 
prices up to Rp. 2 billion with a selling price not exceeding 
Rp 5 billion, and providing administrative assistance as 
well as increasing targets for integrated Welfare Housing 
Assistance. 
The government also reorganized regulations regarding 
residential ownership for foreigners with terms and 
conditions regulated in accordance with the law and other 
regulations issued regarding property ownership by 
foreigners domiciled in Indonesia. By the potential opening 
of the property market to foreigners, it is hoped that it can 
encourage the performance of the property business in 
Indonesia and have a big impact on the national economy.
Apart from that, at the beginning of 2023, the Indonesian 
Government oﬃcially lifted the implementation of 
restrictions on community activities, and in mid-2023 the 
Government oﬃcially lifted the pandemic status in 
Indonesia. So that in 2023, expatriates or foreigners has 
return to Indonesia, and indirectly have a positive impact 
on property market in Indonesia. This also has 
implications for the increasing of visits rate to shopping 
and entertainment tourism in shopping center and the 
increasing of occupancy rate for oﬃces, apartments, and 
hotel during 2023.
The retail business continuous to show improvement in 
2023, several foreign brands rebuild self-conﬁdent to 
entering the Indonesian market. In Jakarta, the reopening 
of several retailers that temporarily closed during the 
pandemic and the commitment of tenants to return to 
business, raised the average of occupancy to ±74% in 2023 
or increased about ±5% compared to 2022 (source: 
Q4-2023-Colliers Quarterly-Jakarta-Retail).

sells land of independent bulding plots, land dan

the Company sell its units in semi-furnished condition.  In 
terms of settlement payment transactions, the Company 
provides several alternative payment methods and also 
collaborates with several ﬁnancial institutions (banks) as 
alternative credit provision to Customers.
If the Customer unable to fulﬁll their payment 
obligations in accordance with the standard sales and 
purchase contract, the Company has the right to cancel 
the sales contract and re-sell the property or unit without 
prior foreclosure proceedings. The company will 
withhold the remaining installments until the property or 
unit is resold.
The Company’s revenue from property sales contributes 
±10% of the Company’s total revenue and net sales. The 
Company implement the revenue recognition method 
where the sales is recognized when the payment has 
reached more than 50%.
