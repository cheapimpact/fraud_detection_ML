Tinjauan Makroekonomi 
Macroeconomic Overview
Perkembangan ekonomi global sepanjang tahun 2024 
ditandai dengan tantangan besar yang berasal dari 
ketidakstabilan geopolitik, kebijakan moneter ketat di 
negara-negara maju, serta fluktuasi harga komoditas 
utama yang mengganggu rantai pasok dunia. Berbagai 
faktor ini menciptakan tekanan terhadap pertumbuhan 
ekonomi di berbagai kawasan. Berdasarkan laporan 
World Economic Outlook dari IMF, pertumbuhan ekonomi 
dunia diperkirakan hanya mencapai 3,3%, lebih rendah 
dibandingkan rata-rata historis 3,7% dalam periode 
2000–2019. Sementara Amerika Serikat menunjukkan 
ketahanan yang lebih kuat dibandingkan kawasan lain, 
Eropa menghadapi ketidakpastian yang cukup tinggi, 
sedangkan perlambatan ekonomi di Tiongkok semakin 
memperburuk stabilitas global.
Di tengah ketidakpastian global yang meningkat, 
perekonomian Indonesia tetap mampu mempertahankan 
stabilitasnya. Berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik 
(BPS), pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional tahun 2024 
tercatat sebesar 5,03% secara tahunan (C-to-C). 
Sektor jasa menjadi motor utama pertumbuhan dengan 
peningkatan 9,80%, sementara konsumsi lembaga 
non-profit yang melayani rumah tangga (PK-LNPRT) 
mengalami lonjakan tertinggi sebesar 12,48% berkat 
berbagai program kesejahteraan dan peningkatan 
belanja sosial oleh pemerintah. Fakta ini menegaskan 
bahwa permintaan domestik tetap solid meskipun 
terdapat tekanan eksternal yang cukup kuat.
Perdagangan internasional Indonesia juga mengalami 
dinamika yang menarik sepanjang tahun 2024. Meskipun 
masih mencatat surplus perdagangan sebesar 31,04 miliar 
dolar AS, angka ini merupakan yang terendah dalam empat 
tahun terakhir. Hal ini disebabkan oleh meningkatnya 
impor bahan baku dan barang modal sebesar 5,31%, 
yang mengindikasikan kebutuhan industri dalam negeri 
yang tetap tinggi. Sementara itu, pelemahan ekspor sektor 
pertambangan akibat fluktuasi harga komoditas global 
turut berkontribusi terhadap penurunan surplus. Dengan 
ekspor yang hanya tumbuh sebesar 2,29%, percepatan 
impor yang lebih tinggi menunjukkan bahwa daya beli dan 
aktivitas industri domestik tetap terjaga meskipun tekanan 
dari faktor eksternal masih terasa.
Global economic developments throughout 2024 were 
marked by significant challenges from geopolitical 
instability, 
tight 
monetary 
policies 
in 
advanced 
economies, and fluctuations in key commodity prices 
that disrupted global supply chains. These factors placed 
pressure on economic growth across various regions. 
According to the International Monetary Fund’s World 
Economic Outlook report, global economic growth is 
projected to reach only 3.3%, lower than the historical 
average of 3.7% during 2000–2019. While the United 
States demonstrated stronger resilience compared to 
other regions, Europe faced considerable uncertainty, 
and the economic slowdown in China further undermined 
global stability.
Amid rising global uncertainty, Indonesia’s economy 
has remained stable. According to data from Statistics 
Indonesia (BPS), the national economic growth in 2024 
reached 5.03% year-on-year (C-to-C). The services 
sector was the main driver of growth, recording an 
increase of 9.80%, while the consumption of non-profit 
institutions serving households (NPISHs) saw the highest 
surge at 12.48%, supported by various government 
welfare programs and increased social spending. These 
figures confirm that domestic demand remained strong 
despite considerable external pressures.
Indonesia’s international trade also experienced notable 
dynamics throughout 2024. Although the country 
recorded a trade surplus of USD 31.04 billion, this was 
the lowest figure in the past four years. The decline 
was primarily driven by a 5.31% increase in imports of 
raw materials and capital goods, indicating sustained 
demand from domestic industries. At the same time, 
weaker export performance in the mining sector due to 
global commodity price fluctuations contributed to the 
reduced surplus. With exports growing by only 2.29%, 
the faster pace of imports reflects resilient domestic 
purchasing power and industrial activity, despite ongoing 
external pressures.

Laju inflasi sepanjang 2024 tetap dalam kondisi 
terkendali dengan angka tahunan sebesar 1,57%, 
meskipun terjadi perbedaan yang cukup signifikan 
antarwilayah. Faktor utama yang mendorong inflasi 
adalah kenaikan harga barang kebutuhan pokok,

Kebijakan moneter yang diterapkan oleh Bank Indonesia 
memainkan peran krusial dalam menjaga stabilitas 
ekonomi nasional. Salah satu tantangan utama yang 
dihadapi adalah fluktuasi nilai tukar Rupiah terhadap 
Dolar AS. Sepanjang tahun, Rupiah mengalami 
pergerakan signifikan dengan nilai tertinggi Rp15.516,19 
per dolar AS pada 2 Januari 2024 dan posisi terendah

memberikan tekanan pada inflasi, terutama untuk barang

Inflation remained under control throughout 2024, with 
an annual rate of 1.57%, although significant regional 
disparities were observed. The main drivers of inflation 
were rising prices of essential goods, particularly food 
and beverages, along with a sharp increase in the cost 
of various services, such as personal care, which rose 
by 7.02%. In response, the government implemented 
more flexible economic policies, including adjustments to 
energy subsidies in regions with high inflation and the 
strengthening of social assistance programs to protect 
the purchasing power of the most affected communities. 
Papua Pegunungan recorded the highest inflation rate at 
5.36%, while North Sulawesi had the lowest at 0.44%.
The monetary policy implemented by Bank Indonesia 
played a vital role in maintaining national economic 
stability. One of the main challenges was the fluctuation 
of the Rupiah against the USD. Throughout the year, 
the Rupiah experienced significant volatility, reaching its 
highest value at Rp15,516.19 per USD on January 2nd, 
2024, and its lowest at Rp16,517.17 per USD on June 
27, 2024. The average annual exchange rate stood at 
Rp16,092.06 for the selling rate and Rp15,931.94 for the 
buying rate, which was weaker than the range projected 
in the 2024 Draft State Budget of Rp14,700–Rp15,300 
per USD. This exchange rate movement was mainly 
influenced by the US Federal Reserve’s interest rate 
policies and Bank Indonesia’s intervention measures to 
stabilize the foreign exchange market.
In response to the depreciation of the Rupiah, Bank 
Indonesia took various intervention measures to 
maintain macroeconomic stability, including adjusting 
interest rates and using foreign exchange reserves. The 
depreciation of the Rupiah put pressure on inflation, 
especially for imported goods, and posed a challenge for 
industries that rely on foreign raw materials. However, 
on the positive side, a weaker Rupiah can enhance 
export competitiveness and reduce the current account 
deficit, provided the manufacturing sector can make the 
most of this situation. Bank Indonesia also continued its 
monetary operations to reduce exchange rate volatility 
by directly intervening in the foreign exchange market 
and managing foreign exchange reserves to maintain 
national economic balance.

Tinjauan Industri Perunggasan
Industri perunggasan Indonesia sepanjang tahun 2024 
menghadapi dinamika yang kompleks, mencakup 
ketidakseimbangan supply-demand, volatilitas harga 
bahan baku, serta kebijakan strategis pemerintah yang 
bertujuan menjaga stabilitas sektor pangan nasional. 
Berdasarkan laporan Kementerian Pertanian melalui 
Direktorat Jenderal Peternakan dan Kesehatan Hewan 
(Ditjen PKH), industri perunggasan tetap menjadi sektor 
utama dalam memenuhi kebutuhan protein hewani 
masyarakat, dengan kontribusi sebesar 80,77% terhadap 
total produksi ternak nasional. Namun, surplus produksi, 
melemahnya daya beli masyarakat, serta kenaikan 
harga pakan menjadi faktor utama yang mempengaruhi 
kinerja industri ini sepanjang tahun.
Dalam tinjauan terhadap kinerja produksi unggas, 
laporan Poultry Indonesia edisi Januari 2025 mencatat 
bahwa produksi ayam broiler pada tahun 2024 mencapai 
3,84 juta ton per tahun, sedangkan permintaan hanya 
mencapai 3,72 juta ton per tahun. Kelebihan pasokan 
sebesar 0,12 juta ton ini menekan harga ayam di tingkat 
peternak, mengakibatkan fluktuasi harga yang signifikan. 
Kondisi serupa juga terjadi pada industri layer, dimana 
produksi telur nasional mencapai 6,3 juta ton per tahun, 
sementara permintaan domestik hanya sekitar 6,2 juta 
ton per tahun, menyebabkan surplus produksi sebesar 
0,17 juta ton. Ketidakseimbangan ini menciptakan 
tekanan ekonomi bagi peternak, terutama peternak 
mandiri dan skala kecil, yang lebih rentan terhadap 
perubahan harga pasar.
Selain itu, berdasarkan data dari Gabungan Pengusaha 
Makanan Ternak (GPMT), biaya produksi pakan unggas 
mengalami 
lonjakan 
akibat 
ketergantungan 
pada 
impor bahan baku seperti jagung dan bungkil kedelai. 
Laporan Gabungan Perusahaan Pembibitan Unggas 
(GPPU) mencatat bahwa fluktuasi harga bahan baku 
ini dipengaruhi oleh kondisi cuaca ekstrem di negara 
produsen utama, seperti Amerika Serikat dan Brasil, 
serta dinamika geopolitik global yang memperburuk 
rantai pasok internasional. Kenaikan harga bahan baku 
ini berdampak langsung pada harga pakan ternak yang 
menjadi komponen utama dalam biaya produksi unggas, 
sehingga menekan profitabilitas peternak.
Poultry Industry Overview
The Indonesian poultry industry in 2024 faced complex 
dynamics, including supply-demand imbalances, raw 
material price volatility, and strategic government 
policies aimed at maintaining the stability of the national 
food sector. According to a report from the Ministry of 
Agriculture through the Directorate General of Livestock 
and Animal Health (Ditjen PKH), the poultry industry 
remains a key sector in meeting the public’s demand for 
animal protein, contributing 80.77% to the total national 
livestock production. However, production surpluses, 
weakened purchasing power, and rising feed prices were 
the main factors affecting the industry’s performance 
throughout the year.
A review of poultry production performance in the 
January 2025 edition of Poultry Indonesia reports that 
broiler chicken production in 2024 reached 3.84 million 
tons per year, while demand was only 3.72 million tons 
per year. This surplus of 0.12 million tons pressured 
chicken prices at the farmer level, leading to significant 
price fluctuations. A similar situation occurred in the layer 
industry, where national egg production reached 6.3 
million tons per year, while domestic demand was only 
around 6.2 million tons per year, resulting in a production 
surplus of 0.17 million tons. This imbalance created 
economic pressure on farmers, especially independent 
and small-scale farmers, who are more vulnerable to 
market price fluctuations.
Additionally, according to data from the Indonesian Feed 
Manufacturers Association (GPMT), the cost of poultry 
feed has increased due to reliance on imported raw 
materials such as corn and soybean meal. A report from 
the Indonesian Poultry Breeder Companies Association 
(GPPU) notes that fluctuations in raw material prices 
are influenced by extreme weather conditions in major 
producing countries like the United States and Brazil, as 
well as global geopolitical dynamics that have worsened 
international supply chains. The rise in raw material 
prices directly affects feed prices, a key component 
of poultry production costs, thus putting pressure on 
farmers’ profitability.

chicken meat and eggs. According to a report from

consumption of animal protein. By weakened purchasing 
power, people became more selective in choosing 
protein sources, which contributed to the stagnation of 
chicken and egg prices at the farmer level.
The government, through the Ministry of Agriculture 
and the Ministry of Trade, responded to this situation by 
implementing various policies, including stabilizing feed 
prices through the Minister of Trade Regulation No. 3 of 
2024, as well as strategic programs such as the Free 
Nutritious Meals (MBG) program. The MBG program 
aims to increase the consumption of chicken meat and 
eggs among students and vulnerable groups, while also 
helping to boost domestic poultry demand. However, the 
Poultry Indonesia report notes that the implementation of 
this program still faces challenges in synchronizing the 
availability of poultry stock with the program’s needs in 
various regions.
Amidst these challenges, the poultry industry has also 
made progress in technology and innovation. According 
to the GPPU report, the Internet of Things (IoT) in farm 
management has been introduced to improve production 
efficiency and reduce losses due to poultry diseases. 
One of the main threats in this industry is the Newcastle 
Disease (ND) outbreak, which, according to the Ditjen 
PKH report, remains the leading cause of high poultry 
mortality across farms in 2024. Artificial intelligence (AI) 
technology for poultry health monitoring is becoming a 
solution increasingly adopted by large companies in this 
sector.
In exports, the Ditjen PKH report mentions that processed 
poultry products from Indonesia are becoming more 
popular in international markets, with exports increasing 
by 40.61% compared to the previous year. However, the 
export of fresh chicken still faces challenges in meeting 
the strict sanitation and phytosanitary standards required 
by destination countries.

Business Prospect
Indonesia’s poultry industry enters 2025 shaped by the 
performance of the previous year, government policies, 
and changes in both domestic and global markets. 
According to a report from the Directorate General of 
Livestock and Animal Health (Ditjen PKH), this industry 
remains a key part of national food security, contributing 
80.77% to the country’s total livestock production. 
Although there was a production surplus in 2024, the 
industry still faces major challenges, including fluctuating 
feed prices, the purchasing power of consumers, and 
evolving export and import regulations.
In the global economic context, the IMF’s World 
Economic Outlook report projects that global economic 
growth remains at 3.3% in 2025. This is influenced by 
tight monetary policies in several major central banks and 
slower economic growth in China. Energy price volatility 
and global trade uncertainty are external factors that may 
affect the economic stability of developing countries, 
including Indonesia. Despite these challenges, the 
Indonesian government aims for 5.2% economic growth 
in 2025. This target is supported by expansionary fiscal 
policies focused on downstream industrial development, 
the transition to green energy, and improving human 
capital through investments in education and healthcare. 
As part of the agricultural sector, the poultry industry 
is expected to benefit from these policies, especially 
through stronger domestic supply chains and more 
stable feed prices.
The oversupply of poultry in Indonesia is one of the 
main factors shaping the prospect of the poultry industry 
in 2025. According to the January 2025 edition of 
Poultry Indonesia, broiler chicken production in 2024

Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan berhasil mencatat laba

keuangan yang didukung oleh efisiensi biaya operasional 
serta strategi bisnis yang lebih optimal sepanjang tahun 
2024.
Meskipun Perseroan mengalami perbaikan kinerja yang 
signifikan, namun Perseroan masih mengalami dampak 
dari ketidakpastian harga Day Old Chicken (DOC). Untuk 
mengatasi kondisi tersebut, Perseroan telah dan akan 
mengambil tindakan-tindakan antara lain:
1.	
Menerapkan pengawasan inisiatif peningkatan margin 
(margin improvement monitoring) pada seluruh lini 
usaha;
2.	
Mendorong pertumbuhan produk pakan di luar pakan 
unggas;
3.	
Mendorong pertumbuhan kemitraan broiler;
4.	
Mengelola kecukupan modal kerja dengan menjaga 
level persediaan bahan baku yang ideal dan aman, 
serta melakukan efisiensi biaya pada seluruh lini 
usaha; serta
5.	
Mengakselerasi pertumbuhan downstream dengan 
menambah kapasitas produksi sebagai rencana 
jangka 
panjang, 
memperkuat 
branding, 
dan 
menambah distribution channel serta inovasi produk.
Untuk mempertahankan tingkat likuiditas, Perseroan 
telah melakukan peningkatan modal tambahan melalui 
penerbitan hak preemptive pada tahun 2024. Oleh karena 
itu, manajemen meyakini bahwa tidak ada keraguan 
signifikan atas kemampuan Perseroan melanjutkan 
kelangsungan usahanya.
In 2024, the Company successfully recorded a net profit 
of Rp3.23 billion, a positive turnaround from the previous 
year’s net loss of Rp17.32 billion. This achievement 
reflects an improvement in financial performance, 
supported by operational cost efficiency and more 
optimal business strategies implemented throughout 
2024.
Although 
the 
Company 
experienced 
significant 
performance improvement, it still faces uncertainties in 
Day Old Chicken (DOC) prices. To address this situation, 
the Company has taken and will take actions including:
1.	
Implementing the margin improvement monitoring 
initiative in all business lines;
2.	
Promoting the growth of feed products beyond 
poultry feed;
3.	
Stimulating the growth of broiler partnerships;
4.	
Managing working capital sufficiency by maintaining 
ideal and safe levels of raw material inventory and 
cost efficiency across all business lines; and
5.	
Accelerating downstream growth by increasing 
production 
capacity 
as 
a 
long-term 
plan, 
strengthening branding, expanding distribution 
channels, and product innovation.
To maintain liquidity levels, the Company has increased 
additional capital through preemptive rights issuances in 
2024. Therefore, management believes that there is no 
significant doubt about the Company’s ability to continue 
its operations.
