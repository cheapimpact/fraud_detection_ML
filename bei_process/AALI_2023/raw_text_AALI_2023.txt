INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
Aktivitas perekonomian global masih terus mengalami 
ketidakpastian di tahun 2023 sebagai akibat dari pemulihan 
ekonomi pasca pandemi Covid-19 dimana kebijakan moneter 
masih cukup ketat, perdagangan global yang masih lemah, 
serta terjadinya inflasi yang cukup tinggi di beberapa negara. 
Selain itu eskalasi geopolitik akibat invasi Rusia ke Ukraina 
di 2022 masih memberikan dampak terutama pada harga 
komoditas global.  
Belum usai konflik Rusia dan Ukraina, gejolak Timur Tengah 
antara Israel dan Palestina (Laut Merah) pada pertengahan 
2023 juga dapat memberikan risiko geopolitik mengingat laut 
merah jalur transportasi untuk seluruh kegiatan perdagangan 
global. Hal ini mengakibatkan jalur pengiriman produk 
membutuhkan waktu yang lebih lama serta biaya yang 
lebih besar. Hingga tahun 2023, pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global hanya meningkat sebesar 2,6% dari tahun 2022 
dimana mengalami penurunan 0,4% dibandingkan dengan 
peningkatan pada tahun 2022 sebesar 3,0% dari tahun 2021.
Harga komoditas yang sangat tinggi pada tahun 2022 yang 
diakibatkan oleh berbagai faktor, seperti pemulihan ekonomi 
pasca pandemi Covid-19, kebijakan ekspor maupun impor 
berbagai negara, maupun invasi Rusia dan Ukraina. Hal 
tersebut membuat sebagian besar komoditas menyentuh 
harga tertinggi sepanjang sejarah dimana bisa dikatakan 
sebagai kondisi anomali. Di tahun 2023, harga kembali 
normal sehingga terdapat gap yang cukup tajam antara 2022 
dengan 2023. Harga rata-rata minyak kelapa sawit pada 
tahun 2023 mengalami penurunan sebesar 28,7% menjadi 
USD 964/ton dibandingkan tahun 2022 sebesar USD 1.352/ 
ton.

uncertainty in 2023 due to the economic recovery after

escalation due to Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022 still

the COVID-19 pandemic, export and import policies of 
various countries, as well as the invasion of Russia and 
Ukraine. Thus, causing most commodities prices to surge 
to recorded high in history, which can be considered 
as an anomaly. In 2023, prices returned to normal, 
those the significant gap between 2022 and 2023. The 
average price of palm oil in 2023 decreased by 28.7% 
to 964 USD/ton compared to 2022 at 1,352 USD/ton.

matahari di pasar global dari Eropa, Turki, serta utamanya 
flashout minyak bunga matahari dari Rusia akibat invasi Rusia

The drastic decline in the price of sunflower oil had

PERKEBUNAN KELAPA SAWIT 
Hingga 31 Desember 2023, Perseroan mengelola perkebunan 
kelapa sawit tertanam seluas 285.387 hektar yang tersebar di 
Sumatra seluas 105.300 hektar (36,9%), di Kalimantan seluas 
129.804 hektar (45,5%), dan di Sulawesi seluas 50.283 hektar 
(17,6%). Kebun kelapa sawit tertanam milik Perseroan terbagi 
menjadi dua kategori, yaitu perkebunan inti sebesar 213.158 
hektar (74,7%) dan perkebunan plasma sebesar 72.229 hektar 
(25,3%).
Perkebunan kelapa sawit Perseroan dibagi menjadi dua 
kategori, yaitu Tanaman Belum Menghasilkan (TBM) sebesar 
19.869 hektar (7,0%) serta Tanaman Menghasilkan (TM) sebesar 
265.518 hektar (93,0%) dimana umur rata-rata per tahun 2023 
adalah 16 tahun. Umur tanaman menjadi salah satu faktor yang 
dapat mempengaruhi produktivitas Perseroan, hal tersebut 
membuat Perseroan terus melakukan penanaman kembali 
terhadap perkebunan milik Perseroan agar dapat menggantikan 
tanaman-tanaman yang sudah memiliki umur di atas 25 
tahun. Hingga 31 Desember 2023, Perseroan telah melakukan 
penanaman kembali seluas 4.713 hektar atau meningkat 3,4% 
dibandingkan tahun 2022 sebesar 4.557 hektar. 
Peningkatan produktivitas tanaman yang menjadi salah satu 
fokus utama Perseroan menjadi salah satu faktor terbentuknya 
tim penelitian dan pengembangan. Berbagai upaya telah 
dilakukan seperti menciptakan bibit-bibit unggul yang 
telah digunakan dalam peremajaan tanaman pada tahun 
2022, dimana bibit unggul tersebut bernama AAL Nirmala, 
AAL Lestari, dan AAL Sejahtera. Dalam mengembangkan 
penelitiannya, tim penelitian dan pengembangan Perseroan 
juga melakukan kerjasama dengan University of Potsdam 
German, University of Newcastle England, serta universitas-
universitas ternama lainnya di Indonesia.

OIL PALM PLANTATION
As of December 31, 2023, the Company manages oil palm 
plantations spanning 285,387 hectares across Sumatra with 
an area of 105,300 hectares (36.9%), Kalimantan with an 
area of 129,804 hectares (45.5%), and Sulawesi with an area 
of 50,283 hectares (17.6%). The plantations are categorized 
into 213,158 hectares (74.7%) of nucleus plantations and 
72,229 hectares (25.3%) of plasma plantations.
The Company’s oil palm plantations are categorized into 
19,869 hectares (7.0%) of Immature Plantations (IP) and 
265,518 hectares (93.0%) of Mature Plantations (MP), with 
an average age of 16 years in 2023. The age of the plants 
significantly impacts the Company’s productivity, prompting 
ongoing replanting efforts to replace trees over 25 years 
old. As of December 31, 2023, the Company had successfully 
replanted 4,713 hectares, marking a 3.4% increase from the 
4,557 hectares replanted in 2022.
In pursuit of enhancing plant productivity, a key focus 
for the Company, the establishment of a research and 
development team was crucial. Significant strides have been 
taken, including the development of superior seeds, such 
as AAL Nirmala, AAL Lestari, and AAL Sejahtera, utilized for 
plant rejuvenation in 2022. The Company’s research and 
development efforts extend globally through collaborations 
with esteemed institutions such as the University of Potsdam 
in Germany, the University of Newcastle in England, and other 
renowned universities in Indonesia.

PALM OIL MILLS
Palm Oil Mills (POM) play a crucial role in processing the 
extraction of Crude Palm Oil (CPO) and palm kernels from 
Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB). The Company owns and operates 
32 POM units with a total capacity of 1,570 tons per hour. 
These units are strategically distributed, with 11 units (34%) in 
Sumatra, 14 units (44%) in Kalimantan, and seven units (22%) 
in Sulawesi. 
PALM KERNEL CRUSHING PLANTS 
As of December 31, 2023, the Company owns and operates 
14 palm kernel crushing plants with a total capacity of 1,500 
tons/day. These plants are distributed, with four units (29%) in 
Sumatra, six units (42%) in Kalimantan, and four units (29%) 
in Sulawesi.
PALM OIL REFINERIES
Ensuring the supply of downstream products in the palm oil 
industry, derived from CPO and Palm Kernel Oil (PKO), to meet 
daily consumer needs is a key focus for the Company in the 
establishment of CPO and PKO refinery plants.
As of December 31, 2023, the Company owns and operates 
two CPO refinery plants, PT Tanjung Sarana Lestari (TSL) in 
North Mamuju Regency, West Sulawesi Province, and a joint 
venture company PT Kreasijaya Adhikarya (KJA) in Dumai, 
Riau Province. Together, these two CPO refineries have a total 
capacity of 3,000 tons/day. Additionally, the Company has 
a PKO refinery plant, PT Tanjung Bina Lestari (TBL) in North 
Mamuju Regency, West Sulawesi Province with a total capacity 
of 400 tons/day.
To enhance and streamline the shipping process, particularly 
for exports, the Company has dedicated docks designed for 
loading and unloading ships with a capacity of 20,000 dwt 
for PT TSL and PT TBL. Marketing initiatives are conducted 
through a joint venture between the Company and KL-Kepong 
Plantation Holdings Sdn. Bhd, namely Astra-KLK Pte. Ltd. 
based in Singapore, aimed at optimizing the sales process.
FERTILIZER MIXING PLANTS
Fertilizer plays a crucial role in enhancing the sustainability 
and productivity of the Company. Accurate dosing and precise 
application of fertilizers are fundamental to optimizing plant 
growth. Given the varying application standards and specific 
plant requirements, the Company has established Fertilizer 
Mixing Plants. The outcomes from these facilities are utilized 
not only in the Company’s plantations but also in those of 
partner organizations.
The Company has undergone significant improvements in 
its composition and quality, driving its decision to own and 
operate two fertilizer mixing plants with a combined capacity 
of 570 tons per day. These plants are strategically situated in 
Pangkalan Bun, Central Kalimantan, and Donggala, Central 
Sulawesi.

PENINGKATAN PRODUKTIVITAS TANAMAN
Peningkatan produktivitas tanaman menjadi salah satu 
hal terpenting bagi Perseroan dimana penerapan Good 
Argicultural Practices (GAP) sudah dijalankan sejak lama. 
Dalam mendukung operasional Perseroan yang efektif dan 
efisien maka terus dikembangka Plantation Information 
Management System (PIMS) yang terdiri dari aplikasi mobile 
maupun sensor/Internet of Thins (IoT). 
Meningkatkan 
produktifitas 
dilakukan 
dengan 
menerapkan 
kebijakan panen excellent dengan ketuntasan panen sebesar 95% 
setiap minggunya, sehingga rotasi panen terus terjaga. Walaupun 
terdapat moratorium dalam pembukaan lahan perkebunan yang 
telah diterapkan oleh Pemerintah sejak tahun 2015, Perseroan terus 
berusaha meningkatkan produktivitas tanaman melalui intensifikasi 
yield dengan peremajaan umur tanaman (replanting) tanpa 
mengabaikan keseimbangan ekosistem. 
Pengembangan pupuk juga terus dilakukan melalui tim RnD 
serta menerapkan 5T (tepat jenis, tepat dosis, tepat waktu, 
tepat tempat, dan tepat cara) dalam pengaplikasiannya. 
Penggunaan pupuk berbahan organik berbentuk padat 
maupun cair dijalankan secara intensif terutama untuk 
tanaman muda dan areal yang marginal. Sementara 
penggunaan pupuk anorganik dilakukan dengan mekanisasi 
dalam upaya meningkatkan efektivitas operasional. Selain 
pemupukan, Perseroan juga melakukan perawatan kebun 
dengan kegiatan kastrasi, sanitasi, pruning, maupun circle, 
path, dan tempat pengumpulan akhir (Carriage Paid To/CPT) 
guna menghasilkan TBS terbaik.

IMPROVING PLANT PRODUCTIVITY
Plant productivity improvement stands as a central focus for 
our Company. We have a robust history of implementing Good 
Agricultural Practices (GAP) to bolster this objective. In our 
pursuit of effective and efficient operations, the Company is 
consistently enhancing the Plantation Information Management 
System (PIMS). This cutting-edge system comprises mobile 
applications and sensors/Internet of Things (IoT).
To enhance productivity, the Company focuses on implementing 
a robust harvest policy that aims for 95% completeness each 
week to maintain a consistent harvest rotation. Despite the 
government-imposed moratorium on opening new plantation 
land since 2015, the Company remains dedicated to boosting 
crop productivity through yield intensification. This is achieved 
by strategically replanting aging plants while ensuring a 
balance with the ecosystem.
Our RnD team has a strong focus on fertilizer development and 
implements the 5R approach (right type, right dosage, right 
time, right place, and right method) to optimize its application, 
prioritizing the use of organic fertilizers in both solid and 
liquid forms, particularly for young plants and marginal areas. 
Mechanization is used when applying inorganic fertilizers to 
improve operational efficiency. In addition to fertilization, the 
Company also prioritizes plantation maintenance practices 
such as castration, sanitization, pruning, circle, path, and final 
collection point (Carriage Paid to/CPT) to ensure high-quality 
FFB production.

PRODUCTION ASPECT
In 2023, the Company produced 4.35 million tons of FFB from its 
nucleus and plasma plantations, marking a 2% increase from 
the 4.27 million tons produced in 2022. On the other hand, FFB 
purchases from third parties decreased by 8% to 2.40 million tons 
compared to the previous year. The increase in production can 
be attributed to enhanced productivity following the extended 
drought in 2019. The Company also implemented an extended 
crop rotation in 2022 in response to the export ban imposed 
by the Government of Indonesia, which is still affecting crop 
productivity until now; in 2023, there was also a drought, which 
resulted a decrease in production in the third quarter. However, 
the efforts made by the Company to improve productivity can 
maintain overall production in 2023.
The El Niño phenomenon in late 2023 led to a 2% decrease 
in CPO production, totaling 1.28 million tons compared to 
2022. The decline in CPO also due to a decrease in FFB 
purchases from third parties, so that the FFB processed 
decreased by 2%. Despite this setback, the Company 
remains committed to its replanting program to boost 
productivity. Furthermore, the implementation of digitization 
in operational processes is underway to drive operational 
excellence. Despite the drop in CPO production, CPO 
derivative products increased by 21% to 598.50 thousand 
tons from 494.15 thousand tons in 2022. The increase in 
CPO derivative products represented an opportunity taken 
by the Company, as demand for CPO derivative products 
rose to meet daily needs.
The Company also recorded a 3% decrease in kernel production, 
dropping from 282.01 thousand tons in 2022 to 272.31 thousand 
tons in 2023. The decrease in production was consistent with the 
drop in kernel derivative output, which decreased by 8% to 101.30 
thousand tons in 2023 from 110.30 thousand tons in 2022.

Pada tahun 2022, harga CPO mengalami peningkatan 
yang cukup signifikan sehingga menjadi harga tertinggi 
sepanjang sejarah. Namun, semenjak diberlakukannya

harga CPO mengalami penurunan sehingga kembali kepada 
harga normal. Harga mungkin tidak akan se-excited tahun 
sebelumnya, namun produksi yang stagnan dan permintaan 
yang mulai pulih serta peningkatan kebutuhan global akan 
biodiesel masih menjadi tema besar pada beberapa tahun 
mendatang yang membuat industri minyak nabati, khususnya 
minyak sawit masih menguntungkan.

Obligation (DMO) dan Domestic Price Obligation (DPO) tidak 
dipungkiri akan masih menjadi suatu tantangan tersendiri 
bagi industri domestik. Namun, Perseroan akan tetap 
mendukung berbagai kebijakan tersebut, sebagai komitmen 
untuk bersama-sama dalam memajukan industri sawit dan 
memberikan kebermanfaatan bagi negara.

dan turunannya mengalami peningkatan menjadi 1,70 juta

serapan penjualan adalah 66% untuk market domestik dan 
34% untuk ekspor ke beberapa negara tujuan seperti China, 
India, Filipina, Pakistan, dan lain sebagainya.

ton pada tahun 2023 atau mengalami kenaikan sebesar 1% 
dibandingkan tahun 2022 sebesar 163,17 ribu ton
Perseroan akan terus melihat peluang terbaik dalam 
memasarkan produk, baik untuk market domestik atau ekspor 
serta untuk setiap segment product baik CPO dan turunannya 
maupun kernel dan turunannya. 
 
MARKETING ASPECT
In 2022, the price of CPO increased significantly enough 
to become the highest price in history. However, since the 
Indonesian Government implemented the export ban in April 
2022, the price of CPO has decreased, so it has returned 
to regular prices. While the excitement of the previous year 
may not be replicated, the edible oil industry, particularly 
palm oil, remains profitable. Stagnant production, recovering 
demand, and a rising global interest in biodiesel are key 
factors that will shape the industry in the years to come.
Various government policies, such as the Domestic Market 
Obligation (DMO) and Domestic Price Obligation (DPO), pose ongoing 
challenges for the domestic industry. Nevertheless, the Company 
remains steadfast in its support for these policies, demonstrating a 
commitment to collaborating towards the advancement of the palm 
oil sector and providing benefits to the country.
In 2023, the Company’s sales volume for CPO and its 
derivatives increased to 1.70 million tons, marking a 13% 
increase from 1.50 million tons in 2022. The sales absorption 
was 66% for the domestic market and 34% for exports to 
destination countries such as China, India, the Philippines, 
Pakistan, and others.  
The Company also recorded an increase in sales of kernels 
and its derivatives by 3% to 273.41 thousand tons in 2023 
from 265.48 thousand tons in 2022. Sales consisted of 
164.62 thousand tons of kernels in 2023, an inrease of 1% 
compared to 2022 of 163.17 thousand tons.

plants, which are old amid the low replanting program and

plantations, which account for 41% of the composition of oil 
palm plantation owners in Indonesia.
