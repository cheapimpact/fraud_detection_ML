PEREKONOMIAN GLOBAL
Pada tahun 2023, risiko ketidakpastian global 
meningkat yang disebabkan beragam faktor yaitu 
inflasi tinggi, ketegangan geopolitik di berbagai 
wilayah, pertumbuhan ekonomi yang tidak merata 
antarnegara dan dunia usaha yang belum sepenuhnya 
pulih dari pandemi Covid-19.
Inflasi yang tetap tinggi mendorong bank sentral 
di banyak negara untuk menaikkan suku bunga 
acuan seperti yang dilakukan bank sentral Amerika 
Serikat The Federal Reserve. Hal ini berdampak pada 
penyaluran kredit yang lebih ketat dan menghambat 
ekspansi dunia usaha di tingkat global.
Fed Fund Rate (FFR) yang menjadi suku bunga 
acuan global menunjukan tren meningkat dan diikuti 
dengan kenaikan yield obligasi Amerika Serikat untuk 
memenuhi kebutuhan pembiayaan utang pemerintah 
AS dan kenaikan premi jangka panjang. Akibatnya, 
terjadi pembalikan arus modal dari negara-negara 
berkembang menuju negara maju dan aset yang lebih 
likuid yang menyebabkan dollar AS menguat terhadap 
mata uang negara lain.
Penguatan 
dollar

meningkatkan 
risiko 
ketidakpastian di pasar keuangan. Untuk menjaga 
kestabilan ekonomi domestik dan nilai tukar, bank 
sentral di banyak negara melakukan intervensi.
Secara 
spasial, 
ekonomi 
Tiongkok 
diprediksi 
melambat karena sejumlah tantangan yang dihadapi. 
Krisis properti dan perlambatan di sektor manufaktur 
merupakan

oleh pemerintah Tiongkok. Sebagai salah satu

GLOBAL ECONOMY
In 2023, the risk of global uncertainty will increase due 
to various factors, namely high inflation, geopolitical 
tensions in various regions, uneven economic growth 
between countries and the business world which has 
not yet fully recovered from the Covid-19 pandemic.
Inflation that remains high has prompted central 
banks in many countries to raise benchmark interest 
rates, as did the United States central bank, The 
Federal Reserve. This has an impact on tighter credit 
distribution and hampers the expansion of the business 
world at the global level.
Fed Funds Rate (FFR), which is the global benchmark 
interest rate, shows an upward trend and is followed 
by an increase in United States bond yields to meet 
the US government's debt financing needs and an 
increase in long-term premiums. As a result, there was 
a reversal of capital flows from developing countries 
towards developed countries and more liquid assets, 
which caused the US dollar to strengthen against the 
currencies of other countries.
The strengthening of the US dollar increases the risk of 
uncertainty in financial markets. To maintain domestic 
economic stability and exchange rates, central banks 
in many countries intervene.
Spatially, China's economy is predicted to slow down 
due to several challenges it faces. The property crisis 
and slowdown in the manufacturing sector are the 
biggest challenges faced by the Chinese government. 
As one of the countries with the largest economy, this 
condition influences world economic conditions.

Ekonomi India diperkirakan menjadi yang tumbuh 
tertinggi di antara negara G20. Sektor infrastruktur, 
manufaktur, investasi dan teknologi menjadi penopang 
pertumbuhan ekonomi India di tahun 2023.
Sektor perdagangan dunia mengalami perlambatan dan

Perdagangan Dunia (WTO) telah menurunkan separuh 
perkiraan pertumbuhan perdagangan global pada tahun

Perlambatan perdagangan terjadi pada berbagai jenis

pembatasan pengiriman selama masa pandemi.
Risiko ketidakpastian ekonomi dan pasar keuangan 
yang meningkat menyebabkan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global diprediksi melambat. World Bank dalam laporan 
berjudul Global Economic Prospects edisi Juni 2023, 
memperkirakan ekonomi dunia tumbuh 2,1% di tahun 
2023, lebih rendah dari pencapaian tahun sebelumnya 
sebesar 3,1%.
PEREKONOMIAN INDONESIA
Di tengah perlambatan ekonomi dunia, perekonomian 
Indonesia tetap tumbuh positif. Mengacu laporan 
Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), ekonomi tumbuh secara 
keseluruhan tahun 2023 sebesar 5.05% (YOY) dengan 
nominal berdasar harga berlaku sebesar Rp20.892,4 
triliun.
Dari sisi pengeluaran, pertumbuhan tertinggi berasal 
dari konsumsi swasta yang diikuti konsumsi rumah 
tangga dan investasi. Sementara secara distribusi, 
konsumsi rumah tangga masih menjadi kontributor 
terbesar dalam pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional. 
Investasi yang tumbuh sejalan dengan penyelesaian 
Program Strategis Nasional. 
India's economy is estimated to be the highest 
growing among the G20 countries. The infrastructure, 
manufacturing, investment and technology sectors 
will support India's economic growth in 2023.
The world trade sector is experiencing a slowdown and 
increasing fragmentation of the global economy. World 
Trade Organization (WTO) has halved its forecast for global 
trade growth in 2023 to 1.7%.
The slowdown in trade occurred in various types of goods, 
especially iron and steel, office and telecommunications 
equipment, textiles and clothing. The decline also 
occurred in commercial services trade. However, 
demand for cars remains high as compensation for 
shipping restrictions during the pandemic.
The increasing risk of economic and financial market 
uncertainty causes global economic growth to be 
predicted to slow down. The World Bank, in a report 
entitled Global Economic Prospects, June 2023 edition, 
estimates that the world economy will grow 2.1% in 
2023, lower than the previous year's achievement of 
3.1%.
INDONESIAN ECONOMY
In the midst of global economic slowdown, Indonesia's 
economy continues to grow positively. Referring to 
the report from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), 
the economy grew overall in 2023 by 5.05% (year-on-
year) with a nominal value based on current prices of 
Rp20,892.4 trillion.
From the expenditure side, the highest growth came 
from private consumption followed by household 
consumption and investment. Meanwhile, in terms of 
distribution, household consumption is still the largest 
contributor to national economic growth. Investment 
is growing in line with the completion of the National 
Strategic Program.

Exports and imports of goods and services experienced 
a contraction due to the slowdown in global trade. 
Exports of goods and services contracted by 4.26% 
and imports contracted by 6.18% on an annual basis. 
The real decline in goods exports was due to reduced 
demand from major trading partner countries, 
especially China, and a decline in commodity prices.
In terms of business, the transportation and 
warehousing sector recorded the highest growth 
at 13,96%, followed by other services at 10,52%. This 
growth was due to increasing community mobility and 
the continued high level of digital trade transactions.
Indonesia's inflation achievement in 2023 was recorded 
at 2.61% (year-on-year). This inflation occurred due to 
an increase in prices as indicated by the increase in all 
expenditure group indices with the largest increases in 
the food, beverage and tobacco groups.
To mitigate the impact of global uncertainty on 
inflation, Bank Indonesia maintained the benchmark 
interest rate to 6% in December 2023. The increase in 
the BI Rate also aims to prevent the outflow of capital 
from the domestic market.

Seperti halnya sektor transporasi yang meningkat, 
sektor ritel juga terus menunjukan pertumbuhan 
positif. Daya beli masyarakat yang tetap terjaga dan 
momentum tahapan pelaksanaan pemilihan umum 
mendorong belanja konsumsi sehingga berdampak 
terhadap peningkatan kinerja ritel modern.
Namun demikian, industri logistik masih dihadapkan 
pada sejumlah tantanan. Dalam rilis World Bank, Logistic 
Performance Index (LPI) Indonesia 2023 memiliki skor 
total 3,0 atau berada di peringkat 63, turun dibandingkan 
LPI 2018 (skor 3,15 atau peringkat 46). 
Dibandingkan dengan negara dalam kawasan ASEAN, 
LPI Indonesia juga masih tertinggal seperti dengan 
Singapura (skor 4,14 atau peringkat 1), Malaysia (skor 
3,15 atau peringkat 32), dan Thailand (skor 3,26 atau 
peringkat 46). 
 
Secara garis besar komponen LPI dapat diklasifikasikan 
menjadi dua kelompok yakni (1) Kelompok input yang 
terdiri dari kepabeanan (customs), infrastruktur, dan 
kualitas layanan, serta (2) kelompok outcomes atau 
kinerja layanan pengiriman yang terdiri dari waktu 
pengiriman, pengiriman internasional, serta tracking 
and tracing. Dari sisi outcome inilah kinerja logistik 
nasional masih perlu terus ditingkatkan. 
Similar to the rising transportation sector, the retail 
sector also continues to show positive growth. The 
sustained purchasing power of the public and the 
momentum of the electoral process drive consumer 
spending, thereby impacting the improvement of 
modern retail performance.
However, the logistics industry still faces several 
challenges. According to the World Bank release, 
Indonesia's Logistic Performance Index (LPI) for 
2023 has a total score of 3.0, ranking 63rd, a decrease 
compared to the LPI of 2018 (score of 3.15, ranking 46th).
 Compared to countries in the ASEAN region, Indonesia's 
LPI still lags behind countries like Singapore (score of 
4.14, ranking 1st), Malaysia (score of 3.15, ranking 32nd), 
and Thailand (score of 3.26, ranking 46th).
Broadly, LPI components can be classified into 
two groups: (1) Input group consisting of customs, 
infrastructure, and service quality, and (2) outcomes 
group or delivery service performance consisting of 
delivery time, international delivery, and tracking and 
tracing. It is in terms of outcomes that the national 
logistics performance still needs to be continuously 
improved.

Integrated Logistics Services, encompassing Motor 
Transport for General Goods, Warehousing and Storage, 
and Cold Storage Activities within the framework of 
business development.
The company's main advantage lies in its "Dedicated 
& Integrated Logistic Support" or comprehensive 
logistics services provided to customers (especially 
B2B). With this service concept, all supporting facilities

ensuring satisfactory service for customers. Through

the optimal utilization of assets and human resources. 
Moreover, customers are satisfied with the services 
provided by the company.
The company consistently maintains service quality

and improve services provided to customers from 
a strategic management perspective. Through this 
formation, the company is committed to enhancing 
customer trust in its services.

KEMAMPUAN MEMBAYAR UTANG
Ability to Pay Debt
Kemampuan Perseroan dalam memenuhi kewajiban 
utang jangka pendek tercermin dari rasio likuiditas 
sedangkan kemampuan membayar utang jangka panjang 
tercermin dari rasio likuiditas dan rasio solvabilitas. 
RASIO LIKUIDITAS 
Rasio likuiditas dapat diukur dari rasio lancar yang 
merupakan perbandingan antara aset lancar dan 
liabilitas lancar. Pada tahun 2023, rasio lancar sebesar 
2,33 kali, meningkat dibanding tahun 2022 sebanyak 
1,01 kali. 
RASIO SOLVABILITAS 
Rasio solvabilitas merupakan rasio untuk menilai 
kemampuan perusahaan dalam melunasi semua 
kewajibannya, baik jangka pendek maupun jangka 
panjang, dengan jaminan aktiva atau kekayaan yang 
dimiliki perusahaan hingga perusahaan tutup atau 
dilikuidasi.
Rasio solvabilitas dapat diperoleh dari perbandingan 
antara total liabilitas dengan jumlah ekuitas yang 
dimiliki. Pada tahun 2023, rasio solvabilitas sebesar 
0,29 kali dan sebesar 0,54 kali di tahun 2022.
Dari rasio-rasio tersebut, tercermin bahwa Perseroan 
memiliki kemampuan membayar seluruh utang yang 
dimiliki baik dalam jangka pendek maupun jangka 
panjang.
The Company's ability to fulfill short-term debt 
obligations is reflected in the liquidity ratio, while the 
ability to pay long-term debt is reflected in the liquidity 
ratio and solvency ratio.
LIQUIDITY RATIO
The liquidity ratio can be measured from the current 
ratio which is a comparison between current assets 
and current liabilities. In 2023, the current ratio will be 
2.33 times, an increase compared to 2022 of 1.01 times.
SOLVENCY RATIO
Solvency ratio is a measure to assess a company's 
ability to settle all its obligations, both short-term 
and long-term, with the guarantee of assets or wealth 
owned by the company until the company closes or is 
liquidated.
The solvency ratio can be obtained by comparing total 
liabilities to the amount of equity held. In 2023, the 
solvency ratio was 0.29 times, and in 2022, it was 0.54 
times.
From these ratios, it is evident that the company has 
the ability to pay off all of its debts, both short-term 
and long-term.
