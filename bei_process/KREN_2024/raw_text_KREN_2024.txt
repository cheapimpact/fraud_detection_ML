Tinjauan Ekonomi 
Economic Review
Pertumbuhan ekonomi global terus mengalami 
perlambatan sepanjang tahun 2024, dipengaruhi 
oleh perubahan dinamika ekonomi dunia yang 
disertai tingkat ketidakpastian yang tinggi. 
Ketegangan politik global tetap tinggi, 
mengakibatkan berlanjutnya konflik di sejumlah 
negara, serta memengaruhi geopolitik dan 
fragmentasi perdagangan internasional. Dalam 
kondisi ini, Bank Indonesia memperkirakan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global akan melambat 
menjadi 3,2% pada 2024 dan diproyeksikan semakin 
menurun hingga 3,1% pada 2026.  
Global economic growth continued to slow throughout 
2024, driven by shifting global economic dynamics 
coupled with high levels of uncertainty. Persistent 
global political tensions led to ongoing conflicts in 
several countries, further impacting geopolitics and 
fragmenting international trade. Under these 
conditions, Bank Indonesia projects global economic 
growth to decelerate to 3.2% in 2024 and decline 
further to 3.1% by 2026.  
Di sisi lain, perekonomian Indonesia terus 
menunjukkan pertumbuhan positif sepanjang tahun 
2024. Pertumbuhan ini didukung oleh tingginya 
permintaan domestik serta stabilitas harga yang 
terjaga, berkat konsistensi kebijakan moneter Bank 
Indonesia dan koordinasi erat dengan kebijakan 
pemerintah. Bank Indonesia memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia berada pada 
kisaran 4,7%-5,5% dan diperkirakan akan terus 
meningkat di tahun-tahun mendatang.  
On the other hand, Indonesia's economy maintained 
positive growth throughout 2024. This growth was 
supported by robust domestic demand and stable 
prices, underpinned by consistent monetary policies 
from Bank Indonesia and close coordination with 
government policies. Bank Indonesia projects 
Indonesia's economic growth to be in the range of 
4.7%-5.5%, with expectations of continued 
improvement in the coming years.  
Selain itu, aliran investasi portofolio hingga triwulan 
III 2024 tercatat tinggi, mencapai USD 11,0 miliar, 
dengan inflow terbesar berasal dari instrumen SRBI, 
diikuti oleh SBN domestik, global, dan saham. 
Sementara itu, nilai tukar Rupiah tetap terkendali. 
Hingga 
Desember 
2024, 
Rupiah 
mengalami 
pelemahan sebesar 4,34% (point-to-point) atau 
3,74% (rerata) dibandingkan dengan level tahun 
2023. 
Namun, 
pelemahan 
ini 
lebih 
rendah 
dibandingkan nilai tukar mata uang negara-negara 
EMEs 
lainnya. 
Dengan 
fundamental 
ekonomi 
Indonesia yang kuat, nilai tukar Rupiah diperkirakan 
akan tetap stabil ke depannya.
Additionally, portfolio investment inflows remained 
strong, reaching USD 11.0 billion by the third quarter 
of 2024, with the largest inflows coming from SRBI 
instruments, followed by domestic and global 
government bonds, as well as equities. Meanwhile, the 
Rupiah exchange rate remained manageable. As of 
December 2024, the Rupiah depreciated by 4.34% 
(point-to-point) or 3.74% (average) compared to its 
level in 2023. However, this depreciation was lower 
than that of other EMEs' currencies. Supported by 
Indonesia’s strong economic fundamentals, the Rupiah 
is expected to remain stable moving forward.

Digital Sector Review

perkembangan

Indonesia's digital economy in 2024 recorded 
significant growth, focusing on operational efficiency 
and profitability. The total digital economy transaction 
value reached USD90 billion (approximately Rp1,420 
trillion), an increase of about 13% compared to the 
previous year. This growth was led by the e-commerce 
sector, which achieved a Gross Merchandise Value 
(GMV) of USD65 billion (approximately Rp1,020 
trillion). Additionally, the digital financial services 
sector showed remarkable growth, with digital 
payments rising by 19% and generating a Gross 
Transaction Value (GTV) of USD404 billion. The online 
transportation 
and 
food 
delivery 
sectors 
also 
demonstrated positive progress, each recording GMVs 
of USD3 billion and USD 6 billion, respectively.
Peningkatan ini didukung oleh investasi besar dalam 
infrastruktur teknologi, termasuk jaringan 4G dan 5G, 
perluasan akses internet, dan pembangunan pusat 
data. Sektor e-commerce menjadi pendorong utama, 
berkat pertumbuhan belanja online, kemudahan 
pembayaran digital, dan strategi promosi yang 
terarah. Digitalisasi layanan keuangan, seperti 
dompet digital dan QRIS, memperluas inklusi 
keuangan dan mempermudah transaksi. Faktor lain 
yang berkontribusi adalah perubahan perilaku 
konsumen sejak pandemi, dukungan pemerintah 
melalui insentif untuk startup serta digitalisasi 
UMKM, dan masuknya investasi asing. Selain itu, 
peningkatan 
literasi 
digital 
melalui 
edukasi 
masyarakat dan pelatihan UMKM menciptakan 
ekosistem digital yang lebih dinamis dan inklusif.
This growth was supported by substantial investments 
in technology infrastructure, including 4G and 5G 
networks, 
expanded 
internet 
access, 
and 
the 
development of data centers. The e-commerce sector 
was the main driver, supported by increased online 
shopping, seamless digital payment options, and 
targeted promotional strategies. The digitalization of 
financial services, such as e-wallets and QRIS, 
enhanced 
financial 
inclusion 
and 
simplified 
transactions. Other contributing factors included shifts 
in consumer behavior since the pandemic, government 
support through incentives for startups and the 
digitalization of MSMEs, and the influx of foreign 
investment. Furthermore, improved digital literacy 
through public education and MSME training fostered 
a more dynamic and inclusive digital ecosystem.

Amendment to PSAK 1, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Long-term Liabilities with Covenants
This amendment clarifies that only covenants that an 
entity is required to comply with on or before the 
reporting date shall affect the classification of 
liabilities as current or non-current.
The entity applies PSAK 1 (October 2020) regarding 
the classification of liabilities as current or non-current 
for annual reporting periods beginning on or after 
January 1, 2024, retrospectively in accordance with 
PSAK 25. If the entity applies the October 2020 
amendment earlier, after the issuance of the December 
2022 amendment on long-term liabilities with 
covenants, the entity shall also apply the December 
2022 amendment in that period. If the amendment is 
applied in an earlier period, the entity discloses this 
fact.
Amendment to PSAK 1, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Classification of Liabilities as Current or 
Non-current
This amendment clarifies that liabilities are classified 
as current or non-current based on rights that exist at 
the end of the reporting period.
The classification is not affected by the entity’s 
expectations or events after the reporting date (e.g., 
receiving a waiver or covenant breach). The 
amendment also clarifies what is meant by 
“settlement” of a liability under PSAK 1.
These changes may affect the classification of 
liabilities, particularly for entities that previously 
considered management’s intentions in determining 
classification, and for some liabilities that are 
convertible into equity.
Amendments to PSAK 2, “Statements of Cash Flows” 
and PSAK 107, “Financial Instruments – Disclosures”: 
Supplier Finance Arrangements
These amendments clarify the characteristics of 
supplier finance arrangements and require additional 
disclosures related to such arrangements. The 
disclosure requirements are intended to help users of 
Financial Statements understand the effects of supplier 
finance arrangements on an entity’s liabilities, cash 
flows, and exposure to liquidity risk.
