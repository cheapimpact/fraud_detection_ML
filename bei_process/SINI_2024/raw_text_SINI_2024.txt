ANALISIS KONDISI PEREKONOMIAN NASIONAL 
ANALYSIS OF NATIONAL ECONOMIC CONDITIONS
Amid high uncertainty and the dynamics of the global economy, 
the Central Statistics Agency (BPS) reported that Indonesia’s 
economy in 2024 managed to grow by 5.03%, slightly lower than 
the previous year’s growth of 5.05%. However, economic growth 
remained at the 5% level, signifying that Indonesia has strong 
economic fundamentals. The solid economic performance 
through the end of the year was marked by positive growth 
across all expenditure components. Household consumption 
grew by 4.91%, contributing 53.08% to the Gross Domestic Prod-
uct (GDP). Consumption remained strong in line with the imple-
mentation of a policy mix to maintain purchasing power.
Meanwhile, Bank Indonesia projected that Indonesia’s econ-
omy in 2024 would grow within the range of 4.7%–5.5%, with a 
midpoint of 5.1%, and is expected to increase in 2025. Sustained 
household consumption, investment, and non-oil and gas ex-
ports continued to drive the economy towards the end of 2024, 
alongside government spending, which supported domestic de-
mand.
Spatially, Indonesia’s economy recorded stable growth. The 
group of provinces on Java Island remains the largest economic 
contributor, accounting for 56.84% of the national GDP, with a 
year-on-year (YoY) growth of 4.92%. Meanwhile, Kalimantan con-
tributed 8.15% to the economy, with a YoY growth of 5.20%.

ANALISIS INDUSTRI BATU BARA 
COAL INDUSTRY ANALYSIS
Pasar batu bara dunia dan domestik cenderung stabil di tahun 
2024 apabila dibandingkan dengan tahun 2023. Hal ini didorong 
oleh peningkatan kebutuhan listrik di negara-negara berkem-
bang seperti India dan Tiongkok. Permintaan yang meningkat 
dari negara-negara berkembang ini berhasil mengimbangi pe-
nurunan permintaan di negara-negara ekonomi maju seperti 
Uni Eropa dan Amerika Serikat. Faktor lain yang mempengaruhi 
stabilitas pasar batu bara adalah kebijakan dan situasi di pasar 
China, serta pergerakan harga gas dan pertumbuhan energi 
terbarukan. Selain itu, potensi ekspor batu bara Indonesia juga 
dipengaruhi oleh kondisi ekonomi negara-negara tujuan ekspor 
seperti China, Jepang, dan India. 
Badan Energi Internasional (IEA) memprediksi konsumsi batu 
bara global mencapai 8,77 miliar ton pada tahun 2024, dan 
diproyeksikan akan bertahan di level tersebut hingga tahun 
2027. Menurut Bank Dunia, rata-rata harga batu bara pada ta-
hun 2024 diproyeksikan mencapai US$125 per ton, turun 27,7% 
dibandingkan tahun 2023. Menurut data dari BP Statistical Re-
view of World Energy 2024, produsen batu bara terbesar di dunia 
masih dipegang oleh Tiongkok yang menghasilkan 3.342 juta ton 
Inflation in 2024 remained low, with the Government and Bank 
Indonesia successfully achieving the inflation target of 1.57% 
(YoY), within the target range of ±1% – 2.5%. This was the result of 
monetary policy, fiscal policy, and coordinated inflation control 
efforts at both the central and regional levels. The 2024 inflation 
realization declined from 2.61% (YoY) in 2023, marking the lowest 
level in the past two decades and is expected to support sus-
tained economic growth.
The global and domestic coal markets remained relatively stable 
in 2024 compared to 2023. This was driven by increased elec-
tricity demand in developing countries such as India and China. 
The growing demand from these countries successfully offset 
the decline in demand from developed economies such as the 
European Union and the United States. Other factors contribut-
ing to market stability included policy developments and market 
conditions in China, fluctuations in gas prices, and the growth of 
renewable energy. Additionally, Indonesia’s coal export potential 
was influenced by the economic conditions of key export desti-
nations such as China, Japan, and India.
The International Energy Agency (IEA) projected global coal 
consumption to reach 8.77 billion tons in 2024, with the level ex-
pected to remain steady through 2027. According to the World 
Bank, the average coal price in 2024 is projected to be US$125 
per ton, a 27.7% decrease from 2023. Data from the BP Statisti-
cal Review of World Energy 2024 shows that China remains the

TINJAUAN OPERASIONAL
OPERATIONAL REVIEW
Berdasarkan Anggaran Dasar Perseroan, disebutkan bahwa 
hingga akhir tahun 2024, Perseroan memiliki segmen usaha pen-
jualan batu bara, penjualan kayu dan pendapatan penginapan.

Usaha Batu bara
Salah satu anak usaha Perseroan adalah PT Dwi Daya Swakarsa 
(DDS) yang memiliki empat anak usaha di bidang pertambangan 
batu bara dengan status belum beroperasi. Perseroan tersebut 
436 million tons per year. The countries that rely most heavily on 
coal for energy are China, followed by India, the United States, 
Japan, and South Korea.
In a press release, Indonesia’s Ministry of Energy and Mineral Re-
sources (ESDM) stated that the country currently holds 97.29 bil-
lion tons of coal resources and 31.71 billion tons of coal reserves. 
Of this total, 70% consists of low-quality coal, while the remain-
ing 30% comprises medium- and high-quality coal. The majority 
of these resources and reserves are located in East Kalimantan, 
South Sumatra, South Kalimantan, Central Kalimantan, and Jam-
bi. The rest are distributed across Riau, North Kalimantan, Aceh, 
Bengkulu, West Sumatra, Papua, West Sulawesi, and western 
parts of Java.
According to the 2024 Work Plan and Budget (RKAB) from the 
Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources, the national coal pro-
duction target for 2024 was set at 955 million tons to maintain 
domestic coal reserves. Actual coal production in 2024 reached 
813.05 million tons, or 117.05% of the target. Meanwhile, the coal 
reference price (HBA) increased toward the end of the year to 
USD 122.51/ton, supported by coal imports to China in Desember.
The rise in production and recent price increases have prompted 
many coal mining and supporting companies to optimize their 
performance. Coal mining companies are implementing effective 
strategies in setting coal transportation service rates to avoid 
significant price spikes that could negatively impact business 
operations. According to the International Energy Agency (IEA), 
in 2024, coal continued to account for approximately 37% of the 
world’s total electricity generation.

Solvency Ratio
The solvency ratio measures the Company’s ability to meet all of 
its liabilities using total assets or equity. The solvency ratio can 
be calculated using 2 (two) approaches as follows:
1.	 Total liabilities divided by total equity (equity solvency)/DER
2.	 Total liabilities divided by total assets (asset solvency)/DAR
Based on the calculations for 2024, the Company’s two solven-

Company is still in a healthy condition. However, it requires more 
attention in debt management to improve its capital structure 
and reduce other financial risks.

Prospek industri batu bara pada tahun 2025 akan sangat dipen-
garuhi oleh dinamika pertumbuhan ekonomi, perubahan kebija-
kan energi, dan permintaan pasar. Kementerian ESDM menarg-
etkan produksi batu bara sebesar 735 juta ton pada tahun 2025. 
Target ini naik 3,52% dibandingkan target produksi di tahun 2024 
yang sebesar 710 juta ton.
Meskipun ada tantangan yang terkait dengan transisi energi, 
peluang tetap ada, terutama di pasar-pasar yang masih bergan-
tung pada batu bara sebagai sumber energi utama. Banyak ne-
gara berkomitmen untuk mengurangi ketergantungan pada batu 
bara dan beralih ke sumber energi terbarukan mengikuti tren 
global menuju transisi energi yang lebih bersih dan berkelanju-
tan, namun transisi energi hijau masih berjalan lambat di berb-
agai negara. 
Pada tahun 2025 diproyeksikan kebutuhan energi masih tinggi, 
dan batu bara sering kali menjadi pilihan utama untuk memenuhi 
permintaan tersebut. Oleh karena itu, industri batu bara dapat 
melihat peluang pertumbuhan di pasar-pasar ini, terutama di 
Asia Tenggara, India, Tiongkok, dan beberapa bagian Afrika.
PT Singaraja Putra Tbk senantiasa berupaya mengelola dan 
mempertahankan kelangsungan usahanya dalam jangka pan-
jang secara maksimal dengan memanfaatkan peluang yang ada, 
mengelola risiko dengan baik, dan beradaptasi dengan peruba-
han pasar, prospek dan kelangsungan usaha Perseroan di tahun 
2025 bisa menjadi positif dengan tetap berhati-hati dan waspa-
da terhadap tantangan dan risiko yang mungkin timbul.

market demand. The Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources 
(ESDM) has set a coal production target of 735 million tons for 
2025, representing a 3.52% increase compared to the 2024 tar-
get of 710 million tons.
Despite challenges related to the energy transition, opportuni-
ties remain—particularly in markets that continue to rely on coal 
as a primary energy source. While many countries have com-
mitted to reducing their reliance on coal and shifting towards 
renewable energy in line with the global trend toward a cleaner 
and more sustainable energy transition, the pace of green ener-
gy adoption remains slow in several regions.
In 2025, energy demand is projected to remain high, with coal 
often being the primary option to meet such demand. Conse-
quently, the coal industry may continue to find growth opportu-
nities in these markets, especially in Southeast Asia, India, China, 
and parts of Africa.
