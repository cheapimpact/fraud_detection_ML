The collectability of accounts receivable provides an 
overview of the Company's ability to collect all of its 
accounts receivable. In its assessment, the Company uses the 
accounts receivable turnover ratio and the average collection 
time of accounts receivable, which can be seen from the age 
of accounts receivable or accounts receivable disbursement 
period. In 2024, the receivables collectibility rate is still in 
the range of 0-90 days. The Company will only deliver 
products if the customer has completed the payment.
Capital Structure
The Company proactively and regularly reviews and manages 
its capital structure with the aim of providing maximum 
returns to Shareholders while reducing the cost of capital. 
Capital structure management is carried out by adjusting 
strategies based on the dynamics of economic conditions. In 
maintaining and adjusting the capital structure, the Company 
may adjust the dividend payment policy to Shareholders, 
issue new shares, or seek additional funding through loans.
The Company assesses its capital structure using the Gearing 
Ratio, which compares net debt to capital. Net debt is 
calculated as total liabilities less cash and cash equivalents, 
while capital includes all equity attributable to equity 
holders of the Company.
