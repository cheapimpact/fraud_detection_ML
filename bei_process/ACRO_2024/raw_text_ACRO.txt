Global Economic Growth in 2024The IMF projects
global economic growth to remain stable at 3.2%
in 2024 and 2025. The increase is driven by a
stronger-than-expected US economy, although
inflationary pressures and tight monetary policy
still loom. Meanwhile, the Chinese and European
economies are slowing due to weak domestic
demand and other structural factors.
However, risks such as geopolitical tensions and
high interest rates remain challenges. The IMF
assesses that global inflation has begun to ease,
but remains above the targets of several central
banks. The global economy is experiencing
moderate growth. Despite challenges such as
geopolitical 
tensions 
and 
financial 
market
volatility, the manufacturing sector, including the
textile

has

resilience 
and
adaptation through innovation and product
diversification.
Meanwhile, the national economy in the fourth
quarter of 2024 strengthened amidst global
financial market uncertainties. According to data
from Statistics Indonesia (BPS), the economy
grew by 5.02% year-on-year (yoy) in Q4 2024,
higher than the 4.95% (yoy) growth recorded in
the previous quarter. With this development,
Indonesia's overall economic growth for 2025 is
projected to remain solid, within a range of 4.7%
to 
5.5% 
(yoy). 
The 
improved 
economic
performance in Q4 2024 was supported by
sustained 
domestic 
activity. 
Household
consumption grew by 4.98% (yoy), in line with
vibrant economic activities and high public
mobility during the National Religious Holidays
(HBKN) period, including Christmas and New Year
celebrations. Investment growth remained robust
at 5.03% (yoy), driven by increased realized
investments. Government spending continued to
expand 
by 
4.17% 
(yoy), 
supported

the

year-end 
expenditures. 
The

Meanwhile,

strong growth of 6.06% (yoy), in line with
increased activity during the 2024 regional
elections (Pilkada). Exports grew by 7.63% (yoy),
supported by sustained demand from key
trading partners, rising prices of several main
export commodities, and higher services exports
driven by an increase in international tourist
arrivals. [203-2]

In 2024, Indonesia's textile and textile products
(TPT) industry Overall, despite recovery efforts
through 
protection 
policies, 
Indonesia's 
TPT
industry in 2024 still faces significant challenges,
including competition with imported products
and a declining labor force. measures such as
safeguard measure import duties (BMTP) are
expected 
to 
support 
the 
recovery 
and
competitiveness of the domestic industry. Long-
term prospects depend on innovation, efficiency,
and sustainable policies.
In the second quarter, the sector contracted by
0.03% on an annual basis (year-on-year/yoy), in
line with the decline in labor due to the closure of
several factories. However, in the third quarter,
the textile industry showed a recovery with
growth reaching 7.43% (yoy). This increase was
driven by the implementation of safeguard
measure import duties (BMTP) on imported fabric
and carpet products, which provided protection
for domestic producers. Bisnis.com
Based on the Review of Data from the Central
Statistics Agency (BPS), the textile and textile
product (TPT) industry experienced challenges
and 
recovery 
in 
2024. 
Initially, 
this 
sector
contracted due to mass layoffs and competition
with 
imported 
products. 
However, 
trade
protection policies helped the industry recover
with positive growth in the second half of this
year.
However, the TPT industry still faces challenges,
especially the surge in imports triggered by the
import relaxation policy in the Minister of Trade
Regulation Number 8 of 2024. This policy has
caused an increase in the volume of TPT imports,
which has an impact on the decline in the utility
of small and medium industries and the closure
of several factories. As a result, workers have
been laid off. ​Cobisnis
