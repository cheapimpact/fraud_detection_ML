Economic Overview
Overall, global economic conditions in 2022 
were still dominated by a slowdown in eco-
nomic growth for many countries around the 
world. According to the International Mone-
tary Fund (IMF), Indonesia is among the 20 
largest economies in the world.
This is noted in the October 2023 edition of 
the World Economic Outlook database. Ru-
piah exchange rate stability is maintained 
supported by Bank Indonesia's stabilization 
policy amidst the strong US dollar.
Indonesia's economy in 2023 faces challeng-
es stemming from weakening global eco-
nomic growth and widening growth diver-
gence between countries. After recording a 
high growth of 3.5% in 2022, world econom-
ic growth in 2023 is forecast to fall to 3.0%, 
and continue to slow down to 2.8% in 2024. 
Optimism for recovery in early 2023 gradu-
ally faded as the scarring effect continued 
and geopolitical-economic fragmentation 
increased.
Rupiah exchange rate stability was main-
tained supported by Bank Indonesia's stabili-
zation policy amidst the strong US dollar. The 
Rupiah exchange rate at the end of 2023 was 
recorded to appreciate by 1.11% (point to point) 
compared to the end of 2022. This develop-
ment is better than the appreciation of region-
al currencies, such as the Thai Baht and Phil-
ippine Peso by 1.03%, and 0.62%, respectively.

Due to Indonesia’s rich nickel reserves, the 
Government has implemented downstream 
policies in the mineral and coal sector via 
Law No. 3/2020 on the Amendments to Law 
No. 4/2009, concerning Mineral and Coal Min-
ing, in order to optimise nickel use from up-
stream to downstream. Through the imple-
mentation of this policy, Indonesia is set to 
become a major producer of nickelbased 
products such as lithium, the material used 
in batteries for electric vehicles. This policy 
marks the start of Indonesia’s green econo-
my development moving forward.
The success of down streaming nickel into 
lithium batteries, along with the electric au-
tomotive industry and its various derivatives, 
demonstrates an ability to encourage higher 
economic growth and strengthen external re-
silience in the Indonesian economy, according 
to the “Endogenous Growth Model” approach.
The increase in the export added value of 
nickel processing products has been able to 
boost growth on the aggregate demand side 
and facilitate a healthier current transaction.
The number of battery-based electric motor-
ized vehicles in 2023 increased significant-
ly compared to the previous year, totaling 
62,409 units of electric motors and 12,248 
units of electric cars.

utama produk-produk barang yang berbasis

kan dalam kendaraan listrik. Kebijakan terse-
but menjadi awal dari pembangunan ekonomi
