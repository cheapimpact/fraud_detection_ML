Tinjauan Ekonomi
Economic Review
Tahun 2023 menjadi saksi dari perlambatan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global, menurut laporan 
International Monetary Fund (IMF). Pertumbuhan 
ekonomi global turun menjadi 3,0% dari 3,5% pada 
tahun sebelumnya. Penurunan ini menjadi sorotan 
utama, mencerminkan tantangan yang dihadapi 
oleh berbagai faktor yang belum terselesaikan di 
berbagai belahan dunia.
Salah satu faktor utama penyebab perlambatan ini 
adalah ketidakpastian yang berkelanjutan dalam 
politik dan ekonomi global. Konflik politik, baik di 
tingkat domestik maupun internasional, telah 
menciptakan ketidakstabilan yang merembet ke 
pasar keuangan global. 
Inflasi juga menjadi penyebab dari perlambatan 
ekonomi global. Gangguan pasokan energi dan 
pangan telah menyebabkan lonjakan inflasi 
yang signifikan di beberapa wilayah, memaksa 
bank sentral untuk merespons dengan kenaikan 
suku bunga. Kenaikan suku bunga global yang 
diantisipasi untuk jangka waktu yang lebih lama 
memperkuat dolar AS dan meningkatkan Federal 
Funds Rate (FFR).
Fenomena "cash is king" juga turut berkontribusi 
dalam 
konteks 
ketidakpastian 
ini. 
Tingginya 
persepsi 
risiko 
di 
kalangan 
investor 
global 
mendorong mereka untuk menarik modal dari 
negara-negara 
berkembang, 
menyebabkan 
aliran modal keluar dari pasar yang lebih rentan.
Namun, di tengah perlambatan ekonomi global, 
Indonesia menunjukkan tanda-tanda pemulihan. 
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia tetap kuat, 
berada di angka 5,05%, sebuah pencapaian yang 
baik jika dibandingkan dengan negara-negara di 
kawasan ASEAN maupun G20. 
In 2023, the global economy witnessed a slowdown 
in growth, according to the International Monetary 
Fund (IMF) report. Global economic growth 
declined to 3.0% from 3.5% in the previous year. 
This decline was a major highlight, reflecting the 
challenges faced by various unresolved factors 
across the globe.
One of the primary causes of this slowdown is 
the ongoing uncertainty in global politics and 
economics. Political conflicts, both domestically 
and internationally, have created instability that 
has spilled over into the global financial markets.
Inflation has also contributed to the global 
economic slowdown. Disruptions in energy and 
food supplies have led to significant inflation 
spikes in several regions, forcing central banks 
to respond with interest rate hikes. Anticipated 
global interest rate hikes for a longer period have 
strengthened the US dollar and increased the 
Federal Funds Rate (FFR).
The 
"cash 
is 
king" 
phenomenon 
has 
also 
contributed to this situation of uncertainty. The 
high perceived risk among global investors has 
led them to withdraw capital from developing 
countries, causing capital outflows from more 
vulnerable markets.
However, amidst the global economic slowdown, 
Indonesia has been showing signs of recovery. 
Indonesia's economic growth remains strong, at 
5.05%, a commendable achievement compared 
to other countries in the ASEAN and G20 regions.
Pemulihan ekonomi Indonesia didorong oleh 
pertumbuhan 
investasi, 
terutama 
di 
sektor 
properti 
yang 
mengalami 
peningkatan 
dari 
tahun sebelumnya. Konsumsi rumah tangga 
juga menunjukkan peningkatan, menciptakan 
momentum pertumbuhan yang berkelanjutan 
bagi ekonomi Indonesia.
Indonesia's economic recovery is driven by 
investment growth, especially in the property 
sector, which has increased from the previous 
year. 
Household 
consumption 
also 
shows 
improvement, 
creating 
sustainable 
growth 
momentum for the Indonesian economy.
Tinjauan Industri
Industrial Review
Sepanjang 
tahun 
2023, 
Gabungan 
Industri 
Kendaraan 
Bermotor 
Indonesia 
(Gaikindo) 
melaporkan penjualan mobil domestik di Indonesia 
menunjukkan kinerja di bawah target yang telah 
ditetapkan sebelumnya, dengan angka penjualan 
mencapai 1,00 juta unit secara wholesales dan 
998,06 unit secara retail. Hal ini menunjukkan 
penurunan sebesar 4% dibandingkan dengan 
tahun 
sebelumnya 
untuk 
penjualan 
secara 
wholesales, dan penurunan sebesar 1,5% untuk 
penjualan secara retail.
Penurunan kinerja penjualan ini disebabkan oleh 
perlambatan pasar otomotif pada semester kedua 
tahun 2023. Faktor utama yang berkontribusi 
terhadap perlambatan tersebut adalah kenaikan 
suku bunga oleh Bank Indonesia (BI). Suku bunga 
acuan BI, yang mencapai tingkat tertinggi dalam 
4,5 tahun terakhir, menjadi salah satu faktor utama 
yang mempengaruhi penurunan penjualan mobil. 
Pada Januari 2023, suku bunga acuan naik dari 
5,5% menjadi 5,75%, dan kemudian dikerek kembali 
pada Oktober 2023 menjadi 6%, yang bertahan 
hingga akhir tahun.
Throughout 2023, the Indonesian Automotive 
Industries 
Association 
(Gaikindo) 
reported 
domestic car sales in Indonesia had showed 
performance below the previously set targets, 
with sales figures reaching 1.00 million units on a 
wholesale basis and 998.06 units on a retail basis. 
This indicates a decrease of 4% compared to the 
previous year for wholesale sales and a decrease 
of 1.5% for retail sales.
The decline in sales performance is attributed 
to the slowdown in the automotive market in the 
second semester of 2023. The main contributing 
factor to this slowdown is the increase in interest 
rates by the Bank of Indonesia (BI). BI's benchmark 
interest rate, which reached its highest level in the 
last 4.5 years, became one of the main factors 
affecting the decline in car sales. In January 2023, 
the benchmark interest rate rose from 5.5% to 
5.75%, and was then raised again in October 2023 
to 6%, which remained until the end of the year.

Prospek Usaha Perusahaan
Company Business Prospect
Industri otomotif Indonesia menghadapi masa 
depan yang cerah dengan optimisme yang tinggi 
pada tahun 2024. Menurut laporan Gaikindo, tren 
investasi di sektor otomotif diprediksi akan tumbuh 
positif dalam tahun mendatang. Peluang besar 
tampaknya terletak pada industri kendaraan listrik 
(Electric Vehicle/EV), yang menandai perubahan 
monumental dalam lanskap otomotif global.
Indonesia dipandang sebagai salah satu pemain 
utama dalam industri kendaraan listrik, dan bukan 
tanpa alasan. Negara ini memiliki potensi besar 
untuk menjadi pusat industri EV karena sumber 
daya alamnya yang melimpah, terutama dalam 
hal bahan baku. Nikel, yang merupakan komponen 
kunci dalam pembuatan baterai kendaraan 
listrik, tersedia dalam jumlah besar di Indonesia. 
Keberadaan sumber daya alam ini memberikan 
keunggulan 
kompetitif 
yang 
signifikan 
bagi 
Indonesia dalam persaingan global.
Dibandingkan dengan negara-negara ASEAN 
lainnya yang juga memiliki industri kendaraan 
bermotor, Indonesia terbilang unggul. Kehadiran 
sumber daya alam yang kaya menjadikan 
Indonesia sebagai pilihan yang sangat menarik 
bagi investor dalam industri otomotif. Pasar 
domestik yang besar dengan kontribusi 31% 
dari total pasar ASEAN juga menjadi daya tarik 
tersendiri bagi investasi dalam pengembangan 
dan produksi kendaraan.
Potensi industri kendaraan bermotor Indonesia 
tidak hanya terbatas pada EV. Meskipun perhatian 
global beralih ke kendaraan ramah lingkungan, 
pertumbuhan 
kendaraan 
konvensional 
juga 
terus meningkat. Dengan didukung populasi 
Indonesia yang besar, yaitu lebih dari 280 juta 
jiwa, mengindikasikan bahwa permintaan akan 
kendaraan bermotor akan terus ada.
The Indonesian automotive industry is facing 
a bright future with high optimism in 2024. 
According 
to 
Gaikindo's 
report, 
investment 
trends in the automotive sector are predicted to 
grow positively in the coming year. Significant 
opportunities seem to lie in the electric vehicle 
(EV) industry, marking a monumental shift in the 
global automotive landscape.
Indonesia is seen as one of the major players 
in the electric vehicle industry, and not without 
reason. The country has great potential to 
become a center for the EV industry due to its 
abundant natural resources, especially in terms 
of raw materials. Nickel, which is a key component 
in the production of electric vehicle batteries, is 
available in large quantities in Indonesia. The 
presence of these natural resources provides a 
significant competitive advantage for Indonesia 
in the global competition.
Compared to other ASEAN countries that also 
have motor vehicle industries, Indonesia is 
considered superior. The presence of rich natural 
resources makes Indonesia a very attractive 
choice for investors in the automotive industry. 
The large domestic market, contributing 31% of 
the total ASEAN market, also serves as a unique 
attraction for investment in the development and 
production of vehicles.
The 
potential 
of 
Indonesia's 
motor 
vehicle 
industry is not limited to EVs. Although global 
attention is shifting to environmentally friendly 
vehicles, the growth of conventional vehicles 
continues to increase. Supported by Indonesia's 
large population, which is over 280 million people, 
indicates that the demand for motor vehicles will 
continue to exist.

baku 
tiba,

Perseroan 
memulai 
tahapan 
produksi komponen tersebut. Setiap komponen

dengan 
peralatan 
khusus

peralatan tersebut tidak dapat digunakan untuk 
memproduksi komponen lainnya. Setelah melalui

The

that 
has

the 
bid 
to 
manufacture 
specific 
components 
for

is based on the agreement and purchase orders 
from the buyer. After ordering the required raw 
materials for each component, and upon the 
arrival of the raw materials, the company ensures 
the quantity and quality of the raw materials before 
they are placed in the warehouse for production.
Subsequently, 
the 
Company 
initiates 
the 
production stage of those components. Each 
component is manufactured using specialized 
equipment that cannot be used to produce other 
components. After undergoing welding/stamping 
processes, the product becomes a semi-finished 
good or Work-in-Progress (WIP). The WIP product 
is then processed through the finishing stage to 
become a finished product.

Dividend Policy
The Company plans to distribute dividends to its 
shareholders with a minimum value of 20.00% 
(twenty percent) of the net profit for the respective 
fiscal year, starting from the year 2022 based on the 
current year's profit for the fiscal year 2021, taking 
into account the decisions of the shareholders in 
the Annual General Meeting (RUPS).
If the RUPS approves the dividend distribution, the 
dividends will be distributed to all shareholders 
recorded on the shareholders' register date 
entitled to the dividends, taking into account 
income tax and withholding tax according to 
applicable regulations, if any.
The determination of the amount and distribution 
of dividends will depend on the recommendation 
of the Company's Board of Directors, considering 
several factors including:
•	
Retained earnings, business and financial 
performance, financial condition, liquidity 
condition, future business prospects (including 
capital expenditure and acquisitions), cash 
needs, business opportunities;
•	
Dividend distribution by the Company; and 
Subsidiaries; and
•	
Other factors deemed relevant by the Board 
of Directors. The Company's Board of Directors 
may change the dividend policy at any time, 
subject to approval from the shareholders 
through the GMS.
