pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia tetap baik, terutama ditopang 
oleh kenaikan investasi dan terjaganya konsumsi rumah tangga, 
serta peningkatan belanja pemerintah. Dari sisi lapangan usaha, 
pertumbuhan ekonomi terjadi pada seluruh lapangan usaha 
dengan kontributor utama dari industri pengolahan, konstruksi, 
serta perdagangan besar dan eceran, reparasi mobil dan sepeda 
motor. Sementara pada sisi pengeluaran, pertumbuhan ekonomi 
tetap solid dengan didukung pertumbuhan pengeluaran konsumsi 
rumah tangga, ekspor barang dan jasa, serta pembentukan modal 
tetap bruto.
Sejalan dengan perbaikan perekonomian Indonesia, industri 
pariwisata Indonesia pada tahun 2024 menunjukkan pertumbuhan 
yang kuat, mencerminkan meningkatnya mobilitas masyarakat 
serta daya tarik berbagai destinasi wisata di Indonesia. Jumlah 
perjalanan wisatawan nusantara mencapai 1,021 miliar perjalanan, 
meningkat 21,61% dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya mencapai 
839,67 juta perjalanan. Sedangkan, jumlah kunjungan wisatawan 
mancanegara (wisman) mencapai 13,90 juta kunjungan, meningkat 
19,05% dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya. Berdasarkan jenis 
pintu masuk, jumlah kunjungan wisatawan mancanegara pada 
tahun 2024 yang melalui pintu masuk utama sebanyak 11,95  juta 
kunjungan atau setara 85,96% dari jumlah kunjungan, sedangkan 
pintu masuk perbatasan sebanyak 1,95 juta kunjungan atau setara 
14,04% dari jumlah kunjungan. 
Secara kumulatif, peningkatan jumlah kunjungan wisman turut 
mendorong Tingkat Penghunian Kamar (TPK) hotel di indonesia. 
Di tahun 2024, TPK hotel bintang mencapai 52,57%, meningkat 
1,30 poin dibandingkan tahun 2023 yang tercatat sebesar 51,27%. 
Hotel bintang 4 mencatat TPK tertinggi sebesar 57,16%, sementara 
hotel non-bintang memiliki tingkat hunian terendah yakni 26,59%. 
The rapidly changing global geopolitical dynamics, accompanied 
by a high level of uncertainty, have influenced the development 
of the global economy throughout 2024. Global economic growth 
recorded a slight slowdown to 3.2% in 2024, down from 3.3% 
in 2023. This was due to geopolitical tensions involving major 
countries, which caused volatility in global commodity prices and 
disruptions in supply chains, ultimately affecting the balance of 
international trade and global investment. Additionally, economic 
policy uncertainty, partly due to leadership transitions in several 
key countries, also impacted market sentiment and long-term 
investment decisions across various sectors.
Similarly, national economic growth was recorded at 5.02% (year-
on-year),, slightly lower than the 5.04% growth achieved in 2023. 
Nevertheless, Indonesia’s economic performance remained solid, 
supported by increased investment, stable household consumption, 
and higher Government spending. From the business sector 
perspective, growth occurred across all sectors, with the main 
contributors being the manufacturing industry, construction, and 
wholesale and retail trade, including the vehicles and motorcycles 
repair. On the expenditure side, growth remained robust, driven by 
household consumption, exports of goods and services, and gross 
fixed capital formation.
In line with Indonesia’s improving economy, the tourism industry 
in 2024 showed strong growth, reflecting increased public mobility 
and the appeal of many tourist destinations in Indonesia. Domestic 
tourist trips reached 1.021 billion, an increase of 21.61% compared 
to 839.67 million trips in the previous year. Meanwhile, international 
tourist arrivals (foreign tourists) reached 13.90 million, marking a 
19.05% increase compared to the previous year. Based on the type 
of entry point, the number of international tourist arrivals in 2024 
through main entry points reached 11.95 million visits, equivalent 
to 85.96% of the total arrivals, while those through border entry 
points was recorded at 1.95 million visits, or 14.04% of the total 
arrivals.

to the rise in Room Occupancy Rates (ROR) in Indonesia. In 2024, 
the ROR of star-rated hotels reached 52.57%, an increase of 1.30 
percentage points from 51.27% in 2023. Four-star hotels recorded 
the highest ROR at 57.16%, while non-star hotels had the lowest

laba kotor Perseroan disebabkan oleh kenaikan pendapatan

laba kotor Perseroan di tahun 2024.

Prospek Usaha
Kondisi perekonomian global diproyeksikan tumbuh pada rentang 
2,7-3,3% pada tahun 2025. Sejumlah risiko ketidakpastian masih 
menjadi tantangan yang menyebabkan dinamika ekonomi global 
mulai dari ketidakpastian kebijakan ekonomi dan geopolitik, 
pelambatan ekonomi sejumlah negara, tingginya inflasi sejumlah 
negara, tekanan pasar keuangan global, perubahan iklim, hingga 
disrupsi rantai pasok. Meski demikian, pertumbuhan ekonomi 
nasional diproyeksikan berada dalam kisaran 4,7-5,5%, dan akan 
meningkat lebih tinggi pada tahun 2026 dalam kisaran 4,8-5,6%. 
Berbagai upaya terus ditempuh untuk mendorong pertumbuhan 
ekonomi, baik dari sisi permintaan maupun dari sisi penawaran. 
Untuk itu, Bank Indonesia memperkuat bauran kebijakan untuk 
mendorong pertumbuhan ekonomi agar lebih tinggi, serta 
bersinergi erat dengan kebijakan stimulus fiskal Pemerintah. Dari 
sisi penawaran, kebijakan reformasi struktural perlu terus diperkuat 
untuk mendorong sektor ekonomi yang dapat menyerap tenaga 
kerja. Upaya tersebut didukung dengan optimalisasi stimulus 
kebijakan makroprudensial dan akselerasi digitalisasi transaksi 
pembayaran yang ditempuh Bank Indonesia. 
Selain itu, pada industri pariwisata Indonesia, Pemerintah optimis 
menargetkan jumlah wisatawan mancanegara yang berkunjung ke 
Indonesia mencapai kisaran 14,6 juta – 16 juta kunjungan, sementara 
perjalanan wisatawan nusantara diharapkan mencapai 1,08 miliar 
perjalanan. Dengan kontribusi sektor pariwisata yang diproyeksikan 
mencapai 4,6% terhadap PDB, serta potensi devisa antara 
USD19 miliar hingga USD22,1 miliar, industri pariwisata masih 
menjadi salah satu pilar utama pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional.

Ketidakpastian Global, 18 Februari 2025”;

Business Outlook
The global economic condition is projected to grow within a 
range of 2.7-3.3% in 2025. A number of uncertainty risks remain a 
challenge that contributes to global economic dynamics, including 
uncertainties in economic and geopolitical policies, economic 
slowdowns in several countries, high inflation in various countries, 
global financial market pressures, climate change, and supply 
chain disruptions. Despite these challenges, national economic 
growth is expected to be within the range of 4.7-5.5% and projected 
much higher in 2026 within the range of 4.8-5.6%. Various efforts 
are continuously being made to push economic growth from 
both the demand and supply sides. To this end, Bank Indonesia is 
strengthening its policy mix to encourage higher economic growth, 
while closely coordinating with the Government’s fiscal stimulus 
policies. On the supply side, structural reform policies need to 
be further strengthened to stimulate sectors of the economy 
that can absorb labor. These efforts are supported by optimizing 
macroprudential policy stimuli and accelerating the digitization of 
payment transactions undertaken by Bank Indonesia.
Furthermore, in the Indonesian tourism industry, the Government 
is optimistic about targeting foreign tourist arrivals to Indonesia, 
which is expected to reach around 14.6 million – 16 million visits, 
while domestic tourist trips are expected to reach 1.08 billion trips. 
With the tourism sector’s contribution to GDP projected to reach 
4.6%, and foreign exchange potential between USD19 billion to 
USD22.1 billion, the tourism industry remains one of the key pillars 
for national economic growth.
