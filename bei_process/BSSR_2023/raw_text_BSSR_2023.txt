Tinjauan Ekonomi 
Economic Overview
Global economic growth in 2023 slowed down with the subsided 
financial market uncertainty amid the widening divergence among 
countries. Based on World Bank data in the Global Economic 
Prospect for January 2024, the global economy is estimated to 
slow down, from previously 3.0% (yoy) in 2022 to 2.6% (yoy) 
in 2023. The United States (US) economy grew quite strongly in 
2023, but still faced a number of risks in line with the increasing 
fiscal pressure, particularly the expense of debt interest payments 
and government debt ratio. Meanwhile, Europe’s economy is still 
weakening and China’s economy is likely to slow down due to the 
continuing property sector crisis and debt pressure on provincial 
governments. On the other side, the downward trend in global 
inflation continues, primarily in the US, thereby holding back 
pressure from increases in the Fed's benchmark interest rate and 
US Treasury yields. 
Amid the global uncertainty and slowdown, Indonesia’s economy 
continues to grow strongly and stably. Based on data from 
Statistics Indonesia in February 2024 edition of the Official News 
of Statistics, Indonesia’s economy in 2023 grew by 5.05%. The 
State Revenue and Expenditure Budget (APBN) acts as a shock 
absorber in maintaining people's purchasing power. Investment 
also had a strengthening trend since the first quarter of 2023 in 
line with the accelerated implementation of National Strategic 
Project (PSN). Meanwhile, the trade balance surplus that continued 
in December 2023 was recorded at USD3.3 billion, affected by 
the export performance of Indonesia's main commodities which 
remained strong, such as coal as well as iron and steel.

International Energy Agency (IEA) dalam Coal 2023 melaporkan 
bahwa permintaan batubara global meningkat sebesar 1,4% 
dan mencapai titik tertinggi sepanjang masa dengan jumlah 
melampaui 8,5 miliar ton untuk pertama kalinya. Meskipun 
permintaan global batubara meningkat, namun secara parsial 
telah terjadi penurunan permintaan dan konsumsi batubara di 
hampir semua negara maju. Penurunan tahunan terbesar terjadi 
di Uni Eropa dan Amerika Serikat sekitar 20%. Hal ini merupakan 
pengaruh dari adanya agenda transisi energi menuju energi 
terbarukan. Sebaliknya, konsumsi batubara di negara-negara 
berkembang masih sangat tinggi. Kenaikan terbesar permintaan 
batubara adalah di India dengan 8% dan Tiongkok sebesar 5%. 
Hal ini terjadi karena adanya peningkatan permintaan listrik dan 
lemahnya pasokan listrik dari pembangkit listrik tenaga air.
Di sisi lain, Indonesia sebagai produsen batubara ketiga terbesar 
di dunia, berhasil meningkatkan produksinya. Kementerian Energi 
dan Sumber Daya Mineral (ESDM) mencatat realisasi produksi 
batubara di 31 Desember 2023 mencapai 775 juta ton, atau 
sekitar 112% dari target yang ditetapkan sebesar 695 juta, 
sedangkan realisasi pemanfaatan batubara domestik (Domestic 
Market Obligation/DMO) mencapai 213 juta ton dan ekspor 518 
juta ton. Realisasi DMO mengalami peningkatan sebesar 177 juta 
ton seiring dengan meningkatnya permintaan untuk tambahan 
Pembangkit Listrik Tenaga Uap (PLTU) baru dari proyek-proyek 35 
GW yang sedang dalam proses penyelesaian dan terganggunya 
pasokan energi alternatif lainnya.
Sementara itu, Harga Acuan Batubara (HBA) mengalami 
penyesuaian baru dengan dikeluarkannya HBA Coal Index Minerba 
(CIM) oleh Kementerian ESDM. Rata-rata HBA tahun 2023 tercatat 
sebesar USD201,49 per ton, menurun dibandingkan tahun 2022 
sebesar USD276,58 per ton. Meskipun demikian, Kementerian 
ESDM melihat bahwa kondisi harga jual batubara dinilai masih 
tetap menguntungkan bagi badan usaha pertambangan batubara. 
Di samping itu, peningkatan produksi batubara dan fluktuatif 
harga batubara juga mendorong peningkatan Penerimaan Negara 
Bukan Pajak (PNBP) sektor mineral dan batubara (minerba). 
Sampai dengan 31 Desember 2023, setor PNPB dari sektor 
minerba mencapai Rp168,78 triliun atau 115,54% dari target 
PNBP Minerba.

APBI-ICMA Monthly Newsletter edisi Desember 2023 dan Januari 2024.
The International Energy Agency (IEA) in Coal 2023 reported 
that global coal demand increased by 1.4% and reached an all-
time high with the amount exceeding 8.5 billion tons for the first 
time. Although the global demand for coal increased, there was 
a partial decline in coal demand and consumption in almost all 
developed countries. The largest annual decline occurred in the 
European Union and the United States at around 20%. This is the 
influence of energy transition agenda towards renewable energy. 
In contrast, coal consumption in developing countries is still very 
high. The largest increase in coal demand occurred in India with 
8% and China with 5%. This occurred due to an increase in 
electricity demand and weak electricity supply from hydroelectric 
power plants.
On the other side, Indonesia, as the third largest coal producer 
in the world, managed to increase its production. The Ministry of 
Energy and Mineral Resources (ESDM) noted that the realization 
of coal production on 31 December 2023 reached 775 million 
tons, or around 112% of the target set at 695 million, while 
the realization of domestic coal utilization (Domestic Market 
Obligation/DMO) reached 213 million ton and exported 518 
million tons. DMO realization increased by 177 million tons in line 
with the increasing demand for additional new Steam Power Plant 
(PLTU) from 35 GW projects currently in the process of completion 
and disruption of other alternative energy supplies.
Meanwhile, Reference Coal Price (HBA) made new adjustments 
with the issuance of HBA Coal Index Minerba (CIM) by the Ministry 
of Energy and Mineral Resources. The average HBA in 2023 
was recorded at USD201.49 per ton, a decrease compared to 
USD276.58 per ton in 2022. Nevertheless, the Ministry of Energy 
and Mineral Resources viewed that coal sales price conditions 
were still considered profitable for coal mining business entities. 
Furthermore, increasing coal production and fluctuating coal 
prices have also encouraged an increase in Non-Tax State Revenue 
(PNBP) in mineral and coal (minerba) sector. As of 31 December 
2023, PNPB deposits from mineral and coal sector reached 
Rp168.78 trillion or 115.54% of Minerba PNBP target.

Business Prospects in 2024
The International Energy Agency (IEA) estimates that there will be 
a downward trend in coal demand throughout the world, starting 
in 2024. Growth in renewable energy that is higher than growth 
in total electricity demand will likely drive a decline in global coal 
consumption.

activities in Vietnam.

Proyeksi Tahun 2024
Dengan mempertimbangkan kondisi perekonomian dan industri 
serta kinerja saat ini dan prospek usaha ke depan, Perseroan telah 
merumuskan target usaha untuk tahun 2024. Volume produksi 
dan penjualan batubara Perseroan dan Entitas Anak ditargetkan 
meningkat sekitar 10%. Selain itu, Perseroan juga menargetkan 
peningkatan pendapatan dan laba tahun berjalan, masing-masing 
sebesar 5.78% dan 5.93%.
Informasi Kelangsungan Usaha
Hal yang Berpengaruh Signifikan terhadap 
Kelangsungan Usaha
Penyesuaian HBA dengan HBA Coal Index Minerba (CIM) oleh 
Kementerian ESDM menyebabkan menurunnya rata-rata HBA di 
tahun 2023. Namun, harga jual batubara dinilai masih dianggap 
menguntungkan, sehingga Perseroan mampu mempertahankan 
kinerja laba tetap positif, meskipun mengalami penurunan jika 
dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya.

Business Development in 2024
Business development strategies that will be carried out by the 
Company to achieve business targets in 2024 include:
1.	 Continuing its exploration activities in order to increase 
reserves or increase reserve accuracy
2.	 Increasing production to approximately 10%;
3.	 Striving for efficiency of operational activities and developing 
existing infrastructure in order to increase independence and 
reduce infrastructure dependency from third parties; and
4.	 Strengthening the Company's position in domestic market 
and continuing to penetrate other ASEAN countries, while 
maintaining the existing market.
Projections in 2024
By considering economic and industrial conditions as well as 
current performance and future business prospects, the Company 
has formulated business targets for 2024. The Company’s and 
Subsidiaries' coal production and sales volume are targeted 
to increase by around 10%. Furthermore, the Company is also 
targeting an increase in revenue and profit for the year, by 5,78% 
and 5,93%.
Information on Business Continuity
Matters with Significant Impact to Business 
Continuity
Adjustments of HBA to HBA Coal Index Minerba (CIM) by the 
Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources caused average HBA to 
decrease in 2023. However, coal sales price was still considered 
profitable, and therefore, the Company managed to maintain 
positive profit performance, though it declined compared to that 
of the previous year.

its business activities.
