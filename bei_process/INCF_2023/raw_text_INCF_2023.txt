ECONOMIC PERFORMANCE 
Closing 2023, geopolitical tensions in global markets 
will have various spillover impacts on the Indonesian 
economy, starting from the geopolitical conflict of the 
Russia-Ukraine and Palestine-Israel wars, to the rivalry 
in the South China Sea between China and the United 
States, which will continue to have an impact on the 
world economy. , including Indonesia. 
 
 
The first KSSK regular meeting Monday 29 January 
2024, Minister of Finance (MenKeu) Sri Mulyani 
Indrawati together with the Governor of Bank 
Indonesia (BI), Chair of the Board of Commissioners of 
the Financial Services Authority (OJK) produced a 
commitment to continue to strengthen coordination, 
synergy, and increase awareness of the risk of 
slowdown economy and continuing global uncertainty 
in 2024, while also being alert to the conditions and 
dynamics of the domestic economy and possible 
spillovers. 
 
 
Minister of Finance (MenKeu) Sri Mulyani Indrawati 
also said that despite the risk of a world economic 
slowdown and global financial market uncertainty, 
2024 will be an important phase for Indonesia's 
continued economic recovery. Solid and maintained 
economic growth and financial system stability mean 
that the domestic economy is still very resilient. On the 
other hand, macroeconomic indicators such as 
inflation, the rupiah exchange rate and foreign 
exchange reserves show stability. All of these factors 
are interrelated and very important to maintain 
people's 
purchasing 
power, 
increase 
export 
competitiveness, and support Indonesia's economic 
and financial stability. 
 
 
Apart from that, Arief Ramayandi, Principal Economist 
at the Department of Economic Research and Regional

said that the moment of the general election (election) 
will encourage the Indonesian economy to grow 5 
percent in 2024, this estimate is supported by the 
prospect of strong domestic demand. . This estimate 
was presented in the Asian Development Outlook 
(ADO) December 2023.

INDUSTRY OVERVIEW 
The challenges of the National Rubber Industry with 
various problems, ranging from low land productivity 
to far from adequate downstreaming, ensnare 
Indonesian rubber farmers. These challenges include 
the downward trend in world rubber prices, conversion 
of rubber plants, as well as issues of climate change 
and environmental sustainability. Apart from that, the 
world natural rubber market is also changing towards 
sustainable demand for natural rubber and its 
derivative products (Kompas, 4 September 2023) 
 
Director General of International Trade Negotiations, 
Ministry of Trade, Mr. Djatmiko Bris Witjaksono, SE, 
MSIE, at the Working Group Meeting on Sustainable 
Natural Rubber (WGSNR) ANRP International Trade 
Association of Natural Rubber Producing Countries 
(ANRPC) and press release on Saturday 2 September 
2023, Indonesia invites natural rubber producing 
countries that are members of ANRPC to continue to 
promote sustainability issues. It is hoped that close 
cooperation between ANRPC member countries can 
bring positive changes to the natural rubber industry 
and jointly maintain the integrity of the rubber sector 
while supporting global sustainability goals. 
 
 
 
According to the General Chairperson of GAPKINDO 
(Indonesian Rubber Entrepreneurs Association) on 
Mediaperkebunan.id that the natural rubber industry is 
currently the main thing that needs to be pursued is to 
increase the supply of raw materials, given the limited 
supply of raw materials due to the current decline in 
national rubber production. This effort is expected to 
ensure the continued existence of natural rubber in 
Indonesia in the future. GAPKINDO encourages the 
government to focus on the smallholder rubber 
rejuvenation 
program 
and 
assists 
farmers 
in 
overcoming disease attacks and asks the government 
to strive to increase national rubber productivity 
through smallholder rubber rejuvenation using 
superior seeds. Looking for diversification alternatives 
that are profitable and can increase farmers' income. 
Increasing the role of upstream and downstream 
institutions in the national rubbr sector.

Revenue and Expense Recognition 
The Company, through its subsidiaries, is engaged in 
the manufacturing natural rubber industry with other 
derivatives. Our customers range from all over 
Indonesia and abroad. 
 
 
In determining revenue recognition, the Company 
perform analysis transaction through the following five 
steps of assessment: 
 
1.  Identify contracts with customers with   certain 
criteria as follows:  
  -   The  contract  has been agreed  by the parties 
involved in the contract. 
       -   The   Group  can  identify  the  rights of relevant 
parties and the term of payment for the goods 
or services to be transferred. 
 -   The contract has commercial  substance. 
       -  It is  probable  that the Group  will receive 
benefits for the goods or services transferred. 
 
 
2. Identify the performance obligations in the 
contract, to transfer distinctive goods or services 
to the customer. 
 
 
3. Determine the transaction price, net of discounts, 
returns, sales incentives, luxury sales tax, value 
added tax and export duty, which an entity 
expects to be entitled in exchange for transferring 
promised goods or services to a customer. 
 
 
 
4. Allocate 
the 
transaction 
price 
to 
each 
performance obligation on the basis of the selling 
prices of each goods or services promised in the 
contract. 
 
5. Recognize revenue when performance obligation 
is satisfied (over time or at a point in time). 
 
 
Revenue from the sale of goods is recognized when the 
control of goods has been transferred to the customer.
