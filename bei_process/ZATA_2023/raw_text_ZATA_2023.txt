TINJAUAN MAKROEKONOMI GLOBAL
Global Macroeconomic Overview
Perekonomian Indonesia terhadap perekonomian 
Global Terbilang cukup baik. Terbukti capaian 
inﬂasi Indonesia selama tahun 2023 menunjuk-
kan stabilitas yang terjaga dan terkendali, berada 
dalam rentang target sasaran sebesar 3%±1. Inﬂasi 
pada tahun tersebut tercatat sebesar 2,61% (yoy), 
menunjukkan penurunan dibandingkan dengan 
tahun sebelumnya, yaitu sebesar 5,51% (yoy) pada 
tahun 2022. Lebih lanjut, pencapaian inﬂasi pada 
tahun 2023 ini merupakan yang terendah sejak 
tahun 2000, khususnya di luar periode terdampak 
pandemi COVID-19 (2020-2021).
Konﬂik Negara Israel dan Palestina juga mempen-
garuhi kondisi ekonomi global, namun berbeda 
hal berbeda kondisi di beberapa negara muslim 
seperti Indonesia. Konsumen muslim dan musli-
mah di Indonesia menunjukkan dukungan kuat 
dan solidaritas melalui tren fesyen. mereka meng-
gunakan mode sebagai sarana untuk mengek-
spresikan solidaritas dan dukungan terhadap 
perjuangan yang terus berlangsung di Palestina.
Tren fesyen muslim yang berkembang di Indone-
sia mencerminkan keinginan untuk mempertah-
ankan nilai-nilai keislaman dalam kehidupan 
sehari-hari, sementara juga memberikan peluang 
bagi perancang busana lokal untuk mengem-
bangkan kreativitas mereka dan menawarkan 
produk yang sesuai dengan aspirasi pasar. Dukun-
gan terhadap Palestina dan nilai-nilai agama 
menjadi bagian integral dari identitas fesyen 
konsumen muslim di Indonesia, menciptakan 
narasi positif di tengah tantangan politik global.
Berbanding terbalik dengan konsumsi pakaian 
jadi dan alas kaki yang meningkat 3,98% dan 
7,02% pada Kuartal I dan II di tahun 2023, Industri 
tekstil justru terseok-seok di periode tersebut. Hal 
ini disebabkan besarnya impor serta ketidak-
mampuan industri dalam negeri memenuhi 
kebutuhan dalam negeri. Selain itu, langkah 
China melakukan “Clearance Sale” dengan men-
ciptakan disinﬂasi pada barang impor mereka. 
Sebenarnya, di satu sisi akan berdampak positif ke 
Indonesia karena imported inﬂation tidak terlalu 
tinggi. Namun, di sisi lain, murahnya produk China 
juga menjadi ancaman karena bisa membanjiri 
pasar Indonesia.
Indonesia's economy against the global economy 
is said to be quite good. It is proven that Indone-
sia's inﬂation achievements during 2023 showed 
maintained and controlled stability, within the 
target range of 3% ± 1. Inﬂation in that year was 
recorded at 2.61% (yoy), showing a decrease com-
pared to the previous year, which amounted to 
5.51% (yoy) in 2022. Furthermore, the achievement 
of inﬂation in 2023 is the lowest since 2000, espe-
cially outside the period affected by the COVID-19 
pandemic (2020-2021).
The conﬂict between Israel and Palestine has also 
affected the global economy, but things are differ-
ent in some Muslim countries such as Indonesia. 
Muslim consumers in Indonesia show their strong 
support and solidarity through fashion trends, 
using fashion as a means to express solidarity and 
support for the ongoing struggle in Palestine
.
The growing Muslim fashion trend in Indonesia 
reﬂects the desire to maintain Islamic values in 
daily life, while also providing opportunities for 
local fashion designers to develop their creativity 
and offer products that match the market's 
aspirations Support for Palestine and religious 
values has become an integral part of the fashion 
identity of Muslim consumers in Indonesia, creat-
ing a positive narrative amidst global political 
challenges.
In contrast to the consumption of apparel and 
footwear, which increased by 3.98% and 7.02% in 
the ﬁrst and second quarters of 2023, the textile 
industry stumbled during this period. This is due 
to large imports and the inability of the domestic 
industry to meet domestic needs. In addition, 
China's move to conduct a "Clearance Sale" by 
creating disinﬂation in their imported goods. 
Actually, on the one hand, it will have a positive 
impact on Indonesia because imported inﬂation is 
not too high. However, on the other hand, cheap 
Chinese products are also a threat because they 
can ﬂood the Indonesian market.

seen as considerable, given the recovering economic 
conditions and the increasing purchasing power of the

years. Elzatta, as a major player in this industry, has

Indonesia, as the largest country in ASEAN, has a signiﬁ-
cant demographic bonus with the majority of its popula-
tion being of productive age. By 2023, Indonesia's popu-
lation will reach 277.43 million, accounting for around 
40.8% of the total population in the ASEAN region. This 
demographic bonus is one of the important factors 
driving Indonesia's economic growth and making it a 
center of economic growth at the global level, as 
highlighted in the IMF report.
Indonesia's robust economic growth has provided impe-
tus for development in various industry sectors. Essential 
spending, which is an important component of house-
hold spending, is expected to continue to dominate with 
more than 71% of total spending through 2026. At the
