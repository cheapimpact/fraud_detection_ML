Komitmen Molindo Terhadap Energi Hijau
Pemerintahan baru di bawah Presiden Prabowo Subianto telah 
menetapkan target ambisius untuk mempercepat pertumbuhan. 
Salah satu pendorong utama dalam upaya ini adalah 
pengembangan industri yang mengolah kekayaan sumber daya 
alam Indonesia. Strategi utamanya adalah mewujudkan ketahanan 
pangan serta mengurangi ketergantungan negara terhadap impor 
bahan bakar, yang sejalan dengan Peraturan Presiden No. 40 
Tahun 2023 tanggal 16 Juni 2023 yang ditandatangani oleh Presiden 
sebelumnya, Joko Widodo, untuk mempercepat swasembada gula

Peraturan tersebut bertujuan untuk mencapai target-target sebagai 
berikut:
a)	 700.000 ha lahan tebu baru, revitalisasi pabrik gula yang

b)	 Pendirian pabrik bioetanol berbasis tebu serta peningkatan

Selain itu, pemerintahan baru berkomitmen penuh mengeksplorasi 
produk pertanian lain yang melimpah di Indonesia untuk 
menghasilkan etanol sebagai energi hijau berkelanjutan. 
Saat ini, hanya ada dua produsen etanol di Indonesia yang 
memiliki izin untuk menjual bioetanol ke Pertamina. Salah 
satunya adalah Molindo, sementara satunya lagi merupakan 
perusahaan etanol milik negara (BUMN). 
Sebagai produsen etanol terbesar di Indonesia, Molindo secara aktif 
berbagi keahlian dan pengalamannya dalam berbagai forum untuk 
mendukung pengembangan regulasi yang diperlukan guna mencapai 
target pemerintah terkait bioetanol, mengurangi ketergantungan 
pada impor bahan bakar, serta memastikan ketersediaan sumber 
bahan baku dalam negeri dan pengaturan harga. 
Economic and Industry Overview 
Despite facing significant global and domestic challenges, 
Indonesia’s economy recorded strong and stable growth of 
5.03% in 2024. This growth was driven by increased investment 
and manufacturing activities, supported by resilient domestic 
demand and a recovering global market.
This was reflected in Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) and 
Domestic Investment (DI), which grew by 20.82%, reaching 
a total of Rp1,714 trillion, surpassing the government’s target. 
In addition, export performance grew by 6.51% year-on-year, 
supported by a rise in goods exports, particularly in iron 
and steel, as well as mineral fuels, which grew by 17.8% and 
7.8%, respectively, in terms of volume. On the other hand, 
goods imports contracted by 7.95% year-on-year.
Molindo’s Commitment to Green Energy
The new government under President Prabowo Subianto has set 
a remarkable target to accelerate growth. One of the key drivers 
to accelerate growth is by developing industries that process 
Indonesia’s rich natural resources. Central to its strategy is to 
make Indonesia self-sufficient in food (“food security”) as well as 
reducing the country’s dependence to fuel imports, which are also 
consistent to the Presidential Decree No. 40 Year 2023 dated 
June 16th, 2023, signed by the former President Joko Widodo 
to accelerate Indonesia’s sugar production self-sufficiency and 
provides bioethanol as biofuel. This decree aims to achieve 
the following:
a)	 700,000 ha of new sugarcane land, revitalization of 
the existing sugar plants as well as establishment of new 
sugar plants; and
b)	 Establishment of sugar cane-based bio-ethanol plants and 
an increase of production to a minimum of 1,200,000 KL of 
bioethanol by 2030.
Moreover, the new government is serious in exploring other 
agricultural products that are abundant in Indonesia to produce 
ethanol for sustainable green energy.
Currently, there are only two ethanol producers in Indonesia 
that are licensed to sell bioethanol to Pertamina. One of 
them is Molindo and the other one is a state-owned ethanol 
company (BUMN).
As the largest ethanol producer in the country, Molindo has been 
actively sharing its expertise and experience in various forums 
to support the development of necessary regulations to achieve 
the government’s target on bioethanol and reducing dependency 
on fuel imports, as well as securing domestic sources of raw 
materials and regulating prices.

Kondisi Industri Etanol Tahun 2024
Tahun 2024 ditandai dengan lemahnya permintaan etanol serta 
tingginya biaya bahan baku (tetes tebu). 
Segmen minuman beralkohol untuk pasar domestik maupun 
internasional mengalami kinerja yang kurang baik sepanjang 
2024, sehingga permintaan etanol food-grade berkualitas 
tinggi menurun. Hal ini menyebabkan pelemahan harga etanol 
di pasar domestik maupun internasional. 
Kondisi industri semakin diperburuk oleh kenaikan biaya bahan 
baku. Harga rata-rata tetes tebu di Indonesia selama musim 
giling gula pada paruh kedua 2024 meningkat sebesar 15%. 
Kenaikan harga bahan baku ini mendorong biaya bahan baku 
mencapai 80% dari total biaya produksi etanol. Akibatnya, 
etanol yang diproduksi secara lokal menjadi kurang kompetitif 
dibandingkan dengan etanol dari negara lain, baik di pasar 
domestik maupun internasional. 
Kondisi ini secara signifikan mengikis margin keuntungan 
produsen etanol di dalam negeri, termasuk Perseroan.

Seperti yang telah diprediksi sebelumnya, Molindo mencatat

domestik dan internasional.

bersih sebesar 5,4%, dari Rp1,448 triliun pada 2023 menjadi

beberapa peralatan guna meningkatkan efisiensi.
Molindo is fully committed to playing a vital role in realizing

in Indonesia. Bioethanol will bring enormous opportunity to 
Molindo and is a crucial factor of Molindo’s long-term business 
strategy.
In addition, Molindo has been exploring other sources of raw material 
to produce bioethanol that demonstrates Molindo’s innovation 
to diversify its ethanol products and raw materials, as well as 
Molindo’s commitment in reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
Ethanol Industry Condition in 2024
The Year 2024 was characterized by weak demand for ethanol 
and high cost of feedstock (molasses). 
The spirits segment for both local and international markets 
underperformed in 2024 resulting to a lower demand for high 
quality food-grade ethanol. This led to the softening of ethanol 
prices in the domestic and international markets. 
The industry condition was further aggravated by the increase 
in raw material cost. The average price of molasses in Indonesia 
during the sugar milling season in the second half of 2024 
increased by 15%. The increase in the price of feedstock pushed 
the cost of raw materials to 80% of the total production cost of 
ethanol. This condition has made locally produced ethanol to be 
less competitive against ethanol from other countries both in 
the domestic and international markets.
These conditions have significantly eroded the profit margin of 
ethanol producers in the country, including the Company.
2024 Projections and Realization
As previously predicted, Molindo posted negative growth 
in turnover and profit due to the increase in molasses price 
and softening of selling price of ethanol in the domestic and 
international markets.
Consequently, Molindo posted a 5.4% decline in net sales from 
Rp1.448 trillion in 2023 to Rp1.370 trillion in 2024, which 
resulted to a lower operating profit of Rp55.3 billion in 2024 
compared to Rp106.7 billion in 2023. 
Capital Expenditures Realization
Molindo has budgeted Rp30 billion for capital expenditures in 
2024, of which Rp19.4 billion had been realized. These were spent 
mostly for modification and restoration of certain equipment to 
improve efficiency.

is the huge amount of investment required in building and

amount

the ethanol business, combined with prudent management

capital expenditure requirements. Moreover, the Company had 
completed its initial public offering in August 2018, and secured

the Company’s solid capital structure and prudence in borrowing.

Prospek Tahun 2025
Dunia menghadapi ketidakpastian yang semakin besar akibat 
risiko perang tarif global, volatilitas nilai tukar, konflik di berbagai 
wilayah, dampak perubahan iklim, dan faktor lainnya. Semua ini 
berkontribusi pada kenaikan inflasi dan penurunan daya beli 
konsumen, yang berdampak pada melemahnya permintaan 
terhadap berbagai barang, termasuk etanol. 
Di sisi positif, panen tebu diharapkan akan lebih baik pada tahun 
ini serta dapat mengurangi tekanan terhadap harga tetes tebu. 
Dengan mempertimbangkan berbagai kondisi tersebut, Molindo 
menargetkan pertumbuhan moderat sebesar 5% dalam omzet 
serta peningkatan profitabilitas pada 2025. Hal ini didukung 
oleh penguatan dolar AS dalam penjualan ekspor, peningkatan 
efisiensi produksi, peningkatan output produksi, fokus pada pasar 
niche, serta efisiensi biaya organisasi. 
Melihat ke depan setelah 2025, meningkatnya permintaan 
terhadap energi hijau dan keberlanjutan memberikan prospek 
cerah bagi bioetanol. Molindo berkomitmen penuh untuk 
memainkan peran penting dalam mewujudkan masa depan 
bioetanol sebagai sumber energi terbarukan yang berkelanjutan 
di Indonesia. Penerapan regulasi dan kebijakan yang mendukung 
bioetanol di Indonesia secara komprehensif akan menjadi peluang 
besar bagi Molindo sekaligus faktor krusial dalam strategi bisnis 
jangka panjang Perseroan. Molindo siap untuk melanjutkan 
penyelesaian pembangunan pabrik etanol kedua guna memenuhi 
lonjakan permintaan bioetanol di masa mendatang.
In addition, the Company’s EBITDA of Rp103.4 billion in 2024 
and Rp193.3 billion in 2023 showed an interest coverage ratio of 
3.0 times and 4.8 times in 2024 and 2023, respectively.
The Company’s sound debt to equity ratio, equity to total assets 
ratio, and interest coverage ratios combined with solid capital 
structure and financial policy enabled it to maintain strong credit 
rating and secure adequate revolving working capital facilities 
at reasonable cost among top tier banks. 
Dividend Policy
At the end of each year, the Company allocates its profit for 
the payment of its maturing bank loans, capital expenditure 
and working capital requirements. Any excess is distributed to 
the shareholders through cash dividends. Cash dividends, if any, 
from the Parent Entity shall not exceed 30% of its annual profit.
The Parent Entity paid cash dividend of Rp10.0 billion in 2024.
Outlook for 2025
The world is facing greater uncertainties from risk of a major 
global tariff war, volatility in exchange rates, conflicts in 
certain parts of the world, effect of changing climates, and 
other factors. All of these influence the rise in inflation and 
erosion of purchasing power of consumers, which diminish 
the demand for goods, including ethanol.
On a positive note, a better sugar cane harvest is expected this year. 
This should alleviate pressure on the price of molasses this year.
Against this backdrop, Molindo aims a modest growth of 5% in 
turnover and an improvement in profitability in 2025 backed 
by strong US dollar on export sales, improvement in production 
efficiency, increase in production output, focus on niche markets, 
and downsizing of organization cost.
Looking beyond 2025, the increasing demand for green energy 
and sustainability offers a bright future for bioethanol. Molindo 
is fully committed to playing a vital role in realizing the future 
of bioethanol as a viable renewable energy source in Indonesia. 
Once the comprehensive laws and regulations supporting 
bioethanol in Indonesia are put in place, these will bring enormous 
opportunity to Molindo and is a crucial factor to Molindo’s long-
term business strategy. Molindo will be ready to commence the 
completion of its second ethanol plant to meet the expected 
surge in demand for bioethanol in the near future.
