TINJAUAN MAKROEKONOMI GLOBAL

TINJAUAN OPERASI PER SEGMEN USAHA
REVIEW OF OPERATIONS PER BUSINESS SEGMENT
Pada tahun 2024, ekonomi global terus menghadapi tantangan 
kompleks yang disebabkan oleh meningkatnya ketegangan 
geopolitik di Eropa Timur dan Timur Tengah. Konflik di wilayah 
tersebut mengakibatkan gangguan rantai pasok global dan 
lonjakan harga komoditas yang berdampak pada inflasi di 
berbagai negara. Sebagai respons, bank sentral di banyak negara 
menerapkan kebijakan moneter ketat dengan menaikkan suku 
bunga acuan guna menekan inflasi dan menjaga stabilitas 
ekonomi.
Di tengah dinamika global yang penuh tantangan, perekonomian 
Indonesia tetap menunjukkan ketahanan dengan pertumbuhan 
sebesar 5,03% sepanjang tahun 2024, sedikit melambat 
dibandingkan pertumbuhan 5,05% pada tahun 2023. Produk 
Domestik Bruto (PDB) atas dasar harga berlaku mencapai 
Rp22.139,0 triliun, dengan PDB per kapita sebesar USD4.960,3. 
Sementara itu, inflasi nasional berhasil dikendalikan di tingkat 
1,57%, yang mencerminkan efektivitas kebijakan pemerintah dan 
bank sentral dalam menjaga stabilitas harga.
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia pada tahun 2024 didorong 
oleh permintaan domestik yang kuat, didukung oleh daya beli 
masyarakat serta optimisme pelaku usaha yang tetap terjaga. 
Indeks Keyakinan Konsumen (IKK) pada Desember 2024 tercatat 
sebesar 123,8, mencerminkan tingkat kepercayaan konsumen 
yang stabil terhadap prospek perekonomian nasional. Selain itu, 
peningkatan investasi di sektor-sektor strategis turut menopang 
pertumbuhan ekonomi, terutama dalam bidang infrastruktur 
dan industri berbasis ekspor.
Salah satu sektor yang mencatat kinerja positif adalah sektor 
transportasi dan pergudangan, yang tumbuh sebesar 9,56% 
pada kuartal II 2024 dibandingkan periode yang sama tahun 
sebelumnya. Pertumbuhan ini menjadikan sektor transportasi 
dan pergudangan sebagai kontributor terbesar kedua terhadap 
PDB nasional setelah sektor penyedia akomodasi dan makan 
minum. Kinerja positif ini didorong oleh peningkatan permintaan 
layanan transportasi dan logistik seiring dengan pertumbuhan 
konsumsi masyarakat serta ekspansi industri e-commerce yang 
semakin pesat.
Selain itu, pemerintah terus berupaya meningkatkan efisiensi 
logistik melalui pembangunan dan perbaikan infrastruktur 
transportasi, termasuk jalan tol, pelabuhan, serta sistem 
manajemen logistik berbasis digital. Inisiatif ini bertujuan untuk 
menekan biaya logistik, meningkatkan daya saing industri 
nasional, serta mempercepat arus barang dan jasa dalam negeri 
maupun ekspor.
Dengan fundamental ekonomi yang solid, prospek perekonomian 
Indonesia pada tahun 2025 tetap optimis. Namun, berbagai 
tantangan eksternal, seperti volatilitas harga komoditas global 
dan dampak lanjutan dari kebijakan moneter ketat di negara-
negara maju, tetap menjadi faktor yang perlu diwaspadai. Oleh 
karena itu, kebijakan yang adaptif serta sinergi antara pemerintah 
dan sektor swasta akan menjadi kunci dalam mempertahankan 
momentum pertumbuhan ekonomi yang berkelanjutan.
In 2024, the global economy continued to face complex challenges 
stemming from escalating geopolitical tensions in Eastern Europe 
and the Middle East. Conflicts in these regions disrupted global 
supply chains and triggered commodity price surges, impacting 
inflation across numerous countries. Consequently, central 
banks in many nations implemented stringent monetary policies 
by raising benchmark interest rates to curb inflation and maintain 
economic stability.
Amidst the challenging global dynamics, the Indonesian economy 
demonstrated resilience, achieving a growth rate of 5.03% 
throughout 2024, slightly slower than the 5.05% growth recorded 
in 2023. The Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at current prices 
reached Rp22,139.0 trillion, with a GDP per capita of USD4,960.3. 
Meanwhile, national inflation was effectively controlled at 1.57%, 
reflecting the efficacy of government and central bank policies in 
maintaining price stability.
Indonesia's economic growth in 2024 was propelled by robust 
domestic demand, supported by sustained consumer purchasing 
power and maintained business optimism. The Consumer 
Confidence Index (IKK) in December 2024 stood at 123.8, 
indicating stable consumer confidence in the national economic 
outlook. Furthermore, increased investment in strategic sectors 
bolstered economic growth, particularly in infrastructure and 
export-oriented industries.
Notably, the transportation and warehousing sector recorded 
positive performance, growing by 9.56% in the second quarter 
of 2024 compared to the same period in the previous year. This 
growth positioned the transportation and warehousing sector 
as the second-largest contributor to the national GDP, following 
the accommodation and food and beverage sector. This positive 
performance was driven by increased demand for transportation 
and 
logistics 
services,

with 
growing 
consumer 
consumption and the rapid expansion of the e-commerce 
industry.
The government continued to enhance logistical efficiency 
through the development and improvement of transportation 
infrastructure, including toll roads, ports, and digital-based 
logistics management systems. These initiatives aim to reduce 
logistical costs, enhance national industrial competitiveness, and 
expedite the flow of domestic and export goods and services.
With solid economic fundamentals, Indonesia's economic outlook 
for 2025 remains optimistic. However, external challenges, 
such as global commodity price volatility and the lingering 
effects of stringent monetary policies in developed economies, 
remain factors to be monitored. Therefore, adaptive policies 
and synergistic collaboration between the government and the 
private sector will be crucial in sustaining the momentum of 
continued economic growth.

the provisions outlined in the Company's Articles of Association,

the decision of the Annual General Meeting of Shareholders 
(AGMS) upon the recommendation of the Company's Board of 
Directors. The Company's Articles of Association also permit the 
distribution of interim dividends, provided that such distribution 
does not result in the Company's net assets being less than 
the paid-up capital plus mandatory reserves. The distribution 
of these interim dividends is determined by the decision of the 
Company's Board of Directors, subject to the approval of the 
Company's Board of Commissioners.

over time, the Company plans to pay cash dividends to all 
Shareholders at least once a year. The amount of dividends to be 
distributed isdetermined by the decision of the Annual General 
Meeting of Shareholders, based on the Company's financial 
statements, and taking into account:
1.	
Net profit achievement;
2.	
In the event of a net loss, the Annual General Meeting of 
Shareholders may consider not distributing dividends;
3.	
The need for working capital and future capital expenditure; 
and
4.	
The need for future business development.
The Company plans to distribute cash dividends up to a maximum 
of 20% of net profit. This distribution will be in accordance with the 
UUPT, prevailing capital market regulations, and the Company's 
dividend distribution policy. The final decision regarding dividend 
distribution will be determined by the Shareholders at the Annual 
General Meeting of Shareholders held annually.

transparency as the primary foundation in implementing Good 
Corporate Governance (GCG). GCG is a strategic pillar that 
strengthens the Company’s competitiveness in an era of dynamic 
industry change. The principles of GCG, namely transparency, 
accountability, 
responsibility, 
independence, 
fairness, 
and 
equality, are the Company’s guidelines in every decision and 
action.
The Company believes that the effective implementation of GCG 
not only plays a role in achieving business targets but also protects 
the rights of all Stakeholders. The balance between economic and
social interests, between individuals and groups, and between 
Shareholders and other Stakeholders, is the Company’s main 
focus. The principles of GCG are the Company’s moral and 
strategic foundation.
The implementation of GCG is not only a legal obligation for the 
Company but also a strategic commitment in managing business 
operations. The Company always strives to comply with Law No. 
40 of 2007, Financial Services Authority regulations, and Indonesia 
Stock Exchange regulations. This compliance is a manifestation of 
the Company’s commitment to increasing the Company’s value, 
protecting Stakeholders, and strengthening accountability at all 
levels of operational activities.
By upholding the standards of Good Corporate Governance in 
every aspect of the business, the Company not only protects the 
interests of Stakeholders but also strengthens its position in facing 
increasingly complex competitive dynamics. The Company’s belief 
in the value of GCG as a primary foundation makes the Company 
more adaptive and able to achieve business goals with significant 
and sustainable progress.
