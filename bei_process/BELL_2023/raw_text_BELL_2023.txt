Tinjauan Ekonomi
Economic Review  
Pemulihan global dari pandemi Covid-19 dan invasi Rusia ke 
Ukraina masih lambat dan tidak merata. Meskipun ada kemajuan 
dalam ekonomi awal tahun ini, aktivitas ekonomi belum mencapai 
level sebelum pandemi, terutama di negara-negara emerging 
market and developing economies (EMDEs) dan berkembang. 
Faktor-faktor seperti konsekuensi jangka panjang dari pandemi, 
perang di Ukraina, dan fragmentasi geoekonomi menghambat 
pemulihan, sementara pengetatan kebijakan moneter, penarikan 
dukungan fiskal, dan peristiwa cuaca ekstrem juga memengaruhi 
pelambatan ekonomi menjadi 3,0% dan inflasi global menjadi 
6,8% di tahun 2023.
Meskipun perekonomian global belum pulih, perekonomian 
nasional berlanjut pulih dengan pertumbuhan sebesar 5,05% 
dengan stabilitas Rupiah, inflasi, dan stabilitas sistem keuangan 
(SSK) yang terkendali. Perekonomian menunjukkan pertumbuhan 
yang baik dan berdaya tahan, didukung oleh kinerja Neraca 
Pembayaran Internasional (NPI) yang memperkuat stabilitas 
eksternal. Selain itu, stabilitas nilai tukar Rupiah tetap terjaga, 
inflasi turun dalam kisaran yang diharapkan, dan likuiditas 
perbankan memadai. Perbaikan signifikan juga terlihat dalam 
kredit dan pembiayaan perbankan. Ketahanan perbankan dan 
SSK dipertahankan dengan baik, dan transaksi Ekspor-Impor dan 
Devisa (EKD) tetap kuat, menegaskan fondasi ekonomi yang stabil 
di tahun 2023.
Selain itu, pertumbuhan perekonomian Indonesia juga ditunjukkan 
pada produk domestik bruto (PDB) seluruh sektor lapangan 
usaha dengan kontribusi terbesar oleh sektor transportasi dan 
pergudangan, jasa lainnya, serta akomodasi dan makan minum 
yang masing-masing tumbuh sebesar 13,96%, 10,52%, dan 
10,01%. Dari sisi pengeluaran juga mengalami pertumbuhan 
dengan kontribusi terbesar dari sektor konsumsi Lembaga Non 
Profit yang melayani Rumah Tangga (LNPRT) tumbuh tinggi 
dengan 9,83%, yang disusul sektor konsumsi rumah tangga.

Global recovery from the Covid-19 pandemic and Russia’s invasion 
of Ukraine remains sluggish and uneven. Despite progress in 
the economy earlier this year, economic activity has not yet 
reached pre-pandemic levels, especially in emerging market and 
developing economies (EMDEs). Factors such as the long-term 
consequences of the pandemic, the war in Ukraine, and geo-
economic fragmentation are hindering recovery, while tightening 
monetary policies, fiscal support withdrawal, and extreme 
weather events are also impacting economic slowdown to 3.0% 
and global inflation to 6.8% in 2023.
While the global economy has not fully recovered, the national 
economy continues to recover with a growth rate of 5.05%, 
accompanied by stable Rupiah, inflation, and controlled 
financial system stability (FSS). The economy demonstrates 
good and resilient growth, supported by the performance of 
the International Balance of Payments (IBP) which strengthens 
external stability. Furthermore, Rupiah exchange rate stability 
remains intact, inflation has decreased within the expected range, 
and banking liquidity is adequate. Significant improvements are 
also seen in bank credit and financing. Banking resilience and FSS 
are well maintained, and Export-Import and Foreign Exchange 
(EIF) transactions remain robust, affirming a stable economic 
foundation in 2023.
Additionally, Indonesia’s economic growth is also evidenced in 
the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) across all sectors of business 
sectors, with the largest contributions from the transportation 
and warehousing sector, other services, and accommodation and 
food services, each growing by 13.96%, 10.52%, and 10.01% 
respectively. On the expenditure side, there is also growth with 
the largest contribution from the Non-Profit Institutions Serving 
Households (NPISH) consumption sector growing significantly by 
9.83%, followed by household consumption sector.

The source of Indonesia’s economic growth by field of business 
in 2023 was supported by the manufacturing industry, which 
experienced the highest growth of 0.95% due to strong domestic 
and global demand. However, the non-oil processing industry 
sector experienced a decline, falling to 4.69% from the previous

industry contracted by 1.98%, a significant decrease compared 
to 2022, which recorded Rp139.33 trillion, dropping to Rp136.57 
trillion.
The slowdown in the textile and garment industry is primarily 
caused by weakening demand from major export countries and 
the invasion of imported products, especially from China. This 
has led to a decrease in the export value of textiles and garments 
by 1.56% and 1.86% respectively, as well as a decline in the 
export volume of textiles and garments by 0.11% and 1.88% 
respectively.

1
Tahap memindahkan benang dari gulungan 
bobbin atau cheese atau cones ke dalam 
gulungan besar (beam) untuk dijadikan benang 
lusi (warp), yakni benang dengan arah sejajar, 
serta dalam jumlah dan panjang benang yang 
sudah ditentukan.
Transferring yarn from bobbin, cheese, or cones 
onto large rolls (beams) to create warp yarn, which 
is yarn aligned in parallel, and in predetermined 
quantities and lengths, is a stage in the process.
Warping
4
Tahap mengolah benang untuk diberi puntiran 
dengan nilai tertentu (twist per meter/tpm) agar 
menjadikan benang semakin kompak dan kuat, 
serta memiliki sifat lain sesuai dengan peruntukan 
desain. Dalam proses ini, dikenal 2 macam 
puntiran, yaitu low twist (<450 tpm) dan high 
twist (>450 tpm).
Processing yarn to impart a specific twist value 
(twist per meter/tpm) to make the yarn more 
compact and strong, as well as to have other 
properties according to design requirements. In 
this process, two types of twisting are recognized: 
low twist (<450 tpm) and high twist (>450 tpm).

Processing yarn from the warping or sizing 
process, which is then combined from several 
beams into a warp beam. This combination can 
consist of the same type of yarn or different types 
of yarn, depending on its intended use.

Tahap untuk menenun benang pakan disilangkan dengan 
benang lusi sehingga teranyam dengan baik. Proses ini 
terdiri dari 5 tahapan, yaitu penguluran lusi (let off motion),

Weaving the weft yarn interlaced with the warp yarn to 
weave them together effectively. This process consists of five 
stages: warp stretching (let-off motion), shedding (shedding 
motion), weft insertion, and beating and take-up motion.

Inserting yarns into droppers, reeds, and heddles according 
to the desired fabric weave pattern is a stage in the 
process. This process requires careful handling as it is 
done manually and takes a considerable amount of time, 
especially for decorative weaves.

Prospek Usaha
Ekonomi global di tahun 2024 diperkirakan terus melemah 
hingga mencapai 2,8%. Meskipun demikian, perekonomian 
nasional diproyeksikan akan terus menunjukkan perbaikan hingga 
mencapai 4,7%-5,5%. 
Asosiasi Produsen Serat dan Benang Filamen Indonesia (APSyFI) 
menyatakan bahwa industri tekstil dan produk tekstil (TPT) di 
tahun 2024 diproyeksikan melambat hinga 1,5%. Meski dengan 
adanya pesta politik di tahun 2024 dan kekuatan konsumsi 
dalam negeri yang masih kuat, sekitar 70% pasar dalam negeri 
dikuasai oleh barang impor ilegal yang berasal dari Tiongkok 
mencerminkan kondisi terburuk dalam 20 tahun terakhir.
Meskipun konsumsi tekstil mengalami pertumbuhan sekitar 
3%-4% dibandingkan tahun 2022, namun hal tersebut belum 
mampu menutupi dampak negatif dari dominasi produk 
impor. Dengan demilian, diperlukan langkah penyelamatan 
terhadap industri TPT meliputi perlindungan terhadap produsen 
lokal, pengendalian impor barang tekstil, serta pembangunan 
infrastruktur yang mendukung pertumbuhan industri.
Upaya yang telah disiapkan pemerintah melalui Kementerian 
Perindustrian untuk mendukung industri TPT di tahun 2024 yaitu:
1.	 Memberikan 
anggaran

restrukturisasi

memperkuat posisi industri, meningkatkan daya saing, dan

2.	 Menyiapkan sumber daya manusia yang kompeten melalui 
pembukaan pelatihan berbasis kompetensi yaitu Pendidikan 
dan Pelatihan Operator Sewing yang akan mendapatkan 
pelatihan, sertifikasi kompetensi, dan penempatan kerja.

Business Prospect
In 2024, the global economy if projected to continue to weaken 
by 2.8%. However, the national economy is projected to continue 
its improvement to reach 4.7%-5.5%. 
The Indonesian Fiber and Filament Yarn Producers Association 
(APSyFI) stated that in 2024, the Textile and Textile Products (TPT) 
industry is projected to slow down by 1.5%. Even with the political 
party in 2024 and robust domestic consumption, around 70% of 
the domestic market is controlled by illegal imported goods from 
China, reflecting the worst condition in the past 20 years.
Although the textile consumption still experienced a growth of 
around 3%-4% compared to 2022, it is not enough to cover the 
negative impact of imported products dominance. Therefore, 
measures to save the TPT industry are required, which include 
protection of the local producers, controlling the imports of textile 
goods, as well as building the infrastructure to support the growth 
of the industry.
The government has prepared several efforts through the Ministry 
of Industry to support the textile industry in 2024 namely:
1.	 Provide Rp52 billion budget for TPT industry machinery 
restructuring program as a tool to strengthen the industry’s 
position, improve the competitiveness, and to obtain larger 
market share; and
2.	 Preparing competent human resources by opening up 
competency-based 
training, 
namely 
Sewing 
Operator 
Education and Training, where participants will receive 
training, competency certificate, and job placement.
