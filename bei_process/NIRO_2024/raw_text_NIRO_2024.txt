The year 2024 was marked by continued high global 
geopolitical dynamics. The prolonged conflict between 
Russia and Ukraine, as well as rising tensions due to Israel’s 
attacks on Palestine, exacerbated global uncertainty. At the 
end of the year, the re-election of President Trump in the 
United States with his “America First” policy also brought 
the potential for significant shifts in the geopolitical and 
global economic order. This instability contributed to a 
slowdown in global economic growth, which was estimated 
at 3.2%, slightly lower than 3.3% in the previous year. Trade 
fragmentation 
became 
more 
apparent, 
accompanied 
by diverging growth across countries. The United States 
continued to record strong economic performance despite 
the risk of overheating due to growing fiscal deficits. In 
contrast, economic growth in China and Europe slowed 
due to weak domestic demand and potential trade barriers. 
Emerging markets such as Indonesia, India, and several 
other Emerging Market Economies (EMEs) demonstrated 
resilience, supported by solid domestic consumption, 
even as exports were constrained by the weakening global 
economy.
In spite all of these, Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.03%, 
slightly lower than the 5.05% achieved in 2023. In terms 
of industry sectors, the highest growth occurred in the 
“other services” sector at 9.80%, followed by transportation 
and warehousing at 8.69%, and accommodation and food 
services at 8.56%. The manufacturing industry, which is the 
main contributor to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP), grew 
by 4.43%. Additionally, national macroeconomic stability was 
reflected in controlled inflation, which remained at 2.8%, 
within the target range set by the Government and Bank 
Indonesia. This achievement resulted from effective fiscal 
and monetary policy synergy, including optimizing state 
revenue, efficient spending, and price control to maintain 
public purchasing power.

The Company’s business activities are classified into several 
segments, as described below.
Shopping Malls
In order to manage its assets optimally and create long-term 
added value, the Company engages in leasing a majority of 
the available space in its shopping malls. This strategy allows 
the Company to selectively manage the tenant mix, ensuring 
the quality of tenants and maintaining a proportional 
balance between anchor tenants and supporting tenants.
Marketing and space offering activities are generally carried 
out in the early stages of property development, even before 
the construction process is completed. This approach aims to 
ensure that the tenant composition aligns with the concept 
and strategic positioning of the planned shopping mall.
Revenue from commercial property leasing is determined by 
the size of the leased area and the applicable rental rates. In 
setting rental prices, the Company considers a range of key 
indicators, including the market price of similar properties 
in relevant locations, historical trends, space availability 
rates, current market conditions, and the characteristics 
and profile of potential tenants, especially whether they are 
anchor tenants or not.

Business Outlook
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projects global 
economic growth at 3.3% in 2025 and 2026, accompanied by 
a decrease in inflation to 4.2% in 2025 and 3.5% in 2026. This 
projection opens the door for the normalization of monetary 
policies. However, performance across regions is expected 
to become increasingly varied. The United States is projected 
to grow by 2.7%, while Europe is expected to grow only 
1.0% due to weak manufacturing sectors and high energy 
costs. Emerging markets and China are expected to remain 
stable, with growth above 4%. Nonetheless, the IMF notes 
that global risks remain high, particularly concerning policy 
uncertainties, fiscal pressures, and potential slowdowns in 
certain regions.
Amid global dynamics, Indonesia’s economy demonstrates 
strong resilience. Bank Indonesia projects national growth 
at 4.7%–5.5% in 2025, supported by fiscal and monetary 
policy synergy and the acceleration of national economic 
transformation 
through 
the 
government’s 
strategic 
programs.
This positive outlook also fuels optimism across various 
domestic industry sectors. The Indonesian Shopping Center 
Association (APPBI) predicts business growth for shopping 
malls at 11%–12% in 2025, continuing the growth trend 
of 9%–10% from the previous year. The largest growth 
is expected to occur outside Jakarta, such as in Bogor, 
Tangerang, and Bekasi, driven by limited land availability in 
the capital city and the growing trend of integrated shopping 
malls with other properties like hotels and apartments. 
Growth potential is also reinforced by the relatively low ratio 
of shopping malls space to Indonesia’s population compared 
to neighboring countries, as well as the Government’s 
program to build 3 million homes.
However, business players should be cautious of declining 
purchasing power and the shift of peak seasons to the first 
quarter of 2025, with key shopping moments like Ramadan, 
Idul Fitri, Chinese New Year, Christmas, and school holidays.

Rapat Umum Pemegang Saham
General Meeting of Shareholders
The General Meeting of Shareholders (GMS) serves as the 
primary forum for the Shareholders to exercise their rights 
and make key decisions regarding their investments in 
the Company, in compliance with applicable laws and the 
Articles of Association. In accordance with the Financial 
Services Authority Regulation No. 15/POJK.04/2020 on 
the Planning and Implementation of General Meetings of 
Shareholders for Public Companies, the GMS is classified 
into two types: the Annual GMS and the Extraordinary GMS. 
The Annual GMS must be held no later than 6 months after 
the end of the financial year. Meanwhile, the Extraordinary 
GMS is conditional, meaning it can be convened at any time 
as needed.
Policy on Fair Treatment of  
the Shareholders [G-08]
The Company has established policies and procedures to 
ensure fair treatment of all Shareholders and to protect 
their rights. These rights include access to the Company’s 
material information on a regular and timely basis, voting 
rights at the GMS, resolution mechanisms in case of 
disagreements regarding corporate actions, and the right 
to receive dividends in accordance with GMS policies and 
decisions. The Company is also committed to facilitating 
the Shareholders’ and stakeholders’ participation through 
transparent and effective communication management.
The policy on fair treatment of the Shareholders also includes 
measures to prevent insider trading, whether by employees, 
members of the Board of Commissioners, or the Directors. 
Transactions, including those involving related parties, 
are conducted free from conflicts of interest to safeguard 
the interests of both the Company and its Shareholders. 
Additionally, the Company provides equal, easily accessible, 
and timely information channels, such as the official website. 
This information includes meeting invitations, GMS results, 
and voting outcomes, ensuring transparency and active 
engagement of all Shareholders.

The implementation of the Audit Committee’s duties and 
responsibilities refers to the Financial Services Authority

Audit Committee Duties and 
Responsibilities
The Audit Committee duties and responsibilities are as 
follows.
1.	 To examine the Company’s compliance with laws and 
regulations on capital market and other laws and 
regulations related to the Company’s business activities;
2.	 To analyze any financial information to be published by 
the Company including Financial Statements, projection, 
and other financial information;
3.	 To examine the audit performed by the Internal Audit;
4.	 To distribute various information and results of study to 
the Board of Commissioners in relation to risks faced by 
the Company and risk management implementation by 
the Directors;
5.	 To examine any Company-related complaints and to 
submit results of such examination to the Board of 
Commissioners; and
6.	 To keep the confidentiality of any Company’s document, 
data, and information.
