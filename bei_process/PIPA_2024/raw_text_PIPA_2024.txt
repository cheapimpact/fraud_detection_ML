Injection molding is a manufacturing process for producing
complex shaped products from thermoplastic or thermoset
materials. The process begins by heating the raw material until
it melts, then the molten material is injected into a mold at high
pressure using a screw or plunger. The mold, usually made of
metal such as steel or aluminum, is designed to fit the desired
product shape. Once the material fills the mold, it is cooled and
hardened to form the product. The product is then removed from
the mold using ejector pins or other mechanisms. The process is
fast, efficient, and allows for mass production with high
precision tolerances, making it ideal for a variety of industrial
applications.
Mesin ekstrusi digunakan untuk membentuk material, biasanya
termoplastik, menjadi profil kontinu dengan memaksa material
melewati cetakan (die). Proses dimulai dengan memasukkan
bahan mentah, seperti pelet atau bubuk, ke dalam laras
pemanas. Sebuah sekrup berputar mendorong material ke
depan sambil memanaskan dan mencampurnya hingga mencair.
Material cair ini kemudian didorong melalui cetakan, yang
membentuknya sesuai dengan penampang yang diinginkan,
seperti pipa, lembaran, atau film. Setelah keluar dari cetakan,
produk didinginkan, biasanya dengan air atau udara, kemudian
dipotong atau digulung. Proses ini efisien untuk memproduksi
produk panjang dengan bentuk yang konsisten.
Extrusion machines are used to shape materials, usually
thermoplastics, into continuous profiles by forcing the material
through a die. The process begins with the raw material, such as
pellets or powder, being fed into a heated barrel. A rotating
screw pushes the material forward while heating and mixing it
until it melts. The molten material is then forced through a die,
which shapes it into the desired cross-section, such as a tube,
sheet, or film. After exiting the die, the product is cooled,
usually with water or air, and then cut or rolled. This process is
efficient for producing long products with consistent shapes.
