In line with the export activities, Indonesia’s import 
performance in 2024 also recorded an increase in both 
value (5.31% year-on-year) and volume (3.37% year-on-year). 
The total value of Indonesia’s imports in 2024 reached USD 
233.66 billion, primarily driven by imports of raw materials 
and capital goods, which accounted for 90.28% of total 
imports throughout the year. The significant contribution 
of raw materials and capital goods imports was in line with 
the expansion of export activities in the manufacturing 
sector. This reflects the sustained performance of the 
domestic manufacturing industry throughout 2024. In terms 
of commodities, imports of electrical machinery/equipment 
and mechanical machinery/appliances recorded growth, 
whereas imports of iron and steel experienced a contraction.
Based on the data from the Association of Indonesian 
Automotive Manufacturers (Gaikindo – Gabungan Industri 
Kendaraan Bermotor Indonesia), domestic car sales in 2024 
reached 865,723 units, decline 13.9% compared to 1,005,802 
units in 2023, but still exceeded the target of 850,000 units. 
The decline was primarily due to the weakened consumer 
purchasing power throughout the year and the impact of 
the political year, which led to a “wait-and-see” consumer 
behaviour. Within this segment, Japanese brand Toyota 
continued to dominate the market with 33.4% market share, 
as reflected in the wholesale sales of 288,982 units and retail 
sales of 293,788 units.  
Gaikindo also stated that battery electric vehicle (BEV) 
sales in 2024 surged to 43,188 units, marking a significant 
annual increase of 153.2% compared to 17,051 units in 2023. 
However, BEV sales still accounted for only 4.98% of total 
national car sales in terms of wholesales. This limited 
contribution was primarily due to the limited number 
of charging infrastructures in Indonesia, which caused 
consumers hesitate to fully shift to BEV.

Aset lancar Perseroan tercatat sebesar Rp8,9 triliun,

Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh meningkatnya posisi kas dan 
setara kas.
Aset Tidak Lancar 
Sementara itu, aset tidak lancar Perseroan tercatat sebesar 
Rp12,1 triliun, meningkat sebesar 4,2% dari Rp11,7 triliun 
pada 2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh kenaikan investasi 
pada entitas asosiasi dan ventura bersama sejalan dengan 
kenaikan laba. 
Jumlah Aset 
Secara keseluruhan, jumlah aset Perseroan tercatat sebesar 
Rp21,0 triliun, meningkat sebesar 7,2% dari Rp19,6 triliun pada 
2023. 
Liabilitas Jangka Pendek
Per akhir 2024, liabilitas jangka pendek Perseroan mencapai 
Rp4,5 triliun, meningkat sebesar 3,4% dari Rp4,3 triliun pada 
2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh pinjaman bank untuk 
operasional perusahaan.
Liabilitas Jangka Panjang
Di sisi lain, liabilitas jangka panjang Perseroan mencapai 
Rp960,3 miliar pada 2024, meningkat sebesar 30,3% dari 
Rp737,2 miliar pada 2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh 
kenaikan liabilitas imbalan kerja jangka panjang.
Jumlah Liabilitas
Secara keseluruhan, jumlah liabilitas Perseroan mengalami 
kenaikan sebesar 7,3% dari Rp5,1 triliun pada 2023 menjadi 
sebesar Rp5,4 triliun pada 2024. 
Jumlah Ekuitas
Per 31 Desember 2024, jumlah ekuitas Perseroan mencapai 
Rp15,6 triliun pada 2024, meningkat sebesar 7,2% dari Rp14,5 
triliun pada 2023. 
Current Assets 
The Company’s current assets were posted at Rp8.9 trillion, 
increased 11.6% compared to Rp8.0 trillion in 2023. The 
increase was due to the increase in the position of cash and 
cash equivalents.
Non-Current Assets 
Meanwhile, the Company’s non-current assets were posted 
at Rp12.1 trillion, increased 4.2% compared to Rp11.7 trillion 
in 2023. The increase was due to the increase in investment 
in associates and joint ventures, in line with the increase in 
profits.
Total Assets
Overall, the Company’s total assets were posted at Rp21.0 
trillion, increased 7.2% compared to Rp19.6 trillion in 2023.
Short-Term Liabilities
As of the end of 2024, the Company’s short-term liabilities 
reached Rp4.5 trillion, increased 3.4% compared to Rp4.3 
trillion in 2023. The increase was due to the increase in bank 
loans for the Company’s operations.
Long-Term Liabilities
On the other hand, the Company’s long-term liabilities reached 
Rp960.3 billion in 2024, increased 30.3% from Rp737.2 billion 
in 2023. The increase was due to the increase in long-term 
employee benefits.
Total Liabilities
Overall, the Company’s total liabilities increased 7.3% from 
Rp5.1 trillion in 2023 to Rp5.4 trillion in 2024.
Total Equity
As of December 31, 2024, the Company’s total equity reached 
Rp15.6 trillion in 2024, increased 7.2% compared to Rp14.5 
trillion in 2023.

Pendapatan Bersih
Perseroan membukukan pendapatan bersih sebesar Rp19,1 
triliun pada 2024, meningkat sebesar 2,3% dari Rp18,6 triliun 
pada 2023. Kenaikan ini sejalan dengan pertumbuhan bisnis 
perdagangan dan ekspor.
Beban Pokok Pendapatan dan Laba Bruto
Peningkatan pendapatan bersih turut memengaruhi beban 
pokok pendapatan, yang mengalami kenaikan sebesar 2,8% 
dari Rp15,6 triliun pada tahun 2023 menjadi Rp16,0 triliun 
pada tahun 2024. Sementara itu, laba bruto pada tahun 2024 
tercatat sebesar Rp3,1 triliun, mengalami penurunan sebesar 
0,4% dibandingkan dengan Rp3,1 triliun pada tahun 2023.
Beban Penjualan
Pada 2024, beban penjualan tercatat sebesar Rp992,3 miliar, 
meningkat sebesar 3,7% dari Rp957,0 miliar pada 2023. 
Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh meningkatnya biaya pengiriman 
yang sejalan dengan pertumbuhan penjualan dalam bisnis 
perdagangan.
Beban Umum dan Administrasi
Pada 2024, beban umum dan administrasi tercatat sebesar 
Rp1,03 triliun, meningkat sebesar 7,9% dari Rp0,95 triliun 
pada 2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh peningkatan biaya 
perbaikan dan perawatan serta biaya jasa konsultan.
Penghasilan (Biaya) Lain-Lain, Bersih
Penghasilan keuangan dan lain-lain, bersih tercatat mencapai 
Rp403,3 miliar pada 2024, meningkat sebesar 106,8% dari 
Rp195,0 miliar pada 2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh 
keuntungan dari penjualan aset tetap yang tidak lagi digunakan 
untuk kegiatan manufaktur, serta peningkatan pendapatan 
bunga yang dipengaruhi oleh posisi kas Perseroan.
Bagian atas Laba Bersih Entitas Asosiasi dan 
Ventura Bersama
Pada 2024, Perseroan membukukan bagian laba bersih 
entitas asosiasi dan ventura bersama setelah pajak sebesar 
Rp1,04 triliun, meningkat sebesar 9,5% dari Rp0,95 triliun 
pada 2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan oleh peningkatan ekspor 
serta pertumbuhan pasar kendaraan roda dua.
Net Revenue
In 2024, the Company posted Rp19.1 trillion in net revenue, 
increased 2.3% compared to Rp18.6 trillion in 2023. The 
increase is in line with the growth of the trade and export 
business.
Cost of Revenue and Gross Profit
The increase in net revenue also affected the cost of revenue, 
which increased by 2.8% from Rp15.6 trillion in 2023 to Rp16.0 
trillion in 2024. Meanwhile, gross profit in 2024 was posted 
at Rp3.1 trillion, decreased 0.4% compared to Rp3.1 trillion in 
2023.
Selling Expenses
In 2024, selling expenses were posted at Rp992.3 billion, 
increased 3.7% compared to Rp957.0 billion in 2023. The 
increase was due to increased shipping costs, which is in line 
with sales growth in the trading business.
General and Administrative Expenses
In 2024, general and administrative expenses were posted at 
Rp1.03 trillion, increased 7.9% from Rp0.95 trillion in 2023. The 
increase was due to the increase in repair and maintenance 
expenses and consulting service expenses.
Other Income (Expenses), Net
The Company posted an increase of 106.8% in net Financial 
and other income, reaching Rp403.3 billion in 2024, from 
Rp195.0 billion in 2023. The increase was due to the gain 
from the sale of fixed assets that were no longer used for 
manufacturing activities, as well as increase in interest 
income in line with the Company’s cash position.
Share of Net Income of Associate Entities and 
Joint Ventures
In 2024, the Company posted Rp1.04 trillion in net profit 
share of associates and joint ventures after tax, increased 
9.5% from Rp0.95 trillion in 2023. The increase was due to 
increased exports and growth of the two-wheeled vehicle 
market.

Management Policy on Capital Structure
The Company consistently maintains the balance of its capital 
structure to maintain business continuity and maximize 
benefits for shareholders. Periodically, the Company reviews 
and manages the optimal capital structure and returns 
to shareholders, taking into account future capital needs 
and capital efficiency of the Company, current and future 
profitability, projected operating cash flow, projected 
capital expenditure, and projected strategic investment 
opportunities.
In order to maintain or adjust the capital structure, 
the Company can adjust total dividend payable to the 
shareholders, issue new shares, or sell assets to reduce debt.
Capital Structure
The Company monitors its capital based on the gearing ratio 
calculated by dividing the total net debt by the total capital. 
Net debt is calculated from the total loans (including short-
term and long-term loans as presented in the consolidated 
financial statements) minus cash and cash equivalents. 
The total capital is calculated from equity (as contained in 
the consolidated financial statements) minus goodwill and 
intangible assets.
The Company retained the financial policy to maintain a 
gearing ratio below 150% (1.5 times) in line with the financial 
ratio maximum limit agreed upon by the Company and the 
bank. Up to the end of 2024, the net debt-to-equity ratio was 
at a very sound level of -19.0%, reflecting the Company’s very 
strong capital structure.

telah menargetkan proyeksi pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia 
mencapai 5,2% pada 2025, sebagaimana ditetapkan dalam 
UU APBN 2025. Pertumbuhan ini akan bergantung pada 
kinerja dan stimulus-stimulus kebijakan yang digulirkan 
pemerintah, serta investasi-investasi baru di berbagai sektor. 
Konsumsi rumah tangga dinilai tetap menjadi kontributor 
utama pertumbuhan ekonomi. 
Kinerja industri otomotif diproyeksikan dapat menurun pada 
2025, salah satu penyebabnya adalah pelemahan daya beli 
masyarakat serta kenaikan suku bunga kredit kendaraan 
bermotor. Menyadari pentingnya sektor otomotif bagi 
kontribusi ekonomi Indonesia dan tantangan yang dihadapi 
pada 2025, Kementerian Perindustrian (Kemenperin) secara 
aktif menyampaikan usulan insentif dan relaksasi kebijakan 
kepada pemangku kepentingan terkait, antara lain PPnBM 
ditanggung pemerintah (PPnBM DTP) untuk kendaraan hybrid 
(PHEV, full, mild) sebesar 3%.
Kemenperin juga mendorong pengusaha di Indonesia untuk 
terus memanfaatkan peluang yang ada di industri komponen 
otomotif dan aftermarket. Berdasarkan data dari Alibaba, 
pasar komponen otomotif aftermarket global diperkirakan 
akan tumbuh dari USD400 miliar pada 2023 menjadi USD550 
miliar pada 2028. Lonjakan ini didorong oleh kemajuan 
teknologi, bertambahnya usia kendaraan, berkembangnya 
preferensi konsumen serta pertumbuhan e-commerce. Pasar 
e-commerce komponen otomotif global diperkirakan akan 
mencapai USD200 miliar pada 2027, dengan pertumbuhan 
CAGR sebesar 14% dari tahun 2023 hingga 2027.
Kebijakan Dividen
Perseroan mendistribusikan dividen tunai kepada pemegang 
saham yang diambil dari laba bersih setelah pajak. Perseroan 
tidak menerapkan kebijakan dividen tertentu, dan pembagian 
dividen dilakukan dengan mempertimbangkan sejumlah 
faktor seperti kondisi keuangan, profitabilitas dan kebutuhan 
kas untuk menunjang kegiatan operasional dan investasi, 
serta keputusan RUPS tahunan.
In the Global Economic Prospects report that was published 
on January 17, 2025, Indonesia’s economy is projected to 
grow by 5.1% in 2024 and 2025. In line with the World Bank’s 
estimates, several international institutions, such as the IMF, 
OECD, and UN also project Indonesia’s economic growth to 
grow steadily in the range of 5% in 2025. The government has 
targeted a projection of Indonesia’s economic growth at 5.2% 
in 2025, as stipulated in the 2025 State Budget Law. This 
growth will depend on the performance and policy stimuli 
rolled out by the government, as well as new investments in 
various sectors. Household consumption is considered to 
remain the main contributor to economic growth. 
The performance of the automotive industry is projected 
to decline in 2025, one of the causes is the weakening of 
people’s purchasing power and the increase in motor vehicle 
credit interest rates. Recognizing the importance of the 
automotive sector for Indonesia’s economic contribution 
and the challenges faced in 2025, the Ministry of Industry 
(Kemenperin) is actively submitting incentive proposals and 
policy relaxations to relevant stakeholders, including PPnBM 
borne by the government (PPnBM DTP) for hybrid vehicles 
(PHEV, full, mild) of 3%.
The Ministry of Industry also encourages Indonesian 
entrepreneurs to continue to take advantage of opportunities 
in the automotive components and aftermarket industry. 
Based on data from Alibaba, the global aftermarket 
automotive components market is expected to grow from 
USD400 billion in 2023 to USD550 billion in 2028. This surge 
is driven by technological advances, increasing vehicle 
age, evolving consumer preferences, and the growth of 
e-commerce. The e-commerce market for global automotive 
components is expected to reach USD200 billion in 2027, with 
a CAGR growth of 14% from 2023 to 2027.
Dividend Policy
The Company distributes cash dividends based on net profit 
after taxes. The Company does not apply a specific dividend 
policy, and dividends are distributed after considering 
several factors, such as the financial condition, profitability, 
and cash requirements to support operational activities and 
investment, as well as resolutions of the annual GMS.
