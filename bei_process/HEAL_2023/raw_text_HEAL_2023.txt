Economic and Industry Review
The global economic recovery faced challenges throughout 
2023 in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic. 
The conflict between Russia and Ukraine in Europe remained 
unresolved, and a new crisis erupted in the Gaza Strip in 
October, raising concerns about potential escalation into a 
broader regional armed conflict. Additionally, persistent trade 
tensions between China and the United States continued 
unabated, leading to a trend towards de-globalization and 
disrupting the global supply chain.
Consequently, the global recovery progressed slowly in 2023. 
Ongoing worries about disruptions in food and energy supply 
chains contributed to a surge in inflation, prompting central 
banks to maintain a tight monetary policy throughout the year, 
thereby putting a strain on economic activity.
Facing these global challenges, Bank Indonesia had to raise 
its benchmark interest rate over the last two years, reaching 
6.00% by the end of 2023.
Despite the persistent global turmoil, the Indonesian economy 
demonstrated resilience and positive prospects. With a growth 
rate of 5.05%, Indonesia's economy performed relatively well. 
While slightly lower than the previous year's 5.31% GDP growth, 
this performance positioned the country favorably among 
major emerging and developing economies, highlighting its 
robust economic fundamentals. Annual inflation hovered at 
2.61% according to the Indonesian Statistics Bureau, despite 
an upward trend in the prices of some food commodities 
throughout the year.
Three years after Covid-19 was declared as a global pandemic 
by the World Health Organization and the termination of the 
pandemic status in June 2023, Indonesia witnessed the return 
of the day-to-day business activities to the pre-pandemic level. 
In the healthcare sector, hospitals reported the resurgence in 
patient visits throughout the year.
Looking back, Indonesia's healthcare sector has shown 
remarkable progress over the past decade. The government's 
commitment to healthcare is evident in the allocated budget 
for healthcare development, reaching Rp187.5 trillion or 5.6% 
of the state expenditure in 2024. Since 2014, the country has 
also successfully implemented the universal health coverage 
scheme, known as Jaringan Kesehatan Nasional (JKN), aimed 
at providing all Indonesian citizens with equal access to 
medical services. By the end of 2023, the total membership

Rumah Sakit Hermina
Hermina didirikan tahun 1985 dengan dibukanya rumah sakit 
bersalinnya yang pertama di Jakarta. Hermina kemudian 
mengubah statusnya dari organisasi nirlaba menjadi korporasi 
dengan nama PT Medikaloka Hermina di tahun 1999. 
Perseroan kemudian berkembang menjadi salah satu grup 
rumah sakit swasta terbesar di Indonesia, baik berdasarkan 
ukuran jaringan rumah sakit dan jumlah dari tempat tidurnya. 
Pada tahun 2018 telah melaksanakan penawaran umum 
saham perdananya, melalui pencatatan saham di Bursa Efek 
Indonesia (simbol ticker: HEAL).
Walaupun seluruhnya dikategorikan dalam kelompok rumah 
sakit umum, rumah sakit-rumah sakit Hermina terutama 
dikenal karena sejarah panjangnya di bidang layanan ibu dan 
surpassed 262.7 million, positioning JKN as one of the largest 
healthcare systems globally.
Despite these impressive achievements, the healthcare 
industry continues to face several serious fundamental 
challenges.
The ratios of hospital beds per 1,000 population remain below 
WHO standards and trail behind other Asia-Pacific countries. 
Additionally, there are disparities in conditions and quality 
of facilities, leading to geographical discrepancies between 
Indonesian regions. The ratio of physicians to the population is 
also below the WHO-recommended threshold, exacerbated 
by an uneven distribution of quality healthcare professionals.
The current scenario positions Indonesia's healthcare sector as 
an underpenetrated market with significant growth potential, 
yet it also underscores the complex structural challenges 
faced by the country.
To tackle these challenges, the Government, through the 
Ministry of Health, has undertaken initiatives to implement 
policies supporting the development of an enhanced and 
more inclusive healthcare system for the people of Indonesia.
The enactment of the omnibus health bill, Law No. 17 of 
2023, in August 2023, represents a comprehensive effort to 
reshape the nation's healthcare services, bolster its healthcare 
workforce, and streamline regulations to enhance access to 
high-quality medical care.
Looking ahead, Indonesia's healthcare industry will continue 
to witness the Government's commitment to providing cost-
effective healthcare services to all Indonesians. The recently 
ratified Omnibus Health Law is anticipated to bring forth both 
promising opportunities and challenges for stakeholders. 
Furthermore, the country's large population and expanding 
middle class instill confidence in the long-term prospects for 
the healthcare market in Indonesia.
Hermina Hospitals

first maternity hospital in Jakarta. Hermina subsequently 
transitioned from a non-profit entity to a corporation named 
PT Medikaloka Hermina in 1999. Over time, it has evolved into 
one of the most extensive private hospital groups in Indonesia, 
both in terms of the scale of its hospital network and the 
quantity of hospital beds. In 2018, the Company underwent an 
initial public offering, listing its shares on the Indonesia Stock 
Exchange (ticker symbol: HEAL). 
While all Hermina hospitals are classified as general hospitals, 
the brand is particularly renowned for its rich legacy in 
women’s and children’s services. Currently, Hermina is also

Business expansion targets major cities with a minimum of

centers of excellence, including those specializing in oncology,

an increasing demand for advanced treatments.

Layanan Radioterapi Hermina
Beroperasi sejak bulan Agustus 2022, Layanan Radioterapi 
Hermina merupakan bagian integral dari Layanan Perawatan 
Onkologi Satu Pintu dari Rumah Sakit Hermina Bekasi.
Perawatan radioterapi kami menawarkan tiga jenis teknik 
bagi para pasien: 3D Conformal Radiotherapy (3D CRT), 
Intensity-Modulated Radiation Therapy (IMRT), dan Volumetric 
Modulated Arc Therapy (VMAT). Seluruh perawatan didukung 
oleh simulasi CT agar dapat secara tepat melokalisir tumor

Net Revenues and Net Income Realization
Following more than two years of disruption owing to the 
pandemic, the Indonesian healthcare sector showed gradual 
transition towards the pre-pandemic situation that impacted 
the overall dynamics of the healthcare industry. Along with 
the recovery of patient visits to hospitals in 2023, number of 
inpatient admissions reached 613,000 from 484,000, while 
outpatient visits reached 7,998,000 from 6,635,523 outpatient 
visits in 2022.
In line with higher patient volume, total net revenues grew by 
18.0% to IDR5,784.1 billion from IDR4,901.8 billion, while net 
income booked 47.7% growth to IDR559.3 billion from IDR378.8 
billion a year earlier.
