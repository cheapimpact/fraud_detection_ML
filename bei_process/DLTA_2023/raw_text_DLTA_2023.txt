Tinjauan Ekonomi Indonesia 
di Tahun 2023 
Indonesia Economic Overview in 2023
Laju pertumbuhan nasional pada tahun 2023 mencapai 
5,1%, menurun dibandingkan capaian tahun lalu sebesar 
5,3% akibat ekspor yang menurun. Meski mengalami 
penurunan, pertumbuhan perekonomian Indonesia 
mampu menunjukkan stabilitas dan ketahanan yang 
kuat di tengah tantangan ekonomi global secara umum. 
Konsumsi rumah tangga, yang berkontribusi lebih dari 
setengah PDB nasional, tetap tumbuh kuat dengan 
peningkatan di sektor industri makanan dan minuman. 
Catatan kinerja pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional 
layak 
mendapatkan 
apresiasi, 
karena 
Indonesia 
mampu menjadi salah satu negara yang mencatatkan 
pertumbuhan yang kuat di Asia Tenggara. Selama tahun 
2023, Indonesia dapat menjaga tingkat inflasi direntang 
2,0% hingga 4,0%, yaitu di rata-rata 3,7%. Tingkat 
inflasi ini menjadi pencapaian penting bagi Indonesia 
karena mampu menjaga fluktuasi harga di tengah 
ketidakpastian akibat peningkatan permintaan dan 
biaya yang semakin tinggi. Pencapaian ini merupakan 
hasil dari upaya sinergi Bank Indonesia melalui inisiatif 
Tim Pengendali Inflasi Pusat dan Daerah (TPIP-TPID). 
In 2023, Indonesia’s national growth rate stood at 
5.1% year-on-year, slightly lower than the previous 
year’s 5.3% due to weaker growth in exports. However, 
this slight decrease does not reflect a weakness in 
Indonesia’s economic foundation, but rather highlights 
the country’s robust stability and resilience amidst 
the challenges   facing the world economy at large. 
Household consumption, which accounts for more 
than half of the country’s GDP, remained strong, with a 
notable increase in the food and beverage sector. 
Indonesia’s economic growth record also merits 
commendation, positioning it among one of the 
front-runners in Southeast Asia.   Throughout 2023, 
Indonesia was able to maintain inflation within the 
target range of 2.0% to 4.0%. At an average of 3.7% 
for the year, this marked a significant achievement for 
Indonesia as it effectively managed price fluctuations 
amidst heightened uncertainty due to increased 
demand and higher input costs. Credit goes in part to 
the coordinated efforts and synergy of the government 
as well as Bank of Indonesia, through the   Central and 
Regional Inflation Control Teams (TPIP-TPID).

Indonesia Economy Outlook in 2024
Kondisi pertumbuhan ekonomi global yang penuh 
tantangan, tidak mengecilkan optimisme Indonesia 
untuk menyambut tahun 2024. Dengan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi yang kuat sebagai landasannya, Indonesia siap 
mencapai pertumbuhan yang positif pada tahun 2024.

lembaga 
memproyeksikan 
bahwa 
pertumbuhan 
ekonomi

2024 masih akan tetap stabil di kisaran 5,0%. Asian 
Development Bank (ADB) dan International Monetary

and Development (OECD) sebesar 5,2%. Proyeksi 
pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional lebih tinggi dari 
proyeksi tingkat pertumbuhan ekonomi global 3,1%. 
Despite challenging global economic conditions, 
Indonesia remains steadfastly optimistic about the year 
ahead. With robust economic growth as its cornerstone, 
Indonesia is poised for positive strides in 2024. 
Projections from reputable institutions bolster this 
optimism, with forecasts suggesting that Indonesia’s 
economic growth will remain stable, hovering around 
5.0%. The Asian Development Bank (ADB) and

rate of 5.0%, whereas the Organization for Economic 
Cooperation and Development (OECD) projects a 
growth rate of 5.2%. Notably, these projections outpace 
the anticipated global economic growth rate of 3.1%.

menjadi Rp736,8 miliar. Penurunan Penjualan Neto 
disebabkan oleh penurunan volume penjualan yang

sebesar 
Rp224,1 
miliar,

dibandingkan tahun lalu sebesar Rp235,8 miliar.

Rupiah atas pembelian barang impor.
Laba Kotor
Perseroan 
mencatatkan 
penurunan 
Laba 
Kotor 
sebesar 5,6% menjadi Rp512,8 miliar dibandingkan

variabel produksi, khususnya kenaikan biaya malt,

Imbasnya, Marjin Laba Kotor tetap terjaga di angka

Beban Usaha
Beban Usaha Perseroan meningkat sebesar 7,0% 
menjadi Rp292,2 miliar pada tahun 2023 dibandingkan 
tahun lalu sebesar Rp273,2 miliar. Hal itu didorong 
oleh peningkatan beban gaji dan upah, jasa layanan 
rekrutmen karyawan pihak ketiga, pelatihan, promosi,

Laba Sebelum Pajak dan Laba Neto Tahun 
Berjalan
Setelah membukukan Laba Sebelum Pajak pada tahun 
2022 sebesar Rp294,2 miliar, Perseroan membukukan 
penurunan sebesar 14,6% pada tahun 2023, menjadi 
Rp251,1 miliar. Meskipun margin laba tetap kuat berkat 
kenaikan harga di bulan April 2023, laba tahun berjalan 
tertekan menjadi lebih rendah oleh karena penurunan 
volume penjualan dan biaya input produksi yang lebih 
tinggi, serta kenaikan beban usaha.  Demikian pula, 
terjadi pada akun Laba Tahun Berjalan yang Dapat 
Diatribusikan kepada Pemilik Entitas Induk menurun 
13,5% dari Rp230,2 miliar di tahun 2022 menjadi 
Rp199,1 miliar pada tahun 2023.   
Net Sales 
The Company recorded a decline in Net Sales of 5.4% 
from IDR778.7 billion in 2022 to IDR736.8 billion in 
2023. The decrease in Net Sales was caused by the 
drop in sales volume tempered by the price increase 
implemented in April 2023.   
Cost of Goods Sold 
In 2023, Cost of Goods Sold was recorded at IDR224.1 
billion, a decrease of 5.0% compared to last year’s 
IDR235.8 billion.  The decline was likewise mainly due 
to the sales volume contraction, negated by higher 
variable production and distribution costs, combined 
with the impact of the depreciation of the IDR currency 
on imported materials.
Gross Profit
Gross Profit decreased by 5.6% to IDR512.8 billion 
from last year’s IDR543.0 billion along with the decline 
in sales volume.  The increase in variable production, 
particularly the rise in cost of malt, adjunct and some 
packaging materials, was mitigated by the price 
increase in April 2023.  Consequently, the Gross Profit 
Margins remained healthy at 69% in both years. 
Operating Expenses
Total Operating Expenses this year rose by 7.0% to 
IDR292.2 billion from IDR273.2 billion as a result of 
higher salaries and wages, third party service providers 
employee training, promotions, royalty, and travel 
expenses.  
Income Before Tax and Income for the Year
After posting a Profit Before Income Tax in 2022 of 
IDR294.2 billion, the Company saw its earnings fall 
by 14.6% to IDR251.1 billion in 2023.  Although the 
profit margins remained strong due to the April 2023 
price increase, the profit for the year was pulled down 
by lower sales volume and higher cost of production 
inputs, and increase in operating expenses.  In the same 
way, Income for the Year Attributable to Owners of 
the Company tapered down by 13,5% from IDR230.2 
billion in 2022 to IDR199.1 billion in 2023.

tahun sebelumnya.
Changes in the Regulations and Its Impact on the 
Company and Beer Industry 
On Excise Taxes 
On December 22, 2018, based on the Finance Ministry 
Decree 158/PMK.010/2018, the Director General 
of Customs and Excise (DGCE) imposed higher excise 
taxes on alcoholic beverages effective January 1, 2019. 
For the beer industry, the excise tax increased to Rp 
15,000 per liter which is still in effect as at December 
31, 2023 .  However, on December 28, 2023 and based 
on the Finance Ministry Decree No. 160 of 2023, the 
DGCE levied another excise tax increase on alcoholic 
beverages with tax on beer beverages increasing to 
IDR16,500 per liter effective January 1, 2024.  
In an effort to curtail smuggling, the DGCE abolished 
the cigarette and alcohol excise tax exemption in 
the Free Trade Zone of Batam, Bintan and Karimun 
effective May 17, 2019 as per DGCE’s Memo No. 
ND-466/BC/2019.  As of end December 2023, this 
regulation remains in effect and all beers sold in these 
areas are currently subject to excise tax of IDR15,000 
per liter, which will increase to IDR16,500 per liter 
beginning January 1, 2024.
On Production and Distribution of Alcohol Products 
Presidential Decree No. 74/2013 regulating the 
production, 
sale 
and 
distribution 
of 
alcoholic 
beverages, and, the Ministry of Trade Regulation No. 
20/2014 requiring all retailers and distributors to 
secure special licenses for the storage, distribution, and 
sale of beer remain in effect.   Off-premise outlets such 
as supermarkets, hypermarkets and grocery stores are 
required to maintain separate sections and shelves 
at the stores solely for alcoholic beverage displays as 
well as to have dedicated storage rooms for alcoholic 
beverages . 
In addition, Minister of Trade Regulation No. 06/2015 
prohibits the sale and distribution of beer and 
other alcoholic beverages in convenience stores, 
minimarkets, and traditional off-premise outlets. This 
regulation has been in effect since April 2015 onwards.
Regardless of the underlying reasons, these regulations 
continue to impact the alcoholic beverage industry and 
the Company’s performance as in previous years.

Undang-undang di atas memberikan dampak serta 
tekanan kepada perusahaan bir dan juga berdampak

RUU Larangan Minuman Beralkohol yang telah 
disahkan sejak 2015 kembali diajukan di Depan 
Perwakilan Rakyat pada tahun 2021 dan ditetapkan 
sebagai RUU Prioritas pada tahun 2022. Pada tahun 
2022, anggota DPR melakukan serangkaian kunjungan 
kerja ke daerah-daerah, terutama di daerah dimana 
konsumsi alkohol adalah bagian dari budaya dan tradisi 
local. Hal ini bertujuan untuk mendapatkan  masukan 
dari masyarakat. RUU ini bertujuan untuk mengatur 
penjualan dan peredaran minuman beralkohol melalui 
mekanisme hukum yang lebih kuat di luar peraturan 
presiden dan peraturan menteri.
Kenaikan Upah Minimum
Upah minimum di Kabupaten Bekasi dimana Perseroan 
berlokasi telah meningkat rata-rata sebesar 8,0% 
per tahun dari tahun 2015 hingga 2021. Pada tahun 
2022, Gubernur Jawa Barat memutuskan untuk 
tidak mengharuskan memberlakukan  kenaikan upah 
minimum pada tahun tersebut dengan menerbitkan Surat 
Keputusan No.561/Kep. 732-Kesra/2021 tentang hal 
ini.  Pada tahun 2023, pemerintah daerah mengeluarkan 
Keputusan No.561.7/ Kep. 776-Kesra/2022 terkait 
dengan kenaikan upah minimum sebesar 7,2% di tahun 
tersebut. Peningkatan ini diikuti oleh provisi lain dalam 
hal menyesuaikan kenaikan upah minimum sebesar 2,0%, 
efektif 1 Januari 2024 sesuai dengan Surat Keputusan No. 
561.7/Kep.B04-Kesra/2023.
Penurunan Tarif PPH Badan Untuk Wajib Pajak 
Perusahaan Dalam Negeri
Pemerintah Indonesia mengeluarkan Undang-Undang 
No. 2 Tahun 2020 yang dijabarkan lebih lanjut di 
Peraturan Pemerintah No. 30 Tahun 2020 (PP30/2020). 
Undang-undang 
ini 
bertujuan 
untuk 
menjaga 
perekonomian dan meringankan beban perusahaan 
dalam negeri dari dampak pandemi COVID-19. 
Salah satu pasalnya adalah pengurangan pajak 
penghasilan badan dari saat ini 25% menjadi 22% untuk 
tahun pajak 2020 dan 2021, dan pengurangan tahap 
lanjut menjadi 20% mulai tahun pajak 2022. Perusahaan 
publik yang minimal 40% sahamnya diperdagangkan di 
Bursa Efek Indonesia berhak memperoleh tambahan 
insentif pajak sebesar 3%. Tetapi pengurangan tarif 
pajak penghasilan badan menjadi 20% pada tahun 2022 
ditangguhkan berdasarkan undang-undang No.7 Tahun 
2021 tentang harmonisasi peraturan perpajakan. 
The foregoing laws continued to put pressure on 
brewers and impact the Company’s performance as 
they have in previous years .
The Alcohol Prohibition Bill which had been put on 
hold since 2015 was reintroduced in the House of 
Representatives’ National Legislation Program in 
2021 and designated as a priority bill in 2022.  In 2022, 
lawmakers conducted a series of working visits to the 
regions, especially in areas where alcohol consumption 
is part of the local culture and tradition, to seek 
feedback from the community.  The bill aims to further 
regulate the sale and distribution of alcohol beverages 
through stronger legal mechanism beyond presidential 
and ministerial regulations. 
Minimum Wage Increase 
The minimum wage in Bekasi Regency where the 
Company is located, has been increasing by an average 
of 8.0% per year from 2015 to 2021.  In 2022, the 
Governor of West Java decided not to impose any 
minimum wage increase for the year and issued Decree 
No. 561/Kep. 732-Kesra/2021 to this effect.  However, 
in 2023, the local government issued Decree No. 561.7/
Kep. 776-Kesra/2022 increasing the minimum wage 
by 7.2% for the year.  This increase was followed by 
another pronouncement to adjust the minimum wage 
by another 2.0% effective January 1, 2024 based on 
Decree No. 561.7/Kep.B04-Kesra/2023. 
Reduction of Corporate Income Tax for Domestic 
Corporate Taxpayers
The Indonesian government issued Law No. 2 of 
2020 (UU 2/2020) which was further elaborated in 
Government Regulation No. 30 of 2020 (PP30/2020) 
to safeguard the economy and ease the burden on 
domestic companies from the impact of the COVID-19 
pandemic .
One of the main changes introduced by this regulation 
was the reduction in corporate income tax from the 
prevailing 25% to 22% for taxable years 2020 and 
2021, and a further reduction in the tax rate to 20% 
beginning taxable year 2022.  Publicly listed companies 
with at least 40% of its shares traded in Indonesia Stock 
Exchange (IDX) will be entitled to an additional 3% 
reduction in tax rate.  However, the reduced corporate 
income tax of 20% by 2022 was indefinitely deferred 
based on the Indonesian Government Law No. 7 of 
2021 concerning the harmonization of tax regulations.

Kenaikan Pajak Pertambahan Nilai (PPN)
Pada Oktober 2021, pemerintah Indonesia menerbitkan 
Undang-Undang No. 7/2021 atau dikenal sebagai UU 
Harmonisasi Peraturan Perpajakan (UU HPP). Undang-
Undang tersebut bertujuan meningkatkan penerimaan 
pajak dan tingkat kepatuhan membayar pajak.
Undang-undang ini juga mengatur bahwa Pajak 
Pertambahan Nilai naik dari 10% menjadi 11%, berlaku 
efektif sejak 1 April 2022, dan akan naik secara 
bertahap menjadi 12% pada 1 Januari 2025. Kenaikan 
ini pada akhirnya akan mempengaruhi harga konsumen 
barang dan jasa termasuk minuman beralkohol, dan 
dampak terhadap inflasi diperkirakan minimal.
Dampak Depresiasi Rupiah
Menutup tahun 2022, Rupiah menguat terhadap 
USD di angka Rp15.565. Rupiah menguat pada 
triwulan I tahun 2023 mencapai posisi terkuatnya 
pada 28 April 2023 sebesar Rp14.665 terhadap USD, 
berkat masuknya modal asing dan dukungan Bank 
Indonesia untuk mengendalikan inflasi domestik di 
tengah gencarnya Federal Reserve AS menaikan suku 
bunga yang mengakibatkan inflasi. Akan tetapi, Rupiah 
terus melemah pada  kuartal keempat tahun 2023, 
dimana telah ambang batas Rp16.000 terhadap USD 
dan mencapai Rp15.935 pada tanggal 27 Oktober 
2023, terendah sepanjang sejarah di mana selama 
tiga setengah tahun terakhir Federal Reserve AS 
terus gencar menaikkan suku bunga. Rupiah menguat 
kembali tahun 2023 di angka Rp 15.395 terhadap USD. 
Pelemahan Rupiah pada tahun 2023 juga memberikan 
tekanan yang tinggi pada biaya produksi, yang berimbas 
pada kenaikan harga bahan baku langsung yang diimpor 
seperti malt (bahan gandum) dan beberapa bahan 
kemasan untuk produksi.
Perubahan Kebijakan Akuntansi yang Berpengaruh 
terhadap Perseroan
Beberapa interpretasi Pernyataan Standar Akuntansi 
Keuangan (PSAK) yang baru dan diamandemen telah 
diterbitkan dan berlaku efektif pada tahun 2022, namun, 
standar tersebut tidak mengakibatkan perubahan yang 
substansial terhadap kebijakan akuntansi Perseroan 
dan tidak memiliki dampak material dan signifikan 
terhadap laporan keuangan Perseroan.
In the case of PT Delta, the Company’s taxable profits 
are currently subject to a reduced income tax rate of 
22%.  However, it is not qualified for the additional 
3% corporate income tax reduction based on the 
percentage of Company’s shares traded in the IDX 
since it is below 40 %.
Increase in Value Added Tax (VAT)
In October 2021, the Indonesian government issued 
Law No. 7/2021, also known as the Harmonization 
of Tax Regulations Law (HPP Law). The law aims to 
increase tax revenue and the level of tax compliance.
The law also stipulates that Value Added Tax will 
increase from 10% to 11%, effective from April 1, 2022, 
and will gradually increase to 12% on January 1, 2025. 
These increases will eventually affect the consumer 
prices of goods and services including alcoholic 
beverages. The impact on inflation is expected to be 
minimal.
Impact of Rupiah Depreciation 
After closing the year 2022 at IDR 15,565, the Rupiah 
strengthened in the first quarter of 2023, reaching its 
strongest position on April 28, 2023 at IDR 14,665 
against the USD, thanks to inflows of foreign capital 
and support by Bank of Indonesia to tame local inflation 
following the US Federal Reserve’s massive interest 
hikes. Thereafter, the Rupiah steadily weakened by 
the fourth quarter of 2023, nearing the psychological 
threshold of IDR 16,000 against the USD and reaching 
IDR 15,935 on October 27, 2023, its lowest in the 
past three and a half years as the US Federal Reserve 
continued to hike interest rates.   The Rupiah gained 
some ground ending the year 2023 at IDR 15,395 
against the USD.   A weaker Rupiah in 2023 placed a 
significant upward pressure on the production costs 
as prices of imported direct materials such as malt and 
several packaging materials became more expensive.
