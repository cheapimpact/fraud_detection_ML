Shipping Segment
The Company, through its subsidiaries, offers the tanker shipping services 
with a total serving fleet as of December 31, 2024 was 30 units of tanker 
vessels. The capacity of the tankers was 1.58 million DWT in total. The 
Company's tankers carry cargoes of oil and gas, as well as other liquid 
cargoes, owned by the ship charterer to be carried to the location as specified 
by the ship charterer.
The provision of the Company’s shipping services covers various lines of 
Indonesian oil and gas supply chain, from upstream to downstream. On 
the upstream side, the Company charters out its tankers as storage for oil 
and gas after the production takes place in the oil and gas fields. From the 
storage center, the tanker will continue to transport the oil and gas to the 
oil and gas refinery for further processing. After processing, the tanker will 
carry the refined products, such as fuel, to the oil and gas depot to be finally 
distributed to the end users.
Large sized tankers usually serve transportation in the upstream segment 
of the oil and gas supply chain. Smaller sized vessels are generally used to 
serve the downstream segment. The Company's fleet consists of various 
sizes, ranging from Very Large Crude Carriers (VLCC), aframax tankers, 
medium range (MR) tankers, General Purpose (GP) tankers, and small tankers.
The Company's tanker fleet also transports crude oil cargo from overseas 
to Indonesia, to meet domestic energy consumption requirements. There 
are also products from oil and gas refineries, such as petrochemicals, which 
require tanker shipping services to be carried to reach the customer points.
For the financial year of 2024, the Company recorded an average utilization 
for its overall fleet at the level of ±86%. The Company was consistently able 
to maintain its tanker vessels utilization at the optimal level, due to the solid 
demand for tanker charters in Indonesia.
Customers generally charter the Company's tankers using time charter 
contract, which is, the contract for the vessel for a certain period of time. 
In a time charter contract, the Company delivers cargo to a location at a 
time schedule according to the customer's instructions, during the agreed 
contract period.

dari berbagai ukuran, mulai dari Very Large Crude Carrier (VLCC), tanker 
aframax, tanker medium range (MR), tanker General Purpose (GP), dan 
tanker ukuran kecil.
Armada tanker Perseroan juga mentransportasikan kargo minyak mentah 
dari luar negeri ke Indonesia, untuk memenuhi kebutuhan konsumsi di 
dalam negeri. Terdapat pula produk sampingan hasil penyulingan dari kilang 
migas, seperti petrokimia yang membutuhkan jasa pelayaran tanker untuk 
sampai kepada titik pelanggan.

Shipyard Segment
The Company, through its subsidiary, has a shipyard which is located in 
Tanjung Balai, Karimun, Riau Islands. The shipyard has the competence to 
work on ship building, provides ship docking services, ship repair services, 
ship conversion services, and other related services. The Company's shipyard 
project portfolios currently have been very diversified, which cover tanker 
vessel, bulk carrier, container ship, Ro-Ro ship, offshore support vessel, 
self-propelled oil barge, navigation ship, pioneer ship, hospital ship, patrol 
ship, dredgers, and other ships.
In providing shipbuilding services, the shipyard work process will start 
with the design drawing of the ship and determination of various technical 
specifications. Both shall obtain approvals from the related parties, such 
as customers and classification bodies. The Company will further continue 
with the suppliers pitching process in order to prepare skilled manpower 
as well as assign ship component providers. Thereafter, the shipbuilding 
process will begin. Shipbuilding will go through the fabrication, assembly, 
and painting processes.
Each stage of the shipbuilding process is inspected by the classification body 
as well as the customer. This inspection procedure is carried out to ensure 
that the construction has been made in accordance with the determined 
specifications. When the ship construction is completed, the Company will 
conduct a sea trial for the ship. After finalization, the ship will be handed 
over to the ship owner.

Industri pelayaran Indonesia juga dilindungi oleh azas cabotage. Azas 
cabotage menentukan bahwa seluruh transportasi domestik antarpulau 
di Indonesia harus dilakukan oleh kapal berbendera Indonesia. Azas 
cabotage telah menguntungkan industri pelayaran nasional sejak  awal 
pemberlakuannya. Pada saat ini hampir seluruh pelayaran domestik telah 
dilakukan oleh kapal berbendera Indonesia. Azas cabotage diharapkan 
akan terus dapat menjadi penopang pertumbuhan kegiatan pelayaran 
nasional dalam jangka panjang.
Kondusifnya aktivitas pelayaran di Indonesia selanjutnya turut membuka 
banyak peluang bagi industri galangan kapal di dalam negeri. Dengan 
kondisi geografis Indonesia yang mencakup 13 ribu pulau, diperlukan 
infrastruktur yang memadai untuk memastikan wilayah perairan Indonesia 
dapat terkoneksi secara optimal. Pemerintah dalam beberapa tahun ini 
telah secara aktif membangun kapal-kapal baru, diantaranya kapal perintis 
yang yang dapat menjangkau pulau terpencil, terdepan, terbelakang, dan 
perbatasan (3TP). Tercatat pembangunan kapal mencapai 75 ribu GT 
pada 2023, meningkat 78% dibandingkan dengan 42 ribu GT pada 2022.
Indonesian shipping industry is also protected by cabotage law. The cabotage 
principle regulates that all domestic inter-island transportation in Indonesia 
must be carried out by Indonesian-flagged vessels. The cabotage law has 
benefited the national shipping industry since its implementation. Currently, 
almost all domestic shipping activities has been carried out by Indonesian-
flagged vessels. The cabotage principle is expected to continue to support 
the growth of national shipping activities in the long term.
Conducive shipping activities in Indonesia have also opened up many 
opportunities for the domestic shipyard industry. With Indonesia's 
geographical conditions which comprising 13 thousand islands, adequate 
infrastructure is necessary to ensure that Indonesian waters are connected 
to each other optimally. In recent years, the Government has been actively 
building new ships, including pioneer ships, in order to reach remote, 
outermost, underdeveloped, and border islands (3TP). Ship construction 
was recorded to reach 75 thousand GT in the year of 2023, an increase of 
78% compared to 42 thousand GT in the year 2022.

Kapal dengan usia yang tinggi yang ada di Indonesia juga merupakan peluang 
permintaan jasa pembangunan kapal di galangan kapal, untuk membangun 
kapal baru menggantikan kapal usia yang tinggi tersebut. Daur ulang kapal 
di Indonesia, yaitu proses pemecahan kapal usia tua yang dilakukan untuk 
mendapatkan suku cadang, bahan baku, atau untuk pembuangan, tercatat 
mencapai 84 ribu GT pada 2023, naik 186% dibandingkan 29 ribu GT 
pada 2022. Usia kapal di Indonesia saat ini rata-rata mencapai sekitar 21 
tahun, dengan kapal jenis general cargo memiliki usia mencapai 30 tahun.
Ships with the relatively high age in Indonesia are also opportunities for 
demand for shipbuilding services at shipyards, to build new ships to replace 
the old ones. Ship recycling in Indonesia, which is, the process of breaking 
down old ships to obtain spare parts, raw materials, or for disposal, was 
recorded to reach 84 thousand GT in the year of 2023, or an increase of 
186% compared with 29 thousand GT in the year of 2022. The ships in 
Indonesia currently have the average age of around 21 years old, with 
general cargo ships have the average age reaching 30 years old.
Untuk dapat layak beroperasi di perairan, seluruh kapal juga harus 
melaksanakan pemeriksaan (survei) secara berkala. Survei kapal tersebut 
dilaksanakan untuk tujuan verifikasi kesesuaian kapal dengan peraturan 
industri. Adanya kebutuhan akan survei kapal menjadi potensi pendapatan 
berulang bagi galangan kapal dari segmen pekerjaan dry docking dan 
perbaikan dan perawatan kapal. Jumlah kapal komersial berbendera 
Indonesia tercatat tumbuh 6% CAGR dalam periode 10 tahun terakhir.
Meski demikian, ditengah peluang yang tersedia dari industri pelayaran 
dan galangan kapal, komitmen untuk menurunkan emisi gas rumah kaca 
akan juga memerlukan penyesuaian penggunaan teknologi pelayaran dan 
galangan kapal yang mana juga membutuhkan investasi. Pemerintah juga 
mulai mendorong penggunaan bahan bakar B40 pada tahun 2025 termasuk 
pula pemakaiannya oleh industri pelayaran.
In order to be able to operate in the sea waters, all ships are also required 
to undergo periodic inspections (surveys). The ship survey is carried out 
to verify the conformity of the ship with the prevailing regulations in the 
industry. The need for ship surveys has become potential recurring income 
for shipyards for dry docking as well as ship repair and maintenance segment. 
The number of Indonesian-flagged commercial ships were reported to have 
the growth of  6% CAGR in the last 10 years period.
Nevertheless, amidst the opportunities available in the shipping and shipyard 
industry, there are also challenges needed to be addressed by shipping 
and shipyard business doers. The commitment to reduce greenhouse gas 
emissions requires various adjustment in using shipping and shipyard 
technology which also demands investment. The Government has also 
encouraged the use of B40 fuel starting 2025, including the usage in the 
shipping industry.
