The Company’s operational activities offer transportation 
services with tugs and barges. The transportation services 
are segmented into 2 (two) types, known as:
Transshipment
This service uses tugboats and barges to haul goods/
commodities to a mother vessel, then continues to the 
transportation process. Transshipment services are 
provided to facilitate the inability of mother vessels to dock 
at loading ports.
Apart from using tugs and barges, transshipment also 
uses a crane barge to transport goods. The range of 
transshipment services is usually at most 8 (eight) miles.
Inter-Island Transportation
This service provides inter-island goods/commodity 
transportation from ports to discharge ports, domestically 
and regionally, all across Indonesia.
Balanced transshipment and interisland transportation 
operations also support the Company’s performance. The 
Company always carefully manages shipment planning to 
adequately fulfill the customer’s compliance scheme.

The Company is committed to establishing harmonious 
and mutually beneficial partnerships with all parties, 
including the regulators, business partners, customers, 
and stakeholders. As a manifestation of this commitment, 
the Company has an active membership in the Indonesian 
National Shipowners Association (INSA). 
INSA accommodates a variety of industrial components, 
both domestic and overseas, to share information and 
collaborate. Through INSA, the Company seizes the 
opportunity to stay abreast of developments in the shipping 
industry, as well as sharing information and establishing 
healthy relationships with competitors and related 
associations. In addition, the Company also cooperates 
with Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency 
to coordinate well in order to maintain smooth operating 
activities which are highly dependent on the latest weather 
conditions.
In financial terms, the Company ensures that debt and 
receivables are being managed properly with the aim of 
maintaining a good cash flow. The Company is committed 
to repaying loans to suppliers and service providers 
in accordance with the predetermined deadlines and 
agreements. Moreover, the Company is trying to reduce 
loans that could potentially incur long-term interest 
expenses. 
Debt and receivable management also depend heavily on 
customer credibility. In an effort to avoid uncollectible debts, 
the Company carries out strict selection and evaluation of 
customers’ conditions and reputation through a number 
of indicators, including track record of debt payments and 
financial condition.
