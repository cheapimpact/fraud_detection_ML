Tinjauan Operasi Per Segmen Usaha
Review of Operations by Business Segment
Pada tahun 2024, perekonomian Indonesia menunjukkan 
pertumbuhan yang stabil dengan proyeksi pertumbuhan PDB 
sebesar 5,3% menurut Laporan Perekonomian Indonesia 2024 
yang dikeluarkan oleh Bank Indonesia. Inflasi selama tahun 
2024 menurun dan berada dalam rentang sasaran 2,5±1% yang 
didukung kebijakan moneter Bank Indonesia yang konsisten 
serta koordinasi yang erat dengan Pemerintah.  
Industri kesehatan Indonesia sendiri menunjukkan pertumbuhan 
yang positif, didorong oleh peningkatan investasi pemerintah 
dan meningkatnya permintaan akan layanan medis berkualitas. 
Anggaran kesehatan pemerintah meningkat menjadi sekitar 
Rp187,5 triliun, mencerminkan komitmen untuk meningkatkan 
infrastruktur dan layanan kesehatan.
Namun, industri kesehatan juga menghadapi tantangan besar, 
seperti meningkatnya klaim kesehatan dan inflasi medis yang 
dipicu oleh perkembangan teknologi. Riset oleh Mercer Marsh 
Benefits (MMB) mengenai Health Trends 2024 melaporkan tren 
peningkatan biaya kesehatan global yang diproyeksikan akan 
tumbuh hingga 11,6% dan Asia sebesar 11,4%. Sedangkan biaya 
kesehatan Indonesia diprediksi akan terus tumbuh hingga 13,0%, 
atau di atas proyeksi tren biaya kesehatan global dan Asia. Oleh 
karena itu, diperlukan strategi yang efektif untuk mengelola 
biaya kesehatan dan meningkatkan efisiensi layanan kesehatan.
Sumber:
	
> Bank Indonesia, “Laporan Perekonomian Indonesia Tahun 2024”.
	
> Kementerian Keuangan Republik Indonesia, “Informasi APBN 2024 - 
Mempercepat Transformasi Ekonomi yang Inklusif dan Berkelanjutan”
	
> Kementerian Sekretariat Negara Republik Indonesia “Formula Jitu 
Pemerintah Atasi Kenaikan Inflasi Medis dan Biaya Kesehatan”. 
In 2024, the Indonesian economy demonstrated stable growth, 
with a projected GDP increase of 5.3%, according to the 
Indonesian Economic Report 2024 issued by Bank Indonesia. 
Inflation during this year declined and remained within the target 
range of 2.5±1%, supported by Bank Indonesia’s consistent 
monetary policy and close coordination with the government.
The healthcare industry in Indonesia also showed positive 
growth, driven by increased government investment and a 
rising demand for quality medical services. The government’s 
health budget rose to approximately Rp187.5 trillion, reflecting 
a commitment to enhancing health infrastructure and services.
However, the healthcare industry faces significant challenges, 
including rising health claims and medical inflation due to 
technological advancements. Research by Mercer Marsh Benefits 
(MMB) on Health Trends 2024 indicates that global health costs 
are projected to grow by 11.6%, while costs in Asia are expected 
to rise by 11.4%. In Indonesia, healthcare costs are predicted to 
increase by as much as 13.0%, surpassing both the global and 
Asian health cost trends. Therefore, effective strategies are 
essential for managing health costs and improving the efficiency 
of healthcare services.
Source:
	
> Bank Indonesia, “Indonesia Economic Report 2024”.
	
> Ministry of Finance of the Republic of Indonesia, “State Budget Information 
2024 - Accelerating Inclusive and Sustainable Economic Transformation”
	
> Ministry of State Secretariat of the Republic of Indonesia, “Government’s 
Surefire Formula to Address Rising Medical Inflation and Health Costs” 
Hingga  akhir  2024,  Perseroan  mengoperasikan  8  (delapan) unit 
rumah sakit EMC Healthcare di lokasi-lokasi strategis di wilayah 
Jabodetabek yaitu RS EMC Alam Sutera, RS EMC Cibitung, RS 
EMC Cikarang, RS EMC Grha Kedoya, RS EMC Pekayon, RS EMC 
Pulomas, RS EMC Sentul, dan RS EMC Tangerang. Kedelapan 
rumah sakit tersebut memiliki total kapasitas maksimal tempat 
tidur sejumlah 1.430 tempat tidur; 937 dokter yang terdiri dari 
dokter spesialis/ subspesialis dan dokter umum, serta 11 Psikolog 
klinis; 2.125 tenaga medis termasuk perawat yang melayani lebih 
dari 740.772 pasien. 
As of the end of 2024, the Company operated 8 (eight) EMC 
Healthcare hospital units in strategic locations throughout the 
Greater Jakarta area. These included RS EMC Alam Sutera, 
RS EMC Cibitung, RS EMC Cikarang, RS EMC Grha Kedoya, 
RS EMC Pekayon, RS EMC Pulomas, RS EMC Sentul, and 
RS EMC Tangerang. Together, these eight hospitals had a total bed 
capacity of 1,430 beds, staffed by 937 doctors, which included 
specialists/subspecialists and general practitioners, and 11 
clinical psychologists. Our medical personnel, consisting of 2,125 
individuals, including nurses, have provided care to over 740,772 
patients.

2023. Di sisi lain, rata-rata lama rawat inap pasien menurun dari

Patient Visits
In 2024, we recorded a total of 54,869 inpatient visits, reflecting 
an increase of 9.0% compared to 50,340 inpatient visits in 
2023. Similarly, the number of outpatient visits rose by 1.8% to 
740,772 visits in 2024, compared to 673,659 visits in 2023.

hospitals was 1,030 beds in 2024, and 1,036 beds in 2023.

rate in 2024 increased to 45.1%, compared to 42.2% in 2023. 
Conversely, the average length of stay for patients decreased 
from 3.2 days in 2023 to 3.1 days.

Management’s Discussion and Analysis of financial performance 
is prepared based on financial data presented in accordance 
with the rules set forth in Indonesian Financial Accounting 
Standards (“SAK”). The following discussion and analysis refers 
to the Company’s Consolidated Financial Statements for the 
dates and years ended 31 December 2024 and 2023 which have 
been audited by Public Accounting Firm Purwantono, Sungkoro 
& Surja (member of Ernst & Young Global Limited) with an 
unqualified opinion signed by Mr. Mento dated 17 March 2025.
Service Revenues
In 2024, the Company booked service revenues of Rp1.7 
trillion, an increase of 11% compared to Rp1.5 trillion in 2023. 
As described in the Revenues by Business Segment section, 
this growth was mainly due to an increase in medical support 
revenues.
Cost of Revenues
The Company booked cost of revenues of Rp1,192.2 billion in 
2024, an 11% increase compared to Rp1,072.3 billion in 2023. 
This increase was mainly due to an increase in medical support, 
and others.
Operating Expenses
Operating expenses in 2024 were recorded at Rp411.8 billion, 
down 0.05% compared to Rp412 billion in 2023. The decrease 
was mainly due to a decrease in general and administrative 
expenses.
Operating Income
Based on the financial results described above, the Company's 
operating income stood at Rp102.4 billion in 2024, an increase 
of 116% compared to Rp47.3 billion in 2023.
Profit for the Year
In 2024, the Company booked a profit for the year of Rp20.6 
billion, an increase of 16% compared to Rp17.7 billion in 2023. 
This increase was due to a decrease in general and administrative 
expenses.
Total Other Comprehensive Income (Loss)
The Company recorded other comprehensive income of Rp133.3 
billion in 2024, compared to other comprehensive loss of Rp2.0 
billion in 2023. This comprehensive income was mainly due to 
land revaluation in 2024.
Total Comprehensive Income
In 2024, the Company booked total comprehensive income 
of Rp153.9 billion, up 881% compared to Rp15.6 billion in 
2023. This increase was mainly due to the increase in other 
comprehensive income.
Current Assets
The Company’s current assets as of 31 December 2024 
amounted to Rp491.2 billion, up 4% compared to Rp472.3 billion 
in the previous year. This increase was mainly due to an increase 
in trade receivables in 2024.
Non-Current Assets
The Company’s non-current assets as of 31 December 2024 
amounted to Rp5.1 trillion, an increase of 3.6% compared to 
Rp4.9 trillion one year earlier. The growth was mainly due to an 
increase in fixed assets.
Total Assets
As a result, the Company’s total assets as of 31 December2024 
amounted to Rp5.6 trillion, an increase of 4% compared to Rp5.4 
trillion as of 31 December 2023.
Current Liabilities
As of 31 December 2024, the Company’s current liabilities 
amounted to Rp663.2 billion, up 22% compared to Rp541.7 
billion at the end of 2023. This increase was mainly due to short-
term bank loans of Rp140 billion in 2024 and an increase in trade 
payables.
Laba Tahun Berjalan
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan membukukan laba tahun berjalan 
sebesar Rp20,6 miliar, naik 16% dibandingkan Rp17,7 miliar pada 
tahun 2023. Kenaikan ini disebabkan karena penurunan pada 
biaya umum dan administrasi.
Total Penghasilan (Rugi) Komprehensif Lain
Perseroan mencatat penghasilan komprehensif lain sebesar 
Rp133,3 miliar di tahun 2024, dibandingkan rugi komprehensif 
lain sebesar Rp2,0 miliar di tahun 2023. Penghasilan komprehensif 
ini terutama disebabkan karena terdapat nilai revaluasi tanah 
pada tahun 2024.
Total Penghasilan Komprehensif
Di tahun 2024, Perseroan membukukan total penghasilan 
komprehensif sebesar Rp153,9 miliar, naik 881% dibandingkan 
Rp15,6 miliar di tahun 2023. Kenaikan ini terutama disebabkan 
oleh naiknya penghasilan komprehensif lain.

Prospek Usaha
Business Outlook
Industri kesehatan di Indonesia diprediksi akan mengalami 
pertumbuhan signifikan pada tahun 2025. Peningkatan anggaran 
kesehatan menjadi Rp217,3 triliun atau 6% dari total APBN 2025 
merupakan wujud komitmen Pemerintah untuk meningkatkan 
layanan kesehatan. Dana ini akan digunakan untuk mendukung 
program 
unggulan 
Presiden 
Prabowo 
Subianto 
dalam 
menciptakan Sumber Daya Manusia yang sehat dan produktif.
Pemerintah fokus pada pembangunan sistem kesehatan 
yang kuat dan responsif melalui program ”Indonesia Sehat”, 
yang merupakan salah satu target transformasi sosial menuju 
Indonesia Emas 2045. Strategi utamanya mencakup penguatan 
tenaga medis, pengembangan layanan kesehatan hingga ke desa, 
peningkatan gizi, pendidikan dokter spesialis, dan pengendalian 
konsumsi produk berbahaya.
Industri layanan kesehatan di Indonesia juga mengalami 
perkembangan signifikan, dengan peningkatan adopsi teknologi 
seperti telemedicine, big data, dan kecerdasan buatan untuk 
diagnosis dan perawatan. Sistem Jaminan Sosial Nasional 
Indonesia (BPJS Kesehatan) juga memainkan peran penting 
dalam memperluas akses layanan kesehatan.
Dalam hal ini, Perseroan memiliki posisi strategis untuk 
memanfaatkan peluang ini dengan mengembangkan kapasitas 
ruangan kamar rawat inap melalui penambahan gedung 
baru. Perseroan juga berencana untuk memperkenalkan 
program-program layanan pencegahan yang komprehensif 
dan berinvestasi dalam peralatan medis mutakhir dan solusi 
kesehatan digital untuk meningkatkan efisiensi operasional dan 
memberikan layanan kesehatan yang unggul.
The healthcare industry in Indonesia is predicted to experience 
significant growth by 2025. The increase in the health budget 
to Rp217.3 trillion or 6% of the total state budget in 2025 is 
a manifestation of the Government’s commitment to improve 
health services. These funds will be used to support President 
Prabowo Subianto’s flagship program in creating healthy and 
productive human resources.
The government is focusing on building a strong and responsive 
health system through the “Indonesia Sehat” (Healthy Indonesia) 
program, which is one of the social transformation targets 
towards “Indonesia Emas 2025 (Golden Indonesia 2045). The 
main strategies include strengthening medical personnel, 
developing health services to villages, improving nutrition, 
educating specialist doctors, and controlling the consumption of 
hazardous products.
The healthcare industry in Indonesia is also undergoing significant 
development, with increased adoption of technologies such as 
telemedicine, big data, and artificial intelligence for diagnosis 
and treatment. Indonesia’s National Social Security System (BPJS 
Kesehatan) also plays an important role in expanding access to 
healthcare services.
The Company is strategically positioned to capitalize on this 
opportunity by expanding its inpatient room capacity through 
the addition of new buildings. The Company also plans to 
introduce comprehensive preventive service programs and 
invest in state-of-the-art medical equipment and digital health 
solutions to improve operational efficiency and deliver superior 
healthcare services.

Financial Statements.
