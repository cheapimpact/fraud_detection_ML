Review of Operations by Business Segment
Among the two business segments operated by the 
Company, the cable business segment again accounted 
for the largest share of total sales in 2004 at 96.41%. By 
comparison, insulation contributed only 3.59% of total 
sales during the year.
The Company's 2024 sales in the cable and insulation 
business segments came to Rp 6.7 trillion and Rp 248.9 
billion respectively, with gross profit at Rp 535.7 billion 
and Rp 60.4 billion. In 2023 by comparison, sales of cable 
and insulation business segments were recorded at Rp 5.6 
trillion and Rp 224.9 billion respectively, with gross profit at 
Rp 419.9 billion and Rp 60.2 billion.
In 2024, the Company's sales in the cable business segment

Rp 6.7 trillion in 2024, representing an increase from 
Rp 5.6 trillion in 2023.
Copper consumption rose from 20,926 metric tons in 
2023 to 21,930 metric tons in 2024, an increase of 4.80%. 
Meanwhile, aluminum consumption grew 2.16% from 4,676 
metric tons in 2023 to 4,777 metric tons in 2024.
Copper prices in 2024 averaged USD 9,298 per metric ton, 
up 9.61% from the 2023 average of USD 8,483 per metric 
ton. Aluminum averaged USD 2,725 per metric ton in 2024, 
21.00% higher in comparison to USD 2,252 per metric ton 
in 2023.
Within the cable business segment, 89% of total cable 
sales in 2024 comprised electrical cable. By comparison, 
telecommunications cable and enameled wire contributed 
only 2% and 9% of total cable sales that year. Analyzed by 
market, the private sector accounted for 81% of cable sales, 
followed by the state electricity company (PLN) with 12% 
and projects with 7%.

Financial Performance Analysis
Consolidated Statement of Financial Position
Total assets at the end of 2024 came to Rp 5.7 trillion, up 
6.99% from the total assets booked by the Company in 
2023 due to expansion in current assets and non-current 
assets that reached Rp 137.9 billion and Rp 234.4 billion, 
respectively. This change in total assets had a positive 
impact on the Company’s statement of financial position.
Current assets mounted by 6.63% to Rp 2.2 trillion at the end 
of 2024, primarily due to increased accounts receivables. 
Accounts receivables expanded from Rp 477.4 billion to 
Rp 609.1 billion. This increase in accounts receivables 
weakened the Company’s receivables collectibility level.
Non-current assets widened by 7.21% to Rp 3.5 trillion at 
the end of 2024, primarily in response to fixed assets and 
investment in an associate that grew by Rp 202.8 billion and 
Rp 37.6 billion, respectively. The increase in fixed assets and 
investment in an associate had a quite significant impact on 
the Company’s total assets.
Total liabilities were down by Rp 60.9 billion or 15.50% 
compared to the previous year. Current liabilities decreased 
by Rp 64.1 billion, while non-current liabilities increased by 
Rp 3.2 billion. This change in total liabilities had a positive 
impact on the Company’s statement of financial position.
Current liabilities at the end of 2024 were recorded at 
Rp 286.0 billion. This represented a decrease of 18.30% or 
Rp 64.1 billion compared to the Company’s current liabilities 
in 2023. This change was primarily due to a decrease of 
Rp 96.1 billion in trade payables. The decrease in current 
liabilities contributed to the increase in the Company's 
current ratio.
