GLOBAL ECONOMIC OVERVIEW
The World Economic Outlook (WEO) report released by 
the International Monetary Fund (IMF) projects global 
economic growth of 3.2% in 2024, reflecting a moderate 
yet stable expansion rate. Nevertheless, various 
challenges continue to cloud the global economic 
outlook, 
including 
sluggish 
global 
trade, 
supply 
chain disruptions, escalating geopolitical tensions—
particularly in Ukraine—and policy uncertainties arising 
from elections in multiple countries.
On the inflation front, the IMF forecasts a continued 
downward trend, with global inflation expected to decline 
to 5.7% in 2024, followed by further reductions to 4.2% in 
2025 and 3.5% in 2026. In advanced economies, inflation 
is easing at a faster pace, dropping from 4.6% in 2023 
to 2.6% in 2024, marking a positive step towards global 
price stability.
Despite signs of economic stabilization, several risks 
remain, including disinflation in advanced economies, 
persistent geopolitical tensions, and ongoing economic 
and trade fragmentation. To maintain macroeconomic 
stability and foster sustainable growth, strategic 
measures such as consistent inflation control, fiscal 
consolidation to ensure financial sustainability, and 
effective coordination between monetary and fiscal 
policies are essential. Additionally, transparent policy 
communication will be a key factor in maintaining 
financial 
sector 
stability 
and 
bolstering 
market 
confidence.
Laporan World Economic Outlook (WEO) yang dirilis oleh 
International Monetary Fund (IMF) memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global sebesar 3,2% pada tahun 
2024, mencerminkan laju ekspansi yang moderat 
namun stabil. Meskipun demikian, berbagai tantangan 
masih membayangi prospek ekonomi dunia, termasuk 
melemahnya perdagangan global, disrupsi rantai pasok, 
meningkatnya eskalasi konflik geopolitik, terutama di 
Ukraina, serta dinamika kebijakan internasional yang 
disebabkan oleh pemilihan umum di berbagai negara.
Dari sisi inflasi, IMF memperkirakan tren penurunan 
berkelanjutan, dengan inflasi global diproyeksikan turun 
menjadi 5,7% pada tahun 2024, kemudian melanjutkan 
tren penurunan menjadi 4,2% pada 2025 dan 3,5% pada 
2026. Di negara-negara maju, inflasi menurun lebih cepat, 
dari 4,6% pada 2023 menjadi 2,6% pada 2024, menandai 
langkah positif menuju stabilitas harga secara global.
Meskipun ekonomi global menunjukkan tanda-tanda 
stabilisasi, berbagai tantangan masih perlu diantisipasi, 
termasuk disinflasi di negara-negara maju, berlanjutnya 
ketegangan geopolitik, serta fragmentasi ekonomi dan 
perdagangan internasional. Untuk menjaga stabilitas 
makroekonomi dan mendorong pertumbuhan yang 
berkelanjutan, diperlukan langkah-langkah strategis 
seperti pengendalian inflasi yang konsisten, konsolidasi 
fiskal guna menjaga keberlanjutan keuangan negara, 
serta koordinasi kebijakan moneter dan fiskal agar 
respons terhadap dinamika ekonomi dapat lebih efektif. 
Selain itu, komunikasi kebijakan yang transparan juga 
menjadi faktor kunci dalam menjaga stabilitas sektor 
keuangan dan meningkatkan kepercayaan pasar.

Management Discussion and Analysis
Pada tahun 2024, perekonomian nasional tetap 
menunjukkan ketahanan meskipun masih dihadapkan 
pada berbagai ketidakpastian global dan domestik. 
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia mencapai 5,03%, 
sedikit melambat dibandingkan tahun 2023 yang 
mencatat pertumbuhan sebesar 5,05%. Namun, seluruh 
sektor utama penyumbang Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) 
tetap mencatatkan pertumbuhan positif, dengan sektor 
industri pengolahan, perdagangan, pertanian, konstruksi, 
dan pertambangan menjadi motor penggerak utama 
ekonomi nasional. Kinerja ekspor tumbuh sebesar 6,51% 
dengan kontribusi sebesar 22,18% terhadap PDB, namun 
laju impor yang lebih tinggi, yakni 7,95%, mencerminkan 
peningkatan permintaan domestik yang tetap kuat dan 
berkontribusi 20,95% terhadap pertumbuhan ekonomi. 
Stabilitas ekonomi nasional juga didukung oleh inflasi 
yang tetap terkendali. Pada Desember 2024, inflasi 
tercatat sebesar 1,57% (yoy), masih berada dalam kisaran 
target 2,5±1% yang ditetapkan pemerintah. Keberhasilan 
ini tidak terlepas dari peran Anggaran Pendapatan 
dan Belanja Negara (APBN) yang terus dioptimalkan 
untuk menjaga daya beli masyarakat, terutama melalui 
berbagai program perlindungan sosial.
Di sektor industri, khususnya industri makanan dan 
minuman (mamin), kinerja tetap positif. Data dari Badan 
Pusat Statistik (BPS) mencatat pertumbuhan industri ini 
mencapai 5,90% pada kuartal keempat 2024, didorong 
oleh peningkatan permintaan baik dari pasar domestik 
maupun ekspor. 
Namun, tantangan masih membayangi industri makanan 
dan minuman sepanjang 2024. Salah satu hambatan yang 
masih dihadapi oleh banyak pelaku usaha adalah terkait 
perizinan Surat Perizinan Air Tanah (SIPA), yang masih 
memerlukan penyelesaian regulasi lebih lanjut. Selain 
itu, penerapan neraca komoditas yang masih menunggu 
kesiapan dari Kementerian Koordinator Bidang Pangan 
juga menjadi perhatian dalam distribusi bahan baku 
industri. Untuk mendukung pertumbuhan industri ini, 
diperlukan peran aktif pemerintah dalam menyediakan 
stimulus atau program khusus guna meningkatkan daya 
beli masyarakat, terutama di segmen kelas menengah ke 
bawah.

OVERVIEW OF THE INDUSTRY AND INDONESIAN ECONOMY
In 2024, the Indonesian economy continues to 
demonstrate resilience despite ongoing global and 
domestic 
uncertainties. 
Indonesia’s 
GDP 
growth 
stood at 5.03%, slightly lower than the 5.05% growth 
recorded in 2023. However, all major economic sectors 
contributing to Gross Domestic Product (GDP) recorded 
positive growth, with manufacturing, trade, agriculture, 
construction, and mining serving as the primary drivers 
of economic expansion. Exports grew by 6.51%, 
contributing 22.18% to GDP, while imports increased at a 
faster rate of 7.95%, reflecting strong domestic demand 
and contributing 20.95% to economic growth. National 
economic stability has also been supported by controlled 
inflation. As of December 2024, inflation stood at 1.57% 
year-on-year (yoy), remaining within the government’s 
target range of 2.5% ± 1%. This achievement is largely 
attributed to the effective implementation of the State 
Budget (APBN), which has been optimized to sustain 
household purchasing power, particularly through social 
protection programs.
In the food and beverage industry, performance has 
remained robust. Data from Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS-
Statistics Indonesia) indicate that the industry grew by 
5.90% in Q4 2024, driven by increasing domestic and 
export demand. 
However, challenges persist in the food and beverage 
sector throughout 2024. A key issue faced by many 
industry players is the licensing process for Groundwater 
Usage Permits (SIPA), which still requires further 
regulatory refinement. Additionally, the implementation 
of the commodity balance policy remains pending, 
awaiting readiness from the Coordinating Ministry for 
Food Affairs, affecting the distribution of raw materials. 
To sustain the sector’s growth, proactive government 
involvement is essential in providing special programs or 
stimulus packages to enhance the purchasing power of 
lower-middle-income groups.

ASSETS
As of 31 December 2024, the Company recorded total 
assets of Rp2.66 trillion, reflecting an increase of 15.99% 
compared to Rp2.30 trillion in 2023. This growth was 
primarily driven by a 14.22% decrease in current assets 
and a 25.12% increase in non-current assets.
Current Assets
In 2024, the Company’s current assets amounted to 
Rp457 billion, representing a decrease of 14.22% from 
Rp533 billion in 2023.
Non-Current Assets
In 2024, the Company’s non-current assets stood at 
Rp2.20 trillion, an increase of 25.12% compared to 
Rp1.76 trillion in 2023.
LIABILITIES
As of 31 December 2024, the Company reported total 
liabilities of Rp733 billion, indicating a decrease of 6.14% 
from Rp781 billion in 2023. This was primarily due to a 
14.05% decrease in short-term liabilities, while long-term 
liabilities increased by 4.13%.
Short-Term Liabilities
In 2024, the Company’s short-term liabilities amounted 
to Rp379 billion, down 14.04% from Rp441 billion in 
2023. This was attributable to a decrease in short-term 
bank loans by Rp72 billion, a decrease in trade payables 
by Rp14 billion, and a decrease in other payables by Rp1 
billion. Meanwhile, tax payables increased by Rp3 billion.
Long-Term Liabilities
In 2024, the Company’s long-term liabilities totaled 
Rp353 billion, reflecting an increase of 4.13% compared 
to Rp339 billion in 2023. This was primarily due to an 
increase in customer security deposits by Rp11 billion, 
an increase in estimated employee benefit liabilities by 
Rp1 billion, and a rise in deferred tax liabilities by Rp12 
billion.
EQUITY
As of 31 December 2024, the Company’s equity reached 
Rp1.93 trillion, representing an increase of 27.41% 
compared to Rp1.51 trillion in 2023. This was primarily 
due to the increase in profit for the year.

STRUKTUR MODAL & KEBIJAKAN MANAJEMEN 
ATAS STRUKTUR MODAL
CAPITAL STRUCTURE & MANAGEMENT POLICY ON CAPITAL STRUCTURE
Struktur modal merupakan penggabungan antara modal 
sendiri (ekuitas) dan hutang (liabilitas). Perseroan 
memaksimalkan struktur modal dengan mengoptimalkan 
modal rata-rata tertimbang yang minimum. Struktur 
modal dengan biaya penggunaan dana yang minim 
dapat berpengaruh terhadap peningkatan nilai saham 
Perseroan, tetapi tidak meningkatkan laba bersih per 
saham. 
KEBIJAKAN MANAJEMEN ATAS 
STRUKTUR MODAL
Kebijakan struktur modal Perseroan ditujukan untuk 
melindungi kemampuan entitas dalam mempertahankan 
kelangsungan usaha, sehingga entitas dapat tetap 
memberikan hasil bagi pemegang saham dan seluruh 
pemangku kepentingan lainnya. Kebijakan struktur 
modal juga ditujukan kepada pemegang saham dengan 
menentukan harga produk dan jasa yang sepadan dengan 
tingkat risiko. Sebagaimana praktik yang berlaku secara 
umum, Perseroan mengevaluasi struktur permodalan 
melalui rasio liabilitas terhadap modal (gearing ratio) 
yang dihitung melalui pembagian antara liabilitas bersih 
dengan modal. Liabilitas bersih adalah jumlah liabilitas 
dikurangi dengan jumlah kas dan setara kas. Sedangkan 
modal meliputi seluruh komponen ekuitas dalam laporan 
posisi keuangan. 
Pada tahun 2024, kebijakan manajemen atas struktur 
modal yakni dengan rasio liabilitas terhadap modal 
sebesar 0,35 lebih kecil dibandingkan dengan tahun 
2023 sebesar 0,36. Rasio liabilitas terhadap modal 
tersebut masih jauh lebih rendah di bawah standar rasio 
liabilitas terhadap modal yang besarnya 2 kali. 
 
Capital structure is a combination between equity and 
liabilities. The Company maximizes capital structure 
by optimizing the minimum average weight capital. 
The capital structure with minimal cost of funds will 
influence the Company’s shares price increment without 
increasing basic earnings per share. 
MANAGEMENT POLICY ON 
CAPITAL STRUCTURE
The Company’s capital structure policy aims to protect 
the entity’s capability to maintain business sustainability, 
therefore, so that the entity will still provide return for 
the Shareholders and other Stakeholders, the capital 
structure policy has prevailed to the shareholders by 
determining price of the products and services in line with 
risk level. Similarly, with generally applied practice, the 
Company evaluates the capital structure through debt- 
to-equity ratio (gearing ratio) calculated by dividing net 
debt to equity ratio. Net debt refers to total consolidated 
liabilities deducted by total cash and cash equivalents. 
However, equity includes entire equity component in 
consolidated financial statements position. 
In 2024, the Company’s capital structure policy resulted 
in a debt-to-equity ratio of 0.35, which was lower than the 
0.36 recorded in 2023. This debt-to-equity ratio remains 
significantly below the standard benchmark, which is set 
at 2 times.

10% dalam industri ini, didorong oleh peningkatan

pemerintah pada Januari 2025. Selain itu, meningkatnya 
kesadaran konsumen terhadap pentingnya konsumsi

permintaan produk AMDK. Namun, industri ini juga 
menghadapi tantangan, termasuk perubahan regulasi

konsumen.

CHANGE IN REGULATION WITH IMPACT ON THE COMPANY
Following the issuance of the Minister of Home Affairs 
Instruction (Inmendagri) Number 53 of 2022 dated 
December 30, 2022, the Government of the Republic 
of Indonesia officially revoked the policy on the 
Enforcement of Restrictions on Community Activities 
(PPKM). The revocation of this policy has affected the 
consumption pattern of Bottled Drinking Water (AMDK), 
as reflected in the increased demand for AMDK products 
as a consequence of the termination of the PPKM status.
Meanwhile, with regard to licensing matters in general, 
there have been no regulatory changes that have had an 
impact on the Company’s operations. The Company has 
obtained a domestic investment principal license in the 
business sectors of the drinking water and mineral water 
industry, as well as the soft drink industry, which was 
issued by the Investment Coordinating Board. In addition, 
the Company has complied with the requirements for 
preparing an Environmental Management Effort and 
Environmental Monitoring Effort (UKL-UPL) report, 
which includes, among others, conducting laboratory 
testing of wastewater produced by the Company’s plant, 
preserving water resources and the natural environment 
surrounding the plant location, and maintaining as well 
as operating infiltration wells. Furthermore, the Company 
has also obtained a Groundwater Extraction and/or 
Utilization Permit (SIPA) issued by the provincial Office 
of Energy and Mineral Resources.
Sejak diterbitkannya Instruksi Menteri Dalam Negeri 
(Inmendagri) Nomor 53 Tahun 2022 pada tanggal 30 
Desember 2022, Pemerintah Republik Indonesia secara 
resmi menghapus kebijakan Pemberlakuan Pembatasan 
Kegiatan Masyarakat (PPKM). Penghapusan kebijakan ini 
memberikan dampak terhadap pola konsumsi Air Minum 
Dalam Kemasan (AMDK), ditandai dengan meningkatnya 
permintaan produk AMDK sebagai konsekuensi dari 
berakhirnya masa PPKM.
Sementara itu, dalam hal perizinan secara umum, tidak 
terdapat perubahan regulasi yang memberikan pengaruh

mengantongi izin prinsip penanaman modal dalam 
negeri di sektor industri air minum dan air mineral, serta 
industri minuman ringan, yang dikeluarkan oleh Badan 
Koordinasi Penanaman Modal. Di samping itu, Perseroan 
juga telah memenuhi persyaratan terkait penyusunan 
dokumen Upaya Pengelolaan Lingkungan dan Upaya 
Pemantauan Lingkungan (UKL-UPL), antara lain dengan 
melakukan uji laboratorium terhadap limbah cair yang 
dihasilkan oleh pabrik, menjaga kelestarian sumber daya 
air dan lingkungan di sekitar area operasional, serta 
melakukan pemeliharaan dan pengoperasian sumur 
resapan. Selain itu, Perseroan juga telah memperoleh 
Surat Izin Pengambilan dan/atau Penggunaan Air Tanah 
(SIPA) yang diterbitkan oleh Dinas Energi dan Sumber 
Daya Mineral di tingkat provinsi.

rights existing at the end of the reporting period; 
it states that such classification is not affected by 
expectations about whether the entity will exercise 
its right to defer settlement of a liability. It further 
clarifies that such rights exist if covenants are 
complied with at the end of the reporting period 
and introduces a definition of ‘settlement’ to clarify 
that settlement refers to the transfer of cash, equity 
instruments, other assets, or services to another 
party.

2.	 PSAK 201 (Amendment), “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Non-current Liabilities with Covenants 
The amendment provides that only covenants with 
which an entity must comply on or before the end 
of the reporting period shall affect the entity’s right 
to defer settlement of a liability for at least twelve 
months after the reporting date (and therefore must 
be considered in assessing the classification of the 
liability as current or non-current). Such covenants 
determine whether the right exists at the end of the 
reporting period, even if compliance is assessed 
only after the reporting date (for example, covenants 
based on the entity’s financial position at the 
reporting date but assessed for compliance only 
after that date).
The Indonesian Financial Accounting Standards 
Board (DSAK-IAI) further states that the right to defer 
settlement of a liability for at least twelve months 
after the reporting period is not affected if the entity 
is required to comply with the covenant only after 
the reporting period. However, if the entity’s right to 
defer settlement depends on its compliance with the 
covenant within twelve months after the reporting 
period, the entity shall disclose information enabling 
users of financial statements to understand the 
risk that the liability could become repayable 
within twelve months after the reporting date. This 
includes information about the covenant (including 
the nature of the covenant and when compliance is 
required), the carrying amount of the related liability, 
and facts and circumstances, if any, that indicate the 
entity may encounter difficulty in complying with the 
covenant.

3.	 PSAK 207 (Amendment), “Statement of Cash Flows”

The amendments introduce a disclosure objective in 
PSAK 207 requiring entities to disclose information 
about their supplier finance arrangements to 
enable users of financial statements to evaluate 
the effects of such arrangements on the entity’s 
liabilities and cash flows. In addition, PSAK 107

The amendments include specific transitional 
provisions for the first annual reporting period in 
which the Group applies the amendments. Under 
these transitional provisions, an entity is not required 
to disclose:
a.	
Comparative information for any reporting 
periods presented before the start of the annual 
reporting period in which the entity first applies 
the amendments;
b.	
Information required by PSAK 207:44H(b)(ii)–
(iii) at the beginning of the annual reporting 
period in which the entity first applies the 
amendments.
4.	 PSAK 116 (Amendment), “Leases”: Lease Liabilities 
in a Sale and Leaseback
The amendment to PSAK 116 introduces subsequent 
measurement requirements for sale and leaseback 
transactions that qualify as sales under PSAK 
115, Revenue from Contracts with Customers. The 
amendment requires seller-lessees to determine the 
‘lease payments’ or ‘revised lease payments’ such 
that no gain or loss is recognized in respect of the 
retained right-of-use asset after the commencement 
date.
