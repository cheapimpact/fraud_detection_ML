Throughout 2023, the global economy continued to face 
uncertainties amidst post-pandemic recovery efforts. Prolonged 
geopolitical tensions impacted inflation rates, which soared to 
6.8% for the year, resulting in slowed economic growth across 
various countries. According to the International Monetary 
Fund (IMF) in its World Economic Outlook report for January 
2024, global economic growth is projected to reach only 3.1% 
in 2023, contracting from the 3.5% growth observed in 2022.
Despite these challenges, Indonesia’s economic growth 
demonstrated resilience, maintaining a robust level at 5.05%. 
Although it represents a slowdown from the previous year,

rupiah exchange rate managed by Bank Indonesia have 
successfully kept inflation at a moderate level of 2.6% down 
from 5.5% in 2022, marking the lowest inflation rate since 2000 
and serving as a cornerstone for national economic stability.

national economic growth was driven by four main industries, 
namely manufacturing, trade, transportation and warehousing, 
and information and communication. Throughout 2023, the 
transportation and warehousing industry emerged as a key 
driver of national economic growth, contributing up to 14.0%. 
This growth was fueled by a positive economic climate coupled 
with a 13.7% increase in Foreign Direct Investment (FDI), 
totaling USD50.267 million. These conditions also acted as a 
positive catalyst for the company as a pioneer in the modern 
warehousing industry in Indonesia, enabling continued growth 
in 2023, evidenced by a revenue increase of 4.1% and an 
EBITDA growth of 10.9%.

Pembagian dividen tunai diatur dalam Keputusan Rapat Umum

dalam Undang-Undang No. 40 Tahun 2007 tentang Perseroan

keuangan sepanjang hal tersebut diperbolehkan oleh Anggaran 
Dasar Perseroan dan tidak menyebabkan aset bersih menjadi 
kurang dari modal ditempatkan dan disetor penuh maupun 
Cadangan wajib Perseroan. Pembagian dividen dilakukan 
setelah ditetapkan oleh Direksi dan mendapat persetujuan 
dari Dewan Komisaris.
Pada 
tahun 
2023, 
Perseroan 
tidak 
melakukan 
pembagian dividen.
The distribution of cash dividends is regulated within the 
Annual General Meeting of Shareholders (RUPS) as stipulated 
in Law No. 40 of 2007 regarding Limited Liability Companies. 
Dividends can be distributed before the end of the financial 
year as long as this is allowed by the Company’s Articles of 
Association and does not cause the net assets to fall below the 
fully placed and paid-up capital or the Company’s mandatory 
reserves. The distribution of dividends is carried out after being 
determined by the Board of Directors and approved by the 
Board of Commissioners.
In 2023, the Company did not distribute dividends.
