The rapidly changing global geopolitical dynamics, coupled with 
high levels of uncertainty, have impacted the global economic 
development throughout 2024. Global economic growth slowed 
to 3.2% in 2024, down from 3.3% in 2023. This was influenced by 
geopolitical tensions involving major countries, which led to global 
commodity price volatility and supply chain disruptions, affecting 
international trade balances and global investments. Additionally, 
economic policy uncertainties, including the leadership changes 
in several major countries, have influenced market sentiment and 
long-term investment decisions across various sectors.
Amid these conditions, Indonesia’s economic recovery continued, 
with a growth rate of 5.02% (year on year). Although this 
achievement was slightly lower than the 2023 growth rate of 
5.04%, Indonesia’s economic growth remained strong, supported 
by increased investments, steady household consumption, and 
higher Government spending. All business sectors demonstrated 
growth. The largest contributors to growth were value-added 
creation in the manufacturing sector, construction sector, as well 
as wholesale and retail trade, and motor vehicle and motorcycle 
repair. On the expenditure side, economic growth was mainly 
driven by household consumption expenditure, exports of goods 
and services, and investment in gross fixed capital formation.

Tinjauan Ekonomi
Economic Review
Dinamika geopolitik dunia yang cepat berubah, disertai 
dengan tingkat ketidakpastian yang tinggi telah memengaruhi 
perkembangan ekonomi global di sepanjang tahun 2024. 
Pertumbuhan ekonomi global tercatat mengalami perlambatan 
menjadi 3,2% pada tahun 2024 dari sebelumnya 3,3% pada 
tahun 2023. Hal tersebut dipengaruhi oleh ketegangan geopolitik 
yang melibatkan negara-negara besar sehingga menyebabkan 
volatilitas harga komoditas global dan gangguan rantai pasok, 
yang kemudian memengaruhi neraca perdagangan internasional 
dan investasi global. Selain itu, ketidakpastian kebijakan ekonomi, 
termasuk akibat pergantian kepemimpinan di sejumlah negara 
utama turut memengaruhi sentimen pasar dan keputusan 
investasi jangka panjang di berbagai sektor. 
Di tengah kondisi tersebut, proses pemulihan ekonomi Indonesia 
dapat terus berlanjut dengan pertumbuhan ekonomi sebesar 
5,02% (year on year). Meskipun capaian ini sedikit lebih rendah 
dari capaian pertumbuhan tahun 2023 sebesar 5,04%, namun 
pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia tetap baik terutama ditopang 
oleh kenaikan investasi dan terjaganya konsumsi rumah tangga, 
serta peningkatan belanja pemerintah. Seluruh sektor lapangan 
usaha mampu menunjukkan pertumbuhan. Penciptaan nilai 
tambah pada sektor industri pengolahan, sektor konstruksi, 
serta sektor perdagangan besar dan eceran, reparasi mobil dan 
sepeda motor menjadi kontributor terbesar dari pertumbuhan. 
Sedangkan, dari sisi pengeluaran, pertumbuhan ekonomi 
terutama ditopang oleh pengeluaran konsumsi rumah tangga, 
ekspor barang dan jasa, serta investasi pada pembentukan modal 
tetap bruto.

Segmen Kendaraan Bermotor dan Suku 
Cadang
Sepanjang tahun 2024, Perseroan mencatatkan penurunan 
pendapatan eksternal segmen kendaraan bermotor dan suku 
cadang sebesar 25,61%, menjadi Rp651,76 miliar di tahun 
2024 dari sebelumnya tercatat sebesar Rp876,19 miliar di tahun 
2023. Dari sisi beban penjualan, segmen kendaraan bermotor 
dan suku cadang mengalami penurunan sebesar 12,73%. Hal 
tersebut berdampak pada penurunan penghasilan segmen 
sebesar 76,41%, dari sebelumnya sebesar Rp31,91 miliar menjadi 
Rp7,53 miliar. Segmen kendaraan bermotor dan suku cadang 
menjadi kontributor terbesar yaitu 96,00% terhadap total 
pendapatan di tahun 2024.   
Segmen Sewa Operasi	
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan mencatatkan pendapatan eksternal 
segmen sewa operasi sebesar Rp27,14 miliar, jumlah tersebut 
meningkat sebesar 5,81% dari tahun sebelumnya yang tercatat 
sebesar Rp25,65 miliar. Sedangkan penghasilan segmen 
sewa operasi mengalami penurunan sebesar 40,03% menjadi 
Rp4,55 miliar dari sebelumnya sebesar Rp7,59 miliar. Segmen 
sewa operasi berkontribusi sebesar 4,00% terhadap total 
pendapatan di tahun 2024.
Segmen Lainnya
Pada akhir tahun 2024 dan 2023, segmen lainnya belum 
membukukan pendapatan eksternal, namun membukukan 
kerugian segmen pada tahun 2024 sebesar Rp28,20 miliar, 
tercatat meningkat sebesar 234,48% dari tahun sebelumnya 
sebesar Rp8,43 miliar.
Profitabilitas  
Secara keseluruhan, profitabilitas Perseroan di tahun 2024 
menunjukkan penurunan dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya, 
sebagaimana tercermin dalam penurunan  penghasilan segmen 
konsolidasi sebesar 158,01%, dari sebelumnya mencatatkan 
penghasilan segmen sebesar Rp20,03 miliar di tahun 2023 
menjadi rugi segmen sebesar Rp12,08 miliar di tahun 2024.
Aspek Pemasaran
Marketing Aspects
Dalam industri otomotif yang sangat kompetitif dan terus 
berkembang, aspek pemasaran memegang peranan yang sangat 
penting dalam menentukan keberhasilan suatu perusahaan. 
Aspek Pemasaran bukan hanya tentang menjual kendaraan, tetapi 
Motor Vehicles and Spare Parts Segment
Over the course of 2024, the Company recorded a 25.61% 
decrease in external revenue from the motor vehicles and 
spare parts segment, decreased to Rp651.76 billion from 
Rp876.19 billion in 2023. On the expense side, selling expenses 
for this segment decreased by 12.73%. This resulted in a 
76.41% decrease in segment revenue, from Rp31.91 billion to 
Rp7.53 billion. Despite the decline, this segment remained the 
largest contributor to total revenue, accounting for 96.00% of 
total revenue in 2024.
Operational Leasing Segment
The Company recorded external revenue from the operational 
leasing segment amounting to Rp27.14 billion in 2024, marking 
a 5.81% increase from Rp25.65 billion in the previous year. 
However, revenue from this segment decreased by 40.03%, 
decreased to Rp4.55 billion from Rp7.59 billion. This segment 
contributed 4.00% to total revenue in 2024.
Others Segment
As of the end of 2024 and 2023, the others segment had not 
recorded any external revenue. However, they recorded a segment 
loss amounting to Rp28.20 billion in 2024, a 234.48% increase 
from the Rp8.43 billion loss in the previous year.
Profitability
Overall, the Company’s profitability decreased in 2024 compared 
to the previous year, as reflected in a 158.01% decrease in 
consolidated segment revenue, from a positive Rp20.03 billion in 
2023 to a segment loss of Rp12.08 billion in 2024.

Rasio Likuiditas
Rasio likuiditas mengukur kemampuan Perseroan dalam memenuhi 
kewajiban jangka pendek. Perseroan menggunakan rasio likuiditas 
yang diukur menggunakan rasio lancar dengan melakukan 
perbandingan antara aset lancar dengan liabilitas jangka pendek. 
Per akhir tahun 2024, rasio lancar menurun menjadi 313,62% dari 
sebelumnya 406,98%. Kondisi ini menunjukkan bahwa Perseroan 
memiliki aset lancar yang memadai untuk melunasi kewajiban 
jangka pendek.
Rasio Solvabilitas
Rasio solvabilitas mengukur kemampuan Perseroan dalam 
memenuhi seluruh kewajibannya, baik jangka pendek maupun 
jangka panjang, yang akan jatuh tempo. Rasio solvabilitas 
Perseroan diketahui dengan menghitung rasio total liabilitas 
terhadap total aset dan rasio total liabilitas terhadap total ekuitas. 
Pada tahun 2024, rasio total liabilitas terhadap total aset dan rasio 
total liabilitas terhadap total ekuitas Perseroan tercatat masing-
masing sebesar 43,21% dan 76,08%, meningkat dibandingkan 
dengan tahun 2023 yang masing-masing tercatat 40,97% dan 
69,41%. Kondisi tersebut menunjukkan bahwa Perseroan memiliki 
cukup modal dan aset untuk membayar seluruh liabilitasnya.

piutang

untuk

The liquidity ratio measures the Company’s ability to meet its 
short-term obligations. The Company uses the current ratio, 
which compares current assets to current liabilities. As of the end 
of 2024, the current ratio decreased to 313.62% from 406.98% 
in the previous year. This condition indicates that the Company 
still has sufficient current assets to settle its short-term obligations.
Solvency Ratio
The solvency ratio measures the Company’s ability to meet all of 
its obligations, both short-term and long-term, upon maturity. The 
Company’s solvency ratio is calculated by comparing total liabilities 
to total assets, and total liabilities to total equity. In 2024, the debt 
to asset ratio and debt to  equity ratio were 43.21% and 76.08% 
respectively, increased from 40.97% and 69.41% in 2023. This 
indicates that the Company has sufficient capital and assets to 
cover its total liabilities.
Receivables Collectability Level
Receivables collectability reflects the Company’s ability to minimize 
the occurrence of bad debts. Therefore, the Company implements 
policies to control receivables collectability through:
1.	 Credit analysis;
2.	 Setting consumer credit limits;
3.	 Write-offs any uncollectible receivables from:
a.	 Debtors experiencing significant financial difficulties;
b.	 Debtors

The condition of the debtor in cases of uncollectible receivables 
can indicate impairment, leading to an allowance for impairment 
losses. This allowance is based on the amount that cannot be 
recovered, determined by past experience. In 2024, the Company 
recorded an average receivables collection period of 30 days for 
service and unit sales, and 30 days for body repair. This indicates 
that the Company maintains a relatively strong receivables 
collectability level.

sisi penawaran. Untuk itu, Bank Indonesia memperkuat bauran

tinggi, serta bersinergi erat dengan kebijakan stimulus fiskal 
Pemerintah. Dari sisi penawaran, kebijakan reformasi struktural 
perlu terus diperkuat untuk mendorong sektor ekonomi yang 
dapat menyerap tenaga kerja. Upaya tersebut didukung dengan 
optimalisasi stimulus kebijakan makroprudensial dan akselerasi

Business Outlook
The global economy condition is projected to grow within 
the range of 2.7-3.3% in 2025. Several risks and uncertainties 
continue to pose challenges that affect global economic dynamics, 
including uncertainty in economic and geopolitical policies, 
economic slowdowns in various countries, high inflation rates in 
several nations, global financial market pressures, climate change, 
and disruptions in supply chains. Nevertheless, national economic 
growth is projected to be within the range of 4.7-5.5%, and is 
expected to rise further in 2026 to between 4.8-5.6%. A number 
of efforts are being made to encourage economic growth, both 
from the demand and supply sides. In this regard, Bank Indonesia 
is strengthening its policy mix to encourage higher economic 
growth, while closely aligning with the Government’s fiscal 
stimulus policies. From the supply side, structural reform policies 
need to be continuously reinforced to boost economic sectors that 
can absorb labor. These efforts are supported by the optimization 
of macroprudential policy stimulus and the acceleration of 
payment transaction digitalization pursued by Bank Indonesia.

potential for growth in demand for components for new four-
wheel vehicles is relatively limited.
