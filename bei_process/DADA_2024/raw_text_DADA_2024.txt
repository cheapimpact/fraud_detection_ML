paska pandemi Covid-19 pada tahun 2020, perekonomian 
nasional​terus menunjukan relefansi dan beranjak pulih 
lebih cepat. Sementara itu, situasi ekonomi dunia ke 
depan mulai menunjukkan keterbukaan terhadap usaha.. 
 
Berikut ini beberapa elemen utama fundamental paska 
covid aktivitas ekonomi domestik tetap bergeliat meski 
kondisi global masih mengalami perkembangan yang 
pasang surut. Bauran berbagai kebijakan dan strategi 
konstruktif yang diambil Pemerintah, salah satunya

Hal tersebut tercermin dari pertumbuhan ekonomi 
Indonesia pada Triwulan IV-2024 yang tumbuh solid 
sebesar 5,03% (coc). pencapaian ini  didorong dg adanya  
peningkatan investasi dan manufaktur serta didukung 
oleh permintaan domestik yang terjaga serta permintaan 
global yang mulai pulih kembali  
 
Dari sisi demand, mayoritas komponen pengeluaran pada 
Triwulan IV-2024 tumbuh sangat kuat dan positif. 
Didukung windfall komoditas unggulan, ekspor mampu 
tumbuh double digit mencapai 2.29% yoy berdasarkan 
PDB 
Nasional 
masih 
cukup 
besar 
walau 
belum 
sepenuhnya pulih. Sementara itu, impor tumbuh 6,25% 
(yoy) dengan didorong oleh kenaikan impor barang 
modal dan bahan baku. Lebih lanjut, laju pertumbuhan 
konsumsi 
sebagai kontributor utama PDB tercatat 
sebesar 4,48% (yoy) seiring dengan pertumbuhan PMTB 
sebesar 3,33% (yoy) dan Konsumsi LNPRT sebesar 5,70% 
(yoy). 
 
Dari sisi supply, seluruh sektor lapangan usaha yang 
berperan signifikan yaitu transportasi dan pergudangan 
menunjukan pertumbuhan yang baik (9-10%) diquartal 
sebelumnya menyumbang sekitar 6% dari PDP yang 
mengalami pertumbuhan positif di Triwulan IV-2024. 
Sektor Transportasi dan Pergudangan menjadi sektor 
dengan pertumbuhan tertinggi yakni sebesar 5,02,% (yoy) 
dan 0.53% dalam periode 2024. 
 
Indikator sektor eksternal Indonesia juga menunjukkan 
kondisi yang relatif baik dan terkendali, tercermin dari 
surplus transaksi berjalan, cadangan devisa yang terus 
meningkat, ekspor impor yang masih positif meski 
melambat, yield obligasi Pemerintah yang melandai, nilai 
tukar rupiah dan IHSG yang menguat, dan rasio Utang 
Luar Negeri Indonesia terhadap PDB dalam level aman. 
 
Having recorded negative growth during the Covid-19 
pandemic in 2020, the national economy-continues to 
show resilience and is recovering more quickly. Meanwhile, 
the world economic situation is start- ing to show signs of 
improvement. The tightening of interest rate policies that 
have begun to be put on hold, various international 
institutions 
have corrected upwards their economic 
predictions for 2024 so that the probability of a recession 
continues to decrease. 
 
Supported by strong national economic fundamentals, 
domestic economic activity continues to thrive even 
though global conditions are still experiencing ups and 
downs. The mix of various constructive policies and strate- 
gies adopted by the Government, one of which is through 
the PC-PEN Program, has succeeded in being the key to 
success in driving the pace of the national economy. 
 
This is reﬂected in Indonesia’s economic growth in the   
fourth quarter of 2024 which grew solidly by 5.01% (yoy). In 
full year, Indonesia’s economic growth throughout  2022  
also recorded an impressive growth of 5.31% (ctc). This 
ﬁgure exceeded the target set by the Government, which 
was 5.2% (ctc), and again reached the 5% level as before 
the pandemic. 
 
From the demand side, the majority of expenditure 
components in Quarter IV-2022 grew strongly. Supported 
by a windfall of superior commodities, exports were able to 
grow double digits, reaching 14.93% (yoy). Meanwhile, 
imports grew 6.25% (yoy) driven by increased imports of 
capital goods and raw materials. Furthermore, the 
consumption growth rate as the main contributor to GDP 
was recorded at 4.48% (yoy) in line with PMTB growth of 
3.33% (yoy) and LNPRT consumption of 5.70% (yoy). 
 
From the supply side, all business sectors experienced 
positive growth in the fourth quarter of 2023. The 
Transportation and Warehousing Sector was the sector 
with the highest growth of 16.99% (yoy) followed by the 
Accommodation and Food and Drink Sector which grew 
by 13.81% (yoy) driven by increased community mobility and 
increased tourist visits by both foreign and domestic 
tourists. The Manufacturing Industry Sector, as the largest 
contributor to GDP, also recorded a positive growth of 
5.64% (yoy). 
 
Indonesia’s external sector indicators also show relatively 
good and  controlled  conditions, reﬂected in the current 
account surplus, increasing foreign exchange reserves, 
positive

government bond yields, strengthening rupiah and JCI 
exchange rates, and debt ratios. Indonesia’s overseas to
