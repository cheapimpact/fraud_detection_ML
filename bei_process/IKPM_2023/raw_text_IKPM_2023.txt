Tinjauan Ekonomi dan Industri
Economic and Industry Review
Bank 
Indonesia 
memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan 
ekonomi global berada di kisaran 3,0% di tahun 2023 
dan melambat di kisaran 2,8% di tahun 2024. Proyeksi 
tersebut dilatarbelakangi oleh perang di Eropa dan Timur 
Tengah yang terus berlanjut, berdampak pada turunnya 
kinerja ekspor, meningkatnya harga komoditas, kenaikan 
suku bunga, inflasi, dan sejumlah tantangan lainnya yang 
diperkirakan terus berlanjut pada periode berikutnya. 
Kondisi ekonomi global memberikan perlambatan pada 
sejumlah kinerja industri, namun dengan serangkaian 
kebijakan yang efektif, ekonomi nasional Indonesia 
berhasil tumbuh dengan baik hingga akhir tahun 2023. 
Berdasarkan data yang dirilis oleh Badan Pusat Statistik, 
ekonomi Indonesia di triwulan IV 2023 tumbuh sebesar 
5,05%. Pertumbuhan ini dilihat dari kontribusi produk 
domestik bruto (PDB), baik dari lapangan usaha maupun 
pengeluaran. 
Dari komponen lapangan usaha, pertumbuhan didominasi 
oleh transportasi dan pergudangan yang tumbuh 
sebesar 10,33% seiring dengan meningkatnya mobilitas 
masyarakat. Kemudian, pertumbuhan disusun oleh 
Bank Indonesia projected global economic growth to be in 
the range of 3.0% in 2023 and slowing to 2.8% in 2024. The 
projection was motivated by the continuing war in Europe 
and the Middle East, which had an impact on the decline 
in export performance, rising commodity prices, rising 
interest rates, inflation, and a number of other challenges 
that are expected to continue in the next period.
Global economic conditions slowed down the performance 
of a number of industries, but with a series of effective 
policies, Indonesia’s national economy managed to grow 
well until the end of 2023. Based on data released by the 
Central Bureau of Statistics, the Indonesian economy in 
the fourth quarter of 2023 grew by 5.05%. This growth was 
observed from the contribution of gross domestic product 
(GDP), both from business fields and expenditure.
From the business field component, growth was dominated 
by transportation and warehousing which grew by 10.33% 
in line with increased community mobility. Then, growth 
was compiled by other services at 10.15%; electricity

Operational Review
Perseroan menjalankan kegiatan usaha memproduksi 
dan memasarkan obat-obatan. Adapun produk yang 
dihasilkan oleh Perseroan terdiri farmasi dan non farmasi, 
serta jasa maklon.
Farmasi
Segmen farmasi yang terdiri dan obat-obat resep Over-
the-Counter (OTC) beroperasi sejak tahun 1978. Perseroan 
menjual berbagai jenis produk obat-obatan resep yang 
hanya dapat dibeli oleh konsumen dengan resep dokter, 
maupun obat-obatan OTC yang dapat dibeli bebas di 
apotik maupun toko obat. Merek produk yang terbesar 
adalah Lactulax, Phenytoin, Ikaphen, Alkohol IKA, dan 
Ikadryl. Perseroan akan memanfaatkan kemampuan 
riset dan pengembangannya (R&D), budaya inovasi, 
dan pengolahan produk sampai dengan pendistribusian 
produk untuk terus mengembangkan segmen farmasi 
yang tetap menjadi inti dari usaha Perseroan.
Non Farmasi
Segmen farmasi Perseroan terbagi atas 2 segmen fast 
moving consumer goods (FMCG), yaitu Baby Huki untuk 
produk-produk botol susu, dot, dan perlengkapan bayi, 
serta merek NR untuk produk-produk perawatan rambut. 
Baby Huki telah dioperasionalkan sejak tahun 1995. Pada 
segmen usaha ini, Perseroan juga menjual produk seperti 
cottonbuds, minyak telon, liquid cleanser, dan berbagai 
jenis perlengkapan bayi lainnya.
Segmen perawatan rambut dimulai sejak tahun 1984 
melalui kerja sama dengan perusahaan Jerman yang 
mempercayai Perseroan dengan transfer of technology 
dan formulasi produk-produknya. Sementara, produk-
produk perawatan rambut Perseroan terdiri dari produk 
profesional yang diformulasikan khusus untuk digunakan 
oleh hairdresser di salon, dan produk perawatan rambut 
yang dijual langsung ke konsumen.  Ke depannya, 
Perseroan berkomitmen untuk dapat mengembangkan 
pangsa pasar ekspor terkait produk perawatan rambutnya 
terutama untuk negara-negara yang menggunakan produk 
perawatan rambut halal.
The Company carries out business activities producing 
and marketing medicines. The products produced 
by the Company consist of pharmaceutical and 
non-pharmaceutical products, as well as contract 
manufacturing services.
Pharmaceutical
The pharmaceutical segment consisting of over-the-
counter (OTC) drugs has been operating since 1978. 
The Company markets various types of prescription 
drugs that can only be purchased by consumers with 
a doctor’s prescription, as well as OTC medicines that 
can be purchased freely at pharmacies and drug stores. 
The largest product brands include Lactulax, Phenytoin, 
Ikaphen, IKA Alcohol, and Ikadryl. The Company utilizes its 
research and development (R&D) capabilities, innovation 
culture, and product processing to product distribution to 
continue developing the pharmaceutical segment which 
remains the core of the Company’s business.
Non Pharmaceutical
The Company’s pharmaceutical segment is divided into 2 
fast moving consumer goods (FMCG) segments, namely 
Baby Huki for milk bottles, pacifiers, and baby equipment 
products, and NR brand for hair care products. Baby 
Huki has been operational since 1995. In this business 
segment, the Company also markets products such as 
cottonbuds, telon oil, liquid cleanser, and various other 
types of baby equipment.
The hair care segment started in 1984 through cooperation 
with a German company that trusted the Company with 
the transfer of technology and formulation of its products. 
Meanwhile, the Company’s hair care products consist 
of professional products specially formulated for use by 
hairdressers in salons, and hair care products marketed 
directly to consumers. Going forward, the Company is 
committed to developing export market share related to 
its hair care products, especially for countries that use 
halal hair care products.

Strategi Pemasaran
Perseroan memiliki profil pelanggan yang terdiri dari 
rumah sakit, apotek, dan toko obat; toko persediaan 
perlengkapan dan perawatan bayi; salon rambut, toko 
perlengkapan kecantikan, dan toko make-up; serta 
supermarket, minimarket, dan retail. Untuk melanggengkan 
hubungan dengan para konsumen, Perseroan melakukan 
strategi pemasaran yang atraktif dan berupaya untuk 
mengembangkan produk sesuai kebutuhan konsumen 
dan perkembangan masa kini melalui inovasi dan riset.
Penjualan Neto Berdasarkan Area Pemasaran
Pada tahun 2023, penjualan neto didominasi oleh ekspor 
ke berbagai negara dengan pendapatan sebesar Rp18,67 
miliar, meningkat sebesar 153,76% atau setara Rp11,31 
miliar, dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya yang tercatat 
sebesar Rp7,36 miliar.
To strengthen its marketing efforts, the Company conducts 
massive promotional activities through advertisements 
in online media, print media, and social media such as 
Instagram, Facebook, Tik Tok, and online marketplaces 
such as Shopee. The Company also developed a website 
and online shop to expand customer reach. 
Market Share
The Company markets and distributes its products in all 
cities in Indonesia, through 3 main distributors and sub-
distributors such as Jakarta, Tangerang, Bogor, Bandung, 
Cirebon, 
Purwokerto, 
Solo, 
Semarang, Yogyakarta, 
Surabaya, Malang, Bali, Palembang, Medan, Lampung, 
Pontianak, Makassar, Banjarmasin, Balikpapan, and 
Manado. In addition, the Company strives to expand its 
international market reach by exporting pharmaceutical 
products to the Philippines and FMCG products to 
Australia.
Marketing Strategy
The Company’s customers include hospitals, pharmacies, 
and drug stores; baby supplies and care stores; hair 
salons, beauty supply stores, and make-up stores; as 
well as supermarkets, minimarkets and retail stores. To 
maintain relationships with consumers, the Company 
carries out attractive marketing strategies and strives 
to develop products according to consumer needs and 
current developments through innovation and research.
Net Sales by Marketing Area
In 2023, net sales were dominated by exports to various 
countries with revenues of Rp18.67 billion, an increase of 
153.76% or equivalent to Rp11.31 billion, compared to the 
previous year which was recorded at Rp7.36 billion.

Business Strategy

therefore the Company implements the following business 
strategy. 
•	 Expand the Company’s product portfolio, especially 
those that have become top-of-mind brands for 
Indonesian consumers.
•	 Improve cost efficiency and production capacity.
•	 Expand distribution and sales networks in Indonesia.
•	 Expand sales opportunities to the global market for 
all of the Company’s business fields, especially to 
developing countries in Southeast Asia.
•	 Seek non-organic growth opportunities.

The Company implements an employee stock allocation 
(ESA) program or share ownership by employees in the 
amount stipulated in capital market regulations and in 
accordance with the Resolutions of the Company’s Board 
of Directors approved in the GMS. The type of shares to be 
allocated in the ESA Program is the Share Awards.
Based on the Deed of Shareholders Resolution No. 236 
dated June 21, 2023 and Board of Directors Decree No. 
No. 05/DIR/VII/2023 dated July 11, 2023 concerning the 
Employee Stock Allocation (ESA) Program, the Company 
allocates 100,000 shares or 0.03% of the shares offered 
in the initial public offering for the ESA Program, in which 
the program is not intended for the Company’s Board of 
Commissioners or Board of Directors.
The objectives of the ESA Program are as follows. 
1.	 Creating a sense of belonging of employees to the 
Company, so as to increase employee motivation and 
morale to achieve the Company’s business goals.
2.	 Becoming one of the rewards to employees who have 
been loyal to provide their energy and services to the 
Company.
3.	 Maintaining and obtaining a skilled and professional 
workforce in the Company.
The Company’s ESA program is applied to 25 employees 
who must meet the following requirements.
1.	 Employees participating in the ESA Program are 
all employees of the Company who are permanent 
employees.
2.	 Employees who are not in the status of administrative 
sanctions at the time of implementation of the ESA 
Program.
3.	 Other 
criteria 
determined 
by 
the 
Company’s 
management.

Saham Penghargaan
1.	 Sebanyak 0,03% jumlah saham yang dialokasikan 
untuk Program ESA adalah saham penghargaan, 
yaitu pemberian saham kepada pegawai sebagai 
penghargaan.
2.	 Saham Penghargaan diberikan oleh Perseroan secara 
cuma-cuma kepada seluruh pegawai peserta Program 
ESA yang memenuhi persyaratan atas nama masing-
masing peserta.
3.	 Saham Penghargaan memiliki lock-up period selama 2 
tahun sejak tanggal distribusi saham.
4.	 Peserta tidak dikenakan biaya atas kepemilikan Saham 
Penghargaan, kecuali pajak penghasilan yang wajib 
dibayar oleh partisipan ESA. Saham Penghargaan 
akan menjadi beban Perseroan, yaitu dibayar penuh 
oleh Perseroan sesuai harga penawaran untuk 
masing-masing saham Perseroan. Dana dalam rangka 
pemberian Saham Penghargaan dalam Program ESA 
berasal dari kas internal  Perseroan.

Saham Penghargaan harus melaksanakan ketentuan, 
yaitu mentaati ketentuan peraturan kepemilikan 
saham Program ESA yang ditetapkan Perseroan dan 
peraturan pasar modal Indonesia.

Shares Awards
1.	 As much as 0.03% of the total shares allocated to 
the ESA Program are share awards, which are shares 
granted to employees as rewards.
2.	 Share awards are granted by the Company free of 
charge to all employees participating in the ESA 
Program who meet the requirements on behalf of each 
participant.
3.	 Share awards have a lock-up period of 2 years from 
the date of distribution of shares.
4.	 Participants are not charged for ownership of share 
awards, except for income tax payable by ESA 
participants. The share awards will be at the Company’s 
expense, which is fully paid by the Company according 
to the offering price for each share of the Company. The 
funds for awarding shard awards in the ESA Program 
come from the Company’s internal cash.
5.	 Employees who receive the allocation of share awards 
must carry out the provisions, namely complying with 
the provisions of the ESA Program share ownership 
regulations set by the Company and Indonesian capital 
market regulations.
Share Distribution and ESA Implementation
1.	 The Company will make payment of the full amount of 
all Share Awards under the ESA Program at the same 
price as the offering price during the public offering 
period. Payment is made to a bank account appointed 
by the underwriter to receive payment for the share 
order in the framework of this public offering with the 
full amount.
	
In the event that an employee who has received the 
Share Awards resigns, the shares will remain the 
employee’s property. However, if the employee resigns 
before the lock-up period ends, the lock-up provisions 
on the employee’s shares will still apply. 
2.	 For the ESA Program, participants will be subject to 
income tax at the applicable rate and must be paid at 
the time the ESA Program participant receives shares. 
Such income tax will be charged to ESA participants.
