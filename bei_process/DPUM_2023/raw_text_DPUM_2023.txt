TINJAUAN MAKRO EKONOMI
Macro Economic Overview
Perekonomian Indonesia tahun 2023 tumbuh sekitar 5 
% karena hingga triwulan ketiga tahun 2023, ekonomi 
nasional secara kumulatif mampu tumbuh 5,05 %. 
Konsumsi rumah tangga tercatat tumbuh 4,9 % (year to 
date/ytd) dan investasi 4,2 % (ytd). Sedangkan ekspor 
tumbuh tipis 1,1 % (ytd) dan impor melemah -2,0 % (ytd) 
imbas pelemahan ekonomi global.
Inflasi Indonesia juga terkendali di level 2,61 % (year 
on year/yoy) per Desember 2023, jauh lebih rendah 
dibandingkan proyeksi 2023 yang sebesar 3,6 %. Inflasi 
volatile food yang menjadi kontributor utama inflasi 
seperti beras, cabai, dan bawang putih juga mulai 
menunjukkan tren menurun di Desember 2023.
Penguatan 
neraca 
perdagangan 
Januari 
hingga 
November 2023 yang mencapai US$33,63 miliar secara 
kumulatif banyak dipengaruhi oleh kinerja produksi. 
Kinerja produksi menunjukkan pertumbuhan yang cukup 
baik. Sektor transportasi, akomodasi makan minum, dan 
infokom menjadi sektor dengan pertumbuhan tertinggi. 
Sektor pertambangan juga mampu tumbuh 5,7 % di 
tengah moderasi harga komoditas global. 
Aktivitas produksi yang cukup kuat tercermin dari PMI 
Manufaktur Indonesia yang terus ekspansif mencapai 
52,2. Konsumsi listrik tumbuh tinggi 14 % untuk bisnis 
dan 6,7 % untuk industri. Dari sisi konsumsi, Indeks 
Keyakinan Konsumen masih terjaga cukup tinggi 
mencapai 123,6. Sementara, Indeks Penjualan Riil 
tumbuh positif mencapai 2,9 %.
The Indonesian economy in 2023 grew by approximately 
5% as, by the third quarter of the year, the national 
economy cumulatively expanded by 5.05%. Household 
consumption recorded growth of 4.9% (year to date/
ytd), and investment grew by 4.2% (ytd). Meanwhile, 
exports saw a modest growth of 1.1% (ytd), and imports 
weakened by -2.0% (ytd) due to the global economic 
slowdown.
Inflation in Indonesia was also well-controlled at the 
level of 2.61% (year on year/yoy) as of December 2023, 
significantly lower than the projected 2023 rate of 3.6%. 
Volatile food inflation, which includes major contributors 
like rice, chili, and garlic, began to show a declining trend 
in December 2023.
The strengthening of the trade balance from January to 
November 2023, reaching US$33.63 billion cumulatively, 
was largely influenced by production performance. 
Production performance indicated substantial growth, 
with the transportation, accommodation, food and 
beverage, and information and communication sectors 
being the highest contributors. The mining sector also 
managed to grow by 5.7% amid moderated global 
commodity prices.
The robust production activity is reflected in the 
Manufacturing Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) 
for Indonesia, which remained expansive at 52.2. 
Electricity consumption showed high growth rates of 
14% for businesses and 6.7% for industries. From the 
consumption perspective, the Consumer Confidence 
Index remained relatively high at 123.6. Meanwhile, the 
Real Sales Index grew positively by 2.9%.

TINJAUAN INDUSTRI
Industry Overview
Pada 2023, Nilai ekspor Indonesia mencapai US$258,8 
miliar. sektor perikanan Indonesia mencatat penurunan 
nilai ekspor dari US$6,2 miliar pada tahun 2022 menjadi 
US$5,6 miliar. Penurunan ini disebabkan oleh berbagai 
faktor, seperti fluktuasi harga komoditas global, 
permintaan pasar yang berubah, atau faktor-faktor 
eksternal lainnya.
Sementara nilai tukar nelayan dan pembudidayaan 
ikan mencapai 105,21 secara nasional, menunjukkan 
stabilitas dalam daya beli nelayan dan pelaku usaha 
perikanan. Meskipun begitu, tantangan tetap ada, dan 
perlu perhatian khusus untuk memastikan keberlanjutan 
dan kesejahteraan para pelaku usaha di sektor perikanan.
Dalam hal pertumbuhan Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) 
perikanan, angka sebesar 6,78% menggambarkan 
pertumbuhan yang positif di sektor ini. Meskipun 
kontribusinya terhadap PDB Nasional sebesar 2,7%, 
nilai ini menunjukkan bahwa sektor perikanan memiliki 
dampak ekonomi yang signifikan dan dapat menjadi 
motor penggerak pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional.
Dengan nilai PDB sebesar Rp214,18 triliun, sektor 
perikanan terus berperan penting dalam mendukung 
perekonomian nasional. Penting untuk dicatat bahwa 
meskipun terjadi penurunan nilai ekspor, pertumbuhan 
PDB yang positif menandakan adanya potensi untuk 
pemulihan dan pengembangan lebih lanjut di masa 
depan.
In 2023, Indonesia’s export value reached US$258.8 
billion. The fisheries sector in Indonesia recorded a 
decrease in export value from US$6.2 billion in 2022 
to US$5.6 billion. This decline is attributed to various 
factors such as fluctuations in global commodity prices, 
changing market demand, or other external factors.
While the exchange rate for fishermen and fish 
farmers reached 105.21 nationally, indicating stability 
in the purchasing power of fishermen and fisheries 
entrepreneurs. Nevertheless, challenges persist, and 
special attention is needed to ensure the sustainability 
and well-being of stakeholders in the fisheries sector.
In terms of the growth of the Fisheries Gross Domestic 
Product (GDP), the figure of 6.78% reflects positive 
growth in this sector. Although its contribution to the 
National GDP is 2.7%, this value indicates that the 
fisheries sector has a significant economic impact and 
can be a driving force for national economic growth.
With a GDP value of Rp214.18 trillion, the fisheries 
sector continues to play a crucial role in supporting the 
national economy. It is important to note that despite the 
decrease in export value, positive GDP growth indicates 
potential for recovery and further development in the 
future.

Penurunan ekuitas ini sebagai dampak dari penurunan 
laba bersih komprehensif selama tahun berjalan.
Asset
The total assets owned by the Company in 2023 
amounted Rp1,200 billion. 11.4% lower or Rp154 billion

increase or Rp8 billion from 2022 which was recorded at

trade receivables from third party.
Non-current assets in 2023 amounted to Rp832 billion 
16.3% lower or Rp162 billion from 2022 which was 
recorded at Rp995 billion. This decrease was mainly due 
to return of some of vessels owned by the Company.
Liabilities

billion, 0.1% higher Rp0.8 billion from 2022 which was

Changes in this structure of liabilities was mainly due to 
the reclassification of bank loans (Eximbank), as a result

Equity

to Rp430 billion, 25.3% lower or Rp146 billion from 2022

This decline in equity was a result of the decline in
