Kondisi Perekonomian
Tahun 2023 ditandai dengan kondisi perekonomian 
global yang masih diliputi ketidakpastian. Dinamika 
ekonomi

Kondisi ini beriringan dengan konflik geopolitik yang

global dan prospek pertumbuhan ekonomi secara 
keseluruhan. Berdasarkan data International Monetary

berada di angka 4,0%, atau mengalami peningkatan 
dari tahun sebelumnya yang sebesar 3,6%. 
Economic Condition
The year 2023 was marked by global economic 
conditions that were  still shrouded in uncertainty. 
Economic dynamics in developed countries, such 
as high-interest rates, increasing fiscal pressure, 
and the erosion of excess savings were factors that 
overshadowed the economic weakening. This condition 
was going hand in hand with the ongoing geopolitical 
conflict in Ukraine and Palestine, which had contributed 
to volatility in global trade and overall economic growth 
prospects. Based on International Monetary Fund (IMF) 
data published in the October 2023 Edition of the World 
Economic Outlook, global economic growth in 2023 
would only reach 3%, a decrease from the previous 
year’s 3.2%. For developed countries, economic growth 
was estimated to be at 1.5%, or corrected by 1.0% from 
2022. However, the economies of developing countries 
could maintain economic growth, namely at 4.0%, an 
increase from the previous year which was 3.6%.

The economic volatility of developed countries after 
COVID-19 had an impact that extended to the global 
scope. In 2023, the United States still faced inflation that 
exceeded the target, rising interest rates, increasing 
fiscal pressure, and geopolitical conditions in Ukraine 
and Palestine, which had an impact on food and 
energy supply disruptions that caused prices to soar 
even further. This condition had resulted in inflationary 
pressure in many developed countries, such as the 
United States and European countries.
In the midst of global inflation and volatile economic 
conditions, Indonesia’s economic growth remained 
strong. Bank Indonesia projected the economy would 
continue to grow in the range of 4.5-5.3%, with an 
inflation target of 3.0 ± 1% in 2023. In December 2023, 
Bank Indonesia also issued a decision to maintain BI 
7-Day (Reverse) The Repo Rate is 6%, the Deposit Facility 
interest rate is 5.25%, and the Lending Facility interest 
rate is 6.75%. This is in line with monetary policy which 
focused on pro-stability, namely to strengthen the 
stabilization of the Rupiah exchange rate, as well as 
as a pre-emptive and forward-looking step to ensure 
inflation remained under control within the target of 2.5

Bank Indonesia continued to optimize and strengthen 
a number of strategies and policies to maintain 
macroeconomic and financial system stability so 
that it could support sustainable economic growth. 
The Coordinating Ministry for Economic Affairs of the 
Republic of Indonesia stated that Bank Indonesia and 
the Government agreed on 5 (five) main strategies to 
suppress high inflation.
First, the Government strengthened policy coordination 
to maintain macroeconomic stability and encourage 
national economic growth. The second step was carried 
out by maintaining inflation in the Volatile Food (VF) 
component in the range of 3.0% - 5.0%. including through 
the National Movement to Control Food Inflation (GNPIP). 
The third step was to implement the food barn program 
and expand inter-regional cooperation to strengthen 
food security. Fourth, strengthening the availability of 
food data to support the formulation of inflation control 
policies, one of which is through the One Food Data 
portal. Fifth, strengthening communication synergies 
to support the management of people’s inflation 
expectations. This was crucial for anticipating inflation 
expectations at important celebratory moments, such 
as during Eid al-Fitr 2023.

menunjukkan laju Inflasi Indeks Harga Konsumen (IHK)

mencapai 5,51%.  Penurunan Inflasi IHK tersebut dipicu 
oleh tren melemahnya inflasi inti yang menjadi tolok ukur 
daya beli masyarakat. Adapun komponen inti tahunan 
mengalami inflasi sebesar 1,8% pada Desember 2023, 
melandai dari 3 (tiga) bulan sebelumnya yang sebesar 
2,0%, 1,91%, dan 1,87% secara berturut-turut. 
Sepanjang tahun 2023, inflasi IHK tertinggi terjadi 
di bulan Februari, dengan angka mencapai 5,47%. 
Lonjakan tersebut terutama dipicu oleh komoditas 
beras dengan tingginya curah hujan yang berimbas 
pada kualitas gabah yang buruk. Meski demikian, 
inflasi kembali melandai di bulan Maret dengan 
penurunan 0,5%, menjadi sebesar 4,97%. Adapun 
Inflasi IHK terendah terjadi di bulan September dengan 
persentase sebesar 2,28 %, menurun sebesar 3,67% dari 
tahun sebelumnya di bulan yang sama. Penurunan 
tersebut terutama didorong oleh inflasi tarif pulsa 
ponsel dan biaya kuliah akademi/perguruan tinggi.
As of December 31 2023, data from the Central Statistics 
Agency (BPS) shows that the Indonesian Consumer 
Price Index (CPI) inflation rate in 2023 was 2.61%, or a 
decrease compared to 2022 which reached 5.51%. The 
decline in CPI inflation was triggered by a weakening 
trend in core inflation, which was measured people’s 
purchasing 
power. 
The 
annual 
core 
component 
experienced inflation of 1.8% in December 2023, down 
from the previous 3 (three) months of 2.0%, 1.91% and 
1.87% respectively.
Throughout 2023, the highest CPI inflation occurred 
in February, with the figure reaching 5.47%. This surge 
was mainly triggered by the rice commodity with high 
rainfall which resulted in poor grain quality. However, 
inflation eased again in March with a decline of 0.5%, 
to 4.97%. The lowest CPI inflation occurred in September 
with a percentage of 2.28%, a decrease of 3.67% from 
the previous year in the same month. This decline was 
mainly driven by inflation in cellphone credit rates and 
academy/university tuition fees.

Industri Kelapa Sawit
Sektor industri kelapa sawit memegang peranan krusial 
bagi perekonomian Indonesia dari tahun ke tahun, 
sehingga perlu senantiasa dikembangkan potensinya 
secara masif. Kebutuhan akan minyak nabati dari hasil 
olahan kelapa sawit tidak terlepas dari kehidupan 
sehari-hari masyarakat. Di samping itu, industri tersebut 
turut berkontribusi terhadap penyerapan tenaga 
kerja, kesejahteraan masyarakat, aliran investasi, alih 
teknologi, hingga menjadi tumpuan bagi penerimaan 
pendapatan Pemerintah Daerah dan Pusat, hingga 
rutin menjadi penyumbang devisa terbesar. 
Indonesia’s inflation rate, which was on a downward 
trend and followed by resilient economic conditions 
in 2023, could not be separated from the numerous 
constructive policies adopted by the government in 
collaboration with Bank Indonesia, partners, and other 
relevant parties. The Republic of Indonesia’s Coordinating 
Ministry for Economic Affairs revealed that this decline 
was driven by strong synergy and coordination among 
various parties via the Central and Regional Inflation 
Control Teams (TPIP and TPID) in controlling price 
fluctuations amidst continued high volatility. This result 
also outperformed inflation in a lot of countries, which 
remained high or over the target.

Indonesia merupakan penghasil  kelapa sawit terbesar 
di dunia sejak tahun 2006. Data Gabungan Pengusaha 
Kelapa Sawit Indonesia (GAPKI) menyebutkan bahwa 
produksi minyak sawit mentah (CPO) Indonesia di 
tahun 2023 mencapai 50,07  juta ton, lebih tinggi 7,15% 
dari tahun sebelumnya yang berada di angka 46,73 ton. 
Kondisi ini didorong oleh relatif tingginya harga minyak 
sawit jelang akhir tahun 2021 dan 2022, perluasan area 
lahan yang telah menghasilkan, dan El Nino yang 
sebelumnya diproyeksikan melanda Indonesia tidak 
berimbas signifikan pada produksi tanaman kelapa 
sawit. Seiring peningkatan produksi, konsumsi minyak 
sawit mentah dalam negeri mengalami kenaikan 
sebesar 10,12%, yakni dari angka 21,14 juta ton pada 2022 
menjadi Rp23,28 juta ton pada tahun 2023. 
Sementara prediksi Gabungan Pengusaha Kelapa 
Sawit Indonesia (GAPKI) menyebutkan ekspor CPO 
Indonesia hanya mencapai 32,90 juta ton, mengalami 
penurunan dibandingkan tahun 2022 yang sebesar 
33,90 juta ton. Penurunan produksi CPO disebabkan 
oleh turunnya harga minyak nabati global di tengah 
penurunan permintaan dan perlambatan ekonomi 
secara global. Faktor tersebut turut berimbas pada 
nilai ekspor CPO beserta turunannya yang hanya 
sebesar US$23,54 miliar per Oktober 2023 dan hingga 
akhir 2023 diproyeksikan lebih rendah dibanding tahun 
2022 sebesar US$ 39,28 miliar. 
Indonesia has been the world’s largest producer of 
palm oil since 2006. Data from the Indonesian Palm 
Oil Entrepreneurs Association (GAPKI) indicates that 
Indonesia’s crude palm oil (CPO) production in 2023 
reached 50.07 million tons, an increase of 7.15% from 
the previous year’s 46.73 tons. This condition was driven 
by the relatively high price of palm oil towards the end 
of 2021 and 2022, expansion of land areas that have 
yielded, and the previously projected El Niño, which 
did not significantly impact the production of palm oil 
trees in Indonesia. Along with the increase in production, 
domestic consumption of crude palm oil experienced a 
10.12% increase, from 21.14 million tons in 2022 to 23.28 
million tons in 2023.
Meanwhile, predictions from the Indonesian Palm 
Oil Entrepreneurs Association (GAPKI) suggest that 
Indonesia’s CPO exports only reached 32.90 million tons, 
experiencing a decrease compared to 2022 which was 
33.90 million tons. The decline in CPO production was 
attributed to the decrease in global vegetable oil prices 
amidst a slowdown in global demand and economic 
activity. These factors also impacted the value of CPO 
exports and its derivatives, which amounted to only 
US$23.54 billion as of October 2023 and were projected 
to be lower by the end of 2023 compared to 2022, which 
was US$39.28 billion.

Sawit merupakan tanaman tahunan yang memiliki 
tahapan produksi terdiri dari tahap tanaman belum 
menghasilkan (TBM) dan tanaman menghasilkan (TM). 
Tanaman sawit mulai menghasilkan buah yang sesuai 
untuk pada umur 4 tahun dan akan terus berproduksi 
secara optimum hingga 30 tahun. Produktivitas kelapa 
sawit akan mengalami peningkatan dan mencapai 
puncak produksi di antara umur 7 (tujuh) sampai 18 
(delapan belas) tahun.  Adapun penurunan produksi 
akan terjadi setelah tanaman melewati usia produktif, 
yaitu di atas 30 tahun sehingga harus dilakukan 
peremajaan melalui penebangan pohon sawit tua 
untuk diganti dengan tanaman sawit baru. Oleh karena 
itu, produktivitas dan kinerja Perusahaan dipengaruhi 
oleh umur tanaman kelapa sawit.  
 FAP Agri Group’s operational activities include managing 
oil palm plantations, palm oil processing factories, 
and palm kernel processing factories. The products 
produced consist of Fresh Fruit Bunches, Crude Palm Oil, 
Palm Kernel Oil, Palm Kernel, and Palm Kernel meat.
Palm oil is an annual plant that has production stages 
consisting of immature plant (TBM) and mature plant 
(TM) stages. Oil palm plants begin to produce suitable 
fruit at the age of 4 years and will continue to produce 
optimally for up to 30 years. Palm oil productivity will 
increase and reach peak production between the 
ages of 7 (seven) and 18 (eighteen) years. The decline 
in production will occur after the plant passes its 
productive age, namely over 30 years, so rejuvenation 
must be carried out by cutting down old oil palm trees 
to replace them with new oil palm plants. Therefore, 
the Company’s productivity and performance are 
influenced by the age of the oil palm plants.

Oil Palm Plantation
FAP Agri’s operations include 9 (nine) concessions 
of palm oil plantations, 6 (six) Palm Oil Factories, 
and 1 (one) Kernel Processing factory. The company 
manages oil palm plantations in North Kalimantan, East 
Kalimantan and Riau with a concession of more than 
110,000 ha. In 2023, the plantation area that had been 
planted reached 77,434 ha of nucleus plantations and 
11,318 ha of plasma pattern plantations. In 2023, the area 
of planted land was 88,752 ha.
The planted land located in Riau was 3,332 ha, all of 
comprising productive plantations. Similarly, in North 
Kalimantan, the total area of planted land was 50,066 
ha, all comprising productive plantations. Meanwhile, in 
East Kalimantan, the area of planted land was 35,354 ha, 
with an area of mature plantations of 30,730 ha, and an 
area of immature plantations of 4,624 ha.
FAP Agri produces Fresh Fruit Bunches as one of its main

 Solvency, Receivables Collectability, and Other Financial Ratios
Kemampuan Membayar Utang
Kewajiban membayar utang Perusahaan, baik jangka 
pendek 
maupun 
jangka 
panjang, 
dapat 
diukur 
melalui rasio likuiditas dan rasio solvabilitas. Rasio 
likuiditas digunakan untuk mengukur kemampuan 
suatu Perusahaan dalam melunasi kewajiban jangka 
pendek, sementara rasio solvabilitas digunakan untuk 
mengukur kemampuan Perusahaan dalam melunasi 
seluruh kewajibannya.
Rasio Likuiditas
Perusahaan menggunakan rasio likuiditas dalam 
memenuhi kewajiban membayar utang yang segera 
jatuh tempo yang diukur melalui rasio kas (Cash 
Ratio), rasio cepat (Quick Ratio), dan rasio lancar 
(Current Ratio). Semakin tinggi nilai rasio likuiditas, 
maka semakin baik kemampuan Perusahaan dalam 
membayar utang jangka pendek. 
Solvency
The Company’s obligation to pay debts, both short 
term and long term, can be measured through liquidity 
ratios and solvency ratios. The liquidity ratio is used 
to measure a company’s ability to pay off short-term 
obligations, while the solvency ratio is used to measure 
the company’s ability to pay off all its obligations.
Liquidity Ratio
The company uses liquidity ratios to fulfill obligations 
to pay debts that are due soon, which are measured 
through the cash ratio, quick ratio and current ratio. The 
higher the liquidity ratio value, the better the company’s 
ability to pay short-term debt.

 KEBIJAKAN DAN PEMBAGIAN DIVIDEN 
 Dividend Policy and Distribution
Kebijakan Dividen 
Perusahaan menerapkan pembagian dividen atas 
laba yang diperoleh dengan mengacu pada ketentuan 
yang diatur dalam Anggaran Dasar Pasal 21 tentang 
Penggunaan Laba dan Pembagian Dividen. Adapun 
ketentuan yang diatur meliputi: 
•	 Laba bersih Perusahaan dalam suatu tahun buku 
seperti tercantum dalam neraca dan perhitungan 
laba rugi yang telah disahkan oleh RUPS tahunan dan 
merupakan saldo laba yang postif, dibagi menurut 
cara penggunaannya yang ditentukan oleh RUPS 
tersebut.
•	 Jika perhitungan laba rugi pada suatu tahun buku 
menunjukkan kerugian yang tidak dapat ditutup 
dengan dana cadangan, maka kerugian itu akan 
tetap dicatat dan dimasukkan dalam perhitungan 
laba rugi dan dalam tahun buku selanjutnya 
Perusahaan dianggap tidak mendapat laba selama 
kerugian yang tercatat dan dimasukkan dalam 
perhitungan laba rugi itu belum sama sekali tertutup 
seluruhnya.
Dividend Policy
The Company distributes dividends on profits obtained 
in accordance with the provisions outlined in Article 
21 of the Articles of Association Governing the Use 
of Profits and Dividend Distribution. The provisions 
regulated include:
•	 The Company’s net income in the financial year as 
stated in the balance sheet and calculation of profit 
or loss that have been ratified by the Annual GMS and 
is positive retained earnings, is distributed according

The General Meeting of Shareholders (GMS) is the 
main organ that serves as the highest authority in the 
Company. Based on Law No. 40 of 2007 concerning 
Limited Liability Companies, the GMS has authority 
that is not granted to the Board of Commissioners or 
Directors within the limits determined by the Articles of 
Association and applicable laws and regulations.
The GMS serves as strategic decision-making forum 
between the Company and Shareholders. The Board of 
Commissioners and Directors may submit accountability 
reports on the implementation and results of the 
Company’s 
performance. 
Meanwhile, 
shareholders 
have the right to express their opinions, get information 
about the Company’s performance, and vote on the 
Company’s strategic decisions.
Aside from that, the GMS also has the authority to 
appoint and dismiss the Board of Commissioners 
and Directors, as well as ask for accountability for the 
management of the Company. The GMS also has the 
authority to determine the remuneration of the Board 
of Commissioners and Directors, approve the annual 
report, including the ratification of the annual financial 
report, allocate profit use, appoint a public accountant, 
approve changes to the Articles of Association, and 
make decisions on corporate actions that require GMS 
approval in accordance with the provisions of the 
Articles of Association.
