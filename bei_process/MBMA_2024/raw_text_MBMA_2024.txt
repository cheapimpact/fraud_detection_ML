Gambaran Umum Industri Nikel Global
Overview of the Global Nickel Industry
Pada tahun 2024, industri nikel global mengalami 
tahun yang penuh kontras—ditandai dengan produksi 
tertinggi yang belum pernah terjadi sebelumnya, 
kondisi pasar yang bergejolak, dan pengawasan yang 
semakin ketat terhadap praktik-praktik lingkungan dan 
ketenagakerjaan. Di tengah dinamika tersebut, Indonesia 
terus menegaskan diri sebagai produsen nikel terkemuka 
di dunia, memainkan peran yang semakin penting dalam 
transisi menuju mobilitas listrik dan penyimpanan energi.
Tren Global dan Kondisi Pasar
Permintaan nikel sebagian besar masih didorong oleh peran 
pentingnya dalam teknologi baterai EV—terutama dalam 
kimia lithium-ion dengan kepadatan energi tinggi seperti 
Nickel-Manganese-Cobalt 
(NMC) 
dan 
Nickel-Cobalt-
Aluminum (NCA). Namun, tahun 2024 mengungkapkan 
kompleksitas yang mendasari narasi pertumbuhan ini.
Meskipun adopsi EV jangka panjang tetap kuat, laju 
ekspansi  permintaan tidak sesuai dengan proyeksi 
sebelumnya. Hal ini, dikombinasikan dengan pasokan 
global yang meningkat pesat—terutama dari Indonesia—
menyebabkan kekhawatiran kelebihan pasokan yang 
signifikan. Harga di LME mencerminkan ketidakseimbangan 
ini, dengan nikel mengalami volatilitas yang tajam sepanjang 
tahun. Faktor-faktor yang berkontribusi termasuk laju 
produksi Indonesia yang dipercepat, ketegangan geopolitik 
yang sedang berlangsung seperti sanksi terhadap Norilsk 
Nickel Rusia, dan lintasan pemulihan ekonomi pasca-
COVID yang tidak merata di Tiongkok, yang berdampak 
pada permintaan stainless steel, konsumen utama nikel.
Keunggulan Indonesia dalam Produksi 
Nikel
Indonesia mempertahankan posisinya sebagai pemain 
dominan dalam lanskap nikel global, menyumbang sekitar 
50% dari pasokan global pada tahun 2024. Dominasi ini 
merupakan hasil dari arah kebijakan yang berkelanjutan, 
investasi asing berskala besar, dan ekspansi agresif dari 
kapasitas penambangan hulu dan pengolahan hilir.
In 2024, the global nickel industry experienced a year of 
contrasts—marked by unprecedented production highs, 
volatile market conditions, and intensifying scrutiny 
over environmental and labour practices. At the heart 
of these dynamics stood Indonesia, which continued to 
assert itself as the world’s foremost nickel producer, 
playing an increasingly central role in the transition to 
electric mobility and energy storage.
Global Trends and Market Conditions
The demand for nickel remained largely driven by its 
critical role in EV battery technology—particularly in high-
energy-density lithium-ion chemistries such as Nickel-
Manganese-Cobalt (NMC) and Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum 
(NCA). However, 2024 revealed the complexities 
underlying this growth narrative.
While long-term EV adoption remains robust, the pace 
of demand expansion fell short of earlier projections. 
This, combined with rapidly rising global supply—
especially from Indonesia—led to significant oversupply 
concerns. Prices on the LME reflected this imbalance, 
with nickel experiencing sharp volatility throughout the 
year. Contributing factors included the accelerated pace 
of Indonesian production, ongoing geopolitical tensions 
such as sanctions on Russia’s Norilsk Nickel, and the 
uneven trajectory of China’s post-COVID economic 
recovery, which impacted stainless steel demand, 
another major consumer of nickel.
Indonesia’s Ascendancy in 
Nickel Production
Indonesia maintained its position as the dominant 
player in the global nickel landscape, accounting 
for approximately 50% of global supply in 2024. This 
dominance is the result of sustained policy direction, 
large-scale foreign investment, and the aggressive 
expansion of both upstream mining and downstream 
processing capacity.

Implikasi Strategis dan Geopolitik

Production growth was driven by the commissioning

spearheaded primarily by Chinese companies—including 
Tsingshan, GEM, and battery giant CATL—underscoring 
Chinese’s entrenched influence across the Indonesian 
nickel value chain.

Indonesia’s 2020 ban on nickel ore exports continued to 
shape the industry landscape, forcing investment into 
domestic smelting and refining capacity. As a result, the 
country rapidly emerged as a major supplier of battery-
grade nickel, producing significant volumes of MHP 
and nickel sulfate for EV applications. Simultaneously, 
Indonesia strengthened its position as the world’s largest 
producer of stainless steel, largely through Tsingshan’s 
vertically integrated operations in Sulawesi and beyond.
Strategic and Geopolitical Implications
Indonesia’s rise as a nickel superpower has attracted 
both opportunity and unease. While Indonesia continues 
to benefit from strong inbound investment—particularly 
from China—Western nations have expressed growing 
concern over the strategic implications of China’s control 
over key elements of the global EV supply chain.
In response, Indonesia has begun exploring ways to 
reduce its overreliance on Chinese capital by attracting 
Western partners. Companies such as Ford and Hyundai 
have entered into partnerships or joint ventures with 
Indonesian entities to secure access to battery-grade 
nickel while diversifying supply away from China. 
Nonetheless, Indonesia’s status as a non-free trade 
partner under U.S. law presents challenges. The Inflation 
Reduction Act (IRA), for instance, incentivises the use of 
critical minerals sourced from U.S. trade allies—placing 
Indonesian nickel outside the scope of eligibility for key 
EV subsidies.
Indonesia’s 
export 
policies 
also 
remain 
a 
point 
of contention. The European Union’s World Trade 
Organisation (WTO) challenge against the nickel ore 
export ban—filed in 2022—remains unresolved. Despite 
this, Indonesia has reiterated its commitment to 
domestic value-add strategies, emphasising national 
control over critical resources.

Tantangan dan Prospek
Terlepas dari dominasinya di tingkat global, Indonesia 
menghadapi beberapa tantangan. Yang paling mendesak 
adalah risiko kelebihan pasokan yang berkelanjutan. 
Karena produksi melebihi permintaan, harga telah turun 
tajam, dan mengakhiri tahun 2024 pada level terendah 
dalam empat tahun terakhir. Untuk mengatasi hal ini, 
Pemerintah 
Indonesia 
sedang 
mempertimbangkan 
pengurangan kuota pertambangan yang signifikan—dari 
272 mt pada tahun 2024 menjadi serendah 150 mt pada 
tahun 2025.
Pada saat yang sama, tekanan terkait ESG semakin 
meningkat. Produsen mobil seperti Tesla dan produsen 
EV Eropa semakin menuntut nikel yang bersumber 
dari sumber yang etis dan rendah karbon. Kemampuan 
Indonesia untuk memenuhi ekspektasi ini kemungkinan 
akan menentukan daya saing jangka panjangnya di pasar 
bahan baterai premium.
Ke depannya, Indonesia diharapkan dapat terus 
mengembangkan kapasitas pemurniannya, terutama 
melalui fasilitas HPAL baru yang ditujukan untuk produksi 
tingkat baterai. Untuk menjaga kepercayaan investor dan 
akses pasar, pemerintah mungkin juga perlu meninjau 
kembali kerangka kerja lingkungan, tenaga kerja, dan 
perdagangannya untuk menyeimbangkan kepentingan 
nasional dengan standar keberlanjutan global.
Challenges and Prospect
Despite its global dominance, Indonesia faces several 
challenges. The most pressing is the risk of sustained 
oversupply. As production outpaces demand, prices have 
fallen sharply, ending 2024 at a four-year low. To address 
this, the Indonesian government is considering a significant 
reduction in mining quotas—from 272 mt in 2024 to as low 
as 150 mt in 2025.
Simultaneously, ESG-related pressure is intensifying. 
Automakers such as Tesla and European EV manufacturers 
are increasingly demanding ethically sourced, low-carbon 
nickel. Indonesia’s ability to meet these expectations will 
likely determine its long-term competitiveness in the 
premium battery materials market.
Going forward, Indonesia is expected to continue expanding 
its refining capacity, particularly through new HPAL facilities 
aimed at battery-grade production. To maintain investor 
confidence and market access, the government may 
also need to revisit its environmental, labour, and trade 
frameworks for balancing national interests with global 
sustainability standards.

Kinerja Segmen Pertambangan
Performance of the Mining Segment
Untuk tahun yang berakhir pada tanggal 31 Desember 
2024, segmen pertambangan mencatatkan pendapatan 
sebesar AS$316,52 juta dan laba sebesar AS$47,03 
juta, dibandingkan dengan tahun sebelumnya yang 
mencatatkan pendapatan sebesar AS$83,61 juta dan 
laba sebesar AS$0,18 juta.
Aset Pertambangan
Per 31 Desember 2024, Grup MBMA memiliki aset 
pertambangan sebagai berikut:

Tambang SCM
Grup MBMA memiliki 51,0% kepemilikan di Tambang SCM, 
sumber daya nikel laterit berbiaya rendah yang signifikan 
secara global yang terletak di konsesi seluas 21.100 hektare. 
Tambang SCM mengandung sekitar 11,3 mt nikel dan 0,8 mt 
kobalt, yang terdiri dari campuran bijih nikel limonit (77%) 
dan bijih nikel saprolit (23%).
Cadangan limonit Tambang SCM yang kaya sangat ideal 
untuk pengolahan HPAL guna menghasilkan MHP, yang 
digunakan untuk memproduksi nikel sulfat sebagai 
bahan baku baterai EV. Bijih nikel saprolit dipasok secara 
internal ke Smelter RKEF di IMIP, di mana bijih tersebut 
diolah menjadi NPI untuk produksi stainless steel.
Tambang SCM dirancang sebagai fondasi bagi integrasi 
jangka panjang dengan operasi hilir Perseroan, termasuk 
fasilitas HPAL yang sedang dikembangkan. Hal ini akan 
mendukung pemrosesan bernilai tambah dan stabilitas 
pasokan jangka panjang untuk pasar baterai dan 
stainless steel. Kepemilikan Perseroan di Tambang SCM 
mengamankan jalur bahan baku yang dapat diandalkan 
selama beberapa dekade dan memperkuat posisi 
strategis Grup MBMA dalam rantai pasokan bahan baku 
baterai global.
Produksi dan Penjualan untuk Segmen 
Pertambangan
Pada tahun 2024, Tambang SCM meningkatkan operasinya 
secara signifikan. Tambang SCM memproduksi sekitar 
10,1 juta wmt bijih nikel limonit dan 4,9 juta wmt bijih 
nikel saprolit selama setahun penuh, meningkat dari 
tingkat produksi pada tahun 2023 seiring dengan 
adanya kontraktor penambangan baru dan peningkatan 
infrastruktur jalan yang meningkatkan hasil produksi dan 
logistik.
For the year ended 31 December 2024, the mining 
segment recorded a revenue of US$316.52 million and a 
profit of US$47.03 million, compared to the previous year 
with US$83.61 million in revenue and US$0.18 million in 
profit.
Mining Assets
As of 31 December 2024, MBMA Group owned the 
following mining asset:

SCM Mine
MBMA Group holds a 51.0% interest in the SCM Mine, a 
globally significant, low-cost laterite nickel resource 
located within a 21,100-hectare concession. The SCM Mine 
contains approximately 11.3 mt of nickel and 0.8 mt of 
cobalt, comprising a balanced mix of limonite nickel ore 
(77%) and saprolite nickel ore (23%).
The SCM Mine’s rich limonite reserves are ideal for HPAL 
processing to produce MHP, which is used to manufacture 
nickel sulphate for EV battery materials. Saprolite Nickel 
ore is supplied internally to Group Company’s RKEF 
Smelter within the IMIP, where it is processed into NPI 
for stainless steel production.
The SCM Mine is designed as the foundation for long-term 
integration with the Company’s downstream operations, 
including HPAL facilities under development. These will 
support value-added processing and long-term supply 
stability for both battery and stainless steel markets. 
The Company’s ownership in the SCM Mine secures a 
reliable feedstock pipeline for multiple decades and 
strengthens MBMA Group’s strategic positioning in the 
global battery materials supply chain.
Production and Sales for 
the Mining Segment
In 2024, the SCM Mine ramped up its operations 
significantly. The SCM Mine produced approximately 
10.1 million wmt of limonite nickel ore and 4.9 million 
wmt of saprolite nickel ore for the full year, an increase 
from 2023 levels as new mining contractors and road 
infrastructure enhancements improved output and 
logistics.

RKEF 
Smelter 
continued 
to 
deliver 
strong 
performance, contributing a total of 82,161 tonnes of 
nickel in NPI and LGNM, a 26% increase from 2023. 
This increase was driven by stable full-year operations 
at ZHN, which was commissioned in mid-2023, and 
optimised production at CSID RKEF Smelter and BSID 
RKEF Smelter, both of which consistently operated at 
above 100% utilisation.
MBMA Group’s operational collaboration with the

through shared utilities, port access, and consumables

increase year-on-year. This converter plays a critical role 
in downstream integration, enabling the Company to 
convert NPI into high-grade nickel matte (>70% Ni), a key 
input for battery materials.

Management Discussion and Analysis
Untuk tahun yang berakhir pada tanggal 31 Desember 2024, 
Grup MBMA melaporkan pertumbuhan pendapatan 
yang kuat, optimalisasi biaya yang berkelanjutan, dan 
mempertahankan profitabilitas yang positif di tengah 
volatilitas harga nikel global.
Pendapatan
Perseroan mencatat pendapatan konsolidasian sebesar 
AS$1.844,70 juta pada tahun 2024, meningkat 38,9% 
dari AS$1.328,33 juta pada tahun 2023. Peningkatan 
ini terutama disebabkan oleh (i) Perseroan mengakui 
kenaikan pendapatan usaha dari penjualan nickel matte 
sebesar AS$283,04 juta, setelah konsolidasi HNMI ke 
dalam hasil operasional Grup MBMA sejak bulan Mei 
2023; dan (ii) kenaikan pendapatan usaha dari Tambang 
SCM pada tahun 2024 sebesar AS$165,03 juta,  setelah 
Tambang SCM mulai membukukan pendapatan usaha ke 
pihak ketiga sejak bulan Desember 2023 dari penjualan 
bijih nikel limonit.
Beban Pokok Pendapatan
Total 
beban 
pokok 
pendapatan 
adalah 
sebesar 
AS$1.730,59 juta, naik 38,4% dari AS$1.250,85 juta pada 
tahun 2023, terutama disebabkan oleh (i) Perseroan 
mengakui kenaikan biaya manufaktur atas produksi 
nickel matte sebesar AS$199,56 juta, setelah konsolidasi 
HNMI ke dalam hasil operasional Grup MBMA sejak bulan 
Mei 2023; dan (ii) Perseroan membukukan kenaikan 
biaya pertambangan dari Tambang SCM pada tahun 2024 
sebesar AS$110,47 juta dibandingkan dengan pada tahun 
2023, setelah Tambang SCM beroperasi komersial sejak 
bulan Agustus 2023.
Laba Kotor
Laba kotor tahun berjalan mencapai AS$114,11 juta, naik 
47,3% dari AS$77,48 juta pada tahun 2023, dengan sedikit 
kenaikan margin yang sebagian besar disebabkan oleh 
kinerja keuangan Tambang SCM sehubungan dengan 
peningkatan volume produksi (ramp up) dan efisiensi 
biaya yang diimbangi dengan penurunan harga jual NPI 
dan nickel matte sepanjang tahun 2024.
Beban Usaha
Beban usaha mencapai AS$34,29 juta, termasuk di 
dalamnya beban penjualan dan pemasaran sebesar 
AS$3,20 juta dan beban umum dan administrasi 
sebesar AS$31,09 juta. Jumlah ini naik sebesar 14,6% 
dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya karena adanya kenaikan 
beban penjualan dan pemasaran sejalan dengan 
kenaikan pendapatan usaha dan kenaikan biaya yang 
terkait dengan operasional AIM yang baru.
For the year ended 31 December 2024, MBMA Group 
reported 
strong 
revenue 
growth, 
continued 
cost 
optimisation, and maintained positive profitability 
despite global nickel price volatility.
Revenue
The 
Company 
recorded 
consolidated 
revenue 
of 
US$1,844.70 million in 2024, up 38.9% from US$1,328.33 
million in 2023. The increase was primarily driven by 
(i) the Company recorded increase in revenue from 
nickel matte sales amounting to US$283.04 million, 
after consolidating HNMI to MBMA Group’s operational 
results since May 2023; and (ii) increase in revenue from 
SCM Mine in 2024 amounting to US$165.03 million, after 
SCM Mine started to record revenue to third party since 
December 2023 from limonite nickel ore sales.
Cost of Revenue
Total cost of revenue was US$1,730.59 million, increased 
by 38.4% from US$1,250.85 million in 2023, due to (i) the 
Company recognized increase in manufacturing costs 
for nickel matte production amounting to US$199.56 
million, after consolidating HNMI to MBMA Group’s 
operasional results since May 2023; and (ii) the Company 
recorded increase in mining costs from SCM Mine in 
2024 amounting to US$110.47 million compared to 2023, 
after SCM Mine started its commercial operation since 
August 2023.
Gross Profit
Gross profit for the year become US$114.11 million, 
increased by 47.3% from US$77.48 million in 2023, 
with a slight margin increase that mainly triggered by 
financial performance of SCM Mine related to increae in 
production volume and cost efficiency that offset with 
decrease in NPI and nickel matte sales price during 2024.
Operating Expenses
Operating expenses totalled US$34.29 million, including 
selling and marketing expenses of US$3.20 million and 
general and administrative expenses of US$31.09 million. 
It increased by 14.6% than the prior year due to increase 
in selling and marketing expenses inline with increase 
in revenue and ramp-up costs associated with AIM 
operations.

tersebut terutama disebabkan oleh peningkatan beban

2023. The decrease was mainly due to the fact that most

the definition of qualifying assets. The finance costs 
arising from these loans are directly attributable to the 
acquisition, construction and production of the qualifying 
assets and are therefore capitalized as acquisition costs 
in accordance with SFAS No. 223, “Borrowing Costs.”

interest, and foreign exchange gain - net.
Income Tax Expense

by 6,354.4% to US$10.6 million from US$0.2 million in

income tax expense arising from business activities of 
Subsidiaries that not benefiting tax holiday facility.

campuran Dolar AS, Yuan Tiongkok, dan Rupiah 
Indonesia, yang memberikan manfaat lindung nilai alami 
dan membantu memitigasi risiko yang terkait dengan
