TINJAUAN MAKROEKONOMI
Tahun 2023 dibayangi sejumlah tantangan akibat ketidakpastian geopolitik 
dan moneter di lingkup makroekonomi global. Sejumlah faktor seperti 
perang, kenaikan harga energi dan pangan, serta tingginya inflasi menjadi 
faktor utama yang memengaruhi kondisi tersebut. International Monetary 
Fund (IMF) memprediksi pertumbuhan ekonomi global hanya mencapai 
3,6% di tahun 2023, menurun dari 6,1% di tahun sebelumnya. 
Di tengah gejolak ini, Indonesia menjadi salah satu negara yang berhasil 
menunjukkan kinerja ekonomi yang relatif stabil. Badan Pusat Statistik 
(BPS) mencatat bahwa ekonomi Indonesia tumbuh sebesar 5,05% di tahun 
2023, melampaui target Pemerintah sebesar 5,0-5,2%. Pertumbuhan ini 
ditopang oleh konsumsi domestik yang kuat, pemulihan investasi, dan 
peningkatan ekspor. Kinerja positif ini turut tercermin dalam tingkat inflasi 
yang terjaga di angka 2,61%, lebih rendah dari target Pemerintah sebesar 
5,0%.
Konsumsi masyarakat sebagai kontributor utama perekonomian, tumbuh 
4,47% pada triwulan IV-2023, atau tumbuh 4,82% sepanjang tahun 2023. 
Daya beli masyarakat yang tetap terjaga dengan tingkat inflasi yang 
terkendali menjadi salah satu faktor penting dalam menjaga konsumsi 
masyarakat. Beberapa kegiatan penting selama tahun 2023, antara lain 
persiapan penyelenggaran pemilu dan percepatan penyelesaian Proyek 
Strategis Nasional, hingga pembangunan Ibu Kota Nusantara (IKN), 
mendorong konsumsi Pemerintah dan kinerja positif investasi yang secara 
langsung menggerakkan aktivitas ekonomi regional. 
Pemulihan pasca pandemi COVID-19 juga terus berlanjut sebagaimana 
tercermin dari peningkatan mobilitas masyarakat yang telah mendorong 
pertumbuhan sektor akomodasi dan makan minum sebesar 10,01% 
year-on-year (yoy) dan sektor transportasi sebesar 13,96% (yoy). 
Penyelenggaraan berbagai event baik level nasional maupun internasional 
mendorong daya tarik Indonesia sebagai destinasi wisata. Selain itu, 
peningkatan mobilitas juga dipengaruhi oleh musim liburan nataru dan 
persiapan penyelenggaraan pemilu.

MACROECONOMIC REVIEW
The year 2023 saw a number of challenges due to geopolitical and 
monetary uncertainties in the global macroeconomic sphere. Factors 
such as war, rising energy and food prices, and high inflation are the main 
factors affecting these conditions. The International Monetary Fund (IMF) 
predicted that global economic growth would only reach 3.6% in 2023, 
down from 6.1% in the previous year. 
In the midst of this turmoil, Indonesia was one of the countries that 
managed to show a relatively stable economic performance. The Central 
Bureau of Statistics (BPS) noted that Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.05% 
in 2023, surpassing the government’s target of 5.0-5.2%. This growth was 
supported by strong domestic consumption, investment recovery, and 
increased exports. This positive performance was also reflected in the 
inflation rate which was maintained at 2.61%, lower than the Government’s 
target of 5.0%.
Public consumption as the main contributor to the economy, grew 4.47% 
in the fourth quarter of 2023, or grew 4.82% throughout 2023. People’s 
purchasing power was maintained with a controlled inflation rate being 
one of the important factors in maintaining public consumption. Several 
important activities during 2023, including preparations for the general 
election and accelerating the completion of National Strategic Projects, 
to the construction of the Capital City of Nusantara (IKN), encouraged 
government consumption and positive investment performance which 
directly drove regional economic activities.
 The recovery after the COVID-19 pandemic also continued as reflected 
in the increase in community mobility which has driven the growth of the 
accommodation and food and beverages sector by 10.01% year-on-year 
(yoy) and the transportation sector by 13.96% (yoy). The organization of 
various events at both national and international levels has boosted 
Indonesia’s attractiveness as a tourist destination. In addition, the 
increase in mobility was also influenced by the Nataru holiday season and 
preparations for the elections.

included the easing of social restrictions that encouraged people to return 
to activities outside the home, including visiting malls, increasing people’s 
purchasing power which encouraged consumption and visits to malls, and 
the rise of e-commerce which encouraged several retailers to open offline

manajemen cash cycle yang optimal dan memperhatikan risiko dan hasil 
yang menguntungkan.

Informasi Transaksi Material yang Mengandung Benturan Kepentingan 
dan Transaksi dengan Pihak Afiliasi
Sepanjang tahun 2023, Perseroan tidak melakukan transaksi yang 
mengandung benturan kepentingan. Namun, Perseroan melakukan 
transaksi afiliasi dengan pihak-pihak berelasi sebagaimana didefinisikan 
dalam PSAK No. 7 mengenai “Pengungkapan Pihak-pihak Berelasi”. Pihak 
berelasi didefinisikan sebagai pihak yang memiliki kemampuan untuk 
mengendalikan (melalui kepemilikan, secara langsung atau tidak langsung) 
atau mempunyai pengaruh signifikan (melalui partisipasi dalam kebijakan 
keuangan dan operasi) atas pihak lain dalam pengambilan keputusan 
keuangan dan operasi. 
Rincian atas sifat transaksi dan hubungan dengan pihak berelasi adalah 
sebagai berikut

to pay a rental deposit in advance. 
Apart from tenants, the Company may also obtain revenue from events 
such as exhibitions that are shorter in nature with a calculation per day. 
Some examples of events are product launching, product introduction, 
product sales, sports events, and others. Other revenues come from 
parking services and utility services (water and telephone) provided by the 
Company and its Subsidiaries. 
The Company’s revenue is affected by the number of visits where the 
presence of visitors to the mall also has a seasonal nature. Some of 
the times when malls are full of visitors are during vacation periods, 
especially school holidays. To increase the attractiveness for visitors, mall 
manager always prepare for national events such as Lebaran, Christmas, 
Independence Day, and so on by creating decorations and holding events 
that support these themes. The presence of large-scale events greatly 
affects the number of visitors and of course this increases revenue for both 
tenants and the Company.
