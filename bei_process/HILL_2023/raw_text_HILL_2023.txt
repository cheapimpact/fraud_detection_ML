World Economy
Throughout 2023, the global economy exhibited 
strengthening, albeit with a slow and uneven 
recovery underway. Current global activity has yet 
to return to pre-pandemic levels. There is also a 
widening divergence in growth across regions and 
ongoing challenges stemming from the long-term 
consequences of COVID-19, the Ukraine conflict, 
and increasing geo-economic fragmentation, tight 
monetary policies to address inflationary pressures, 
reduced fiscal stimulus due to high debt levels, and 
implications of extreme weather.
In the January 2024 edition of the World Economic 
Outlook (WEO), the International Monetary Fund 
(IMF) projected global economic growth to slow 
from 3.5% in 2022 to 3.1% in 2023. Underpinning this 
performance, five key changes were highlighted 
and acknowledged by countries attending the 2023 
IMF-World Bank Annual Meetings in Morocco.
First, global economic growth slowed from an initial 
estimate of 2.9% to 2.8%, alongside widening growth 
divergences among countries. This condition 
led to increased uncertainty in global financial 
markets, resulting in more selective capital flows to 
developing countries. The situation was exacerbated 
by continued high risks in the global financial 
system, including issues in China’s property sector 
and the bankruptcy of several high-exposure banks 
in the United States (US) to the technology sector. 
Additionally, the European economy weakened due 
to the escalation of geopolitical tensions between 
Russia and Ukraine. Meanwhile, the US economy 
outperformed initial forecasts, driven by increased 
consumption supported by wage hikes and high 
savings utilization. With these developments, there 
was a shift in the source of global growth from China 
to the US, implicating tighter monetary policies in 
advanced economies.
Second is the increasing geopolitical tension. As 
2023 drew to a close, the global economy faced 
escalating geopolitical risks. While the Russia-
Ukraine conflict remained unresolved, the outbreak 
of Hamas-Israel conflict further shook the world.

Perekonomian Indonesia
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia tetap kuat di 
tengah ketidakpastian kondisi perekonomian global. 
Data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS) menunjukkan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia triwulan IV 2023 
tetap tumbuh kuat sebesar 5,04% (yoy), meskipun 
sedikit melambat dari pertumbuhan pada tahun 
sebelumnya yang tercatat sebesar 5,31% (yoy). 
Dengan perkembangan tersebut, Bank Indonesia 
menutup pertumbuhan ekonomi 2023 tetap pada 
kisaran 4,5-5,3%.
Pertumbuhan yang tetap kuat tersebut terutama 
bersumber dari kuatnya permintaan domestik 
sejalan dengan kenaikan pertumbuhan konsumsi 
rumah tangga dan pemerintah serta peningkatan 
investasi, di tengah kinerja ekspor yang menurun 
karena pelemahan perekonomian dan harga 
komoditas dunia. 
At least for now, tensions seem likely to evolve into 
prolonged conflicts, albeit relatively contained. The 
impact on global commodity prices and inflation 
remained subdued due to already tight conditions. 
Overall, these circumstances are predicted to lead 
to record-high oil prices and reveal the onset of a 
global economic recession. Financial markets will 
be negatively affected, with the highest risks in 
the equity markets, potentially seeing a decline of

widening credit spreads.

And fifth, its impact, where the US dollar continues 
to strengthen, weakening the currencies of many

condition is not disconnected from the financial 
market’s tendency or investors’ preference for 
holding US Dollar cash, also known as the “cash is 
king” phenomenon.
Indonesian Economy
Indonesia’s economic growth remains robust 
amidst global economic uncertainties. Data from 
the Central Statistics Agency (BPS) shows that 
Indonesia’s economy in the fourth quarter of 2023 
continued to grow strongly by 5.04% (yoy), albeit 
slightly slower than the previous year’s growth of 
5.31% (yoy). With this development, Bank Indonesia 
forecasts economic growth for 2023 to remain 
within the range of 4.5-5.3%.
This sustained strong growth is primarily driven 
by robust domestic demand, alongside increases 
in household and government consumption, and 
investment, amid declining export performance 
due to global economic weakness and commodity 
prices.

INDUSTRI NIKEL
Nickel Industry 
Overview 
Nikel dalam negeri berpotensi menjadi salah 
satu komoditas masa depan ditengah upaya 
dunia menekan emisi karbon dari bahan bakar 
fosil. Permintaan nikel dunia akan mengalami 
peningkatan seiring dengan mulai gencarnya 
pengembangan kendaraan listrik. Berdasarkan 
Booklet Tambang Nikel 2020 Kementerian ESDM, 
cadangan nikel Indonesia mencapai 72 juta ton 
dari cadangan nikel dunia berjumlah 139,4 juta ton, 
dengan kata lain 52% cadangan nikel berada di 
Indonesia. Tak hanya itu, Indonesia juga menjadi 
produsen nikel terbesar di dunia sebesar 800.000 
ton dari total produksi nikel dunia sebanyak 2,68 
juta ton. Setelah Indonesia, ada Australia dengan 
cadangan nikel mencapai 15%, lalu Brazil 8%, Rusia 
5%, dan gabungan sejumlah negara lainnya seperti 
Filiphina, Cina, Kanada, dan lainnya 20%.
Sejak tahun 2014, Indonesia melarang ekspor 
bijih nikel untuk mendorong hilirisasi industri nikel 
domestik yang akan berdampak positif bagi 
perekonomian. Sejak diterapkannya kebijakan 
tersebut, investasi pabrik pengolahan bijih nikel di 
Indonesia mengalami kenaikan yang cukup tajam. 
Pada tahun 2021, sekitar 37% dari total kapasitas 
produksi smelter nikel dunia adalah dari Indonesia. 
Dari keseluruhan kapasitas produksi yang ada di 
Indonesia, sebagian besar menghasilkan produk 
FeroNickel (“FeNi”) dan Nickel Pig Iron (“NPI”) yang 
merupakan 
bahan 
baku 
utama 
pembuatan 
baja nirkarat. Maraknya investasi smelter nikel di 
Indonesia tentu saja patut disyukuri. Disamping 
selaras dengan upaya hilirisasi industri nikel yang 
digaungkan oleh pemerintah, efek pengganda 
yang ditimbulkan untuk kemajuan ekonomi di 
wilayah proyek pengembangan nikel juga sangat 
dirasakan oleh masyarakat. Dilain sisi, kenaikan 
pasokan produk olahan nikel dari Indonesia, 
terutama dalam bentuk FeNi dan NPI, dikhawatirkan 
bisa menyebabkan pasar dalam kondisi surplus 
dan memberikan tekanan pada harga jual nikel. 
Untungnya, industri mobil listrik juga sedang 
berkembang 
secara 
signifikan. 
Munculnya 
kebijakan 
atau 
wacana 
kebijakan 
terkait 
pembatasan emisi karbon di beberapa negara, 
terutama di Eropa, telah mendorong pertumbuhan 
industri mobil listrik dan baterai mobil listrik. Hal ini 
tentu saja membawa angin segar pada pasar nikel 
dunia. Teknologi baterai yang dominan dipakai 
oleh manufaktur mobil listrik saat ini menggunakan 
nikel sebagai salah satu bahan baku utama.
Domestic nickel has the potential to become one 
of the future commodities amid the world’s efforts 
to reduce carbon emissions from fossil fuels. World 
demand for nickel will increase in line with the 
incessant development of electric vehicles. Based 
on the 2020 Nickel Mining Booklet of the Ministry of 
Energy and Mineral Resources, Indonesia’s nickel 
reserves reach 72 million tons out of the world’s 
nickel reserves of 139.4 million tons, in other words 
52% of nickel reserves are in Indonesia. Furthermore, 
Indonesia is also the world’s largest nickel producer, 
in the amount of 800,000tons from the world’s 
total nickel production of 2.68 million tons. After 
Indonesia, is Australia with nickel reserves reaching 
15%, then Brazil 8%, Russia 5%, and a combination of 
a number of other countries such as the Philippines, 
China, Canada, and others 20%. 
Since 2014, Indonesia has banned exports of 
nickel ore to encourage the downstreaming of the 
domestic nickel industry which will have a positive 
impact on the economy. Since the implementation 
of this policy, investment in nickel ore processing 
plants in Indonesia has increased quite sharply. In 
2021, around 37% of the world’s total nickel smelter 
production capacity was from Indonesia. Of the 
total existing production capacity in Indonesia, 
most of it produces FeroNickel (“FeNi”) and Nickel 
Pig Iron (“NPI”) products which are the main raw 
materials for making stainless steel. The rise 
of investment in nickel smelters in Indonesia is 
deserves to be grateful for. Besides being in line 
with the efforts to downstream the nickel industry 
echoed by the government, the multiplier effect 
for economic progress in the nickel development 
project area is also being felt by the community. On 
the other hand, the increase in the supply of refined 
nickel products from Indonesia, especially in the 
form of FeNi and NPI, is feared to cause a surplus in 
the market and put pressure on nickel selling prices. 
Fortunately, the electric car industry is also growing 
significantly. The emergence of policies or policy 
discourse related to limiting carbon emissions 
in several countries, especially in Europe, has 
encouraged the growth of the electric car industry 
and electric car batteries. This is breath of fresh air 
to the world nickel market. The dominant battery 
technology used by electric car manufacturers 
currently uses nickel as one of the main raw 
materials.

jasa kontraktor pertambangan nikel
Berdasarkan data United States geological survey di 
tahun 2022, cadangan nikel Indonesia diperkirakan 
mencapai 21 juta metrik ton atau sekitar 21% dari 
total cadangan nikel dunia, dan produksi nikel 
Indonesia di tahun 2022 mencapai 1,6 juta metrik 
ton dan terbesar di dunia. Kekayaan sumber daya 
alam Indonesia khususnya nikel telah menarik 
minat investasi dari para investor asing. Permintaan

jasa pertambangan nikel. Pada saat ini, Grup 
telah memperoleh sejumlah proyek kontraktor 
pertambangan nikel penting yang sedang berjalan, 
dan Grup berencana untuk tetap meningkatkan 
fokusnya dalam sektor jasa pertambangan nikel di 
masa depan.

Group’s employees

sectors, the availability and continuous training of 
employees is one of the key factors to ensure that 
important processes are carried out safely and 
efficiently. The Group intends to continue to invest in,

in the field and at the headquarter, by conducting 
periodic training to update employee knowledge,

improve their skills, and implementing a rigorous 
hiring process to acquire employees with the best 
skills available. The Group believes that investing 
in the Group’s employees will help to improve 
the quality and efficiency of the Group’s business 
processes and services provided to customers.

The Company Group is engaged in two business 
segments, which consist of mining services for nickel 
and coal mining projects, as well as civil construction 
services. In the year ended on December 31, 2023,

Mining Service
The Company offers a wide range of mining 
services to its customers, including mine planning 
with the support of experienced experts, land 
clearing for mining areas, construction of mining 
area infrastructure, overburden removal, as well 
as mining of nickel ore and coal using a variety 
of heavy equipment. As an important part of its 
business strategy, the Group continues to develop 
the proportion of nickel mining services business 
activities to its overall business activities, and we 
believe that the Group is well positioned to take 
advantage of the dynamics of the nickel market and 
the Indonesian industrial sector and the growing use 
of refined metals. In the year ended on December 
31, 2023, mining services for the production of nickel 
and coal generated Rp2.076.958 million, or 51,34%, 
and Rp1.784.831 million, or 44,12%, of the Group’s 
total revenue, respectively.
Civil Construction Service
The Group’s civil construction services include 
earthworks, road construction works, watershed, 
and weir development projects. The Group draws 
on its experience in the civil construction services 
sector to undertake projects involving specialized 
earthworks, such as high grade nickel ore projects. 
In the project, the prevention of contamination 
from soil and impurities is an important part of the 
services offered by the Group. The company plans 
to continue to take advantage of the synergies 
between its civil construction services business 
segment and its mining services business segment 
while continuing to develop its business activities, 
especially in the nickel mining services sector. In 
the year ended on December 31, 2023, construction 
services generated Rp147,736 million, or 4,29% of the 
Group’s total revenue.
