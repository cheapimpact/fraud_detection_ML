TINJAUAN MAKRO EKONOMI
Ekonomi global terus melambat sejak pemulihan pasca 
pandemi Covid-19. Inflasi yang masih tinggi dan penurunan 
produktivitas berkontribusi atas perlambatan ekonomi 
yang terjadi. Hingga semester pertama 2024, negara-
negara dunia masih menghadapi ketidakpastian. Tensi 
geopolitik yang semakin naik ditambah suku bunga 
kebijakan negara maju yang masih tinggi menyebabkan 
arus modal keluar dari negara-negara berkembang. Selain 
itu, fragmentasi geoekonomi dan perubahan iklim menjadi 
risiko perekonomian ke depan. Dengan berbagai tantangan 
yang ada, pertumbuhan ekonomi global diproyeksikan 
stagnan di level 3,2% hingga 2025 nanti di tengah inflasi 
yang diproyeksikan melambat (IMF 2024).
Indonesia sebagai negara berkembang ikut terdampak 
kondisi perekonomian global. Ekonomi dan inflasi nasional 
pada 2024 turut melambat di tengah tekanan dunia. Pada 
triwulan I dan II 2024 ini, perekonomian tumbuh solid namun 
dengan tren yang menurun. Meskipun demikian, ekonomi 
Indonesia tetap stabil di tengah dinamika perekonomian 
global, menurut data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS) secara 
keseluruhan perekonomian Indonesia tumbuh sebesar 
5,03%, melambat dibanding capaian tahun 2023 yang 
mengalami pertumbuhan sebesar 5,05% (c-to-c). 
Pertumbuhan ekonomi tahun 2024 didorong oleh beberapa 
sektor utama yaitu industri pengolahan sebagai kontributor 
terbesar, 
sektor 
ini 
tumbuh 
sebesar 
4,5%. 
Sektor 
perdagangan besar dan eceran yang mencatat pertumbuhan 
4,8%, sektor informasi dan komunikasi dengan pertumbuhan 
tertinggi mencapai 9,3%, serta sektor pengeluaran dan 
konsumsi.
Selain itu, kinerja ekspor barang dan jasa Indonesia 
tumbuh sebesar 3,2%, sementara impor meningkat 3,5%. 
Meskipun demikian, neraca perdagangan tetap terjaga 
dengan baik. Tingkat inflasi sepanjang tahun 2024 tercatat

MACROECONOMIC OVERVIEW
The global economy has continued to decelerate following 
the post-COVID-19 recovery. Persistently high inflation 
and declining productivity have contributed to the ongoing 
economic slowdown. As of the first half of 2024, uncertainty 
remains prevalent across the globe. Escalating geopolitical 
tensions and sustained high interest rates in advanced 
economies have triggered capital outflows from developing 
countries. In addition, geo-economic fragmentation and 
climate change pose significant risks to the global economic 
outlook. Amidst these challenges, global economic growth is 
projected to stagnate at around 3.2% through 2025, even as 
inflation is expected to ease (IMF, 2024).
As a developing country, Indonesia has not been immune to 
the impact of the global economic environment. In 2024, both 
national economic growth and inflation have shown signs of 
slowing under global pressures. During the first and second 
quarters of 2024, the Indonesian economy maintained a solid 
pace of growth, albeit with a downward trend. Nevertheless, 
Indonesia’s economy has remained stable amid global 
economic volatility. According to Statistics Indonesia (BPS), 
the national economy grew by 5.03% on a cumulative basis 
(c-to-c), slightly down from 5.05% growth recorded in 2023.
Economic growth in 2024 has been primarily driven by 
several key sectors. The manufacturing industry, as the 
largest contributor, grew by 4.5%. The wholesale and retail 
trade sector expanded by 4.8%, while the information and 
communications sector posted the highest growth at 9.3%. 
Growth was also supported by household spending and 
consumption.
In addition, Indonesia’s export performance in goods and 
services increased by 3.2%, while imports rose by 3.5%. 
Despite this, the trade balance remained well-maintained. 
Inflation for the full year of 2024 stood at 2.8%, within the 
target range set by the government and Bank Indonesia. This 
reflects the government’s effectiveness in maintaining price 
stability and supporting public purchasing power.
COAL MINING INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
The mining sector remains one of the key contributors to 
Indonesia’s economic performance, particularly through non-
tax state revenues (PNBP), which have totaled approximately 
Rp1,800 trillion over the past decade. Moreover, Indonesia is 
one of the world’s largest coal exporters, with annual exports 
nearing 600 million metric tons.
According to data from the Ministry of Energy and Mineral 
Resources’ Minerba One Data Indonesia (MODI) system, 
domestic coal production reached 831.05 million tons in 
2024. This figure represents 117.05% of the 2024 production 
target of 710 million tons. Of the total production, domestic 
absorption reached 378.95 million tons, while exports 
amounted to 434.11 million tons. The realization of the 
Domestic Market Obligation (DMO) stood at 209.93 million 
tons. Total coal sales in 2024 reached 813.06 million tons, 
achieving 114.52% of the 2023 coal sales target.
The surplus in coal production reflects the reality that coal 
continues to dominate Indonesia’s energy mix. As of June 
2024, coal accounted for 39.5% of the energy mix, followed 
by oil at 29.9%, natural gas at 16.69%, and renewable 
energy at 13.93%.

TINGKAT PERPUTARAN PIUTANG
Tingkat perputaran piutang usaha (receivable turnover) 
mengukur seberapa efisien Perusahaan mengelola piutang 
usahanya dan seberapa cepat modal kerja yang tertanam 
di pos piutang usaha berputar selama periode tertentu. 
Pada tahun 2024, Perusahaan mengalami penurunan dalam 
tingkat perputaran piutang usaha, dari 18,19 kali menjadi 
8,54 kali. Perubahan ini menunjukkan penurunan dalam 
pengelolaan piutang, di mana jumlah piutang yang dibayar 
oleh pelanggan lebih lambat, yang pada akhirnya dapat 
menurunkan likuiditas dan penggunaan yang kurang optimal 
modal kerja.
Selain itu, kemampuan Perusahaan dalam mencairkan 
piutang usaha (average collection period) pada tahun 2024 
lebih lambat dibandingkan tahun 2023, dengan perubahan 
dari 40,13 hari menjadi 75,99 hari. Hal ini menunjukkan 
bahwa Perusahaan kurang efektif dalam menagih piutang 
dari pelanggan, yang berdampak pada penurunan likuiditas 
dan penggunaan modal kerja.
RECEIVABLES TURNOVER RATIO
The receivables turnover ratio measures how efficiently the 
Company manages its trade receivables and how quickly 
working capital tied up in receivables is converted into cash 
over a given period. In 2024, the Company experienced a 
decline in its receivables turnover ratio, from 18.19 times 
to 8.54 times. This indicates a deterioration in receivables 
management, where payments from customers were 
received more slowly, ultimately impacting liquidity and 
leading to less optimal use of working capital.
In addition, the Company’s ability to collect its receivables, 
as reflected in the average collection period, also declined 
in 2024. The average collection period increased from 40.13 
days in 2023 to 75.99 days in 2024. This suggests that the 
Company was less effective in collecting receivables from 
customers, negatively affecting liquidity and working capital 
efficiency.

STRATEGY AND MARKET SHARE
The Company is an integral part of a corporate group with 
a proven track record in delivering high-quality projects 
across various industrial sectors. Since its establishment 
in 1983 in Samarinda and its relocation to Jakarta in 2022, 
the Company has grown to become one of the leading 
players in Indonesia’s oil and gas as well as coal-supporting 
construction services industry.
The Company’s vision, “To Grow with the Nation,” inspires us 
to continuously make meaningful contributions to Indonesia’s 
progress through the projects we undertake. Our mission is 
to deliver satisfaction to our business partners by being a 
loyal and trustworthy partner. Our motto, “A Trusted Loyal 
Partner,” serves as the guiding principle in every step of our 
business.
Our core philosophy is: “Strive for excellence, foster strong 
teamwork among employees, and uphold our commitment 
to customers and business partners.” This philosophy 
forms the foundation of our marketing strategy. The 
Company is committed to conducting its business with a 
high level of professionalism, effectiveness, and efficiency, 
while maintaining a proven reputation and an unwavering 
dedication to customer satisfaction.
With regard to our reliance on projects from the parent group, 
the Company transparently acknowledges that its marketing 
strategy is aligned with the guidance and requirements of 
the group’s central management. Nevertheless, our primary 
focus remains on delivering projects with the highest quality, 
maintaining close collaboration with other business units 
within the group, and remaining committed to long-term 
sustainability and growth.
By clearly communicating our vision, mission, and values 
in this annual report, the Company aims to strengthen 
stakeholder 
confidence 
and 
reaffirm 
our 
steadfast 
commitment to delivering significant added value in every 
project we undertake.
