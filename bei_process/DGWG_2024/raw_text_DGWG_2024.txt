Global economic growth is experiencing a divergence 
in growth patterns between countries, continuing with 
increased economic and trade fragmentation worldwide. 
In several Emerging Market Economies (EMEs) such as 
India and Indonesia, economic growth remains strong. 
This is supported by domestic demand amidst export 
constraints due to economic slowdown and global trade 
fragmentation. Global inflation is expected to decrease 
from 6.2% in 2023 to 5.2% in 2024; however, it is projected

the U.S. and several of its major trading partners, as well

Indonesia, is expected to improve, with growth rising from

growth.

of a possible decrease in the Fed Fund Rate (FFR) to 
mitigate high volatility and foreign portfolio outflows to 
EME countries, including Indonesia, in the form of bonds 
and stocks.

National Economic Performance
Indonesia’s economic performance has shown a positive 
trend in recent years and demonstrates strong resilience 
in facing the impacts of global spillovers. This is influenced 
by macroeconomic stability, strong coordination of 
macroeconomic policies, particularly fiscal policies by the 
government and monetary policies by Bank Indonesia, 
as well as Indonesia’s leadership in several international 
collaborations.
Since 2018, Indonesia’s economic growth has consistently 
reached 5%, except during the Covid-19 pandemic period. 
This growth has been accompanied by inflation that 
remains under control, averaging below 3% from 2018 to 
2023, a low fiscal deficit of under 3% of GDP, and financial 
stability reflected in banking capital adequacy ratios above 
20%. Banking credit has also grown significantly and plays 
an important role in supporting economic growth.

National Agriculture and 
Industry Sector Overview
Indonesia is the largest country by population in 
Southeast Asia, with a population of 282.48 million in 
2024. Indonesia’s population is relatively young, with the 
majority in the productive age group, where 67.9% of the 
total population is between the ages of 15-64. This provides 
Indonesia with a demographic advantage that is expected 
to drive the country’s economic growth and attract more 
foreign investment.
With abundant natural resources, Indonesia is a producer 
and exporter of key commodities such as thermal coal, 
palm oil, rubber, and more. Indonesia is also a source of 
export for agrochemical raw materials, as the country’s 
large production capacity allows local companies to 
supply both domestic and international markets. This trend 
is particularly evident in the pesticide industry, where local

of Indonesia’s workforce. Indonesia is the largest producer

cocoa, and coffee to global markets. The majority of 
farmers (68%) are smallholders operating on land of less 
than one hectare.

pesticides for both domestic consumption and export.

Industry Overview
Indonesia is one of the largest fertilizer importers in 
the world. The country imports a significant amount of 
compound fertilizers, such as NPK fertilizers, as well as 
raw materials for fertilizer production. During the review 
period, the total value of fertilizer imports grew rapidly with 
a CAGR of 7.4%, reaching Rp30.2 trillion in 2023, primarily 
driven by a significant increase in import prices in 2021 
and 2022 due to disruptions in fertilizer supply from major 
exporting countries such as China, Russia, and Belarus. 
Indonesia has a large fertilizer production industry, and 
the government has encouraged more local fertilizer 
production in recent years, as existing local production 
and imports have not been able to meet domestic demand. 
Indonesia’s fertilizer exports are relatively small compared 
to the size of the domestic consumption market, with the 
majority being urea fertilizer. In 2023, fertilizers worth 
Rp9.6 trillion were exported from Indonesia, recording a 
CAGR of 2.4% between 2019 and 2023.
The total size of the fertilizer consumption market in 
Indonesia reached Rp103.6 trillion in 2023, up from Rp58.4 
trillion in 2019. Agricultural applications accounted for 
more than a third of fertilizer sales in 2023, while other 
applications included plantations, land rehabilitation, turf 
and field management, as well as landscaping, among 
others. The majority of agricultural application users 
are smallholder farmers who have access to subsidized 
fertilizers and purchase fertilizers through distributors and 
retailers.
The fertilizer supply chain consists of producers, distributors, 
and retailers. The fertilizer supply chain for plantation 
applications differs quite significantly from agricultural 
applications. Farmers typically purchase fertilizers from 
retailers, while agricultural companies usually buy directly 
from producers in the open market through tender contracts 
and are not included in the government’s subsidy scheme. 
Typically, agricultural companies prioritize price when 
making purchasing decisions, given the large quantities 
they consume. On the other hand, farmers tend to show 
greater brand loyalty and often prefer to continue using 
brands and products they are familiar with and trust.
There are two markets for fertilizer distribution for agricultural 
applications in Indonesia: the subsidized market and the 
free market. The subsidized market distributes fertilizers 
at subsidized prices under the government’s fertilizer 
subsidy program. Fertilizers distributed in the subsidized 
market are usually non-premium base fertilizers, and

Strategi Pemasaran
Kegiatan pemasaran Perseroan dilakukan melalui dua 
model yaitu Business to Business (B2B) maupun dengan 
Business to Business to Consumer (B2B2C). Pada model 
bisnis B2B, Prinsipal Perseroan melakukan penjualan 
langsung kepada pasar perkebunan yang ada sedangkan 
untuk penjualan kepada pasar ritel (free market) 
Perseroan menggunakan model bisnis B2B2C dengan 
menjual produk-produk kepada kios tani (direct retailer) 
baik menggunakan unit usaha distribusi internal maupun 
bekerja sama dengan distributor eksternal. Hal tersebut, 
mendorong peluang produk-produk yang dihasilkan 
prinsipal Perseroan menjangkau pasar yang lebih luas 
dan dalam di seluruh wilayah di Indonesia.

Marketing Strategy
The company’s marketing activities are carried out through 
two models: Business to Business (B2B) and Business to 
Business to Consumer (B2B2C). In the B2B business 
model, the company’s principal sells directly to the existing 
plantation market, while for sales to the retail market (free 
market), the company uses the B2B2C business model by 
selling products to kiosks (direct retailers), either using its 
internal distribution units or collaborating with external 
distributors. This enables the company’s principal products 
to reach a broader and deeper market across Indonesia.
The marketing strategies implemented by the company 
include:
1.	 Establishing a solid distribution and marketing network 
to reach the retail (free market) and plantation markets 
throughout Indonesia by building strategic partnerships 
with kiosks for the free market and participating in the 
procurement processes for agricultural supply products 
in domestic agricultural/plantation companies.
2.	 Deploying 
agricultural 
experts 
(agronomists) 
proportionally in agricultural areas across Indonesia 
to socialize and demonstrate the company’s products, 
thereby encouraging demand for the company’s 
products.
3.	 Carrying out Demand Creation activities such as field 
visits (Farmers Field Day/FFD), meetings with farmers 
for socialization and promotion (Farmers Meeting/
FM), and large-scale meetings followed by various 
interesting activities (Big Farmers Meeting/Big FM).
4.	 Providing the DGW Solutions digital platform accessible 
via smartphones, which can detect plant disturbances 
and offer solutions through the company’s products. 
This strategy is one form of Demand Creation using 
advanced Information Technology.
5.	 Providing after-sales warranty services to ensure product 
safety against potential crop damage from using the 
company’s products. This service offers security to 
consumers, increasing the potential for new customers. 
The after-sales service is supported by competent quality 
assurance personnel, particularly in comprehensively 
analyzing the impact of product usage.

Basis of Dividend Distribution Policy
In accordance with the Limited Liability Company Law 
(UUPT) and the Articles of Association of the Company, 
all of the Company’s net profit, after being deducted for 
mandatory reserve provisions, may be distributed to 
shareholders as dividends, unless otherwise determined in 
the General Meeting of Shareholders (GMS). The Company 
may only distribute dividends if the Company has a positive 
retained earnings balance.
As per the prevailing regulations in Indonesia and 
the Company’s Articles of Association, the decision 
regarding dividend distribution is made through the 
approval of shareholders at the annual GMS based on the 
recommendation from the Company’s Board of Directors. 
The Company may distribute dividends in a year where 
it records net profit. The Company’s Board of Directors 
establishes the dividend distribution policy to shareholders 
by considering factors such as:
1.	 Compliance with applicable regulations;
2.	 The financial health of the Company;
3.	 Future capital adequacy targets and projections;
4.	 The Company’s future business plans and prospects;
5.	 The Company’s capital position; and
6.	 Other factors deemed relevant by the Board of 
Directors.
In the prospectus, after conducting the initial public offering 
(IPO) in January 2024, and considering the decision made 
by the shareholders at the GMS, the Company plans to 
distribute a dividend to its shareholders, with a maximum 
value of 30% of the net profit for the relevant fiscal year. 
If the GMS approves the dividend distribution, the dividends 
will be paid to all shareholders listed on the shareholders 
register as entitled to the dividend, with tax calculations 
and deductions made in accordance with applicable 
regulations.
