Tinjauan Ekonomi
Economic Overview
In 2024, the global economy was marked by uncertainty stemming 
from political tensions, trade wars, and protectionism, all of 
which constrained growth potential. Inflation showed a declining 
trend in several countries, while others experienced an increase. 
Monetary policies in advanced economies started to ease amid 
economic pressures. Global economic growth stagnated at 
3.2%. The ASEAN region had shown relatively greater resilience 
compared to the slowdown in the United States (US) and 
Europe. China faced pressure from weak domestic demand and 
a property sector crisis, while India continued to grow despite 
being shadowed by food inflation. Other global challenges 
included escalating geopolitical tensions, climate change, and 
debt pressures in developing countries. Strategic policies were 
needed to maintain stability and seize opportunities[1].
Amid these dynamics, Indonesia’s economy continued to 
demonstrate resilience, recording a growth rate of 5.03%, driven 
by strong domestic consumption, increased investment, and 
improved export performance. Key commodities such as mineral 
fuels, animal or vegetable fats and oils, as well as iron and steel 
had contributed to Indonesia’s Balance of Payments (BoP) surplus, 
which reached USD2.2 billion[2].

Industrial Overview
Pada tahun 2024, konsumsi batubara global mencatatkan rekor 
baru diperkirakan mencapai 8,77 miliar ton, didorong oleh krisis 
gas global akibat invasi Rusia ke Ukraina. Peningkatan permintaan 
terutama berasal dari Tiongkok dan India, yang masing-masing 
mengonsumsi 4,9 miliar ton dan 1,3 miliar ton. Sementara itu, 
negara-negara maju, seperti Amerika Serikat dan Uni Eropa 
mencatat penurunan konsumsi seiring transisi menuju energi 
terbarukan. Meski demikian, sektor kelistrikan tetap menjadi 
penyumbang utama, yakni dua pertiga dari total konsumsi 
batubara global [1].
Sejalan dengan perkembangan global tersebut, industri batubara 
Indonesia juga menunjukkan kinerja signifikan sepanjang 2024. 
Produksi nasional mencapai 836,13 juta ton, melampaui target yang 
ditetapkan sebesar 710 juta ton. Hal ini mencerminkan kapasitas 
produksi yang semakin meningkat untuk memenuhi kebutuhan 
domestik maupun ekspor, sekaligus memperkuat ketahanan energi 
nasional dan kontribusi terhadap perekonomian Indonesia [2].
Pemenuhan Domestic Market Obligation (DMO) juga mencatatkan 
kinerja positif dengan realisasi sebesar 232,64 juta ton, melebihi 
target sebesar 181,28 juta ton. Pencapaian ini menunjukkan 
komitmen Indonesia dalam memastikan pasokan batubara bagi 
kebutuhan dalam negeri, khususnya pembangkit listrik yang 
masih mengandalkan batubara sebagai sumber energi utama [2].
Harga Batubara Acuan (HBA) Indonesia mengalami fluktuasi 
sepanjang tahun. Pada April 2024 tercatat sebesar USD121,13 per 
ton, turun menjadi USD114,06 per ton pada Mei, lalu meningkat 
menjadi USD131,17 per ton pada Juli. Rata-rata HBA tahun 2024 
tercatat di kisaran USD117,76 per ton, mencerminkan dinamika 
pasar global dan pengaruh faktor eksternal [3].
Pemerintah Indonesia terus memperkuat fokus pada program 
hilirisasi batubara, khususnya pengembangan industri pengolahan 
batubara menjadi gas dan produk turunan lainnya. Inisiatif 
ini diharapkan dapat memberikan nilai tambah, mendukung 
kemandirian energi nasional, membuka lapangan kerja baru, serta 
meningkatkan daya saing Indonesia di pasar global [4].

In 2024, global coal consumption booked a new record, estimated 
at 8.77 billion tons, driven by the global gas crisis following 
Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. The surge in demand primarily 
came from China and India, which consumed 4.9 billion tons and 
1.3 billion tons, respectively. Meanwhile, developed countries 
such as the United States and the European Union recorded 
a decline in consumption in line with their transition to renewable 
energy. Nonetheless, the power generation sector remained 
the main contributor, accounting for two-thirds of total global 
coal consumption [1].
In line with these global developments, Indonesia’s coal industry 
also demonstrated significant performance throughout 2024. 
National production reached 836.13 million tons, surpassing 
the set target of 710 million tons. This reflected an increasing 
production capacity to meet both domestic and export demands, 
while also strengthening national energy security and contributing 
to Indonesia’s economy [2].

showed positive results, with actual realization reaching 
232.64 million tons, exceeding the target of 181.28 million tons. 
This achievement reflected Indonesia’s commitment to ensuring 
coal supply for domestic needs, particularly for power plants that 
still relied heavily on coal as a primary energy source [2].
Indonesia’s Reference Coal Price (HBA) fluctuated throughout 
the year. It was recorded at USD121.13 per ton in April 2024, 
decreased to USD114.06 per ton in May, and rose again to USD131.17 
per ton in July. The average HBA for 2024 stood at approximately 
USD117.76 per ton, reflecting global market dynamics and the 
influence of external factors [3].
The Indonesian government continued to strengthen its focus on 
coal downstreaming programs, particularly in the development 
of industries that process coal into gas and other derivative 
products. This initiative was expected to add value, support 
national energy independence, create new job opportunities, and 
enhance Indonesia’s competitiveness in the global market [4].

Share Ownership Program by Employees 
and/or Management
As part of efforts to strengthen the relationship with employees, 
the Company implemented the Employee Stock Allocation (ESA) 
program on 8 November 2020, in accordance with Regulation No. 
IX.A.7 – Attachment to the Chairman of Bapepam-LK Decision 
No. Kep-691/BL/2011 on the Securities Booking and Allotment in 
a Public Offering. In this program, the Company allocated up to 
10% of the total shares offered to the public for distribution to 
employees.
The ESA program is intended for all permanent employees 
of the Company and its Subsidiaries who are registered in 
the employment system, with the exception of the Board of 
Commissioners and Board of Directors, as they have already 
received separate stock allocations in the public offering. 
A total of 26,150,000 shares were allocated for this program at 
a price of Rp1,950 per share, and the program was valid for five years 
from its implementation. Following the conclusion of the program, 
in 2024, the Company no longer implemented the ESA, so 
there is no additional information to be disclosed in this Annual 
Report.

The adjustment of the Reference Coal Price (HBA) formula to 
the HBA Coal Index Minerba (CIM) by the Ministry of ESDM resulted 
in a decline in the average HBA throughout 2024. Nevertheless, 
coal selling prices were still considered favorable, enabling 
the Company to continue recording a positive profit, although 
lower than the previous year.
Management Assessment to Manage 
Significantly Affecting Potential
Management conducted an assessment of the Company’s ability 
to manage potential risks that might affect its business continuity 
in the future. This assessment was based on well-prepared 
business planning as outlined in the 2024 development strategy. 
The results indicated that there were no material uncertainties 
that could cast significant doubt on the Company’s ability to 
continue its operations.

Assessment

the following assumptions.

Financial performance.
2.	 Capital structure.
3.	 Regulation.
4.	 Various risks, such as risks of commodity price, credit, foreign 
currency, business interruption, interest rates, and liquidity.
