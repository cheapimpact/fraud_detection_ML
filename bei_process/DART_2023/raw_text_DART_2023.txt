ECONOMIC AND INDUSTRIAL REVIEW
Global Economic Overview
Global economic conditions throughout 2023 will still face 
various challenges and uncertainties. The ongoing conﬂict 
between Russia and Ukraine has caused an increase in 
food and energy prices which has resulted in global inﬂation 
remaining high. This has forced world central banks to 
maintain high interest rate policies for a long time as a 
response to reduce the surge in inﬂation. These various 
challenges have caused global economic growth to slow 
down again.
Based on data from the 2023 Indonesian Economic Report 
from Bank Indonesia, after recording high growth of 3.5% in 
2022, world economic growth in 2023 is predicted to fall to 
3.0%, and will continue to slow to 2.8% in 2024. With the 
hope of easing geopolitical tensions, the world economy is 
predicted to improve again with growth of 3.0% in 2025.
In general, the future performance and prospects of the 
global economy will be faced by the following ﬁve things that 
each country needs to be aware of, anticipate and respond 
to appropriately. First, global economic growth is predicted 
to weaken and be accompanied by increasingly widening 
growth divergences between countries. Second, increasing 
geopolitical tensions are pushing energy prices to remain 
high and food prices to increase, resulting in a slow decline 
in global inﬂation. Third, to control inﬂation which is still high, 
the high interest rate policy is predicted to persist for a 
longer period of time. Fourth, continued capital outﬂows 
from developing countries to developed countries and to 
more liquid assets due to high interest rates in developed 
countries and uncertainty in global ﬁnancial markets. Fifth, 
the US dollar exchange rate will remain strong and exert 
weakening pressure on various world currencies. These 
various challenges ultimately require strengthening policy 
responses to mitigate negative impacts on economic 
resilience in developing countries, including Indonesia.
National Economic Overview
In the midst of global economic conditions which are still 
facing various challenges and slowdowns, the Indonesian 
economy has shown strong and stable resilience. Based on 
data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), Indonesia's 
economic growth in 2023 was recorded at 5.05%, a slight 
decrease compared to growth in 2022 which was 5.31%.

Property Industry Overview

important factor in the recovery of the tourism, hospitality

has a positive impact on retail occupancy levels which will 
begin to increase in 2023. However, this must be 
accompanied by various interesting strategies and 
breakthroughs by retail owners in order to increase levels.

competitors. Meanwhile, the ofﬁce sector will still

Office Sector
In general, demand in the ofﬁce sector in 2023 will increase 
more than in 2022. This is a positive signal that more and 
more business sectors need new ofﬁce space in line with 
improving economic growth in Indonesia. In addition, the 
end of the pandemic emergency has encouraged many 
companies to start working from the ofﬁce again, thereby 
increasing demand for ofﬁce space. 
However, it is estimated that the ofﬁce sector will still be 
faced with the same challenges, such as the current large 
number of ofﬁce spaces that have not been absorbed due to 
the impact of the pandemic and also the additional supply 
coming from new ofﬁce buildings that will be completed in 
the future.
In 2023, there will only be one ofﬁce building in the Jakarta 
CBD area whose construction has been completed, 
increasing the total supply of ofﬁce space in the Jakarta 
CBD area to 7.4 million M2. It is estimated that in the 
coming years the number of new ofﬁce buildings that will be 
completed will begin to be limited and this is a positive 
signal for the ofﬁce sector in the future.
As for ofﬁces outside the Jakarta CBD, in 2023 there will be 
one new ofﬁce building completed, increasing the total 
supply of ofﬁce space to 3.75 million M2 or an increase of 
2% compared to 2022. It is estimated that there will be 
several new ofﬁce buildings completed which will increase 
the supply of ofﬁce space will increase signiﬁcantly in 2024.
The ofﬁce occupancy rate in 2023 will experience a slight 
decline. The average occupancy rate in the Jakarta CBD 
was recorded at 73.5% in 2023, a slight decrease of around 
1% compared to 2022. Meanwhile, the average occupancy 
rate outside the CBD was recorded at 72.5% in 2023 or 
experienced a slight increase compared to previous year. 
With the addition of quite large new ofﬁce space and also 
the general election in 2024, it will still be a challenge for the 
ofﬁce sector in the future.

CAPABILITY TO REPAY DEBT AND COLLECTIBILITY LEVEL
The Company always strives to maintain the level of 
ﬁnancial health so that the ability to pay short-term and 
long-term debt is maintained. Liquidity ratio is used to 
measure the ability of Company to pay off the debts and to 
measure the Company’s ability to meet all its obligations, 
solvability ratio is used.
Liquidity Ratio
Liquidity ratio refers to current ratio which is the level of the 
Company’s ability to meet Short-Term Liabiities as 
measured by the ratio between Current Assets and 
Short-Term Liabilities. This current ratio showed the level of 
liquidity of the Company in fulﬁlling its obligations with 
available Current Assets available.
The Company's liquidity ratio in 2023 is 0.2 times or an 
increase compared to 2022 which was 0.1 times.
Solvabilty Ratio
Solvability ratio demonstrates the Company’s ability to 
settle all obligations which are measured by comparing the 
Total Liabilities against the Total Equity and the Total 
Liabilities to Total Assets.
In 2023, the Company's solvency ratio based on the 
comparison between Total Liabilities and Total Assets was 
recorded at 72.5% or an increase compared to 2022 which 
was 67.8%.
Meanwhile, the Company's solvency ratio based on the 
comparison between Total Liabilities and Total Equity in 
2023 was recorded at 263.4% or an increase compared to 
2022 which was 210.4%.
Receivables Collectability Rate
The level of collectibility of receivables shows the speed of 
collection of receivables from sales. At the end of 2023, the 
Company's Accounts Receivables were recorded at Rp17.5 
billion, a decrease of 77.5% compared to the position at the 
end of 2022 of Rp51.2 billion. This reﬂects the increasingly 
better level of collectibility of the Company's receivables. As 
one of the most prominent property companies who have 
been in the property business for more than 40 years, the 
Company has a track record of good collectibility rate. The 
Company ensures this by establishing method of payment 
schemes and collection policy that enable the buyers and 
tenants to render payments in a timely manner. With such 
manner, the Company does not experience substantial 
problems in collecting its receivables.
