Laporan Tahunan 2023
In 2023, global economic conditions remain precarious 
due to the unstable global environment and ongoing 
geopolitical tensions affecting economic growth and 
weakening trade activity. The global economic growth 
is slowing down this year to 2.1% (down from 3.1% in 
2022). However in this challenging global environment, 
Indonesia’s economy remains relatively stable and its 
economic growth is normalize at 5.0% this year (down 
from 5.3% in 2022) as the commodity boom wanes and 
domestic demand normalizes following the post-pandemic 
jump last year. Inflation is remain low at 2.61% in 2023. 
This is the result of consistency of monetary policy and 
close synergy between Bank Indonesia and Indonesia 
government.
The robustness of Indonesia’s manufacturing sector has 
seen the sector remain the largest industry contributor 
to Indonesia’s gross domestic product (GDP), making up 
18.3% (2022) of total GDP, driven by strong domestic 
consumption. The leading manufacturing sub-sectors 
include basic metal, machinery, leather and footwear, 
textiles, transportation tools, electronics, pulp and paper, 
and food & beverage. These sub-sectors support  other 
down-stream industries such as energy, information 
technology, communication, transportation, and logistics. 
In July 2023, Indonesia reacquired its status as an ‘upper-
middle-income country’, according to the World Bank, 
putting it back on track to pursue its high-income status 
goal – an ambition for 2045 and the centenary of 
independence.
Pada tahun 2023, kondisi ekonomi global masih belum 
stabil karena lingkungan global yang tidak stabil dan 
ketegangan geopolitik yang sedang berlangsung yang 
memengaruhi pertumbuhan ekonomi dan melemahnya 
aktivitas perdagangan. Pertumbuhan ekonomi global 
melambat tahun ini menjadi 2,1% (turun dari 3,1% di 
tahun 2022). Namun, dalam kondisi global yang penuh 
tantangan ini, ekonomi Indonesia tetap relatif stabil dan 
pertumbuhan ekonominya kembali normal di 5,0% tahun 
ini (turun dari 5,3% di tahun 2022) seiring dengan 
berkurangnya lonjakan komoditas dan permintaan domestik 
yang kembali normal setelah lonjakan pascapandemi 
tahun lalu. Inflasi tetap stabil di angka 2,61% pada tahun 
2023. Hal ini disebabkan oleh konsistensi kebijakan moneter 
dan eratnya sinergi Bank Indonesia dengan Pemerintah.
Kuatnya sektor manufaktur Indonesia telah membuat 
sektor ini tetap menjadi kontributor industri terbesar 
terhadap produk domestik bruto (PDB) Indonesia, 
menyumbang 18,3% (2022) dari total PDB, didorong oleh 
konsumsi domestik yang kuat. Sub-sektor manufaktur 
utama termasuk logam dasar, mesin, kulit dan alas kaki, 
tekstil, alat transportasi, elektronik, pulp dan kertas, dan 
makanan & minuman. Sub-sektor ini mendukung industri 
hilir lainnya seperti energi, teknologi informasi, komunikasi, 
transportasi, dan logistik. Pada bulan Juli 2023, Indonesia 
memperoleh kembali statusnya sebagai 'negara 
berpenghasilan menengah ke atas', menurut Bank Dunia, 
yang menempatkan Indonesia kembali ke jalur yang 
tepat untuk mengejar target status negara berpenghasilan 
tinggi - sebuah ambisi untuk tahun 2045 dan seratus 
tahun kemerdekaan.

2023 Annual Report 
Goodyear is one of the world’s leading tire companies, 
with one of the most recognizable brand names. Goodyear 
manufactures its products in 57 facilities in 23 countries 
and has operations in most regions of the world. Its two 
Innovation Centers in Akron, Ohio and Colmar-Berg, 
Luxemburg strive to develop state-of-the-art products 
and services that set the technology and performance 
standard for the industry.
Meanwhile, The Company is the first Company that 
manufactures, exports, imports and markets tires, inner 
tubes, flaps and rubber derivative products in Indonesia. 
Thus, it becomes the Company’s strength to be one of 
the largest tire manufacture in Indonesia.
The Company’s tire products are distributed through domestic 
and foreign distribution channels. The Company collaborates 
with a network of outlets in major cities to deliver high 
quality products and services to customers, and give 
communication access for all information about products 
and services as well as the mechanism of filing complaints.
The Company is engaged in the automotive tire 
manufacturing industry with wide-range market, such 
as the large fleet and Original Equipment (OE) segments. 
There are 2 (two) market segments of the Company’s 
product categories, which are passenger vehicle tires 
and commercial vehicles tires.

Dividend Policy
Kebijakan Dividen
Amount of dividends paid by the Company is adjusted 
to the Company’s performance. In line with applicable 
law in Indonesia, dividend distribution decisions are 
made through a shareholder decision at the Annual 
General Meeting of Shareholders (Annual GMS) based 
on the Board of Directors’ recommendation, subject to 
the approval of the Board of Commissioners. The Company 
may distribute dividends in years in which the Company 
obtains retained earnings that has not been determined 
for its use. Unless otherwise specified, dividends are 
paid in Rupiah. In 2023, the Company decided not to
