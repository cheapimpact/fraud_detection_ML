Tahun 2024 mencatat berlanjutnya tekanan eksternal terhadap 
stabilitas ekonomi global, yang dipicu oleh dinamika geopolitik di 
berbagai kawasan. Laporan dari World Economic Insight Forum 
mencatat bahwa konflik berkepanjangan antara Rusia dan Ukraina, 
serta meningkatnya ketegangan di kawasan Timur Tengah, telah 
mengganggu kelancaran rantai pasok energi dan pangan dunia. 
Kondisi ini turut mendorong kenaikan harga minyak dan gas alam 
secara global, meskipun belum mencapai level tertinggi seperti 
yang terjadi pada awal tahun 2022.
Situasi ini memperburuk kondisi inflasi global, terutama di 
kawasan Uni Eropa yang sangat bergantung pada pasokan energi 
impor. World Economic Outlook Update menempatkan proyeksi 
pertumbuhan global pada kisaran 3,2% untuk 2024—suatu angka 
yang mencerminkan tekanan simultan dari disrupsi geopolitik, 
perlambatan perdagangan internasional, dan konsumsi rumah 
tangga yang menurun di banyak negara maju.
Amerika Serikat menutup tahun dengan pertumbuhan sebesar 2,8%, 
sedikit di bawah capaian tahun sebelumnya. Ketidakpastian arah 
kebijakan fiskal pasca pemilihan umum—terutama terkait suku 
bunga dan belanja negara—membuat investor cenderung menahan 
ekspansi. Sementara itu, kawasan Eropa belum berhasil keluar dari 
fase stagnasi, dengan tekanan inflasi dan krisis energi yang belum 
terpecahkan. Dari Asia, Tiongkok mengalami pelambatan karena 
ketergantungan terhadap permintaan eksternal serta konsumsi 
domestik yang belum sepenuhnya pulih pasca pandemi.
Berbeda dengan tren global, Indonesia menunjukkan daya tahan 
yang cukup solid. Data dari Kementerian Keuangan dan Bank 
Indonesia menunjukkan pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional sebesar 
5,03% pada 2024, hanya mengalami koreksi ringan dari 5,05% di 
tahun sebelumnya. Faktor stabilnya harga barang kebutuhan pokok 
serta penguatan belanja pemerintah menjadi penopang utama. 
Selain itu, inflasi yang terkendali memberikan ruang bagi konsumsi 
masyarakat untuk tetap tumbuh secara moderat.
Distribusi kontribusi terhadap Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) 
didominasi oleh Lapangan Usaha Industri Pengolahan sebesar 
18,98%; diikuti oleh Perdagangan Besar dan Eceran, Reparasi 
Mobil dan Sepeda Motor sebesar 13,07%; Pertanian, Kehutanan, 
dan Perikanan sebesar 12,61%; Konstruksi sebesar 10,09%; serta 
Pertambangan dan Penggalian sebesar 9,15%. Peranan kelima 
lapangan usaha ini dalam perekonomian Indonesia mencapai 
63,90%. 
The year 2024 marks the continuation of external pressures on 
global economic stability, driven by geopolitical dynamics in various 
regions. A report from the World Economic Insight Forum notes that 
the ongoing conflict between Russia and Ukraine, as well as rising 
tensions in the Middle East, have disrupted global energy and food 
supply chains. This situation has also contributed to the global rise 
in oil and natural gas prices, although they have not reached the 
peak levels seen in early 2022.  
This situation has worsened global inflation, particularly in the 
European Union, which heavily relies on imported energy supplies. 
The World Economic Outlook Update projects global growth at 
around 3.2% for 2024— a figure that reflects simultaneous 
pressures from geopolitical disruptions, a slowdown in international 
trade, and declining household consumption in many developed 
nations.  
The United States closed the year with a growth rate of 2.8%, 
slightly below the previous year’s performance. The uncertainty 
regarding fiscal policy post-election, particularly regarding interest 
rates and government spending, has led investors to hold back on 
expansion. Meanwhile, the European region has not yet managed to 
emerge from stagnation, facing unresolved inflationary pressures 
and an energy crisis. From Asia, China experienced a slowdown due 
to its dependence on external demand and domestic consumption 
that has not fully recovered post-pandemic.  
In contrast to global trends, Indonesia has shown considerable 
resilience. Data from the Ministry of Finance and Bank Indonesia 
show national economic growth of 5.03% in 2024, experiencing 
only a slight correction from 5.05% in the previous year. The stability 
of staple food prices and the strengthening of government spending 
have been key supporting factors. Additionally, controlled inflation 
has provided room for moderate consumer growth.  
The distribution of contributions to Gross Domestic Product (GDP) 
is dominated by the Manufacturing Sector, contributing 18.98%, 
followed by Wholesale and Retail Trade, Repair of Motor Vehicles 
and Motorcycles at 13.07%, Agriculture, Forestry, and Fisheries 
at 12.61%, Construction at 10.09%, and Mining and Quarrying 
at 9.15%. These five sectors collectively contribute 63.90% to 
Indonesia’s economy.

Outlook 2024, tren pertumbuhan industri nasional menunjukkan

In the year-end report released by Indonesia Industrial Outlook 
2024, the trend of national industrial growth shows a relatively 
stable pattern, with positive performance in several key sectors. 
Domestic consumption activity, which remains a mainstay of the 
economy, grew by 4.98% year-on-year in the fourth quarter of 2024. 
This sector contributed approximately 2.60 percentage points to 
total national growth.  
Export performance also showed a promising recovery. The 
final quarter recorded a 7.63% increase in exports of goods and 
services compared to the same period last year. This indicates 
that the national industrial sector, despite external pressures, has 
remained competitive in the global market through efficiency and 
product diversification, particularly from the mining, CPO, and light 
electronics sectors.  
Other sectors such as manufacturing, construction services, and 
information and communication technology have shown consistent 
improvement. Innovations in digital adoption and the acceleration 
of supply chain transformation have become key drivers in 
maintaining business continuity and growth. The resilience of the 
industrial sector in facing global disruptions is also aided by the 
adaptability of business players and government stimulus, including 
fiscal incentives and MSME financing support.  
Overall, 2024 became a reflective moment for economic players in 
Indonesia: although global challenges increased, domestic resilience 
and policy continuity became crucial assets in maintaining market 
trust and driving inclusive growth.

Tinjauan per Segmen Usaha 
Review per Business Segment
Perseroan menargetkan posisi sebagai produsen terdepan di 
Indonesia dalam kategori popok bayi, pembalut wanita, dan popok 
dewasa. Saat ini, ketiga kategori tersebut telah mencapai status 
sebagai pemimpin pasar. Berdasarkan nilai pasar ritel, pangsa pasar 
popok bayi mencapai 36,7%, pembalut wanita 45,9%, dan popok 
dewasa 36,9% pada tahun 2024.
Sebagai bagian dari Unicharm Corporation (UC Jepang), struktur 
kepemilikan saham Perseroan mengalami perubahan pasca-IPO, 
dengan UC Jepang menguasai 59,2%, PT APP Purinusa Ekapersada 
20,8%, kepemilikan publik 20%. Dengan dukungan UC Jepang, 
Perseroan terus mengembangkan teknologi non-woven canggih yang 
meningkatkan daya serap serta kenyamanan produk kebersihan 
sekali pakai, seperti MamyPoko, Charm, dan Lifree. Komitmen 
terhadap inovasi, keamanan, dan kualitas menjadi faktor utama 
dalam mempertahankan kepercayaan konsumen dan memperkuat 
reputasi merek di pasar.
Kategori produk inti Perseroan adalah:
• 	
Diapers: Pendapatan segmen diaper Perseroan pada tahun 
yang berakhir pada 31 Desember 2024 dan 31 Desember 2023 
adalah 74,6% dan 77,9%.
• 	
Non diapers: Pendapatan segmen non diaper Perseroan pada 
tahun yang berakhir pada 31 Desember 2024 dan 31 Desember 
2023 adalah 25,4% dan 22,1%.
The Company aims to establish itself as the leading manufacturer 
in Indonesia in the categories of baby diapers, feminine sanitary 
napkins, and adult diapers. Currently, all three categories hold market 
leadership positions. Based on retail market value, the market share 
for baby diapers reached 36.7%, feminine hygiene products 45.9%, 
and adult diapers 36.9% in 2024.
As part of Unicharm Corporation (UC Japan), the Company’s 
ownership structure changed following the IPO, with UC Japan 
holding 59.2%, PT APP Purinusa Ekapersada holding 20.8%, and 
public ownership accounting for 20%. With support from UC Japan, 
the Company continues to develop advanced non-woven technology 
that enhances the absorbency and comfort of disposable hygiene 
products such as MamyPoko, Charm, and Lifree. The Company’s 
commitment to innovation, safety, and quality remains a key factor 
in maintaining consumer trust and strengthening its brand reputation 
in the market.
The Company’s core product categories are:
• 	
Diapers: Revenue from the Company’s diaper segment for the 
years ending December 31, 2024, and December 31, 2023, 
was 74.6% and 77.9%, respectively.
• 	
Non-diapers: Revenue from the Company’s non-diaper 
segment for the years ending December 31, 2024, and 
December 31, 2023, was 25.4% and 22.1%, respectively.
Prospek Usaha
Business Prospects 
Perseroan mengandalkan jaringan distribusi nasional yang 
luas, terdiri dari 101 distributor independen pihak ketiga, yang 
mencakup 98 kota dan 416 kabupaten di seluruh Indonesia. Dengan 
memberikan hak eksklusif kepada distributor di wilayah operasinya 
masing-masing, Perseroan memastikan efisiensi distribusi dan 
meminimalkan persaingan internal. Pendekatan ini juga memberikan 
wawasan pasar yang lebih dalam untuk meningkatkan strategi 
bisnis.
Produk Perseroan didistribusikan melalui tiga jalur utama:
1.	
Perdagangan umum – mencakup toko kelontong, kios, dan 
toko semi permanen, yang menyumbang 59,4% dari total 
penjualan domestik pada tahun 2024.
2.	
Perdagangan modern – terdiri dari supermarket, hipermarket, 
minimarket, dan toko serba ada, dengan kontribusi 40,6% dari 
total penjualan domestik.
3.	
Jalur lainnya – termasuk e-commerce (Lazada, Shopee, 
Sociolla), ekspor ke Australia, Thailand, Malaysia, Tiongkok, 
India, dan Vietnam, serta penjualan langsung ke rumah sakit, 
klinik, dan apotek.
The Company relies on a vast national distribution network 
consisting of 101 independent third-party distributors, covering 98 
cities and 416 districts across Indonesia. By granting exclusive rights 
to distributors in their respective operational areas, the Company 
ensures efficient distribution while minimizing internal competition. 
This approach also provides deeper market insights to enhance 
business strategies.  
The Company’s products are distributed through three main channels: 
1. 	 General trade – includes small retail stores, kiosks, and semi-
permanent shops, contributing 59.4% of total domestic sales in 
2024.  
2. 	 Modern trade – consists of supermarkets, hypermarkets, 
minimarkets, and convenience stores, accounting for 40.6% of 
total domestic sales.  
3. 	 Other channels – including e-commerce platforms (Lazada, 
Shopee, Sociolla), exports to Australia, Thailand, Malaysia, 
China, India, and Vietnam, as well as direct sales to hospitals, 
clinics, and pharmacies.

Filosofi manajemen Grup Unicharm selalu menjadi landasan 
utama bagi Perseroan dalam menciptakan produk dan layanan 
berkualitas tinggi yang berfokus pada peningkatan kualitas hidup 
pelanggan. Di tengah persaingan pasar yang semakin ketat, 
Perseroan terus berkomitmen untuk menjaga keunggulan dengan 
mengimplementasikan filosofi “NOLA & DOLA” (Necessity of Life with 
Activities & Dreams of Life with Activities). Filosofi ini sudah menjadi 
inti dari strategi Grup Unicharm sejak sebelum Perseroan berdiri 
pada 1997, dan kini menjadi pondasi dalam terus mengembangkan 
teknologi inovatif pada bahan non-woven serta material penyerap 
yang lebih efisien.
Perseroan menerapkan strategi bisnis sebagai berikut:
1. 	 Ekspansi Penjualan dan Pangsa Pasar di Seluruh 
Segmen
	
Perseroan memiliki peluang besar untuk tumbuh dalam 
segmen popok bayi, pembalut wanita, dan popok dewasa. 
Dengan 
memanfaatkan 
posisi 
strategisnya 
di 
pasar, 
Perseroan berupaya memperkuat pangsa pasar dengan terus 
menghadirkan inovasi, seperti kapas kecantikan, masker, 
dan produk perawatan hewan peliharaan. Dengan fokus 
pada optimalisasi manufaktur, operasional, dan pemasaran, 
Perseroan berupaya mempertahankan kepemimpinan pasar 
serta memenuhi permintaan konsumen yang terus meningkat.
	
	
Dalam segmen pembalut wanita, meningkatnya permintaan 
terhadap produk premium mendorong Perseroan untuk 
menghadirkan berbagai varian, seperti Charm Maxi, dengan 
fitur kenyamanan, varian malam, desain langsing, serta pilihan 
beraroma. Keberagaman produk ini memastikan bahwa 
Perseroan dapat memenuhi kebutuhan pasar modern yang 
semakin berorientasi pada produk berkualitas tinggi.
	
Untuk 
meningkatkan 
kesadaran 
konsumen, 
Perseroan 
merancang kampanye pemasaran yang memperlihatkan 
bagaimana produk popok dewasa mampu meningkatkan 
kualitas hidup pelanggan, terutama lansia.
	
Dalam kategori Perawatan Hewan Peliharaan (Pet Care), 
Perseroan berupaya menciptakan pasar baru dengan 
menghadirkan produk inovatif yang mendukung kesejahteraan 
hewan peliharaan. Sejak 2022, Perseroan telah memperluas 
jangkauannya di pasar ini melalui produk perawatan kebersihan 
hewan.
2. 	 Peningkatan Kesadaran Konsumen melalui Strategi 
Pemasaran
	
Perseroan menerapkan strategi pemasaran yang bertujuan 
untuk meningkatkan kesadaran merek dengan mengandalkan 
berbagai platform promosi, mulai dari iklan televisi, kampanye 
digital, hingga promosi langsung di toko. Target utama Perseroan 
adalah konsumen kelas menengah ke atas di kota-kota besar, 
yang menunjukkan peningkatan permintaan terhadap produk 
berkualitas tinggi.
The Unicharm Group’s management philosophy has always served 
as the foundation for the Company in creating high-quality products 
and services that focus on improving customers’ quality of life. Amid 
increasingly intense market competition, the Company remains 
committed to maintaining its competitive edge by implementing 
the philosophy of “NOLA & DOLA” (Necessity of Life with Activities 
& Dreams of Life with Activities). This philosophy has been the core 
of the Unicharm Group’s strategy even before the Company was 
established in 1997, and today it continues to be the foundation 
for developing innovative technologies in non-woven materials and 
more efficient absorbent materials.
The Company implements the following business strategies:  
1. 	 Expansion of Sales and Market Share Across All 
Segments  
	
	
The Company has substantial growth opportunities in the baby 
diaper, feminine sanitary napkin, and adult diaper segments. By 
capitalizing on its strategic market position, the Company aims 
to strengthen its market share through continuous innovation, 
including beauty cotton, facial masks, and pet care products. 
With a focus on optimizing manufacturing, operations, and 
marketing, the Company strives to maintain market leadership 
while addressing the growing consumer demand.
	
In the feminine hygiene product segment, the increasing 
demand for premium offerings has prompted the Company 
to introduce a range of variants, such as Charm Maxi, which 
features comfort-enhancing attributes, night-time options, slim 
designs, and scented choices. This product diversity ensures 
the Company’s ability to meet the modern market’s rising 
demand for high-quality products.
	
To raise consumer awareness, the Company has launched 
marketing campaigns that highlight how adult diapers can 
significantly enhance the quality of life for elderly customers.
	
In the Pet Care category, the Company seeks to create a new 
market by introducing innovative products that support pet 
welfare. Since 2022, the Company has expanded its reach in 
this segment through pet hygiene care products.  
2. 	 Increasing Consumer Awareness Through Marketing 
Strategies  
	
The Company implements marketing strategies aimed at 
enhancing brand awareness through various promotional 
platforms, 
including 
television 
advertisements, 
digital 
campaigns, and in-store promotions. The Company’s primary 
target is the upper-middle-class consumer segment in major 
cities, which has shown a growing demand for high-quality 
products.

Perseroan terus berinvestasi dalam riset dan pengembangan

Perseroan terus mengoptimalkan efisiensi operasional dengan 
mengurangi limbah produksi, meningkatkan otomatisasi, 
serta menerapkan strategi pengendalian biaya. Dengan 
memanfaatkan posisi dominan UC Jepang dalam rantai pasok 
global, Perseroan mampu mengendalikan harga bahan baku, 
mengurangi sisa bahan yang tidak memenuhi standar, serta 
menekan biaya operasional dan administrasi.
	
To add value, the Company is gradually shifting consumer 
demand from the economic segment to the premium segment. 
For example, standard baby diapers are now positioned within 
the premium category to enhance product appeal.  
3. 	 Optimizing Distribution Channels and Strengthening the 
Network  
	
The Company relies on its distributor network as the backbone 
for distributing products to general trade and modern retail 
across Indonesia. Long-term relationships with distributors 
enable better communication to support marketing strategies 
and optimize product distribution across market segments.  
	
	
The Company also continues to refine its distribution channels 
by ensuring efficiency in delivery processes, including direct 
shipments from production facilities to modern distribution 
centers to expedite product availability in the market.  
4. 	 Continuous Product Innovation  
	
The Company continues to invest in research and product 
development in collaboration with UC Japan to ensure that 
its products remain relevant to market trends. Research is 
conducted through customer surveys, distributor interviews, 
and lifestyle analysis of Indonesian consumers.  
	
In the baby diaper category, innovation focuses on improving 
softness and absorbency. Meanwhile, in the feminine sanitary 
napkin segment, product development emphasizes shape, 
length, and enhanced absorbency for maximum nighttime 
protection.  
5. 	 Expansion of Production Capacity  
	
To support business growth, the Company plans to increase 
production capacity by investing in new machinery over the 
next three years, particularly in the adult diaper and feminine 
sanitary napkin categories. These new machines will improve 
production efficiency and allow for the customization of new 
product specifications that could not be achieved with previous 
machines.  
6. 	 Operational Efficiency and Cost Control  
	
The Company continuously optimizes operational efficiency 
by reducing production waste, increasing automation, and 
implementing cost control strategies. By leveraging UC Japan’s 
dominant position in the global supply chain, the Company can 
manage raw material costs, minimize substandard material 
waste, and reduce operational and administrative expenses.

Laporan Tahunan | 2024 | Annual Report 
PT Uni-Charm Indonesia Tbk
Perseroan telah melakukan evaluasi menyeluruh terhadap berbagai 
faktor yang dapat mempengaruhi kelangsungan usaha dalam tahun 
buku terakhir. Berdasarkan hasil analisis yang tercantum dalam 
Laporan Direksi dan Laporan Dewan Komisaris, tidak ditemukan 
aspek material yang berpotensi mengancam operasional Perseroan.
Dewan Komisaris dan Direksi juga telah menguraikan prospek bisnis 
secara komprehensif berdasarkan kinerja tahun buku sebelumnya. 
Sebagai langkah antisipatif, Perseroan terus menerapkan strategi 
manajemen risiko guna memitigasi potensi dampak dari perubahan 
kondisi ekonomi yang tidak terduga. Berdasarkan hasil peninjauan 
risiko tahun 2024, manajemen meyakini bahwa tidak ada faktor 
signifikan yang dapat mengganggu keberlanjutan operasional 
Perseroan. Informasi lebih rinci mengenai kebijakan Manajemen 
Risiko dapat ditemukan dalam Laporan Tahunan Perseroan.
The Company has conducted a comprehensive evaluation of various 
factors that may affect business continuity in the most recent fiscal 
year. Based on the analysis outlined in the Board of Directors’ Report 
and the Board of Commissioners’ Report, no material aspects were 
identified that could potentially threaten the Company’s operations.  
The Board of Commissioners and the Board of Directors have also 
outlined the business outlook comprehensively based on the previous 
fiscal year’s performance. As a precautionary measure, the Company 
continues to implement risk management strategies to mitigate 
potential impacts from unforeseen economic conditions. Based on the 
2024 risk review, management believes that there are no significant 
factors that could disrupt the Company’s operational sustainability. 
More detailed information regarding the Risk Management Policy can 
be found in the Company’s Annual Report.
