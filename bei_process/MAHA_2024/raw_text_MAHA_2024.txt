PEREKONOMIAN DUNIA
Perekonomian global cenderung melambat 
pada tahun 2024 dengan pertumbuhan yang 
tidak merata antarnegara. Ekonomi negara 
maju seperti Amerika Serikat bertumbuh positif 
sejalan membaiknya konsumsi dan turunnya 
angka pengangguran. Selain itu, prospek inflasi 
lebih rendah yang memicu Bank Sentral AS 
menurunkan suku bunga acuan.
Berbeda dengan AS, perekonomian negara di 
kawasan Euro dan Jepang melambat. Ekonomi 
negara 
Euro 
melemah 
terutama 
karena 
menurunnya aktivitas industri. Sedangkan 
perekonomian 
Jepang 
yang 
melambat 
disebabkan daya konsumsi yang turun.
Perekonomian 
negara 
berkembang 
utama 
(emerging market) seperti Tiongkok dan India 
tumbuh positif. Paket stimulus ekonomi yang 
dikeluarkan pemerintah China turut membantu 
perbaikan 
ekonomi 
di 
negara 
tersebut. 
Selain itu, aktivitas ekspor yang meningkat 
juga menjadi salah satu faktor utama yang 
menopang pertumbuhan ekonomi.
India menjadi salah satu motor pertumbuhan 
ekonomi dunia yang ditopang dari peningkatkan 
aktivitas investasi dan konsumsi. Industri 
manufaktur juga meningkat yang membutuhkan 
pasokan sumber energi listrik. 
Di pasar komoditas batu bara global, harga 
cenderung lebih rendah dibanding tahun 
sebelumnya. Gangguan pasokan batu bara ke 
pasar internasional karena semakin tingginya 
intensitas perang Rusia dan Ukraina menjadi 
salah satu faktor turunnya harga batu bara. 
Rusia merupakan salah satu negara pemasok 
terbesar batu bara dunia.
GLOBAL ECONOMY
The global economy remained sluggish in 
2024, with uneven growth across countries. 
Advanced economies such as the United 
States experienced positive growth, supported 
by improved consumer spending and a decline 
in unemployment. Additionally, lower inflation 
prospects prompted the U.S. Federal Reserve 
to cut benchmark interest rates.
In contrast to the U.S., the economies of 
the Eurozone and Japan slowed down. The 
Eurozone economy weakened mainly due 
to declining industrial activity. Meanwhile, 
Japan’s economic slowdown was driven by 
lower consumer spending.
Major emerging markets, such as China 
and India, posted positive growth. China’s 
economic 
recovery 
was 
supported 
by 
government-issued stimulus packages, while 
rising export activity also played a key role in 
sustaining economic growth.
India emerged as a key driver of global 
economic 
growth, 
fueled 
by 
increased 
investment and consumption. The country’s 
manufacturing sector also expanded, driving 
higher demand for electricity supply.
In the global coal commodities market, prices 
were generally lower compared to the previous 
year. Supply disruptions in the international 
coal market, driven by the intensifying Russia-
Ukraine conflict, were among the key factors 
behind the price decline. Russia remains one 
of the world’s largest coal suppliers.

NATIONAL ECONOMY
Amid a global economic slowdown, Indonesia’s 
economy demonstrated resilience with positive 
growth. Domestic economic expansion was primarily 
driven by strong consumer spending, followed by 
improvements in investment and export activities.
Household 
consumption 
remained 
stable, 
accompanied by positive investment growth. The 
increase in investment was mainly due to ongoing 
infrastructure development by both the government 
and the private sector. Global investors continue 
to view Indonesia as an attractive investment 
destination.
In line with steady consumption and investment, 
export activities expanded, supported by rising oil 
and gas, non-oil and gas, and service exports. The 
increase in exports, coupled with controlled imports, 
resulted in Indonesia maintaining a trade surplus 
throughout 2024. Meanwhile, inflation remained 
manageable at 1.57% year-over-year in 2024.
By industry, the mining sector was one of the 
largest contributors to national economic growth. 
In addition, the manufacturing, agriculture, trade, 
and construction sectors also played a crucial role 
in supporting the economy.
According to the Statistics Indonesia (BPS), 
Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.03% year-over-
year in 2024. This positive growth was in line with 
adequate foreign exchange reserves and a stable 
Rupiah exchange rate, despite the strengthening of 
the U.S. Dollar.

National coal production increased throughout 2024. 
According to the Minerba One Data Indonesia (MODI) 
report from the Ministry of Energy and Mineral 
Resources (ESDM), coal production reached 834.1 
million tons in 2024, up from 770.94 million tons in 
the previous year. This increase was driven by the 
need to supply domestic power plants and meet 
export market demand.

rising from 406.67 million tons in 2023. Meanwhile, 
the Domestic Market Obligation (DMO) for coal stood 
at 232.6 million tons in 2024, slightly higher than the 
212.86 million tons recorded in the previous year.

Kemampuan Membayar Utang 
Kemampuan Perseroan dalam memenuhi kewajiban 
utang jangka pendek tercermin dari rasio likuiditas 
sedangkan kemampuan membayar utang jangka 
pendek dan jangka panjang tercermin dari rasio 
solvabilitas. 
Rasio Likuiditas 
Salah satu ukuran rasio likuiditas adalah rasio lancar 
yang merupakan perbandingan antara aset lancar 
dan liabilitas jangka pendek. Pada tahun 2024, rasio 
lancar sebesar 1,45 kali dan di tahun 2023 sebesar 
1,93 kali.
Rasio Solvabilitas 
Rasio solvabilitas merupakan rasio untuk menilai 
kemampuan perusahaan dalam melunasi semua 
kewajibannya, baik jangka pendek maupun jangka 
panjang, dengan jaminan aktiva atau kekayaan yang 
dimiliki perusahaan hingga perusahaan tutup atau 
dilikuidasi. Rasio solvabilitas dapat diperoleh dari 
perbandingan antara total liabilitas dengan jumlah 
ekuitas (debt to equity ratio/DER). 
Pada tahun 2024, DER sebesar 0,44 kali dan di 
tahun 2023 sebesar 0,41 kali. Berdasarkan rasio-
rasio tersebut, Perseroan memiliki kemampuan 
yang sangat memadai dalam melunasi seluruh 
kewajibannya baik dalam jangka pendek maupun 
jangka panjang.
Debt Repayment Capability 
The Company’s capacity to fulfill its short-term debt 
obligations is assessed through liquidity ratios, 
while its ability to settle both short-term and long-
term debt is evaluated using solvency ratios.
Liquidity Ratio
One aspect of the liquidity ratio is the current ratio, 
which compares current assets to current liabilities. 
In 2024, the current ratio stood at 1.45 times, 
compared to 1.93 times in 2023.
Solvency Ratio
The solvency ratio measures a company’s ability to 
meet all its obligations, both short and long term, 
using its assets or wealth until the company ceases 
operations or undergoes liquidation. The solvency 
ratio can be calculated by comparing total liabilities 
to equity (debt to equity ratio/DER).
In 2024, the DER stood at 0.44 times, compared to 
0.41 times in 2023. Based on these indicators, the 
Company demonstrates a strong capability to fulfil 
its obligations in both the short and long term.
