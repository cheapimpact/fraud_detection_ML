TINJAUAN MAKRO EKONOMI GLOBAL 
DAN NASIONAL
Kondisi perekonomian global selama tahun 2024 masih 
dipenuhi dengan berbagai tantangan yang mempengaruhi 
ketidapastian 
pertumbuhan 
dan 
stabilitas 
ekonomi. 
Berdasarkan laporan World Economic Outlook yang dirilis 
oleh International Monetary Fund (IMF) pada Oktober tahun 
2024, pertumbuhan ekonomi global untuk tahun 2024 
diproyeksi berada di angka 3,2%, lebih besar 0,1% dari angka 
yang diproyeksikan pada awal tahun. Meskipun demikian, 
laju pertumbuhan ini masih lebih lambat dibandingkan 
dengan periode sebelum pandemi Covid-19, dimana hal 
ini menandakan bahwa pemulihan ekonomi global belum 
sepenuhnya optimal.
Ketegangan politik yang masih terjadi di beberapa wilayah di 
dunia seperti perang Rusia-Ukrania, konflik di Timur Tengah 
dan di Laut Cina Selatan memiliki dampak yang signifikan pada 
gangguan rantai pasokan global, lonjakan harga komoditas 
dan voltalitas di pasar keuangan. Selain itu, Bank sentral 
di berbagai negara, terutama Federal Reserve AS (The Fed) 
dan Bank Sentral Eropa (ECB), masih mempertahankan suku 
bunga tinggi untuk mengendalikan inflasi yang berdampak 
pada pelemahan di sektor properti dan suku bunga pinjaman 
yang masih tinggi. Sebagai tambahan, sepanjang tahun 
2024 ada lebih dari 60 negara yang melangsungkan pemilu, 
termasuk salah satunya adalah pemilihan Presiden Amerika 
Serikat. Berlangsungnya pemilu di banyak negara pada tahun 
2024 diperkirakan akan membawa implikasi pada perubahan 
kebijakan ekonomi dan perdagangan internasional serta 
ketidakpastian pasar di tahun-tahun mendatang.
Di tengah terjadinya ketidakpastian ekonomi global, kondisi 
perekonomian Indonesia pada tahun 2024 masih mampu 
mencatatkan pertumbuhan. Berdasarkan data dari Badan 
Pusat Statistik (BPS), ekonomi Indonesia secara kumulatif 
tumbuh sebesar 5,03% (c-to-c), melambat jika dibandingkan 
dengan pencapaian tahun 2023 yang mencatatkan angka 
5,05% (c-to-c). Pertumbuhan positif terjadi pada seluruh 
sektor, dimana sektor yang berkontribusi terbesar terhadap 
pertumbuhan ekonomi adalah sektor industri pengolahan, 
diikuti sektor perdagangan, pertanian, konstruksi dan 
pertambangan.

GLOBAL AND NATIONAL 
MACROECONOMIC REVIEW
The global economic conditions during 2024 are still filled 
with various challenges that affect the uncertainty of 
economic growth and stability. Based on the World Economic 
Outlook report released by the International Monetary Fund 
(IMF) in October 2024, global economic growth for 2024 is 
projected to be at 3.2%, 0.1% higher than the figure projected 
at the beginning of the year. However, this growth rate is still 
slower than the period before the Covid-19 pandemic, which 
indicates that the global economic recovery is not yet fully 
optimal.
Political tensions that are still occurring in several regions 
in the world such as the Russia-Ukrania war, conflicts in the 
Middle East and in the South China Sea have a significant 
impact on disruptions to the global supply chain, spikes 
in commodity prices and volatility in financial markets. In 
addition, central banks in various countries, especially the 
US Federal Reserve (The Fed) and the European Central Bank 
(ECB), are still maintaining high interest rates to control 
inflation which has an impact on weakening the property 
sector and high loan interest rates. In addition, throughout 
2024 there will be more than 60 countries holding elections, 
including one of which is the United States Presidential 
election. The holding of elections in many countries in 2024 
is expected to have implications for changes in international 
economic and trade policies as well as market uncertainty in 
the coming years.
In the midst of global economic uncertainty, Indonesia's 
economic conditions in 2024 were still able to record growth. 
Based on data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), the 
Indonesian economy cumulatively grew by 5.03% (c-to-c), 
slowing down when compared to the achievement in 2023 
which recorded 5.05% (c-to-c). Positive growth occurred in 
all sectors, where the sector that contributed the most to 
economic growth was the manufacturing sector, followed 
by the trade, agriculture, construction and mining sectors.

TINJAUAN INDUSTRI
Pada awal tahun 2024, Asosiasi Perusahaan Pembiayaan 
Indonesia (APP) memproyeksikan pertumbuhan piutang 
perusahaan pembiayaan berada di angka 7%-8%. Akan 
tetapi, berdasarkan data statistik perusahaan pembiayaan 
yang dirilis oleh Otoritas Jasa Keuangan (OJK), piutang 
pembiayaan dari Multifinance per bulan Desember 2024 
mencapai Rp530,4 Triliun atau tumbuh sebesar 6,82% (YoY). 
Meskipun pencapaian ini masih di bawah proyeksi APPI, 
pertumbuhan masih berada di angka positif yang didorong 
oleh pertumbuhan pada sektor pembiayaan investasi 
sebesar 10,47%, pembiayaan modal kerja sebesar 9,56% 
dan pembiayaan mulitiguna sebesar 3,52%. Peningkatan 
pembiayaan alat berat memberikan kontribusi besar pada 
pertumbuhan pembiayaan investasi, dimana pembiayaan 
tumbuh dari Rp42,1 Triliun di tahun 2023 menjadi Rp44,4 
Triliun di tahun 2024 atau tumbuh sebesar 5,39% (YoY). 
Meningkatnya permintaan alat berat di sektor pertambangan 
dan konstruksi turut menjadi faktor yang mendorong 
pembiayaan alat berat di tahun 2024.

Domestic economic stability throughout 2024 is also 
supported by the government's efforts to keep inflation under 
control within the national target range of 2.5% ± 1%. After 
experiencing a spike in 2022 and 2023, domestic inflation 
in December 2024 was recorded at 1.57% (YoY), lower than 
the same period in 2023 which was 2.61% (YoY). Meanwhile, 
the Indonesian Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index 
(PMI) in August 2024 was recorded at an expansionary level 
of 51.2, driven by an increase in purchases of raw materials, 
both from within and outside the country.
Optimism for Indonesia's economic growth is expected to 
continue in 2025, although there are still several challenges 
looming such as fluctuations in global commodity prices, 
ongoing geopolitical tensions and the threat of a global 
economic recession. The World Bank projects that the 
Indonesian economy will continue to experience positive 
growth of 5.2% in 2025, supported by policy reforms, 
adoption of digitalization, and investment in strategic 
sectors.
INDUSTRY REVIEW
In early 2024, the Association of Indonesian Financing 
Companies (APP) projected that the growth of financing 
company receivables would be at 7%-8%. However, based 
on statistical data from financing companies released by 
the Financial Services Authority (OJK), financing receivables 
from Multifinance as of December 2024 reached IDR 530.4 
trillion or grew by 6.82% (YoY). Although this achievement 
is still below APPI's projection, growth is still in the positive 
figures driven by growth in the investment financing 
sector of 10.47%, working capital financing of 9.56% and 
multipurpose financing of 3.52%. The increase in heavy 
equipment financing has contributed greatly to the growth 
of investment financing, where financing grew from Rp42.1 
trillion in 2023 to Rp44.4 trillion in 2024 or grew by 5.39% 
(YoY). The increasing demand for heavy equipment in the 
mining and construction sectors also became a factor 
driving heavy equipment financing in 2024.

Financing Distribution by Product
After transforming its business from consumptive 
financing to productive financing since 2020, the 
Company has recorded significant growth over the 
past 5 (five) years. In 2024, the Company recorded a 
total financing distribution of Rp 5.22 Trillion. This 
figure increased by 82.05% (YoY) when compared to 
the previous year which amounted to Rp 2.87 Trillion. 
Factoring financing products contributed 58.79% of the 
total financing disbursement, followed by ABF financing 
products at 28.31% and Business Capital Facility (FMU) 
financing products at 12.90%.
FACTORING FINANCING
The Company provides Factoring financing facilities in the 
form of purchase of trade receivables aimed at meeting the 
working capital needs of Debtors. In 2024, the distribution 
of financing for Factoring products amounted to Rp 3.07 
Trillion. Factoring financing distribution in 2024 increased by 
71.4% (YoY) compared to 2023 with a total of Rp 1.79 Trillion.
