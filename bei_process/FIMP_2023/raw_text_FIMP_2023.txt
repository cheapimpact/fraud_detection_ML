TINJAUAN EKONOMI GLOBAL DAN NASIONAL
Perekonomian global mengalami perlambatan
pada tahun 2023 dengan berbagai tantangan
diantaranya mulai dari potensi perlambatan
ekonomi, peningkatan tensi geopolitik, risiko
inflasi, hingga perubahan iklim. Namun Bank
Indonesia menilai pertumbuhan ekonomi global
masih akan didorong oleh ekonomi Amerika
Serikat (AS) yang ditopang oleh konsumsi rumah
tangga dan sektor jasa yang berorientasi
domestik. Namun pandangan positif tersebut,
masih dibayangi dengan perlambatan yang
terjadi pada perekonomian Tiongkok yang
dipengaruhi oleh pelemahan konsumsi dan
penurunan kinerja sektor properti.
Sementara Tahun 2023 menjadi tahun yang
penuh tantangan bagi perekonomian Indonesia.
Pertumbuhan ekonomi melambat menjadi 5,05
persen, sedikit lebih rendah dari tahun
sebelumnya yang mencapai 5,31 persen.
Namun, di tengah badai ekonomi global dan
inflasi 
yang 
tinggi, 
Indonesia 
masih
menunjukkan ketangguhan.
Kapal ekonomi
Indonesia 
terus 
melaju, 
meski 
diterpa
gelombang ketidakpastian.
Dengan berbagai capaian kondisi perekonomian
nasional tersebut, Indonesia mampu menjadi
salah satu negara yang tumbuh kuat dan
persisten berada di level yang tinggi ketimbang
sejumlah negara lain. Pertumbuhan ekonomi
nasional 
sepanjang 
tahun 
2023 
mampu
melampaui 
beberapa 
negara peers seperti
Malaysia (3,77%) dan Korea Selatan (1,36%).
Ekonomi Indonesia juga tumbuh lebih tinggi dari
perekonomian negara G-20 seperti Amerika
Serikat (2,5%), Perancis (0,9%) maupun Jerman
yang mengalami kontraksi (-0,3%).
Global and National Economic Review
The global economy will experience a slowdown
in 2023 with various challenges including the
potential for economic slowdown, increasing
geopolitical tensions, inflation risks, to climate
change. However, Bank Indonesia assesses that
global economic growth will still be driven by the
United States (US) economy which is supported
by household consumption and the domestic-
oriented service sector. However, this positive
view is still overshadowed by the slowdown in
the Chinese economy which is influenced by
weakening 
consumption 
and 
declining
performance in the property sector.
Meanwhile, 2023 will be a year full of challenges
for the Indonesian economy. Economic growth
slowed to 5.05 percent, slightly lower than the
previous year which reached 5.31 percent.
However, amidst the global economic storm and
high inflation, Indonesia is still showing
resilience. Indonesia's economic ship continues
to sail, even though it is hit by waves of
uncertainty.
With these various achievements in national
economic conditions, Indonesia is able to
become one of the countries that grows strongly
and persistently at a high level compared to a
number of other countries. National economic
growth throughout 2023 will be able to exceed
several peer countries such as Malaysia (3.77%)
and South Korea (1.36%). Indonesia's economy
also grew higher than the economies of G-20
countries such as the United States (2.5%),
France (0.9%) and Germany which experienced
contraction (-0.3%).

Overview of The Indonesian Construction Market
National economic growth in the fourth quarter
of 2023 reached 5.04% (year on year/yoy), or
higher than the third quarter of 2023 of 4.94%
(yoy). The construction sector recorded growth
of 7.68% (yoy), which contributed the second
largest growth after the processing industry. On
an annual basis, national economic growth
throughout 2023 also recorded growth of 5.05%
(ctc).
Based on records from the Central Statistics
Agency (BPS), construction sector growth grew
7.68% on an annual basis or (year on year/yoy)
in the fourth quarter of 2023, with a contribution
to the total Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of
10.49%.
In stimulating the national economy in Quarter
IV-2023, the government succeeded in producing
growth in the performance of the construction
sector, also resulting in household consumption
growing by 4.82% (yoy) and Gross Fixed Capital
Formation of 4.40 (yoy) to support economic
