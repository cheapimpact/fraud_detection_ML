INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
Pada tahun 2024, produksi minyak nabati mengalami 
penurunan yang utamanya disebabkan oleh minyak 
kelapa sawit, minyak bunga matahari, maupun minyak 
rapeseed. Hal ini membuat harga minyak nabati 
mengalami peningkatan akibat kekhawatiran akan 
supply, sementara itu tingkat konsumsi terus meningkat 
seiring dengan penguatan ekonomi pasca-pandemi 
Covid-19 dan pertambahan penduduk. Namun,  produksi 
minyak kedelai pada tahun 2024 melimpah sehingga 
harganya terkoreksi bahkan harga rata-ratanya lebih 
murah dibandingkan kelapa sawit. Hal ini membuat 
beberapa negara beralih untuk mengkonsumsi minyak 
kedelai terutama China dan India untuk memenuhi 
kebutuhan feedstock dan minyak makan. Harga rata-rata 
minyak kedelai pada tahun 2024 berdasarkan Dutch, fob 
ex-mill sebesar US$ 1,045/ton sedangkan harga rata-rata 
minyak kelapa sawit pada tahun 2024 berdasarkan Cif 
Rotterdam sebesar US$ 1,084/ton. Walaupun demikian, 
pasokan produksi minyak kedelai masih jauh dalam 
memenuhi kebutuhan minyak nabati global sehingga 
ketergantungan akan minyak kelapa sawit masih tinggi. 
Saat ini pangsa pasar minyak nabati global didominasi oleh 
minyak kelapa sawit yakni 36% (sumber oil world, 2024) 
dengan komposisi ekspor kelapa sawit Indonesia ke pasar 
global yakni sebesar 51%. Indonesia sendiri sebagai tulang 
punggung kebutuhan pangan global berbasis kelapa sawit 
kini tengah mengalami stagnasi produksi yang telah terjadi 
selama empat tahun terakhir dengan rata-rata produksi 50 
juta ton dan diprediksi oleh para ahli minyak nabati akan 
mengalami tren yang menurun dalam beberapa tahun ke 
depan.

In 2024, vegetable oil production decreased, mainly 
due to palm oil, sunflower oil, and rapeseed oil. This 
increases vegetable oil prices due to supply concerns 
while consumption levels continue to increase, along 
with the economy strengthening after the Covid-19 
pandemic 
and 
population 
growth. 
Meanwhile, 
soybean oil production in 2024 is abundant, so 
the price is corrected, and even the average price 
is cheaper than palm oil. This has made several 
countries switch to consuming soybean oil, especially 
in China and India, to meet the needs of feedstock 
and edible oil. The average price of soybean oil in 
2024 based on Dutch, fob ex-mill is US$ 1,045/ton, 
while the average price of palm oil in 2024 based 
on Cif Rotterdam is US$ 1,084/ton. Nevertheless, 
the supply of soybean oil production is still far from 
meeting global vegetable oil needs, so dependence 
on palm oil is still high.
Currently, the global vegetable oil market share is 
dominated by palm oil at 36% (Source Oil World, 
2024), with the composition of Indonesia's palm oil 
exports to the global market at 51%. As the backbone 
of palm oil-based global food needs, Indonesia is now 
experiencing production stagnation over the past 4 
years, with an average production of 50 million tons, 
and is predicted by vegetable oil experts to experience 
a downward trend in the next few years.

The stagnation of Indonesia's palm oil production is 
mainly due to the agronomic side, namely old plants 
and entering the replanting period, pollinating insects 
whose productivity is now weakening and plantation 
governance that is not yet optimal, especially in 
smallholder plantations which comprehensively control 
40% of oil palm plantation ownership. As a national 
strategic commodity, various efforts have been made 
by the government to maintain the competitive value of 
Indonesian palm oil. The government has created a Palm 
Oil Replanting (Peremajaan Sawit Rakyat/PSR) program 
for smallholder plantations by providing incentives 
for the replanting process, but until now, the annual 
implementation is only around 30% of the target. In 
addition, R&D continues to be developed agronomically, 
as well as the development of downstream palm 
oil products. Since 2022, the government has been 
enforcing the Domestic Market Obligation (DMO) policy 
implemented through the Domestic Price Obligation 
(DPO) to maintain the domestic demand for cooking oil.
For commodities that are one of the economic drivers 
in realizing the vision of "Golden Indonesia 2045", 
where economic growth is expected to reach 7-8%, 
the downstream development of the palm oil industry 
continues to be promoted. In 2024, the government 
fully implemented a diesel fuel mix policy with palm 
oil of 35%, known as the mandatory Biodiesel (B35) 
policy. Ending 2024, the government has also released 
a mandatory B40 program that will be implemented in 
2025. Previously, the B35 policy has increased domestic 
consumption by 2 million tons and is expected to 
increase by 4 million tons with the implementation of 
B40 amid the stagnation of national palm oil production.
Sustainable-focused development in the palm oil 
industry is consistently implemented with the mandatory 
Indonesian Sustainable Palm Oil (ISPO) certificate policy. 
The Indonesian government also implements various 
policies that pose a challenge to the palm oil industry, 
such as the policy of the Facility for Development of 
Surrounding Community Plantations (FPKM) and the 
issuance of the extension of oil palm plantations in 
forest areas as part of the Job Creation Law (UUCK), as 
well as other policies. Meanwhile, protectionism in the 
form of trade and non-trade barriers is still a challenge 
that must be faced by this industry. Although the 
European Union Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) policy 
has been postponed to 2024, Indonesia's struggle with 
the World Trade Organization (WTO) is not over yet.

ASPEK PEMASARAN
Pasar pada dua hingga tiga tahun terakhir menunjukan 
volatility yang semakin meningkat. Berbagai aspek dan 
faktor dari pandemi Covid-19 di awal 2021, ketegangan 
geopolitik di beberapa negara, isu cuaca seperti El 
Nino, serta kebijakan DMO & DPO dan export ban di 
2022 turut menjadi penyemarak dalam memengaruhi 
pergerakan supply dan demand yang secara langsung 
mempengaruhi pergerakan harga komoditas minyak 
nabati di pasar. Namun, semenjak pandemi mulai 
mereda dan diiringi recovery dari segi perekonomian 
global, pasar menunjukkan tren yang positif dengan 
mulai stabilnya tingkat permintaan. Di sisi lain, 
peningkatan permintaan tidak diimbangi dengan 
pergerakan pasokan. Kondisi supply dan demand 
ini dipercaya akan menjadi narasi utama dalam 
beberapa periode ke depan.
Growing global concern mengenai sustainability 
aspect juga menjadi perhatian bagi Perseroan 
untuk mematuhi global sustainability platform yang 
ada dan menunjukkan komitmen akan kebijakan 
keberlanjutan.
Pada tahun 2024, volume penjualan Perseroan untuk 
CPO dan produk turunannya mengalami penurunan 
sebesar 9% menjadi 1,55 juta ton dari tahun 2023 di 
1,70 juta ton. Perseroan juga mencatat penurunan 
penjualan kernel dan produk turunannya sebesar 
22% dari 273,41 ribu ton pada tahun 2023 menjadi 
213,69 ribu ton pada tahun 2024.
Proporsi serapan penjualan adalah 69% untuk 
pasar domestik dan 31% untuk ekspor ke beberapa 
negara tujuan seperti China, India, Filipina, dan lain 
sebagainya.
 
MARKETING ASPECT
The market over the past two to three years has 
exhibited increasing volatility. Various aspects and 
factors, including the Covid-19 pandemic in early 
2021, geopolitical tensions in several countries, 
weather issues such as El Niño, as well as DMO and 
Domestic DPO policies and export bans in 2022, have 
contributed to fluctuations in supply and demand, 
directly impacting the price movements of vegetable oil 
commodities in the market. However, as the pandemic 
has begun to subside and global economic recovery 
has taken hold, the market exhibited a positive trend 
with demand levels gradually stabilizing. On the other 
hand, the increase in demand has not been matched 
by corresponding supply movements. This supply-
demand imbalance is expected to remain a key 
narrative in the coming periods.
Growing global concerns regarding sustainability 
aspects have also drawn the Company's attention to 
comply with existing global sustainability platforms 
and demonstrate its commitment to sustainability 
policies.
In 2024, the Company's sales volume for CPO and its 
derivative products decreased by 9% to 1.55 million 
tons, from 1.70 million tons in 2023. The Company also 
recorded a 22% decrease in sales of kernel and its 
derivative products from 273.41 thousand tons in 2023 
to 213.69 thousand tons in 2024. 
The sales distribution comprised 69% for the domestic 
market and 31% for exports to several destination 
countries, including China, India, Philippines, etc.

DIVIDEN
Kebijakan Dividen
Perseroan secara konsisten mendistribusikan dividen tunai 
kepada pemegang saham dari laba bersih setelah pajak. 
Perseroan tidak memiliki kebijakan pembayaran dividen 
tertentu dan dividen yang dibagikan mempertimbangkan 
kondisi keuangan, profitabilitas, dan kebutuhan kas untuk 
menunjang kegiatan operasional dan investasi, serta 
keputusan RUPST.
Manajemen Perseroan akan mengajukan usulan jumlah 
dividen untuk mendapatkan persetujuan RUPST. Dengan 
ketentuan bahwa posisi keuangan Perseroan memungkinkan 
dan 
Dewan 
Komisaris 
menyetujui, 
maka 
manajemen 
Perseroan dapat memutuskan pembagian dividen interim 
sebagai bagian dari dividen tahunan yang akan ditetapkan 
pada RUPST berikutnya.
Pengajuan dan Pembayaran Dividen
Berdasarkan Keputusan Rapat Direksi yang telah memperoleh 
persetujuan Dewan Komisaris pada tanggal 23 September 
2024 diputuskan untuk membagikan dividen kas interim atas 
laba tahun buku 2024 sebesar Rp 161.674 juta atau Rp 84 
(Rupiah penuh) per saham kepada pemegang saham yang 
tercatat pada tanggal 7 Oktober 2024.
Dalam Rapat Umum Tahunan Pemegang Saham yang 
diselenggarakan pada tanggal 23 April 2024, pemegang 
saham menyetujui Pembagian dividen kas atas laba tahun 
buku 2023 sebesar Rp 475.398 juta atau Rp 247 (Rupiah penuh) 
per saham kepada pemegang saham yang tercatat pada 
tanggal 6 Mei 2024. Dari jumlah dividen tersebut, termasuk di 
dalamnya pembagian dividen kas interim sebesar Rp 157.824 
juta atau Rp 82 (Rupiah penuh) per saham kepada pemegang 
saham yang tercatat pada tanggal 10 Oktober 2023, yang 
telah diputuskan oleh Rapat Direksi dan disetujui oleh Dewan 
Komisaris pada tanggal 25 September 2023.
DIVIDEND
Dividend Policy
The Company consistently distributes cash dividends to 
shareholders from net profit after tax. The Company does not 
have a specific dividend payment policy, and the dividends 
distributed take into account the Company's financial 
condition, profitability, and cash requirements to support 
operational and investment activities, as well as the decisions 
of the AGMS.
The Company's management will propose the dividend 
amount for approval by the AGMS. Provided that the 
Company's financial position allows and the Board of 
Commissioners approves, the Company's management may 
decide to distribute interim dividends as part of the annual 
dividend to be determined at the next AGMS.
Declaration and Payment of Dividend
Based on the Director’s Meeting’s decision which was 
approved by the Board of Commisioners on 23 September 
2024, it was decided to distribute interim cash dividends from 
the 2024 profit of Rp 161,674 million or Rp 84 (full amount) per 
share to the shareholders registered as at 7 October 2024.
In the Annual General Shareholders’ Meeting held on 23 April 
2024, the shareholders approved, The distribution of cash 
dividends from the 2023 profit of Rp 475,398 million or Rp 247 
(full amount) per share to the shareholders registered as at 6 
May 2024. These dividends included the distribution of interim 
cash dividends of Rp 157,824 million or Rp 82 (full amount) 
per share to the shareholders registered as at 10 October 
2023 which have been decided by the Directors’ Meeting and 
approved by the Board of Commissioners on 25 September 
2023.

berlaku umum dengan memenuhi prinsip transaksi yang 
wajar (arms-length principle) dengan pengawasan Dewan 
Komisaris dan Komite Audit.
