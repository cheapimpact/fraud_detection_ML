Management Discussion and Analysis
Pada tahun 2023, ketidakpastian ekonomi global tetap tinggi 
dengan kecenderungan ekonomi Tiongkok yang melambat dan 
ekonomi Amerika Serikat (AS) yang semakin kuat. Inflasi di negara 
maju masih tetap tinggi karena berlanjutnya tekanan inflasi, 
keketatan pasar tenaga kerja, dan meningkatnya harga minyak. 
Perkembangan tersebut mendorong tetap tingginya suku bunga 
kebijakan moneter di negara maju, terutama Federal Funds Rate 
(FFR) AS, yang mengakibatkan meningkatnya ketidakpastian 
pasar keuangan global. Oleh karena itu, tekanan aliran modal 
keluar dan pelemahan nilai tukar di negara berkembang semakin 
tinggi, termasuk di Indonesia.
Di dalam negeri, Bank Indonesia menyebutkan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi Indonesia tetap baik ditopang oleh permintaan 
domestik. Konsumsi rumah tangga diperkirakan tumbuh kuat 
sejalan dengan keyakinan masyarakat yang masih tinggi, 
termasuk generasi muda yang meningkatkan konsumsi jasa. 
Kinerja investasi tetap baik sejalan dengan berlanjutnya 
penyelesaian Proyek Strategis Nasional. 
In 2023, global economic uncertainty remained high, with the 
tendency of the Chinese economy slowing and the US economy 
strengthening. Developed countries’ inflation rates remained 
high due to ongoing inflationary pressures, tight labor markets, 
and rising oil prices. These developments have encouraged 
continued high monetary policy interest rates in developed 
countries, particularly the US Federal Funds Rate (FFR), leading 
to increased global financial market uncertainty. As a result, 
developing countries, including Indonesia, are experiencing 
increased pressure on capital outflows and weakening exchange 
rates.
Domestically, Bank Indonesia stated that Indonesia’s economic 
growth remains strong, driven by domestic demand. Household 
consumption is projected to grow strongly in line with public 
confidence, which remains high, particularly among the younger 
generation, who are increasing their consumption of services. 
Investment performance remains strong, in line with the ongoing 
completion of National Strategic Projects.

Secara keseluruhan, Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS) mencatat 
pertumbuhan ekonomi domestik pada tahun 2023 tercatat 
sebesar 5,05%, sedangkan pada tahun 2022 mencapai 5,31%. 
Kinerja Neraca Pembayaran Indonesia (NPI) tetap baik dengan 
Nilai tukar Rupiah tetap terjaga. Ketahanan sistem keuangan, 
khususnya perbankan, juga tetap terjaga. 
Sementara Bank Indonesia menetapkan suku bunga BI 7-Day 
Reverse Repo Rate (BI7DRR) naik sebesar 25 bps menjadi 
6,00%, suku bunga Deposit Facility sebesar 25 bps menjadi 
5,25%, dan suku bunga Lending Facility sebesar 25 bps menjadi 
6,75%. Kenaikan ini untuk memperkuat kebijakan stabilisasi nilai 
tukar Rupiah dari dampak meningkatnya ketidakpastian global 
serta sebagai langkah pre-emptive dan forward-looking untuk 
memitigasi dampaknya terhadap inflasi barang impor, sehingga 
inflasi tetap terkendali. Bank Indonesia mencatat inflasi tahun 
2023 tercatat sebesar 2,61% (yoy), lebih rendah dibandingkan 
dengan inflasi pada tahun sebelumnya sebesar 5,51% (yoy).
Menghadapi tahun 2023, kredit/pembiayaan perbankan terus 
meningkat pada seluruh sektor ekonomi. Kredit perbankan 
pada Agustus 2023 tumbuh 9,06% yang terutama ditopang oleh 
kinerja sektor Jasa Dunia Usaha, Perdagangan, dan Jasa Sosial. 
Sejalan dengan pertumbuhan kredit perbankan secara agregat, 
pembiayaan syariah tumbuh tinggi mencapai 14,52%. 
Sementara pada industri multifinance, Otoritas Jasa Keuangan 
(OJK) melaporkan piutang industri pembiayaan tumbuh 13,23% 
secara tahunan hingga Desember 2023 menjadi Rp470,86 
triliun. Profil risiko perusahaan pembiayaan juga tercatat terjaga 
dengan rasio Non Performing Financing (NPF) net tercatat 
sebesar 0,64% dan NPF gross sebesar 2,44%. Gearing ratio 
perusahaan pembiayaan tercatat sebesar 2,26 kali, jauh di 
bawah batas maksimum sebesar 10 kali. Pertumbuhan piutang 
pembiayaan 
didukung 
pembiayaan 
investasi, 
multiguna, 
dan modal kerja yang masing-masing tumbuh sebesar 
8,98%, 13,85%, dan 15,10%. Piutang pembiayaan mobil baru 
menyumbang porsi Rp146,77 triliun, mobil bekas Rp78,13 triliun, 
sepeda motor Rp99,70 triliun, piutang pembiayaan mobil 
pengangkutan Rp59,10 triliun, sementara alat berat sebesar 
Rp42,16 triliun.
Overall, the Central Bureau of Statistics (BPS) reported domestic 
economic growth of 5.05% in 2023, compared to 5.31% in 2022. 
Indonesia’s Balance of Payments (NPI) remains strong, with the 
Rupiah exchange rate remaining steady. The resilience of the 
financial system, particularly banking, is also preserved.
Meanwhile, Bank Indonesia set the 7-Day Reverse Repo Rate 
(BI7DRR) interest rate to increase by 25 bps to 6.00%, the 
Deposit Facility interest rate by 25 bps to 5.25%, and the Lending 
Facility interest rate by 25 bps to 6.75%. This increase is intended 
to strengthen the policy of stabilizing the Rupiah exchange rate 
in response to rising global uncertainty and as a pre-emptive 
and forward-looking step to mitigate its impact on inflation in 
imported goods, ensuring that inflation remains under control. 
Bank Indonesia reported that inflation in 2023 was 2.61% (yoy), 
lower than the previous year’s 5.51% (yoy).
Banking credit/financing continued to grow across all economic 
sectors in 2023. In August 2023, bank credit increased by 9.06%, 
mainly due to the performance of the Business Services, Trade, 
and Social Services sectors. Sharia financing increased strongly 
at 14.52%, in line with the growth of aggregate banking credit.
Meanwhile, in the multifinance industry, the Financial Services 
Authority (OJK) reported that the financing industry’s receivables 
increased by 13.23% annually until December 2023, reaching 
Rp470.86 trillion. The risk profile of finance companies was 
also recorded as well maintained, with a net Non-Performing 
Financing (NPF) ratio of 0.64% and a gross NPF of 2.44%. The 
finance company’s gearing ratio was recorded at 2.26 times, 
significantly lower than the maximum limit of 10 times. The 
growth in financing receivables was supported by investment, 
multipurpose, and working capital financing, which increased 
by 8.98%, 13.85%, and 15.10%, respectively. New car financing 
receivables contributed a portion of Rp146.77 trillion, used cars 
Rp78.13 trillion, motorbikes Rp99.70 trillion, transportation car 
financing receivables Rp59.10 trillion, and heavy equipment 
amounted to Rp42.16 trillion.

Strategi Bisnis Perseroan 2023
Company’s Business Strategy in 2023
Perseroan menghadapi tahun 2023 dengan optimis dapat 
meraih peluang untuk terus bertumbuh serta meraih pangsa 
pasar yang lebih tinggi. Terlebih, isu resesi ekonomi yang 
ada sejak tahun sebelumnya dinilai tidak terlalu berdampak 
besar terhadap kinerja perekonomian nasional. Sepanjang 
tahun 2023, strategi dan kebijakan Perseroan mengarah pada 
pelaksanaan Rencana Bisnis Tahunan (RBT) dengan lebih realistis, 
untuk mencapai pertumbuhan usaha dengan tetap menjalankan 
prinsip kehati-hatian. 
Perseroan juga tetap berfokus pada segmen pembiayaan 
konsumen 
dan 
sewa 
pembiayaan 
dengan 
melakukan 
diversifikasi produk dan portofolio untuk menjaga pertumbuhan 
bisnis. Kemudian, Perseroan juga secara konsisten berupaya 
meningkatkan kompetensi Sumber Daya Manusia, implementasi 
Teknologi Informasi terkini, serta produktif dan selektif 
dalam penyaluran pembiayaan baru. Selain itu, Perseroan 
senantiasa menjaga hubungan kerja sama kepada mitra bisnis 
dan meningkatkan service level untuk menghindari terjadinya 
kendala. Lebih lanjut, Perseroan juga mengadakan program-
program inovasi, sehingga produk-produk yang dimiliki akan 
lebih diutamakan oleh mitra bisnis. 
Dalam hal pendanaan, Perseroan berhasil mempertahankan 
kepercayaan dunia perbankan dan menerima fasilitas term loan 
dari beberapa bank. Untuk itu, Perseroan tetap fokus pada 
pinjaman bank serta belum memiliki rencana untuk menerbitkan 
surat berharga. Dari mitra-mitra bank kreditur, Perseroan juga 
masih mendapatkan rate yang kompetitif, sehingga pelanggan 
Perseroan dapat memperoleh kredit dengan suku bunga terbaik.
In 2023, the Association of Indonesian Automotive Industries 
(GAIKINDO) recorded sales of wholesale four-wheeled vehicles 
reduced by 4.03% to 1.00 million units compared to 1.05 million 
units in 2022. The retail segment recorded a decreased of 1.53% 
in 2023, to 998.06 thousand units from 1.01 million units in the 
previous year. Meanwhile, the Indonesian Motorcycle Industry 
Association (AISI) reported that domestic wholesale two-
wheeled vehicle sales in 2023 increased by 19.45% to 6.24 million 
units from 5.22 million in 2022. According to data compiled by 
the Indonesian Heavy Equipment Industry Association (HINABI), 
national heavy equipment production in 2023 was recorded at 
8,066 units, a decrease of 8.61% from 2022, which was 8,826 
units.
The Company faces 2023 with optimism, as it believes it will be 
able to take advantage of opportunities to continue growing 
and gain a higher market share. Furthermore, the issue of 
the economic recession that began the previous year was 
not considered to have had a significant impact on national 
economic performance. Throughout 2023, the Company’s 
strategy and policies aim to more realistically implement the 
Annual Business Plan (RBT), resulting in business growth while 
adhering to the prudent principle.
To maintain business growth, the Company continues to focus 
on the consumer financing and finance leasing segments by 
diversifying its products and portfolio. The Company also 
consistently strives to improve human resource competency, 
implement 
advanced 
information 
technology, 
and 
be 
productive and selective in the distribution of new financing. In 
addition, the Company maintains cooperative relationships with 
business partners and improves service levels to avoid problems. 
Furthermore, the company holds innovation programs to ensure 
that its products are prioritized by business partners.
In terms of funding, the Company was successful in maintaining 
the trust of the banking community, receiving term loan 
facilities from several banks. As a result, the Company remains 
focused on bank loans and does not intend to issue securities. 
The Company continues to receive competitive rates from its 
creditor bank partners, allowing its customers to obtain loan at 
the best interest rates.

SEWA PEMBIAYAAN 
Pada segmen usaha sewa pembiayaan, Perseroan senantiasa 
berfokus dalam menjalankan bisnis yang mengedepankan 
prinsip kehati-hatian dan terukur. Pada segmen ini, Perseroan 
menerapkan beragam strategi dan kebijakan dengan tujuan 
untuk tetap menjaga pertumbuhan serta kualitas portofolio aset 
melalui penyaluran pembiayaan yang selektif dan tepat. 
Kinerja penyaluran pembiayaan baru pada tahun 2023 untuk 
sektor sewa pembiayaan tercatat sebesar Rp793,15 miliar, turun 
8,61% dari tahun 2022 yang tercatat sebesar Rp867,86 miliar.

pembiayaan sebesar 44,05% dari sebelumnya sebesar 37,83%,

16,48%.

FINANCIAL LEASE 
In the finance leasing business segment, the Company is always 
committed to running a business that prioritizes prudence 
and measurability principles. In this segment, the Company 
implements a variety of strategies and policies with the objective 
of maintaining the value and quality of its asset portfolio through 
selective and appropriate financing disbursements.
The performance of new financing disbursement in the year 
2023 for the financial lease sector was recorded at Rp793.15 
billion, a decrease of 8.61% from 2022, which was Rp867,86 
billion. The mining sector increased its contribution to the 
finance lease portfolio to 44.05% from 37.83% previously, 
followed by the service sector, which increased to 21.70% from 
16.48% previously. 
The reduction in new financing disbursement in 2023 was 
influenced by the decline in commodity prices, particularly 
coal, which had a GAR of less than 4,000 at the beginning of 
2023. Furthermore, the nickel mining sector experienced a 
decline in the second semester of 2023 as several companies’ 
2023 RKAB quotas began to expire. This condition, combined 
with the situation leading up to the presidential election, which 
has caused some entrepreneurs to decide to wait and see, 
has had an impact on the level of capital goods investment in 
2023, resulting in a decrease in the volume of new financing 
disbursements.

PROYEKSI MAKRO EKONOMI 2024
Ke depan, kondisi makro ekonomi tahun 2024 diproyeksikan 
masih akan penuh dengan tantangan serta ketidakpastian. 
Dalam laporan World Economic Outlook (WEO) edisi Oktober 
2023, International Monetary Fund (IMF) memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global akan melambat menjadi sekitar 
2,9% pada tahun 2024. Adapun proyeksi pertumbuhan tahun 
2024 tersebut lebih rendah dibanding proyeksi sebelumnya 
yang sempat diperkirakan tumbuh mencapai 3%.

ekonomi dalam negeri pada tahun 2024 akan tumbuh sekitar

semakin menguat, namun risiko perlambatan ekonomi global 
akibat dinamika kondisi dunia tetap perlu diwaspadai.
Pemilihan Umum (Pemilu) serentak akan memiliki dampak pada

2024 MACROECONOMIC PROJECTION
Looking ahead, macroeconomic conditions in 2024 are 
projected to remain challenging and uncertain. In the October 
2023 edition of the World Economic Outlook (WEO) report, 
the International Monetary Fund (IMF) projected that global 
economic growth would slow to around 2.9% in 2024. The 
growth projection for 2024 is lower than the previous projection, 
which was for 3% growth.
In 2024, global monetary conditions will be the most important 
factor influencing the domestic economy. Increasing global risks 
are also expected to have an impact on Indonesia’s economic 
growth, which is projected to reach 5.2% (source: ekon.go.id). 
Meanwhile, the Institute for Economic and Community Research 
- Faculty of Economics and Business, University of Indonesia 
(LPEM FEB UI) estimates 5.0%–5.1% growth in the domestic 
economy in 2024. Even though the momentum for national 
economic recovery is increasing, the risk of a global economic 
slowdown due to global market dynamics still needs to be 
considered.
The simultaneous General Elections will have an impact on 
growth and other macroeconomic indicators next year. On the 
other hand, for the first time, Indonesia will hold simultaneous 
elections at the national, district, and city levels, encouraging the 
injection of large amounts of liquidity into the economy through 
campaigning and public spending. The large multiplier effect 
on the economy will trigger domestic consumption in 2024, 
given that elections at the provincial and regency/city levels are 
anticipated to take place near the end of the year.
Inflation is predicted to remain at around 2.8%, with the average 
Rupiah exchange rate moving around Rp15 thousand per US 
Dollar and the average 10-year Government Securities (SBN) 
interest rate estimate at 6.7%. The price of Indonesian crude oil 
(ICP) is estimated at USD.80 per barrel. Meanwhile, oil and gas 
lifting is projected to reach 625 thousand barrels per day and 
1.03 million barrels of oil equivalent per day (source: ekon.go.id).
The financing industry is predicted to face difficult challenges in 
2024, as business players tend to make conservative projections 
for the core financing objects. The automotive, motorcycle, 
and heavy equipment industries are expected to achieve only 
moderate sales growth.
