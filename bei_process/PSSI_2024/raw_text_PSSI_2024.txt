TINJAUAN EKONOMI GLOBAL & NASIONAL TAHUN 2024
GLOBAL & NATIONAL ECONOMIC OVERVIEW IN 2024
Pada tahun 2024, perekonomian global diproyeksikan 
tumbuh pada kisaran 3% hingga 3,20%, didukung oleh 
peningkatan investasi di sektor infrastruktur dan teknologi 
hijau di negara maju serta kebangkitan konsumsi domestik 
di negara berkembang. Proyeksi ini didasarkan pada laporan 
dari International Monetary Fund (IMF) dan World Bank, 
yang mencatat adanya pemulihan meski tetap dihadapkan 
pada tantangan seperti inflasi global, kebijakan moneter 
ketat, dan ketidakpastian geopolitik.
Sektor energi dan logistik tetap menjadi pendorong utama 
dalam perdagangan internasional, dengan harga komoditas 
cenderung stabil setelah fluktuasi yang signifikan pada 
tahun-tahun sebelumnya. International Energy Agency 
(IEA) mencatat peningkatan permintaan energi di kawasan 
Asia, yang menjadi pasar utama bagi ekspor batubara dan 
logistik energi global.
Di sektor dry bulk shipping, laporan dari asosiasi industri 
pelayaran internasional, BIMCO (Baltic and International 
Maritime Council) pada Oktober 2024 memperkirakan 
pertumbuhan armada dry bulk sebesar 3% pada tahun 2024 
dan 2-3% pada tahun 2025 dan 2026. Lembaga tersebut juga 
menunjukkan permintaan pengangkutan bahan baku seperti 
batubara dan bijih besi yang diproyeksikan meningkat 2,50-
3,50%pada tahun 2024 terutama di Asia dan Eropa. Hal ini 
sejalan dengan pemulihan aktivitas industri dan pembangunan 
infrastruktur besar-besaran di negara berkembang.
Ekonomi Nasional
Perekonomian Indonesia menunjukkan ketahanan yang 
kuat dengan pertumbuhan sebesar 4,95% (year-on-year) 
pada Triwulan III-2024. Menurut data dari Kementerian 
Koordinator Bidang Perekonomian, sektor utama yang 
mendorong pertumbuhan ini adalah pertambangan, industri 
pengolahan, serta transportasi dan pergudangan, sejalan 
dengan meningkatnya ekspor komoditas seperti batubara 
dan kelapa sawit.
Laporan Kementerian Keuangan mencatat bahwa konsumsi 
domestik tetap menjadi motor utama pertumbuhan, dengan 
kontribusi sebesar 55%-60% terhadap PDB. Investasi juga 
mengalami peningkatan, terutama di bidang infrastruktur 
energi dan transportasi, dengan realisasi Penanaman Modal 
Asing (PMA) yang tumbuh 7,20% dibandingkan periode 
yang sama tahun sebelumnya.
Pada sektor energi, data dari MODI ESDM menunjukkan 
bahwa realisasi produksi batubara nasional mencapai 831,57 
juta ton di tahun 2024, dengan peningkatan ekspor ke 
negara-negara Asia seperti Tiongkok dan India. Sementara 
itu, Asosiasi Pertambangan Batubara Indonesia (APBI) 
mencatat bahwa harga batubara acuan (HBA) tetap stabil 
pada kisaran USD 114-131 per ton, didukung oleh permintaan 
yang kuat dari sektor energi global.
In 2024, the global economy is estimated to grow at a rate of 
3% to 3.20%, driven by increased investment in infrastructure 
and green technology in developed countries and a 
revival in domestic consumption in developing countries. 
This projection is based on reports from the International 
Monetary Fund (IMF) and the World Bank, which recorded 
a recovery despite global inflation, tight monetary policy, and 
geopolitical uncertainty.    
Energy and logistics remain primary drivers of international 
trade, with commodity prices remaining stable following 
significant fluctuations in previous years. The International 
Energy Agency (IEA) recorded a growing energy demand 
in Asia, a main market for coal exports and global energy 
logistics.
In the dry bulk shipping sector, a report from the international 
shipping 
industry 
association, 
BIMCO 
(Baltic 
and 
International Maritime Council), released in October 2024, 
estimated a 3% growth in the dry bulk fleet in 2024, followed 
by 2-3% growth in 2025 and 2026. The institution also 
indicated that the demand for transporting raw materials, 
such as coal and iron ore, is projected to increase by 2,50-
3,50% in 2024, particularly in Asia and Europe. This aligns 
with the recovery of industrial activity and the massive 
infrastructure development in developing countries.
National Economy
The Indonesian economy demonstrated strong resilience, 
with growth of 4.95% (year-on-year) in the third quarter of 
2024. According to data from the Coordinating Ministry for 
Economic Affairs, the main sectors contributing to this growth 
were the mining, processing industry, and transportation and 
warehousing sectors, which are in line with increasing exports 
of commodities such as coal and palm oil.
The Ministry of Finance report stated that domestic 
consumption remained the primary driver of growth, 
contributing 55%-60% to the country’s GDP. Investment 
has also increased, particularly in energy and transportation 
infrastructure, with Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) growing 
7,20% compared to the same period in the previous year.
In the energy sector, data from MODI ESDM indicated that 
national coal production reached 831.57 million tons in 
2024, with increased exports to Asian countries, including 
China and India. Meanwhile, the Indonesian Coal Mining 
Association (APBI) reported that the benchmark coal price 
(HBA) remained stable at approximately USD 114-131 per ton, 
supported by strong demand from the global energy sector.

TINJAUAN OPERASI PER SEGMEN USAHA
REVIEW OF OPERATIONS PER BUSINESS SEGMENT
Sektor logistik juga mencatat pertumbuhan positif, didukung 
oleh peningkatan efisiensi transportasi laut. Pelabuhan 
Tanjung Priok dan Pelabuhan Balikpapan menjadi pusat 
distribusi utama untuk pengangkutan batubara dan barang-
barang konsumsi domestik, dengan peningkatan volume 
pengiriman hingga 12% dibandingkan tahun lalu.
Dengan fundamental ekonomi yang solid, pemerintah optimis 
bahwa pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia untuk keseluruhan 
tahun 2024 akan berada di atas 5%. Meskipun ada tantangan 
seperti fluktuasi harga dan regulasi lingkungan, prospek 
untuk industri dry bulk shipping di Indonesia tetap positif 
dengan potensi pertumbuhan yang signifikan.
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan terus mengoptimalkan 
armadanya untuk mendukung solusi logistik yang terintegrasi. 
Dengan perekonomian Indonesia yang terus tumbuh, sektor 
logistik domestik, terutama untuk pengangkutan batubara, 
tetap berkembang pesat.
Hingga akhir tahun 2024, Perseroan memiliki 52  unit armada 
yang terdiri dari 24 unit Kapal Tunda, 19 unit Tongkang, 3  unit 
Tongkang Derek Apung (Floating Crane), 1 unit Fasilitas 
Muatan Apung (FLF), dan 5 unit Kapal Kargo Curah (Mother 
Vessel/ MV).  Armada ini difokuskan pada pengangkutan 
batubara serta komoditas lain seperti nikel, alumina, 
tembaga, dan pupuk.
Batubara tetap menjadi kargo utama yang didorong 
oleh permintaan tinggi dari pasar domestik. Sementara 
itu, diversifikasi layanan ke sektor lain turut memberikan 
nilai tambah yang berkelanjutan bagi kinerja operasional 
Perseroan. Dengan fokus pada pasar domestik dan 
diversifikasi layanan, Perseroan optimis dapat memberikan 
kontribusi signifikan terhadap pertumbuhan ekonomi dan 
meningkatkan kinerja operasional yang berkelanjutan.
The logistics sector also recorded favourable growth, 
supported by increased efficiency of sea transportation. 
Tanjung Priok Port and Balikpapan Port are the main 
distribution centres for the transportation of coal and 
domestic consumer goods, with an increase of 12% in 
shipping volume compared to the previous year.
With solid economic fundamentals, the government is 
optimistic that Indonesia’s economic growth for 2024 will 
exceed 5%. Despite challenges such as price fluctuations 
and environmental regulations, Indonesia’s outlook for the 
dry bulk shipping industry remains positive, with significant 
growth potential.
In 2024, the Company continued to optimize its fleet to 
support integrated logistics solutions. With the continued 
growth of the Indonesian economy, the domestic logistics 
sector, particularly for coal transportation, remained robust. 
As of the end of 2024, the Company had a fleet of 52 units, 
consisting of 24 Tugboats, 19 Barges, 3 Floating Cranes, 1 
Floating Loading Facility (FLF), and 5 Bulk Carriers (MV). 
This fleet is focused on the transportation of coal and other 
commodities such as nickel, alumina, copper, and fertilizer.
Coal remained the primary cargo, driven by strong domestic 
market demand. Meanwhile, service diversification into other 
sectors continued to deliver sustainable added value to the 
Company’s operational performance. With a focus on the 
domestic market and service diversification, the Company 
is optimistic that it can make a significant contribution to 
economic growth and enhance its sustainable operational 
performance.

Fasilitas Muatan Apung 
(FLF)
Floating Load Facility 
(FLF)
Armada yang digunakan untuk memindahkan muatan angkut dari Tongkang ke Kapal Curah Besar yang 
berlokasi di perairan lepas dan dioperasikan oleh 38–40 awak kapal termasuk operator. Perseroan saat ini 
memiliki 1      unit FLF dengan kapasitas muatan 25.000 MT per hari. Seluruh FLF yang dioperasikan dilengkapi 
dengan sistem pengecekan otomatis sampler batubara (automatic coal sampler) dan sistem pemisah besi 
(metal catcher) yang menjadi salah satu keunggulan kompetitif aset Perseroan dibandingkan dengan 
kompetitor, selain mempunyai kemampuan untuk blending batubara.
This fleet is used to transfer cargo from Barges to Bulk Carriers in high waters and operated by 38–40 crew 
members including operators. The Company currently has 1 FLF unit with a load capacity of 25,000 MT per day. 
All FLFs operated are equipped with automatic coal sampler checking system and metal catcher system which 
is one of the competitive advantages of the Company’s assets compared to competitors, in addition to having 
the ability to blend coal.
Tongkang Derek Apung 
(FC)
Floating Crane Barge
Perseroan memiliki      3 unit Tongkang Derek Apung dengan kapasitas muatan 18.000 MT per hari. Tongkang 
derek apung ini dirancang untuk memiliki mobilitas tinggi, memungkinkan fleksibilitas operasional di berbagai 
lokasi, baik di pelabuhan, sungai, maupun perairan lepas, guna mendukung efisiensi dan produktivitas 
perusahaan dalam kegiatan bongkar muat.
The Company has 3 Floating Crane Barges with a loading capacity of 18,000 MT per day. These floating crane 
barges are designed for high mobility, enabling operational flexibility in various locations, including ports, rivers, 
and high seas, to support the Company’s efficiency and productivity in stevedoring activities.
Kapal Kargo Curah (MV)
Bulk Carriers
Kapal angkutan dengan muatan besar yang digunakan untuk mengangkut dan memindahkan batubara, 
mineral, maupun komoditas lainnya untuk rute antar-pulau dalam negeri atau pun antar-negara. Kapal 
dilengkapi dengan 4 cargo crane yang mampu mengangkat sampai dengan 30 ton (Safety Working Load), 
mempunyai 4 set kubik meter grab dan 5 ruang muat. Kecepatan jelajah kapal sampai dengan 14 knot. Terdiri 
dari 5 unit Kapal Kargo Curah (MV), yaitu 2 unit kelas Supramax dengan kapasitas masing-masing 56 ribu metrik 
ton dan 3 unit kelas Handysize dengan kapasitas masing-masing 32 ribu metrik ton. Hal ini merupakan salah 
satu strategi diversifikasi bisnis yang memperluas pangsa pasar dengan tidak hanya mengandalkan komoditas 
angkut batubara
Cargo ships with large loads used to transport and move coal, minerals, and other commodities for domestic or 
inter-country routes. The ship is equipped with 4 cargo cranes capable of lifting up to 30 tons (Safety Working 
Load), has 4 sets of cubic meter grab and 5 loading spaces. The cruising speed of the ship is up to 14 knots. The 
Company has 5 Bulk Carriers (MV), namely 2 units of the Supramax class with a capacity of 56 thousand metric 
tons each and 3 units of the Handysize class with a capacity of 32 thousand metric tons, respectively. This is one 
of the business diversification strategies that expand market share by not only relying on coal transportation 
commodities.

Nickel Market Share
In 2024, Indonesia strengthened its position as a dominant 
player in the global nickel industry. Data from the Indonesian 
Nickel Miners Association (APNI) showed that in 2024, 
Indonesia contributed around 53% to 65% of the world’s 
total nickel production and supply. In addition, a report from 
the International Energy Agency (IEA) in its “Global Critical 
Mineral Outlook 2024” reported that Indonesia’s market 
share in the nickel industry continues to increase, making 
this country one of the world’s leading nickel producers. This 
increase was mainly supported by the downstream policy that 
has banned the export of raw nickel ore since 2020, driving 
investment in domestic processing and smelting facilities. As 
a result, Indonesia’s processed nickel production is projected 
to increase from 1,7 million tons in 2024 to 3.8 million tons 
in 2033. With abundant nickel reserves, Indonesia has the 
potential to serve as an electric vehicle (EV) export base in 
Asia, considering that nickel remains the main component in 
electric batteries, with a market share of 60%.
