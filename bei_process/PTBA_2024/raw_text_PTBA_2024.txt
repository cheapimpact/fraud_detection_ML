Coal Industry Analysis

stable compared to 2023. According to the International 
Energy Agency (IEA), global coal consumption reached 
8.77 billion tons in 2024 and is projected to remain at that 
level until 2027.
Despite stable global demand, coal prices are under 
pressure. Throughout 2024, global coal prices fluctuated 
with a downward trend compared to the previous year. 
According to the World Bank, the average coal price in 
2024 is projected to reach USD 125 per ton, down 27.7% 
compared to 2023, and the lowest since 2021.
Data from the Indonesian Ministry of Energy and Mineral 
Resources (ESDM) shows that the highest Reference 
Coal Price (HBA) in 2024 was recorded in October, at 
USD 131 per ton, while the lowest point was in March 
2024 at USD 109 per ton.

Secara operasional, Perusahaan juga terus melakukan

2.	 Pilar Logistik, Infrastruktur, dan  Trading
Posisi tambang batu bara Bukit Asam yang berada 
di tengah pulau Sumatra mengharuskan adanya 
transportasi batu bara dari tambang ke area pelabuhan. 
Oleh karena itu, peningkatan kapasitas angkutan 
batu bara merupakan salah satu prioritas utama 
bagi Perusahaan. Di tahun 2024, untuk mendukung 
pengembangan  peningkatan kapasitas angkutan 
batu bara relasi TE-Kramasan yang merupakan bentuk 
sinergi dengan PT Kereta Api Indonesia, PTBA 
melakukan upaya seperti percepatan pembangunan 
sarana prasarana Train Loading Station (TLS) dan 
Coal Handling Facility (CHF) 6 dan 7 di Tanjung Enim 
dengan kapasitas total 20 juta ton per tahun yang 
diharapkan akan beroperasi pada Q2-2026.
Selain itu, optimasi kapasitas angkut kereta api 
eksisting juga terus ditingkatkan, salah satunya 
dengan penambahan gerbong dalam setiap rangkaian 
kereta sehingga pada tahun ini dapat terlihat adanya 
peningkatan yang cukup signifikan dari segi jumlah 
angkutan. Tidak hanya optimasi angkutan melalui 
mode kereta api, tetapi perusahaan juga telah berhasil 
meningkatkan kapasitas angkutan batu bara dengan 
menggunakan jalur trucking khusus batu bara.
1.	 Mining Pillar
In the mining sector, the Company continues striving 
to maintain existing reserves and control reserves 
through mapping of economic mining potentials. In 
addition, the Company also continues to optimize the 
utilization of existing coal reserves, as shown by the 
trend of coal production which continues to increase 
each year.
The Company also encourages increased production 
of its subsidiary, namely PT Internasional Prima Coal 
which operates in East Kalimantan. This is shown 
by the increase in production by PT IPC from the 
previous year.
Operationally, the Company also continues to carry 
out cost leadership, one of which is by digitizing the 
end-to-end business process through the CISEA 
application, an application independently developed 
by the company internally. In addition, the success 
in optimizing mining is indicated by the success in 
maintaining the strip ratio at 6.23 x (times) and also 
lower than the target of 6.44 x (times).
2.	 Logistics, Infrastructure, and Trading Pillars
The location of the Bukit Asam coal mine in the 
middle of Sumatra Island requires coal transportation 
from the mine to the port area. Therefore, increasing 
coal transportation capacity is one of the Company’s 
top priorities. In 2024, to support the development 
and capacity enhancement of coal transportation for 
the TE-Kramasan route as part of the synergy with 
PT Kereta Api Indonesia, PTBA undertook efforts 
such as accelerating the construction of Train Loading 
Station (TLS) and Coal Handling Facility (CHF) 6 and 
7 in Tanjung Enim, with a total capacity of 20 million 
tons per year, which are expected to be operational 
by Q2-2026.
In addition, optimization of existing train transportation 
capacity is also continuously improved, including 
by adding carriages in each train to bring significant 
increase in terms of the number of transportation. 
Besides optimization of transportation through train 
mode, the company has also succeeded in increasing 
coal transportation capacity using special coal trucking 
lanes.

Coal Segment Challenges and Strategy

Directors’ Statement on the Fairness of 
Affiliated and Related Transactions
The Board of Directors ensures that all transaction 
activities have gone through adequate procedures and 
are in accordance with the arm’s length principle. Before 
conducting material transactions related to related 
parties or transactions that have the potential to contain 
elements of conflict of interest in the Company, the 
approval of the Company’s Board of Commissioners 
must be based on the recommendations/considerations 
of the Audit Committee before material transactions with 
related parties are carried out, as stated in the PTBA 
Audit Committee Charter. In addition, in accordance

Transactions and Conflict of Interest Transactions and the 
Decree of the Board of Directors of PT Bukit Asam Tbk 
No. 067/0100/2023 concerning Guidelines for Affiliated 
Transactions and Conflict of Interest Transactions, these 
transactions must be published to the public and reported 
to the FSA.

Business Prospects
The Company’s business is currently still dominated 
by coal sales, so future business prospects will still be 
greatly influenced by demand conditions and coal price 
fluctuations, both in the global and domestic markets. In 
2024, global demand for seaborne thermal coal reached 
its highest point in history. Indonesia is still the largest 
thermal coal exporter with a production of more than 770 
million tons, which is also the highest production figure in 
Indonesian history.
The decline in coal prices in early 2025 has continued 
due to a global oversupply compared to demand, with 
countries like China and India prioritizing the use of 
domestically produced coal. Coal prices are projected 
to increase in mid-year in line with rising demand during 
the summer season, which drives electricity usage for air 
conditioning.

1.	 Mining
In this pillar, the development strategy includes 
strategic initiatives in the fields of reserves, 
production, and operations. In terms of reserves, the 
Company focuses on maintaining existing reserves 
and controlling new reserves. Maintaining existing 
reserves is carried out through strict monitoring of 
existing boundaries and extending mining business 
permits in a timely manner. Then, in relation to 
controlling new reserves, the Company is open to 
every option, whether through acquisition, auction, or 
assignment from the State.
Furthermore, regarding the strategy in the production 
sector, the company will gradually increase production 
each year, in order to accelerate the monetization of 
coal reserves. In addition, the increase in production 
is expected to increase efficiency in operational 
cost units, since it is in line with the principle of 
economies of scale. Moreover, the implementation 
of digitalization of operations and the application of 
technology allows for reduced environmental impacts 
and increased work safety for greater operational cost 
efficiency.
2.	 Logistics, Infrastructure, and Trading
This pillar is related to development in the fields 
of logistics, infrastructure, and trading. The main 
objective of this pillar is to support a sustainable 
supply chain through reliable mining operations and 
coal distribution to ports, as well as additional coal 
sales destination options. This includes unlocking 
logistics, sustainable infrastructure development, and 
effective trading. In relation to logistics development, 
the Company continues to increase rail transport 
capacity and seek other transportation mode options 
to keep up with the rate of production increase. 
For infrastructure, the Company also continues to 
make selective new investments that aim to ensure 
the continuity of the company’s business. Lastly 
the development of the Trading business does not 
only include buying and selling mining products, 
but also seeking ways to diversify market sources, 
and ensuring that products can reach consumers 
efficiently and safely.

This pillar refers to the processing of coal into more 
valuable derivative products, the development of 
alternative energy commodity businesses, and 
increasing energy efficiency. In addition, this pillar also 
includes innovation in renewable energy technology 
and implementing cleaner and more efficient 
solutions. This includes utility management and the 
provision of reliable and sustainable energy services

and better waste management. This pillar also includes

initiatives from this pillar includes PTBA’s involvement

business segments.
Decarbonization Strategy
In line with business development, the Company is 
also committed to decarbonizing the entire company’s 
value chain as a form of support for achieving Net Zero 
Emission (NZE) in the Indonesian energy sector by 2060. 
The Company’s decarbonization strategy is based on 3 
(three) main pillars, namely Operational Decarbonization, 
Transition to a Cleaner Portfolio, and Emission Offsets.
