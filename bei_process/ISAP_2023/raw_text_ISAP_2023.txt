Tinjauan Ekonomi
International Monetary Fund (IMF) memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global di tahun 2023 
mencapai 3,1% dengan gambaran “resilient but 
slow” atau “tangguh meskipun lambat.” Proyeksi ini 
dilatarbelakangi adanya peningkatan di Tiongkok, 
Amerika Serikat, dan negara-negara berkembang, 
serta adanya asumsi bahwa komoditas bahan 
bakar dan non-bahan bakar yang turun di tahun 
2024-2025 dan tingkat suku bunga di negara-
negara maju akan menurun. 
Namun demikian, sejumlah risiko yang bisa 
menganggu proyeksi pertumbuhan ekonomi global 
di tahun 2024 dapat terus terjadi, terutama invasi 
di Jalur Gaza dan Laut Merah, dan cuaca ekstrem. 
Selain itu, Bank Indonesia turut memproyeksikan 
beberapa risiko global tetap perlu diwaspadai, 
seperti adanya pelemahan ekonomi di sejumlah 
negara utama, termasuk Tiongkok, serta kepastian 
waktu dan besarnya penurunan suku bunga 
moneter negara maju, khususnya Fed Funds Rate 
(FFR).
Sementara, ekonomi Indonesia terus bertumbuh 
kuat di sepanjang tahun 2023 yang ditopang oleh 
permintaan domestik, investasi sejalan dengan 
akselerasi belanja Pemerintah pada akhir tahun, 
serta percepatan penyelesaian beberapa Proyek 
Strategis Nasional (PSN). Berdasarkan Badan Pusat 
Statistik, ekonomi Indonesia tumbuh sebesar 5,05%, 
yang mana pertumbuhan tersebut ditopang 
oleh produk domestik bruto (PDB), baik menurut 
lapangan usaha dan pengeluaran.
Pada komponen lapangan usaha, pertumbuhan 
tertinggi dicapai oleh sektor transportasi dan 
pergudangan sebesar 10,33%; disusul oleh jasa 
lainnya dan pengadaan listrik & gas masing-
masing sebesar 10,15% dan 8,68%. Sedangkan 
komponen pengeluaran, pertumbuhan tertinggi 
dicapai oleh konsumsi LNPRT sebesar 9,83%; disusul 
oleh konsumsi rumah tangga dan PMTB masing-
masing sebesar 4,82% dan 4,40%.

Economic Review
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projects 
global economic growth in 2023 to reach 3.1% 
with a picture of "resilient but slow". This projection 
is motivated by increases in China, the United 
States, and developing countries, as well as the 
assumption that fuel and non-fuel commodities 
will decline in 2024-2025 and interest rates in 
developed countries will decline. 
However, a number of risks that could disrupt 
global economic growth projections in 2024 may 
continue, especially the invasion of the Gaza Strip 
and Red Sea, and extreme weather. In addition, Bank 
Indonesia also projects that several global risks still 
require vigilance, such as economic weakness in 
a number of major countries, including China, as 
well as certainty on the timing and magnitude of 
monetary interest rate cuts in advanced countries, 
particularly the Fed Funds Rate (FFR).
Meanwhile, Indonesia's economy continues to grow 
strongly throughout 2023, supported by domestic 
demand, investment in line with the acceleration 
of government spending at the end of the year, 
and the acceleration of the completion of several 
National Strategic Projects (NSP). Based on the 
Central Bureau of Statistics, Indonesia's economy 
grew by 5.05%, which growth was supported by 
gross domestic product (GDP), both in terms of 
business and expenditure.
In the business sector component, the highest 
growth was achieved by the transportation and 
warehousing sector of 10.33%; Followed by other 
services and electricity & gas procurement by 
10.15% and 8.68% respectively. As for the expenditure 
component, the highest growth was achieved 
by LNPRT consumption of 9.83%; followed by 
household consumption and PMTB at 4.82% and 
4.40% respectively.

Industry Review
Growth occurred from various sectors, such as the 
transportation & warehousing sector, as well as 
construction which are projected to grow by 13.96% 
and 4.91% respectively. This condition is in line with 
the increasing purchasing power and mobility 
of the community which affects the Company's 
business conditions. In addition, the Company's 
business activities refer to the number of vehicles 
sold throughout the year. Reporting from data from 
the Association of Indonesian Automotive Industries 
(GAIKINDO) noted that the number of exports made 
in Indonesia increased by 6.7% or 505,134 units.
Likewise, construction growth grew strongly with a 
contribution of 9.86% of GDP. This growth is driven by 
the continuation of National Strategic Projects (NSP) 
such as roads, bridges, toll roads construction, and 
several house constructions. With this condition, 
the Company is optimistic that it can improve the 
quality and service in the provision of machinery 
and machine tools, both in the automotive and 
non-automotive industries, as well as expand 
business and the provision of products related to 
the construction sector.
