Tahun 2024 adalah periode penting dalam perjalanan 
energi Indonesia. Dengan tantangan global yang semakin

Perubahan iklim global, transisi energi menuju sumber

akibat pertumbuhan ekonomi dan jumlah penduduk 
menjadi beberapa faktor yang mendasari pentingnya

Outlook Energi Indonesia 2024 menyimpulkan antara lain:
Konsumsi listrik diproyeksikan meningkat dari 315 TWh 
pada tahun 2023 menjadi 713 TWh pada tahun 2034. 
Dalam 10 tahun kedepan akan terjadi pergeseran 
pemakaian listrik di sektor Rumah Tangga yang 
pangsanya 39% pada 2023 akan turun menjadi 26% - 32%

37% per tahun, disebabkan tumbuhnya kendaraan listrik 
yang cukup signiﬁkan.
2024 is a crucial period in Indonesia's energy journey. 
With increasingly complex global challenges, such as 
energy price fluctuations, pressure to accelerate the 
energy transition, and demands to fulﬁll commitments to 
reduce carbon emissions, the Indonesian government is 
required to ensure that national energy policies remain 
relevant, competitive, and sustainable.
Global climate change, energy transition to renewable 
energy sources, and increasing energy needs due to 
economic growth and population are some of the factors 
that underlie the importance of planning and managing 
the energy sector more effectively and efﬁciently. In 
addition, Indonesia is also committed to meet global 
targets, such as those stated in the Paris Agreement 
related to reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, as 
well as contributing to achieve the Sustainable 
Development Goals (SDGs).
Indonesia Energy Outlook 2024 concludes among others:
Electricity consumption is projected to increase from 315 
TWh in 2023 to 713 TWh in 2034. In the next 10 years, 
there will be a shift in electricity use in the Household 
sector, whose share was 39% in 2023, will decrease to 
26% - 32% in 2034. Meanwhile, the substitution of 
electricity-based equipment in the Industrial sector will 
have an impact on the increase in the share of electricity 
consumption in the industrial sector from 37% in 2023 to 
47% in 2034. Speciﬁcally, the Transportation sector, 
whose electricity use is currently below 1%, will 
experience an increase of around 23% - 37% per year, due 
to the signiﬁcant growth of electric vehicles.

The share of renewable energy in primary energy supply 
will increase from 13.3% in 2023 to 26% in 2034 if the 
market share of electric vehicles continues to grow, 
substituting LPG for electric stoves, and increasing 
biofuels as well as the construction of more massive 
renewable power plants, especially Biomass Coﬁring 
and solar power plant because they contribute the most 
to achieving the mix.
On the other hand, Government Regulation Number 79 
of 2014 concerning the National Energy Policy, targets 
the portion of New Renewable Energy (NRE) in the 
national energy supply or energy mix of 23% in 2025 
and at least 31% in 2050. 
The implementation of the National Energy Policy as 
stated in Government Regulation Number 79 of 2014 
has experienced signiﬁcant dynamics due to changes in 
the strategic environment, both at the national and 
global levels. Several main factors influencing these 
changes include Indonesia's economic growth target to 
become a developed country by 2045, the acceleration 
of energy technology development and diversiﬁcation of 
NRE types that substantially increase the contribution of 
NRE in the national primary energy mix, as well as the 
major role of the energy sector in supporting the 
achievement of the Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emission 
reduction target and Net-Zero Emissions (NZE) by 2060.
For this reason, the government is currently preparing 
the formulation of the Draft of Government Regulation 
on National Energy Policy (RPP KEN) as a replacement 
for Government Regulation Number 79 of 2014, where in 
this new RPP KEN, the target for the NRE mix in 2060 is 
increased to 70%. This is because based on the 
realization of the EBT mix in previous years which was 
always below the target. Therefor, by increasing the 
target to 70% in 2060, it is hoped that the realization can 
reach above 50% so that net zero emissions can be 
achieved in 2060. Other changes to the new RPP KEN 
are the economic growth rate adjusted for Post-COVID, 
which is 4-5% and the equivalence of nuclear energy 
with New Renewable Energy (NRE). Nuclear energy in 
Government Regulation Number 79 of 2014 is the last 
option, while in this new RPP KEN it is equivalent to 
other NRE.

meningkatkan pasokan listrik dari sumber EBT dalam 
kebijakan energi nasional, kapasitas pembangkit listrik

pembangkit listrik EBT pada tahun 2025 mencapai 16 GW. 
Ke depan, kami optimis bahwa prospek usaha Perseroan 
akan terus bertumbuh dengan baik karena kebutuhan 
listrik khususnya yang bersumber dari EBT akan terus 
meningkat dan menjadi fokus pemerintah Indonesia 
maupun global.

increase electricity supply from EBT sources in national 
energy policy, renewable electricity generation capacity 
is projected to continue to grow. For the short term, 
namely 2025, the Ministry of Energy and Mineral 
Resources has lowered the target for the NRE mix to 17% 
to 19% from the previous target of 23%. However, in the 
long term, the renewable energy mix target has been 
signiﬁcantly increased, namely from 30% to 58% - 61% in 
2050 and 70% - 72% in 2060.
The Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources is also 
targeting an increase in the installed capacity of 
renewable energy power plants by 1.2 GW in 2025, so that 
the installed capacity of renewable energy power plants 
in 2025 will reach 16 GW.
Going forward, we are optimistic that the Company's 
growth prospects will be bright because the need for 
electricity, especially that sourced from NRE, will 
continue to increase and become the focus of the 
Indonesian and global governments.
