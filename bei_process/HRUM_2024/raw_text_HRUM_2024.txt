Industrial Overview
The national economic resilience, despite the weakening 
global economy, had a positive impact on business 
development across various sectors. The mining and 
excavation industry recorded a growth of 4.9% in 2024. 
Representing 9.2% of GDP, this sector remains the fifth 
largest contributor and a key pillar for national economic 
growth. Furthermore, non-tax state revenue (PNBP) from 
the Energy and Mineral Resources (ESDM) sector exceeded 
the target by 115%, with realization reaching Rp269.5 trillion 
from the target of Rp234.2 trillion. The mineral and coal 
sector contributed Rp140.5 trillion.
In line with that, the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources 
reported that domestic coal production in 2024 reached 
831.1 million tons, exceeding the target of 710 million tons 
by 117%. This is a positive achievement, considering that 
coal production in 2023 only reached 775 million tons. 
The increase in domestic coal production also contributed 
to the increase in domestic coal consumption, with 
utilization reaching 233 million tons in 2024 from 213 million 
tons in 2023. Furthermore, coal exports in 2024 were 
recorded at 555 million tons or equivalent to 30-35% of 
global consumption.
Regarding HBA in Indonesia, the coal price in 2024 closed 
at USD122.5/ton, an increase of 4.4% compared to 
USD117.4/ton in 2023. However, the average HBA price for 
2024 decreased to USD121.1/ton from USD201.1/ton in 
2023.

Business Prospects
Global and national economic growth projections for 2025 
show dynamics that warrant close attention from economic 
actors and policy makers. The International Monetary Fund 
(IMF) in its report predicts that global economic growth 
will remain steady at 3.2% in 2025. This prediction was 
slightly revised from the previous 3.3%, citing increasing 
geopolitical risks and trade protectionism that could 
hamper the pace of global economic growth.
Despite global challenges, the Indonesian government is 
optimistic that the national economy will grow positively. 
In the 2025 Draft State Budget (RAPBN), the government 
is targeting economic growth of 5.2%, slightly higher than 
Bank Indonesia’s projection, which estimates national 
economic growth to be in the range of 4.7% to 5.5% in 2025. 
The government’s optimism is based on efforts to maintain 
economic stability and encourage domestic demand. The 
strategies taken include controlling inflation, creating jobs, 
and distributing social assistance and subsidies to maintain 
people’s purchasing power.
Indonesia’s coal and nickel industries faced a range 
of prospects, influenced by global market dynamics, 
government policies, and industry trends. In the coal 
industry, despite global pressure to reduce fossil fuel use, 
domestic and global coal demand is projected to remain 
high in 2025. The Indonesian Coal Mining Association 
(APBI) is optimistic about the industry performance, with 
increased demand driven by domestic energy needs and 
exports. The increase in domestic demand can be seen 
from the potential increase in the coal Domestic Market 
Obligation (DMO) for 2025, which is expected to reach 
229.3 million tons or 4.1% higher than the DMO target for 
2024, which is 220 million tons. Data from the International 
Energy Agency (IEA) projects that global coal demand 
will reach a new record by 2027. The IEA projects that coal 
demand will increase to almost 8.9 billion tons in 2027, or 
around 1% higher than the 2024 level.
The nickel industry faced its own opportunities and 
challenges. Indonesia’s nickel ore demand in 2025 is 
estimated to reach 220 million to 230 million tons, with 
production projections not much different from that of 
2024. In 2025, the government reduces the nickel ore quota 
through the approved RKAB, which is around 246 million 
tons, down from 271 million tons in 2024. This reduction 
continues in 2026, with a quota set at around 198 million 
tons. This policy is part of an effort to organize and evaluate

Perjanjian Signifikan
Perseroan mengadakan Perjanjian Utang Piutang dengan

Perseroan sebagai pihak pertama memiliki kesepakatan

ditinjau kembali setiap waktu oleh pihak pertama. Jangka 
waktu pemberian utang menjadi 5 tahun terhitung sejak 
tanggal yang tercantum dalam Addendum-3 dan demikian 
menjadi berakhir tanggal 22 Maret 2025.

The Company entered into a Debt Agreement with TBH

signed a Debt Agreement on February 7, 2011, which was last 
amended by Addendum-2 to the Debt Agreement dated 
January 2, 2017. In Addendum-3, TBH as the second party 
and the Company as the first party agreed to a revolving

at an interest rate of 5% per year and USD5,000,000 
at an interest rate of 2% per year. Interest on debt in Rupiah 
and USD will be due at the end of each year and can be 
reviewed at any time by the first party. The term of the loan 
is 5 years starting from the date stated in Addendum-3, and 
thus, ends on March 22, 2025.
