Tahun 2024 ditandai oleh meningkatnya ketidakpastian global 
yang dipicu oleh eskalasi konflik geopolitik di Timur Tengah 
serta perang antara Rusia dan Ukraina yang belum menunjukkan 
tanda-tanda mereda. Kedua konflik tersebut telah menyebabkan 
disrupsi pada rantai pasok global dan mendorong volatilitas 
harga komoditas, terutama bahan baku industri, yang pada 
akhirnya berdampak pada biaya operasional di berbagai sektor 
usaha. Di sisi lain, ketegangan perdagangan antara Amerika 
Serikat dan Tiongkok kembali memanas, menambah beban 
terhadap prospek pemulihan ekonomi global, terutama bagi 
negara-negara berkembang dan emerging markets (EMDE) 
yang bergantung pada perdagangan internasional dan arus 
investasi asing.
Perekonomian Amerika Serikat mencatatkan pertumbuhan 
sebesar 2,4% secara tahunan pada kuartal keempat 2024, 
melambat dari 3,1% pada kuartal sebelumnya. Perlambatan ini 
utamanya disebabkan oleh menurunnya aktivitas investasi dan 
ekspor, meskipun konsumsi domestik tetap menjadi penopang 
utama. Sementara itu, inflasi AS mulai melandai, namun arah 
kebijakan suku bunga oleh Federal Reserve yang masih penuh 
ketidakpastian tetap menjadi faktor risiko signifikan bagi 
stabilitas pasar global.
Di kawasan Eropa, tekanan inflasi energi dan penurunan 
konsumsi publik mengakibatkan stagnasi ekonomi. Dua ekonomi 
terbesar di zona Euro—Jerman dan Prancis—masing-masing 
mengalami kontraksi sebesar 0,2% dan 0,1%, memberikan 
dampak tambahan pada sentimen pasar dunia. Sementara itu, 
Tiongkok mencatatkan pertumbuhan sebesar 5,4% sepanjang 
tahun, menunjukkan tren pemulihan yang cukup stabil meskipun 
belum sepenuhnya kembali ke level sebelum pandemi di kisaran 
6–7%.
Di 
tengah 
tantangan 
global 
tersebut, 
negara-negara 
berkembang, termasuk Indonesia, menghadapi tekanan arus 
modal keluar serta fluktuasi harga komoditas global. Namun 
demikian, ekonomi domestik Indonesia menunjukkan resiliensi 
yang kuat. Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia mencapai 5,03% 
sepanjang tahun 2024, didorong oleh peningkatan investasi 
dan konsumsi dalam negeri. Salah satu indikator penting adalah 
pertumbuhan Pembentukan Modal Tetap Bruto (PMTB) sebesar 
4,61%, tertinggi dalam enam tahun terakhir, yang mencerminkan 
optimisme pelaku pasar terhadap prospek ekonomi nasional.
Stabilitas ekonomi nasional juga ditopang oleh tingkat inflasi 
yang terkendali, di mana inflasi tahunan tercatat sebesar 1,57% 
pada akhir 2024—berada di batas bawah target Bank Indonesia. 
Kondisi ini memberikan ruang bagi sektor usaha untuk beroperasi 
dalam iklim bisnis yang lebih stabil dan terukur. Sektor industri 
pengolahan turut memberikan kontribusi signifikan terhadap 
Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) nasional, yakni sebesar 18,98%, 
yang tidak hanya berperan sebagai pendorong utama ekspor 
tetapi juga mendorong pertumbuhan sektor-sektor pendukung 
lainnya seperti manufaktur dan energi.
The year 2024 was marked by increasing global uncertainty driven 
by the escalation of geopolitical conflicts in the Middle East and 
the ongoing war between Russia and Ukraine, which showed 
no signs of subsiding. Both conflicts have caused disruptions in 
global supply chains and led to volatility in commodity prices, 
particularly industrial raw materials, which ultimately impacted 
operational costs across various business sectors. On the other 
hand, trade tensions between the United States and China have 
once again heated up, adding further burden to the prospects 
of global economic recovery, especially for developing countries 
and emerging markets (EMDE) that rely heavily on international 
trade and foreign investment flows.
The U.S. economy recorded a growth of 2.4% year-on-year in the 
fourth quarter of 2024, slowing down from 3.1% in the previous 
quarter. This slowdown was mainly caused by a decrease in 
investment and exports, although domestic consumption 
remained the main driver. Meanwhile, U.S. inflation began to 
subside, but the Federal Reserve’s interest rate policy, still full of 
uncertainty, remained a significant risk factor for global market 
stability.
In Europe, energy inflation pressures and declining public 
consumption led to economic stagnation. The two largest 
economies in the Eurozone—Germany and France—both 
contracted by 0.2% and 0.1%, respectively, which further 
dampened global market sentiment. Meanwhile, China recorded 
a growth rate of 5.4% for the year, showing a relatively stable 
recovery trend, though still far from returning to pre-pandemic 
levels of around 6-7%.
In the midst of these global challenges, developing countries, 
including Indonesia, faced pressures from capital outflows and 
fluctuating commodity prices. However, Indonesia’s domestic 
economy showed strong resilience. Indonesia’s economic 
growth reached 5.03% throughout 2024, driven by increased 
investment and domestic consumption. One key indicator was 
the growth of Gross Fixed Capital Formation (GFCF) by 4.61%, 
the highest in six years, reflecting the optimism of market players 
regarding the prospects of the national economy.
The national economic stability was also supported by controlled 
inflation, which was recorded at 1.57% year-on-year at the end 
of 2024—well within the Bank Indonesia target range. This 
condition provided space for business sectors to operate in a 
more stable and measured business climate. The manufacturing 
sector also made a significant contribution to the national Gross 
Domestic Product (GDP), accounting for 18.98%, playing a 
major role not only in driving exports but also in boosting the 
growth of supporting sectors such as manufacturing and energy.
