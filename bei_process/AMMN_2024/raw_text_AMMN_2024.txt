INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
Indonesia posted economic growth of 5.03% in 2024, below the 
official target of 5.2% and last year’s growth of 5.05%, marking 
the slowest growth in last three years. This was mainly due to 
a global climate of uncertainty fueled by geopolitical tensions, 
growing fragmentation, and ongoing economic stagnation. For 
2025, the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) growth target remains 
at 5.2%. However, Bank Indonesia recently revised its growth 
forecast for this year to 4.7%-5.5%, down from 4.8%-5.6%, 
citing US tariff disruptions. 
The country’s macroeconomic fundamentals remained robust, 
supported by inflation of 1.57% in 2024, which remained under 
control within the 2.5% ±1% target for the 2024-2025 period. 
Meanwhile, Bank Indonesia’s prudent monetary policy, which 
focused on stimulating growth while maintaining stability, has 
allowed the country to navigate external shocks and attract 
investment.
Ekonomi Indonesia tumbuh sebesar 5,03% pada tahun 2024, di 
bawah target resmi 5,2% dan pertumbuhan tahun sebelumnya 
sebesar 5,05%, yang menandai pertumbuhan terendah dalam 
tiga tahun terakhir. Hal ini terutama disebabkan oleh kondisi 
ketidakpastian global yang dipicu oleh ketegangan geopolitik, 
meningkatnya fragmentasi, dan stagnasi ekonomi yang terus 
berlanjut. Untuk tahun 2025, target pertumbuhan Produk 
Domestik Bruto (PDB) tetap sebesar 5,2%. Namun, Bank 
Indonesia baru saja merevisi perkiraan pertumbuhan untuk tahun 
ini menjadi 4,7%-5,5%, turun dari 4,8%-5,6%, karena adanya 
gangguan tarif AS. 
Fundamental makroekonomi Indonesia tetap kuat, yang 
didukung oleh inflasi sebesar 1,57% pada tahun 2024 dan tetap 
terkendali dalam rentang target 2,5% ± 1% untuk periode 2024-
2025. Sementara itu, kebijakan moneter Bank Indonesia yang 
berhati-hati dan berfokus pada stimulasi pertumbuhan dengan 
tetap menjaga stabilitas, telah memungkinkan Indonesia untuk 
menghadapi guncangan eksternal dan menarik investasi.
Copper is one of the most versatile metals, and an 
essential commodity in numerous applications, ranging 
from electrical wiring, electronics, piping, and plumbing to 
renewable energy infrastructure and artificial intelligence. 
The global drive towards electrification, digitalization and 
clean energy amid rabid urbanization and the increasingly 
urgent climate crisis has made copper one of the most 
sought-after metals over the last two decades. Copper 
consumption from electric vehicles, renewables, and 
electrical network infrastructure will drive higher production 
of associated copper semis production, such as copper wire 
rod and foil, over the next decade.
This surging demand, particularly from the energy sector, and 
tightening supply drove copper prices to a record high in the 
second quarter of 2024. Prices strengthened again in Q3 on 
the back of China’s announcement of a substantial economic 
stimulus package. Nevertheless, the market remains subject 
to volatility contributed by macroeconomic conditions, 
industry-specific factors, seasonal trends, and speculative 
positioning.

The Group has a comprehensive array of infrastructure assets. 
In addition to our processing plant, which is currently under 
expansion, we recently completed a copper smelter. The site 
energy needs are met by our 112 MW coal-fired power plant, 
45 MW diesel-fired back-up power plant, 26.8 MWp solar 
power plant and Combine Cycle Power Plant (“CCPP”), which 
will have an installed capacity of 450 MW when completed. 
Our deepwater port at Benete boasts a ferry terminal, air 
services, power plant and three shipping jetties. Meanwhile, 
our townsite, which accommodates around 10,000 people, 
is fully serviced by access roads, a school, clinics and other 
support facilities, including water desalination plants. 
Ore from the Batu Hijau mine first goes through the primary 
crusher, which is adjacent to the open pit mine, before 
being sent by an overland conveyor to the processing plant’s 
270,000-ton stockpile. In addition to the stockpile, the 
processing plant comprises two semi-autogenous grinding 
(SAG) mills, four ball mills, and flotation circuits. Copper 
concentrate is then transported via an 18 km long pipeline to 
Benete, where it is dewatered and stored.
AMMAN’s strong commitment to minimizing our environmental 
impact is reflected in the actions we have taken over the last 
eight years since acquiring Batu Hijau. In addition to making 
significant improvements in the management of tailings 
and other waste, water efficiency and quality, and energy 
efficiency, we have adopted innovative approaches and state-
of-the-art technology as part of our award-winning mine 
reclamation program. At the same time, we are accelerating 
the decarbonization of our energy supplies with our ground-
mounted solar PV facility and the recent integration of our 
gas-fired CCPP. Full details about our Environmental, Social 
and Governance (“ESG”) programs and initiatives can be 
found in our Sustainability Report.

MINING AND PROCESSING 
AMMAN through its subsidiary, AMNT, achieved another record 
in mining productivity, largely due to favorable weather 
conditions, with total material mined in 2024 rising by 2%, 
from 316 Mt to 322 Mt. This resulted in the highest quantity 
of ex-pit material mined since Batu Hijau’s first production in 
2000. Phase 8 waste removal continued as planned and ore 
production is expected to begin in 2025. The mining cost per 
unit in 2024 was relatively stable compared to 2023, despite 
longer haul distances and inflationary pressures on equipment 
and labor, which were balanced by efficiencies and a higher 
volume of material mined.
Moreover, AMNT’s processing operations achieved record-
breaking gold production from higher gold grade ore from 
Phase 7, with total mill throughput in 2024 rising by 6%, from 
36 Mt to 39 Mt. Due to favorable weather condition, more 
fresh ore and less stockpile ore was fed to the processing 
plant in 2024: fresh ore rose by 102% from 11 Mt to 22 Mt 
and stockpile was down by 35% from 26 Mt to 17 Mt. This 
resulted in the highest gold production since Batu Hijau’s first 
production in 2000.

Sales/revenue
Net sales increased by 31.0%, reaching US$2,664 million, 
compared to US$2,033 million in 2023. This growth was 
largely driven by higher gold sales volume, from production of 
high-grade ore, along with favorable realized gold and copper 
prices, which rose by 23.1% and 9.9%, respectively. The strong 
financial performance was mainly attributable to gold sales, 
which accounted for approximately 55.0% of total sales, up 
from 45.0% in 2023. AMNT stockpiled a portion of copper 
concentrate production for the smelter commissioning and 
ramp-up, resulting in approximately 190,000 dmt in inventory 
by the end of 2024, with sales deferred until smelter 
production starts in H1 2025. 
Profitability
EBITDA for 2024 increased by 39,9%, reaching US$1,426 
million, up from US$1,019 million in 2023, primarily driven by 
higher net sales. Additionally, the EBITDA margin improved 
from 50.1% to 53.5%.
Net income experienced a substantial year-on-year increase 
of 147.9%, climbing from US$259 million in 2023 to US$642 
million in 2024. This remarkable growth was primarily due to 
higher net sales, which led to an increase in the net income 
margin from 12.7% to 24.1%.

The Elang deposit is one of the world’s largest undeveloped 
porphyry copper and gold deposits, containing 3.8 billion 
tonnes of mineral resources, including 2.5 billion tonnes of 
ore reserves, according to 2024 JORC-compliant reserves 
and resources estimates. Elang’s reserves have increased 
significantly since the 2020 feasibility study. Compared to the 
previous year’s JORC report, the Elang ore reserves rose by 
78.5% increase from 1.4 billion tonnes to 2.5 billion tonnes. 
This growth includes a 71.0% rise in contained copper, from 
10,396 Mlbs to 17,777 Mlbs, and a 76.0% increase in gold 
contained, from 15.0 Moz to 26.4 Moz. As of 2024, we are still 
undertaking the definitive feasibility study for Elang and is 
expected to conclude by H1 2025. 
DEVELOPMENT AND OPERATION OF SUPPORT 
FACILITIES
Smelter and Precious Metal Refinery (“PMR”)
In 2024 we completed the construction and began 
commissioning our copper smelter. The smelter, which is 
located 18 km from the Batu Hijau mine and 1.5 km from 
Benete Port, has an annual production capacity of up to 
220,000 tonnes of copper cathode and 830,000 tonnes of 
sulfuric acid. The PMR will process 970 tonnes of anode slime 
from the smelter, yielding annual outputs of 579 koz of gold 
bullion with a purity of 99.99%, 1.8 Moz of silver bullion with 
a purity of 99.95% and 77 tonnes of selenium with a purity of 
95.00%. 
Commissioning, which began on 31 May 2024, is being 
carried out with a prudent, safety-focused approach to 
ensure reliability over the long term. We successfully reached 
another milestone in the smelter project with the production 
of our first copper anodes on February 12, 2025. The electro 
refinery is also in operation and the first copper cathodes 
were harvested in late March 2025. Given the complexity of 
the smelting process, the plant stabilization and ramping up 
will take some time, careful monitoring and steering. We will 
gradually increase capacity and ensure stable operations. PMR 
commissioning will commenced Q2 2025. The first gold and 
silver bullion is anticipated in H2 2025. By focusing on safety 
and prudent management, we aim to ensure the facility’s 
continued operability and reliability.

Smelter dan Precious Metal Refinery (“PMR”)
Pada tahun 2024, kami menyelesaikan pembangunan dan 
memulai komisioning smelter. Smelter yang berlokasi 18 km 
dari tambang Batu Hijau dan 1,5 km dari Pelabuhan Benete 
ini memiliki kapasitas produksi tahunan hingga 220.000 ton 
katoda tembaga dan 830.000 ton asam sulfat. PMR akan 
memproses 970 ton lumpur anoda dari smelter dengan 
produksi tahunan sebesar 579 ribu ons emas batangan dengan 
tingkat kemurnian 99,99%, 1,8 juta ons perak batangan dengan 
tingkat kemurnian 99,95%, dan 77 ton selenium dengan tingkat 
kemurnian 95,00%. 
Komisioning, yang dimulai pada 31 Mei 2024, dilakukan dengan 
pendekatan yang hati-hati dan berfokus pada keselamatan 
untuk memastikan keandalan dalam jangka panjang. Kami 
berhasil mencapai tonggak sejarah lain dalam proyek 
smelter dengan produksi anoda tembaga pertama kami pada 
tanggal 12 Februari 2025. Fasilitas electro refinery juga telah 
beroperasi dan produksi katoda tembaga pertama tercapai 
pada akhir Maret 2025. Mengingat kompleksitas proses 
peleburan, stabilitasi dan peningkatan kapasitas pabrik akan 
membutuhkan waktu, pemantauan dan pengarahan yang 
cermat. Kami akan secara bertahap meningkatkan kapasitas 
dan memastikan operasi yang stabil. Komisioning PMR akan 
dimulai pada kuartal kedua tahun 2025. Produksi emas dan 
perak batangan pertama diperkirakan akan dimulai pada paruh 
kedua tahun 2025. Dengan berfokus pada keselamatan dan 
manajemen yang bijaksana, kami berupaya untuk memastikan 
keberlanjutan pengoperasian dan keandalan fasilitas tersebut.
 
Kedua fasilitas tersebut telah dirancang dengan 
mengutamakan aspek lingkungan dan keselamatan. Smelter 
ini dilengkapi dengan slag concentrator untuk memulihkan 
tambahan tembaga dan emas dari discard slag dan 
mengembalikan material berharga ke smelter utama. Selain

Processing plant expansion 
Our ore processing plant is currently being expanded to 
double its input capacity to 85-90 Mt per annum. This will 
enable the plant to process the increased volumes of ore 
from Phase 8 and the future Elang mine and ensure supplies 
of sufficient copper concentrate for our smelter. The 
processing plant expansion is expected to achieve mechanical 
completion by Q4 2025. 
The processing plant expansion includes the following 
facilities: 
	›
Two new grinding circuits, each consisting of one large 
SAG mill paired with one large ball mill. 
	›
Two new double-deck vibrating pebble screens and a 
secondary pebble crushing area with four cone crushers. 
	›
Two new trains of flotation cells, with each train 
comprising three rougher cells followed by four scavenger 
cells. 
CCPP 
We are currently building a gas-fired CCPP with an installed 
capacity of 450 MW to supply electricity to Batu Hijau’s 
mining and processing operations, smelter and PMR. We have 
completed the commissioning of two 50 MW dual-fired gas 
turbines in the second quarter of 2024 and they are now 
powering the smelter. The remaining four gas turbines are on 
standby, with firing schedules aligned to meet Batu Hijau’s 
power demand.

LNG regasification and storage facilities 
To supply the CCPP and the smelter, we are constructing an 
LNG terminal consisting of regasification facility, LNG storage 
tank, a supply jetty, auxiliaries, and other utilities. The LNG 
storage facility will have a consumption capacity of 30 days 
to ensure that mining, processing and smelting operations 
are not disrupted by gas shortages. LNG supply from these 
facilities is expected to be completed by the end of Q4 2025. 
QUALITY CONTROL
Our Quality Assurance and Quality Control (“QA/QC”) 
laboratory and environmental laboratory analyze AMNT’s 
output at key stages of the process to ensure that it meets 
the highest international standards.
The QA/QC laboratory undertakes grade control during 
ore mining, monitors processing recoveries at the process 
plant and ensures that the copper, gold and silver content 
of concentrates is accurately measured prior to shipping. In 
addition, the QA/QC laboratory regularly tests tailings samples 
to guarantee compliance with Government quality standards. 
Our environmental laboratory, which is certified and 
accredited for SNI ISO/IEC 17025:2017 (testing and 
calibration laboratories) by Indonesia’s National Accreditation 
Committee, regularly tests wastewater samples and 
environmental conditions around the site to ensure 
compliance with government quality standards. 
Fasilitas regasifikasi dan penyimpanan Gas Alam Cair 
(”LNG”)
Untuk memasok PLTGU dan smelter, kami membangun 
terminal LNG yang terdiri dari fasilitas regasifikasi, tangki 
penyimpanan LNG, dermaga pemasok LNG, fasilitas 
pendukung, dan utilitas lainnya. Fasilitas penyimpanan 
LNG akan memiliki kapasitas konsumsi selama 30 hari 
untuk memastikan operasi penambangan, pemrosesan dan 
peleburan tidak terganggu oleh kekurangan gas. Penyaluran 
LNG dari fasilitas ini diharapkan akan selesai pada akhir kuartal 
keempat tahun 2025.
PENGENDALIAN MUTU
Laboratorium Quality Assurance dan Quality Control (”QA/
QC”) dan lingkungan kami menganalisa hasil produksi AMNT 
pada tahap-tahap utama proses untuk memastikan bahwa 
hasil produksi tersebut memenuhi standar internasional 
tertinggi.
Laboratorium QA/QC melakukan pengendalian kadar selama 
penambangan bijih, memantau perolehan hasil pengolahan 
di pabrik konsentrator, dan memastikan bahwa kandungan 
tembaga, emas, dan perak dalam konsentrat telah diukur 
secara akurat sebelum pengiriman. Selain itu, laboratorium 
QA/QC dan pengendalian mutu secara rutin menguji sampel 
tailing untuk menjamin kepatuhan terhadap standar kualitas 
Pemerintah.
Laboratorium lingkungan kami, yang telah tersertifikasi 
dan terakreditasi SNI ISO/IEC 17025:2017 (laboratorium 
pengujian dan kalibrasi) oleh Komite Akreditasi Nasional 
Indonesia, secara teratur menguji sampel air limbah dan 
kondisi lingkungan di sekitar site untuk memastikan kepatuhan 
terhadap standar kualitas pemerintah.

was prepared with reference to the consolidated financial

Current Assets
Current assets decreased by 8.3% from US$2,544,318 
thousand as of 31 December 2023 to US$2,332,417 thousand 
as of 31 December 2024, primarily due to decreases in cash 
and cash equivalents, restricted cash – current portion and 
trade receivables, partially offset by increases in inventories, 
net, stockpiles – current portion, and other tax receivables.
Non-Current Assets 
Non-current assets increased by 34.1% from US$6,552,735 
thousand as of 31 December 2023 to US$8,789,071 thousand 
as of 31 December 2024, largely attributable to increases in 
property, plant and equipment, net, due to the addition of 
fixed assets related to the development of Phase 8 of the 
Batu Hijau mine, the smelter and PMR, the processing plant 
expansion, the CCPP and LNG storage and regasification 
facilities, and deferred stripping costs, net, due to the 
capitalization of waste removal costs for Phase 8 of the Batu 
Hijau mine, which was partially offset by the amortization of 
waste removal costs for Phase 7 of the Batu Hijau mine.

Total assets increased by 22.3% from US$9,097,053 thousand

Strong demand for copper will continue to be driven 
by population growth, urbanisation, electrification, and 
decarbonisation trends over the short to medium term, 
according to analysts, with global refined and total copper 
consumption growth projected at a CAGR of 2.0% and 2.5% 
respectively, between 2024 and 2034.5  As the global energy 
transition gains pace, the anticipated expansion of renewable 
energy, EVs and power transmission will accelerate the uptake 
of copper-intensive technologies.
Copper prices are forecast to remain high over the next 
decade, with global copper demand expected to outpace 
supply despite an anticipated increase in the use of copper 
scrap. A number of mine disruptions and lower-than-
expected mine growth in 2024 have increased expectations 
of a deficit in copper supply in 2025. As a result, the annual 
average copper price is forecast to reach US$9,913 per tonne 
in 2025.6 Downside risks to this forecast include a potential 
trade war triggered by the imposition of high import tariffs by 
the US, which could slow the pace of global economic growth 
and negatively impact copper demand and prices. 
Construction of PT Amman Mineral Industri (“AMIN”)’s 
900ktpa input capacity copper smelter was completed in 
2024. The first copper cathodes were harvested from the 
smelter in late March 2025. Commissioning of the PMR is 
scheduled to begin in Q2 2025, with the first gold and silver 
bullion anticipated in Q3 2025. Given the higher value of 
copper cathodes and gold and silver bullion compared to the 
unrefined concentrates, we expect them to generate higher 
revenue over time. However, due to the complexity of the 
smelting process, we will increase capacity gradually to ensure 
operational stability.
The Batu Hijau mine completed Phase 7 production in 2024. 
Phase 8 waste removal continued during 2024; ore production 
is scheduled to begin in 2025 and continue until 2030. 
AMNT is currently developing the Elang copper-gold deposit. 
This is one of the world’s largest undeveloped porphyry 
copper and gold deposits, with ore reserves of 2.5 billion 
tonnes, according to 2024 JORC-compliant reserves and 
resources estimates. This includes reserves of 17.8 Mlbs of 
copper and 26.4 Moz of gold, an increase of almost 80% 
from the estimates yielded by the 2020 feasibility study. We 
expect to complete the definitive feasibility study on the Elang 
development project in 2025 and develop the Elang mine 
concurrently with Phase 8 ore mining, with a view to beginning 
production in 2031. Based on current assessments, Elang’s 
life-of-mine will extend through 2050.

Chinese ports or at customers’ premises.

KEBIJAKAN DIVIDEN
DIVIDEND POLICY  
As mandated by the laws of Indonesia and our Articles of 
Association, the decision on the distribution of dividends 
rests with the shareholders through the annual meeting of 
shareholders based on the recommendation of the Board 
of Directors. Dividends may be declared if the Company has 
positive retained earnings. The decision on the payment 
of a dividend as well as the amount and the payment 
arrangements are made by the Board of Directors and Board 
of Commissioners, subject to the approval of the GMS. The 
determination of the dividend amount considers various 
factors, including, but not limited to, the following: 
1.	
the retained earnings, operating performance, cash flows, 
future business prospects and financial condition of the 
Company, and 
2.	 other factors deemed relevant by the shareholders of the 
Company. 
Dividends may be declared in US Dollars or any other 
currencies in accordance with the prevailing laws and 
regulations. 
In accordance with our Articles of Association, an interim 
dividend may be paid out before the end of a financial year, 
on the condition that the distribution of such interim dividend 
does not result in our net assets falling below the amount of 
the Company’s total issued and paid-up capital and statutory 
reserves. The distribution of interim dividends is determined 
by the Board of Directors, subject to the approval of the 
Board of Commissioners. If, after the end of the financial year 
concerned, the Company incurs losses, the interim dividend 
that has already been distributed must be returned by the 
shareholders to the Company. If the interim dividend is not 
returned, the Board of Directors and Board of Commissioners 
will be held jointly and severally responsible. 
In compliance with the laws and regulations of Indonesia 
and our Articles of Association, our intention is to declare 
dividends to be approved by the shareholders once the 
Company achieves positive retained earnings. In the event 
that we declare a dividend, the said dividend will be paid 
in Rupiah. Shareholders who are already registered on the 
relevant record dates will be entitled to the full amount of any 
dividends approved, subject to the imposition of Indonesian 
withholding tax. Dividends received by a non-Indonesian 
shareholder will be subject to 20% Indonesian withholding tax. 
Sebagaimana diamanatkan oleh hukum di Indonesia dan 
Anggaran Dasar Perseroan, keputusan mengenai pembagian 
dividen berada di tangan para pemegang saham melalui rapat 
umum pemegang saham tahunan berdasarkan rekomendasi 
Direksi. Dividen dapat dibagikan apabila Perseroan memiliki 
saldo laba yang positif. Keputusan mengenai pembayaran 
dividen serta jumlah dan tata cara pembayarannya dilakukan 
oleh Direksi dan Dewan Komisaris, dengan persetujuan RUPS. 
Penentuan jumlah dividen mempertimbangkan berbagai 
faktor, termasuk, namun tidak terbatas pada, hal-hal berikut 
ini: 
1.	
saldo laba, kinerja usaha, arus kas, prospek usaha di masa 
depan dan kondisi keuangan Perseroan, dan 
2.	 faktor-faktor lain yang dianggap relevan oleh pemegang 
saham Perseroan. 
Dividen dapat dibayarkan dalam mata uang Dolar Amerika 
Serikat atau mata uang lainnya sesuai dengan peraturan 
perundang-undangan yang berlaku. 
Sesuai dengan Anggaran Dasar Perseroan, dividen interim 
dapat dibayarkan sebelum tahun buku berakhir, dengan 
syarat pembagian dividen interim tersebut tidak mengurangi 
aset bersih Perseroan terhadap jumlah modal ditempatkan 
dan disetor serta cadangan wajib Perseroan. Pembagian 
dividen interim ditentukan oleh Direksi dengan persetujuan 
Dewan Komisaris. Apabila setelah berakhirnya tahun buku 
yang bersangkutan, Perseroan mengalami kerugian, maka 
dividen interim yang telah dibagikan harus dikembalikan

laba yang positif. Apabila kami mengumumkan dividen, maka 
dividen tersebut akan dibayarkan dalam mata uang Rupiah.

CHANGES IN LAWS AND REGULATIONS THAT MATERIALLY IMPACT THE 
COMPANY
The following regulations issued in 2024 had an impact on the 
operations and/or financial performance of the Group:
1.	
Government Regulation No. 25 of 2024 on the Amendment 
to the Government Regulation No. 96 of 2021 on the 
Implementation of Mineral and Coal Mining Business 
Activities;
2.	 Regulation of the Minister of Energy and Mineral Resources 
No. 6 of 2024 on the Completion of the Construction of 
Domestic Mineral Refinery Facilities;
3.	 Regulation of Minister of Trade No. 21 of 2024 on Second 
Amendment to the Regulation of the Minister of Trade No. 23 
of 2023 on Export Policy and Provision; and
4.	 Regulation of the Minister of Finance No. 38 of 2024 on 
Determination of Export Goods Subject to Export Duty and 
Export Duty Rates.
Peraturan-peraturan berikut yang diterbitkan pada tahun 
2024 telah berdampak pada kinerja operasional dan/atau 
finansial Grup:
1.	
Peraturan Pemerintah No. 25 Tahun 2024 tentang Perubahan 
atas Peraturan Pemerintah No. 96 Tahun 2021 tentang 
Pelaksanaan Kegiatan Usaha Pertambangan Mineral dan 
Batubara;
2.	 Peraturan Menteri Energi dan Sumber Daya Mineral No. 6 
Tahun 2024 tentang Penyelesaian Pembangunan Fasilitas 
Pemurnian Mineral Logam di Dalam Negeri;
3.	 Peraturan Menteri Perdagangan No. 21 Tahun 2024 tentang 
Perubahan Kedua atas Peraturan Menteri Perdagangan No. 
23 Tahun 2023 tentang Kebijakan dan Pengaturan Ekspor; 
dan
4.	 Peraturan Menteri Keuangan No. 38 Tahun 2024 tentang 
Penetapan Barang Ekspor yang Dikenakan Bea Keluar dan 
Tarif Bea Keluar.
