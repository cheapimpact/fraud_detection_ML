TINJAUAN EKONOMI
International Monetary Fund (IMF) telah memproyeksikan 
hingga akhir tahun 2024 perekonomian global diproyeksikan 
dengan laju pertumbuhan sebesar 3,2%, masih mengalami 
perlambatan. Kondisi ini dipengaruhi oleh beberapa faktor 
diantaranya kebijakan moneter, fiskal, gejolak politik, 
hingga cuaca ekstrem yang terjadi di beberapa wilayah di 
seluruh dunia.  
Jika ditinjau dari wilayah selain Eropa dan Amerika, 
zona ASEAN mengalami pertumbuhan yang jauh lebih 
baik. Berdasarkan data IMF, wilayah ASEAN mengalami 
pertumbuhan dengan proyeksi sebesar 5,3% di tahun 
2024. Dimana Tiongkok mampu bertahan dengan laju 
pertumbuhan sebesar 4,8%, dan India tumbuh dengan laju 
pertumbuhan sebesar 7,0%.
Berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), pertumbuhan 
nasional mengalami penguatan dan tumbuh dengan stabil 
diangka 5,03%. Lebih lanjut, secara spasial kontribusi 
pertumbuhan terbesar terjadi di wilayah Pulau Jawa 
tumbuh sebesar 56,84%, Pulau Sumatera sebesar 22,30%, 
dan Pulau Kalimantan sebesar 8,15%.
TINJAUAN INDUSTRI
Menurut data Asosiasi Real Estate Indonesia (REI), pada 
triwulan pertama tahun 2024, penjualan dan harga 
rumah tapak primer mengalami peningkatan signifikan. 
Berdasarkan Survei Harga Properti Residensial (SHPR) 
oleh Bank Indonesia, penjualan properti residensial pada 
triwulan I 2024 meningkat signifikan sebesar 31,16 persen 
(YoY), jauh lebih tinggi dibandingkan triwulan sebelumnya 
yang hanya 3,37 persen (YoY). 
Didukung dengan program Fasilitas Likuiditas Pembiayaan 
Perumahan (FLPP) rumah subsidi dari Pemerintah. Hingga 
Juni 2024, FLPP mencapai 80.134 unit atau mendekati 
50% dari total kuota 166.000 unit. Selain itu, Pemerintah 
telah mencanangkan Program 3 juta rumah dan berencana 
memperpanjang program insentif PPN 100% untuk pasar 
perumahan. Program 3 Juta Rumah per tahun terdiri 
dari pembangunan dua juta rumah di pedesaan dan 
pembangunan satu juta apartment di wilayah perkotaan. 
Kondisi tersebut memberikan masa depan cerah untuk 
pembangunan perumahan dan properti di Indonesia.

ECONOMIC OVERVIEW 
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has projected that 
by the end of 2024, the global economy is projected to 
grow at a rate of 3.2%, still experiencing a slowdown. 
This condition is influenced by several factors, including 
monetary and fiscal policies, political uncertainties, and 
extreme weather conditions in several regions around the 
world. 
In terms of regions outside Europe and America, the ASEAN 
zone experienced significantly better growth. Based on data 
from the IMF, the ASEAN region is projected to grow by 
5.3% in 2024. China is expected to maintain a growth rate 
of 4.8%, while India is projected to grow at a rate of 7.0%.
According to data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), 
national growth has strengthened and grown steadily at 
5.03%. Furthermore, in terms of spatial distribution, the 
largest contribution to growth came from the Java region, 
which grew by 56.84%, followed by Sumatra at 22.30%, 
and Kalimantan at 8.15%.
INDUSTRY OVERVIEW 
According to data from the Indonesian Real Estate 
Association (REI), in the first quarter of 2024, sales and 
prices of primary landed houses increased significantly. 
Based on the Residential Property Price Survey (SHPR) by 
Bank Indonesia, residential property sales in the first quarter 
of 2024 increased significantly by 31.16 percent (YoY), far 
higher than the previous quarter’s 3.37 percent (YoY).
This was supported by the Government’s Housing Financing 
Liquidity Facility (FLPP) program for subsidized housing. 
As of June 2024, FLPP reached 80,134 units, or nearly 
50% of the total quota of 166,000 units. In addition, the 
government has launched the 3 Million Homes Program 
and plans to extend the 100% VAT incentive program for 
the housing market. The 3 Million Homes Program per year 
consists of the construction of two million homes in rural 
areas and one million apartments in urban areas. These 
conditions provide a bright future for housing and property 
development in Indonesia.

the 100% VAT incentive program for the housing market, 
which was originally set to expire in December 2024, until 
2025. This measure is expected to be the main driver for 
property sales growth in 2025. The Value-Added Tax (VAT) 
incentive, where the government bears the tax liability

apartment units with a maximum selling price of 5 billion 
rupiah per unit. This provision is regulated under Minister 
of Finance Regulation No. 120 of 2023, effective as of 
November 21, 2023.

the development of housing and property in Indonesia. The 
government is striving to help low-income communities to 
own decent homes. The 3 Million Homes per year program 
consists of the construction of two million homes in rural 
areas and one million apartments in urban areas.
