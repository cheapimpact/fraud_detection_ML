Perekonomian Global 
Sepanjang tahun 2024, perubahan yang cepat mewarnai 
dinamika perekonomian global, disertai dengan tingkat

pada perekonomian Indonesia. Ketegangan politik 
global yang masih tinggi pada awal tahun 2024 akibat 
berlanjutnya konflik Rusia-Ukraina, makin meningkat 
setelah serangan Israel ke Palestina. Menjelang akhir 
tahun 2024, terpilihnya kembali Presiden Trump 
di Amerika Serikat (AS) dengan kebijakan America 
First kembali meningkatkan ketidakpastian global 
karena berpotensi mengubah lanskap geopolitik 
dan perekonomian dunia. Berbagai perkembangan 
terkait hasil Pemilihan Umum di AS ini telah berisiko 
meningkatkan fragmentasi perdagangan dan investasi 
dunia, menurunkan pertumbuhan ekonomi dunia, dan 
kemudian makin meningkatkan ketidakpastian pasar 
keuangan global.

Global Economy
Throughout 2024, the global economy experienced 
rapid changes, marked by heightened uncertainty that 
posed challenges to Indonesia’s economic landscape. 
Geopolitical tensions remained high at the beginning of 
the year, fueled by the ongoing Russia-Ukraine conflict 
and further escalated by Israel’s attack on Palestine. 
Toward the end of 2024, the re-election of President 
Trump in the United States (US) and the reinstatement 
of the “America First” policy added to global uncertainty, 
with the potential to reshape the geopolitical and 
economic landscape. Developments surrounding the 
US presidential election results have increased the risk 
of trade and investment fragmentation, dampened 
global economic growth, and further heightened 
volatility in financial markets.
The rapid shifts in global geopolitical dynamics 
have significantly influenced economic and financial 
market trends throughout 2024. Overall, global 
economic growth slowed from 3.3% in 2023 to 
3.2% in 2024. Growth disparities between countries 
persisted, exacerbated by rising economic and trade 
fragmentation. The US economy was projected to 
remain strong in 2024 and 2025, although concerns 
over overheating lingered amid widening fiscal deficits 
and increasing government debt.
China and the European region were expected to 
experience slower growth due to weak domestic 
demand and potential tariff hikes imposed by the US 
government. Meanwhile, economic growth in India, 
Indonesia, and several Emerging Market Economies 
(EMEs) 
remained 
relatively 
strong, 
supported 
by domestic demand despite export constraints 
stemming from economic slowdowns and global trade 
fragmentation. Global inflation declined from 6.2% in 
2023 to 5.2% in 2024, although the possibility of a trade 
war between the US and its key trading partners, as 
well as disruptions in global supply chains, continued to 
pose risks. (Source: Indonesia Economic Report 2024, 
Bank Indonesia).

Non-oil and gas exports recorded relatively strong

Indonesia’s Balance of Payments (BoP) is expected 
to post a surplus in 2024, supported by a controlled 
current account deficit and a rising surplus in the capital 
and financial account. The current account deficit is

Q3 2024, reaching USD 11.0 billion, with the largest 
inflows coming from SRBI instruments, followed by 
domestic and global government bonds (SBN) and

exchange reserves, which stood at USD 155.7 billion by

external debt payments—well above the international

Rupiah exchange rate was well maintained, supported

In 2024, the Mining Sector maintains positive growth,

The role of strategic commodities as key drivers of 
economic growth is being enhanced through processing 
to generate greater added value. The downstream 
policy is focused on several high-potential commodities, 
with nickel being one of the main priorities. Domestic 
nickel has the potential to become one of the future 
commodities amid the world’s efforts to reduce carbon 
emissions from fossil fuels. 
World demand for nickel will increase in line with the 
incessant development of electric vehicles. Based 
on the 2020 Nickel Mining Booklet of the Ministry 
of Energy and Mineral Resources, Indonesia’s nickel 
reserves reach 72 million tons out of the world’s 
nickel reserves of 139.4 million tons, in other words 
52% of nickel reserves are in Indonesia. Furthermore, 
Indonesia is also the world’s largest nickel producer, 
in the amount of 800,000 tons from the world’s total 
nickel production of 2.68 million tons. After Indonesia, 
is Australia with nickel reserves reaching 15%, then 
Brazil 8%, Russia 5%, and a combination of a number of 
other countries such as the Philippines, China, Canada, 
and others 20%. 
Since 2014, Indonesia has banned exports of nickel 
ore to encourage the downstreaming of the domestic 
nickel industry which will have a positive impact on 
the economy. Since the implementation of this policy, 
investment in nickel ore processing plants in Indonesia 
has increased quite sharply. In 2021, around 37% of 
the world’s total nickel smelter production capacity 
was from Indonesia. Of the total existing production 
capacity in Indonesia, most of it produces FeroNickel 
(“FeNi”) and Nickel Pig Iron (“NPI”) products which are 
the main raw materials for making stainless steel. The

Modal Perseroan terbagi menjadi dua bagian utama,

memberikan nilai tambah kepada pemegang saham 
dan manfaat kepada pemangku kepentingan lainnya. 
Selain itu, Perseroan juga bertujuan memelihara 
optimalisasi struktur permodalan guna mengurangi 
biaya modal (cost of capital). Penyesuaian terhadap

struktur 
permodalan.

berlaku. Perseroan memastikan bahwa setiap saat, 
optimalisasi nilai Pemegang Saham tetap terjaga tanpa 
mengurangi kecukupan modal.
