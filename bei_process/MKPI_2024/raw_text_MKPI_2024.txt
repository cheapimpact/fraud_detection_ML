GENERAL AND INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
In 2024, Indonesia’s economy showed stable growth despite 
the global challenges, achieving a growth rate of ±5,03%, in 
line with the previous year’s growth. This growth was 
driven by increased investment, domestic consumption, and 
the continuation of infrastructure projects from the 
previous government.
Indonesia's property industry in 2024 recorded positive 
growth, driven by various economic factors and strategic 
government policies. Price stability was maintained 
through various inﬂation control measures, with a 
year-on-year inﬂation rate of ±1,57% recorderd in 
December 2024. In addition, several government policies 
have also strengthened the property sector, including the 
relaxation of the Loan To Value (LTV) ratio for property 
loans from banks to a maximum of 100% (without down 
payment), as well as the provision of Value Added Tax DTP 
(Government Borne) for houses priced up to Rp 2 billion. 
With a maximum selling price limit of Rp 5 billion. Overall, 
Indonesia's property industry in 2024 shows promising 
prospects, thanks to proactive government policy support, 
economic stability, and increased investment in this sector.
The results of Bank Indonesia's Survey of Residential 
Property Prices (SHPR) indicate that residential property 
prices in the primary market in the fourth quarter of 2024 
experienced limited growth. This is reﬂected in the 
Residential Property Price Index (IHPR), which during that 
period grew by ±1,39% (yoy). Sales of residential properties 
in the primary market in Q4 2024 experienced a decline, 
especially for small and medium-sized houses, while sales of 
large houses increased. From the consumer side, the 
majority of house purchases in the primary market are still 
made through the Housing Loan (KPR) ﬁnancing scheme, 
with a market share reaching ±72,54% of total ﬁnancing.
Retail business (shopping centers) experienced signiﬁcant 
dynamics in 2024, with an average occupancy rate in the 
4th quarter of 2024 reaching ±77,7%. The growth of the 
retail business is inﬂuenced by various factors, including 
ﬂuctuations in inﬂation and interest rates that aﬀect the 
prices of goods and consumer purchasing power. In 
addition, changes in consumer lifestyle, behavior and 
preferences also contribute to the dynamics of retail 
business growth.
Throughout 2024, Pondok Indah Mall continues to adapt to 
the dynamics of market trends and technological 
advancements, actively analyzing consumer behavior and 
preferences, especially Gen-Z and millennials (Gen-Y), while
