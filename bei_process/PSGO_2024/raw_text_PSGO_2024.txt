Economic Overview
According to data from the Indonesian Palm Oil 
Association (IPOA), the consumption of palm oil 
products for food, biodiesel, and oleochemicals 
reached 23.9 million tons, reflecting a 2.78% 
increase from 2023. Biodiesel consumption made 
up a substantial portion of this ﬁgure, contributing 
11.4 million tons, which represents a 7.51% rise 
from 10.6 million tons in 2023. This growth was 
supported by the government’s mandatory B35 
biodiesel policy, which continually enhances 
domestic palm oil consumption.
The average price of palm oil rose to USD 963/ton in 
2024, up from USD 886/ton in 2023 (World Bank). 
This increase in palm oil prices was resulted from 
government policy interventions and a decrease 
in production in 2024, which was attributed to 
climate changes, the aging proﬁle of oil palm 
plants that requiring rejuvenation, and restrictions 
on expansion areas.
Indonesian palm oil production in 2024 was 
48.2 million tons, reﬂecting a decrease of 3.80% 
compared to the 50.1 million tons produced in 2023. 
This decline in production, combined with stable 
domestic consumption, has driven up global palm 
oil prices. As a result, Indonesian palm oil exports 
decreased by 8.3%, falling from 32.2 million tons 
to 29.5 million tons.
Berdasarkan data Gabungan Pengusaha Kelapa 
Sawit Indonesia (GAPKI), konsumsi produk kelapa

pencapaian ini dengan kontribusinya sebesar 
11,4 juta ton, naik 7,51% dari 10,6 juta ton di tahun 
2023 didukung dengan kebijakan mandatori 
biodiesel B35 pemerintah yang secara konsisten 
meningkatkan konsumsi minyak kelapa sawit dalam 
negeri.
Harga minyak kelapa sawit mengalami peningkatan 
harga rata-rata di tahun 2024 menjadi USD 963/

2023 (World Bank). Kenaikan harga minyak kelapa 
sawit ini merupakan efek dari intervensi kebijakan 
Pemerintah serta penurunan produksi di tahun

dan memerlukan peremajaan serta pembatasan 
areal ekspansi.

Increasing Production Eﬀectiveness
The Company emphasizes optimizing plant productivity to

Pest and Disease Control
As a producer of plantation commodity products, the Company 
is at risk of exposure to pests and diseases. To mitigate this 
risk, the Company implements early prevention strategies 
against potential pest attacks, including ﬁreworms, termites, 
rats, horned beetles, bagworms, and fungal infections, through 
regular pest and plant disease census. If signs of a pest attack 
are detected, the Company promptly initiates rapid control 
measures to ensure optimal plant productivity and health.
To manage plant pests in an environmentally friendly manner, 
the Company grows natural enemies such as conserving owls, 
cultivating cordyceps mushrooms, and planting Antigonon 
and Turnera subulata as host plants for Sycanus sp., which are 
predators of ﬁreworm pests.

development policies.

various challenges, including geopolitical shifs and 
global economic ﬂuctuations, particularly bolstered
