Throughout

in

quite resilient although it is still lower than the period

Industry Overview 
 
Industri kreatif di Indonesia terus berkembang dan 
berkontribusi besar pada perekonomian nasional. Dalam 
perkembangannya selama semester I 2024, nilai ekspor 
ekonomi kreatif Indonesia mencapai 12,36 miliar dolar AS. 
Sementara pada tahun 2020, kontribusi subsektor ekonomi 
kreatif terhadap PDB nasional mencapai Rp1.211 triliun. 
Adapun Indonesia mampu menyerap hingga 17 juta tenaga 
kerja pada sektor ekonomi kreatif pada tahun 2019. Salah 
satu subsektor industri kreatif di Indonesia yakni industri 
perfilman. Dalam perkembangannya industri perfilman 
selalu bergerak sejalan dengan kondisi ekonomi makro, 
terutama terkait laju pertumbuhan ekonomi, daya beli 
masyarakat maupun kondisi demografi.  
 
Industri 
perfilman 
Indonesia 
selama 
tahun 
2024 
mengalami pertumbuhan yang signifikan, baik dari segi 
jumlah 
penonton, 
produksi, 
maupun 
kualitas. 
Perkembangan ini didukung oleh berbagai kebijakan dan 
insentif pemerintah, serta kemajuan teknologi dan 
digitalisasi. Sehingga pada tahun 2024, jumlah penonton 
film Indonesia mencapai lebih dari 81 juta penonton. 
Pertumbuhan jumlah penonton tersebut terus meningkat 
atau tumbuh 50% dibandingkan pada tahun 2022, dimana 
pada saat itu jumlah penonton film Indonesia sebesar 54 
juta penonton. Adapun selama tahun 2024, terdapat lebih 
dari 200 film Indonesia dirilis. 
 
Televisi terrestrial masih merupakan aspek utama dalam 
kehidupan masyarakat. Sebagai salah satu media masa 
utama, televisi terestrial sangat berperan penting bagi 
masyarakat dalam menilai dan mempelajari banyak hal 
tentang dunia sekitarnya. Kehadiran televisi terrestrial 
dapat mengubah cara pandang, nilai, atau pola hidup yang 
ada di masyarakat. Selain itu, televisi terestrial juga menjadi 
alat hiburan utama bagi sebagian besar masyarakat 
Indonesia. Menurut statistik, saat ini lebih dari 90% 
masyarakat Indonesia yang berusia di atas 10 tahun 
menonton televisi terestrial. Di akhir tahun 2022, 
pemerintah sempat menerapkan digitalisasi siaran televisi 
terestrial Indonesia secara nasional. Saat itu jumlah 
penonton televisi terestrial sempat turun. Namun menurut 
Nielsen Indonesia, jumlah penonton televisi terestrial 
berangsur pulih menuju ke sebelum penerapan digitalisasi. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
The creative industry in Indonesia continues to grow and 
contribute greatly to the national economy. In its 
development during the first semester of 2024, the export 
value of Indonesia's creative economy reached 12.36 
billion US dollars. While in 2020, the contribution of the 
creative economy subsector to national GDP reached 
Rp1,211 trillion. Indonesia was able to absorb up to 17 
million workers in the creative economy sector in 2019. 
One of the creative industry subsectors in Indonesia is the 
film industry. In its development, the film industry always 
moves in line with macroeconomic conditions, especially 
related to the rate of economic growth, people's 
purchasing power and demographic conditions.  
 
The Indonesian film industry during 2024 experienced 
significant growth, both in terms of audience, production, 
and quality. This development is supported by various 
government 
policies 
and 
incentives, 
as 
well 
as 
technological advances and digitalization. So that in 2024, 
the number of Indonesian movie viewers reached more 
than 81 million viewers. The growth in audience continues 
to increase or grow 50% compared to 2022, where at that 
time the number of Indonesian film viewers was 54 million 
viewers. As for 2024, there are more than 200 Indonesian 
films released. 
 
 
Terrestrial television remains a key aspect of community 
life. As one of the main mass media platforms, terrestrial 
television plays a significant role in enabling the public to 
observe and learn about various aspects of the 
surrounding world. The presence of terrestrial television 
has the ability to influence perspectives, values, and 
lifestyles within society. In addition, terrestrial television 
continues to serve as the primary source of entertainment 
for the majority of the Indonesian population. 
According to statistics, more than 90% of Indonesians aged 
over 10 years currently watch terrestrial television. At the 
end of 2022, the government initiated the nationwide 
digitalization 
of 
Indonesia’s 
terrestrial 
television 
broadcasts, which initially led to a decline in viewership. 
However, according to Nielsen Indonesia, the number of 
terrestrial television viewers has gradually recovered to 
levels 
close 
to 
those 
prior 
to 
the 
digitalization implementation.

One of the flagship programs on terrestrial television 
stations in Indonesia is electronic cinema (sinetron). 
Sinetron is in high demand by most Indonesians. For the 
past two decades, sinetron programs have dominated 
prime time viewing on various top television stations in 
Indonesia. Prime time takes place every day between 18.00 
and 22.00. Major television stations such as RCTI, SCTV, 
MNCTV, ANTV, Indosiar, all rely on sinetron as their main 
program. The biggest portion of advertisements obtained 
comes from these soap operas. 
 
In addition to terrestrial television, television today is also 
starting to be saturated by Over The Top (“OTT”) services, 
which run through internet services. Examples of OTT in 
Indonesia are Netflix, WeTV, Video.com, Viu, and Disney+. 
These services also continue to grow amidst the growing 
use of the internet in Indonesian society. 
 
Since its inception, the Company has been engaged in 
making soap operas for terrestrial television stations, with 
very good ratings. Several years ago, the Company also 
entered the OTT service market by airing several mini-
series. With sufficient experience, this year the Company 
will also begin to enter the making of Indonesian cinema 
films which continue to grow in Indonesian society.

antara lain sebagai berikut: 
1.  Memperluas pangsa pasar terutama pada segmen 
UMKM, yang didukung oleh pengembangan software 
Product Management berbasis cloud dengan biaya

2.  Inovasi berkelanjutan tidak hanya pada produk digital

3.  Fokus memberikan solusi terbaik bagi klien yang 
merupakan pengguna layanan dan produk digital 
Perseroan dan entitas anak 
 
 
In 2024, the Company prepared the following strategies: 
 
1.  Expanding market share, especially in the MSME 
segment, which is supported by the development of 
affordable cloud-based Product Management; 
2.  Carrying on innovations not only in digital products, i.e. 
software developed by the Company and subsidiaries, 
but also in digitizing physical products as well, among 
others by continuing the development of Meta-Comm, 
which will serve as a solution for MSMEs in which we 
offer a new experience of transacting their physical 
products, especially for accessories and fashion 
categories. 
3.  Focus on providing the best solutions for clients who are 
users of digital services and products of the Company

Performances Of Each Operational Segment 
 
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan membukukan pendapatan 
yang berasal dari beberapa segmen utama, yakni produksi 
sinetron, film televisi (FTV), mini seri, film layar lebar, serta 
lisensi digital. Segmen produksi sinetron tetap menjadi 
kontributor terbesar terhadap pendapatan Perseroan, 
meskipun mengalami penurunan dibandingkan tahun 
sebelumnya seiring berkurangnya jumlah episode yang 
diproduksi. Sementara itu, segmen FTV dan mini seri 
memberikan kontribusi stabil sebagai proyek jangka 
pendek, dan pendapatan dari film layar lebar bersifat 
fluktuatif tergantung pada keberhasilan judul yang dirilis. 
Di sisi lain, distribusi dan lisensi digital melalui platform 
over-the-top (OTT) menunjukkan tren pertumbuhan 
positif, 
sejalan 
dengan 
meningkatnya 
permintaan 
terhadap konten digital di pasar lokal maupun 
internasional. 
In 2024, the Company recorded revenue derived from 
several main segments, namely soap opera production, 
television films (FTV), mini-series, feature films, and digital 
licensing. The soap opera production segment remained 
the largest contributor to the Company’s revenue, despite 
a decline compared to the previous year due to a reduced 
number of episodes produced. Meanwhile, the FTV and 
mini-series segments provided stable contributions as 
short-term projects, and revenue from feature films was 
volatile, depending on the commercial success of each 
title released. On the other hand, digital distribution and 
licensing through over-the-top (OTT) platforms showed a 
positive growth trend, in line with increasing demand for 
digital content in both domestic and international 
markets.

Prospects 
The development of the film industry has always moved in 
line with macroeconomic conditions, especially regarding 
the rate of economic growth, people's purchasing power 
and demographic conditions. The government's belief that 
economic growth in fiscal year 2025 will be above 5 
percent has triggered optimism in various business sectors, 
including the film industry. Referring to the basic 
macroeconomic assumptions in the 2025 State Budget 
(APBN), the government and the DPR agreed on a basic 
assumption of economic growth of 5.2 percent. According 
to the official report of the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), 
Indonesia's cumulative economic growth in 2024 was 5.03 
percent and is believed to have improved further this year. 
In fact, the Prabowo Subianto administration is trying to 
push economic growth to reach 8 percent, so this 
condition further opens up opportunities for increased 
business activities.
