TINJAUAN INDUSTRI
Pada tahun 2024, harga minyak mentah global mengalami 
fluktuasi yang dipengaruhi oleh berbagai faktor ekonomi dan 
geopolitik. Berdasarkan data dari U.S. Energy Information 
Administration (EIA), harga rata-rata minyak mentah Brent tercatat 
sebesar USD81 per barel, sedikit lebih rendah dibandingkan 
dengan USD82 per barel pada tahun 2023. Sepanjang tahun, harga 
Brent bergerak dalam kisaran USD70 hingga USD90 per barel, di 
mana tekanan dari peningkatan produksi negara-negara non-
OPEC+ serta permintaan yang melemah menjadi faktor utama 
penurunan harga. Sementara itu, harga minyak mentah West 
Texas Intermediate (WTI) menunjukkan tren yang serupa dengan 
Brent, didukung oleh rekor produksi minyak di Amerika Serikat 
yang mencapai 13,5 juta barel per hari pada Oktober 2024.
 
Di sisi lain, harga minyak mentah Indonesia (Indonesian Crude Price/
ICP) juga mengalami fluktuasi sepanjang tahun 2024. Berdasarkan 
laporan Kementerian Energi dan Sumber Daya Mineral (ESDM), 
ICP tercatat sebesar USD83,79 per barel pada Maret 2024, 
mengalami peningkatan USD3,69 dari bulan sebelumnya. Namun, 
pada Juni 2024, ICP mengalami penurunan menjadi USD79,31 
per barel, yang berlanjut hingga Desember 2024 dengan harga 
sebesar USD71,61 per barel. Penurunan ini terutama dipengaruhi 
oleh pelemahan ekonomi global serta penguatan nilai tukar dolar 
AS yang menekan harga komoditas energi. Perkembangan harga 
minyak sangat berpengaruh pada kinerja Perseroan. Jika harganya 
stabil, berarti semakin banyak aktivitas migas yang dilaksanakan 
sehingga menjadi peluang pekerjaan untuk jasa yang dilayani oleh 
Perseroan. 
 
With the global economic slowdown, geopolitical dynamics, 
and global market uncertainty can affect energy demand and 
increase energy price volatility which impacts the Company’s 
operating costs. However, Indonesia’s economic growth shows 
stable economic growth amidst global uncertainty, this provides 
an opportunity for the Company to strengthen the domestic 
market, which can be a reference for the Company’s financial 
performance growth. 
The Company can take advantage of the growth of other service

strengthening the infrastructure sector provides an opportunity 
for the Company to develop projects and maintain stable 
performance. 
INDUSTRY REVIEW 
In 2024, global crude oil prices fluctuate influenced by various 
economic and geopolitical factors. Based on data from the U.S. 
The Energy Information Administration (EIA), the average price of 
Brent crude oil was recorded at USD 81 per barrel, slightly lower 
than USD 82 per barrel in 2023. Throughout the year, Brent prices 
moved in the range of USD 70 to USD 90 per barrel, where pressure 
from increased production from non-OPEC+ countries and weak 
demand were the main factors in the price decline. Meanwhile, the 
price of West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil showed a similar 
trend to Brent, supported by record oil production in the United 
States which reached 13.5 million barrels per day in October 2024.
On the other hand, the price of Indonesian Crude Price (ICP) also 
fluctuated throughout 2024. Based on the report of the Ministry 
of Energy and Mineral Resources (ESDM), the ICP was recorded at 
USD 83.79 per barrel in March 2024, an increase of USD 3.69 from 
the previous month. However, in June 2024, the ICP decreased to 
USD 79.31 per barrel, which continued until December 2024 at a 
price of USD 71.61 per barrel. This decline was mainly influenced 
by the weakening global economy and the strengthening of the 
US dollar exchange rate, which put pressure on energy commodity 
prices. The development of oil prices greatly affects the Company’s 
performance. If the price is stable, it means that more oil and gas 
activities are being carried out, thus creating job opportunities for 
the services provided by the Company.

On the lifting side, in 2024, Indonesia’s oil lifting experienced 
a significant decline. The rate of decline in lifting reached 579.7 
Thousand Barrels of Oil Per Day (mbopd), down from 605.5 
(mbopd) in the previous year. This realization is also below the 
State Budget (APBN) target of 645 (mbopd). This decline indicates 
ongoing challenges in the upstream oil sector, including technical 
production constraints, declining reserves in old fields, and delays 
in new well development projects. 
In contrast to oil, natural gas lifting showed an increase. In 2024, 
the realization of natural gas lifting was recorded at 1,026.1 million 
standard cubic feet per day (mmscfd), up from 960 (mmscfd) in 
the previous year. However, this figure is still slightly below the 
2024 APBN target of 1,033 (mmscfd). The increase in natural 
gas lifting can reflect improvements in production, optimization 
of more efficient gas projects, and increased domestic demand 
that drives more stable production. Currently, 67% of natural gas 
consumption is used for domestic needs, while 33% is exported.
The fluctuations in upstream oil and gas lifting performance are a 
key factor in determining the Company’s future business strategies. 
The higher the oil and gas lifting, the greater the opportunity for 
the Company to sell its services, both in the exploration, drilling, 
and well maintenance sectors. In addition, with the increasing role 
of natural gas in the proportion of national energy, the Company 
can take advantage of this opportunity to develop capabilities in 
services related to gas infrastructure.

SEGMEN PENJUALAN BARANG DAN JASA 
DISTRIBUSI DAN LOGISTIK ENERGI
Elnusa menjalankan kegiatan bisnis hilir di bidang jasa distribusi 
logistik energi dikelola oleh entitas anak, yaitu Elnusa Petrofin 
(EPN). EPN bergerak di bidang jasa logistik dan distribusi BBM, 
terutama Jasa Transportasi BBM, Energy Storage Management 
(Fuel, Gas, Aviation), Industry and Marine Fuel, Lubricant dan 
Chemical Integrated Service. Elnusa Petrofin merupakan salah satu 
ujung tombak PT Pertamina (Persero) untuk menyalurkan BBM 
PSO (Public Service Obligation) ke seluruh penjuru Nusantara.
Segmen usaha ini bertujuan untuk mendukung bisnis utama 
Perseroan di bidang jasa hulu hilir migas. Fokus kegiatan operasinya 
mencakup:
1. Pengelolaan angkutan Bahan Bakar Minyak (BBM) dari 
Terminal BBM Pertamina ke Stasiun Pengisian Bahan Bakar 
Umum (SPBU) dan Agen Premium Minyak Solar (APMS) serta 
Stasiun Pengisian Bahan Bakar Bunker (SPBB).
2. Pengelolaan Depo BBM milik swasta, mulai dari kegiatan 
administrasi 
pembelian 
BBM, 
pengangkutan 
BBM, 
penyimpanan BBM dan penyaluran BBM di lokasi pelanggan.
3. Pengelolaan Depo LPG Milik sendiri (Amurang).
4. Penjualan BBM keekonomian (non subsidi) kepada pelanggan 
Industri & Marine. 
5. Penjualan specialty chemical dan commodity chemical untuk 
industri migas. 
6. Penjualan pelumas Pertamina kepada pelanggan swasta.
7. Pengelolaan operasional SPBU dan SPBE milik sendiri dan milik 
swasta dengan metode profit sharing, yang menjual produk 
BBM dan BBK serta pengisian produk LPG bersubsidi produksi 
Pertamina.
SEGMENT OF GOODS AND SERVICES SALES OF 
ENERGY DISTRIBUTION AND LOGISTICS 
Elnusa carries out downstream business activities in the field of 
energy logistics distribution services managed by a subsidiary, 
namely Elnusa Petrofin (EPN). EPN is engaged in the field of fuel 
logistics and distribution services, especially fuel transportation 
services, Energy Storage Management (Fuel, Gas, and Aviation), 
Industry and Marine Fuel, Lubricant and Chemical Integrated 
Service. Elnusa Petrofin is one of the forefronts of PT Pertamina 
(Persero) to distribute PSO (Public Service Obligation) fuel 
throughout the archipelago. 
This business segment aims to support the Company’s main 
business in the field of upstream and downstream oil and gas 
services. The focus of its operational activities includes:
1. Management of fuel transportation from Pertamina’s fuel 
terminal to public fuel filling stations (SPBU) and premium 
diesel fuel agents (APMS) and bunker fuel filling stations 
(SPBB).
2. Management of privately-owned fuel depots, starting from 
administrative activities of fuel purchases, fuel transportation, 
fuel storage and fuel distribution at customer locations.
3. Management of self-owned LPG depots (Amurang).
4. Sales of economic fuel (non-subsidized) to Industrial & Marine 
customers.
5. Sales of specialty chemicals and commodity chemicals for the 
oil and gas industry.
6. Sales of Pertamina lubricants to private customers.
7. Operational management of self-owned and privately-owned 
gas stations and gas stations with a profit-sharing method, 
which sells fuel and gas products as well as filling subsidized 
LPG products produced by Pertamina.

POLICY ON CAPITAL STRUCTURE

business continuity through an optimal capital structure by 
reducing capital costs. Capital consists of a number of equity

and the Company’s loans or debts.

against the total debts (bank loans) to total equity. Meanwhile,

Management Policy on the Company’s Capital 
Structure 
Capital management is intended to maintain business continuity 
and provide maximum business returns to stakeholders by 
maintaining an optimal capital structure. These efforts include 
debt and equity to reduce capital costs. The Company has a 
policy to maintain a strong capital base as a basis for selecting 
management policies on capital structure by considering business 
conditions, capital return rates, and dividends distributed. The 
Company monitors capital based on the ratio of total liabilities to 
equity in the range of 30%–70%.
Basis for Selecting Management Policy on Capital 
Structure
The Company’s capital structure policy also follows the ratio 
required by creditors. The condition of the oil and gas industry 
is also a consideration for the Company in determining its 
capital structure policy. The fluctuating condition of the oil 
and gas industry makes management prioritize the Company’s 
capitalization using equity even though the Company’s financial 
ratio is still considerably good in a net cash position. The use of 
equity is the Company’s strategy to reduce financial expenses and 
utilize the Company’s liquidity.

Companies, the Company allocates reserve funds that cannot 
be distributed up to 20% of the issued and fully paid share 
capital. The external capital requirements are considered by the 
Company in the General Meeting of Shareholders. The Company 
and its subsidiaries are required to maintain a certain level of 
capitalization by bank loan agreements. The Company also has a 
policy and strategy to maintain a maximum debt-to-equity ratio of

barrels of oil per day (BOPD) and gas production of 12 billion

companies supporting the upstream oil and gas industry.
