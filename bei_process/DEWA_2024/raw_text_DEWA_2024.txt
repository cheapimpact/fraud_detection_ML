Coal Industry and Economic Outlook 
Pada 2024, aktivitas ekonomi global cenderung lemah 
dengan kinerja yang bervariasi antar kawasan. Kinerja 
ekonomi negara-negara maju masih cukup resilien meski 
masih lebih rendah dibanding periode sebelum pandemi. 
Bersamaan 
dengan 
ketegangan 
politik 
global 
yang 
meningkat, preferensi investor makin besar terhadap aset 
keuangan AS. Tingginya suku bunga The Fed membuat indeks 
mata uang dolar AS (DXY) berada dalam tren meningkat 
yang semakin menambah tekanan pelemahan berbagai 
mata uang dunia. Selain itu, pemulihan ekonomi China yang 
lambat juga menambah beban pemulihan ekonomi global.
Secara keseluruhan, data World Economic Outlook (WEO) 
yang dirilis oleh IMF pada Januari 2025 mencatat bahwa 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global pada tahun 2025 diproyeksikan 
sebesar 3,3%, atau di bawah rata-rata historis pada 2000 hingga 
2019 sebesar 3,7%. IMF menyatakan perekonomian global tetap 
stabil, meskipun tingkat pengaruhnya bervariasi antar negara. 
Berbagai faktor yang terjadi pada level global memberikan 
dampak terhadap penurunan harga minyak mentah yang 
signifikan di pasar internasional. Meskipun produksi minyak 
OPEC+ mengalami peningkatan, namun penurunan permintaan 
global terus menekan harga minyak. Penurunan harga minyak 
mentah juga dipengaruhi oleh perlambatan ekonomi China 
sebagai salah satu konsumen terbesar.
In 2024, global economic activity was expected to be weak, 
with varying performances across different regions. The 
economic performance of developed countries remained 
relatively resilient, though still lower than the levels seen 
before the pandemic. Rising global political tensions also 
contributed to an increased preference among investors for 
U.S. financial assets. The Federal Reserve's high interest rates 
led to an upward trend in the U.S. dollar currency index (DXY), 
which further exerted weakening pressure on various world 
currencies. Additionally, China's slow economic recovery 
added to the challenges of global economic recovery.
Overall, the World Economic Outlook (WEO) data released by 
the IMF in January 2025 projected global economic growth 
for 2025 at 3.3%, which is below the historical average 
of 3.7% from 2000 to 2019. The IMF noted that while the 
global economy remains stable, the level of influence varies 
between countries. Global factors contributed to a significant 
decline in crude oil prices in the international market. Despite 
an increase in OPEC+ oil production, declining global demand 
continued to suppress oil prices. China's economic slowdown, 
being one of the largest consumers, further influenced the 
drop in crude oil prices.

Sebagai dampaknya, harga minyak mentah dunia mengalami 
penurunan sepanjang 2024. Sempat menyentuh US$90-an 
per barel pada bulan Juni, harga minyak tahun 2024 ditutup 
pada angka US$70-an per barel. Pada 31 Desember 2024, 
harga minyak mentah Brent tercatat US$74,64 per barel. 
Sementara acuan West Texas Intermediate (WTI) menutup 
2024 di US$71,72 per barel. Kinerja harga minyak mentah 
Brent sepanjang 2024 melemah 3,12%, sedangkan WTI 
menguat tipis 0,10%. Penurunan juga terjadi pada industri 
batubara global. Tercatat, harga batubara Newcastle di akhir 
tahun tahun 2024 anjlok hingga ke angka US$127 per ton 
karena kelebihan pasokan. Di dalam negeri, Harga Batubara 
Acuan (HBA) stabil di kisaran USD130.
Di sisi lain, ekonomi Indonesia menunjukkan ketahanan yang 
kuat di tengah ketidakpastian global. Pertumbuhan ekonomi 
tahun 2024 tercatat 5,03%, lebih rendah dibanding tahun 
sebelumya sebesar 5,05%. Pertumbuhan ditopang terutama 
oleh kenaikan investasi dan terjaganya konsumsi rumah 
tangga, serta peningkatan belanja Pemerintah. Pemilihan 
kepala daerah (Pilkada) serentak pada bulan November 2024 
dan musim libur di akhir tahun, termasuk Natal dan Tahun 
Baru, menjadi faktor positif penopang ekonomi Indonesia. 
Komite Stabilitas Sistem Keuangan (KSSK) menyatakan 
bahwa ekonomi Indonesia tetap terjaga di tengah divergensi 
pertumbuhan ekonomi dunia serta ketidakpastian pasar 
keuangan global yang meningkat.
Di sisi eksternal, Indonesia mencatatkan surplus neraca 
perdagangan berturut-turut untuk tahun ke-5 pada 2024, 
disertai indeks PMI Manufaktur Indonesia di bulan Desember 
2024 kembali ke zona ekspansif. Nilai tukar Rupiah pun 
tetap terkendali di tengah ketidakpastian global yang tinggi, 
didukung oleh kebijakan stabilisasi BI. Secara keseluruhan 
tahun 2024, hingga 31 Desember 2024, rupiah tercatat di 
level Rp16.095, melemah 4,34% dibanding akhir tahun 2023.
Mengutip data Indonesian Mining Association, realisasi 
produksi batu bara RI hingga 31 Desember 2024 mencapai 
827,07 juta ton, naik signifikan sebesar 116,49% dibandingkan 
tahun sebelumnya. Capaian ini melampaui target yang 
ditetapkan, yaitu 710 juta metrik ton. Beberapa faktor yang 
memengaruhi kenaikan ini, antara lain tingginya kebutuhan 
untuk sektor kelistrikan dan smelter di dalam negeri. 
Sementara dalam hal ekspor, kebutuhan batu bara ekspor 
masih didominasi oleh permintaan dari China dan beberapa 
negara di kawasan ASEAN.
Berdasarkan data Minerba One Data Indonesia (MODI), 
realisasi domestic market obligation (DMO) juga mengalami 
peningkatan drastis hingga 115,79%. Pada tahun 2024, 
rencana DMO sebesar 181,30 juta ton, namun realisasinya 
mencapai 209,93 juta ton.
Consequently, world crude oil prices fell throughout 2024. 
After reaching US$90 per barrel in June, the price closed at 
US$70 per barrel for the year. On December 31, 2024, the 
price of Brent crude oil was recorded at US$74.64 per barrel, 
while the West Texas Intermediate (WTI) benchmark closed 
the year at US$71.72 per barrel. Throughout 2024, Brent 
crude oil prices weakened by 3.12%, whereas WTI saw a slight 
increase of 0.10%. The global coal industry also experienced 
a decline, with Newcastle coal prices plummeting to US$127 
per ton by the end of 2024 due to oversupply. Domestically, 
the Reference Coal Price (HBA) stabilized around US$130.
In contrast, Indonesia's economy demonstrated strong 
resilience amid global uncertainty. Economic growth in 2024 
was recorded at 5.03%, slightly lower than the previous year's 
5.05%. This growth was primarily supported by increased 
investment, stable household consumption, and heightened 
government spending. The simultaneous regional head 
elections (Pilkada) in November 2024, along with the holiday 
season at the end of the year—including Christmas and New 
Year—were positive factors for the Indonesian economy. 
The Financial System Stability Committee (KSSK) stated 
that Indonesia's economy remained resilient despite the 
divergence in global economic growth and heightened global 
financial market uncertainty.
On the external front, Indonesia recorded consecutive 
trade balance surpluses for the fifth year in a row in 2024. 
Additionally, Indonesia's Manufacturing PMI index returned to 
the expansionary zone in December 2024. Despite high global 
uncertainty, the rupiah exchange rate remained manageable, 
supported by Bank Indonesia's stabilization policy. By 
December 31, 2024, the rupiah was valued at IDR16,095, 
which was 4.34% weaker than at the end of 2023.
According to data from the Indonesian Mining Association, 
Indonesia's coal production realized by December 31, 2024, 
reached 827.07 million tons, marking a significant increase 
of 116.49% compared to the previous year. This achievement 
exceeded the target of 710 million metric tons. Several factors 
contributed to this increase, including high demand from the 
electricity sector and smelters within the country. In terms 
of exports, coal export needs continued to be dominated 
by demand from China and several countries in the ASEAN 
region.
Based on data from Minerba One Data Indonesia (MODI), 
the realization of the domestic market obligation (DMO) also 
increased dramatically to 115.79%. In 2024, the DMO plan was 
set at 181.30 million tons, but the actual realization reached 
209.93 million tons.

In response to these challenges and opportunities, the 
Company launched a comprehensive improvement program 
in 2024 aimed at standardizing mining operations aligned 
with Good Mining Practice (GMP) principles. This initiative 
reflects the Company’s commitment to improving operational 
efficiency, safety and sustainability across all mine sites.
One of the key milestones of this program was the successful 
pilot project at the KPC Project, where significant progress 
has been made in terms of productivity and mining sequence 
optimization. Through careful planning and execution, the 
Company refined its operational workflow, resulting in more 
efficient resource utilization and improved overall mine 
performance.
One of the most impactful initiatives was the Hauler Speed 
Improvement project. This project involved the redesign of 
strategic mine access roads, which included improvements 
such as increased road material hardness and standardized 
road widths. These measures contributed to a substantial 
increase in hauler speed and reduced cycle times, ultimately 
resulting in significant efficiency improvements. By optimizing 
transport routes and minimizing delays, the project improved 
fleet performance, reduced fuel consumption, and improved 
overall cost effectiveness. Not only that, the Company also 
automatically contributed to the reduction of its carbon 
footprint in line with sustainable development goals.

COVID-19 pandemic, which led to a decline in coal demand. 
As economic activity recovered and demand rebounded, the 
Company progressively ramped up its mining operations, 
resulting in a steady increase in overburden removal until 
2023. Substantial growth during this period was driven by 
higher coal demand, particularly from key markets in Asia 
such as India and ASEAN countries, as well as the domestic 
market.

less fluctuation. In 2020, the Company produced 16.49 million 
metric tons (MT) of coal, which slightly decreased to 16.22 
million MT in 2021 due to the ongoing impact of the pandemic. 
The following years saw a steady increase, reaching 17.03 
million MT in 2022, and 18.27 million MT in 2023. The increase 
in coal production from 2022 onwards was mainly driven by 
rising demand and improved operational efficiency before 
finally declining to 17.29 million MT in 2024.

by several key initiatives. Fundamental improvements in mining 
practices, investment in the addition and upgrade of heavy 
mining equipment, and the continuous development of skilled 
human resources have played an important role in optimizing 
performance. By strategically increasing manpower and 
ensuring the availability of well-maintained heavy equipment, 
the Company seeks to strengthen operational execution and 
efficiency. In addition, the Company has also increasingly 
reduced reliance on subcontractors for material removal at 
mine sites, allowing for better control and cost efficiency in 
its operations.
Despite the decline in overburden removal and production 
in 2024, the overall trajectory of the Company’s mining 
operations demonstrated resilience and adaptability in 
response to market dynamics. Going forward, the Company 
remains committed to achieving operational excellence, 
efficiency improvement, and strategic resource management 
to sustain long-term growth and stability in the mining sector.

Pendapatan
Pada tahun 2024,  Perseroan mencatatkan penurunan 
pendapatan sebesar 18,18% menjadi sebesar Rp6,031 triliun 
dari Rp7,372 triliun di tahun sebelumnya. Hal ini sejalan 
dengan menurunnya volume pemindahan material maupun 
produksi batubara.
Kontribusi pendapatan terbesar diperoleh dari proyek 
Bengalon  (PT  Kaltim  Prima  Coal)  yang   mencapai nilai 
sebesar Rp4,213 triliun, proyek  Asam  Asam (PT Arutmin 
Indonesia)  sebesar  Rp1,532 triliun, ditambah pendapatan 
dari PT Batuta Chemical Industrial Park, senilai Rp702,39 juta. 
Nilai pendapatan dari ketiga proyek penambangan tersebut 
masing-masing di tahun sebelumnya adalah sebesar 
Rp4,598 triliun, Rp2,076 triliun dan Rp275,52 juta. 
Sementara jika dilihat dari hubungan relasi, pendapatan dari 
keempat pelanggan berelasi tersebut di tahun 2024 mencakup 
95,28% total pendapatan, sedikit menurun dari tahun 
sebelumnya yang sebesar 98,95%. Pendapatan Perseroan 
dari pihak ketiga di tahun 2024, termasuk dari kontrak di luar 
kegiatan kontraktor penambangan mencapai Rp284,86 miliar 
naik 268,66% dari tahun sebelumnya sebesar Rp77,27 miliar.
Beban Pokok Pendapatan
Perseroan sejak awal tahun telah mencanangkan program 
efisiensi operasional dengan ketat, dan mengelola beban 
maupun biaya yang berada dalam kendali perusahaan 
dengan sebaik-baiknya. Perseroan berupaya menggunakan 
seluruh kompetensi sumber daya manusia yang tersedia 
dalam menjalankan kegiatan operasional pertambangan, 
baik dalam melakukan pemeliharaan peralatan maupun 
dalam kegiatan penambangan, dengan mengoptimalisasikan 
alat-alat pertambangan yang dimiliki maupun menyewa alat- 
alat pertambangan untuk menambah kapasitas produksi 
dan mengambil alih kegiatan penambangan di area yang 
sebelumnya dilaksanakan oleh subkontraktor.
Perseroan 
juga 
merealisasikan 
penyesuaian 
jumlah 
karyawan dengan hati-hati sehubungan dengan dimulainya 
peningkatan efisiensi operasional di area tambang lainnya.
Berbagai program yang dilaksanakan tersebut membuat 
Perseroan mampu menekan sebagian besar beban maupun 
biaya pokok. Hanya terdapat 3 (tiga) item akun beban pokok 
pendapatan yang meningkat, yakni:
1.	
Biaya pengangkutan batubara, di tahun 2024 naik 17,71% 
menjadi sebesar Rp639,29 miliar dari Rp543,09 miliar 
akibat meningkatnya volume coal hauling.
2.	 Biaya perbaikan dan pemeliharaan, di tahun 2024 naik 
9,61% menjadi sebesar Rp563,78 miliar dari Rp514,34 miliar.
3.	 Biaya bahan baku, di tahun 2024 naik 21,28% menjadi 
sebesar Rp62,78 miliar dari Rp51,76 miliar.
Persentase penurunan Beban Pokok Pendapatan sebesar 
17,91% yang lebih kecil dari persentase kenaikan Pendapatan 
yang sebesar 18,18%, membuat Perseroan di tahun 2024 
mencatatkan laba kotor sebesar Rp400,58 miliar, menurun 
21,84% dari nilai laba kotor tahun sebelumnya yang  mencapai 
nilai sebesar Rp512,51 miliar.
Revenues
In 2024, the Company experienced an 18.18% decline in 
revenues, totaling Rp6.031 trillion compared to Rp7.372 
trillion in the previous year. This decrease was attributed 
to a reduction in both material transfer volume and coal 
production.
The largest revenues contributions came from the Bengalon 
project (PT Kaltim Prima Coal), which generated Rp4.213 
trillion, followed by the Asam Asam project (PT Arutmin 
Indonesia) with Rp1.532 trillion, and revenues from PT Batuta 
Chemical Industrial Park amounting to Rp702.39 million. In 
the previous year, revenues from these three mining projects 
was Rp4.598 trillion, Rp2.076 trillion, and Rp275.52 million 
respectively.
Additionally, in 2024, revenues from four related customers 
accounted for 95.28% of total revenues, a slight decrease 
from 98.95% in the previous year. The Company also recorded 
revenues from third parties, including contracts outside of 
mining contracting activities, which reached Rp284.86 billion. 
This represented a significant increase of 268.66% from 
Rp77.27 billion in the previous year.
Cost of Revenues
Since the beginning of the year, the Company has implemented 
a comprehensive program focused on operational efficiency. 
We have managed expenses and costs within our control 
as effectively as possible. The Company has aimed to fully 
utilize our available human resources in mining operations, 
including equipment maintenance and mining activities. 
We have optimized our owned mining equipment, rented 
additional machinery to boost production capacity, and 
taken over mining operations in areas previously handled by 
subcontractors.
Additionally, the Company has made careful adjustments 
to our workforce as part of the operational efficiency 
improvements in various mining regions.
The various programs implemented enabled the Company to 
reduce most of its expenses and cost of revenues. There were 
only 3 (three) cost of revenues account items that increased, 
namely:
1.	
Hauling cost, increased by 17.71% to Rp639.29 billion from 
Rp543.09 billion in 2024 due to the increase in volume 
coal hauling.
2.	 Repairs and maintenance cost, in 2024 increased by 
9.61% to Rp563.78 billion from Rp514.34 billion.
3.	 Material cost, in 2024 increased by 21.28% to Rp62.78 
billion from Rp51.76 billion.
The percentage decrease of 17.91% in Cost of Revenues, 
which was smaller than the percentage increase of 18.18% in 
Revenues, made the Company in 2024 record a gross profit 
of Rp400.58 billion, a decrease of 21.84% from the previous 
year's gross profit value which reached a value of Rp512.51 
billion.

general and administrative expenses, which totaled Rp233.54

billion, which was a significant reduction of 65.42% from the
