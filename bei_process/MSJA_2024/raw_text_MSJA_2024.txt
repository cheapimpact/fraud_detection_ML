Tinjauan Ekonomi dan Industri
Sepanjang tahun 2024, ekonomi dunia mengalami 
perlambatan 
akibat 
berbagai 
tantangan 
global, 
termasuk penurunan pertumbuhan ekonomi, berlanjutnya 
konflik di beberapa negara, serta fragmentasi dalam 
perdagangan internasional. Dalam kondisi ini, Bank 
Indonesia memproyeksikan pertumbuhan ekonomi dunia 
sebesar 3,2%, dengan tren perlambatan yang diperkirakan 
berlanjut hingga tahun 2026.
Di tengah tantangan global tersebut, perekonomian 
Indonesia tetap menunjukkan ketahanan dan penguatan, 
didukung oleh peningkatan permintaan domestik, investasi, 
serta berlanjutnya pelaksanaan Proyek Strategis Nasional 
(PSN) pada tahun 2024. Bank Indonesia memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia pada tahun 2024 dan 
2025 berada dalam kisaran 4,7% hingga 5,5%, dengan 
potensi peningkatan lebih lanjut pada tahun 2026 dalam 
kisaran 4,8% hingga 5,6%.  Pertumbuhan ini ditopang oleh 
kontribusi Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) dari berbagai 
sektor, termasuk industri pengolahan yang mencatat 
pertumbuhan sebesar 4,89%,  mencerminkan peran 
penting sektor manufaktur dalam perekonomian nasional.
Sejalan 
dengan 
pertumbuhan 
industri 
pengolahan, 
industri non-woven di Indonesia juga mencatatkan 
kinerja yang positif sepanjang tahun 2024. Berdasarkan 
laporan 6Wresearch, pasar kain non-woven di Indonesia 
diproyeksikan 
tumbuh 
dengan 
Compound 
Annual 
Growth Rate (CAGR) sebesar 5,8% pada periode 2020–
2026, didorong oleh meningkatnya permintaan di sektor 
kesehatan, 
otomotif, 
konstruksi, 
dan 
pengemasan. 
Selain itu, secara global, pasar non-woven diperkirakan 
mencapai USD 72,19 miliar dalam lima tahun ke depan, 
dengan CAGR sebesar 6,02%.
Dukungan kebijakan pemerintah juga turut berperan 
dalam memperkuat daya saing industri non-woven di 
Indonesia. Penetapan Standar Kompetensi Kerja Nasional 
Indonesia (SKKNI) untuk industri ini menjadi langkah 
strategis dalam meningkatkan kualitas tenaga kerja dan 
efisiensi produksi.
Meski demikian, industri non-woven tetap menghadapi 
tantangan dari fluktuasi harga bahan baku yang 
dipengaruhi oleh ketidakpastian global dan fragmentasi 
perdagangan internasional. Namun, dengan fundamental 
ekonomi nasional yang solid, serta strategi ekspansi dari 
pelaku industri, prospek industri non-woven di Indonesia 
tetap cerah dan berpotensi terus berkembang dalam 
beberapa tahun ke depan.
Economy and Industry Overview
Throughout 2024, the global economy experienced a 
slowdown due to various global challenges, including 
declining economic growth, ongoing conflicts in several 
countries, and fragmentation in international trade. 
Under these conditions, Bank Indonesia projected global 
economic growth at 3.2%, with a continued slowdown 
trend expected until 2026.
Amid these global challenges, Indonesia’s economy 
remained resilient and strengthened, supported by 
increasing domestic demand, investment, and the 
continued implementation of National Strategic Projects 
(PSN) in 2024. Bank Indonesia projected Indonesia’s 
economic growth for 2024 and 2025 to be within the 
range of 4.7% to 5.5%, with further potential growth in 
2026 within the range of 4.8% to 5.6%. This growth was 
driven by contributions to the Gross Domestic Product 
(GDP) from various sectors, including the manufacturing 
industry, which recorded a growth of 4.89%, reflecting the 
significant role of the manufacturing sector in the national 
economy.
In line with the growth of the manufacturing industry, the 
non-woven industry in Indonesia also recorded positive 
performance throughout 2024. According to a report 
by 6Wresearch, Indonesia’s non-woven fabric market is 
projected to grow at a Compound Annual Growth Rate 
(CAGR) of 5.8% during the 2020–2026 period, driven 
by increasing demand in the healthcare, automotive, 
construction, and packaging sectors. Additionally, on a 
global scale, the non-woven market is expected to reach 
USD 72.19 billion in the next five years, with a CAGR of 6.02%.
Government policy support has also played a role in 
enhancing the competitiveness of the non-woven industry 
in Indonesia. The establishment of the Indonesian National 
Work Competency Standards (SKKNI) for this industry 
represents a strategic step in improving workforce quality 
and production efficiency.
Nevertheless, the non-woven industry continues to 
face challenges from raw material price fluctuations, 
influenced by global uncertainties and international trade 
fragmentation. However, with a solid national economic 
foundation and expansion strategies by industry players, 
the outlook for Indonesia’s non-woven industry remains 
promising and is expected to continue growing in the 
coming years.

Spunbond
Proses spunbond adalah proses pembuatan kain non-
woven yang digunakan untuk membuat lembaran atau 
sekumpulan lembaran serat sintesis yang terikat, umumnya 
proses ini digunakan pada industri tekstil, pertanian, medis, 
dan konstruksi untuk memproduksi berbagai macam produk 
seperti jubah medis sekali pakai, penghalang hama tanaman, 
popok hingga pembalut.
Proses ini dilakukan dengan cara mengekstrusi pelet polimer, 
yang biasanya terbuat dari bahan polypropylene (PP) dan 
polyester (PET) yang dilelehkan dan diekstrusi menjadi serat 
halus yang panjang untuk dicetak sesuai dengan permintaan 
pelanggan. Kemudian, serat halus ini dikumpulkan di atas 
conveyor belt atau cetakan secara acak, sehingga terbentuk 
suatu lembaran serat-serat yang masih bersifat renggang. 
Hasil produk tersebut akan dipanaskan kembali lalu diberi 
tekanan, sehingga serat tersebut menjadi meleleh dan 
menciptakan ikatan serat yang lebih kuat. Tekanan panas 
yang diberikan juga bervariatif tergantung ada kebutuhan 
gramasi dan tingkat kekuatan kain non-woven yang diminta.
Hasil dari kain spunbond terkenal dengan ciri khasnya, yakni 
memiliki kekuatan yang tinggi, breathable fabric, ketahanan 
terhadap kelembaban sehingga membuat kain ini cocok 
untuk berbagai aplikasi. Spunbond non-woven juga semakin 
diminati karena bisa diaplikasikan untuk produk sekali pakai, 
serta tahan lama dan biaya produksi yang relatif rendah.
The spunbond process involves creating non-woven fabric 
used to produce sheets or a cluster of bonded synthetic fibers. 
This process is commonly utilized in textile, agriculture, medical, 
and construction industries to manufacture various products 
such as disposable medical gowns, plant pest barriers, diapers, 
and sanitary pads.
This process begins with the extrusion of polymer pellets, 
typically made of materials like polypropylene (PP) and 
polyester (PET), which are melted and extruded into long fine 
fibers to be formed according to customer demand. These fine 
fibers are then randomly collected on a conveyor belt or mold, 
forming a sheet of loosely bound fibers. The resulting product is 
reheated and subjected to pressure, causing the fibers to melt 
and create stronger fiber bonds. The heat and pressure levels 
vary depending on the required fabric weight and strength of 
the nonwoven fabric requested.
Spunbond fabric is known for its high strength, breathability, 
and moisture resistance, making it suitable for various 
applications. Spunbond non-woven fabric is increasingly 
popular for disposable products due to its durability and 
relatively low production costs.
Meltblown
Proses meltblown adalah proses pembuatan kain non-
woven yang menghasilkan serat-serat yang sangat halus 
dengan cara melelehkan material polimer termoplastik dan 
membuatnya melalui nosel udara berkecepatan tinggi. 
Proses ini menciptakan jaringan serat micro yang terdistribusi 
secara acak dan terikat, menghasilkan material yang 
berpori dan ringan. Proses meltblown dilakukan dengan cara 
mengekstruksi polimer yang biasanya terbuat dari bahan 
polypropylene (PP), polythylene (PE), dan polyester (PET), 
sehingga menjadi serat yang sangat halus dengan ukuran 
berkisar 0,1 – 10 mikrometer. Kain meltblown dikenal karena 
diameter seratnya yang sangat kecil dibandingkan dengan 
kain dari hasil proses spunbond.
Karena diameter serat dari proses ini terhitung sangat kecil, 
kain meltblown mempunyai sifat filtrasi dan penghalang yang 
unik, sehingga kain ini banyak digunakan dalam berbagai 
industri termasuk perawatan kesehatan, penyaringan, dan 
pembuatan perlengkapan perlindungan diri (PPE) seperti 
masker medis dan bedah, penyaringan udara dan cairan, 
produk absorben, dan pakaian pelindung medis.
The meltblown process involves creating non-woven fabric 
by producing extremely fine fibers through the melting 
of thermoplastic polymer materials and extruding them 
through high-speed air nozzles. This process forms a network 
of randomly distributed and bonded microfibers, resulting 
in porous and lightweight material. The meltblown process 
begins with the extrusion of polymers, typically made of 
materials such as polypropylene (PP), polyethylene (PE), and 
polyester (PET), which are then transformed into very fine fibers 
ranging in size from 0.1 to 10 micrometers. Meltblown fabric is 
renowned for its significantly smaller fiber diameter compared 
to fabrics produced through the spunbo
Due to its extremely small fiber diameter, meltblown fabric 
possesses unique filtration and barrier properties, making it 
widely used across various industries, including healthcare, 
filtration, and manufacturing of personal protective equipment 
(PPE) such as medical and surgical masks, air and liquid 
filtration, absorbent products, and medical protective clothing.

Spunmelt
Proses spunmelt adalah proses pembuatan kain non-woven 
yang 
menggabungkan 
elemen-elemen 
dari 
teknologi 
spunbond dan meltblown untuk menciptakan bahan yang 
memiliki kekuatan, filtrasi, dan kemampuan penghalang 
yang baik. Proses spunmelt sama dengan spunbond dan 
meltblown, yaitu mengektruksi polimet yang terbuat dari 
bahan polypropylene (PP), polythylene (PE), dan polyester 
(PET), dimana hasil dari proses spunmelt digunakan untuk 
lapisan tengah, sementara spunbond untuk bagian dalam 
dan luar.
Produk dari proses spunmelt digunakan dalam berbagai 
aplikasi seperti pembuatan popok bayi, popok dewasa, 
masker medis dengan kualitas tinggi, kain pelindung, serta 
produk filtrasi udara dan cairan. Kain spunmelt sering 
digunakan dalam situasi yang memerlukan filtrasi yang baik 
dan penghalang terhadap mikroorganisme dan partikel.
The spunmelt process involves creating non-woven fabric by 
combining elements of spunbond and meltblown technologies 
to produce material with good strength, filtration, and barrier 
capabilities. The spunmelt process is similar to spunbond 
and meltblown, where polymers made of materials such as 
polypropylene (PP), polyethylene (PE), and polyester (PET) are 
extruded. The resulting product from the spunmelt process is 
used for the middle layer, while spunbond is used for the inner 
and outer layers.
Products from the spunmelt process are used in various 
applications such as baby diapers, adult diapers, high-
quality medical masks, protective fabrics, as well as air and 
liquid filtration products. Spunmelt fabric is often used in 
situations requiring excellent filtration and barriers against 
microorganisms and particles.

Amendments to PSAK 201, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Non-current Liabilities with Covenants
	
This amendment clarifies that only covenants with 
which entities must comply on or before the reporting 
date will affect a liability’s classification as current or 
non-current.
	
Entities apply retrospectively amendments to PSAK 
201 (October 2020) regarding the classification of a 
liability as current or non-current for financial reporting 
starting on or after January 1, 2024 in accordance with 
PSAK 208. If entities apply the amendments to PSAK 
201 (October 2020) in a period that is earlier after the 
issuance of the amendment to PSAK 201 (December 
2022) regarding non-current liabilities with covenants, 
entities also apply the amendment to PSAK 201 
(December 2022) in that period. 
	
If entities apply the amendments to PSAK 201 (October 
2020) for the previous period, the entity shall disclose 
this fact.

Amendments to PSAK 201, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Classification of Liabilities as Current or 
Non-current
	
The 
narrow-scope 
amendments 
to 
PSAK 
201, 
“Presentation of Financial Statements” clarify that 
liabilities are classified as either current or non-
current, depending on the rights that exist at the end 
of the reporting period. Classification is unaffected 
by the expectations of the entity or events after the 
reporting date (e.g the receipt of a waiver or a breach 
of covenant). The amendments also clarify what 
PSAK 201 means when it refers to the ‘settlement’ of a 
liability.
	
The amendments could affect the classification 
of liabilities, particularly for entities that previously 
considered management’s intentions to determine 
classification and for some liabilities that can be 
converted into equity.

Amendments to PSAK 207, “Statement of Cash Flows” 
and PSAK 107, “Financial Instruments - Disclosures”: 
Supplier Finance Arrangements
	
These amendments clarify the characteristics of 
supplier finance arrangements and require additional 
disclosure of such arrangements. The disclosure 
requirements in the amendments are intended to 
assist users of financial statements in understanding 
the effects of supplier finance arrangements on an 
entity’s liabilities, cash flows and exposure to liquidity 
risk.
