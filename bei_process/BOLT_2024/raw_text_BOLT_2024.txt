TINJAUAN MAKROEKONOMI
Perekonomian global sepanjang tahun 2024 menghadapi 
tantangan besar akibat meningkatnya ketegangan geopolitik, 
kebijakan moneter ketat di negara maju, serta volatilitas harga 
komoditas yang mengganggu rantai pasok dunia. Tekanan ini 
tercermin dalam laporan World Economic Outlook dari IMF, yang 
memperkirakan pertumbuhan ekonomi global hanya mencapai 
3,3%, lebih rendah dari rata-rata historis 3,7% pada periode 
2000–2019. Amerika Serikat menunjukkan ketahanan yang lebih 
kuat dibandingkan kawasan lain, sementara Eropa menghadapi 
ketidakpastian tinggi, dan perlambatan ekonomi Tiongkok 
semakin memperburuk stabilitas global.
Di

Berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), pertumbuhan 
ekonomi nasional pada 2024 mencapai 5,03% secara tahunan 
(C-to-C), yang mencerminkan stabilitas di tengah tekanan 
eksternal dan domestik. Sektor jasa menjadi pendorong utama 
pertumbuhan dengan kenaikan sebesar 9,80%, sementara 
konsumsi lembaga non-profit yang melayani rumah tangga 
(PK-LNPRT) tumbuh tertinggi sebesar 12,48%, didorong oleh 
belanja sosial dan program kesejahteraan pemerintah. Data ini 
menunjukkan bahwa permintaan domestik tetap kuat meskipun 
ekonomi global masih bergejolak.
Dalam perdagangan internasional, Indonesia mencatat surplus 
sebesar 31,04 miliar dolar AS, meskipun angka ini merupakan

5,31%, yang menunjukkan tingginya permintaan industri 
domestik, serta pelemahan ekspor sektor pertambangan 
akibat fluktuasi harga komoditas global. Sementara ekspor 
masih tumbuh sebesar 2,29%, percepatan pertumbuhan impor 
menandakan bahwa daya beli dan aktivitas industri di dalam 
negeri tetap terjaga meskipun tekanan dari faktor eksternal masih 
berlangsung.
MACROECONOMIC OVERVIEW
The 2024 global economy faced significant challenges due to 
rising geopolitical tensions, tight monetary policies in developed 
countries, and commodity price volatility disrupting global supply 
chains. This pressure is reflected in the IMF's World Economic 
Outlook report, which forecasts global economic growth at just 
3.3%, lower than the historical average of 3.7% from 2000 to 
2019. The United States showed stronger resilience compared 
to other regions, while Europe faced high uncertainty, and 
China's economic slowdown further worsened global stability.
Amid global uncertainty, Indonesia's economy has demonstrated 
resilience. According to data from the Central Statistics Agency 
(BPS), the national economic growth in 2024 reached 5.03% 
year-on-year (C-to-C), reflecting stability despite external and 
domestic pressures. The services sector was the main driver 
of growth, with an increase of 9.80%, while consumption by 
non-profit institutions serving households (PK-LNPRT) grew 
the most at 12.48%, driven by social spending and government 
welfare programs. This data shows that domestic demand 
remains strong despite ongoing global economic volatility.
In international trade, Indonesia recorded a surplus of USD 
31.04 billion, although this is the lowest figure in the last 
four years. This is due to a 5.31% increase in imports of 
raw materials and capital goods, indicating strong domestic 
industrial demand, and a decline in mining exports due to global 
commodity price fluctuations. While exports still grew by 2.29%, 
the faster growth in imports suggests that domestic purchasing 
power and industrial activity remain strong despite ongoing 
external pressures.

Inflasi pada tahun 2024 relatif terkendali di angka 1,57%, 
meskipun terdapat disparitas antarwilayah. Kenaikan harga 
barang kebutuhan pokok, terutama makanan dan minuman, 
serta lonjakan biaya jasa seperti perawatan pribadi yang 
meningkat hingga 7,02%, menjadi faktor utama yang mendorong 
inflasi. Untuk merespons kondisi ini, pemerintah menerapkan 
kebijakan ekonomi yang lebih fleksibel, termasuk penyesuaian 
subsidi energi di daerah dengan inflasi tinggi serta penguatan 
program bantuan sosial guna menjaga daya beli masyarakat 
yang paling terdampak. Papua Pegunungan mencatat inflasi 
tertinggi sebesar 5,36%, sedangkan Sulawesi Utara memiliki 
inflasi terendah sebesar 0,44%.
Ketidakstabilan nilai tukar Rupiah menjadi salah satu tantangan 
utama dalam menjaga stabilitas ekonomi nasional. Sepanjang 
tahun, Rupiah mengalami pergerakan signifikan dengan posisi 
tertinggi Rp15.516,19 per dolar AS pada 2 Januari 2024 dan posisi 
terendah Rp16.517,17 per dolar AS pada 27 Juni 2024. Rata-
rata nilai tukar tahunan tercatat sebesar Rp16.092,06 untuk kurs 
jual dan Rp15.931,94 untuk kurs beli, lebih lemah dibandingkan 
asumsi RAPBN 2024 yang memproyeksikan rentang Rp14.700–
Rp15.300 per dolar AS. Fluktuasi ini dipengaruhi oleh kebijakan 
suku bunga The Fed serta langkah intervensi Bank Indonesia 
dalam menjaga stabilitas pasar valuta asing.
Untuk menghadapi pelemahan Rupiah, Bank Indonesia melakukan 
berbagai intervensi guna menjaga stabilitas makroekonomi, 
termasuk kebijakan suku bunga serta penggunaan cadangan 
devisa. Depresiasi Rupiah memberikan tekanan terhadap inflasi, 
terutama pada barang impor, serta menimbulkan tantangan bagi 
sektor industri yang bergantung pada bahan baku luar negeri. 
Namun, di sisi lain, pelemahan Rupiah juga dapat meningkatkan 
daya saing ekspor dan menekan defisit transaksi berjalan jika 
sektor manufaktur dapat memanfaatkan kondisi ini dengan 
optimal. Bank Indonesia juga terus menjalankan operasi moneter 
untuk meredam volatilitas nilai tukar melalui intervensi langsung 
di pasar valuta asing serta pengelolaan cadangan devisa.
Dari sisi kebijakan fiskal, APBN 2024 menunjukkan upaya 
pemerintah dalam menjaga keseimbangan antara belanja 
dan pendapatan negara. Fokus utama kebijakan fiskal tahun 
ini diarahkan pada percepatan transformasi ekonomi melalui 
penguatan sektor hilirisasi, transisi menuju energi hijau, serta 
reformasi perpajakan. Selain itu, pemerintah terus meningkatkan 
alokasi belanja infrastruktur guna mendukung pertumbuhan 
ekonomi jangka panjang dan memperkuat daya saing industri 
domestik. Dengan kebijakan fiskal yang pro-pertumbuhan serta 
koordinasi erat dengan kebijakan moneter, stabilitas ekonomi 
nasional tetap terjaga meskipun menghadapi tekanan global 
yang tidak menentu.
Inflation in 2024 remained relatively controlled at 1.57%, despite 
disparities between regions. The rise in the prices of essential 
goods, particularly food and beverages, and the increase in 
service costs like personal care, which grew by 7.02%, were 
key factors driving inflation. In response, the government 
implemented more flexible economic policies, including adjusting 
energy subsidies in regions with high inflation and strengthening 
social assistance programs to support the purchasing power of 
the most affected communities. Papua Pegunungan recorded 
the highest inflation at 5.36%, while North Sulawesi had the 
lowest at 0.44%.
The instability of the Rupiah exchange rate has been one of 
the main challenges in maintaining national economic stability. 
Throughout the year, the Rupiah experienced significant 
fluctuations, with the highest rate of Rp15,516.19 per USD on 
January 2, 2024, and the lowest rate of Rp16,517.17 per USD 
on June 27, 2024. The average annual exchange rate was 
recorded at Rp16,092.06 for the selling rate and Rp15,931.94 
for the buying rate, which is weaker than the 2024 State Budget 
assumption of a range of Rp14,700–Rp15,300 per USD. These 
fluctuations were influenced by the Federal Reserve's interest 
rate policies and Bank Indonesia's interventions to maintain 
foreign exchange market stability.
To address the weakening of the Rupiah, Bank Indonesia 
implemented various interventions to maintain macroeconomic 
stability, including interest rate policies and the use of foreign 
exchange reserves. The depreciation of the Rupiah puts 
pressure on inflation, particularly on imported goods, and creates 
challenges for industries that rely on foreign raw materials. 
However, on the positive side, the weakening of the Rupiah can 
boost export competitiveness and reduce the current account 
deficit if the manufacturing sector can optimize this situation. 
Bank Indonesia also continues to carry out monetary operations 
to curb exchange rate volatility through direct interventions in 
the foreign exchange market and the management of foreign 
exchange reserves.
From a fiscal policy perspective, the 2024 State Budget reflects 
the government's efforts to maintain a balance between state 
spending and revenue. This year, the main focus of fiscal policy 
is to accelerate economic transformation by strengthening the 
downstream sector, transitioning to green energy, and reforming 
the tax system. In addition, the government continues to 
increase infrastructure spending to support long-term economic 
growth and enhance the competitiveness of domestic industries. 
With a growth-oriented fiscal policy and close coordination 
with monetary policy, national economic stability is maintained 
despite facing uncertain global pressures.

TINJAUAN INDUSTRI OTOMOTIF
Industri otomotif Indonesia sepanjang tahun 2024 mengalami 
dinamika yang cukup signifikan di tengah tantangan ekonomi 
global dan domestik.Berdasarkan data Gabungan Industri 
Kendaraan Bermotor Indonesia (GAIKINDO), produksi mobil 
nasional sepanjang tahun 2024 mencapai 1,19 juta unit, 
mengalami penurunan dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya. 
Penjualan kendaraan roda empat juga mengalami kontraksi 
sebesar 14% dengan total penjualan mencapai 865.723 unit, 
jauh dari target awal yang ditetapkan sebesar 1,1 juta unit 
sebelum direvisi menjadi 850.000 unit.
Hal ini mencerminkan adanya tekanan terhadap daya beli 
masyarakat yang disebabkan oleh pelemahan nilai tukar rupiah, 
tingginya suku bunga, serta kenaikan harga bahan bakar 
minyak (BBM).
Pada sektor produksi, laporan GAIKINDO mengungkapkan 
bahwa produksi mobil nasional pada bulan September 2024 
tercatat sebanyak 101.688 unit, mengalami penurunan sebesar 
11,09% dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya. Secara kumulatif, total 
produksi kendaraan roda empat dari Januari hingga September 
2024 mencapai 881.574 unit, turun 17,1% dibandingkan periode 
yang sama pada 2023.
Overall, Indonesia's economy in 2024 remains on a stable 
growth path despite facing external and domestic challenges. 
The mining, manufacturing, and commodity export sectors have 
been the most affected by global uncertainties, significantly due 
to fluctuations in raw material prices and changes in international 
trade policies. However, the combination of flexible monetary 
policies and a pro-growth fiscal strategy has enabled Indonesia 
to maintain economic recovery momentum and strengthen its 
fundamentals to face challenges in 2025.

AUTOMOTIVE INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
The Indonesian automotive industry experienced significant 
changes throughout 2024 amid global and domestic economic 
challenges. According to data from the Indonesian Automotive 
Industry Association (GAIKINDO), national car production 
reached 1.19 million units in 2024, a decline compared to the 
previous year. Four-wheeled vehicle sales also contracted by 
14%, with total sales reaching 865,723 units, falling short of 
the initial target of 1.1 million units, which was later revised to 
850,000 units.
This reflects the pressure on consumer purchasing power 
caused by the rupiah depreciation, high interest rates, and 
increasing fuel prices.
In the production sector, GAIKINDO's report shows that 
national car production in September 2024 reached 101,688 
units, a decrease of 11.09% compared to the previous year. 
Cumulatively, the total production of four-wheeled vehicles from 
January to September 2024 was 881,574 units, a decline of 
17.1% compared to the same period in 2023.

harga bahan baku kemungkinan dipengaruhi oleh fluktuasi

SEGMENT PROFITABILITY
In 2024, the Company recorded total sales of Rp1,475.27 billion, 
a slight increase of 0.67% compared to the previous year. This 
growth was mainly driven by an increase in sales from the Other 
Industries segment, which rose by 3.78%, as well as stable 
growth in the Motorcycle Parts segment at 0.46%. However, 
overall growth was restrained by a significant decline of 16.37% 
in the Auto Parts segment, due to weakening demand in the 
four-wheeler market.
Gross profit declined by 4.22% to Rp278.78 billion, despite 
the increase in sales. This decline was primarily due to rising 
costs of raw materials and direct labor, which compressed 
overall profit margins. The increase in raw material costs was 
likely influenced by global price fluctuations and inflationary 
pressures, while the rise in labor costs reflected adjustments to 
minimum wages and the demand for skilled labor in production 
lines.
In addition, total unallocated operating expenses increased by 
3.68% to Rp137.33 billion, indicating higher general operating 
costs. As a result, operating profit fell by 10.82%, from Rp158.61 
billion to Rp141.45 billion.
The Company recognizes the importance of operational 
efficiency amid rising cost pressures, and going forward, will 
continue to optimize its production processes and review its cost 
structure to maintain sustainable profitability.

Rasio Solvabilitas
Solvabilitas adalah kemampuan Perseroan untuk memenuhi

Liabilitas terhadap Total Aset
Rasio ini menunjukkan seberapa besar aset perusahaan

Solvency Ratio
Solvency refers to the Company’s ability to meet all interest-
bearing debts using its assets or equity. Solvency is assessed 
through the Debt to Total Assets ratio by comparing the total 
interest-bearing liabilities to total assets, and the Debt to Equity 
ratio by comparing total interest-bearing liabilities to total equity.
Debt to Total Assets
This ratio indicates the extent to which the Company's assets 
are financed by debt. The lower the ratio, the fewer the assets 
funded by debt, which is a stronger indicator of solvency. In this

2023 to 2024, indicating that most of the Company’s assets are 
financed through equity.

growth. Despite the rise in liabilities, the ratio remains within 
a healthy range, showing that the Company continues to rely 
on strong equity for operational funding. This demonstrates 
a well-balanced use of debt and equity, ensuring business 
sustainability and financial flexibility to seize future expansion 
opportunities.

PROSPEK USAHA TAHUN 2025
Memasuki tahun 2025, perekonomian dunia masih dihadapkan 
pada ketidakpastian yang cukup tinggi akibat ketegangan 
geopolitik yang belum mereda, kebijakan moneter ketat di 
Amerika Serikat dan Eropa, serta perlambatan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi di Tiongkok. Selain itu, volatilitas harga energi dan 
ketidakpastian kebijakan perdagangan global memberikan 
tekanan terhadap stabilitas ekonomi dunia. Berdasarkan laporan 
World Economic Outlook yang dirilis oleh International Monetary 
Fund (IMF), pertumbuhan ekonomi global diproyeksikan tetap 
berada di level 3,3% dengan inflasi global yang diperkirakan 
turun hingga 4,2%. Namun, perlambatan ekonomi di Tiongkok 
serta ketidakpastian kebijakan perdagangan Amerika Serikat 
dapat memberikan dampak negatif bagi pertumbuhan ekonomi 
negara-negara berkembang, termasuk Indonesia.
Di tengah tantangan global ini, ekonomi Indonesia diperkirakan 
tetap tumbuh dengan stabil. Berdasarkan proyeksi pemerintah, 
pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional pada tahun 2025 ditargetkan 
sebesar 5,2%, didukung oleh strategi fiskal yang berfokus pada 
akselerasi transformasi ekonomi yang inklusif dan berkelanjutan. 
Kebijakan fiskal yang diterapkan dalam APBN 2025 bersifat 
ekspansif namun tetap berhati-hati, dengan fokus utama pada 
penguatan sektor industri berbasis hilirisasi, percepatan transisi 
energi hijau, serta peningkatan kualitas sumber daya manusia 
melalui investasi di sektor pendidikan dan kesehatan. Selain 
itu, pemerintah berencana meningkatkan rasio pajak terhadap 
Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) guna memperkuat ketahanan 
fiskal dalam jangka panjang.
Sejalan dengan kebijakan fiskal, kebijakan moneter Indonesia 
diperkirakan akan tetap akomodatif dengan langkah-langkah 
Bank Indonesia dalam menjaga stabilitas nilai tukar rupiah 
serta memastikan likuiditas yang cukup di pasar keuangan. 
Penurunan suku bunga pada awal tahun 2025 diharapkan 
dapat mendorong pertumbuhan kredit yang diproyeksikan naik 
sebesar 11-13%, sehingga dapat mendukung ekspansi dunia 
usaha, termasuk industri otomotif.
BUSINESS OUTLOOK FOR 2025
Entering 2025, the global economy still faces significant 
uncertainty due to unresolved geopolitical tensions, tight 
monetary policies in the United States and Europe, and the 
slowdown in economic growth in China. Additionally, volatility 
in energy prices and uncertainties in global trade policies are 
putting pressure on global economic stability. According to the 
World Economic Outlook report released by the International 
Monetary Fund (IMF), global economic growth is projected 
to remain at 3.3%, with global inflation expected to decrease 
to 4.2%. However, the economic slowdown in China and the 
uncertainty surrounding U.S. trade policies may have negative 
effects on the growth of emerging economies, including 
Indonesia.
Amid these global challenges, Indonesia's economy is expected 
to maintain stable growth. According to the government's 
projections, national economic growth in 2025 is targeted at 
5.2%, supported by fiscal strategies focusing on accelerating 
inclusive and sustainable economic transformation. The fiscal 
policy applied in the 2025 State Budget is expansive but 
cautious, with the main focus on strengthening the downstream-
based industrial sector, accelerating the transition to green 
energy, and improving human capital through investments 
in education and health sectors. Additionally, the government 
plans to increase the tax-to-GDP ratio to strengthen fiscal 
resilience in the long term.
In line with fiscal policy, Indonesia's monetary policy is expected 
to remain accommodative, with measures from Bank Indonesia 
to maintain the stability of the rupiah exchange rate and ensure 
sufficient liquidity in financial markets. A reduction in interest 
rates at the beginning of 2025 is expected to stimulate credit 
growth, projected to rise by 11-13%, thereby supporting 
business expansion, including in the automotive industry.

MARKET SHARE
PT Garuda Metalindo Tbk maintains its dominance in the 
Indonesian automotive fastener market, particularly as a key 
supplier for both two-wheeler and four-wheeler vehicles. With 
long-term relationships with major manufacturers like Honda 
and Mitsubishi, along with support from the TKDN policy, the 
company remains a leading player in the local component 
industry. To reduce reliance on the automotive sector, the 
company has begun to explore non-automotive markets, 
such as infrastructure, heavy machinery, manufacturing, and 
electronics, in response to the growing demand for fasteners 
in these sectors.

America, India, and Thailand. With products that comply with 
international regulations and the shifting global supply chain 
due to the US-China trade war, the company has significant 
opportunities to grow its export market. Additionally, the 
Company is now focusing on electric vehicles (EV) by forming 
strategic partnerships with Chinese EV manufacturers, such as 
BYD and YADEA.
Although specific data on the total production of the automotive

diversification strategies have been key drivers of sustainable 
business growth, leading to an increased market share, higher 
investor confidence, and a stronger reputation for the Company 
in the fastener industry, domestically and globally.
Through global expansion strategies, business diversification, 
and product innovation, the Company has strengthened its 
position domestically and improved its competitiveness in 
international markets, ensuring sustainable growth amidst the 
dynamics of the global automotive and manufacturing industries.
