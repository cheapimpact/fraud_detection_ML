Economic Review
In 2024, the global economy experienced a 
slowdown due to ongoing geopolitical tensions 
and 
increasing 
trade 
fragmentation. 
In 
the

inward-looking economic strategies are projected 
to impact the global economy. These policies 
include higher trade tariffs and stricter immigration 
regulations. These conditions led to 2024 global 
economic growth recorded at 3.2%, remained below 
the historical average.
Amid 
global 
challenges, 
Indonesia’s 
economy 
continues 
to 
demonstrate 
resilience. 
In 
2024, 
economic 
growth 
was 
recorded 
at 
5.03%

the 
middle-to-upper-income 
segments, 
as

increased

supported by the ongoing development of National 
Strategic Projects (PSN). National inflation remained 
controlled within the target range of 2.5±1%, reflecting 
the effectiveness of consistent monetary policies 
and strong synergy between Bank Indonesia and the 
government in maintaining price stability.
On the other hand, the stability of the financial 
services sector also remains intact, despite the need 
to stay vigilant against the uncertainties of the global 
economy. In this environment, Indonesia remains 
optimistic about sustaining its economic growth 
momentum by leveraging the domestic stability that 
has been established.

Tinjauan Industri
Industri kelapa sawit terus memperkuat perannya 
sebagai komoditas strategis dalam perekonomian 
Indonesia, meskipun berada dalam tren yang 
fluktuatif. Berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik 
(BPS), sektor pertanian tumbuh sebesar 1,69% pada 
tahun 2024, dengan kelapa sawit sebagai salah 
satu  kontributor utama. Meskipun ekspor sawit 
mengalami penurunan sebesar 8,32% dari 32,21 juta 
ton di tahun 2023 menjadi 29,53 juta ton di tahun 
2024, penurunan ini disebabkan oleh meningkatnya 
kebutuhan domestik untuk biodiesel serta konsumsi 
dalam negeri yang terus bertumbuh, di tengah 
kapasitas produksi yang relatif stagnan.
Di sisi lain, industri ini menghadapi tantangan 
dari fluktuasi harga CPO dan penerapan regulasi 
bebas deforestasi di Eropa. Namun, berdasarkan 
data Kementerian Perdagangan, harga referensi 
(HR) minyak kelapa sawit (CPO) per Desember 
2024 tercatat sebesar USD1.071,68 per metrik ton, 
yang mengalami peningkatan seiring dengan 
naiknya permintaan global, kebijakan perdagangan 
internasional, dan dinamika produksi. Sementara itu, 
data Gabungan Pengusaha Kelapa Sawit Indonesia 
(GAPKI) menunjukkan bahwa pada Agustus 2024, 
produksi CPO Indonesia menurun sebesar 10,6% 
menjadi 48,164 ribu ton dibanding bulan sebelumnya. 
Konsumsi domestik dan ekspor juga mencatatkan 
kenaikan masing-masing sebesar 23,859 ribu ton 
dan 2,060 ribu ton pada periode yang sama.
Sebagai 
sektor 
andalan 
dalam

perekonomian 
nasional,

terus

kelapa sawit. Fokus kebijakan diarahkan pada

Dengan dukungan kebijakan yang efektif, industri

Industry Review
The palm oil industry continues to strengthen its role 
as a strategic commodity in Indonesia’s economy, 
despite being in a fluctuating trend. According to 
data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), the 
agricultural sector grew by 1.69% in 2024, with palm 
oil as one of its main contributors. Although palm oil 
exports declined by 8.32% from 32.21 million tons in 
2023 to 29.53 million tons in 2024, this decrease was 
driven by the rising domestic demand for biodiesel 
and increasing domestic consumption amidst 
relatively stagnant production capacity.  
On the other hand, the industry faces challenges 
from fluctuating CPO prices and the implementation 
of deforestation-free regulations in Europe. However, 
according to data from the Ministry of Trade, 
the reference price (HR) for crude palm oil (CPO) as 
of December 2024 was recorded at USD1,071.68 per 
metric ton, reflecting an increase driven by rising 
global demand, international trade policies, and 
production dynamics. Meanwhile, data from the 
Indonesian Palm Oil Association (GAPKI) showed 
that in August 2024, Indonesia’s CPO production 
decreased by 10.6% to 48.164 thousand tons 
compared to the previous month. Both the domestic 
and export consumptions recorded increases of 
23.859 thousand tons and 2.060 thousand tons 
respectively, in the same period.
As a key sector in supporting the national economy, 
the government continues to demonstrate its 
commitment through various strategic policies 
aimed 
at 
enhancing 
the 
competitiveness 
of 
the palm oil industry. Policy efforts focus on 
increasing productivity in the upstream sector 
and strengthening downstream activities through 
initiatives such as smallholder palm oil rejuvenation 
programs, research and development, workforce 
training, biodiesel incentives, and the provision of 
infrastructure to bolster the industry’s resilience.  
With the support of effective policies, the palm oil 
industry is expected to remain resilient in 2025. 
Despite 
facing 
global 
challenges, 
the 
sector 
remains a cornerstone in maintaining national 
economic stability and fostering long-term growth 
opportunities. Sustainability and innovation are the 
primary keys to ensuring the industry’s continued 
contribution to Indonesia’s economy.

TINJAUAN OPERASIONAL
 Operational Review
Kegiatan usaha Perseroan terbagi atas 3 segmen 
utama, yaitu perkebunan kelapa sawit, pabrik 
pengolahan kelapa sawit, dan energi terbarukan. 
Ketiga segmen ini saling terintegrasi, dengan 
kekuatan utama pada bidang perkebunan dan 
pengolahan kelapa sawit menjadi crude palm oil 
(CPO), yang semakin diperkuat dengan keberadaan 
kernel crushing plant (KCP) yang mendukung 
pengolahan inti kelapa sawit (palm kernel) menjadi 
produk bernilai tambah, sementara biogas power 
plant (BPP) memanfaatkan limbah cair untuk 
menghasilkan 
energi 
terbarukan 
yang 
ramah 
lingkungan. Integrasi ini mencerminkan komitmen 
Perseroan 
terhadap 
efisiensi 
operasional 
dan 
keberlanjutan.
The Company’s business activities are divided 
into three main segments: oil palm plantations, 
palm oil processing plants, and renewable energy. 
These segments are interrelated, with core strengths 
in plantations and the processing of palm oil into 
crude palm oil (CPO), further enhanced by the 
presence of a kernel crushing plant (KCP) that 
processes palm kernels into value-added products. 
Meanwhile, the biogas power plant (BPP) utilizes 
liquid waste to generate environmentally friendly 
renewable energy. This integration reflects the 
Company’s commitment to operational efficiency 
and sustainability.

2.	 Penerimaan dan Pengolahan
	
Tahap penerimaan dan pengolahan dimulai 
dengan penerimaan TBS di pabrik kelapa sawit, 
dilanjutkan dengan pengolahan TBS menjadi 
minyak kelapa sawit (crude palm oil/CPO) dan 
inti kelapa sawit (palm kernel/PK). Selain itu, 
pada tahun 2024, Perseroan juga melakukan 
pengolahan inti sawit menjadi minyak inti 
kelapa sawit mentah (crude palm kernel oil) dan 
bungkil kelapa sawit (palm kernel expeller) guna 
meningkatkan nilai tambah dan diversifikasi 
produk.
3.	 Penyimpanan dan Pengiriman
	
Minyak kelapa sawit dan minyak inti sawit 
yang dihasilkan akan disimpan dalam tangki 
penyimpanan, sedangkan inti kelapa sawit 
dan bungkil kelapa sawit akan disimpan dalam 
gudang penyimpanan. Keempat produk tersebut 
akan dikirim kepada pelanggan menggunakan 
truk atau kapal milik sendiri dan pihak ketiga. 
Ketentuan 
pengiriman 
yang 
disediakan 
di 
antaranya ex-mills, yaitu syarat penjualan di 
mana penyerahan barang dilakukan di gudang 
penjual, 
free-on-board 
(FOB), 
yaitu 
syarat 
penjualan di mana penyerahan barang dilakukan 
di atas kapal pembeli, serta cost, insurance, 
and freight (CIF), yaitu syarat penjualan di 
mana pengiriman barang dilakukan penjual 
sampai ke gudang atau tangki timbun pembeli 
menggunakan kapal atau truk penjual.
Perkebunan Kelapa Sawit
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan mengelola 8 Entitas 
Anak yang bergerak di bidang perkebunan kelapa 
sawit di Kalimantan Timur, dengan total luas area 
sebesar 60.515 Ha. Area tersebut terdiri dari 48.542 Ha 
perkebunan inti dan 11.973 Ha perkebunan plasma.
The Company conducts an integrated palm oil 
production process through the following stages.
1.	
Development, Harvesting, and Maintenance  
	
The 
development 
stage 
begins 
with 
land 
acquisition, followed by nursery, land preparation, 
and 
planting. 
During 
the 
growth 
process, 
maintenance is carried out in accordance with 
best operational standards, including fertilization, 
weed control, pest management, supervision, and 
monitoring. Once the plants reach a certain age 
and are deemed ready for harvest, the harvesting 
process is conducted, yielding fresh fruit bunches 
(FFB) that are delivered to the palm oil mill.  
2.	 Reception and processing  
	
The reception and processing stage begins with 
the receipt of FFB at the palm oil mill, followed by 
processing FFB into crude palm oil (CPO) and palm 
kernel (PK). In addition, in 2024, the Company also 
processed the palm kernel into crude palm kernel 
oil and palm kernel expeller to improve its value-
added and product diversification.
3. 	 Storage and Delivery  
	
The output CPO and CPKO will be stored in 
storage tanks, while the palm kernel and palm 
kernel expeller will be stored in warehouses. These 
four products will be delivered to customers using 
Company’s own or third-party trucks or vessels. 
Delivery terms include ex-mills, where goods are 
handed over at the seller’s warehouse; free-on-
board (FOB), where goods are handed over on the 
buyer’s vessel; and cost, insurance, and freight 
(CIF), where goods are delivered by the seller to 
the buyer’s warehouse or storage tanks using the 
seller’s ships or trucks.

Dividend Policy
The Company applies a dividend policy in accordance 
with applicable regulations, namely Law No. 40 of 
2007 concerning Limited Liability Companies (UUPT) 
and the Articles of Association, and considers other 
factors such as retained earnings, financial condition, 
liquidity condition, future business prospects, and 
cash requirements. The dividend distribution policy 
is also the responsibility of the Board of Directors 
with the approval of the Board of Commissioners 
for interim dividends and with the approval of the 
General Meeting of Shareholders (GMS) for cash 
dividend distribution.
Interim dividends are distributed before the end 
of the fiscal year, provided they do not cause the 
Company’s net assets to fall below the issued 
capital, fully paid-up capital, and required reserves. 
If the distribution of interim dividends causes a loss 
after the end of the fiscal year, the Shareholders 
are required to return the dividend to the Company. 
In this situation, the Board of Directors and the Board 
of Commissioners are fully responsible for ensuring 
the return process proceeds in accordance with 
applicable regulations.
Meanwhile, cash dividends are distributed to the 
Shareholders on the recording date, granting full 
entitlement to the dividend amount. These cash 
dividends are subject to income tax based on the 
taxation regulations in Indonesia, which also apply to 
Shareholders residing abroad.

 Business Prospects in 2025
Industri kelapa sawit Indonesia pada tahun 2025 
diproyeksikan tetap kuat, didukung oleh tingginya

konsumsi domestik minyak sawit. Program ini 
bertujuan untuk mengurangi ketergantungan pada

pemerintah 
terus

sawit

dan praktik pertanian berkelanjutan. Di sisi lain, 
pengembangan sektor hilir seperti oleokimia dan 
bioenergi menjadi fokus utama untuk mengurangi 
ketergantungan pada ekspor CPO mentah yang 
masih rentan terhadap fluktuasi harga global.
Namun, industri ini juga menghadapi berbagai 
tantangan, terutama dari regulasi internasional 
seperti European Union Deforestation Regulation 
(EUDR), yang dapat membatasi akses pasar ekspor 
ke Uni Eropa. Dalam menghadapi tantangan ini, 
pemerintah berkomitmen untuk memperkuat prinsip 
keberlanjutan dengan mendorong pemanfaatan 
lahan terdegradasi untuk ekspansi perkebunan serta 
penguatan sertifikasi berkelanjutan guna memenuhi

teknologi 
pengolahan, 
terus 
diperkuat 
guna 
memperkokoh daya saing industri sawit di pasar

stabilitas

menciptakan

The Indonesian palm oil industry in 2025 is projected 
to 
remain 
strong, 
supported 
by 
high 
global

The implementation of the mandatory Biodiesel B40 
program, which will be enforced starting in 2025, 
is expected to strengthen Indonesia’s position in 
the international market and boost domestic palm 
oil consumption. This program aims to reduce 
dependence on fossil energy, increase the added 
value of the palm oil industry, and support Indonesia’s 
commitment to sustainable energy transition.
Additionally, the government continues to accelerate 
the rejuvenation of smallholder palm oil plantations 
and strengthen industry downstreaming as strategic 
steps to improve productivity and added value of 
palm oil products. The rejuvenation aims to increase 
production yields through the use of superior seeds 
and sustainable farming practices. On the other 
hand, the development of downstream sectors such 
as oleochemicals and bioenergy has become a key 
focus to reduce dependence on raw CPO exports, 
which remain vulnerable to global price fluctuations.
However, the industry also faces various challenges, 
especially 
from 
international 
regulations 
such 
as the European Union Deforestation Regulation 
(EUDR), which may limit export market access to the

enhancing 
sustainable 
certification 
to 
meet 
international 
standards. 
Additionally, 
various 
incentives, such as support for the biodiesel 
program and the strengthening of processing 
technologies, are continually reinforced to enhance 
the competitiveness of the palm oil industry in the 
global market. With these strategies in place, the 
palm oil industry is expected to remain a key pillar 
in supporting the national economy, maintaining 
foreign exchange stability, and creating sustainable 
and inclusive long-term growth opportunities.
