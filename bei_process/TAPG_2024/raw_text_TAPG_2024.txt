Economy Overview
Pergerakan ekonomi global sepanjang tahun 2024 masih 
sarat dengan ketidakpastian dan kerentanan, akibat tensi 
geopolitik yang berkepanjangan, volatilitas harga energi 
dan komoditas, serta kondisi iklim yang berdampak pada 
pertanian dan produksi. Bank Dunia memperkirakan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global berada di sekitar 2,4%. 
The fluctuations in the global economy in 2024 continue 
to be marked by uncertainty and vulnerability. This is 
mainly due to persistent geopolitical tensions, instability 
in energy and commodity prices, and climatic conditions 
impacting agriculture and production. The World Bank 
forecasts global economic growth at around 2.4%.

Berdasarkan rilis BPS, pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia 
tercatat mencapai 5,03% pada 2024. Pencapaian ini 
didukung oleh tingkat inflasi yang cukup terkendali di 
level 1,57%. Pertumbuhan tersebut terjadi pada semua 
lapangan usaha. Dari sisi produksi, pertumbuhan 
tertinggi terjadi pada Lapangan Usaha Jasa Lainnya 
sebesar 9,80%. Sementara itu, pertumbuhan tertinggi 
dari sisi pengeluaran dicapai oleh Komponen Pengeluaran 
Konsumsi Lembaga Nonprofit yang Melayani Rumah 
Tangga (PK-LNPRT) sebesar 12,48%. Ekonomi yang tetap 
tumbuh dan bergerak akan berdampak pada permintaan 
terhadap produk Perseroan.
Gabungan Pengusaha Kelapa Sawit Indonesia (GAPKI) 
menyambut baik komitmen pemerintah yang menegaskan 
bahwa kelapa sawit adalah aset strategis bangsa yang 
harus dijaga oleh seluruh elemen masyarakat. Penegasan 
ini turut menjelaskan visi pemerintahan baru terhadap 
peran strategis sawit dalam mendukung ketahanan 
pangan dan energi nasional. Program kemandirian energi, 
seperti penerapan B40 bergantung pada sawit sebagai 
bahan baku.
Minyak kelapa sawit berperan penting sebagai minyak 
nabati yang paling banyak dikonsumsi dunia sepanjang 
tahun 2024 dengan pangsa pasar Indonesia sekitar 60% 
dari pangsa pasar produsen CPO dunia. Namun, industri 
sawit Indonesia masih menghadapi berbagai tantangan 
domestik seperti stagnasi produksi, kebutuhan replanting 
tanaman tua, serta dinamika kebijakan dalam negeri.
Di sisi eksternal, tantangan muncul dari fluktuasi pasokan 
dan permintaan minyak nabati lainnya, kampanye negatif 
terkait keberlanjutan rantai pasok, hingga tekanan 
geopolitik dari Eropa dan Timur Tengah. 
According to the BPS report, Indonesia’s economy 
grew by 5.03% in 2024. This growth was supported by 
a relatively stable inflation rate of 1.57%, occurring across 
all sectors. Notably, the Other Service Business sector 
experienced the highest production growth at 9.80%. On 
the expenditure side, the highest growth was observed in 
the Consumption Expenditure Component of Non-Profit 
Institutions Serving Households (PK-LNPRT) at 12.48%. 
Continued economic growth will influence the demand 
for the Company’s products.
The Indonesian Palm Oil Association (IPOA) commends 
the government’s commitment to recognizing palm oil as 
a vital national asset that requires collective protection. 
This statement also reflects the new government’s vision 
of palm oil’s strategic significance in bolstering national 
food and energy security. Initiatives focused on energy 
independence, such as the B40 implementations, depend 
on palm oil as a primary raw material.
In 2024, palm oil remains the most widely used vegetable 
oil globally, with Indonesia accounting for approximately 
60% of the market share in crude palm oil production. 
However, the Indonesian palm oil sector is facing several 
domestic challenges, including stagnant production 
levels, the need to replant aging trees, and dynamic 
domestic policies.
Externally, challenges arise from fluctuations in supply 
and demand for other vegetable oils, negative publicity 
regarding supply chain sustainability, and geopolitical 
pressures from Europe and the Middle East.

Liabilitas Jangka Pendek

Current Assets
In 2024, the Company recorded current assets of Rp3,247 
billion, representing an increase of 33% from Rp2,446 
billion in 2023, driven by a rise in cash and accounts 
receivable alongside growing revenue.
Current Assets
Meanwhile, non-current assets totaled Rp11,061 billion 
in 2024, down 3% from Rp11,422 billion in 2023. This 
decline was due to reduced investment in joint ventures, 
lower dividend receipts from associated companies, and 
a decrease in deferred tax assets.
Total Assets
The Company’s total assets rose by 3%, increasing from 
Rp13,867 billion in 2023 to Rp14,307 billion in 2024. 
This growth was attributable to a rise in the Company’s 
current assets.
Current Liabilities
As of December 31, 2024, the Company’s short-term liabilities 
amounted to Rp2,362 billion, reflecting a 21% increase from 
Rp1,959 billion in 2023. This surge was primarily attributed 
to a rise in short-term loans and an increase in tax payables, 
consistent with the growth in the Company’s profit.
Non-Current Liabilities
On the other hand, the Company’s long-term liabilities 
were recorded at Rp669 billion in 2024, reflecting a 17% 
increase from Rp569 billion in 2023, driven by an increase 
in bank debt and deferred tax liabilities.

Prospek Usaha
Business Outlook
Minyak kelapa sawit terus mendominasi sebagai minyak 
nabati yang paling banyak dikonsumsi dunia sepanjang 
tahun 2024 dan diperkirakan masih akan terus berlanjut 
demikian untuk beberapa tahun mendatang. Produk 
minyak sawit Indonesia memiliki peran signifikan dalam 
pasar global, baik di sektor konsumen maupun energi 
baru terbarukan (EBT). Sekitar 60% dari total produksinya 
diekspor ke lebih dari 160 negara, menjadikan komoditas 
ini esensial dalam perdagangan internasional.
Pemerintah berupaya mengintegrasikan kebijakan tata 
kelola kelapa sawit yang berkelanjutan melalui penerbitan 
Peraturan Presiden (Perpres) tentang Strategi dan Aksi 
Nasional Kelapa Sawit Berkelanjutan (SANAS KSB) Tahun 
2025-2029. Tak hanya itu, program mandatori biodiesel 
juga merupakan konsepsi nyata dari penerapan hilirisasi 
produk kelapa sawit. Mandatori B40 ditargetkan dimulai 
pada 2025, dengan penyaluran sebesar 16,08 juta KL dan 
potensi penghematan devisa sebesar Rp158,86 triliun.  
Di samping itu, pemerintah juga sedang melakukan 
proses pengembangan Palm Kernel Expeller (PKE) atau 
bungkil sawit yang berpotensi menjadi pakan ternak serta 
dapat diolah menjadi bioetanol yang diharapkan dapat 
masuk dalam daftar Carbon Offsetting and Reduction 
Scheme for International Aviation (CORSIA) Sustainable 
Aviation Fuel (SAF) yang diakui oleh International Civil 
Aviation Organization (ICAO).   
Dalam menjawab tantangan global atas produk-produk 
kelapa sawit yang berkelanjutan, terutama dalam 
menghadapi kebijakan European Union Deforestation-
free Regulation (EUDR) yang diberlakukan pada akhir 
Desember 2025 , pemerintah telah membangun Dasbor 
Nasional Data dan Informasi Komoditi Berkelanjutan 
Indonesia yang meliputi komoditas yang terdampak 
kebijakan EUDR, yaitu kelapa sawit, kakao, karet, kopi, 
dan kayu.
Palm oil remains the leading vegetable oil globally in 2024, 
with expectations to maintain this status in the coming 
years. Indonesian palm oil significantly influences the 
global market, impacting both consumer sectors and 
renewable energy (EBT). Approximately 60% of its total 
production is exported to over 160 countries, underscoring 
the commodity’s crucial role in international trade.
The government aims to integrate sustainable palm oil 
governance through a Presidential Regulation (Perpres) 
that establishes the National Strategy and Action for 
Sustainable Palm Oil (SANAS KSB) for 2025-2029. 
Additionally, the mandatory biodiesel program represents 
a significant effort to promote the use of downstream 
palm oil products. The B40 mandate is set to start in 
2025, with a planned distribution of 16.08 million KL and 
anticipated foreign exchange savings of Rp158.86 trillion.
Furthermore, the government is promoting the 
development of Palm Kernel Expeller (PKE), also referred 
to as palm kernel meal. This product shows potential 
as an animal feed ingredient and can be converted into 
bioethanol. It is expected that PKE will be included in the 
Carbon Offsetting and Reduction Scheme for International 
Aviation (CORSIA) Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) list 
endorsed by the International Civil Aviation Organization 
(ICAO).
To address global sustainability challenges related 
to palm oil, particularly in light of the European Union 
Deforestation-free Regulation (EUDR) set to take effect 
in December 2025, the government has created a 
National Dashboard for Sustainable Commodity Data and 
Information in Indonesia. This dashboard encompasses 
commodities impacted by the EUDR policy, including palm 
oil, cocoa, rubber, coffee, and wood.

Amendments to PSAK 201 (previously 
referred to as PSAK 1): Presentation of 
Financial Statements Classification of 
Liabilities as Current or Non-current and 
Noncurrent Liabilities with Covenants
The amendments specify the requirements for classifying 
liabilities as current or non-current and clarify:
1.	 what is meant by a right to defer settlement;
2.	 the right to defer must exist at the end of the reporting 
period,
3.	 classification is not affected by the likelihood that an 
entity will exercise its deferral right, and
4.	 only if an embedded derivative in a convertible liability 
is an equity instrument would the terms and conditions 
of a liability will not impact its classification.

Amendemen PSAK 116 (sebelumnya dirujuk 
sebagai PSAK 73): Liabilitas Sewa dalam Jual 
Beli dan Sewa-balik
Amendemen PSAK 116 Sewa menetapkan persyaratan 
yang digunakan penjual penyewa dalam mengukur 
kewajiban sewa yang timbul dalam transaksi jual beli 
dan sewa-balik, untuk memastikan penjual penyewa tidak 
mengakui jumlah setiap keuntungan atau kerugian yang 
terkait dengan hak guna yang dipertahankan.
Amendemen PSAK 207 (sebelumnya dirujuk 
sebagai PSAK 2) dan PSAK 107 (sebelumnya 
dirujuk sebagai PSAK 60): Pengaturan 
Pembiayaan Pemasok
Amendemen PSAK 207 dan PSAK 107 mengklarifikasi 
karakteristik pengaturan pembiayaan pemasok 
dan mensyaratkan pengungkapan tambahan atas 
pengaturan pembiayaan pemasok tersebut. Persyaratan 
pengungkapan dalam amendemen ini dimaksudkan 
untuk membantu pengguna laporan keuangan dalam 
memahami dampak pengaturan pembiayaan pemasok 
terhadap liabilitas, arus kas, dan eksposur terhadap risiko 
likuiditas suatu entitas.
Amandemen ini tidak mempunyai dampak terhadap 
laporan keuangan konsolidasian Perseroan.
In addition, a requirement has been introduced to require 
disclosure when a liability arising from a loan agreement 
is classified as non-current and the entity’s right to defer 
settlement is contingent on compliance with future 
covenants within twelve months.
Amendments of PSAK 116 (previously 
referred to as PSAK 73): Lease Liability in a 
Sale and Leaseback
The amendments to PSAK 116 Leases specify the 
requirements that a sellerlessee uses in measuring the 
lease liability arising in a sale and leaseback transaction, 
to ensure the seller-lessee does not recognize any amount 
of the gain or loss that relates to the right of use it retains.
Amendments of PSAK 207 (previously 
referred to as PSAK 2) and PSAK 107 
(previously referred to as PSAK 60): Supplier 
Finance Arrangements
The amendments to PSAK 207 and PSAK 107 clarify the 
characteristics of supplier finance arrangements and 
require additional disclosure of such arrangements. The 
disclosure requirements in the amendments are intended 
to assist users of financial statements in understanding 
the effects of supplier finance arrangements on an entity’s 
liabilities, cash flows, and exposure to liquidity risk.
The amendments did not have any impact on the Group’s 
consolidated financial statements.
