2023 sudah terlihat sejak akhir tahun 2022. Walaupun 
ada indikasi pemulihan ekonomi dari dampak pandemi 
dan invasi Rusia ke Ukraina, tetapi masih berjalan 
sangat lambat dan tidak merata. Selain masalah

dan negara-negara kawasan Eropa Barat atau kawasan

dan negara-negara Eropa akhirnya berimbas pada pada 
negara-negara mitra dagang AS dan Eropa.2) 3)
Sementara ekonomi China berhasil rebound dengan 
mencatat tren pertumbuhan positif setelah pelonggaran 
Zero-COVID Policy menjelang akhir tahun 2022. Namun 
pencapaian tersebut termasuk kinerja perekonomian

yang lemah mempengaruhi permintaan komoditas 
global.4) 5)
Dinamika perekonomian dan geopolitik global tentu 
secara langsung dan tidak langsung mempengaruhi
perekonomian domestik. Tetapi di tengah melambatnya 
perekonomian global dan menurunnya harga komoditas 
pokok, Indonesia berhasil mempertahankan kinerja 
pertumbuhannya sebesar 5,05%, walaupun melambat 
dibandingkan 5,31% pada tahun 2022. Kegiatan 
manufaktur mampu bertahan di zona ekspansi sejak 
pandemi berakhir. Artinya, Indonesia termasuk di dalam 
kategori negara dengan ekonomi resilien dan kegiatan 
manufaktur yang tetap positif dan ekspansif.6) 7) 
Global economic risks and uncertainties in 2023 were 
evident since late 2022. Despite indications of economic 
recovery from the pandemic’s impact and Russia’s 
invasion of Ukraine, progress was slow and uneven. 
Alongside economic issues, geopolitical conditions also 
posed signifi cant risks, with conﬂ icts in Ukraine and 
Palestine showing no signs of resolution, which raised 
downside risk of negative economic growth prospects.1)
The global economic slowdown primarily resulted from 
weakened economies of developed countries, such 
as the United States (US), Japan, South Korea and 
Eurozone countries. Economic slowdown in the US and 
Europe eventually aﬀ ected trading partners of both 
regions.2) 3)
Meanwhile, China’s economy rebounded, showing 
positive growth trends after the easing of Zero-
COVID Policy towards the end of 2022. However, 
this achievement reﬂ ected China’s weak economic 
performance due to struggles in overcoming the 
property sector crisis, which in turn aﬀ ects global 
commodity demand.4) 5)
The dynamics of global economy and geopolitics 
directly 
and 
indirectly 
inﬂ uenced 
the 
domestic 
economy. Despite the global economic slowdown and 
declining commodity prices, Indonesia managed to 
maintain its growth performance at 5.05%, although 
slower than the 5.31% recorded in 2022. Manufacturing 
activities remained in the expansion zone since the end 
of the pandemic, indicating Indonesia’s resilience as an 
economy with positive and expansive manufacturing 
activities.6) 7)

Industrial Review
Industri semen dalam negeri masih menghadapi kondisi 
kelebihan pasokan yang tinggi, karena para pemain 
baru mulai melakukan produksi, sehingga persaingan 
antar produsen semakin ketat. Dari 119.9 
juta ton 
kapasitas pabrik yang terpasang seluruh Indonesia, 
hanya terserap sekitar 55% di pasar domestik.
Penjualan semen domestik pada tahun 2023 tumbuh 
sebesar 3,5% dengan volume penjualan semen sebesar 
65,5 juta ton. Pertumbuhan tersebut lebih tinggi 
daripada tahun 2022 yang terkontraksi sebesar -2,9%. 
Sementara volume ekspor semen dan terak juga tumbuh 
20,7% pada tahun 2023 dengan volume ekspor sebesar 
10,7 juta ton, sedangkan volume ekspor 2022 adalah 8,9 
juta ton, sehingga pertumbuhan volume ekspor adalah 
20,22%. Dengan demikian, total penjualan semen 
domestik dan ekspor tahun 2023 tercatat sebesar 76,2 
juta ton atau tumbuh 5,54% dibandingkan tahun 2022.10)
Peningkatan penjualan semen domestik disebabkan 
oleh penjualan semen curah yang tumbuh 13,6% 
dibandingkan 
tahun 
sebelumnya. 
Semen 
curah 
umumnya digunakan untuk konstruksi infrastruktur. 
Sementara itu, penjualan semen kantong yang 
umumnya 
digunakan 
untuk 
konstruksi 
properti 
perumahan terkontraksi sebesar -1,5% pada tahun 
2023. Proporsi penjualan semen kantong sebesar 
71% dari total penjualan semen domestik sementara 
proporsi penjualan semen curah sebesar 29% dari total 
penjualan semen domestik selama tahun 2023.11)
Pertumbuhan tertinggi penjualan semen terjadi di 
Kalimantan yang tumbuh sebesar 22,1%. Pertumbuhan 
tinggi penjualan semen di Kalimantan didorong 
oleh penjualan semen di Kalimantan Timur karena 
pembangunan IKN Nusantara dan Kalimantan Utara 
yang tumbuh sebesar 64,6% karena pembangunan 
Kawasan Industri Tanah Kuning.12) 
Pada penjualan ekspor, volume ekspor terak tumbuh 
tinggi sebesar 25,1% pada tahun 2023. Sebaliknya 
volume ekspor semen mengalami kontraksi sebesar 
-6,1%. Proporsi ekspor semen didominasi oleh bentuk 
ekspor terak, yakni 88% dari total ekspor semen dan 
terak pada 2023. Negara tujuan ekspor terak terbesar 
adalah Bangladesh, Australia dan Taiwan.13)
Domestic cement industry continued to face high 
oversupply condition as new players enter production, 
intensifying competition among manufacturers. Out of 
Indonesia’s total installed plant capacity of 119.9 million 
tons, only around 55% is absorbed in the domestic 
market.
Domestic cement sales grew by 3.5% in 2023, with a 
sales volume of 65.5 million tons. This growth surpassed 
2022 contraction of -2.9%. Meanwhile, cement and 
clinker export volumes grew by 20.7% in 2023, reaching 
10.7 million tons, while the export volume in 2022 is 8.9 
million tons, hence export volume growth is 20.22%. 
Consequently, total domestic and export cement sales 
in 2023 amounted to 76.2 million tons, a 5.54% increase 
compared to 2022.10)
The increase in domestic cement sales was driven by 
bulk cement sales, which grew by 13.6% compared 
to the previous year. Bulk cement is primarily used 
for infrastructure construction. Meanwhile, bagged 
cement sales, typically used for residential property 
construction, contracted by -1.5% in 2023. Bagged 
cement sales and bulk cement sales accounted for 71% 
and 29%, respectively, of total domestic cement sales 
in 2023.11)
The highest growth in cement sales occurred in 
Kalimantan, with a growth rate of 22.1%. This growth 
was primarily driven by cement sales in East Kalimantan 
due to the development of IKN Nusantara and 
North Kalimantan, which grew by 64.6% due to the 
development of Tanah Kuning Industrial Estate.12)
In terms of export sales, clinker export volumes saw 
signifi cant growth of 25.1% in 2023. However, cement 
export volumes contracted by -6.1%. Clinker exports 
dominated the proportion of cement and clinker exports, 
accounting for 88% of total exports in 2023. The main 
export destinations for cement were Bangladesh, 
Australia and Taiwan.13)

One positive catalyst for cement sales is the 0% VAT 
incentive policy for the purchase of ready-to-use 
houses and apartments priced below Rp5 billion, 
which has been in eﬀ ect since November 2023 until 
June 2024, followed by a 50% VAT incentive from July 
2024 to December 2024. Another positive catalyst is 
the acceleration of IKN Nusantara development and 
increased infrastructure spending, which are expected 
to drive cement demand in 2024.14)
The infrastructure sector represents the largest market 
for Indonesian cement products. The government 
has two priorities related to infrastructure in 2023. 
Firstly, accelerating the completion of national priority 
infrastructure development. Secondly, promoting the 
growth of new economic centers, including the new 
capital of IKN Nusantara. The core government center 
in the IKN Nusantara area is being developed with the 
state budget (APBN). Meanwhile, for the development of 
support areas, the government is oﬀ ering opportunities 
to private investors.15)
The government allocated a budget of Rp392 trillion 
for infrastructure development in 2023 State Budget, 
an increase of 7.2% from 2022 budget of Rp365.8 
trillion. The Ministry of Public Works and Public Housing 
(PUPR) initially received a budget allocation of Rp125.22 
trillion, which was later increased to Rp125.99 trillion. 
This budget allocation is larger than the 2022 budget 
allocation of Rp116.37 trillion.16) 
In implementing the infrastructure spending program 
for 2023 fi scal year, the Ministry of PUPR focused on 
completing ongoing construction works, particularly 
National Strategic Projects (PSN), before the term 
of Indonesia Maju Cabinet ends. Several priority 
infrastructure and basic infrastructure projects or 
connectivity projects were targeted to be completed in 
2023, or at the latest in the fi rst semester of 2024. 17) 18)

Teknologi Injeksi Hidrogen untuk Masa Depan Netral Karbon
Pabrik Narogong 1 (NAR1) berhasil mengimplementasikan Teknologi Injeksi Hidrogen dalam proses 
produksi seperti operasi kiln. Teknologi Injeksi Hidrogen adalah solusi berkelanjutan yang diharapkan dapat 
memitigasi dampak perubahan iklim. Teknologi yang juga dikenal sebagai Hydrogen Rich Gas (HRG) ini 
mengedepankan pemanfaatan gas hidrogen, bahan bakar yang bersih dan sangat mudah terbakar, sebagai 
bahan tambahan Dalam proses pembakaran.
Injeksi gas hidrogen membantu meningkatkan kecepatan proses pembakaran sehingga konsumsi bahan 
bakar menjadi lebih efi sien dan mengurangi adanya hidrokarbon yang tidak terbakar. Selain itu, suhu yang 
lebih tinggi dalam proses pembakaran dapat membantu penguraian polutan dan mengurangi emisi CO2.
Dengan pembakaran yang lebih cepat dan menyeluruh, bahan bakar yang terbuang menjadi lebih sedikit, 
sehingga menghasilkan efi siensi sumber daya energi. Peningkatan efi siensi energi dan pengurangan emisi 
karbon ini sejalan dengan tujuan keberlanjutan yang berkontribusi dalam mengurangi jejak lingkungan 
secara keseluruhan dalam proses industri. Keberhasilan penerapan teknologi ini berpotensi membuka 
jalan bagi implementasi serupa di kiln lain di lingkungan Perseroan maupun di lingkungan Grup SIG. Hasil 
sementara implementasi Teknologi Injeksi Hidrogen pada bulan Agustus 2023 adalah peningkatkan 
produktivitas kenaikan kiln feed sebesar 4% dan penurunan konsumsi termal sebesar 2%.
Hydrogen Injection Technology for a Carbon-Neutral-Future
Narogong 1 (NAR1) plant has successfully implemented Hydrogen Injection Technology in its production 
processes, including kiln operations. Hydrogen Injection Technology, also known as Hydrogen Rich Gas 
(HRG), is a sustainable solution aimed at mitigating the impact of climate change. This technology emphasizes 
the utilization of hydrogen gas, a clean and highly combustible fuel, as an additional component in the 
combustion process.  Hydrogen gas injection helps enhance the combustion process speed, thereby making 
fuel consumption more effi  cient and reducing unburned hydrocarbons. Additionally, higher temperatures 
during the combustion process can aid in the breakdown of pollutants and reduce CO2 emissions.
With faster and more thorough combustion, less fuel is wasted, resulting in energy resource effi  ciency. The 
improvement in energy effi  ciency and reduction in carbon emissions align with sustainability objectives 
that contribute to reducing the overall environmental footprint in industrial processes. The successful 
implementation of this technology has the potential to pave the way for similar implementations in other kilns 
within the Company’s environment and across the SIG Group. Preliminary results from the implementation 
of Hydrogen Injection Technology in August 2023 show a 4% increase in kiln feed productivity and a 2% 
decrease in thermal consumption.
Perseroan terus berinovasi pada proses produksi di Pabrik Narogong dengan menghadirkan teknologi injeksi hidrogen untuk 
masa depan yang lebih baik dan berkelanjutan.
The Company continues to innovate the production process at the Narogong Plant by presenting hydrogen injection technology 
for a better and more sustainable future.

Saat ini sampah menjadi persoalan umum yang dihadapi 
semua kota di dunia. Teknologi Refused-Derived Fuel
(RDF) adalah upaya pengelolaan sampah berkelanjutan

alternatif terbarukan dan dapat mengurangi emisi CO2. 
Setelah 
diresmikan

Koordinator 
Kemaritiman dan Investasi, Luhut Binsar Panjaitan pada

residu (zero waste to landfi ll).
Pemanfaatan sampah domestik menjadi RDF mampu 
mensubstitusi penggunaan batubara menjadi bahan 
bakar alternatif di SBI Pabrik Cilacap hingga 7% 
Substitusi Energi Panas (Thermal Substitution Rate/TSR) 
dari seluruh energi panas yang dibutuhkan. Kesuksesan 
proyek RDF di Kabupaten Cilacap menjadi inspirasi bagi 
kota-kota lain di Indonesia dalam upaya penyelesaian 
permasalahan sampah diantaranya kolaborasi bersama 
dengan 
Pemerintah 
Kabupaten 
Banyumas, 
DKI

UTILIZATION 
INTO 
RDF 
(REFUSED-

FUEL) 
WITH 
REGIONAL 
GOVERNMENTS
Currently, waste is a common problem faced by all cities 
in the world. Refused-Derived Fuel (RDF) technology 
is a sustainable waste management eﬀ ort to convert 
waste into renewable alternative energy and reduce 
CO2 emissions.
After being inaugurated by the Coordinating Minister for 
Maritime Aﬀ airs and Investment, Luhut Binsar Panjaitan 
on 21 July 2020, the RDF facility at Jeruklegi TPA, 
Cilacap Regency is fully operational with a capacity of 
160 tons of fresh waste per day producing 70-80 tons 
of RDF per day. All processed fresh waste can be fully 
utilized without leaving residue (zero waste to landfi ll).
Utilization of domestic waste into RDF is able to 
substitute the use of coal into alternative fuels at the 
SBI Cilacap Plant up to 7% Thermal Substitution Rate 
(TSR) of all the required heat energy. The success of 
the RDF project in Cilacap Regency has inspired other 
cities in Indonesia to work on solving waste problems, 
including collaboration with the govermnent of 
Banyumas Regency, DKI Jakarta Province, Bali Provnce, 
City of Banda Aceh, Aceh Besar Regency, Temanggung 
Regency and Sleman Regency.

TARGET/PROYEKSI TAHUN 2024
2024 Targets/Projection
Tahun 2024, Perseroan menargetkan pertumbuhan 
pendapatan dan laba bersih yang lebih baik. Untuk 
itu, Perseroan merencanakan belanja modal untuk 
menjaga kelangsungan dan meningkatkan keunggulan 
operasional, meningkatkan kapabilitas keberlanjutan, 
dan dekarbonisasi.
In terms of infrastructure, the Company is developing 
pier and production facilities in Tuban, East Java, which 
are targeted to be operational in 2024. Along with the 
completion of the port development for export, the 
Company is optimistic that it can achieve an export 
volume of 500,000 to 1 million tons of cement per year.
In line with the principles of sustainable development, 
the Company through its 2030 Sustainability Road Map 
targets a CO2 reduction of around 30% from the 2010 
baseline. One of the eﬀ orts made is to increase effi  ciency 
or reduce the clinker factor, so that the Company’s 
cement products become low carbon (green products). 
The company has launched environmentally friendly 
concrete products such as SpeedCrete and ThruCrete. 
The company is currently discussing intensively 
with the Ministry of Industry to issue standards for 
environmentally friendly cement that can support the 
government’s target to reduce CO2 emissions.
In connection with the energy transition, the Company 
has long used alternative energy to replace fossil fuels 
such as industrial waste, biomass and municipal waste 
which is processed into RDF. It takes time and a number 
of technical adjustments for the Company to 100% 
switch from the use of fossil fuels. Currently, the use of 
alternative energy in the Company has reached 21-22% 
in reducing fossil fuels and will continue to be increased.
The Company strives to improve its performance to 
be better from year to year. In 2023, the Company 
recorded a revenue of Rp12.371,3 billion, grew 0.89% 
from the previous year. In the midst of oversupply 
market conditions and tight price competition, the 
Company was able to record revenue growth in all 
segments. Cement rose 0.25%, fi nished concrete rose 
4.53%, other construction services rose 6.75%, and 
aggregate rose 31.33%.
In 2024, the Company targets better revenue and 
net profi t growth. For this reason, the Company 
plans capital expenditure to maintain continuity and 
improve operational excellence, increase sustainability 
capabilities and decarbonization.
