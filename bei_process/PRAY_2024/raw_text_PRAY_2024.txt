Tinjauan Perekonomian dan Industri 
Economic and Industry Overview
Tahun 2024 merupakan periode yang penuh dinamika bagi 
perekonomian global dan nasional, yang turut mempengaruhi 
perkembangan berbagai sektor industri, termasuk layanan 
kesehatan. Dalam kondisi ketidakpastian global yang 
berlanjut, Indonesia mampu mempertahankan pertumbuhan 
ekonominya di tengah tantangan eksternal dan domestik. 
Stabilitas makroekonomi, komitmen pemerintah dalam 
memperkuat sektor kesehatan, serta akselerasi adopsi 
teknologi menjadi faktor-faktor penting yang membentuk 
lanskap industri layanan kesehatan nasional.
ANALISIS EKONOMI GLOBAL
Perkembangan ekonomi global tahun 2024 diwarnai oleh 
perlambatan pertumbuhan di beberapa negara maju dan 
pemulihan bertahap di negara berkembang. Menurut laporan 
World Economic Outlook (WEO) IMF edisi Oktober 2024, 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global diperkirakan mencapai 3,2%. 
Angka ini relatif stabil dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya, 
namun menunjukkan adanya ketidakpastian akibat 
ketegangan geopolitik, fragmentasi perdagangan, dan 
penyesuaian kebijakan moneter global.
Dari sisi inflasi, meskipun terjadi tren penurunan, tekanan 
harga masih cukup tinggi di beberapa kawasan. IMF mencatat 
bahwa inflasi global diperkirakan berada pada kisaran 5,9% 
pada tahun 2024, turun dari puncaknya di tahun 2022, namun 
masih di atas target pra-pandemi. Kondisi ini mendorong 
bank sentral di berbagai negara mempertahankan suku 
bunga tinggi dalam waktu yang lebih lama (higher for longer), 
yang berimplikasi pada penurunan investasi sektor riil, 
termasuk sektor kesehatan.
Teknologi kesehatan menjadi salah satu sektor yang tetap 
resilient di tengah ketidakpastian ekonomi. Menurut laporan 
World Bank Global Economic Prospects dan data dari OECD 
Health Statistics 2024, terdapat percepatan adopsi teknologi 
digital dalam layanan kesehatan, seperti penggunaan 
telemedicine, AI-based diagnostics, dan remote patient 
monitoring, yang diprediksi terus bertumbuh lebih dari 14% 
CAGR hingga 2028.
Perubahan demografi juga menjadi faktor pendorong 
utama. Penuaan populasi di negara maju, dengan proporsi 
penduduk usia di atas 65 tahun mencapai lebih dari 20%, 
serta urbanisasi pesat di negara berkembang mendorong 
peningkatan permintaan layanan kesehatan jangka panjang, 
manajemen penyakit kronis, dan kebutuhan layanan 
kesehatan berbasis komunitas.
The year 2024 was a dynamic period for both the global 
and national economies, significantly influencing the 
development of various industrial sectors, including 
healthcare services. Amid ongoing global uncertainties, 
Indonesia successfully maintained its economic growth 
despite facing external and domestic challenges.
Macroeconomic stability, the government’s commitment to 
strengthening the healthcare sector, and the acceleration 
of technology adoption have become key factors shaping 
the landscape of the national healthcare services industry.
GLOBAL ECONOMIC ANALYSIS
The global economy in 2024 was characterized by a slowdown 
in growth among several developed countries and a gradual 
recovery in emerging markets. According to the International 
Monetary Fund’s (IMF) World Economic Outlook (WEO) 
October 2024 edition, global economic growth was projected 
at 3.2%. While relatively stable compared to the previous 
year, this figure reflects prevailing uncertainties driven by 
geopolitical tensions, trade fragmentation, and adjustments 
to global monetary policies.
In terms of inflation, although a downward trend was 
observed, price pressures remained high in several regions. 
The IMF estimated global inflation at approximately 5.9% 
in 2024, down from its peak in 2022, yet still above pre-
pandemic targets. This situation led central banks across 
the globe to maintain high-interest rates for a longer period 
(higher for longer), which impacted real sector investments, 
including healthcare.
Healthcare technology emerged as one of the resilient 
sectors amidst economic uncertainty. Reports from the 
World Bank Global Economic Prospects and OECD Health 
Statistics 2024 highlighted a rapid adoption of digital 
technologies in healthcare services, such as telemedicine, 
AI-based diagnostics, and remote patient monitoring, which 
are projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate 
(CAGR) exceeding 14% through 2028.
Demographic shifts also remained a major driving factor. 
Aging populations in developed countries—with over 20% 
of their population aged over 65—combined with rapid 
urbanization in developing countries, spurred increased 
demand for long-term healthcare services, chronic disease 
management, and community-based healthcare solutions.

NATIONAL ECONOMIC ANALYSIS
According to data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), 
Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.03% (c-to-c) in 2024, slightly 
lower than the 5.05% growth recorded in 2023. Nevertheless, 
this achievement reflects the resilience of the national 
economy amid ongoing global uncertainties (BPS, February 
2025). The growth was primarily supported by household 
consumption, which grew by 4.98%, and gross fixed capital 
formation (GFCF) which rose by 5.03%, although exports 
of goods and services only increased by 1.33%, reflecting 
weakening global demand.
Macroeconomic stability was further evidenced by controlled 
inflation rates. Throughout 2024, annual inflation was 
recorded at 1.57% in December 2024, remaining within 
Bank Indonesia’s target range of 1.5%–3.5% (Bank Indonesia, 
January 2025). However, pressures on the exchange rate 
persisted. The rupiah depreciated by more than 4% against 
the US dollar during 2024, reaching around IDR 16,860/USD 
by April 2025. To maintain monetary stability, Bank Indonesia 
kept its benchmark interest rate at 5.75% (Reuters, April 
2025). Exchange rate fluctuations could potentially increase 
the costs of importing medical equipment, pharmaceutical 
raw materials, and medical technologies, thereby impacting 
the cost structure of domestic healthcare service industries.
On the fiscal side, the government demonstrated strong 
commitment to supporting the healthcare sector. The 
healthcare budget allocation in the 2024 State Budget (APBN) 
was set at IDR 187.5 trillion, representing approximately 
5.6% of total government spending, up from IDR 185.5 
trillion in the previous year (Ministry of Finance, 2024). By 
the end of November 2024, the realization of healthcare 
spending had reached IDR 164.3 trillion, reflecting

ANALISIS INDUSTRI LAYANAN 
KESEHATAN
Industri layanan kesehatan di Indonesia sepanjang tahun 
2024 menunjukkan dinamika yang positif di tengah tantangan 
global dan domestik. Sejalan dengan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
nasional, sektor ini mengalami percepatan transformasi, 
terutama melalui adopsi teknologi digital, peningkatan 
kesadaran kesehatan masyarakat, dan dukungan kebijakan 
pemerintah.
Salah satu tren utama adalah percepatan digitalisasi 
layanan kesehatan. Pandemi COVID-19 mempercepat 
adopsi teknologi seperti telemedicine, electronic health 
records (EHR), dan remote patient monitoring. Menurut 
laporan Kementerian Kesehatan RI, penggunaan layanan 
telemedicine meningkat lebih dari 60% dibandingkan masa 
sebelum pandemi, dengan pemain utama seperti Halodoc, 
accelerated implementation of priority programs, including 
strengthening primary healthcare services, expediting 
vaccination programs, and managing both communicable 
and non-communicable diseases.
Efforts to expand healthcare access were actively pursued 
through the construction and revitalization of healthcare 
infrastructure across various regions. Key projects include 
the development of the Southern Regional Hospital in 
Bojonegoro, the construction of the Binong Community 
Health Center in Tangerang, and the revitalization of 
the Kraton and Pakualaman Community Health Centers 
in Yogyakarta (Ministry of Health, 2024). Furthermore, 
the Ministry of Health developed new vertical hospitals 
specializing in maternal and child healthcare across five 
strategic provinces.
In terms of regulation, 2023 marked a pivotal year with the 
enactment of Law No. 17/2023 on Health, which adopted 
an omnibus law approach to streamline and integrate 
healthcare regulations (Ministry of Health RI, 2023). This 
law emphasizes the integration of promotive, preventive, 
curative, and rehabilitative healthcare services within the 
national healthcare system. Concurrently, telemedicine 
regulations were strengthened to expand access to 
quality digital healthcare services and improve hospital 
accreditation standards to ensure service quality.
Overall, Indonesia’s relatively stable macroeconomic 
conditions, increased healthcare budget allocations, massive 
healthcare infrastructure development, and regulatory 
reforms serve as fundamental factors strengthening the 
foundation for the healthcare services industry’s growth 
amid dynamic global challenges.

health records (EHRs), and remote patient monitoring.

hospital networks and premium clinic segments.

the industry through various programs and regulations.

sustainable healthcare system.

Sesuai dengan peraturan perundang-undangan Indonesia,

pada RUPS berdasarkan rekomendasi Direksi Perseroan. 
Pembayaran dividen hanya dapat dilakukan apabila Perseroan 
mencatatkan laba bersih yang positif. Anggaran dasar 
Perseroan memperbolehkan pembagian dividen interim 
dengan ketentuan pembagian tersebut tidak menyebabkan 
kekayaan bersih Perseroan lebih kecil dari modal ditempatkan

Perseroan setelah mendapatkan persetujuan dari Dewan

Perseroan mengalami kerugian, dividen interim yang telah 
dibagikan harus dikembalikan oleh para pemegang saham 
kepada Perseroan. Dalam hal pemegang saham tidak dapat 
mengembalikan dividen interim, maka Direksi dan Dewan 
Komisaris Perseroan akan bertanggung jawab secara 
tanggung renteng atas kerugian Perseroan.

MATERIAL TRANSACTIONS
Material Transaction is any transaction carried out by a public 
company or a controlled company that meets the transaction 
value limit equal to 20% (twenty percent) or more of the 
equity of the Public Company based on the Financial Services 
Authority Regulation Number 17/POJK.04/2020 concerning 
Material Transactions and Changes in Business Activities.
In the event that the transaction as intended is carried out by 
a Public Company that has negative equity, the transaction 
is categorized as a Material Transaction if the transaction 
value is equal to 10% (ten percent) or more of the total assets 
of the Public Company.

7
Risiko Kredit
Credit Risk
Perseroan hanya melakukan transaksi hanya dengan pihak ketiga yang memiliki reputasi dan 
kredibilitas yang baik. Kebijakan Perseroan dalam mengelola risiko kredit dari pelanggan 
adalah dengan menetapkan batasan jumlah risiko yang dapat diterima untuk masing-masing 
pelanggan. Selain itu, saldo piutang dipantau secara terus menerus dengan tujuan bahwa 
eksposur Perseroan terhadap piutang tak tertagih tidak signifikan. Selain itu, Kas dan setara 
kas juga ditempatkan pada lembaga keuangan yang memiliki reputasi dan kredibilitas yang 
baik
The Company only conducts transactions with third parties who have a good reputation and 
credibility. The Company's policy in managing credit risk from customers is to set a limit 
on the amount of risk that can be accepted for each customer. In addition, the balance of 
receivables is monitored continuously with the aim of ensuring that the Company's exposure 
to uncollectible receivables is not significant. Furthermore, cash and cash equivalents are 
also placed in financial institutions that have a good reputation and credibility.
8
Risiko Likuiditas
Liquidity Risk
Perseroan memonitor kebutuhan likuiditas dengan memonitor jadwal pembayaran utang atas 
liabilitas keuangan, terutama utang pihak berelasi dan memonitor arus kas keluar sehubungan 
dengan aktivitas operasional setiap hari. Selain itu, Manajemen Perseroan juga secara 
terus-menerus menilai kondisi pasar keuangan untuk melihat peluang mendapatkan sumber 
pendanaan yang optimal
The Company monitors liquidity needs by overseeing the schedule of debt repayment for 
financial liabilities, especially related debts, and monitoring cash outflows in connection 
with daily operational activities. In addition, the Company's Management also continuously 
assesses financial market conditions to identify opportunities for obtaining optimal 
funding sources.
9
Risiko Permodalan
Capital Risk
Sebagaimana praktik yang berlaku umum, Grup mengevaluasi struktur permodalan melalui 
rasio utang terhadap modal (gearing ratio) yang dihitung melalui pembagian antara utang neto 
dengan modal
As is generally practiced, the Group evaluates its capital structure through a gearing ratio 
calculated by dividing net debt by capital.
