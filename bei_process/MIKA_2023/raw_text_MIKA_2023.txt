seluruh dunia terus menghadapi berbagai tantangan di 
tahun 2023.

ketika konflik antara Rusia dan Ukraina menemui jalan 
buntu, sedangkan terjadi ledakan ketegangan di jalur 
Gaza di bulan Oktober, yang menimbulkan kekhawatiran 
meningkatnya potensi menjadi konflik regional yang 
lebih luas. Konflik dagang antara dua perekonomian 
global terbesar, Tiongkok dan Amerika Serikat yang 
tidak mereda, juga mendorong terjadinya proses 
deglobalisasi yang mengganggu rantai pasok dunia 
yang telah dibangun selama beberapa dekade. Selain 
itu, berlanjutnya kekhawatiran terjadinya gangguan 
pada rantai pasokan pangan dan energi telah memicu 
terjadinya lonjakan inflasi, yang memaksa bank-bank 
sentral di banyak negara untuk mempertahankan 
kebijakan moneter yang ketat sepanjang tahun 2023, 
yang menghambat kegiatan ekonomi global.

MACROECONOMIC REVIEW
Post the Covid-19 pandemic, the worldwide economic 
recovery continued to face many challenges in 2023. 
The global geopolitical landscape remained volatile 
through the year, as the conflict between Russia and 
Ukraine reached a deadlock, while tensions flared 
up in the Gaza Strip in October, raising fears of a 
potential escalation into a broader regional conflict. 
Additionally, the trade tensions between the two 
largest global economies, China and the United States, 
remained unabated, contributing to a trend towards de-
globalization that disrupted the extensive global supply 
chain established over decades. Further, persistent 
concerns about disruptions in food and energy supply 
chains resulted in a surge in inflation that forced central 
banks in many countries to leave the tight monetary 
policy unchanged throughout 2023, weighing on the 
global economic activity.
Facing a slew of domestic interest rate hikes, falling 
commodity prices and weakening global growth, 
Indonesia’s economy grew only by 5.05% in 2023, 
slowing from a 5.31% expansion the previous year, 
driven primarily by household consumption. Amidst 
the weakening of prices for the country’s main export 
commodities, coal and crude palm oil (CPO), exports 
rose only 1.32%, down sharply from 16.23% in 2022. 
As prices for some food commodities rose, the year-
on-year (y-on-y) inflation rate in December 2023 was 
2.61% according to the Indonesia’s Central Bureau of 
Statistics (Biro Pusat Statistik, or BPS), with a Consumer 
Price Index (CPI) of 116.56.
Despite a slight decrease from the previous year’s GDP 
growth, Indonesia’s economic achievement in 2023 
positioned the country in a favorable position among 
key emerging and developing economies, highlighting 
the country’s strong economic fundamentals.

THE NATIONAL HEALTHCARE AND HOSPITAL 
INDUSTRY
Three years since WHO declared Covid-19 as a global 
pandemic in March 2020, Indonesia’s healthcare sector 
has returned to its pre-Covid-19 trajectory in 2023. As 
business activities recover, health centers and hospitals 
witnesses the return of patient visits throughout the 
year.
Despite facing disruptions due to the Covid-19 
pandemic, the Indonesian healthcare sector exhibits 
an impressive progress in the last decade. The 
significance of healthcare issues on the Government’s 
agenda is demonstrated in the allocated budget for 
healthcare development, amounting to Rp187.5 trillion 
or 5.6% of the state expenditure in 2024. Since 2014, 
the country also witnessed the successful launch of 
the country’s universal health coverage scheme, or 
Jaringan Kesehatan Nasional (JKN), aiming at providing 
all Indonesian citizens with equal access to medical 
services. By the end of 2023, total membership 
reached over 262.7 million, making it one of the largest 
healthcare systems in the world.

As the middle-income class expands at a rapid pace and 
shows a growing interest to their wellbeing, Indonesia

involvement of the private sector, as evidenced in the 
increasing number of hospitals in recent years.
However, the ratios of hospital beds to 1,000 Indonesia’s

disparities between Indonesian regions. The ratio of

as an underpenetrated market with robust growth 
potential, but it also highlights the country’s complex 
structural challenges.

Ministry of Health, the Government has put efforts to 
introduce policies that will support the development of 
an improved and more inclusive healthcare system for 
the people of Indonesia.
The enactment of the omnibus health bill, Law No. 17 of 
2023, in August 2023, represents a sweeping move to 
reshape the nation’s healthcare services, strengthening 
its healthcare workforce and streamlining regulations to 
improve access to high-quality medical care.
Going forward, Indonesia’s healthcare industry will 
continue to see Government’s commitment to provide 
cost-effective healthcare services to all Indonesians. 
The newly ratified Omnibus Health Law will bring 
forth both promising opportunities and anticipated 
challenges for the stakeholders. Further, the country’s 
vast population and growing middle class base instill 
confidence in the long-term prospects for the country’s 
healthcare market.

segments.

Total patient volume increased to 3.16 million in 2023 
from 2.89 million in 2022.

Rp2,668.1 billion in 2022, while outpatient net revenues 
also

from 
Rp1,380.8 billion a year earlier.
Contribution from inpatient services reached 67.4% 
from 65.9% a year earlier, while outpatient services 
accounted to the remaining 32.6% of the Company’s 
total revenue for the year.
Net revenues from inpatient services comprises of net 
revenues from drugs and medical supplies, inpatient 
rooms, medical support services, operating and 
delivery room, administration and professional fees. Net 
revenues from drugs and medical supplies remained the 
largest contributor with 45.5% contribution, followed by 
net revenues from inpatient rooms and medical support 
services with 19.7% and 15.2% contributions respectively 
to net revenues from inpatient services in 2023.
Net revenues from outpatient services comprises of 
revenues from medical support services, drugs and 
medical supplies, professional fees and registration 
fees. Net revenues from medical support services was 
the largest contributor to outpatient net revenues with 
43.9% contribution, followed by net revenues from 
drugs and medical supplies and professional fees with 
38.0% and 12.9% contribution respectively to 2023 
outpatient net revenues.

Risiko Kredit
Risiko kredit merupakan risiko kegagalan rekanan 
dalam memenuhi kewajiban kontraktualnya yang 
mengakibatkan kerugian bagi Perseroan.
Risiko kredit yang dihadapi Perseroan terutama berasal 
dari kredit yang diberikan kepada pelanggan. Untuk 
mengurangi risiko ini, Perseroan melakukan hubungan 
usaha hanya dengan pihak ketiga yang dapat dipercaya, 
memiliki kondisi keuangan yang kuat serta terbukti 
mempunyai reputasi dan sejarah kredit yang baik. 
Perseroan memiliki kebijakan untuk semua pelanggan 
yang ingin bertransaksi secara kredit harus tunduk pada 
prosedur verifikasi kredit. Perseroan memiliki kebijakan 
yang membatasi jumlah kredit untuk pelanggan tertentu 
dan mengharuskan setoran jaminan untuk pelanggan 
tertentu. Sebagai tambahan, saldo piutang dipantau 
secara terus menerus untuk mengurangi kemungkinan 
piutang yang tidak tertagih.
Ketika pelanggan tidak mampu melakukan pembayaran 
dalam jangka waktu yang telah diberikan, Perseroan 
akan menghubungi pelanggan untuk menindaklanjuti 
piutang yang telah lewat jatuh tempo. Jika pelanggan 
tidak melunasi piutang yang telah jatuh tempo dalam 
jangka waktu yang telah ditentukan, Perseroan akan 
menindaklanjuti melalui jalur hukum. Berdasarkan 
pada penilaian Perseroan, penyisihan khusus mungkin 
dibentuk jika piutang dianggap tidak tertagih. Untuk 
meringankan risiko kredit, Perseroan akan menghentikan 
pemberian jasa atau produk kepada pelanggan jika 
terjadi keterlambatan dan/atau gagal bayar dan akan 
menggunakan setoran jaminan sebagai pembayaran 
atas gagal bayar tersebut.
Perseroan juga menghadapi risiko kredit yang berasal 
dari penempatan dana di bank dalam bentuk rekening 
lancar maupun deposito berjangka serta reksadana dan 
obligasi korporasi. Untuk mengatasi risiko ini, Perseroan 
memiliki kebijakan untuk menempatkan dananya hanya 
di bank-bank dan pengelola dana unit reksadana yang 
mempunyai reputasi yang baik.
Risiko Nilai Tukar Mata Uang Asing
Risiko nilai tukar mata uang asing merupakan risiko 
fluktuasi nilai wajar dari arus kas masa depan yang 
berasal dari instrumen keuangan akibat perubahan nilai 
tukar mata uang asing.
Perseroan mengelola risiko nilai tukar mata uang asing 
dengan melakukan pengawasan terhadap fluktuasi nilai 
tukar mata uang asing secara terus menerus sehingga 
dapat melakukan tindakan yang tepat untuk mengurangi 
risiko nilai tukar mata uang asing.
Credit Risk
Credit risk is the risk that a party to a financial instrument 
will fail to discharge its obligation and will result in a 
financial loss to the Company.
The Company is exposed to credit risk arising from the 
credit granted to its customers. To mitigate this risk, 
it has policies in place to ensure that the Company 
only makes business transactions with creditworthy 
customers which have good financial condition and 
proven track record or good credit history. It is the 
Company’s policy that all customers who wish to trade 
on credit are subject to credit verification procedures. 
The Company has policies that limit the amount of credit 
exposure to any particular customer, and requiring 
certain customers to provide guarantee deposits. In 
addition, receivable balances are monitored on an 
ongoing basis to reduce the exposure to bad debts.
When a customer fails to make payment within the 
credit term granted, the Company contacts the 
customer to act on the overdue receivable. If the 
customer does not settle the overdue receivable within a 
reasonable time, the Company proceeds to commence 
legal proceedings. Depending on the Company’s 
assessment, specific provisions may be made if the 
debt is deemed uncollectible. To mitigate credit risk, the 
Company ceases the supply of all services and products 
to the customer in the event of late payment and/or 
default and applies the customer’s guarantee deposit as 
payment for such customer’s account in default.
The Company is also exposed to credit risk arising from 
the funds placed in banks under current accounts and 
time deposits also placement in mutual funds unit and 
corporate bond. To mitigate this risk, the Company 
has a policy to place its funds only in banks and asset 
management of mutual funds that have good reputation.
Foreign Currency Exchange Rate Risk
Foreign currency exchange rate risk is the risk that the 
fair value or future cash flows of a financial instrument 
will fluctuate because of changes in foreign exchange 
rates.
The Company manages the risk of foreign currency 
exchange rate by monitoring the fluctuations in foreign 
exchange rate continuously so as to perform approriate 
actions to reduce the risk of foreign currency exchange 
rate.

Liquidity Risk
Liquidity risk is the risk that the Company will encounter 
difficulty in obtaining funds to meet commitments 
associated with financial liabilities.
The Company manages liquidity risk by maintaining 
sufficient cash and cash equivalents to enable the 
Company to fulfill its commitment to its normal 
operations. In addition, the Company also monitors 
the projected and actual cash flows and continuous 
supervision of maturity of financial assets and liabilities.
