Economic Overview
Pertumbuhan ekonomi global pada tahun 2024 diperkirakan 
mencapai 3,2%, sedikit lebih rendah dari proyeksi sebelumnya, 
akibat ketidakpastian geopolitik, kebijakan moneter ketat di 
negara maju, serta perlambatan ekonomi di Tiongkok. Selain 
itu, fluktuasi harga komoditas dan pelemahan investasi serta 
perdagangan global semakin menekan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
dunia. 
Sementara itu, ekonomi Indonesia tetap stabil di tengah 
dinamika global yang penuh dengan tantangan. Badan Pusat 
Statistik melaporkan bahwa pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia 
tahun 2024 mencapai 5,03%, sedikit lebih rendah dibandingkan 
pertumbuhan tahun 2023 yang sebesar 5,05%. Meskipun 
demikian, aktivitas ekonomi domestik tetap terjaga, di mana 
konsumsi rumah tangga meningkat sebesar 4,98% (yoy), 
didukung oleh aktivitas ekonomi dan mobilitas masyarakat yang 
tinggi selama periode Hari Besar Keagamaan Nasional (HBKN) 
seperti Natal dan Tahun Baru (Nataru). Konsumsi pemerintah 
tumbuh sebesar 4,17% (yoy) seiring dengan penyelesaian 
belanja akhir tahun, sedangkan konsumsi lembaga non-profit 
yang melayani rumah tangga (LNPRT) mencatat pertumbuhan 
6,06% (yoy), yang selaras dengan peningkatan aktivitas selama 
periode Pilkada 2024.
Kepercayaan masyarakat terhadap kondisi ekonomi semakin 
menguat, sebagaimana tercermin dalam Indeks Keyakinan 
Konsumen (IKK) yang mencapai 127,7 pada Desember 2024, 
meningkat dibandingkan bulan sebelumnya yang sebesar 
125,9. Hal ini menunjukkan optimisme masyarakat terhadap 
prospek ekonomi, terutama dengan adanya perbaikan pada 
sektor ketenagakerjaan dan ekspektasi penghasilan yang lebih 
baik.
Namun demikian, beberapa tantangan masih membayangi 
perekonomian Indonesia. Perlambatan permintaan global 
berpotensi menekan sektor ekspor, meskipun pada triwulan 
IV-2024 ekspor barang dan jasa masih mampu tumbuh 7,63% 
(yoy). Selain itu, ketidakpastian ekonomi global dan potensi 
kebijakan proteksionisme di beberapa negara mitra dagang 
utama tetap menjadi faktor yang harus diwaspadai dalam 
menjaga surplus neraca perdagangan nasional.

Global economic growth in 2024 was projected to reach 3.2%, 
slightly lower than previous estimates due to geopolitical 
uncertainties, tight monetary policies in advanced economies, 
and economic slowdown in China. Additionally, fluctuations in 
commodity prices, as well as weakening investment and global 
trade, have further pressured global economic growth.
Meanwhile, 
Indonesia’s 
economy 
remains 
stable 
amid 
global challenges. According to Statistics Indonesia (BPS), 
Indonesia’s economic growth in 2024 stood at 5.03%, 
slightly lower than the 5.05% growth in 2023. Despite this, 
domestic economic activity remained robust, with household 
consumption increased by 4.98% (yoy), supported by strong 
economic activity and high mobility during National Religious 
Holidays (HBKN), such as Christmas and New Year (Nataru). 
Government spending grew by 4.17% (yoy), driven by year-end 
budget disbursements, while non-profit institution household 
consumption (LNPRT) recorded growth of 6.06% (yoy), in 
line with increased activities during the 2024 Regional Head 
Elections (Pilkada).
Public confidence in economic conditions continues to 
strengthen, as reflected in the Consumer Confidence Index 
(CCI), which reached 127.7 in December 2024, increased from 
125.9 in the previous month. This indicates optimism regarding 
economic prospects, particularly due to improvements in 
employment conditions and higher income expectations.
However, several challenges remain for Indonesia’s economy. 
The global demand slowdown may pressure the export sector, 
although exports of goods and services grew by 7.63% (yoy) 
in Q4-2024. Additionally, global economic uncertainty and 
potential for protectionist policies from several key trading 
partner countries remain factors that must be carefully 
monitored to maintain Indonesia’s trade balance surplus.

Tinjauan Industri
Industrial Overview
The Fast-Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG) industry in 
Indonesia continued to show significant growth throughout 
2024, reflecting consumer adaptation to dynamic economic 
conditions and shifting shopping behaviors. According to the 
FMCG Monitor FY 2024 by Kantar, the total FMCG market value 
in Indonesia grew by 7% compared to the previous year. This 
growth was mainly driven by increased spending per shopping 
visit and a recovery in volume since the beginning of the year.
A number of FMCG categories experienced varied growth rates. 
The beauty sector remained the fastest-growing segment, 
recording double-digit growth, indicating a rising consumer 
awareness of self-care. In addition, the food and dairy 
categories, particularly those related to holiday celebratory 
occasions, contributed to the overall FMCG industry expansion. 
Meanwhile, personal care and household care experienced 
more stable growth, with pricing adjustments playing a key role 
in maintaining consumer purchasing power.
Supermarkets remained the primary distribution channel, with 
Hypermarket/Supermarket (HSM) penetration increasing, 
attracting more new shoppers. However, e-commerce did not 
merely compete with but also complemented traditional retail, 
accelerating the shift toward omnichannel shopping, where 
consumers combine in-store and online purchases.

Amendments to Accounting Policies
Sejak 1 Januari 2024, Perseroan dan Entitas Anak menerapkan 
Pernyataan Standar Akuntansi Keuangan (PSAK) baru dan 
revisi yang mulai efektif pada tanggal tersebut. Perubahan 
kebijakan akuntansi telah dilakukan sesuai dengan ketentuan 
transisi dalam masing-masing standar. Adapun PSAK yang 
diterapkan sebagai berikut:
1.	
Amendemen PSAK 116, “Sewa”: Liabilitas Sewa pada 
Transaksi Jual dan Sewa-balik
	
Amendemen ini menetapkan persyaratan yang digunakan 
penjual-penyewa dalam mengukur kewajiban sewa yang 
timbul dalam transaksi jual beli dan sewa-balik, untuk 
memastikan penjual-penyewa tidak mengakui jumlah 
setiap keuntungan atau kerugian yang terkait dengan hak 
guna yang dipertahankan.
2.	 Amendemen PSAK 201, “Penyajian Laporan Keuangan”: 
Liabilitas Jangka Panjang dengan Kovenan
	
Amendemen ini mengklarifikasi bahwa hanya kovenan 
yang harus dipatuhi entitas pada atau sebelum tanggal 
pelaporan yang akan memengaruhi klasifikasi liabilitas 
sebagai lancar atau tidak lancar.
	
Entitas menerapkan amendemen PSAK 201 (Oktober 2020) 
tentang klasifikasi liabilitas sebagai jangka pendek atau 
jangka panjang pada periode pelaporan tahunan yang dimulai 
pada atau setelah 1 Januari 2024 secara retrospektif sesuai 
dengan PSAK 208. Jika entitas menerapkan amendemen 
PSAK 201 (Oktober 2020) pada periode yang lebih awal 
setelah terbitnya amendemen PSAK 201 (Desember 2022) 
tentang liabilitas jangka panjang dengan kovenan, maka 
entitas juga menerapkan amendemen PSAK 201 (Desember 
2022) pada periode tersebut.
3.	 Amendemen PSAK 201, “Penyajian Laporan Keuangan”: 
Klasifikasi Liabilitas sebagai Jangka Pendek atau Jangka 
Panjang
	
Amendemen PSAK 201, “Penyajian Laporan Keuangan” 
mengklarifikasi bahwa kewajiban diklasifikasikan sebagai 
lancar atau tidak lancar, berdasarkan pada hak yang ada 
pada akhir periode pelaporan. Klasifikasi tidak terpengaruh 
oleh ekspektasi entitas atau peristiwa setelah tanggal 
pelaporan (misalnya penerimaan waiver atau pelanggaran 
perjanjian). Amendemen tersebut juga mengklarifikasi apa 
yang dimaksud PSAK 201 perihal “penyelesaian” liabilitas.
	
Amendemen tersebut dapat memengaruhi klasifikasi 
liabilitas, terutama untuk entitas yang sebelumnya 
mempertimbangkan intensi manajemen untuk menentukan 
klasifikasi dan untuk beberapa liabilitas yang dapat 
dikonversi menjadi ekuitas.
Since 1 January 2024, the Company and its Subsidiaries have 
implemented new and revised Indonesian Financial Accounting 
Standards (PSAK) that became effective on that date. The 
changes in accounting policies have been made in accordance 
with the transition provisions in each of the standards. The 
PSAKs that have been applied are as follows:
1.	
Amendment to PSAK 116, “Leases”: Lease Liabilities in Sale 
and Leaseback Transactions
	
This amendment specifies the requirements that a seller-
lessee uses in measuring the lease liability arising in a sale 
and leaseback transaction, to ensure the seller-lessee does 
not recognize any amount of the gain or loss that relates to 
the right of use it retains. 
2.	 Amendment to PSAK 201, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Non-Current Liabilities with Covenants
	
This amendment clarifies that only covenants with which 
entities must comply on or before the reporting date will 
affect a liability’s classification as current or non-current. 
	
Entities apply retrospectively amendments to PSAK 201 
(October 2020) regarding the classification of a liability 
as current or non-current for financial reporting starting 
on or after 1 January 2024 in accordance with PSAK 208. 
If entities apply the amendments to PSAK 201 (October 
2020) in a period that is earlier after the issuance of the 
amendment to PSAK 201 (December 2022) regarding non-
current liabilities with covenants, entities also apply the 
amendment to PSAK 201 (December 2022) in that period. 
3.	 Amendment to PSAK 201, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Classification of Liabilities as Current or Non-
current
	
The narrow-scope amendments to PSAK 201, “Presentation 
of Financial Statements” clarify that liabilities are classified 
as either current or non-current, depending on the rights 
that exist at the end of the reporting period. Classification is 
unaffected by the expectations of the entity or events after 
the reporting date (e.g the receipt of a waiver or a breach 
of covenant). The amendments also clarify what PSAK 201 
means when it refers to the ‘settlement’ of a liability. 
	
The amendments could affect the classification of liabilities, 
particularly for entities that previously considered 
management’s intentions to determine classification and 
for some liabilities that can be converted into equity.
