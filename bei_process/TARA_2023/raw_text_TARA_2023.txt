Perekonomian dunia terus menunjukkan perlambatan pada 
akhir tahun 2023, di tengah ketegangan pasar keuangan dan 
ketidakpastian global. Meskipun perekonomian di Amerika Serikat 
(AS) dan India mampu didukung oleh konsumsi rumah tangga dan 
investasi, perekonomian Tiongkok mengalami perlambatan akibat 
pelemahan konsumsi rumah tangga dan investasi, terutama akibat 
penurunan kinerja sektor properti dan keterbatasan stimulus 
fiskal. Kondisi tersebut menjadikan pertumbuhan perekonomian 
global di tahun 2023 mencapai 3,0% (yoy), lebih rendah dari 
2022 yang berada pada level 3,5% (yoy), diikuti oleh penurunan 
inflasi dunia pada level 6,8% (yoy).
Di tengah perlambatan ekonomi global dan penurunan harga 
komoditas ekspor unggulan, ekonomi Indonesia tahun 2023 
tumbuh 5,05% (yoy), didukung oleh konsumsi dan investasi, 
seiring dengan percepatan belanja pemerintah dan percepatan 
penyelesaian beberapa Proyek Strategis Nasional (PSN). Selain itu, 
inflasi juga mengalami penurunan dan tetap terjaga dalam kisaran 
sasaran. Tercatat inflasi indeks harga konsumen (IHK) Desember 
2023 mencapai 2,61% (yoy).
Sejalan dengan pemulihan ekonomi nasional, perkembangan 
harga properti residensial di pasar primer menunjukkan kinerja 
yang memuaskan. Indeks harga properti residensial (IHPR) triwulan 
IV 2023 tercatat meningkat sebesar 1,74% (yoy), didukung oleh 
kenaikan harga properti tipe kecil sebesar 2,15% (yoy) dan harga 
rumah tipe besar yang tumbuh 1,58%. Sementara harga rumah 
tipe menengah mengalami perlambatan pada level 2,44% (yoy).
Di lain sisi, indeks harga properti komersial (IHPK) 2023 berada 
pada level 0,65% (yoy). Demikian pula indeks harga kategori sewa 
dan jual pada triwulan IV 2023 tumbuh positif sebesar 2,03% 
(yoy) dan 0,64% (yoy). Peningkatan tersebut dipengaruhi oleh 
menurunnya indeks harga pada segmen hotel di Banten, disertai 
peningkatan indeks harga segmen perkantoran jual di Surabaya 
dan segmen lahan industri di Semarang.
Sumber:
•	 Laporan Kebijakan Moneter – Triwulan IV 2024, Bank Indonesia;
•	 Survei Harga Properti Residensial di Pasar Primer - Triwulan IV 2023, Bank Indonesia; 
dan
•	 Perkembangan Properti Komersial – Triwulan IV 2023, Bank Indonesia.
The world economy continues its slowdown well into the 
end of 2023, amidst the financial market tensions and global 
uncertainties. While there was a strong support from household 
consumption and investment on the United States (US) and India 
economy, China’s economy experienced a slowdown from the 
weakening household consumption and investment, particularly 
from the declining performance of the property sector and limited 
fiscal stimulus. Such conditions lead to a meager global economic 
growth in 2023 of 3.0% (yoy), lower than 3.5% (yoy) recorded 
in 2022, followed by a decline in world inflation to 6.8% (yoy).
Amidst the global economic slowdown and declining prices of 
leading export commodities, Indonesian economy in 2023 grew 
5.05% (yoy), backed by consumption and investment, coupled 
with accelerated government spending and completion of several 
of the National Strategic Projects (PSN). In addition, inflation also 
recorded a decrease and remain well within its target range. The 
Consumer Price Index (CPI) recorded a 2.61% (yoy) inflation in 
December 2023.
In line with the recovering national economic, development of 
residential property price in primary market showed satisfactory 
performance. The Residential Property Price Index (IHPR) in quarter 
IV of 2023 recorded 1.74% (yoy) increase, contributed by 2.15% 
(yoy) increase in small-type property prices and 1.58% growth 
in large-type house prices. While the middle-class house prices 
experienced 2.44% (yoy) slowdown.
On the other hand, the 2023 Commercial Property Price Index 
(CPPI) reached 0.65% (yoy). Similarly, the price index for rental 
and sales categories in quarter IV of 2023 grew positively by 
2.03% (yoy) and 0.64% (yoy). Such increase was affected by the 
declining price index for hotel segment in Banten, coupled with an 
increase in price index for office-for-sale segment in Surabaya and 
the industrial land segment in Semarang.
Sources:
•	 Monetary Policy Report – Quarter IV 2024, Bank Indonesia;
•	 Residential Property Price Survey in Primary Market - Quarter IV 2023, Bank Indonesia; 
and
•	 Commercial Property Development – Quarter IV 2023, Bank Indonesia.

Pada tahun 2023, Perseroan belum membukukan penjualan 
segmen lain-lain dan tercatat masih mengalami rugi tahun berjalan

In 2023, the Company has not recorded sales from other segment 
and is still recorded loss for the year of Rp4.00 billion, a 0.48% 
increase or Rp19 million from Rp3.99 billion in 2022. The decrease 
occurred in assets of 8.65 % or Rp1.27 billion, from Rp14.67 
billion in 2022 to Rp13.41 billion in 2023. Meanwhile, liabilities 
increased by 72.37% or Rp961 million, from Rp1.33 billion to 
Rp2.29 billion in 2023.
Strategi Pemasaran
Pada tahun 2023, Perseroan menerapkan sejumlah strategi 
pemasaran dalam rangka meningkatkan kinerja keuangan. 
Strategi tersebut antara lain:
1.	 Mengidentifikasi kondisi pasar dan kebutuhan pelanggan 
terkait tipe dan harga yang dapat diterima di pasar;
2.	 Menyasar pelanggan kelas menengah ke atas sebagai target 
pasar;
3.	 Membentuk tim pemasaran yang andal dalam meningkatkan 
pemasaran produk Perseroan;
4.	 Menyelenggarakan virtual properti; dan
5.	 Menjalin kerja sama dengan agen penjualan eksternal yang 
memiliki reputasi di bidang penjualan properti.
Pangsa Pasar
Dengan mempertimbangkan keterbatasan sumber daya manusia 
serta permintaan masyarakat, jangkauan pemasaran Perseroan 
pada tahun 2023 terbatas pada tingkat regional, yakni pada 
Kawasan Jabodetabek dan Jawa Tengah.
Marketing Strategy
In 2023, the Company implemented several marketing strategies 
to improve the financial performance. The strategies are as 
follows:
1.	 Identify market conditions and customer needs related to the 
type and price acceptable in the market; 
2.	 Targeted the middle-upper class customers as the target 
market; 
3.	 Establish a reliable marketing team to improve the marketing 
of Company’s products; 
4.	 Organizing virtual property; and
5.	 Built cooperation with reputable external property marketing 
agent.
Market Share
By considering the limited human resources as well as community 
demand, the Company’s marketing reach in 2023 was limited to 
regional level, namely Jabodetabek and Central Java areas.

Business Prospects 
As reflected by the 5.05% (yoy) improvement of Indonesia’s 
economic growth in 2023, Bank Indonesia predicts Indonesia’s 
growth in 2024 will be in the range of 4.7-5.5% (yoy). Close 
synergy and the mix of short-term economic management policies 
and real sector transformation policies is predicted to be able to 
strengthen future economic growth. Also, domestic demand that 
remains solid amidst the world economic slowdown and high 
consumer confidence is expected to be able to increase public 
consumption.
On the other hand, the property sector in 2024 is predicted to 
remain at a positive level. Easy home ownership credit financing 
scheme and reopening of the previously closed rental apartments 
are expected to improve the residential property price index. While 
the commercial property price index is believed to rely on the 
office segment and industrial land, following the increase in office 
supply and intensifying industries post Covid-19.
In supporting the property sector growth in 2024, the government 
has implemented a policy on the establishment of incentive policy 
of government-borne value added tax (VAT DP) for every purchase 
of new home from November 2023 to December 2024. This 
policy is expected to sustain the growth of the property sector and 
develop in the coming future.
