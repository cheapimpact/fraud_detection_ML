TINJAUAN EKONOMI 
Menjelang akhir tahun 2024, ekonomi global masih diwarnai 
ketidakpastian. Inflasi umumnya menurun, meski beberapa negara 
mencatat kenaikan. Kebijakan moneter di negara maju mulai 
melonggar, sementara negara berkembang menunjukkan respons 
beragam sesuai tantangan domestik yang tengah dihadapi. Harga 
komoditas global mayoritas melemah, kecuali minyak sawit yang naik 
karena tingginya permintaan dan kekhawatiran terhadap kecukupan 
pasokan. Pertumbuhan ekonomi global stagnan di 3,2% untuk 2024 
dan 2025. ASEAN menunjukkan ketahanan lebih baik, sementara 
Eropa dan AS menghadapi perlambatan akibat risiko politik dan 
proteksionisme. Tiongkok menghadapi tekanan dari lemahnya 
permintaan domestik dan krisis properti, sementara India tetap tumbuh 
meski melambat karena inflasi pangan. Perekonomian global masih 
menghadapi 
tantangan 
yang 
mencakup 
tensi 
geopolitik, 
proteksionisme, perubahan iklim, dan tekanan utang di negara 
berkembang. 
Kebijakan 
yang 
strategis 
diperlukan 
untuk 
mempertahankan stabilitas sekaligus memanfaatkan peluang untuk 
pertumbuhan. 
 
Di 
tengah 
perlambatan 
global, 
perekonomian 
Indonesia 
menunjukkan daya tahan. Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia 
tercatat 5,03% (ctc) hingga triwulan III, didukung oleh konsumsi 
domestik yang kuat, peningkatan investasi, serta kinerja ekspor 
yang membaik. Sektor utama seperti industri pengolahan, 
perdagangan, dan konstruksi. terus tumbuh, sementara pariwisata 
meningkat signifikan berkat tingginya kunjungan wisatawan dan 
penyelenggaraan berbagai acara berskala besar. Pemerintah 
berhasil menjaga stabilitas ekonomi melalui pengendalian inflasi, 
optimalisasi belanja APBN, dan dukungan terhadap daya beli 
masyarakat. Langkah seperti penurunan suku bunga acuan dan 
penguatan program perlindungan sosial menjadi kunci menjaga 
momentum 
pertumbuhan. 
Kebijakan 
hilirisasi, 
investasi 
infrastruktur, dan transformasi ekonomi terus diperkuat untuk 
mendorong daya saing dan keberlanjutan ekonomi. Dengan 
proyeksi pertumbuhan global yang stagnan di 2024-2025, 
perekonomian Indonesia diharapkan tetap tumbuh solid di atas 5%. 
Sinergi kebijakan fiskal, moneter, dan sektor keuangan akan terus 
diperkuat untuk menghadapi risiko global sekaligus menciptakan 
peluang bagi kesejahteraan masyarakat. 
 
Laju inflasi terkendali, namun Pemerintah terus mengantisipasi risiko 
gejolak harga ke depan. Menjelang akhir 2024, inflasi nasional 
terkendali dalam kisaran sasaran 2,5% ±1%, dengan tingkat inflasi 
pada November sebesar 1,6% yoy, turun dari 2,12% pada Agustus. 
Penurunan ini didorong oleh harga pangan yang melandai akibat 
melimpahnya pasokan hortikultura selama masa panen serta kebijakan 
stabilisasi harga. Inflasi komponen inti menguat mencapai 2,26%, 
didorong oleh kelompok sandang, perawatan jasmani, dan pendidikan, 
sementara inflasi komponen harga yang diatur pemerintah melemah 
menjadi 0,82% karena penurunan harga BBM nonsubsidi. Meski 
demikian, tantangan ke depan mencakup potensi kenaikan harga 
pangan akibat musim hujan dan kecukupan pasokan menjelang Hari 
Besar Keagamaan Nasional dan tahun baru. 
 
 
 
ECONOMIC REVIEW 
 
As we approach the end of 2024, the global economy is still marked by 
uncertainty. Inflation generally declines, although some countries 
have recorded increases. Monetary policies in developed countries 
have begun to loosen, while developing countries have shown mixed 
responses according to the domestic challenges they are facing. 
Global commodity prices have mostly weakened, except for palm oil 
which has risen due to high demand and concerns about adequate 
supply. Global economic growth is stagnant at 3.2% for 2024 and 
2025. ASEAN has shown better resilience, while Europe and the US 
face a slowdown due to political risks and protectionism. China faces 
pressure from weak domestic demand and the property crisis, while 
India continues to grow, albeit slowly, due to food inflation. The global 
economy still faces challenges that include geopolitical tensions, 
protectionism, climate change, and debt pressures in developing 
countries. Strategic policies are needed to maintain stability while 
taking advantage of opportunities for growth. 
 
 
 
Amid the global slowdown, the Indonesian economy has shown 
resilience. Indonesia's economic growth was recorded at 5.03% (ctc) 
until the third quarter, supported by strong domestic consumption, 
increased investment, and improved export performance. Key sectors 
such as the manufacturing industry, trade, and construction continued 
to grow, while tourism increased significantly thanks to high tourist 
visits and the holding of various large-scale events. The government 
has succeeded in maintaining economic stability by controlling 
inflation, optimizing APBN spending, and supporting people's 
purchasing power. Steps such as lowering the benchmark interest rate 
and strengthening social protection programs are key to maintaining 
growth momentum. Downstream policies, infrastructure investment, 
and economic transformation continue to be strengthened to 
encourage economic competitiveness and sustainability. With global 
growth projections stagnant in 2024-2025, the Indonesian economy is 
expected to continue to grow solidly above 5%. The synergy of fiscal, 
monetary, and financial sector policies will continue to be 
strengthened to face global risks while creating opportunities for 
people's welfare. 
 
 
 
The inflation rate is under control, but the Government continues to 
anticipate the risk of future price volatility. Towards the end of 2024, 
national inflation is under control within the target range of 2.5% 
±1%, with the inflation rate in November at 1.6% yoy, down from 
2.12% in August. This decline was driven by declining food prices due 
to the abundant supply of horticulture during the harvest season and 
price stabilization policies. Core component inflation strengthened to 
2.26%, driven by the clothing, physical care, and education groups, 
while inflation in the government-regulated price component 
weakened to 0.82% due to the decline in non-subsidized fuel prices. 
However, future challenges include the potential for rising food prices 
due to the rainy season and adequate supply ahead of National 
Religious Holidays and the New Year.
