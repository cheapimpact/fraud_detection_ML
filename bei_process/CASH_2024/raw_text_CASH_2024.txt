Tinjauan Makroekonomi 
Macroeconomic Overview
Tahun 2024 menjadi periode yang penuh tantangan 
bagi ekonomi global, dipengaruhi oleh meningkatnya 
ketegangan 
geopolitik, 
kebijakan 
moneter 
yang 
lebih ketat, serta volatilitas harga komoditas utama 
yang berdampak pada rantai pasok dunia. Laporan 
Dana Moneter Internasional (IMF) memperkirakan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global sebesar 3,3%, lebih rendah 
dibandingkan rata-rata historis 3,7% pada periode 2000-
2019. Amerika Serikat tetap menunjukkan daya tahan 
ekonomi yang lebih kuat dibandingkan kawasan lain, 
sementara Eropa menghadapi ketidakpastian signifikan, 
dan perlambatan ekonomi di Tiongkok turut memberikan 
tekanan terhadap stabilitas ekonomi global.
Di 
tengah 
dinamika 
global 
yang 
bergejolak, 
perekonomian Indonesia tetap tumbuh stabil dengan 
pertumbuhan 
tahunan 
(C-to-C) 
sebesar 
5,03%, 
berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS). Sektor 
jasa menjadi penggerak utama dengan pertumbuhan 
9,80%, sedangkan konsumsi lembaga non-profit (PK-
LNPRT) mengalami peningkatan sebesar 12,48%, 
didorong oleh program kesejahteraan pemerintah yang 
semakin luas. Sementara itu, surplus perdagangan 
mengalami penurunan menjadi 31,04 miliar Dolar AS, 
angka terendah dalam empat tahun terakhir. Penurunan 
ini terutama disebabkan oleh meningkatnya impor 
bahan baku dan barang modal sebesar 5,31%, yang 
mengindikasikan tingginya permintaan industri domestik, 
serta melemahnya ekspor di sektor pertambangan akibat 
fluktuasi harga komoditas global.
Tingkat inflasi pada tahun 2024 tetap terkendali di angka 
1,57%, meskipun terdapat variasi signifikan antar wilayah. 
Faktor utama yang mendorong inflasi adalah kenaikan 
harga barang kebutuhan pokok, terutama di sektor makanan 
dan minuman, serta lonjakan biaya jasa seperti perawatan 
pribadi yang meningkat hingga 7,02%. Perbedaan inflasi 
antar daerah memerlukan kebijakan ekonomi yang lebih 
fleksibel, termasuk penyesuaian subsidi energi di wilayah 
dengan inflasi tinggi serta penguatan program bantuan 
sosial untuk menjaga daya beli masyarakat yang terdampak 
lebih besar. Provinsi Papua Pegunungan mencatat inflasi 
tertinggi sebesar 5,36%, sedangkan Sulawesi Utara 
mengalami inflasi terendah hanya sebesar 0,44%.
2024 is a challenging period for the global economy, 
influenced by rising geopolitical tensions, tighter 
monetary policies, and volatility in key commodity prices, 
which impact global supply chains. The International 
Monetary Fund (IMF) forecasts global economic growth 
at 3.3%, lower than the historical average of 3.7% from 
2000 to 2019. The United States continues to show 
stronger economic resilience compared to other regions, 
while Europe faces significant uncertainty, and the 
economic slowdown in China adds pressure to global 
economic stability.
Amidst the volatile global dynamics, Indonesia’s economy 
has remained stable, with an annual growth rate (C-to-C) 
of 5.03%, according to data from the Central Statistics 
Agency (BPS). The services sector was the main driver, 
growing by 9.80%, while consumption by non-profit 
institutions (PK-LNPRT) increased by 12.48%, driven by 
expanding government welfare programs. Meanwhile, 
the trade surplus decreased to 31.04 billion US Dollars, 
the lowest in the past four years. This decline was mainly 
due to a 5.31% rise in imports of raw materials and 
capital goods, reflecting strong demand in the domestic 
industry, and a weakening of mining exports due to 
global commodity price fluctuations.
Inflation in 2024 remained controlled at 1.57%, despite 
significant variations across regions. The main factors 
driving inflation were higher prices for essential goods, 
particularly in the food and beverage sectors, and a sharp 
increase in service costs, such as personal care, which 
rose by 7.02%. The inflation disparity between regions 
requires more flexible economic policies, including 
adjustments to energy subsidies in high-inflation areas 
and strengthening social assistance programs to support 
purchasing power for the most affected communities. 
Papua Pegunungan recorded the highest inflation at 
5.36%, while North Sulawesi had the lowest inflation at 
just 0.44%.

Dalam menghadapi tekanan eksternal, kebijakan moneter 
yang diterapkan oleh Bank Indonesia memainkan peran 
kunci dalam menjaga stabilitas ekonomi nasional. 
Sepanjang tahun, nilai tukar Rupiah terhadap Dolar 
AS mengalami fluktuasi yang cukup signifikan, dengan 
nilai tertinggi pada 2 Januari 2024 sebesar Rp15.516,19 
per Dolar AS dan nilai terendah pada 27 Juni 2024 
sebesar Rp16.517,17 per Dolar AS. Rata-rata nilai 
tukar sepanjang tahun berada di angka Rp16.092,06 
untuk kurs jual dan Rp15.931,94 untuk kurs beli, lebih 
lemah dibandingkan asumsi dalam RAPBN 2024 yang 
memperkirakan rentang Rp14.700–Rp15.300 per Dolar 
AS. Fluktuasi ini dipengaruhi oleh kebijakan suku bunga 
The Fed serta intervensi Bank Indonesia dalam menjaga 
stabilitas pasar valuta asing.
Pelemahan 
Rupiah 
mendorong 
Bank 
Indonesia 
untuk melakukan intervensi guna menjaga stabilitas 
makroekonomi, termasuk kebijakan suku bunga dan 
pengelolaan cadangan devisa. Depresiasi ini juga 
berdampak pada inflasi, terutama untuk barang-
barang impor, serta menambah tantangan bagi sektor 
industri yang bergantung pada bahan baku dari luar 
negeri. Namun, di sisi lain, pelemahan Rupiah dapat 
meningkatkan daya saing ekspor dan membantu 
mengurangi defisit transaksi berjalan apabila sektor 
manufaktur mampu memanfaatkan momentum tersebut. 
Bank Indonesia juga aktif melakukan operasi moneter 
guna meredam volatilitas nilai tukar, termasuk intervensi 
langsung di pasar valas dan pengelolaan cadangan 
devisa yang lebih ketat.
Sejalan dengan upaya menjaga stabilitas nilai tukar, 
kebijakan suku bunga yang diterapkan oleh Bank 
Indonesia turut menjadi faktor kunci dalam pengelolaan 
ekonomi makro. Pada awal tahun 2024, Bank Indonesia 
mempertahankan kebijakan suku bunga ketat dengan 
BI 7-Day Reverse Repo Rate di level 6,00%. Namun, 
dalam Rapat Dewan Gubernur Januari 2025, Bank 
Indonesia mengejutkan pasar dengan menurunkan suku 
bunga acuan sebesar 25 basis poin menjadi 5,75% guna 
mendukung pertumbuhan ekonomi. Kebijakan ini diambil 
sebagai respons terhadap pelemahan nilai tukar Rupiah 
yang sempat mengalami volatilitas akibat ketidakpastian 
pasar global, dengan harapan dapat mendorong 
investasi dan konsumsi domestik agar tetap tumbuh di 
tengah tekanan eksternal.
Facing 
external 
pressures, 
the 
monetary 
policy 
implemented by Bank Indonesia plays a key role in 
maintaining national economic stability. Throughout the 
year, the exchange rate of the Rupiah against the US Dollar 
fluctuated significantly, with the highest value recorded 
on January 2nd, 2024, at IDR15,516.19 per US Dollar, 
and the lowest on June 27th, 2024, at IDR16,517.17 per 
US Dollar. The average exchange rate for the year was 
IDR16,092.06 for the selling rate and IDR15,931.94 for 
the buying rate, which was weaker than the assumption 
in the 2024 State Budget (RAPBN), which estimated a 
range of IDR14,700–IDR15,300 per US Dollar. These 
fluctuations were influenced by the Federal Reserve’s 
interest rate policy and Bank Indonesia’s interventions to 
maintain the stability of the foreign exchange market.
The weakening of the Rupiah prompted Bank Indonesia 
to intervene to maintain macroeconomic stability, 
including through interest rate policies and foreign 
exchange reserve management. This depreciation also 
affected inflation, particularly for imported goods, and 
added challenges for industries that rely on foreign raw 
materials. However, on the positive side, the weaker 
Rupiah can improve export competitiveness and help 
reduce the current account deficit if the manufacturing 
sector can take advantage of this trend. Bank Indonesia 
has also actively carried out monetary operations 
to dampen exchange rate volatility, including direct 
interventions in the foreign exchange market and tighter 
management of foreign exchange reserves.
Aligned with efforts to maintain exchange rate stability, the 
interest rate policy implemented by Bank Indonesia has 
also been a key factor in macroeconomic management. 
At the beginning of 2024, Bank Indonesia kept its tight 
policy with the BI 7-Day Reverse Repo Rate at 6.00%. 
However, in the January 2025 Governors’ Meeting, 
Bank Indonesia surprised the market by lowering the 
benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points to 5.75% to 
support economic growth. This policy decision was made 
in response to the volatility in the Rupiah’s exchange rate, 
which had been affected by global market uncertainties, 
to encourage investment and domestic consumption to 
continue growing despite external pressures.

Tinjauan Industri Pembayaran 
Payment Industry Overview
Industri pembayaran di Indonesia sepanjang tahun 2024 
mengalami perkembangan pesat yang didukung oleh 
berbagai faktor, termasuk pertumbuhan ekonomi digital, 
inovasi teknologi finansial, serta kebijakan strategis dari 
Bank Indonesia dan Otoritas Jasa Keuangan. Dalam 
laporan Perekonomian Indonesia 2024 yang dirilis oleh 
Bank Indonesia, disebutkan bahwa defisit transaksi 
berjalan juga terkendali dalam rentang 0,5% hingga 
1,3% dari Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB), sementara 
nilai tukar Rupiah tetap berada di kisaran Rp15.700 
hingga Rp16.000 per Dolar AS. Stabilitas ekonomi ini 
menciptakan ekosistem yang mendukung perkembangan 
industri penyedia jasa pembayaran.
Berdasarkan laporan Rapat Dewan Gubernur Bank 
Indonesia 2025 yang mengkaji kinerja tahun 2024, 
transaksi ekonomi dan keuangan digital mengalami 
pertumbuhan yang signifikan. Bank Indonesia mencatat 
bahwa transaksi penyedia jasa pembayaran mencapai 
43,5 miliar transaksi sepanjang tahun 2024, mengalami 
peningkatan 36,1% secara tahunan (year-on-year). 
Dari jumlah tersebut, transaksi melalui aplikasi mobile 
meningkat 
sebesar 
39,1%, 
sementara 
transaksi 
berbasis internet naik 4,4%. Selain itu, volume transaksi 
menggunakan Quick Response Code Indonesian 
Standard (QRIS) mengalami lonjakan sebesar 159.4%, 
dengan jumlah pengguna mencapai 55 juta dan 35.9 juta 
merchant yang telah terdaftar.
Berdasarkan laporan Statistik E-Commerce 2023 yang 
diterbitkan oleh Badan Pusat Statistik, perkembangan 
transaksi digital dalam sektor e-commerce juga 
menunjukkan tren yang positif. Nilai transaksi usaha 
berbasis e-commerce meningkat signifikan dengan 
pertumbuhan 
tahunan 
lebih 
dari 
18,5%, 
yang 
mencerminkan pergeseran pola konsumsi masyarakat 
ke arah digital. Laporan ini juga mengungkapkan bahwa 
lebih dari 80% usaha mikro, kecil, dan menengah (UMKM) 
di Indonesia telah mengadopsi metode penyedia jasa 
pembayaran, dengan 65% di antaranya memanfaatkan 
layanan dompet digital dan QRIS untuk transaksi harian 
mereka.
The payment industry in Indonesia experienced rapid 
growth throughout 2024, supported by various factors, 
including the growth of the digital economy, financial 
technology innovations, and strategic policies from Bank 
Indonesia and the Financial Services Authority (OJK). 
According to the 2024 Indonesian Economic Report 
released by Bank Indonesia, the current account deficit 
remained controlled, ranging from 0.5% to 1.3% of Gross 
Domestic Product (GDP), while the exchange rate of the 
Rupiah remained between IDR15,700 and IDR16,000 per 
US Dollar. This economic stability created an ecosystem 
that supports the growth of the digital payment industry.
According to the 2025 Bank Indonesia Governors’ 
Meeting report, which reviewed the performance of 2024, 
digital economic and financial transactions experienced 
significant growth. Bank Indonesia reported that digital 
payment transactions reached 43.5 billion in 2024, 
marking a 36.1% year-on-year increase. Of this total, 
transactions through mobile apps grew by 39.1%, while 
internet-based transactions rose by 4.4%. Additionally, 
the volume of transactions using Quick Response Code 
Indonesian Standard (QRIS) surged by 159.4%, with 55 
million users and 35.9 million registered merchants.
According to the 2023 E-Commerce Statistics report 
published by the Central Statistics Agency, digital 
transactions in the e-commerce sector have also shown a 
positive trend. The value of e-commerce-based business 
transactions has increased significantly, with an annual 
growth rate of over 18.5%, reflecting a shift in consumer 
behavior toward digital platforms. The report also reveals 
that more than 80% of micro, small, and medium-sized 
enterprises (MSMEs) in Indonesia have adopted digital 
payment methods, with 65% using e-wallet services and 
QRIS for their daily transactions.

Laporan AMS 2024 dari Asosiasi Fintech Indonesia 
(AFTECH) menegaskan bahwa pertumbuhan industri 
fintech tetap kuat meskipun menghadapi tantangan 
ekonomi global. Sebagian besar fintech di Indonesia 
masih dalam fase ekspansi, dengan 59,5% perusahaan 
berusia 6-10 tahun, sedangkan 26% berusia di bawah 
5 tahun. Industri penyedia jasa pembayaran semakin 
didukung oleh perkembangan regulasi yang mendukung 
inovasi, di mana 89,3% perusahaan fintech merasa 
regulasi pemerintah mendorong pertumbuhan industri. 
Regulasi seperti UU P2SK dan POJK No. 3 Tahun 2024 
memberikan kepastian hukum yang lebih jelas bagi 
industri ini.
Dari sisi keamanan transaksi, laporan AMS 2024 
menunjukkan bahwa ancaman terbesar dalam sistem 
penyedia jasa pembayaran berasal dari phishing 
(33,6%), malware (12,9%), dan hacking (6,8%). Untuk 
mengatasi tantangan ini, 93,9% perusahaan fintech 
telah menerapkan Disaster Recovery Plan, dan 97,7% 
fintech telah mengadopsi standar keamanan ISO 27001 
guna meningkatkan perlindungan konsumen. Selain 
itu, penggunaan kecerdasan buatan (AI) dalam industri 
pembayaran semakin meningkat, dengan 55% fintech 
telah mengadopsi AI, terutama untuk data analytics 
(54,2%), facial recognition (44,1%), dan fraud detection 
(35,6%).
Dalam siaran pers yang dirilis oleh Otoritas Jasa 
Keuangan terkait penguatan sektor jasa keuangan, 
disebutkan bahwa sektor perbankan dan layanan 
keuangan digital juga mengalami pertumbuhan yang 
signifikan. Kredit perbankan tumbuh sebesar 10,92% 
pada Oktober 2024, mencapai total Rp7.656,90 triliun. 
Pertumbuhan ini terutama didorong oleh peningkatan 
aktivitas konsumsi dan investasi masyarakat yang 
semakin mengandalkan layanan digital banking. Selain 
itu, Bank Indonesia melaporkan bahwa penggunaan 
sistem pembayaran instan BI-FAST mencapai 3,4 miliar 
transaksi dengan nilai mencapai Rp8,9 triliun, meningkat 
sebesar 62,4% dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya. Di sisi 
lain, penggunaan kartu kredit tumbuh 2.5% mencapai 
38.9 juta transaksi, sementara kartu ATM/Debit justru 
mengalami penurunan 16.4%, menunjukkan pergeseran 
preferensi konsumen ke penyedia jasa pembayaran.
The 2024 AMS report from the Indonesian Fintech 
Association (AFTECH) confirms that the fintech industry 
continues to grow strongly despite global economic 
challenges. Most fintech companies in Indonesia are 
still in the expansion phase, with 59.5% of companies 
being 6-10 years old, and 26% under 5 years old. The 
digital payment industry is supported by regulatory 
developments that encourage innovation, with 89.3% of 
fintech companies stating that government regulations 
promote industry growth. Regulations such as the P2SK 
Law and POJK No. 3 of 2024 provide clearer legal 
certainty for the industry.
From a transaction security perspective, the 2024 AMS 
report shows the biggest threats to digital payment 
systems are phishing (33.6%), malware (12.9%), and 
hacking (6.8%). To address these challenges, 93.9% 
of fintech companies have implemented a Disaster 
Recovery Plan, and 97.7% have adopted the ISO 27001 
security standard to improve consumer protection. 
Additionally, the use of AI in the payment industry is 
increasing, with 55% of fintech companies adopting 
AI, mainly for data analytics (54.2%), facial recognition 
(44.1%), and fraud detection (35.6%).
In a press release issued by the Financial Services 
Authority regarding the strengthening of the financial 
services sector, it was stated that the banking and 
digital financial services sectors have also experienced 
significant growth. Bank credit grew by 10.92% in 
October 2024, reaching IDR7,656.90 trillion. This growth 
was mainly driven by an increase in consumption and 
investment activities, with more people relying on 
digital banking services. Additionally, Bank Indonesia 
reported that the use of the BI-FAST instant payment 
system reached 3.4 billion transactions, with a total of 
IDR8.9 trillion, marking a 62.4% increase compared to 
the previous year. Meanwhile, credit card usage grew 
by 2.5%, reaching 38.9 million transactions, while ATM/
Debit card usage declined by 16.4%, reflecting a shift in 
consumer preference towards digital payments.

The government continues to invest in the expansion of

Although the digital payment industry is growing rapidly, 
security challenges remain a primary concern. According 
to data released by the Southeast Asia Freedom of

attacks in Indonesia nearly doubled compared to the

investment fraud, money laundering, illegal gambling,

Bank Indonesia have launched various education and 
training programs to improve digital financial literacy 
and expand the network of bank agents and branchless 
banking services (Laku Pandai) to reach communities 
not yet served by formal financial systems.

ecosystem is expected to mature further and contribute 
to more inclusive economic growth.

Produk
Produk yang disediakan Perseroan adalah berupa 
aplikasi yang memberikan fasilitas pembayaran yang 
menerima pembayaran dengan menggunakan kartu 
kredit, kartu debit bahkan pembayaran digital seperti

Reader.
Mekanisme dan Proses Penggunaan Jasa
Posisi Perseroan sebagai penyelenggara payment 
gateway dengan prosesnya adalah sebagai berikut:
1.	
Konsumen/ pelanggan (Consumer) memutuskan 
untuk membeli suatu produk di Merchant (bisa

akan

transaksi

Visa, Mastercard, CUP, AMEX & GP.
Products
The Company provides applications that offer payment 
services, accepting payments through credit cards, debit 
cards, and digital payment methods such as QR Code, 
Cashlez Link, OVO, and Go-Pay. To accept payments 
via credit and debit cards, merchants must have the 
company’s product, which is a card reader.
Service Usage Mechanism and Process
The Company operates as a payment gateway provider, 
and the process is as follows:
1.	
The consumer/customer decides to purchase a 
product from a merchant (either a physical store or 
an online store) and makes the payment using a 
credit or debit card.
2.	
The Company, acting as the Payment Gateway, 
forwards the transaction information to the bank’s 
payment processor (Acquirer Bank).
3.	
The payment processor then sends the transaction 
information to the card-issuing bank (Issuer) through 
a card network (Switch), such as Visa, Mastercard, 
CUP, AMEX, or GP.

Debt Paying Ability
Perseroan secara konsisten berkomitmen untuk menjaga

terhadap liabilitas jangka pendek. Pada tahun 2024,

Rasio Solvabilitas
Rasio 
solvabilitas 
digunakan 
untuk

The Company remains firmly committed to maintaining

liquidity and solvency ratio analysis as part of its prudent 
financial risk management approach.
Liquidity Ratio
The liquidity ratio reflects the Company’s ability to meet its 
short-term obligations, calculated by comparing current

equity.
In addition, the debt-to-asset ratio also rose to 0.48 
times, up from 0.33 times in the previous year. This 
increase implies a higher reliance on debt to finance the 
Company’s assets.

Capital Structure
Capital Structure Policy
The primary goal of the Company’s capital management is 
to maintain a healthy capital ratio that effectively supports 
business operations and maximizes shareholder value. 
Although the Company is not subject to specific capital 
requirements, it actively manages its capital structure, 
making adjustments in response to changes in economic 
conditions.
To monitor capital health, the Company uses gearing ratio 
analysis, calculated by dividing net debt by total capital. 
Net debt is derived from total borrowings (both short-
term and long-term), minus cash and cash equivalents 
and restricted time deposits. Capital is represented by 
total equity as reflected in the consolidated financial 
statements. This approach allows the Company to 
prudently adjust its capital structure in response to 
economic dynamics, supporting long-term growth and 
stability.

Performance Realization 2024 vs. 2023

of IDR30.42 billion in 2023, reflecting a 14.34% increase.

in system and digital infrastructure development were 
the main contributing factors to this result.

Kebijakan Dividen
Dividend Policy
Sesuai Undang-Undang No. 40 Tahun 2007 tentang 
Perseroan Terbatas, pembagian dividen kas dilakukan 
berdasarkan keputusan Rapat Umum Pemegang Saham 
Tahunan (RUPST) atau Rapat Umum Pemegang Saham 
Luar Biasa (RUPSLB).
Berdasarkan keputusan RUPST pada tanggal 31 Mei 
2024, Perseroan menyetujui untuk tidak melaksanakan 
pembagian dividen kepada para pemegang saham 
dikarenakan pada tahun buku 2023 Perseroan masih 
mengalami kerugian.
In accordance with Law No. 40 of 2007 regarding Limited 
Liability Companies, the distribution of cash dividends 
is decided based on the Annual General Meeting of 
Shareholders (AGMS) or the Extraordinary General 
Meeting of Shareholders (EGMS).
Based on the decision from the AGM on May 31st, 2024, 
the Company has agreed not to distribute dividends to 
shareholders due to the losses incurred during the 2023 
fiscal year.

The Company conducted its Initial Public Offering (IPO) 
on January 4th, 2024, and successfully raised funds of 
IDR108,750,000,000. After deducting offering-related 
expenses, the net proceeds received by the Company 
amounted to IDR101,860,000,000.
As of December 31st, 2024, the realization of the use of 
proceeds from the public offering was allocated as follows:
•	
Approximately 51.82% was used for the purchase of 
raw material inventory.
•	
Approximately 22.15% was used for payment of 
trade payables to suppliers.
•	
Approximately 13.97% was allocated for capital 
expenditures, including the purchase of production 
machinery and equipment.
The remaining 12.06% was still placed in bank accounts, 
which is planned to be used for distribution expansion 
and future business development.
All information regarding the realization of the use of 
proceeds from the public offering is fully disclosed in 
Note 33 to the Consolidated Financial Statements as of 
December 31st, 2024.
