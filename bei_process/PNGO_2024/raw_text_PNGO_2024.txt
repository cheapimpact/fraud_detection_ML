Tinjauan Industri 
Industry Review 
Industri kelapa sawit Indonesia pada tahun 2024 mengalami sejumlah 
tantangan signifikan, meskipun masih memiliki potensi untuk bertumbuh 
dengan langkah-langkah strategis yang tepat. Produksi minyak sawit 
mentah (CPO) Indonesia tercatat mengalami penurunan sebesar 3,8% 
dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya, dengan total produksi mencapai 48,16 
juta ton. Penurunan ini dipengaruhi oleh beberapa faktor, di antaranya 
adalah fenomena El Niño yang mengurangi curah hujan di beberapa 
wilayah perkebunan sawit. Dampak dari perubahan iklim ini mengakibatkan 
kualitas tanaman kelapa sawit menurun, sehingga mengurangi hasil 
panen. Selain itu, semakin banyaknya tanaman yang memasuki usia tua 
juga menjadi kendala, karena tanaman yang lebih tua menghasilkan lebih 
sedikit buah, yang pada akhirnya mempengaruhi total produksi CPO.
Meskipun produksi CPO mengalami penurunan, sektor hilir kelapa sawit, 
seperti oleokimia, menunjukkan kinerja yang positif. Ekspor produk 
oleokimia Indonesia mengalami kenaikan sebesar 3,4%, dengan total 
ekspor mencapai 4,79 juta ton. Hal ini menunjukkan bahwa permintaan 
global terhadap produk turunan kelapa sawit tetap stabil, yang 
memberikan kontribusi penting bagi perekonomian Indonesia. Selain 
itu, kebijakan pemerintah Indonesia dalam mendukung penggunaan 
biofuel melalui program biodiesel B40 menjadi salah satu faktor kunci 
yang dapat mendorong serapan CPO domestik. Program biodiesel ini 
diproyeksikan akan menyerap hingga 13,6 juta ton CPO pada tahun 2025, 
yang memberikan harapan bagi kelangsungan dan pertumbuhan sektor 
kelapa sawit Indonesia meskipun menghadapi tantangan eksternal.

The Indonesian palm oil industry in 2024 faced a number of significant 
challenges, although it still has the potential to grow with the right 
strategic measures. Indonesia’s crude palm oil (CPO) production recorded 
a decline of 3.8% compared to the previous year, with total production 
reaching 48.16 million tons. This decline was influenced by several 
factors, including the El Niño phenomenon, which reduced rainfall in 
several palm oil plantation areas. The impact of climate change resulted 
in a decline in the quality of palm oil crops, thereby reducing crop yields. 
In addition, the increasing number of older plants also posed a challenge, 
as older plants produce fewer fruits, which ultimately affects total CPO 
production.
Despite the decline in CPO production, the downstream palm oil sector, 
such as oleochemicals, showed positive performance. Exports of 
Indonesian oleochemical products increased by 3.4%, with total exports 
reaching 4.79 million tons. This indicates that global demand for palm 
oil derivatives remains stable, contributing significantly to Indonesia’s 
economy. Additionally, the Indonesian government’s policies supporting 
the use of biofuels through the B40 biodiesel program are a key factor 
driving domestic CPO absorption. This biodiesel program is projected to 
absorb up to 13.6 million tons of CPO by 2025, offering hope for the 
sustainability and growth of Indonesia’s palm oil sector despite external 
challenges.

To address the decline in production, the Indonesian government, 
together with industry players, is striving to increase productivity 
through several strategic measures. One of these is the replanting of oil 
palm trees that have reached maturity, as well as the utilization of more 
efficient agricultural technologies. Additionally, projections for 2025 
indicate significant recovery potential, with estimated CPO production 
reaching 53.6 million tons. With the support of sustainable policies and 
improvements in upstream sector management, Indonesia’s palm oil 
sector has the potential to rebound and strengthen its position in the 
global market.
Meanwhile, Indonesia’s rubber industry faces more challenging 
conditions in 2024. Indonesia’s natural rubber production is estimated to 
decline to 2.15 million tons, compared to 2.24 million tons in the previous 
year. This decline is largely due to reduced demand from the global 
automotive industry, which is the main consumer of natural rubber, 
especially for vehicle tires. Intense competition with major rubber-
producing countries such as Thailand and Malaysia has also impacted 
Indonesia’s competitiveness in the international market. Rubber exports 
have seen a sharp decline, with export value for the January–September 
2024 period reaching only US$ 0.45 billion, significantly lower than US$ 
2.95 billion during the same period the previous year.
To address this decline, the Indonesian government has intensified its 
rubber product downstreaming program. One strategic step taken is to 
encourage the use of rubber asphalt in national infrastructure projects. 
The use of rubber asphalt not only adds value to rubber products but 
also opens up a broader domestic market. Additionally, the development 
of other downstream products, such as processed rubber goods for 
medical use, the automotive industry, and consumer goods, is expected 
to strengthen Indonesia’s rubber export competitiveness. These efforts 
toward product diversification and downstream processing hold great 
promise for the future of Indonesia’s rubber industry, aiming for greater 
sustainability and competitiveness in the global market.

Prospek Usaha 
Business Prospects 
Prospek usaha industri sawit dan karet pada tahun 2025 diperkirakan 
akan mengalami tantangan dan peluang yang signifikan. Di tengah 
ketidakpastian ekonomi global, permintaan terhadap produk-produk 
berbasis kelapa sawit tetap stabil, terutama untuk biodiesel dan produk 
makanan. Indonesia, sebagai salah satu produsen utama, diperkirakan 
akan terus mengandalkan ekspor ke pasar-pasar utama seperti Eropa, 
India, dan China. Namun, fluktuasi harga minyak sawit mentah (CPO) dan 
tekanan dari kebijakan lingkungan yang lebih ketat dapat mempengaruhi 
daya saing industri ini, memaksa perusahaan untuk berinovasi dalam 
aspek keberlanjutan dan pengelolaan sumber daya alam.
Industri karet, di sisi lain, menghadapi tantangan yang lebih berat 
terkait dengan harga komoditas yang cenderung volatile. Meskipun 
permintaan untuk karet alam diperkirakan akan meningkat seiring 
dengan pertumbuhan sektor otomotif global, ketergantungan pada 
ekspor dan ketidakstabilan harga karet dapat mengganggu kinerja 
industri. Oleh karena itu, keberhasilan sektor ini akan sangat bergantung 
pada kemampuan untuk mengelola pasokan dengan efisien serta 
memperkenalkan produk karet olahan dengan nilai tambah yang lebih 
tinggi untuk mengurangi ketergantungan pada harga internasional.
Pada tahun 2025, keberlanjutan akan menjadi fokus utama dalam 
kedua industri ini, dengan banyak perusahaan yang diharapkan untuk 
mengadopsi praktik pertanian yang lebih ramah lingkungan. Pemerintah 
Indonesia sendiri telah mengimplementasikan berbagai kebijakan 
yang mendorong sertifikasi keberlanjutan, seperti RSPO (Roundtable 
on Sustainable Palm Oil) untuk industri kelapa sawit, serta upaya 
meningkatkan produksi karet yang berkelanjutan. Penerapan teknologi 
ramah lingkungan dan peningkatan efisiensi energi juga akan menjadi 
faktor penentu dalam menjaga daya saing perusahaan-perusahaan 
sawit dan karet di pasar global.
Di sisi positif, peluang untuk diversifikasi produk dari kelapa sawit dan 
karet akan terus berkembang. Untuk industri sawit, pengembangan 
produk turunan seperti kosmetik, bahan bakar alternatif, dan bahan kimia 
berbasis kelapa sawit akan membuka pasar baru. Begitu juga dengan 
karet, di mana inovasi dalam produk karet sintetis dan pengembangan 
teknologi baru yang lebih efisien dalam proses produksi dapat 
mendorong daya saing. Dengan adaptasi yang tepat terhadap tantangan 
dan pemanfaatan peluang ini, industri sawit dan karet Indonesia dapat 
terus berkembang dan mempertahankan posisinya sebagai salah satu 
pemain utama dalam pasar global.

The palm oil and rubber industry business outlook in 2025 is expected 
to face significant challenges and opportunities. Amid global economic 
uncertainty, demand for palm oil-based products remains stable, 
especially for biodiesel and food products. Indonesia, as one of the main 
producers, is expected to continue to rely on exports to key markets such 
as Europe, India, and China. However, fluctuations in the price of crude 
palm oil (CPO) and pressure from stricter environmental policies can 
affect the competitiveness of this industry, forcing companies to innovate 
in the aspects of sustainability and natural resource management.
The rubber industry, on the other hand, faces tougher challenges related 
to volatile commodity prices. Although demand for natural rubber is 
expected to increase in line with the growth of the global automotive 
sector, dependence on exports and the instability of rubber prices 
can disrupt the industry’s performance. Therefore, the success of this 
sector will depend heavily on the ability to efficiently manage supply 
and introduce higher value-added processed rubber products to reduce 
dependence on international prices.
By 2025, sustainability will be a major focus in both of these industries, 
with many companies expected to adopt more environmentally 
friendly agricultural practices. The Indonesian government itself has 
implemented various policies that encourage sustainability certification, 
such as the RSPO (Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil) for the palm oil 
industry, as well as efforts to increase sustainable rubber production. 
The application of environmentally friendly technology and increased 
energy efficiency will also be a determining factor in maintaining the 
competitiveness of palm oil and rubber companies in the global market.
On the positive side, opportunities for product diversification from 
palm oil and rubber will continue to grow. For the palm oil industry, 
the development of derivative products such as cosmetics, alternative 
fuels, and palm oil-based chemicals will open up new markets. Likewise 
with rubber, where innovation in synthetic rubber products and the 
development of new, more efficient technologies in the production 
process can boost competitiveness. With the right adaptation to these 
challenges and utilization of opportunities, Indonesia’s palm oil and 
rubber industries can continue to grow and maintain their position as 
one of the major players in the global market.
