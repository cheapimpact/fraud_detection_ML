TINJAUAN MAKROEKONOMI
Pada tahun 2023, pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia mencapai 
5,05%, yang tercermin dari Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB) 
sebesar Rp20.892,4 triliun. PDB per kapita mencapai Rp75,0 
juta atau US$4.919,7, menunjukkan perkembangan positif 
dalam ekonomi Indonesia. Faktor-faktor pendukungnya meliputi 
konsumsi domestik yang stabil dan performa perdagangan 
internasional yang baik, meskipun dihadapi ketidakpastian 
ekonomi global akibat inflasi, perubahan iklim, konflik geopolitik, 
dan perlambatan ekonomi di beberapa negara.
Dalam Anggaran Pendapatan dan Belanja Negara (APBN) 
tahun 2023, Pendapatan Negara dan Hibah meningkat 
menjadi Rp2.774,30 triliun, naik 5,25% dari tahun sebelumnya. 
Kontribusi signifikan dari penerimaan perpajakan, Penerimaan 
Negara Bukan Pajak, dan Hibah berperan penting dalam 
menjaga keseimbangan fiskal. APBN 2023 menjadi alat yang 
mendukung pencapaian sasaran pembangunan, meredam 
dampak gejolak ekonomi global, dan menjaga kesejahteraan 
masyarakat.
Bank Indonesia mencatat stabilitas nilai tukar Rupiah meski 
Dolar AS menguat, dengan Rupiah menguat 1,11% pada 
akhir 2023 dibandingkan dengan akhir 2022. Stabilitas ini 
memberikan dampak positif dalam mengendalikan inflasi impor 
dan mendukung stabilitas ekonomi secara keseluruhan. Inflasi 
Harga Konsumen (IHK) Desember 2023 mencapai 2,61% yoy, 
menurun dari 5,51% yoy pada tahun sebelumnya.
Penurunan inflasi dipengaruhi oleh kebijakan moneter pro-
stabilitas Bank Indonesia dan kerjasama erat antara Bank 
Indonesia, Pemerintah Pusat, dan Daerah. Inflasi inti tetap 
rendah pada 1,80% yoy tahun 2023, dipengaruhi oleh inflasi 
impor yang rendah, ekspektasi inflasi yang terkendali, dan 
kapasitas ekonomi yang responsif terhadap permintaan 
domestik.
Ditinjau dari sisi Lapangan Usaha, berdasarkan data Badan 
Pusat Statistik (BPS), kontribusi pertumbuhan ekspor produk 
otomotif sepanjang tahun 2023 mencapai 5,96% (yoy), 
meningkat dari 5,14% pada tahun 2022. Sektor Perdagangan 
Besar & Eceran dan Reparasi Mobil & Sepeda Motor juga 
menggambarkan gambaran positif, dengan PDB atas dasar 
harga berlaku mencapai Rp2.702,4 triliun, naik 7,38% dari 
tahun sebelumnya.
MACROECONOMIC OVERVIEW 2023
In 2023, Indonesia's economic growth reached 5.05%, 
reflected in a Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of Rp20,892.4 
trillion. GDP per capita reached Rp75.0 million or US$4,919.7, 
indicating positive developments in the Indonesian economy. 
Supporting factors include stable domestic consumption and 
good international trade performance, despite global economic 
uncertainty due to inflation, climate change, geopolitical 
conflicts, and economic slowdown in several countries.
In the 2023 State Budget ("APBN"), State Revenues and 
Grants increased to Rp2,774.30 trillion, up 5.25% from the 
previous year. Significant contributions from tax revenue, non-
tax revenue, and grants play an important role in maintaining 
fiscal balance. The 2023 State Budget is a tool that supports 
the achievement of development goals, mitigates the impact of 
global economic turmoil, and maintains public welfare.
Bank Indonesia recorded the stability of the Rupiah exchange 
rate despite the strengthening of the US Dollar, with the Rupiah 
strengthening by 1.11% at the end of 2023 compared to the 
end of 2022. This stability has a positive impact in controlling 
imported inflation and supporting overall economic stability. 
Consumer Price Inflation (CPI) in December 2023 reached 
2.61% yoy, down from 5.51% yoy in the previous year.
The decrease in inflation was influenced by Bank Indonesia's 
pro-stability monetary policy and close cooperation between 
Bank Indonesia, Central and Local Governments. Core inflation 
remains low at 1.80% yoy in 2023, influenced by low imported 
inflation, manageable inflation expectations, and economic 
capacity responsive to domestic demand.
In terms of business field, based on data from the Central 
Statistics Agency (BPS), the contribution of automotive product 
export growth throughout 2023 reached 5.96% (yoy), an 
increase from 5.14% in 2022. The Wholesale & Retail Trade 
and Car & Motorcycle Repair sectors also illustrate a positive 
outlook, with GDP at current prices reaching Rp2,702.4 trillion, 
up 7.38% from the previous year.

AUTOMOTIVE INDUSTRY OVERVIEW 2023
Performance data compiled by the Car and Motorcycle 
Equipment Industry Association ("GIAMM") in 2023 shows 
that Indonesia's automotive industry may experience slowing 
growth. The decrease in domestic sales suggests that vehicle 
demand in Indonesia may be slowing down. This may be caused 
by several factors, such as inflation, rising interest rates, and the 
global economic slowdown.
Domestic Sales in 2023 amounted to 1,005,802 units, a 
decrease of 4.03% over the 2022 achievement of 1,048,047 
units. Meanwhile, GIAMM Exports were relatively stable, in 
2023 amounting to 570,915 units increased by 0.14% compared 
to the 2022 achievement of 570,142 units. In terms of imports, 
it increased in 2023 by 88,915 units which increased by 6.74% 
compared to the achievement in 2022 of 83,298 units.
The Indonesian Motorcycle Industry Association ("AISI") 
recorded that the Indonesian motorcycle industry experienced 
significant development in 2023, as the export domestic 
statistical distribution data provides an interesting illustration of 
motorcycle sales trends at home and abroad.
First, motorcycle sales in the domestic market reached an 
astonishing 6,236,992 units. There was an increase of 19.45% 
compared to the previous year which reached 5,221,470 
units.  However, the export side shows a different trend. Export 
motorcycle sales in 2023 reached 570,004 units, a decrease of 
23.34% from the previous year which reached 743,551 units.

Looking at the motorcycle category, there are interesting 
variations. Scooters were the favorites with a market share 
of 89.73%, an increase of 1.79% from the previous year.

Motorcycle recorded a market share of 5.08%, while Sport 
motorcycles reached 5.19%. Despite the decrease, both still 
hold an important position in the market, showing the diversity 
of consumer preferences.
However, in terms of electric motorcycles, the Government has a 
sales target of 200,000 units of electric motorcycles throughout 
2023, with efforts to increase sales through incentives in the form 
of a Rp 7 million discount. However, data from the Indonesian 
Motorcycle Industry Association (AISI) shows that from 2017 to 
2023, electric motorcycle sales only reached 54,000 units, far 
below the government's expectations.
According to AISI, the low public interest in electric motorcycles 
is caused by several factors, including doubts regarding 
range, charging time, and vehicle price. This makes electric 
motorcycles still not competitive in the Indonesian automotive 
market, with consumers preferring motorcycles based on fuel 
oil or gasoline.
To overcome this challenge, adequate infrastructure support 
is needed for electric motorcycles to compete. Therefore, 
several steps that can be taken by the government to increase 
interest in electric motors involve standardizing electric motor 
batteries. This standardization is expected to increase public 
trust, so electric motorcycles can become a more attractive and 
competitive choice in the Indonesian automotive market.

motorcycle industry. While sales in the domestic market 
continue to experience positive growth, challenges in the export 
market and shifting consumer preferences between categories 
show the importance of adaptability in future motorcycle 
industry strategies. Continued market stability can be utilized by 
PT Garuda Metalindo Tbk. to plan strategic initiatives to create 
momentum and increase growth in the automotive component 
sector.
Optimizing production, developing product innovations, and

consumer behavior, and identifying opportunities or risks that 
may arise. By adopting a proactive approach and showing 
adaptability, PT Garuda Metalindo Tbk. has the potential to 
strengthen its position in the automotive component industry.

Rasio Likuiditas
Rasio lancar mengukur kemampuan perusahaan untuk 
memenuhi kewajiban jangka pendeknya dengan menggunakan 
aset lancar. Semakin tinggi rasio lancar, semakin lancar 
perusahaan dalam membayar utangnya. Dalam hal ini, rasio 
lancar tahun 2023 tercatat 1,9 lebih tinggi daripada tahun 2022 
yang sebesar 1,6, hal ini menunjukkan bahwa perusahaan 
memiliki lebih banyak aset lancar relatif terhadap kewajiban 
jangka pendeknya pada tahun 2023.
Rasio Solvabilitas
Solvabilitas adalah kemampuan Perseroan untuk memenuhi 
seluruh utang berbunga dengan menggunakan aset atau 
ekuitas. Solvabilitas diukur dari rasio Liabilitas terhadap Total 
Aset dengan membandingkan jumlah seluruh utang berbunga 
dengan aset, serta rasio Liabilitas terhadap Total Ekuitas dengan 
membandingkan jumlah seluruh utang berbunga dengan jumlah 
ekuitas. 
Liabilitas terhadap Total Aset
Rasio ini menunjukkan seberapa besar aset perusahaan 
didanai oleh hutang. Semakin rendah rasio ini, semakin sedikit 
aset yang didanai oleh hutang, yang merupakan indikator 
solvabilitas yang lebih baik. Dalam hal ini, rasio total liabilitas 
terhadap total aset tetap stabil dari tahun 2022 hingga 2023, 
yaitu 0,4, menunjukkan bahwa sebagian besar aset perusahaan 
didanai oleh modal sendiri. 
Liabilitas terhadap Total Ekuitas
Rasio ini menunjukkan seberapa besar hutang perusahaan 
dibandingkan dengan ekuitasnya. Semakin rendah rasio ini, 
semakin sedikit hutang yang digunakan perusahaan untuk 
mendanai operasinya. Dalam hal ini, rasio total liabilitas 
terhadap total ekuitas menurun dari 0,7 pada tahun 2022 
menjadi 0,5 pada tahun 2023, yang menunjukkan peningkatan 
dalam struktur modal perusahaan yang lebih sehat.
Berdasarkan rasio likuiditas dan solvabilitas yang disajikan, 
dapat disimpulkan bahwa perusahaan memiliki kemampuan 
yang baik untuk membayar hutangnya. Rasio lancar yang 
lebih tinggi dan rasio solvabilitas yang stabil atau menurun 
menunjukkan bahwa perusahaan memiliki cukup aset lancar 
dan modal sendiri untuk menutupi kewajiban dan hutangnya, 
serta memiliki struktur modal yang lebih sehat pada tahun 2023 
dibandingkan dengan tahun sebelumnya.
Liquidity Ratio
The current ratio measures a company's ability to meet its short-
term obligations using current assets. The higher the current 
ratio, the more liquid the company is in paying its debts. In this 
case, the current ratio in 2023 recorded 1.9, higher than the 1.6 
in 2022, indicating that the company has more current assets 
relative to its short-term liabilities in 2023.
Solvency Ratio
Solvency is the Company's ability to meet all interest-bearing 
obligations using assets or equity. Solvency is measured by 
the Debt to Assets Ratio by comparing the total amount of 
interest-bearing debt with assets, and the Debt to Equity Ratio 
by comparing the total amount of interest-bearing debt with total 
equity.
Debt to Assets Ratio
This ratio indicates how much of the company's assets are 
funded by debt. The lower the ratio, the fewer assets are funded 
by debt, which is a better indicator of solvency. In this case, the 
total liabilities to total assets ratio remained stable from 2022 to 
2023, at 0.4, indicating that most of the company's assets are 
funded by equity.
Debt to Equity Ratio
This ratio indicates how much debt the company has compared 
to its equity. The lower the ratio, the less debt the company uses 
to finance its operations. In this case, the total liabilities to total 
equity ratio decreased from 0.7 in 2022 to 0.5 in 2023, indicating 
an improvement in the company's healthier capital structure.
Based on the presented liquidity and solvency ratios, it can be 
concluded that the company has a good ability to pay its debts. 
A higher current ratio and stable or decreasing solvency ratios 
indicate that the company has enough current assets and equity 
to cover its obligations and debts, and has a healthier capital 
structure in 2023 compared to the previous year.

obligations that affect its financial and operational management.

strategies related to employee benefits are crucial to ensure 
the Company's compliance with applicable regulations and the 
proper and efficient fulfillment of its employee benefit obligations.
