The food and beverage industry is a strategic sector that 
has an important role in sustaining Indonesia's economic 
growth. The Central Statistics Agency (BPS) announced 
that the food and beverage industry contributed 39.10% 
to the gross domestic product (GDP) of the non-oil and 
gas industry and 6.55% to the national GDP. The food and 
beverage industry sector experienced growth of 4.47% in 
2023 (year on year). The sector also recorded a positive 
trade balance of USD 25.21 billion with exports in 2023 
reaching USD 41.70 billion and imports of USD 16.49 
billion. In terms of investment, the food and beverage 
industry sector also continues to grow with investment 
reaching IDR 85.10 trillion in 2023. 
Indonesia's food resilience was also threatened by the 
El Nino phenomenon with a long drought during 2023. 
This affected the rice commodity produced in Indonesia. 
BPS noted that Indonesia's rice production in 2023 was 
estimated at 30.90 million tons, a decrease of 31 million 
tonnes compared to 2022.  For 2023, the consumption of 
the population estimated to be around 30.90 million tonnes, 
a decrease of 645.09 thousand tonnes (2.05%) compared to 
2022 at 31.54 million tons.

The potential utilization of Indonesia's maritime economy 
affects the fisheries sector. The fisheries sector plays a role 
in supporting inflation control efforts. This is inseparable 
from its considerable role as one of the main sources of 
animal food for the people of Indonesia. In the calculation 
of national inflation, the fish group is included in the 
volatile food group which has a contribution of around 2% 
to the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The fishery commodity 
inflation is mostly influenced by fresh fish. Along with the 
increasing consumption trend, efforts to maintain the 
sustainability of fish production are important to ensure 
the availability of supply can support price stability. 
Regarding meat consumption, referring to data from the 
Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development 
(OECD) Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), in 2023 
national beef consumption will be 2.25 kilograms/capita/

meat processing industry in Indonesia is rapidly growing, 
namely the meat processing industry in 2023 totalling 64 
companies with an investment value of IDR3.45 trillion and 
a workforce of 25,839 people.

of processed meat products (HS 1601 and 1602) in 2023

indicates that the export potential of processed meat 
products is quite high and has significant growth.

increase in consumption affected the performance of the

Pendapatan
Pada tahun 2023, Perseroan membukukan peningkatan 
pendapatan sebesar Rp31,60 miliar atau 8,41%, 
dari tahun 2022 sebesar Rp375,89 miliar meningkat 
menjadi sebesar Rp407,49 miliar. Peningkatan ini 
disebabkan oleh meningkatnya pendapatan bahan baku 
dan bahan pembantu sebesar Rp28,96 miliar. Pada 
tahun 2023, tercatat pendapatan usaha untuk segmen 
usaha makanan dan minuman serta waralaba juga 
mengalami peningkatan  sebesar Rp1,65 miliar dan 
Rp996,84 miliar.
Beban Pokok Pendapatan  
Beban pokok penjualan tahun 2023 sebesar Rp315,70 
miliar, menurun sebesar 2,48% dibandingkan dengan 
tahun 2022 sebesar Rp323,73 miliar. Penurunan 
tersebut terutama disebabkan menurunnya beban 
pokok pendapatan usaha dari segmen usaha bahan 
baku maupun usaha makanan dan minuman. 
Laba Bruto
Laba bruto tahun 2023 mencapai sebesar Rp91,79 miliar, 
meningkat 76,01% dibandingkan dengan tahun 2022 
sebesar Rp52,15 miliar. Peningkatan ini disebabkan 
oleh meningkatnya pendapatan usaha Perseroan. 
Beban Usaha
Beban usaha tahun 2023 sebesar Rp45,80 miliar, 
meningkat 58,91% dibandingkan dengan tahun 2022 
sebesar Rp28,82 miliar. Peningkatan tersebut berasal 
dari meningkatnya penyusutan aset hak guna dan aset 
tetap serta peningkatan  beban gaji dan tunjangan.
Laba Usaha
Laba usaha tahun 2023 mencapai Rp45,99 miliar, 
meningkat 97,59% dibandingkan dengan tahun 2022 
mengalami keuntungan sebesar Rp23,33 miliar. Hal 
tersebut naiknya laba bruto usaha yang berasal dari 
meningkatnya pendapatan usaha. 
Pendapatan (Beban) Lain-Lain 
Pendapatan lain-lain tahun 2023 sebesar Rp88,98 
miliar, meningkat 1.081,25% dibandingkan dengan 
tahun 2022 tercatat beban lain – lain  sebesar Rp9,07 
miliar. Peningkatan tersebut berasal dari meningkatnya 
revaluasi pembelian properti investasi.

mencapai

juta,

komprehensif lain sebesar Rp155,78 juta. Peningkatan 
tersebut berasal dari meningkatnya pos-pos yang tidak 
akan direklasikasikan ke laba rugi dari pengukuran 
kembali atas imbalan pasca kerja. 
Revenue
In 2023, the Company posted an increase in revenue of 
IDR31.60 billion or 8.41%, from 2022 of IDR375.89 billion 
to IDR407.49 billion. This increase was due to the increase 
in raw material and auxiliary material revenue of IDR28.96 
billion. In 2023, it was recorded that operating revenue for 
the food and beverage and franchise business segment 
reached IDR1.65 billion and IDR996,84 billion.
Cost Of Revenue  
Cost of revenue in 2023 amounted to IDR315.70 billion, 
an decrease of 2.48% compared to 2022 of IDR323.73 
billion. The decrease was mainly due to the decrease in 
cost of operating revenue from the raw materials, food and 
beverage business segments.
Gross Profit
Gross profit in 2023 reached IDR91.79 billion, an increase 
of 76.01% compared to 2022 of IDR52.15 billion. This 
increase was due to the increase in the Company's 
operating income.
Operating Expenses
Operating expenses in 2023 amounted to IDR45.80 billion, 
an increase of 58.91% compared to 2022 of IDR28.82 billion. 
The increase came from the increasing depreciation 
right of use assets and fixed assets as well as salary and 
allowance expenses. 
Operating Profit
Recorded operating profit for each business segment 
in 2023 reached Rp46.06 billion, an increase of 97.42% 
compared to 2022 of IDR 23.33 billion. This increase was 
due to an increase in operating profit in the raw material 
business segment of IDR 28.79 billion or 136.97% from IDR 
21.02 billion in 2022 to IDR 49.81 billion in 2023.
Other Revenue (Expenses)

increase of 1.081.25% compared to 2022 of  recorded other 
expenses of IDR9.07 billion. The increase came from the 
increased Revaluation of Investment Properties.
Other Comprehensive Income

million, an increase of 137.11% compared to 2022 which 
recorded other comprehensive loss of IDR155.78 million.

reclassified to profit and loss from re-measurement of 
post-employment benefits.

Dividend Policy
New Shareholders from the Public Offering have the same 
and equal rights in all aspects with the Company's old 
Shareholders including the right to dividend distribution in 
accordance with the provisions of the Company's Articles 
of Association and applicable laws and regulations.
By taking into account the Company's financial condition

Pemegang Saham jangka panjang.
Dividend Policy Projections

distribute dividends of up to 20% (twenty percent) of 
the Company's net profit for the current year after tax 
which ends on 31 December 2023 and in the future or 
in accordance with the Company's cash capacity. The 
Company's policy in the distribution of dividends will be 
decided by the Annual General Meeting of Shareholders 
("AGMS") which is held every year or other amounts 
proposed by the Company's shareholders and approved 
at the AGMS, taking into account the Company's financial 
condition and business sustainability.
The Company can also distribute dividends in the year in 
which the company records a positive profit balance after 
deducting reserves based on the UU PT. In addition, the 
Company also measures the Company's growth rate in 
the future which is an important consideration in dividend 
distribution. All things considered, are expected to be 
aligned with the Company's goal of maximizing long-term 
shareholder value.
