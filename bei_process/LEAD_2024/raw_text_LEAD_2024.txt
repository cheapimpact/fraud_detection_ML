MANAGEMENT DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
Lingkungan Ekonomi Global 
Pada tahun 2024, ekonomi global berada dalam fase 
pemulihan yang beragam, yang dibentuk oleh tekanan 
inflasi yang terus berlanjut, pergeseran kebijakan moneter, 
dan ketegangan geopolitik. Dana Moneter Internasional 
(IMF) memproyeksikan pertumbuhan PDB global sekitar 
3,1%, laju yang moderat yang mencerminkan pengetatan 
kondisi keuangan di negara-negara maju dan pemulihan 
yang lebih lambat dari yang diantisipasi di pasar-pasar 
negara berkembang.
Di AS, produksi minyak mentah mencapai 13,2 juta barel per 
hari (b/d) pada tahun 2024 dan diperkirakan akan melebihi 
13,4 juta b/d pada tahun 2025, yang merupakan rekor baru. 
Pertumbuhan produksi diproyeksikan akan terus berlanjut 
selama dua tahun ke depan, didorong oleh peningkatan 
efisiensi.
Pertumbuhan produksi sedikit lebih cepat dari pertumbuhan 
permintaan, sehingga memungkinkan persediaan untuk 
meningkat secara moderat, yang dapat memberikan 
sedikit tekanan ke bawah pada harga minyak mentah. 
Perkembangan terbaru di Timur Tengah telah meningkatkan 
risiko gangguan pasokan, yang dapat menyebabkan 
harga yang lebih tinggi dan lebih tidak stabil. Sektor kapal 
pendukung lepas pantai diuntungkan oleh utilisasi rig yang 
lebih tinggi dan aktivitas proyek yang diperbarui, tetapi terus 
menghadapi tekanan dari biaya input yang meningkat dan 
armada OSV global yang menua. 
Global Economic Environment
In 2024, the global economy navigated a mixed recovery 
path, 
shaped 
by 
persistent 
inflationary 
pressures, 
shifting monetary policies, and geopolitical tensions. The 
International Monetary Fund (IMF) projected global GDP 
growth of approximately 3.1%, a modest pace reflecting 
tightening financial conditions in advanced economies and 
slower-than-anticipated recoveries in key emerging markets.
In the U.S., crude oil production reached 13.2 million barrels 
per day (b/d) in 2024 and is expected to exceed 13.4 million 
b/d in 2025, setting new records. Production growth is 
projected to continue over the next two years, driven by 
improvements in well efficiency. 
Production growth slightly outpaced demand growth, 
allowing inventories to build modestly, which could exert 
some downward pressure on crude oil prices. Recent 
developments in the Middle East have heightened the risk 
of supply disruptions, which could lead to higher and more 
volatile prices. The offshore support vessel sector benefited 
from higher rig utilization and renewed project activity, but 
continued to face pressure from inflationary input costs and 
an aging global OSV fleet.

Prospek Ekonomi Indonesia
Perekonomian Indonesia tetap tangguh di tahun 2024, 
dengan perkiraan pertumbuhan PDB sebesar 5,1%, 
didorong oleh konsumsi domestik yang kuat, pembangunan 
infrastruktur, dan ekspor komoditas yang kuat. Komitmen 
pemerintah terhadap transisi energi dan modernisasi 
infrastruktur, termasuk inisiatif terkait pengembangan ibu 
kota Nusantara dan infrastruktur logistik kelautan, terus 
memacu investasi di sektor transportasi dan maritim.
Industri minyak  dan gas di Indonesia tetap menjadi 
kontributor utama pendapatan nasional. Meskipun ada 
pergeseran jangka panjang ke arah energi terbarukan, proyek-
proyek minyak dan gas lepas pantai-terutama di wilayah 
timur-terus menarik investasi. Pertamina dan beberapa 
operator asing melanjutkan kampanye pengeboran lepas 
pantai, sehingga meningkatkan permintaan akan OSV. 
Rupiah mengalami depresiasi yang tidak terlalu besar 
terhadap dolar AS, yang dipengaruhi oleh pengetatan 
moneter global. Hal ini memberikan tantangan dan peluang 
bagi operator OSV dengan pendapatan dalam mata uang 
dolar dan struktur biaya berbasis rupiah. 
Beberapa 
tantangan 
termasuk 
meningkatnya 
biaya 
operasional akibat inflasi dan volatilitas harga bahan 
bakar serta fluktuasi mata uang yang berdampak pada 
perencanaan keuangan dan pengadaan. Namun demikian, 
masih terdapat beberapa peluang seperti: kegiatan 
eksplorasi dan pengeboran lepas pantai, pembangunan 
infrastruktur di Indonesia bagian timur dan cekungan 
perbatasan, serta posisi strategis sebagai pusat maritim di 
kawasan Indo-Pasifik.
Di tengah kondisi ekonomi makro ini, Perseroan tetap fokus 
pada efisiensi operasional, penggunaan aset strategis, dan 
kerja sama yang erat dengan para klien untuk memenuhi 
kebutuhan logistik yang terus berkembang di sektor lepas 
pantai Indonesia.
Indonesia Economic Outlook
Indonesia’s economy remained resilient in 2024, with 
estimated GDP growth of 5.1%, driven by strong domestic 
consumption, infrastructure development, and robust 
commodity exports. The government’s commitment to 
energy transition and infrastructure modernization, including 
initiatives related to the development of the Nusantara 
capital city and marine logistics infrastructure, continued to 
spur investment in transport and maritime sectors.
The oil and gas industry in Indonesia remained a key 
contributor to national revenue. Despite a long-term shift 
toward renewables, offshore oil and gas projects—especially 
in the eastern regions—continued to attract investment. 
Pertamina and several foreign operators resumed offshore 
drilling campaigns, bolstering demand for OSVs.
The Indonesian Rupiah experienced modest depreciation 
against the US dollar, influenced by global monetary 
tightening. This presented both challenges and opportunities 
for OSV operators with dollar-denominated revenues and 
rupiah-based cost structures. 
Some challenges include rising operational costs due to 
inflation and fuel price volatility and currency fluctuations 
impacting financial planning and procurement. However 
certain opportunities remain such as: renewed offshore 
exploration and drilling activity, infrastructure development 
in eastern Indonesia and frontier basins and strategic 
positioning as a maritime hub in the Indo-Pacific region.
Amid these macroeconomic conditions, the Company 
continues to focus on operational efficiency, strategic asset 
deployment, and close collaboration with clients to meet 
evolving logistical needs in Indonesia’s offshore sector.

Aset
In 2024, the Company reported total assets of US$129.98 
million, reflecting a 0.35% increase—or US$0.46 million—
compared to 2023. This growth was primarily driven by 
an increase in cash, resulting from improved operational 
revenue.
Current Aset 
As of 2024, the Company’s current assets increased by 41.14%, 
or US$11.30 million, reaching US$38.76 million compared 
to US$27.46 million in 2023. The increase was primarily 
attributable to a US$8.51 million addition to restricted cash 
under Other current financial assets. This amount represents 
funds held in a custodian bank account, established and 
maintained in the name of the custodian, designated for 
the settlement of long-term loan principal and interest 
obligations to OCP Asia Fund IV and OCP Asia Fund V. The 
funds are recorded under “Other Current Financial Assets” 
and reflect a reallocation from the Company’s cash flow from 
operations.
In addition, two of the Company’s vessels, Logindo Progress 
and Synergy, were reclassified from fixed assets to current 
assets held for sale, with a combined carrying value of 
US$4.84 million.

to-equity swap, which further contributed to the overall 
reduction in liabilities.
Current Liabilities

significantly to US$35.76 million, representing a 207.64% rise 
or an increase of US$24.13 million from US$11.62 million in 
2023. This increase was primarily driven by a new short-term 
loan arising from the restructuring of the Company’s long-
term bank facilities, which were acquired by OCP Asia Fund. 
Under the new loan agreement, a portion of the facility is 
now classified as a current liability as it falls due within the 
year.
Non-Current Liabilities
In 2024, the Company’s Non-Current Liabilities were US$49.51 
million decreasing 47.37% or US$44.57 million from US$94.08 
million in 2023. This was caused by long term bank loans to 
UOB and DBS were acquired by OCP Asia Fund. Under the 
new loan agreement, a portion of the facility is now classified 
as a long-term liability as they are not expected to mature 
within one year.
Equity
In 2024, the Company’s equity increased significantly to 
US$44.71 million, representing a growth of 87.70% or 
US$20.89 million from US$23.82 million in the previous year. 
This increase was primarily attributable to the issuance of 
1,750,000,000 new shares in connection with the conversion 
of debt to equity, as part of the execution of an option for 
partial debt repayment.

Pendapatan
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan mencatatkan pendapatan 
sebesar AS$46,69 juta, meningkat 42,46% atau AS$13,91 juta 
dibandingkan AS$32,77 juta pada tahun 2023. Pertumbuhan 
ini terutama didorong oleh kenaikan rata-rata tarif sewa 
harian sebesar 12%, seiring dengan tingkat utilisasi yang 
lebih tinggi untuk beberapa jenis kapal.
Beban Pokok Pendapatan
Pada tahun 2024, beban pokok pendapatan Perseroan 
mencapai AS$31,15 juta, meningkat 11,09% atau AS$3,11 
juta dari AS$28,04 juta pada tahun sebelumnya. Peningkatan 
ini terutama disebabkan oleh meningkatnya beban 
penyusutan aset tetap, serta meningkatnya biaya sewa kapal. 
Hal ini disebabkan oleh kebutuhan untuk menyewa kapal 
pihak ketiga ketika kapal-kapal milik Perseroan tidak tersedia 
karena adanya jadwal perawatan kapal atau komitmen untuk 
proyek-proyek lain. 
Laba Bruto
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan melaporkan laba bruto sebesar 
AS$15,53 juta, yang merupakan peningkatan substansial 
sebesar 228,36% dibandingkan dengan AS$4,73 juta pada 
tahun 2023. Pertumbuhan yang signifikan ini terutama 
didorong oleh pendapatan yang lebih tinggi, ditambah 
dengan peningkatan beban pokok pendapatan yang relatif 
lebih rendah. Sebagai hasilnya, marjin laba bruto Perseroan 
meningkat menjadi 33,27% pada tahun 2024.
Beban Umum dan Administrasi
Pada tahun 2024, beban umum dan administrasi Perseroan 
adalah sebesar AS$6,51 juta, meningkat 39,77% atau 
AS$1,85 juta dibandingkan AS$4,65 juta pada tahun 2023. 
Peningkatan ini terutama disebabkan oleh meningkatnya 
beban jasa profesional dan pengakuan bunga yang dianggap 
sebagai bunga atas pinjaman OCP yang tidak berbunga. 
Meskipun pinjaman ini tidak memiliki bunga kontraktual, 
standar akuntansi mengharuskan pengakuan bunga yang 
diperhitungkan. Selain itu, Perseroan mencatat lebih tinggi 
penyisihan kerugian kredit yang diharapkan (ECL) atas 
piutang usaha selama tahun berjalan.
Laba (Rugi) Usaha
Pada tahun 2024, laba usaha Perseroan melonjak menjadi 
AS$10,85 juta, meningkat 1.363,29% atau AS$10,11 juta 
dibandingkan AS$0,74 juta pada tahun 2023. Peningkatan 
yang signifikan ini terutama didorong oleh peningkatan laba 
kotor sebesar 228,36%, yang mencerminkan pertumbuhan 
pendapatan yang kuat dan peningkatan efisiensi biaya.
Revenues
In 2024, the Company recorded revenues of US$46.69 
million, representing an increase of 42.46% or US$13.91 
million compared to US$32.77 million in 2023. This growth 
was primarily driven by a 12% average increase in daily 
charter rates, along with higher utilization rates for certain 
vessel types.
Cost of Revenue
In 2024, the Company’s cost of revenues amounted to 
US$31.15 million, an increase of 11.09% or US$3.11 million 
from US$28.04 million in the previous year. The increase was 
primarily attributable to higher depreciation expenses on 
fixed assets, as well as increased vessel lease costs. The latter 
arose from the need to charter third-party vessels when the 
Company’s own vessels were unavailable due to scheduled 
maintenance dockings or commitments to other projects.
Gross Profit
In 2024, the Company reported a gross profit of US$15.53 
million, representing a substantial increase of 228.36% 
compared to US$4.73 million in 2023. This significant growth 
was primarily driven by higher revenues, coupled with a 
comparatively lower increase in cost of revenue. As a result, 
the Company’s gross profit margin improved to 33.27% in 
2024.
General and Administrative Expenses
In 2024, the Company’s general and administrative expenses 
amounted to US$6.51 million, representing an increase of 
39.77% or US$1.85 million compared to US$4.65 million 
in 2023. This increase was primarily driven by higher 
professional services expenses and the recognition of 
deemed interest on certain non-interest-bearing tranches 
of the OCP loan. Although these tranches do not carry 
contractual interest, accounting standards require the 
recognition of imputed interest. Additionally, the Company 
recorded a higher provision for expected credit losses (ECL) 
on trade receivables during the year.
Operating Profit (Loss)
In 2024, the Company’s operating profit surged to US$10.85 
million, representing an increase of 1,363.29% or US$10.11 
million compared to US$0.74 million in 2023. This significant 
improvement was primarily driven by the 228.36% increase in 
gross profit, reflecting strong revenue growth and improved 
cost efficiency.

Liquidity Ratio

measures the Company’s ability to meet its short-term 
obligations by comparing current assets to current liabilities.

ratio was 1.08x, a decrease from 2.36x in 2023. This decline 
was primarily due to an increase in current liabilities that are 
due to mature within the next year. Despite the decrease, the 
current ratio indicates that the Company is still positioned to 
meet its short-term obligations.
Solvency Ratio
The solvency ratio is assessed by comparing the Company’s 
liabilities to equity (Debt-to-Equity Ratio or DER) and 
liabilities to assets (Debt-to-Asset Ratio or DAR). As of 
December 31, 2024, the Company reported a Debt-to-Equity

Strategi Pemasaran
Prospek bisnis diperkirakan akan terus berkembang, tidak 
hanya dalam hal jumlah proyek yang sedang berjalan-seperti 
kegiatan pengeboran, konstruksi lepas pantai, dan operasi-
tetapi juga dalam hal anggaran yang dialokasikan oleh 
Pemerintah untuk sektor Minyak dan Gas. SKKMIGAS, badan 
yang mengawasi kegiatan minyak dan gas di Indonesia, 
telah terlibat dalam diskusi dengan perusahaan-perusahaan 
minyak dan gas besar dan pemilik kapal lepas pantai untuk 
mengevaluasi dinamika pasar (penawaran vs. permintaan).
Hal ini merupakan perkembangan yang positif, karena 
SKKMIGAS telah mendapatkan wawasan terkini mengenai 
harga sewa kapal lepas pantai di seluruh industri. Terlihat 
bahwa beberapa perusahaan minyak dan gas mulai 
menyesuaikan proses pengadaan dan tender mereka, 
terutama dengan meningkatkan anggaran untuk penyewaan 
kapal lepas pantai.
SKKMIGAS menyadari bahwa persaingan pasar di sektor 
minyak dan gas juga harus memperhitungkan penawaran 
dan permintaan di negara-negara tetangga di Asia Pasifik 
dan bahkan di Timur Tengah. Hal ini dibuktikan dengan 
banyaknya pemilik kapal lepas pantai yang memilih untuk 
menyewakan kapalnya di luar negeri dengan harga sewa 
yang jauh lebih tinggi dibandingkan dengan harga sewa di 
Indonesia.
Perseroan telah berhasil menyewakan kapal untuk proyek-
proyek di Bintulu, Malaysia, dan negara-negara lainnya. 
Memperoleh penyewaan kapal internasional tetap menjadi 
pilihan yang menarik dan strategis bagi Perseroan di masa 
mendatang.

Marketing strategy
Business prospects are expected to continue to grow, not 
only in terms of the number of ongoing projects—such as 
drilling activities, offshore construction, and operations—
but also in the budgets allocated by the government for the 
Oil and Gas sector. SKKMIGAS, the body overseeing oil and 
gas activities in Indonesia, has engaged in discussions with 
major oil and gas companies and offshore vessel owners to 
evaluate market dynamics (supply vs. demand).
This is a positive development, as SKKMIGAS has gained up-
to-date insights into offshore vessel rental prices across the 
industry. It is observed that several oil and gas companies are 
beginning to adjust their procurement and tender processes, 
notably by increasing budgets for offshore vessel rentals.
SKKMIGAS recognizes that market competition within the 
oil and gas sector must also account for supply and demand 
in neighboring Asia Pacific countries and even the Middle 
East. This is evidenced by the fact that many offshore vessel 
owners have opted to lease their ships abroad, commanding 
rental prices significantly higher than those typically found in 
the Indonesian market.
The Company, for its part, has successfully chartered vessels 
for projects in Bintulu, Malaysia, and other countries. 
Securing international vessel charters remains an attractive 
and strategic option for the Company moving forward.
