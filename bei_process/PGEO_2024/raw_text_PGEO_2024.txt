kemudian menyentuh level tertinggi US$90 per barel

Economic Overview 2024
The year 2024 was characterized by a dynamic and 
challenging global landscape, shaped by escalating 
geopolitical tensions, the intensifying effects of the 
climate crisis, and a wave of general elections across 
several major economies, including Indonesia and the 
United States. These developments contributed to a 
cautious market sentiment, leading to a general 
slowdown in economic activity as stakeholders adopted 
a wait-and-see approach.
Global uncertainties have driven significant price 
volatility across several key indicators throughout the 
year. One notable example is Brent crude oil, which 
opened at USD80 per barrel and fell to USD68.68 per 
barrel in March 2024—marking its first drop below the 
USD70 threshold since November 2021. The price 
later surged to a peak of USD90 per barrel on 
April 16, 2024, before settling at USD74.64 per barrel 
by year-end.
The rupiah depreciated against the US dollar, reaching 
Rp16,157 as of 31 December 2024 — exceeding the 
2024 State Budget (APBN) assumption of Rp15,000 
per US dollar.
Domestically, President of the Republic of Indonesia, 
Prabowo Subianto, emphasized his commitment to 
achieving 
national 
energy 
self-sufficiency. 
He 
highlighted the strategic importance of enhancing 
energy security through the expanded use of New and 
Renewable Energy (NRE), including geothermal, as a 
key pillar in supporting Indonesia’s pathway toward 
Net Zero Emissions (NZE) by 2060.
President Prabowo has expressed confidence in 
Indonesia’s potential to reach economic growth of up 
to 8% as part of the Golden Indonesia 2045 vision. In 
2024, the national economy grew by 5.03% year-on-
year (yoy), slightly lower than the 5.05% growth 
recorded in the previous year. However, this result fell 
short of the 2024 State Budget target, which projected 
a growth rate of 5.2%.
Excluding 
the 
2020–2021 
pandemic 
period, 
Indonesia’s economy has consistently maintained a 
stable growth rate of around 5% over the past decade—
well above the global average of approximately 2.6%. 
This sustained growth reflects the resilience of the 
Indonesian economy and underscores its potential to 
drive increasing energy demand and consumption in 
the years ahead.

Industrial Overview
The government has identified 362 potential geothermal 
sites throughout Indonesia, with a combined capacity 
of up to 23,592 MW. This positions Indonesia as the 
country with the second-largest geothermal reserves 
globally, trailing only the United States.
According to data from the Ministry of Energy and 
Mineral Resources (MEMR), as published in the 2023 
Handbook of Energy & Economic Statistics of Indonesia, 
of the total geothermal potential, 14,411 MW are 
classified as reserves (possible, probable, proven) with 
development potential. Meanwhile, 9,181 MW are 
categorized as speculative and hypothetical resources.
The integration of renewable energy, including 
geothermal power, into the national energy mix remains 
suboptimal, primarily due to cost challenges, as the 
price of renewable energy still struggles to compete 
with that of fossil fuels.
Several factors contribute to this, including the absence 
of a carbon tax in Indonesia, which would address 
emissions from fossil fuel combustion, as well as the 
underdevelopment of New and Renewable Energy 
(NRE) technologies and supply chains within the country.
In line with the Government's vision for national energy 
self-sufficiency and security, the Ministry of Energy and 
Mineral Resources has reiterated its commitment to 
increasing the share of New and Renewable Energy 
(NRE) in the national energy mix, with geothermal 
energy being a key focus. By the end of 2024, NRE 
accounted for just 14.1% of the energy mix, with a 
target set to reach 17-19% by 2025.
According to data from Statistics Indonesia (BPS), 
household consumption—which accounts for 53% of the 
country’s Gross Domestic Product (GDP)—grew by 
4.94% in 2024. This growth was supported by 
continued government social assistance programs, 
ongoing infrastructure development, and a rise in 
investment activity.
Indonesia's inflation rate in 2024 stood at 1.57% year-
on-year (yoy), marking the lowest level since 1958—the 
year Statistics Indonesia (BPS) began recording 
inflation in the Jakarta area. This historically low 
inflation was driven by stable food and energy prices, 
a decline in airfares, and the prudent monetary policy 
stance maintained by Bank Indonesia (BI).

Pemanfaatan energi panas bumi di Indonesia pada

PGE’s Position in the National  
Geothermal Industry
As of 2024, Indonesia’s geothermal energy utilization 
reached 2.6 GW, generated from 18 Geothermal 
Power Plants (PLTPs) across the country. The Company 
operates nine of these PLTPs, making PGE the largest 
geothermal operator in Indonesia, with an installed 
capacity contributing up to 28.91% of the national 
total.
Pemerintah melalui Kementerian ESDM juga berjanji 
akan memberikan dukungan insentif untuk mendorong

Berdasarkan data Kementerian ESDM, hingga akhir

bumi telah mencapai 5,3% dari total bauran energi 
nasional, atau sekitar 40% dari bauran EBT. Dengan

kapasitas Pembangkit Listrik Tenaga Panas Bumi (PLTP) 
mencapai 1,2 GW, sehingga total kapasitas terpasang

rumah serta mengurangi emisi Gas Rumah Kaca (GRK) 
sebesar 17,4 juta ton CO2 per tahun. 
The Government, through the Ministry of Energy and 
Mineral Resources, has committed to providing 
incentive support to accelerate the growth of the 
geothermal industry. This includes reducing the licensing 
process from 18 months to just five days and enhancing 
Return on Investment (ROI) to attract more investors. 
These measures are aimed at fostering greater 
collaboration and advancing the national geothermal 
sector.
According to data from the Ministry of Energy and 
Mineral Resources, by the end of 2024, geothermal 
energy accounted for 5.3% of the national energy mix, 
representing approximately 40% of the New and 
Renewable Energy (NRE) share. This significant 
contribution underscores geothermal's crucial role in 
advancing 
decarbonization 
within 
Indonesia's 
electricity sector, positioning the country as the second-
largest geothermal electricity producer globally.
Between 2014 and 2024, the capacity of Geothermal 
Power Plants (PLTP) increased by 1.2 GW, bringing the 
total installed geothermal capacity to 2.6 GW, or 
approximately 11% of the nation's total geothermal 
potential. This additional capacity is sufficient to power 
1.3 million households and contribute to a reduction of 
17.4 million tons of CO2 emissions annually.

Per akhir tahun 2024, PGE memiliki Wilayah Kuasa 
Pengusahaan (WKP) panas bumi yang dikelola sendiri 
(own operation) sebanyak enam lapangan yang sudah 
beroperasi komersial dengan total kapasitas terpasang

Hingga akhir tahun 2024, empat proyek PGE masih

Tiga, 
Tanjung 
Tiga,

Terdapat lima lapangan yang dioperasikan melalui

empat lapangan sudah beroperasi komersial yaitu 
Gunung Salak (377 MW), Darajat (271 MW) dan 
Wayang- Rindu (227 MW) yang dioperasikan oleh 
Star Energy Geothermal Ltd., Sarulla (330 MW) yang 
dioperasikan oleh Sarulla Operation Ltd., dan satu 
lapangan dalam tahap eksplorasi yaitu di Bedugul, 
Bali yang dioperasikan oleh Bali Energy Ltd.
Berdasarkan studi yang dilakukan dan diverifikasi oleh 
William (Bill) Cumming dan LAPI ITB pada tahun 2024, 
didapatkan hasil analisa bahwa potensi panas bumi di 
seluruh WKP panas bumi PGE mencapai 3 GW. Artinya 
PGE masih memiliki peluang pengembangan panas

telah menargetkan kapasitas terpasang hingga >1 
GW pada tahun 2028.
By the end of 2024, PGE operated six geothermal 
Working Areas (WKP) under its own management that 
had entered commercial operation, with a total 
installed capacity of 673 MW. These fields include 
Kamojang (235 MW), Ulubelu (220 MW), Lahendong 
(120 MW) including a 0.5 MW binary plant for 
internal use, Sibayak (12 MW), Karaha (30 MW), and 
Lumut Balai (55 MW).
As of the end of 2024, PGE has four geothermal 
projects in the development phase, namely Lumut Balai 
Units 2 and 3 (each 55 MW), Hululais Units 1 and 2 
(totaling 110 MW), and Lahendong Units 7 and 8 
(totaling 40 MW). In addition, several other fields 
remain in the exploration stage, including Gunung 
Tiga, 
Tanjung 
Tiga, 
Seulawah, 
Sungai 
Penuh, 
Kotamobagu, and Way Ratai.
PGE also collaborates with partners through Joint 
Operation Contracts (JOC) for five geothermal fields. 
Four of these fields are already in commercial operation: 
Gunung Salak (377 MW), Darajat (271 MW), and 
Wayang Windu (227 MW), operated by Star Energy 
Geothermal Ltd., as well as Sarulla (330 MW), 
operated by Sarulla Operations Ltd. One additional 
field, located in Bedugul, Bali and operated by Bali 
Energy Ltd., remains in the exploration stage.
A study conducted and verified in 2024 by geothermal 
expert William (Bill) Cumming in collaboration with 
LAPI ITB concluded that the total geothermal potential 
across all of PGE’s Working Areas (WKP) reaches 
approximately 3 GW. This highlights the significant 
untapped development opportunities within PGE’s 
existing concessions. Aligned with this potential, PGE 
has set a strategic target to increase its installed 
capacity to more than 1 GW by 2028.

GEOTHERMAL UTILIZATION SCHEME
SKEMA PEMANFAATAN PANAS BUMI
Panas bumi memiliki fleksibilitas pemanfaatan yang 
tinggi karena tidak bergantung pada kondisi iklim 
maupun cuaca. Secara umum pemanfaatan energi 
panas bumi dapat dibagi menjadi dua, yakni 
pemanfaatan tidak langsung untuk Pembangkit Listrik 
Tenaga Panas Bumi (PLTP), dan pemanfaatan langsung 
untuk kegiatan lain.
Geothermal energy has high utilization flexibility since 
it does not depend on climate or weather conditions. In 
general, geothermal energy utilization is divided into 
two categories: indirect utilization for Geothermal 
Power Plants (PLTP), and direct utilization for other 
activities.
Skema Pemanfaatan Panas Bumi 
Secara Tidak Langsung
Pemanfaatan energi panas bumi secara tidak langsung 
untuk PLTP dijalankan dari hasil produksi fluida panas 
bumi dari sumber daya panas bumi (reservoir), yang 
kemudian uap panasnya dialirkan ke turbin untuk 
menggerakkan generator melalui beberapa tahapan:
Indirect Geothermal Utilization 
Scheme
Indirect utilization of geothermal energy for PLTP is 
conducted from the production result of geothermal 
fluid from geothermal resources (reservoirs), which then 
the hot steam is channeled to the turbine to drive the 
generator through several stages:
1.	 Pengeboran sumur dilakukan di kawasan dan/
atau area yang memiliki reservoir dengan 
kedalaman 700 - 3.000 meter.
2.	 Pada lapangan panas bumi dominasi uap, fluida 
yang mengalir dari sumur hanya berupa uap.
3.	 Pada lapangan panas bumi dominasi air, fluida 
yang mengalir dari sumur berupa uap dan air 
panas (brine).
4.	 Fluida panas bumi dialirkan menggunakan pipa 
menuju separator untuk dipisahkan fasa-nya 
menjadi uap dan brine.
5.	 Brine yang telah dipisahkan diinjeksi kembali ke 
reservoir melalui sumur injeksi.
6.	 Uap tersebut dialirkan menuju turbin untuk memutar 
generator.
7.	 Listrik yang dihasilkan oleh generator dialirkan 
menuju transformer untuk disesuaikan tegangannya, 
kemudian dialirkan menuju jaringan listrik PLN.
8.	 Melalui jaringan listrik tersebut, listrik kemudian 
didistribusikan ke konsumen di sekitar pembangkit 
listrik.
1.	 Well drilling is conducted in regions and/or areas 
that have reservoirs with a depth of 700 - 3,000 
meters.
2.	 In geothermal fields dominated by steam, the fluid 
flowing from the well is only steam.
3.	 In geothermal fields dominated by water, the fluid 
flowing from the well is steam and hot water 
(brine).
4.	 Geothermal fluid is channeled using pipes to the 
separator to separate its phase into steam and 
brine. 
5.	 The separated brine is re-injected into the reservoir 
through the injection well.
6.	 The steam is channeled to the turbine to rotate the 
generator.
7.	 The electricity generated by the generator is 
channeled to the transformer to adjust the voltage, 
then channeled to the PLN electricity network.
8.	 Through the electricity network, electricity is then 
distributed to consumers around the power plant.

Direct Geothermal Utilization 
Scheme
Direct utilization refers to the use of geothermal energy 
for non-electrical purposes, without converting heat or 
fluids into other forms of energy. This can be achieved 
in two primary ways by harnessing the energy that 
naturally occurs on the Earth's surface:
1.	 Geothermal fluid is directed to a low-pressure 
separator, where hot steam and brine are 
separated. The steam is then routed to the turbine, 
while the hot water from the separator is sent to a 
heat exchanger to warm the working fluid (water).

Anggaran Perusahaan (RKAP) dan Rencana Jangka 
Panjang Perusahaan (RJPP), Perseroan terus melakukan 
kegiatan eksplorasi dan pengembangan di seluruh 
WKP agar dapat menjamin ketersediaan uap panas 
bumi untuk memenuhi kontrak jual beli dan usaha yang 
berkelanjutan.
Consistent with the business strategy set forth in the 
Company's Work Plan and Budget (RKAP) and Long-
Term Plan (RJPP), the Company is dedicated to 
progressing exploration and development activities 
across all WKPs, ensuring a steady supply of 
geothermal steam to meet sales and purchase 
agreements 
and 
support 
long-term 
business 
sustainability.
Exploration activities
Exploration activities encompass a range of geoscience 
studies, including geology, geochemistry, geophysics, 
and geomatics. These activities begin with surveys and 
extend through data processing, modeling, analysis, 
evaluation, 
and 
interpretation, 
culminating 
in 
exploration drilling until a project is prepared for 
electricity generation.

As part of its development strategy, the Company has 
identified opportunities to enhance installed capacity 
by up to 230 MW through the application of binary 
technology across its geothermal working areas, 
optimizing the use of existing geothermal resources for 
increased efficiency and output.
Priority development will focus on the Ulubelu area with a 
30 MW Binary Unit (BU) and the Lahendong area with a 
15 MW BU 1. The signing of the Joint Development 
Agreement (JDA) and Consortium Agreement between 
PGE and PT PLN Indonesia Power underscores PGE’s strong 
commitment to accelerating project commercialization and 
advancing geothermal development.
To ensure steam availability and maintain stable steam 
and electricity distribution in its operating areas, the 
Company is carrying out make-up well drilling and Well 
Repair Workover (KURS) activities—including workover, 
hole cleaning, and acidizing. Of the six wells targeted 
for 2024, four are scheduled for execution. Meanwhile, 
KURS activities for two wells in the Sibayak Area will 
proceed following the completion of a study on the 
application of modular Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) 
geothermal power plant technology.

BUSINESS OUTLOOK	
PROSPEK USAHA	
Pemerintah berkomitmen mewujudkan Asta Cita atau 
visi misi Presiden Prabowo dalam mewujudkan 
ketahanan 
dan 
swasembada 
energi, 
melalui 
peningkatan proporsi EBT dalam bauran energi 
nasional dengan panas bumi sebagai andalan utama.
Menteri ESDM Bahlil Lahadalia pada bulan Desember 
2024 menyatakan akan memaksimalkan pemanfaatan 
panas bumi sebagai sumber energi yang ramah 
lingkungan dan memiliki tingkat keandalan tinggi, 
mengingat Indonesia memiliki potensi panas bumi yang 
sangat besar hingga 23,6 GW, nomor dua terbesar di 
dunia setelah Amerika Serikat. 
Agar rencana tersebut dapat terlaksana, serta untuk 
mendukung tercapainya percepatan hilirisasi sumber 
daya alam, Pemerintah Indonesia telah mengambil 
langkah strategis dengan membentuk Satuan Tugas 
Percepatan Hilirisasi dan Ketahanan Energi Nasional 
melalui Keputusan Presiden No. 1 Tahun 2025.
Keputusan ini dilatarbelakangi oleh kebutuhan untuk 
menyelaraskan 
kebijakan 
di 
berbagai 
sektor, 
memastikan ketersediaan lahan, dan menyelesaikan 
berbagai hambatan yang ada secara terkoordinasi 
antara Kementerian/lembaga dan Pemerintah Daerah. 
Kementerian ESDM juga berencana untuk mendorong 
perkembangan industri geotermal melalui berbagai 
insentif, seperti mempercepat proses perizinan dan 
pengoperasian pembangkit sebagai sweetener bagi 
investor.
Melihat berbagai faktor di atas, PGE optimis bahwa 
dukungan Pemerintah akan mendukung pertumbuhan 
industri panas bumi dan prospek pertumbuhan usaha 
Perseroan di masa mendatang. Selain itu, pertumbuhan 
ekonomi domestik yang positif juga akan mendorong 
permintaan energi, termasuk dari EBT seperti panas 
bumi.
Menurut data Kementerian ESDM, hingga akhir tahun 
2024 kontribusi listrik yang dihasilkan dari panas bumi 
telah mencapai 5,3% dari total bauran energi nasional, 
atau sekitar 40% dari bauran EBT. Dengan demikian, 
energi panas bumi telah berperan penting dalam 
mendukung 
upaya 
dekarbonisasi 
sektor 
ketenagalistrikan di Indonesia. 
Kapasitas PLTP terpasang sejak tahun 2014 telah 
meningkat sebesar 1,2 GW, sehingga total kapasitas 
terpasang panas bumi Indonesia kini mencapai 2,6GW 
atau setara 11% dari total potensi panas bumi 
Indonesia. 
The Government is committed to realizing President 
Prabowo's vision and mission, known as Asta Cita, to 
achieve energy resilience and self-sufficiency through 
the increased proportion of NRE in the national energy 
mix with geothermal as the mainstay.
In December 2024, Minister of Energy and Mineral 
Resources Bahlil Lahadalia stated that he would 
maximize the use of geothermal energy as an 
environmentally friendly energy source with a high 
level of reliability, considering that Indonesia has a 
very large geothermal potential of up to 23.6 GW, the 
second largest in the world behind the United States.
In order for this plan to be implemented, and to support 
the achievement of accelerated downstreaming of 
natural resources, the Indonesian Government has taken 
strategic steps by establishing a Task Force for the 
Acceleration of Downstreaming and National Energy 
Resilience through Presidential Decree No. 1 of 2025.
This decision was motivated by the need to align 
policies in various sectors, ensure land availability, and 
resolve various obstacles in a coordinated manner 
between 
Ministries/Institutions 
and 
Regional 
Governments. The Ministry of Energy and Mineral 
Resources also plans to encourage the development of 
the geothermal industry through various incentives, 
such as accelerating the licensing process and 
operating power plants as a sweetener for investors.
Given the various factors above, PGE is optimistic that 
the Government support will drive the growth of the 
geothermal industry and the Company's business 
growth prospects in the future. Additionally, positive 
domestic economic growth will also drive energy 
demand, including from NRE such as geothermal.
Data from the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources 
revealed that by the end of 2024 the contribution of 
electricity generated from geothermal energy has 
reached 5.3% of the total national energy mix, or 
around 40% of the renewable energy mix. Thus, 
geothermal energy has played a crucial role in 
supporting efforts to decarbonize the electricity sector 
in Indonesia.
The installed capacity of PLTP since 2014 has increased 
by 1.2 GW, making the total installed capacity of 
Indonesia's geothermal energy now reaching 2.6 GW 
or equivalent to 11% of Indonesia's total geothermal 
potential.

Peningkatan Manfaat Panas Bumi
Dalam menjalankan usaha ke depan, Perseroan akan 
tetap berfokus pada tiga strategi utama yaitu 
“Geothermal Development, Downstream Industry, dan 
Green by-Product and Industrial Estate”, dengan 
mendorong pertumbuhan kapasitas di dalam negeri 
(organic 
growth), 
menangkap

peningkatan manfaat panas bumi atau Beyond Energy.
PGE melihat potensi energi panas bumi yang belum 
termanfaatkan sepenuhnya di negara-negara di dunia 
yang memiliki cadangan dan pengembangan panas 
bumi yang terbukti, serta memiliki relasi Pemerintah 
yang kuat dengan Indonesia akan pemanfaatan energi 
panas bumi. Contohnya seperti Amerika Serikat, New 
Zealand, Kenya, Turkiye, Filipina, dan Meksiko, yang 
dapat 
menjadi 
peluang 
bagi 
Perseroan 
untuk 
mendorong pertumbuhan bisnis melalui Merger & 
Acquisition (M&A).
Perseroan juga tengah melakukan riset pengembangan 
berbagai usaha produk turunan panas bumi seperti 
green hydrogen, methanol, silica, pemanfaatan listrik 
geotermal secara off-grid untuk data center, High 
Performance Computing (HPC), dan beberapa produk 
lainnya yang diharapkan dapat menjadi sumber 
pendapatan baru bagi PGE di masa depan. Selain itu, 
PGE juga telah masuk ke dalam bisnis penunjang 
geotermal yaitu Layanan Analisis Laboratorium Panas 
Bumi dan manufaktur heat exchanger.
Ke depannya, Perseroan berkomitmen membangun 
ekosistem 
dan 
infrastruktur 
geotermal, 
seperti 
membangun pabrik komponen mesin geotermal yang

bagi 
PGE, 
namun 
juga 
dapat 
mendorong 
perkembangan industri dan perekonomian Indonesia.
Increasing Geothermal Benefits 
(Beyond Energy)
In running the business going forward, the Company 
will continue to focus on three key strategies, namely 
"Geothermal Development, Downstream Industry, 
Green by-Product and Industrial Estate", by encouraging 
domestic capacity growth (organic growth), seizing 
prospective inorganic expansion opportunities, and 
increasing geothermal benefits or Beyond Energy.
PGE sees the potential for geothermal energy that has 
not been fully utilized in countries in the world that 
have proven geothermal reserves and development, 
and have strong government relations with Indonesia 
for the leverage of geothermal energy. Examples 
include the United States, New Zealand, Kenya, 
Turkiye, the Philippines, and Mexico, which serve as 
opportunities for the Company to encourage business 
growth through Merger & Acquisition (M&A).
The Company is also conducting research into the 
development of various geothermal derivative product 
businesses such as green hydrogen, methanol, silica, 
off-grid utilization of geothermal electricity for data 
centers, High Performance Computing (HPC), and 
several other products that are expected to become 
new sources of income for PGE in the future. 
Additionally, PGE has also entered into the geothermal 
supporting business, namely Geothermal Laboratory 
Analysis Services and heat exchanger manufacturing.
Going forward, the Company is committed to building 
a geothermal ecosystem and infrastructure, such as 
building a geothermal engine component factory that 
will not only become a new source of income for PGE, 
but can also drive the development of the Indonesian 
industry and economy.

MARKETING ASPECTS AND MARKET SHARE
ASPEK PEMASARAN DAN PANGSA PASAR
Energi panas bumi bersifat spesifik karena tidak dapat 
dipindah 
atau 
disimpan, 
namun 
harus 
melalui 
pemanfaatan lokal (in-situ). Hal tersebut memengaruhi 
beberapa faktor, seperti kemampuan pasar, aspek 
bisnis, teknis, dan kebijakan yang ditetapkan.
Aspek Pemasaran
Kekhususan produk yang dihasilkan menjadikan 
pelanggan Perseroan saat ini hanya PT Perusahaan 
Listrik Negara (Persero) atau PLN. Dengan demikian, 
Perseroan tidak melaksanakan program pemasaran 
atau promosi secara bebas dan terbuka, namun tetap 
menjaga kesinambungan PJBU dan PJBL dalam jangka 
panjang.
Geothermal energy is specific because it cannot be 
moved or stored, but must be utilized locally (in-situ). 
This affects several factors, such as market capacity, 
business aspects, technical aspects, and established 
policies.
Marketing Aspects
The specificity of the products produced makes the 
Company's current customers only PT Perusahaan Listrik 
Negara (Persero) or PLN. Thus, the Company does not 
carry out marketing or promotion programs freely and 
openly, but still maintains the continuity of PJBU 
and PJBL in the long term.
Pangsa Pasar 
Dengan total pengelolaan mencapai 12 WKP dan tiga 
wilayah kerja IPB, serta kapasitas terpasang PLTP yang 
dikelola sendiri sebesar 673 MW atau sekitar 32% 
dari total kapasitas terpasang nasional 2.121 MW 
(berdasarkan publikasi kementerian ESDM), PGE 
menjadi pengelola panas bumi terbesar di dalam 
negeri dan mengalami persaingan usaha yang terbatas 
dengan IPP panas bumi lainnya.
Per akhir tahun 2024, Perseroan telah berhasil 
menyelesaikan fase Mechanical Completion Fluid 
Collection and Reinjection System (FCRS) proyek Lumut 
Balai unit 2. Jumlah total kapasitas terpasang PGE 
mencapai 1.878 MW yang terdiri dari 673 MW yang 
dioperasikan sendiri, dan 1.205 MW dikelola melalui 
KOB.
Market Share 
With a total management of 12 WKPs and three IPB 
work areas, and an installed capacity of PLTP own 
operations of 673 MW or around 32% of the total 
national installed capacity of 2,121 MW (based on 
the publication of the Ministry of EMR), PGE is the 
largest geothermal operator in the country and 
experiences limited business competition with other 
geothermal IPPs.
By the end of 2024, the Company has successfully 
completed the Mechanical Completion Fluid Collection 
and Reinjection System (FCRS) phase of the Lumut Balai 
Unit 2 project. PGE's total installed capacity reaches 
1,878 MW, consisting of 673 MW own operations, 
and 1,205 MW managed through JOC.

DIVIDEND PAYMENT POLICY
KEBIJAKAN PEMBAYARAN DIVIDEN
Sesuai Undang-undang Perseroan Terbatas (UUPT) dan 
Anggaran Dasar Perseroan, keputusan mengenai 
pembagian 
dividen 
diambil 
melalui 
keputusan 
pemegang saham dalam Rapat Umum Pemegang 
Saham (RUPS) tahunan, berdasarkan usulan Direksi. 
Perseroan dapat membagikan dividen jika memiliki 
saldo laba yang positif. Sebelum tahun buku berakhir, 
Perseroan 
dapat 
membagikan 
dividen 
interim 
sepanjang tidak mengakibatkan kekayaan bersih 
Perseroan 
menjadi 
kurang 
dari 
jumlah 
modal 
ditempatkan dan disetor dan cadangan wajib. 
Pembagian dividen interim ditentukan oleh Direksi 
Perseroan setelah terlebih dahulu disetujui oleh Dewan 
Komisaris Perseroan. Jika setelah akhir tahun buku yang 
bersangkutan Perseroan mengalami kerugian, maka 
dividen interim yang dibagikan harus dikembalikan 
oleh pemegang saham kepada Perseroan, dan Direksi 
dan Dewan Komisaris Perseroan akan bertanggung 
jawab secara tanggung renteng jika dividen interim 
tidak dikembalikan. 
Pemegang saham Perseroan pada tanggal pencatatan 
yang berlaku akan berhak atas sejumlah penuh dividen 
yang disetujui, dan tunduk kepada pemotongan pajak 
Indonesia yang dikenakan. Dividen yang diterima oleh 
pemegang saham bukan warga negara Indonesia 
akan dikenakan pemotongan pajak Indonesia sebesar 
20%, dengan pengurangan tarif tersedia tergantung 
pada perjanjian pajak terkait yang ditandatangani 
oleh Indonesia dan mitra perjanjiannya.

2024

dividen 
untuk 
tahun 
buku 
2023 
sebesar 
Rp1.982.367.600.000 atau Rp47,77 per lembar 
saham, yang dibayarkan secara tunai kepada 
pemegang saham. Pembayaran dividen tersebut 
mencapai 78,50% dari perolehan laba bersih 
Perseroan.
Under the Limited Liability Company Law (UUPT) and 
the Company's Articles of Association, decisions 
regarding dividend distribution are taken through 
shareholder resolutions at the Annual General Meeting 
of Shareholders (GMS), based on proposals from the 
Board of Directors.
The Company may distribute dividends if it has a 
positive profit balance. Before the end of the financial 
year, the Company may distribute interim dividends so 
long as it does not result in the Company's net assets 
becoming less than the amount of issued and paid-up 
capital and mandatory reserves.
The distribution of interim dividends is determined by 
the Company's Board of Directors upon the approval of 
the Company's Board of Commissioners. If after the end 
of the relevant financial year the Company experiences 
a loss, then the interim dividends distributed must be 
returned by the shareholders to the Company, and the 
Board of Directors and Board of Commissioners of the 
Company will be jointly and severally liable if the 
interim dividends are not returned.
The Company's shareholders on the applicable record 
date will be entitled to the full amount of the approved 
dividends, and are subject to applicable Indonesian 
withholding taxes. Dividends received by non-
Indonesian shareholders will be subject to Indonesian 
withholding tax of 20%, with reduced rates available 
depending on the relevant tax treaty signed by 
Indonesia and its treaty partners.

and make mitigation efforts to maintain business 
continuity, particularly ensuring aspects of operational 
safety and security considering the topographic

generally located in highlands or mountains that have 
the potential for geohazard risks.
In collaboration with various parties including 
universities and related agencies, the Company 
conducts geohazard studies as a reference in 
developing an early warning system which is the best 
means of detecting potential geohazards around the 
PGE Area and Project.

been equipped with a gas detector device to detect 
gas leaks and the presence of toxic gases, ensuring the
