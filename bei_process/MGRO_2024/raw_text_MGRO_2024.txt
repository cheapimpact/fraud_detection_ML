Industry Overview
Sebagai salah satu negara penghasil kelapa sawit terbesar, Indonesia 
memiliki peran yang sangat strategis dalam pemenuhan kebutuhan 
minyak sawit dunia. Hasilnya, industri kelapa sawit memiliki peran strategis 
bagi perekonomian Indonesia.
Berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS) pada Desember 2024, 
kinerja ekspor minyak sawit mentah (CPO) Indonesia mengalami 
penurunan 9,34% menjadi sebesar USD1,89 miliar. Penurunan ini sudah 
terjadi sejak 2022 dan 2023 hingga 2,5 juta ton per tahun. Sejalan dengan 
naiknya kebutuhan CPO untuk pasokan biodiesel hingga meningkatnya 
kebutuhan untuk konsumsi, sementara produksi dalam negeri cenderung 
stagnan di kisaran 51 juta ton per tahun. Bagi produsen sawit, kenaikan 
penyerapan sawit dalam negeri mampu menopang kinerja positif 
perusahaan sepanjang 2024.
Sebagai industri strategis, pemerintah berkomitmen untuk mendukung 
sektor perkebunan kelapa sawit sebagai salah satu komoditas strategis 
nasional dan mendukung perekonomian. Dengan kebijakan pemerintah 
saat ini juga ditujukan untuk meningkatkan sektor hulu dengan peningkatan 
produktivitas dan kesejahteraan pekebun serta mendorong hilirisasi 
nasional. Program tersebut antara lain peremajaan sawit rakyat dengan 
meningkatkan produktivitas perkebunan rakyat menggunakan bibit 
bersertifikat, penelitian dan pengembangan melalui pendanaan penelitian 
hingga siap ke tahap komersial, pengembangan SDM melalui pendidikan 
dan pelatihan, adanya insentif biodiesel, bantuan sarana prasarana, dan 
program promosi dan kemitraan untuk menciptakan citra positif dari 
produk kelapa sawit baik di dalam maupun di luar negeri.
As one of the largest palm oil-producing countries, Indonesia plays a 
crucial role in meeting the world's palm oil demands. Consequently, the 
palm oil industry is vital to the Indonesian economy.
According to data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS) as of 
December 2024, Indonesia's crude palm oil (CPO) exports declined 
by 9.34%, totaling USD1.89 billion. This downturn follows a trend from 
2022 and 2023, leading to an annual figure of 2.5 million tons. This 
occurs alongside a rising demand for CPO driven by biodiesel needs, 
contrasted with stagnant domestic production, which remains around 
51 million tons annually. For palm oil producers, the growth in domestic 
palm oil consumption was able to enhance their positive performance 
throughout 2024.
Recognizing the strategic importance of palm oil, the government 
is dedicated to fostering this sector as a key national commodity 
and a focal point for economic support. Current policies target 
enhancements in the upstream sector by boosting planter productivity 
and welfare while promoting national downstreaming initiatives. 
Programs encompass the rejuvenation of smallholder oil palms aimed 
at increasing the productivity of these plantations using certified seeds, 
providing research and development funding until they reach market 
readiness, advancing human resource skills through education and 
training, implementing biodiesel incentives, improving infrastructure 
support, and launching promotional and partnership initiatives to 
cultivate a positive perception of palm oil products both domestically 
and abroad.

In the preliminary stage, the fruit is processed by 
softening the flesh at a temperature of 90°C. The 
softened flesh is separated from the kernels and palm 
kernel shells by pressing through a hollow cylinder 
machine using heating and pressing techniques. 
Afterward, it passes through a sedimentation system, 
allowing the remaining palm kernel shells to settle at 
the bottom.
After being released from the bunch, oil palm fruit is 
processed into two main products: CPO, extracted 
from the mesocarp or fruit flesh, and PKO, extracted 
from the hard seed in the center. The first step involves 
pressing the fruit to extract CPO from the mesocarp. 
Next, the palm oil is filtered and purified to prevent 
cross-contamination, and then it is dried to meet CPO 
standard specifications.
Additionally, CPO is sent to the refinery plant to be 
processed into RBDPO and PFAD, which serve as raw 
materials for vegetable oil processing companies 
(including 
cooking 
oil, 
cream, 
and 
margarine), 
oleochemicals (applied in detergents and lubricants), 
biodiesel (used as fuel), and lauric acid (used in 
cosmetics and soaps).
Regarding the leftover oil palm kernels, the palm kernel 
shells are broken apart and separated. Subsequently, 
the PK shells are collected for sale as biofuels, while the 
kernels are crushed to create PKO and PKE.
PKO can serve as raw material for companies that 
produce food products, like non-dairy cream and ice 
cream, while the remaining production from PKE is 
typically utilized to manufacture animal feed.
Segment Performance
In 2024, the effects of price competition and the 
availability of high-quality raw materials for production 
were highly significant. This situation notably affected 
production volumes, stock levels, and product prices, 
which in turn raises production costs.
In 2024, the operational performance in this segment 
experienced a decline in production and sales 
compared to last year. Nevertheless, the Company 
remains committed to boosting sales through careful 
analysis of market demands and product pricing. 
Control over raw materials’ purchase volumes and 
prices, along with efforts to enhance operational 
efficiency and effectiveness, continues to be prioritized 
to achieve improved performance.

Segment Performance
The performance of the logistics segment increased 
rapidly in 2024 due to the internal’s reduction in the

costs for other customers. Additionally, the increase in 
customers utilizing Palm Acid Oil (PAO) product storage,

comply with Indonesian Law No. 39 of 2014 regarding

20% of palm oil raw materials come from the company’s

materials for production to subsidiaries.
