Laporan Tahunan 2023
Sebagai induk perusahaan, sebagian besar kegiatan usaha 
Perseroan dilaksanakan masing-masing anak perusahaan 
yang sebagian besar bergerak di bidang pertambangan, 
sementara sisanya bergerak di bidang non-tambang namun 
mendukung usaha pertambangan secara terpadu.
Kegiatan Pertambangan 
Perseroan melalui anak perusahaannya telah memproduksi 
49,7 juta MT batubara pada tahun 2023, meningkat 27,8% 
dibandingkan produksi tahun 2022 sebesar 38,9 juta MT. 
Peningkatan produksi di tahun 2023 terutama disebabkan 
peningkatan kinerja perusahaan.
PT Gunungbayan Pratamacoal (GBP)
GBP merupakan pemegang PKP2B generasi kedua yang terdiri 
atas Blok 1 dan Blok 2, yang keduanya berlokasi di Kalimantan 
Timur. GBP Blok 2 memproduksi batubara dengan kalori tinggi 
mencapai 6.500 kcal/Kg GAR, sementara GBP Blok 1 memiliki 
sejumlah kecil deposit batubara semi-soft coking.
Pada tahun 2023, GBP tidak melakukan kegiatan produksi.
PT Wahana Baratama Mining (WBM)
WBM merupakan pemegang PKP2B generasi ketiga yang 
berlokasi di Satui, Kalimantan Selatan. WBM memproduksi 
batubara kualitas bituminous dengan kandungan kalori sekitar 
6.300-6.500 kcal/Kg GAR. Untuk mendukung operasional 
pengiriman batubara, WBM memanfaatkan terminal khusus 
yang terletak di pantai Sungai Cuka - Satui. Batubara tersebut 
kemudian diangkut dengan tongkang ke floating crane yang 
berada di perairan Kalimantan Selatan, yang dapat melayani 
kapal Handymax, Panamax dan/atau Capesize.
WBM memproduksi 2,0 juta MT pada tahun 2023, atau 52,7% 
lebih tinggi dibandingkan dengan produksi tahun 2022 sebesar 
1,3 juta MT dan sedikit lebih rendah dari anggaran sebesar 1,9 
juta MT. Rasio pengupasan tanah aktual sebesar 17,3 lebih 
tinggi dibandingkan anggaran rasio pengupasan tanah sebesar 
15,1, terutama dikarenakan mobilisasi tambahan overburden 
excavator pada tahun tersebut untuk mempercepat pekerjaan 
di area pertambangan Tahap 6 di Barat. 
As a holding company, most of the business activities of the 
Company are performed by subsidiaries which are mostly 
engaged in the mining sector, with the remaining subsidiaries 
engaged in the non-mining sector that offer integrated support 
to the mining business.
Mining Operations
The Company, through its subsidiaries, had produced 49.7 
million MT in 2023, a 27.8% increase from 2022 production of 
38.9 million MT. The increase of production in 2023 was mainly 
due to the company’s performance.
PT Gunungbayan Pratamacoal (GBP)
GBP is the holder of the second generation CCoW consisting of 
Block 1 and Block 2, which are both located in East Kalimantan. 
GBP Block 2 produced high calorific coal of up to 6,500 kcal/
Kg GAR and GBP Block 1 has a small deposit of semi-soft 
coking coal.
In 2023, GBP did not produce coal.
PT Wahana Baratama Mining (WBM)
WBM is the holder of the third generation CCoW located in 
Satui, South Kalimantan. WBM produces bituminous quality 
coal with calorific content of 6,300-6,500 kcal/Kg GAR. In order 
to support coal deliveries, WBM utilizes a special port located 
at Cuka River - Satui. The coal is then transported by barges to 
a floating crane in South Kalimantan waters, which can serve 
Handymax, Panamax and/or Capesize vessels.
WBM produced 2.0 million MT in 2023, which was 52.7% higher 
than the 2022  production of 1.3 million MT and slightly lower 
than the budget of 1.9 million MT. The actual stripping ratio of 
17.3 was higher than the budget stripping ratio of 15.1 primarily 
due to the mobilisation of additional overburden excavators in 
the year to speed up works in the Stage 6 mining area in the 
West.

PT Perkasa Inakakerta (PIK)
PIK merupakan pemegang PKP2B generasi ketiga yang 
berlokasi di Kutai Timur, Kalimantan Timur. PIK memproduksi 
batubara kualitas sub-bituminous dengan kandungan kalori 
4.600-4.700 Kcal/kg GAR. Untuk mendukung pengiriman 
batubara, PIK menggunakan terminal khusus yang terletak di 
Pantai Sekerat. Terminal Khusus ini dapat memuat langsung 
ke kapal Handymax atau Panamax.
PIK memproduksi 1,2 juta MT pada tahun 2023 atau 32,0% 
lebih tinggi dibandingkan dengan produksi tahun 2022 sebesar 
1,0 juta MT dan 5,2% lebih tinggi dibandingkan dengan 
anggaran sebesar 1,2 juta MT. Rasio pengupasan tanah aktual 
sebesar 7,1 sejalan dengan anggaran rasio pengupasan tanah 
sebesar 7,2. Selama tahun ini, PIK menandatangani surat 
penunjukkan dengan PT Antareja Mahada Makmur (AMM) 
untuk memperluas pertambangan di PIK ke depannya. Dalam 
hal ini, sesuai dengan revisi izin terkait, PIK berharap dapat 
meningkatkan produksi menjadi lebih dari 3 mtpa pada tahun-
tahun yang akan datang. AMM diperkirakan memulai mobilisasi 
perlengkapan dan tenaga kerja ke PIK selama semester 1 
tahun 2024.
PT Teguh Sinarabadi (TSA)
TSA merupakan pemegang PKP2B generasi ketiga yang 
berlokasi di Kutai Barat, Kalimantan Timur. TSA memproduksi 
batubara kualitas bituminous dengan kandungan kalori sekitar 
5.700-6.000 Kcal/kg GAR. Untuk mendukung operasional 
pengiriman batubara, TSA menggunakan terminal khusus 
yang terletak di Sungai Mahakam.
TSA memproduksi 0,7 juta MT pada tahun 2023 atau 40,7% 
lebih rendah dibandingkan produksi tahun 2022 sebesar 1,2 
juta MT dan sejalan dengan anggaran sebesar 0,7 juta MT. 
Rasio pengupasan tanah aktual sebesar 12,5 berada di atas 
anggaran rasio pengupasan tanah sebesar 11,9.
PT Firman Ketaun Perkasa (FKP)
FKP merupakan pemegang PKP2B generasi ketiga yang 
berlokasi di Kutai Barat, Kalimantan Timur. FKP memproduksi 
batubara kualitas bituminous dengan kandungan kalori sekitar 
5.700-6.000 Kcal/kg GAR. Lokasi tambang FKP dan TSA saling 
berdekatan sehingga FKP menggunakan terminal khusus yang 
sama dengan TSA untuk pengiriman batubaranya.
FKP memproduksi 2,0 juta MT pada tahun 2023 atau 39,1% 
lebih tinggi dibandingkan dengan produksi tahun 2022 sebesar 
1,4 juta MT dan 3,3% sedikit lebih rendah dibandingkan 
dengan anggaran sebesar 2,1 juta MT. Hal ini terutama 
dikarenakan dampak curah hujan di kawasan tersebut yang 
memengaruhi produktivitas armada terlepas dari tambahan 
armada yang dimobilisasi oleh kontraktor. Rasio pengupasan 
tanah aktual sebesar 12,5 berada sedikit di bawah anggaran 
rasio pengupasan tanah sebesar 12,7.
PT Perkasa Inakakerta (PIK)
PIK is the holder of the third generation CCoW located in Kutai 
Timur, East Kalimantan. PIK produces sub-bituminous quality 
coal with calorific value of 4,600-4,700 Kcal/kg GAR. In order 
to support coal deliveries, PIK utilizes a special port located at 
Sekerat Beach, which is able to load directly to Handymax or 
Panamax vessels.
PIK produced 1.2 million MT in 2023 which was 32.0% higher 
than the 2022 production of 1.0 million MT and 5.2% higher 
than the budget of 1.2 million MT. The actual stripping ratio 
of 7.1 was largely in line with the budget stripping ratio of 7.2. 
During the year, PIK signed a letter of award with PT Antareja 
Mahada Makmur (AMM) to expand the mining at PIK going 
forward. In this regard, subject to the relevant permit revisions, 
PIK expect to be increasing production to more than 3 mtpa 
in coming years.  AMM are expected to commence mobilising 
equipment and personnel to PIK throughout 1H 2024.
PT Teguh Sinarabadi (TSA)
TSA is the holder of the third generation CCoW located at Kutai 
Barat, East Kalimantan. TSA produces bituminous quality coal 
with calorific content of around 5,700-6,000 Kcal/kg GAR. To 
support coal delivery operations, TSA utilizes a special port 
located on the Mahakam River.
TSA produced 0.7 million MT in 2023, which was 40.7% lower 
than the 2022 production of 1.2 million MT but in line with the 
budget of 0.7 million MT. The actual stripping ratio of 12.5 was 
above the stripping ratio budget of 11.9.
PT Firman Ketaun Perkasa (FKP)
FKP is the holder of the third generation CCoW located in Kutai 
Barat, East Kalimantan. FKP produces bituminous quality coal 
with calorific content of around 5,700-6,000 Kcal/kg GAR. FKP 
and TSA mine locations are in close proximity, therefore FKP 
uses the same special port as TSA for its coal shipment.
FKP produced 2.0 million MT in 2023 which was 39.1% higher 
than the 2022 production of 1.4 million MT and 3.3% slightly 
lower than the budget of 2.1 million MT. This was primarily 
due to the impact of rainfall in the region affecting the fleet 
productivity despite additional fleets being mobilised by the 
contractor. The actual stripping ratio of 12.5 was slightly below 
the stripping ratio budget of 12.7.

Proyek Tabang
FTB merupakan gabungan dari 3 perusahaan tambang yang 
terdiri atas FSP, BT dan BAS, yang memiliki IUP Operasi Produksi 
yang berlokasi di Kutai Kartanegara, Kalimantan Timur. Proyek 
Tabang memproduksi batubara sub-bituminous yang ramah 
lingkungan dengan kandungan belerang rendah. Kandungan 
kalori batubaranya sekitar 4.000-4.400 Kcal/kg GAR.
Pakar Utara merupakan integrasi dari 3 perusahaan tambang 
yang bersebelahan, yaitu TA, TJ, dan DE yang berlokasi di 
Kutai Kartanegara, Kalimantan Timur. Pakar Utara berdekatan 
dengan FTB dan juga memiliki batubara sub-bituminous yang
ramah lingkungan dengan kandungan kalori sekitar 3.700-
4.200 Kcal/kg GAR.
Pakar Selatan merupakan integrasi dari 6 perusahaan tambang 
yang berdampingan, yaitu SK, OM, SA, BS, AU dan CA. Pakar 
Selatan di selatan Pakar Utara dan juga memiliki batubara 
sub-bituminous yang ramah lingkungan dengan kandungan 
kalori sekitar 3.000 Kcal/kg GAR. FTB, Pakar Utara, dan Pakar 
Selatan secara kolektif disebut Proyek Tabang.
Proyek Tabang saat ini menggunakan Pelabuhan Khusus 
Gunung Sari yang terletak di Sungai Belayan, Pelabuhan 
Khusus Senyiur yang terletak di Sungai Kedang Kepala, dan 
Pelabuhan Khusus Muara Pahu di Sungai Mahakam.
Secara total Proyek Tabang menghasilkan 43,8 juta MT pada 
tahun 2023, atau 28,6% lebih tinggi dibandingkan produksi 
tahun 2022 sebesar 34,0 juta MT, dan 2,7% lebih tinggi dari 
anggaran sebesar 42,6 juta MT. Hal ini terutama dikarenakan 
kenaikan produksi TA yang dipengaruhi oleh armada tambahan 
yang dimobilisasi oleh kontraktor pertambangan kami, BUMA 
pada 2023, serta kondisi kemarau pada kuartal ke-3 dan ke-4, 
sehingga operasional pertambangan berjalan lancar. 
PT Mamahak Coal Mining (MCM)
MCM merupakan pemegang IUP produksi yang berlokasi di 
Kutai Barat, Kalimantan Timur dan memiliki cadangan batubara 
dengan kualitas bituminous.
Tidak ada kegiatan operasional pertambangan di MCM pada 
tahun 2023 dan 2022 karena Perseroan sedang fokus pada 
Proyek Tabang.
Pendukung Operasi Pertambangan
Balikpapan Coal Terminal (BCT)
BCT adalah pelabuhan khusus batubara yang dikelola oleh 
anak perusahaan Perseroan, yaitu DPP yang terletak di 
Kalimantan Timur. Sebagian batubara Perseroan diekspor 
melalui BCT. Pada tahun 2023, BCT memuat 25,5 juta MT 
batubara untuk berbagai pelanggan atau 15,7% lebih tinggi 
dari 22,0 juta MT yang dimuat pada tahun 2022. Peningkatan 
ini sebagian besar disebabkan oleh peningkatan tonase 
batubara yang diterima dari Proyek Tabang.
Tabang Project
FTB is the combination of 3 mining companies comprising FSP, 
BT, and BAS, having the Production Operation of IUP located 
in Kutai Kartanegara, East Kalimantan. The Tabang Project 
produces sub-bituminous, environmentally friendly, lowsulfur 
coals. The calorific content of the coal is around 4,000-4,400 
Kcal/kg GAR.
North Pakar is the integration of 3 adjoining mining companies, 
namely TA, TJ, and DE located in Kutai Kartanegara, East 
Kalimantan. North Pakar is adjacent to FTB and also has 
environmentally friendly sub-bituminous coal with calorific 
content of around 3,700-4,200 Kcal/kg GAR.
South Pakar is the integration of 6 adjoining mining companies, 
namely SK, OM, SA, BS, AU and CA. South Pakar is south 
of North Pakar and also has environmentally friendly sub-
bituminous coal with calorific content of around 3,000 Kcal/
kg GAR. FTB, North Pakar, and South Pakar are collectively 
called the Tabang Project.
The Tabang Project currently uses the Gunung Sari Special 
Port located on the Belayan River, the Senyiur Special Port 
located at the Kedang Kepala River and Muara Pahu Special 
Port on the Mahakam River.
In total, the Tabang Project produced 43.8 million MT in 2023, 
or 28.6% higher than the 2022 production of 34.0 million MT, 
and 2.7% higher than the budget of 42.6 million MT. This was 
primarily due to increase production in TA was largely the result 
of additional fleets being mobilised by our mining contractor, 
BUMA, in 2023 as well as dry conditions in Q3 and Q4 allowing 
smooth mining operations.  
PT Mamahak Coal Mining (MCM)
MCM has Production Operation IUP located in West Kutai, 
East Kalimantan and has a deposit of bituminous quality coal.
There were no mining operations at MCM in 2023 and 2022 
because the company is currenty focusing on Tabang Project 
expansion.
Mining Operations Support
Balikpapan Coal Terminal (BCT)
BCT is a special coal terminal managed by the Company’s 
subsidiary, DPP, which is located in East Kalimantan. A portion 
of the Company’s coal is exported through the BCT. In 2023, 
the BCT loaded 25.5 million MT of coal for various customers 
or 15.7% higher than the 22.0 million MT loaded in 2022. 
This increase was largely attributable to the increase in coal 
tonnages being received from the Tabang Project.

Aset Lancar 
Penurunan keseluruhan aset lancar sebesar US$499,2 juta 
terutama disebabkan oleh penurunan kas dan setara kas 
sebesar US$1.091,9 juta. Penurunan ini  terutama karena 
penurunan secara keseluruhan pada kas dari operasional 
sebagai akibat dari; penurunan pendapatan penjualan 
dikarenakan oleh penurunan harga jual yang jauh lebih rendah 
dari 2022, peningkatan pembayaran pajak sebesar US$257,5 
juta disebabkan oleh cicilan bulanan yang didasarkan pada 
beban pajak final tahun sebelumnya dan peningkatan sebesar 
US$341,8 juta untuk pembayaran kepada pemasok dan 
karyawan karena kenaikan biaya. Penurunan aset lancar 
sebagian diimbangi oleh kas dan setara kas yang dibatasi 
penggunaannya sebesar US$182,4 juta yang disebabkan oleh 
kebijakan baru pemerintah tentang DHE, kenaikan pada pajak 
dibayar dimuka sebesar US$391,6 juta dan peningkatan pada 
persediaan sebesar US$83,6 juta.
Aset Tidak Lancar
Penurunan aset tidak lancar sebesar US$1,9 juta terutama 
disebabkan oleh penurunan pada pajak dibayar di muka 
sebesar US$143,9 juta dan penurunan pada aset pajak 
tangguhan. Hal ini sebagian diimbangi dengan kenaikan 
aset tetap sebesar US$153,5 juta terutama disebabkan oleh 
penambahan bangunan dan infrastruktur yang sebagian besar 
terdiri dari lanjutan jalan pengangkutan Mahakam 101 km dan 
Jetty Muara Pahu.
Liabilitas
Jumlah liabilitas Perseroan sebesar US$1.465,5 juta pada 
tanggal 31 Desember 2023 lebih rendah 24,9% dibandingkan 
dengan US$1.950,2 juta yang tercatat pada tanggal 31 
Desember 2022. Hal ini disebabkan oleh penurunan liabilitas 
jangka pendek sebesar 26,9% atau setara US$492,9 juta.
Liabilitas Jangka Pendek
Penurunan liabilitas jangka pendek sebesar US$492,9 
juta terutama disebabkan oleh penurunan utang dividen, 
penurunan utang pajak (karena menurunnya laba tahun 
berjalan Perseroan), dan penurunan akrual (yang disebabkan 
oleh pengurangan denda dan akrual kompensasi DMO tahun 
lalu). Hal ini sebagian diimbangi dengan peningkatan pinjaman 
bank dan peningkatan utang usaha.
Liabilitas Jangka Panjang
Liabilitas jangka panjang sebesar US$126,9 juta meningkat 
7% dibanding dengan tahun 2022 terutama karena kenaikan 
pada provisi untuk pembongkaran, pemindahan, reklamasi 
dan restorasi.
Ekuitas
Jumlah ekuitas Perseroan sebesar US$1.978,8 juta turun 
0,8% dari US$1.995,3 juta pada tahun 2022 karena dividen 
yang diumumkan sedikit lebih tinggi dari laba tahun berjalan 
setelah memperhitungkan transaksi dengan kepentingan 
nonpengendali.
Current Assets
The overall decrease in current assets of US$499.2 million 
was mainly due to a decrease in cash and cash equivalents 
of US$1,091.9 million. This decrease was principally due to an 
overall decrease in cash generated from operations as a results 
of; decreased sales revenue caused by the significantly drop in 
average selling prices from 2022, an increase in payments of 
taxes of US$257.5 million due to the monthly installments being 
based on the previous year’s final tax expense and an increase 
of US$341.8 million in payments to suppliers & employees due 
to cost increases. The decrease in current assets was partially 
offset by the increase in restricted cash and cash equivalents 
of US$182.4 million which was caused by the government’s 
new DHE policy, an increase in prepaid taxes of US$391.6 
million and an increase in inventory of US$83.6 million.
Non Current Assets
The decrease in non-current assets of US$1.9 million was 
primarily due to an decrease in prepaid taxes of US$143.9 
million and a decrease in deferred tax assets. This was 
partially offset with an increase in fixed asset of US$153.5 
million principally due to additional building and infrastructure 
which mainly comprised of the continuing spend on the 101 km 
Mahakam haul road and the Muara Pahu Jetty.
Liabilities
The Company’s total liabilities of US$1,465.5 million as at 31 
December 2023 were 24.9% lower than the US$1,950.2 million 
recorded as at 31 December 2022. This was due to a decrease 
in current liabilities of 26.9% or US$492.9 million.
Current Liabilities
The decrease in current liabilities of US$492.9 million was 
mainly due to a decrease in dividend’s payable, a decrease in 
taxes payable (due to the decrease in the Company’s profit for 
the year) and a decrease in accruals (due to a reduction of last 
year DMO fines and compensation accrual). This was partially 
offset with an increase in bank loan and an increase in trade 
payables.
Non Current Liabilities
Non-current liabilities of US$126.9 million increased 7% 
compared to 2022 levels, primarily due to an increase in the 
provision for decommissioning, demobilization, reclamation 
and restoration.
Equity
The Company’s equity of US$1,978.8 million decreased by 
0.8% from US$1,995.3 million in 2022 due to the dividends 
declared being slightly higher than the profit of the year after 
taking into account transactions with non-controlling interests.

Pendapatan 
Pendapatan Perseroan sebesar US$3.581,4 juta pada tahun 
2023 lebih rendah 23,9% dari total pendapatan US$4.703,6 
juta pada tahun 2022. Hal ini terutama dikarenakan penurunan 
harga jual rata-rata batubara yang signifikan dari US$117,9/MT 
pada 2022 menjadi US$75,8/MT tahun 2023.
Beban Pokok Pendapatan
Beban pokok pendapatan pada tahun 2023 sebesar US$1.917,2 
juta lebih tinggi 24,2% dari tahun 2022 sebesar US$1.543,6 
juta. Hal ini terutama disebabkan oleh peningkatan biaya 
overburden karena lebih tingginya rasio pengupasan tanah 
rata-rata tertimbang, peningkatan biaya pertambangan dan 
pengangkutan batubara karena peningkatan produksi batubara, 
peningkatan biaya pengangkutan karena meningkatnya volume 
digabungkan dengan rendahnya debit air di Senyiur untuk suatu 
periode dalam setahun yang mengakibatkan meningkatnya 
penggunaan tongkang kecil dan lebih mahal dan lebih tingginya 
harga  sewa kosong dan peningkatan biaya karyawan sebagai 
akibat dari kenaikan bonus sesuai dengan tahun-tahun 
sebelumnya meningkatkan kinerja keuangan. Hal ini diimbangi 
dengan penurunan pembelian batubara karena harga beli yang 
lebih rendah secara signifikan.
Beban Penjualan
Beban penjualan pada tahun 2023 sebesar US$(28,4) juta jauh 
lebih rendah 120,3% dari tahun 2022 sebesar US$140,4 juta. 
Hal ini terutama disebabkan pembalikan akrual kompensasi 
DMO 2022 yang telah dibebankankan sebelumnya.
Laba Bruto
Laba bruto Perseroan sebesar US$1.664,2 juta pada tahun 
2023 jauh lebih rendah 47,3% dari US$3.160,1 juta pada tahun 
2022. Hal ini disebabkan penurunan harga jual rata-rata yang 
rendah diimbangi dengan sedikit penurunan biaya tunai.
Jumlah Penghasilan Komprehensif
Pada tahun 2023, jumlah penghasilan komprehensif tahun 
berjalan sebesar US$1.336,3 juta atau menurun sebesar 
39,7% dari angka tahun 2022 sebesar US$2.217,5 juta.
Laba Tahun Berjalan
Laba tahun berjalan Perseroan sebesar US$1.279,6 juta 
lebih rendah 44,4% dari US$2.301,6 juta pada tahun 2022. 
Penurunan tersebut terutama disebabkan penurunan harga 
jual rata-rata yang sebagian diimbangi peningkatan volume 
penjualan dan sedikit penurunan biaya tunai.
The Company’s financial performance based on comprehensive 
income can be summarized as follows:
Revenue
The Company’s revenue of US$3,581.4 million in 2023 was 
23.9% lower than total revenue of US$4,703.6 million in 2022. 
This was principally due to a significant decrease in the average 
selling price of coal from US$117.9/MT in 2022 to US$75.8/MT 
in 2023.
Cost of Revenue
Cost of revenue in 2023 of US$1,917.2 million was 24.2% 
higher than US$1,543.6 million in 2022. This was mainly due 
to an increase in overburden removal costs due to the higher 
average stripping ratio, an increase in coal mining and hauling 
due to higher coal production, an increase in barging costs 
due to increased volumes combined with low water level’s 
at Senyiur for a period of the year which cause an increased 
usage of smaller and more expensive  barges and higher dead 
freight costs and an increase in employee costs as a result 
of an increase bonus in line with the previous years increase 
financial performance. This was partially offset with a decrease 
in coal purchases due to the significantly lower purchase price.
Selling Expenses
Selling expenses in 2023 of US$(28.4) million was 120.3% 
lower than US$140.4 million in 2022. This was mainly due to 
the reversal of the 2022  DMO compensation accrual that had 
already been previously charged.
Gross Profit
The Company’s gross profit of US$1,664.2 million in 2023 was 
47.3% lower than the 2022 gross profit of US$3,160.1 million. 
This was mainly due to the significantly lower average sales 
price partially offset with slightly lower cash costs.
Total Comprehensive Income
In 2023, the Company’s total comprehensive income for the 
year of US$1,336.3 million was 39.7% lower than the 2022 
total comprehensive income of US$2,217.5 million.
Profit for the Year
The Company’s profit for the year of US$1,279.6 million was 
44.4% lower than the 2022 net profit of US$2,301.6 million. 
The decrease was caused by the significantly lower average 
sales price partially offset with an increase in sales volumes 
and slightly lower cash costs.

2.	 Coal Market Outlook
	
The coal market outlook for 2024 continues to remain 
relatively finely balanced between supply and demand. 
This means that shocks to either side could have a dramatic 
impact on coal prices during the year. We believe the 
following topics and events will be items to watch out for in 
2024:
(i)	 China imported record volumes of coal in 2023 which was 
over 50% higher than the previous year and accounted 
for approximately 25% of the total seaborne thermal 
coal market.  Any material variations to Chinese imports 
would obviously have an immediate and pronounced 
impact on coal market prices;
(ii)	Weather related events will continue to have impacts 
particularly on the supplies out of Australia and 
Indonesia. Both El Nino and La Nina events in recent 
years have had dramatic impacts on the supplies of coal. 
These impacts can be either positive or negative and can 
impact the mine and the logistics chain required to bring 
the product to port in different ways. For example, dry 
weather is good for coal production but is not good for 
barging; and
(iii)	Geopolitical tensions are flaring up in a number of 
countries at the moment with Ukraine/Russia, Israel/
Gaza and US/Iran being at the top of the list.  All of 
these locations are quite influential to the supply of oil 
and in some cases gas.  Disruptions to the oil and gas 
markets could have knock-on impacts on the coal market 
particularly where oil and gas prices spike dramatically 
when tensions escalate.

Whilst rainfall and/or more specifically water levels on 
the Kedang Kepala River did present some issues for the 
Company in 2023, we do not expect that this will be as a 
material issue going forward as we now have alternate 
barge loading facilities constructed directly on the Mahakam 
River. Whilst Senyiur will continue to be an important barge 
loading facility for us going forward, in the event that water 
levels dictate either limited or no barging from here then 
we have the ability to switch our trucking to the new Muara 
Pahu facility that will not face those same water depth 
limitations.  Whilst we may lose some capacity in doing so 
as the Muara Pahu facility is a longer haul, we only expect 
that this will occur for limited periods during any extremely 
dry season.
5.	 Inflation
	
Inflation continues to be a hot topic and we have seen 
certain indices increase quite significantly in 2023. This is 
expected to continue into 2024, however such impacts will 
be industry-wide and impact all coal producers. Those coal 
producers that occupy the middle and upper echelons of the 
cost curve will obviously be impacted the worst and this may 
lead to certain mines or deposits becoming uneconomic 
and closing.  Whilst the Company will not be immune to the 
impacts of inflation, given that we are in the front part of the 
lowest quartile of costs on the energy-adjusted seaborne 
thermal coal market, we will not feel the impacts of such 
cost increases as others will.
6.	 Summary
	
In short, we are cautiously optimistic that coal prices will 
continue to remain at reasonably robust levels compared 
to historical averages. In the longer term, we believe 
Newcastle prices of approximately US$100 and ICI4 in the 
low-US$50’s will become the floor that if breached will see 
many producers operate in the red. In this event, production 
will be cut thereby providing a solid baseline of support 
for the market price. Under this environment, with our low 
cost base, we will be well protected and with our strong 
growth profile going forward we believe we are well placed to 
outperform the industry standard.

Pasar batubara global mengalami pergolakan selama tiga 
tahun. Permintaan menurun tajam selama pandemi Covid,

Saat ini, batubara masih menjadi sumber energi terbesar untuk 
pembangkit listrik, pembuatan baja, dan produksi semen, 
sehingga menjaga peran intinya tetap terjaga dalam dunia

memenuhi target iklim internasional.
Permintaan batubara global mencapai rekor tertingginya pada

Pada 2023, permintaan batubara mengalami penurunan di

Industry Review
The global coal market has experienced turbulence for three 
years. Demand dropped sharply during the Covid pandemic, 
only to leap during the post-Covid rebound and following 
Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. In 2022, global coal demand 
reached its highest level ever. Today, coal remains the 
largest energy source for electricity generation, steelmaking 
and cement production – maintaining a central role in the 
world economy. At the same time, coal is the largest source 
of man-made carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions, and curbing 
consumption is essential to meeting international climate 
targets. 
Global coal demand reached a record high in 2022 amid the 
global energy crisis, rising by 4% year-on-year to 8.42 billion 
MT. 
In 2023, coal demand declined in almost all advanced 
economies. The biggest drops in consumption occurred in the 
European Union and the United States.  
The El Nino event has been associated with less rain-related 
mining disruption in Indonesia, Colombia, and South Africa 
than during the La Nina period from 2020-2023. If El Nino 
conditions persist into the middle of year 2024, they could 
increase the prospect of hotter and drier weather in India, 
which could support air-conditioning demand and negatively 
impact monsoon rainfalls, weighing on hydropower generation 
and supporting demand for coal imports.
Europe’s energy crisis continues to impact consumers as the 
volatile oil and gas markets remain under pressure from current 
conflicts and a weak global economy. Whilst crude produced 
some price swings, it has been, of late, the natural gas prices 
that have absorbed most of the impact as a result of the conflict 
in the Middle East.
European imports collapsed in 2023 amid low demand and 
plentiful stocks. However, growth in China, which recorded 
its highest imports ever in 2023, will more than offset these 
declines. Despite strong domestic production in China, 
exporters have benefitted from robust demand and massive 
stock building due to energy security concerns.

Permintaan 
Pada tahun 2023, permintaan batubara global mengalami 
peningkatan tipis sebesar 1,4% walaupun mencapai nilai 
tertingginya sepanjang waktu sebesar 8.536 juta MT. 
Pertumbuhan permintaan batubara kehilangan momentumnya 
karena prospek ekonomi yang bersifat moderat, yang diimbangi 
dengan melemahnya faktor yang mendorong pembangkit 
listrik tenaga batubara pada 2022. Sementara itu, permintaan 
batubara global diharapkan untuk terus bergerak ke arah timur, 
dengan Tiongkok, India, dan negara-negara ASEAN, yang 
apabila digabungkan mengonsumsi tiga per empat permintaan

35% lebih rendah dibandingkan pangsa gabungan Uni Eropa 
dan Amerika Serikat pada waktu tersebut.
Secara absolut, permintaan batubara pada 2023 telah 
meningkat paling kuat di Tiongkok (naik 220 juta MT, atau 
4,9%), diikuti oleh India (naik 98 juta MT, atau 8%), dan 
Indonesia (naik 23 juta MT, atau 11%). 
Demand 
In 2023, global coal demand has increased only marginally 
by 1.4%, albeit reaching a new all-time high of about 8,536 
million MT. Coal demand growth is losing its momentum due 
to lukewarm economic prospects paired with the weakening 
of the factors that pushed coal-fired power generation in 
2022. Meanwhile, global coal demand is expected to continue 
moving eastwards, with China, India, and ASEAN countries 
combined consuming three-quarters of global demand. At the 
start of the century, that share was around 35%, lower than the 
combined share of the European Union and the United States 
at that time. 
In absolute terms, coal demand in 2023  has increased most 
strongly in China (up 220 million MT, or 4.9%), followed by 
India (up 98 million MT, or 8%) and Indonesia (up 23 million 
MT, or 11%).

2024 Forecast
In 2023, coal prices remain at high levels as the Russia/
Ukraine war continues and the demand for energy increases 
with China returning from strict pandemic lock down 
measures. Thermal coal will still be commodity of choice for 
China after the importance of boosting coal output for energy 
security was reiterated during China’s national congress in 
October 2022. Developments in China will give the largest 
impact on the outlook for global coal demand, given that 
China’s power sector accounts for one third of global coal 
consumption.
Chinese thermal coal imports are expected to remain strong 
in 2024 after likely hitting an all-time high in 2023, supported 
by strong power sector demand.
The power demand is expected to remain strong next 
year with economists pencilling in an uptick in China’s 
manufacturing sector, although the pace of overall economic 
growth might ease. The Chinese economy is expected to 
grow at 4.6pc in 2024. Chinese infrastructure, real estate, 
and manufacturing sectors might continue to see steady 
investments next year, coupled with favourable policy and 
fiscal support to buoy economic growth. 
In terms of power demand, thermal power accounts for the 
bulk of China’s electricity generation, and coal-fired power 
has the largest share in the thermal power mix. The country’s 
thermal power output during the first 11 months of the year 
rose by 5.7pc from a year earlier to 5,617.8 TWh, accounting 
for around 70pc of China’s overall generation.
Coal-based utilities are expected to continue raising 
generation to meet any rise in power demand next year. 
The onset of the drier El Nino weather phenomenon 
could prolong heatwaves in the wider region, which could 
exacerbate residential power demand and China’s reliance 
on coal-fired generation. The weather pattern could also 
potentially affect seasonal rains and pressure hydro-power 
generation. Demand for high-CV coal could also increase 
from non-power sectors, such as cement.

Terkait DMO
Pada tanggal 17 November 2023, Menteri Energi dan Sumber 
Daya Mineral (“MESDM”) mengeluarkan Keputusan Menteri 
No 399.K/MB.01/MEM.B/2023 tentang Perubahan Atas 
Keputusan Menteri Energi Dan Sumber Daya Mineral Nomor 
267.K/MB.01/MEM.B/2022 tentang Pemenuhan Kebutuhan 
Batubara Dalam Negeri (“Kepmen 399”).
Kepmen 399 ini menetapkan persentase penjualan batubara 
untuk kebutuhan dalam negeri terhadap Pemegang IUP tahap 
Operasi Produksi, IUPK tahap Operasi Produksi, PKP2B tahap 
Operasi Produksi, dan IUPK sebagai Kelanjutan Operasi 
Kontrak/Perjanjian Komoditas Batubara (selanjutnya disebut 
“Pemegang Izin atau Perjanjian”) sebesar 25% dari realisasi 
produksi batubara pada tahun berjalan, bagi:
a.	Penyediaan tenaga listrik untuk kepentingan umum dan 
kepentingan sendiri; dan
b.	Bahan baku/bahan bakar untuk industri.
Dalam hal Pemegang Izin atau Perjanjian tidak memenuhi 
persentase penjualan dalam negeri tersebut, maka dikenai 
kewajiban pembayaran dana kompensasi.
Dana kompensasi dihitung dengan tarif kompensasi (US$/MT) 
berdasarkan kualitas batubara dan perubahan Harga Batubara
Acuan (HBA) dikali kewajiban pasokan domestik dikurangi 
dengan realisasi pemenuhan kebutuhan batubara dalam 
negeri.
Basis perhitungan persentase DMO yang diatur dalam 
Kepmen 399 ini berbeda dari Kepmen sebelumnya yang

Related to DMO
On 17 November 2023, the Minister of Energy and Mineral 
Resources (“MoEMR”) issued Ministerial Decree No. 399.K/
MB.01/MEM.B/2023 on Amendments to the Decree of the 
Minister of Energy and Mineral Resources Number 267.K/
MB.01/MEM.B/2022 
concerning 
Coal 
Domestic 
Market 
Obligation (“MoEMR 399”).
This MoEMR 399 stipulates the percentage of coal sales for 
domestic needs towards IUP Holders in the Production Operation 
stage, IUPK for the Production Operation stage, PKP2B for the 
Production Operation stage, and IUPK as a Continuation of 
Coal Commodity Contract/Agreement Operations (hereinafter 
referred to as “Permit or Agreement Holders”), which is 25% of 
therealization of coal production in the current year, for: 
a.	Provision of electric power for public and self-interest; and 
b.	Raw materials/fuels for industry.
In the event the Permit or Agreement Holder does not meet 
the domestic sales percentage, then it is subject to payment 
obligation in the form of compensation fund. 
The compensation fund is calculated at a compensation 
rate (US$/MT) based on the coal quality and changes in the 
reference coal price (HBA) multiplied by domestic supply 
obligations which are deducted by the realization of domestic 
coal obligation.
The basis for calculating the DMO percentage under MoEMR 
399 is different from the previous Ministerial Decree which 
based the calculation on the Company’s annual work plan 
and budget (RKAB). The provisions for fine sanctions in the 
previous regulations were removed, and companies that do 
not meet the DMO sales percentage are only subject to the 
obligation to pay the compensation fund.
Related to Foreign Exchange Export 
On 12 July 2023, the Government issued Government 
Regulation No. 36/2023 concerning Foreign Exchange Export 
Proceeds from Natural Resources Exploitation, Management, 
and/or Processing Activities (DHE SDA), which comes into 
effect on 1 August 2023  
In accordance with PP Number 36 of 2023, there are four 
commodity sectors that are required to include foreign 
exchange in the form of DHE SDA into the Indonesian financial 
system, namely the mining, plantation, forestry and fisheries 
sectors. DHE SDA from this sector must be included and placed 
in the Indonesian financial system through a Special DHE SDA 
Account at the Indonesian Export Financing Institution; and/
or Banks that conduct business activities in foreign currency.
