Tinjauan Makro Ekonomi Global
Sepanjang tahun 2024, dunia menghadapi berbagai 
tantangan yang kompleks dan beragam. Pertama, 
konflik geopolitik. Kedua, perubahan kepemimpinan 
politik di banyak negara. Ketiga, proyeksi ekonomi 
global dan negara-negara besar di dunia yang masih 
lemah. Saat ini dunia berada dalam situasi global 
yang tidak pasti. Efek perang Ukraina-Rusia masih 
terasa. Konflik geopolitik di Timur Tengah dan di Laut 
Cina Selatan juga masih belum usai, bahkan ada 
kecenderungan kian memanas.
Dana Moneter Internasional (International Monetary 
Fund/IMF) memproyeksikan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global stagnan di level 3,2% secara tahunan (year 
on year) untuk tahun ini. Sedangkan perekonomian 
Amerika Serikat tumbuh 2,5% yoy seiring solidnya 
kinerja ekonomi domestik. Pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global tetap stabil dan diperkirakan berkisar 2,5%, 
peningkatan sebesar 0,2% dari proyeksi IMF pada 
Oktober 2024. Perkiraan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global terus membaik hingga 3,2% di 2024. IMF 
memperkirakan pertumbuhan ekonomi yang lebih baik 
di AS, di mana kebijakan moneter ketat masih bekerja 
dan menunggu untuk diturunkan.
Tiongkok yang selama beberapa tahun terakhir dapat 
tumbuh di atas 10%, kini mengalami perlambatan laju 
ekonomi dan tumbuh di bawah 5%. Berbagai dinamika 
situasi global ini sedikit banyak memberi dampak 
terhadap perekonomian Indonesia.
Tinjauan Makro Ekonomi Nasional
Ditengah ketidakpastian ekonomi global, kinerja 
ekonomi Indonesia masih tetap solid dan lebih 
baik dibandingkan beberapa negara maju maupun 
berkembang 
lainnya.

Q4-2024, 
Indonesia 
mencatatkan pertumbuhan ekonomi sebesar 5,02% 
(yoy), lebih tinggi dibandingkan peer countries 
seperti Singapura (4,3%), Arab Saudi (4,4%), dan 
Malaysia (4,8%). Sementara itu di sepanjang tahun 
2024, 
pertumbuhan

Indonesia 
mampu 
membukukan capaian sebesar 5,03%.
Global Economic Macro Review
Throughout 2024, the world grappled with a series 
of complex and diverse challenges. These included 
escalating geopolitical conflicts, shifts in political 
leadership across multiple nations, and continued 
weaknesses in the global economic landscape. 
The 
global 
situation 
remained 
uncertain, 
with 
lingering repercussions from the Ukraine-Russia war. 
Additionally, geopolitical tensions in the Middle East 
and the South China Sea persisted, with indications 
that they may intensify further.
The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projected 
global economic growth to stagnate at 3.2% year-on-
year in 2024. Meanwhile, the United States economy 
expanded by 2.5% year-on-year, reflecting the 
resilience of domestic economic performance. Global 
growth remained stable, estimated at approximately 
2.5%, marking a 0.2% increase from the IMF’s October 
2024 projection. Encouragingly, forecasts suggested 
that global economic growth would improve to 3.2% 
in 2024. The IMF anticipated stronger U.S. economic 
performance, with tight monetary policies still in place, 
awaiting for potential easing.
China, 
which 
previously 
achieved 
growth 
rates 
exceeding 10% in past years, was experiencing an 
economic slowdown, with growth falling below 5%. 
These global economic dynamics inevitably impacted 
Indonesia’s economic conditions, highlighting the 
interconnectedness of global markets and regional 
economies.
Macroeconomic Review
Amid 
ongoing 
global 
economic 
uncertainty, 
Indonesia’s 
economy 
demonstrated 
resilience, 
performing better than many advanced and emerging 
economies. In Q4-2024, Indonesia recorded year-

that of peer countries such as Singapore (4.3%), Saudi 
Arabia (4.4%), and Malaysia (4.8%). In 2024, Indonesia 
achieved a stable annual growth rate of 5.03%.

Palm Oil Industry
In 2024, global vegetable oil production declined, 
primarily due to reductions in palm oil, sunflower oil, and 
rapeseed oil output. This decline triggered an increase 
in vegetable oil prices, driven by concerns over supply 
shortages, while consumption continued to rise amid 
post-pandemic economic recovery and population 
growth. In contrast, soybean oil production remained 
abundant, leading to a drop in prices, with its average 
value falling below that of palm oil. As a result, key 
consumer markets, particularly China and India, began 
shifting toward soybean oil to meet their feedstock 
and cooking oil needs. Based on Dutch FOB ex-mill 
terms, the average price of soybean oil in 2024 was 
recorded at US$1,045 per ton, while palm oil, according 
to CIF Rotterdam terms, averaged US$1,084 per ton. 
Despite the increase in soybean oil availability, global 
vegetable oil demand remains heavily reliant on palm 
oil, which continued to dominate the market with a 36% 
share. Indonesia accounted for 51% of total palm oil 
exports worldwide, solidifying its role as the backbone 
of global palm oil-based food supply. However, 
Indonesia’s palm oil production has stagnated for the 
past four years, maintaining an average annual output 
of 50 million tons. Experts predict this trend may soon 
turn into a decline.

Stagnasi produksi kelapa sawit Indonesia disebabkan 
terutama 
karena 
sisi 
agronomi, 
yakni 
tanaman 
yang tua dan memasuki masa replanting, serangga 
penyerbuk yang kini produktivitasnya melemah serta 
tata kelola perkebunan yang belum optimal utamanya 
di kebun petani yang secara komposisi menguasai 
40% kepemilikan perkebunan kelapa sawit. Sebagai 
komoditas strategis nasional, berbagai upaya telah 
dilakukan 
pemerintah 
untuk 
mempertahankan 
nilai kompetitif sawit Indonesia. Pemerintah telah 
membuat program Peremajaan Sawit Rakyat (PSR) 
bagi kebun petani dengan memberikan insentif untuk 
proses replanting namun hingga kini implementasi 
secara tahunan hanya berkisar 30% dari target. 
Selain itu R&D terus dikembangkan secara agronomi 
maupun pengembangan produk hilir kelapa sawit. 
Untuk menjaga kebutuhan minyak goreng domestik, 
sejak tahun 2022 hingga saat ini Pemerintah masih 
memberlakukan 
kebijakan 
Domestic 
Market 
Obligation (DMO) yang diimplementasikan melalui 
Domestic Price Obligation (DPO). Terhadap komoditas 
yang 
menjadi 
salah 
satu 
penggerak 
ekonomi 
dalam mewujudkan visi Indonesia Emas 2045 yakni 
pertumbuhan ekonomi diharapkan mencapai 7-8% 
maka pengembangan hilir industri kelapa sawit 
terus digalakkan. Pada tahun 2024, pemerintah 
telah sepenuhnya mengimplementasikan kebijakan 
bauran solar dengan minyak sawit sebesar 35% atau 
dikenal dengan kebijakan mandatori Biodiesel (B35). 
Mengakhiri tahun 2024, pemerintah juga telah merilis 
program mandatori B40 yang akan diimplementasikan 
di tahun 2025. Sebelumnya kebijakan B35 telah 
meningkatkan konsumsi domestik hingga dua juta 
ton dan diperkirakan akan bertambah sebesar empat 
juta ton dengan implementasi B40 di tengah stagnasi 
produksi sawit nasional.
Sustainable focused development di industri kelapa 
sawit secara konsisten diimplementasikan dengan 
kebijakan mandatori sertifikat Indonesian Sustainable 
Palm Oil (ISPO). Pemerintah Indonesia juga menerapkan 
berbagai kebijakan yang menjadi tantangan tersendiri 
untuk industri kelapa sawit seperti kebijakan Fasilitas 
Pembangunan Kebun Masyarakat Sekitar (FPKM) 
serta penerbitan keterlanjuran kebun sawit di kawasan 
hutan yang menjadi bagian dari Undang-Undang Cipta 
Kerja (UUCK), maupun kebijakan-kebijakan lainnya. 
Sementara itu, proteksionisme berupa trade maupun 
non trade barrier masih menjadi tantangan yang harus 
dihadapi industri ini. Meskipun kebijakan European 
Union Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) telah ditunda 
di tahun 2024, namun perjuangan Indonesia di World 
Trade Organization (WTO) belum berakhir.
The stagnation in Indonesia’s palm oil production 
was largely attributed to agronomic challenges, 
including aging plantations entering the replanting 
phase, declining productivity of pollinating insects, 
and inefficient plantation management, particularly 
among smallholder farmers, who control 40% of 
palm oil plantation ownership. Recognizing palm oil’s 
strategic importance, the government has undertaken 
various initiatives to safeguard its competitive value. 
The Smallholder Palm Oil Rehabilitation Program (PSR) 
was introduced to incentivize replanting; however, 
annual implementation has only reached about 30% 
of the target. Concurrently, research and development 
efforts continue to focus on enhancing agronomic 
practices 
and 
advancing 
downstream 
product 
development. To ensure a stable domestic cooking 
oil supply, the government has upheld the Domestic 
Market Obligation (DMO) policy since 2022, enforced 
through the Domestic Price Obligation (DPO). As palm 
oil remains a key economic driver in Indonesia’s long-
term vision of becoming a developed nation by 2045, 
with an anticipated economic growth rate of 7–8%, 
the government continues prioritizing the expansion 
of downstream industries. In 2024, the mandatory 
Biodiesel (B35) policy—requiring a 35% palm oil blend 
in diesel—was fully implemented. Later in the year, the 
government launched the B40 mandate, scheduled 
for implementation in 2025. The B35 policy previously 
increased domestic palm oil consumption by two 
million tons, and the B40 policy is expected to further 
boost demand by an additional four million tons, 
despite stagnating national production.
Sustainable palm oil development continued through 
the mandatory Indonesian Sustainable Palm Oil 
(ISPO) certification policy. However, the industry 
faced additional regulatory challenges, including the 
Community-Based Plantation Development Facility 
(FPKM) initiative, the issuance of palm oil plantation 
permits in forest areas under the Job Creation Law 
(UUCK), and various other new policy measures. 
Meanwhile, trade protectionism—manifested through 
tariff and non-tariff barriers—remained a significant 
challenge. Although the European Union Deforestation 
Regulation (EUDR) had been postponed until 2024, 
Indonesia’s efforts at the World Trade Organization 
(WTO) to address these restrictions were ongoing.

ANALISIS OPERASI PER SEGMEN
OPERATIONAL ANALYSIS BY SEGMENT
Kegiatan operasional Grup FAP Agri meliputi pengelolaan 
perkebunan kelapa sawit, pabrik pengolahan kelapa 
sawit dan pabrik pengolahan inti sawit. Produk yang 
dihasilkan terdiri dari Tandan Buah Segar, Minyak Sawit 
Mentah, Minyak Inti Sawit, Inti Sawit, dan Bungkil Inti Sawit.
Sawit merupakan tanaman tahunan yang memiliki 
tahapan produksi terdiri dari tahap tanaman belum 
menghasilkan (TBM) dan tanaman menghasilkan (TM). 
Tanaman sawit mulai menghasilkan buah yang sesuai 
untuk pada umur 4 tahun dan akan terus berproduksi 
secara optimum hingga 30 tahun. Produktivitas kelapa 
sawit akan mengalami peningkatan dan mencapai 
puncak produksi di antara umur 7 (tujuh) sampai 18 
(delapan belas) tahun. Adapun penurunan produksi 
akan terjadi setelah tanaman melewati usia produktif, 
yaitu di atas 30 tahun sehingga harus dilakukan 
peremajaan melalui penebangan pohon sawit tua 
untuk diganti dengan tanaman sawit baru. Oleh karena 
itu, produktivitas dan kinerja Perusahaan dipengaruhi 
oleh umur tanaman kelapa sawit.
Perkebunan Kelapa Sawit
Operasional FAP Agri mencakup 9 (sembilan) konsesi 
perkebunan sawit, 6 (enam) Pabrik Kelapa Sawit, 
dan 1 (satu) pabrik Pengolahan Kernel. Perusahaan 
mengelola lahan perkebunan kelapa sawit yang berada 
di Kalimantan Utara, Kalimantan Timur, dan Riau dengan 
luas konsesi lebih dari 110.000 ha. Pada tahun 2024, 
luas area perkebunan yang telah ditanam mencapai 
78.315 ha kebun inti dan 10.386 ha kebun pola plasma. 
Pada tahun 2024, luas lahan tertanam ialah seluas 
88.702 ha.
Lahan tertanam yang berlokasi di Riau sebesar 3.282 
ha, seluruhnya merupakan tanaman menghasilkan. 
Adapun di Kalimantan Utara luas lahan yang tertanam 
sebesar 50.066 Ha, seluruhnya merupakan tanaman 
menghasilkan. Sementara, di Kalimantan Timur, luas 
lahan tertanam sebesar 35.354 ha, dengan luas 
tanaman menghasilkan sebesar 34.286 ha, dan luas 
tanaman belum menghasilkan seluas 1.068 ha.
FAP Agri memproduksi Tandan Buah Segar sebagai 
salah satu komoditas utamanya. Produksi Tandan 
Buah Segar tahun 2024 ialah sebesar 1.312.257 
ton, meningkat sebesar 50.437 ton atau 4,00% 
dibandingkan tahun 2023 sebesar 1.261.820 ton. Hal ini 
terutama dipengaruhi oleh peningkatan luas tanaman 
menghasilkan dan umur rata-rata tanaman.
The operational activities of the FAP Agri Group include 
the management of oil palm plantations, palm oil mills and 
palm kernel processing plants. The products produced 
consist of Fresh Fruit Bunches, Crude Palm Oil, Palm 
Kernel Oil, Palm Kernel, and Palm Kernel Expeller.
Oil palm is an annual plant with production stages 
consisting of the immature plant stage (TBM) and 
the mature plant stage (TM). Oil palm plants begin to 
produce fruit at the age of 4 years and will continue 
to produce optimally for up to 30 years. Oil palm 
productivity will increase and reach peak production 
between the ages of 7 (seven) and 18 (eighteen) 
years. The decrease in production will occur after the 
plant passes the productive age, which is above 30 
years. Rejuvenation must be carried out by cutting 
down old oil palm trees to be replaced with new oil 
palm plants. Therefore, the Company’s productivity 
and performance are influenced by the age of the oil 
palm plants.
Oil Palm Plantations
FAP 
Agri 
operates 
nine 
oil 
palm 
plantation 
concessions, six Palm Oil Mills, and one Kernel 
Processing Plant. The company manages plantations 
across North Kalimantan, East Kalimantan, and Riau, 
covering a total concession area of over 110,000 
hectares. By 2024, the planted plantation area had 
expanded to 88,702 hectares, comprising 78,315 
hectares of nucleus plantations and 10,386 hectares 
of plasma-pattern plantations.
In Riau, the entire planted area of 3,282 hectares 
consists of producing plants. In North Kalimantan, 
the 50,066-hectare plantation is fully composed 
of producing plants. Meanwhile, in East Kalimantan, 
the planted area spans 35,354 hectares, with 34,286 
hectares dedicated to producing plants and 1,068 
hectares consisting of immature plants.
One of FAP Agri’s primary commodities is fresh fruit 
bunches. In 2024, the Company produced 1,312,257 
tons of fresh fruit bunches, marking a 4.00% increase 
(or 50,437 tons) compared to the 1,261,820 tons 
recorded in 2023. This growth was largely driven by 
the expansion of productive plantation areas and the 
increasing maturity of oil palm trees.

effects of market restrictions. China, one of the largest

the United States. Although China met its 5% growth

world’s largest producer and exporter of palm oil, 
Indonesia continues to grapple with low productivity, 
particularly as international efforts to boost production 
and replanting programs intensify competition in 
vegetable oil markets. Meanwhile, domestic demand 
for palm oil is on the rise, particularly with the shift from 
the B35 biodiesel program to the new B40 mandate.

remains high, potentially limiting Indonesia’s export 
performance. The Domestic Market Obligation (DMO)

Transactions with Affiliated and/or Related 
Parties
In the normal course of business, FAP Agri conducts 
transactions with related parties. All transactions with 
related parties are conducted on the same terms and 
conditions as those with non-related parties. Related 
parties are persons or entities related to the Group:
1.	
A person or immediate family member is related to 
the reporting entity if that person:
a.	 a. Has control or joint control over the reporting 
entity,
b.	 b. Have significant influence over the reporting 
person or reporting entity, or
c.	 c. Key management personnel of the reporting 
entity or a parent of the reporting entity.
2.	 An entity is related to the reporting entity if it fulfills 
any of the following:
a.	 The entity and the reporting entity are 
members of the same group (meaning that the 
parent entity, subsidiaries, and subsequent 
subsidiaries are related to each other),
b.	 One entity is an associate or joint venture of 
another entity (or an associate or joint venture 
that is a member of a group of which the other 
entity is a member),
c.	 Both entities are joint ventures of the same third 
party,
d.	 One entity is a joint venture of a third entity and 
the other entity is an associate of the third entity,
e.	 The entity is a post-employment benefit plan for 
employee benefits of either the reporting entity 
or an entity related to the reporting entity. If the 
reporting entity is the entity that administers the 
plan, then the sponsoring entity is also related 
to the reporting entity,
f.	
Entities controlled or jointly controlled by a 
person identified in (a),
g.	 The person identified in (a) item (1) has significant 
influence over the entity or the key management 
personnel of the entity (or a parent of the entity),
h.	 The entity or a member of a group of which 
the entity is a part, provides key management 
personnel services to the reporting entity or to 
a parent of the reporting entity.
All material transactions and balances with related 
parties are disclosed in the consolidated financial 
statements.
