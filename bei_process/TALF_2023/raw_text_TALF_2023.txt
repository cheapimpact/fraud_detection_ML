General Economic Overview 
In 2023, Indonesia's economy will continue to
recover sustainably and grow steadily at
around 5.05%. In the fourth quarter of 2023,
the resilience of Indonesia's domestic economic
activity continues. This positive achievement
can be seen in the continued expansion of the
Indonesian Manufacturing PMI, high growth in
electricity consumption in the business and
industrial sectors, as well as the continued
maintenance of the consumer confidence index
and 
real 
sales 
index. 
Meanwhile, 
the
unemployment rate decreased to 5.32% and
the poverty rate reached 9.36%. This stable
growth is supported by domestic demand, and
people's purchasing power which is well
maintained, as well as a balance of payments
that will always be positive throughout 2023.
Global economic growth in 2023 will slow down
compared to the previous year. This is caused
by 
ongoing 
political 
and 
economic
fragmentation and aggressive monetary policy
tightening in developed countries. The World
Bank in its Global Economic Prospect published
in January 2024 estimates that global economic
growth will slow down from the previous 3% in
2022 to only 2.6% year on year in 2023 and
decline again to weaken to 2.4% in 2024.
Economic dynamics in Europe, the US and
China also triggered global financial market
volatility, both in the stock market, debt
securities 
and 
exchange 
rate 
markets,
accompanied by outflows from the majority of
emerging market financial markets, including
the Indonesian financial market.
Indonesia's economic growth continues to be
stable, driven by strong domestic demand. The
Indonesian economy in 2023 will grow by 5.05
percent, lower than the achievement in 2022
which experienced growth of 5.31 percent. In
terms of production, the highest growth
occurred 
in 
the 
Transportation 
and
Warehousing Business Field at 13.96 percent.
