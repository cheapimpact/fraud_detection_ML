Macroeconomic Review
Tinjauan Makroekonomi
In 2024, the global economy faces challenges amid geopolitical 
uncertainty, changes in monetary policy, and a slowdown in 
several key sectors. The International Monetary Fund (IMF) 
projects global economic growth of 3.2%, slightly down from 
3.3% the previous year. The decline in global inflation from 9.4% 
in 2022 to 3.5% by the end of 2024 provides scope for central 
banks in various countries to ease interest rate policies, supporting 
the recovery of the real sector. Meanwhile, the Indonesian 
economy recorded growth of 5.03%, driven by household 
consumption which increased by 4.98% and investment which 
grew by 5.03% on an annual basis. National economic stability 
is maintained through fiscal policies that focus on strengthening 
infrastructure, accelerating the green economy, and digital 
transformation.
The global automotive industry is showing a gradual recovery 
despite facing supply chain challenges and fluctuations in raw 
material prices. Demand for electric vehicles (EVs) continues 
to experience significant growth, driven by government incentives 
and increasing public awareness of sustainability. However, in 
Indonesia, the automotive industry is under pressure with a 
13.9% drop in car sales compared to the previous year, to 865,723 
units. However, automotive exhibitions such as the Indonesia 
International Motor Show (IIMS) continue to show public 
enthusiasm, especially for electric and hybrid vehicles. Government 
support through tax incentives for environmentally friendly 
vehicles is expected to accelerate the adoption of clean energy-
based vehicles in the future.
In line with the slowdown in the automotive industry, the tire 
industry sector in Indonesia has also experienced a decline in 
performance. Tire sales are recorded to have decreased by up 
to 10% throughout 2024, reflecting the challenges facing the 
domestic automotive market. However, companies in this sector 
continue to strive to innovate through the development of 
environmentally friendly tire products and raw material efficiency 
technologies to meet the needs of an increasingly environmentally 
conscious market. Demand for tires in the commercial vehicle 
segment remains stable, supported by increased logistics 
activity, while expansion into international markets is the main 
strategy in dealing with domestic market pressures.
