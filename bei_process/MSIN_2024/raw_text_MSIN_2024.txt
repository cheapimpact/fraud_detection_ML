The media industry in Indonesia is diverse, with significant 
investment across multiple channels, reflecting the 
country’s vast and growing population, which exceeds 
270 million. While the landscape has shifted in recent 
years, traditional media, especially Free-to-Air (FTA) 
television, remains a dominant force. FTA TV continues 
to attract the highest share of advertising spend due to 
its extensive reach and widespread popularity. National 
TV networks maintain a stronghold in the market, 
with their broad viewership across all age groups and 
demographics, both urban and rural. Despite a growing 
preference for digital media, the large-scale penetration 
of FTA TV in Indonesian households ensures its central 
role in advertising strategies.
However, the rapid expansion of digital platforms is 
reshaping the media industry, especially with the 
increasing availability of smartphones and internet 
access. Digital advertising has witnessed a significant 
uptick in recent years, as brands shift their focus towards 
platforms like Google, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, 
and increasingly, e-commerce sites like Tokopedia 
and Shopee. With the rise of Indonesia’s digital-savvy 
population, advertisers are investing heavily in targeted 
and data-driven campaigns, capitalizing on the ability 
to reach specific consumer segments in real time. Social 
media platforms, in particular, are gaining prominence 
as an essential avenue for brands to engage with the 
younger demographic, which is becoming the core of 
consumer spending in Indonesia.
Indonesia’s 
over-the-top 
(OTT) 
industry 
has 
also 
experienced rapid growth in recent years, driven 
by increasing internet penetration and affordable 
smartphones. 
Domestic 
platforms 
have 
gained 
significant traction, offering diverse content ranging 
from local dramas and sports to international films and 
series. Local players are increasingly investing in original 
content to cater to cultural preferences and compete with 
global giants. With supportive government initiatives 
around digital transformation and a growing appetite 
for streaming services, the Indonesian OTT market holds 
strong long-term prospects, poised to become one of 
Southeast Asia’s leading digital entertainment hubs.
Industri

mencerminkan populasi negara yang besar dan terus 
berkembang, yang melebihi 270 juta masyarakat. 
Meskipun lanskapnya telah bergeser dalam beberapa 
tahun terakhir, media tradisional, terutama televisi Free-
to-Air (FTA) TV, tetap dominan. FTA TV terus menarik porsi 
terbesar dari belanja iklan karena jangkauan yang luas 
dan popularitasnya yang merata. Jaringan TV nasional 
mempertahankan dominasi mereka di pasar, dengan 
jumlah pemirsa yang luas di semua kelompok usia dan 
demografi, baik di kota besar maupun daerah pedesaan. 
Meskipun ada preferensi yang semakin berkembang 
terhadap media digital, penetrasi FTA TV yang besar di 
rumah tangga Indonesia memastikan peran sentralnya 
dalam strategi periklanan.
Namun, ekspansi pesat pada platform digital telah 
mengubah industri media, terutama dengan semakin 
banyaknya akses ke smartphone dan internet. Iklan digital 
telah mengalami lonjakan signifikan dalam beberapa 
tahun terakhir, karena brands mulai mengalihkan fokus 
mereka ke platform seperti Google, Facebook, Instagram, 
YouTube, dan semakin banyak situs e-commerce 
seperti Tokopedia dan Shopee. Dengan meningkatnya 
jumlah penduduk Indonesia yang melek digital, para 
pengiklan berinvestasi dalam kampanye yang lebih 
terarah dan berbasis data, memanfaatkan kemampuan 
untuk menjangkau segmen konsumen tertentu secara 
real-time. Platform media sosial, khususnya, semakin 
menjadi saluran penting bagi brands untuk terhubung 
dengan demografi muda, yang kini menjadi sumber inti 
dari belanja konsumen di Indonesia.
Industri over-the-top (OTT) Indonesia juga mengalami 
pertumbuhan pesat dalam beberapa tahun terakhir, 
didorong

penetrasi

dan harga smartphone yang terjangkau. Platform 
domestik telah memperoleh daya tarik yang signifikan, 
menawarkan beragam konten mulai dari drama lokal 
dan olahraga hingga film dan serial internasional. Para 
pemain lokal semakin banyak berinvestasi dalam konten 
orisinil untuk memenuhi preferensi budaya dan bersaing 
dengan raksasa global. Dengan inisiatif pemerintah yang 
mendukung transformasi digital dan meningkatnya 
minat terhadap layanan streaming, pasar OTT Indonesia 
memiliki prospek jangka panjang yang kuat, siap 
menjadi salah satu pusat hiburan digital terkemuka di 
Asia Tenggara.
Despite the rise of digital, traditional media such as print 
and radio have seen their share of advertising spending 
diminish. The print media sector, which once played a 
pivotal role in the advertising market, is experiencing 
a decline as readership shifts to online news outlets 
and mobile applications. While print publications 
like newspapers and magazines still have a niche 
audience, their advertising revenue has been largely 
cannibalized by digital alternatives. Similarly, radio 
remains an important medium, especially in non-metro 
areas and smaller cities, but its share of the advertising 
pie is shrinking. Radio’s relevance is often tied to local 
advertisers and regional audiences, but even here, the 
competition from digital platforms offering targeted, 
measurable ad campaigns has affected radio’s growth.
Out-of-Home (OOH) advertising is another key player 
in the Indonesian media landscape, with increasing 
investment in outdoor formats like billboards, digital 
screens, and transport advertising. As Indonesia’s 
urbanization accelerates, OOH media becomes an 
essential tool for advertisers seeking to capture the 
attention of commuters and city dwellers. The high-
traffic nature of OOH locations in major cities such as 
Jakarta and Surabaya makes them valuable for brand 
visibility. While OOH spending still lags behind TV and 
digital, its growth potential is considerable, especially as 
advertisers look for more integrated and immersive ways 
to reach their audiences. The rise of digital billboards 
and interactive OOH formats is further contributing to 
this sector’s expansion.
Overall, the Indonesian media landscape reflects a 
balancing act between the enduring dominance 
of traditional media and the explosive growth of 
digital platforms. As the country continues to digitize, 
advertising budgets are increasingly being allocated 
to online and performance-based channels. However, 
television, radio, print, and OOH will continue to play 
vital roles, adapting to the changing preferences and 
behaviours of the Indonesian consumer. The evolving 
media consumption habits provide opportunities for 
advertisers to develop multi-platform strategies that 
target consumers across different touchpoints.

TINJAUAN INDUSTRI MEDIA
Media Industry Overview
PT MNC Digital Entertainment Tbk (“MSIN” or “the 
Company”) has evolved and expanded its focus beyond 
its previous endeavours. This transformation indicates 
a strategic shift towards diversification and a broader 
scope within the digital entertainment industry, hence 
potentially open up new revenue streams and solidify 
the Company’s position.
MSIN has generated a revenue of Rp3.5 trillion this 
year, EBITDA of Rp760 billion with 22% EBITDA margin. 
Additionaly, MSIN has successfully recorded a net 
income of Rp399 billion.
The Company’s content production subsidiaries cover a 
wide range of genres, ensuring a comprehensive content 
portfolio. Furthermore, MSIN owns the digital rights for 
content produced under MNC Media & Entertainment 
totalling 300,000 hours which is not only strengthens the 
Company’s position as the largest IP owner in Indonesia, 
but also provides ample opportunities for monetization 
and distribution across various digital platforms. This 
broad distribution strategy enhances the Company’s 
reach and audience engagement while generating 
revenue through licensing agreements, advertising, 
subscription models, and other monetization avenues.
Moreover, MSIN also owns Movieland, the state-of-art 
facility, which represents a significant milestone for 
the Company. Situated within the Special Economic 
Zone (SEZ) MNC Lido City spanning across 21 hectares, 
Movieland stands as the largest and most integrated 
outdoor-based studio complex in the region.
MSIN’s business portfolio extends beyond content 
production. With approximately 400 artists and 200 
influencers under its management, the Company 
boasts the largest talent pool in Indonesia. These talents 
represent a diverse range of expertise across various 
entertainment fields. In addition, MSIN also managed 
music labels under Hits records (pop) & Swara Bintang 
(dangdut and Malay).
The inclusion of Indonesia’s largest OTT, RCTI+ (AVOD) 
and Vision+ (SVOD) marks a significant move by the 
Company, reflecting MSIN’s proactive stance in adapting 
to the rapidly evolving digital landscape. This strategic 
move has significantly expanded their respective user 
base, with both platforms collectively surpassing 100 
million monthly active users and 2.85 million paid 
subscribers by December 2024, solidifying their position 
as Indonesia’s top OTT services.
Overall, all the strategic moves that has been taken 
underscores MSIN’s commitment to innovation and 
growth, equipping the Company to thrive in the digital 
era while maintaining its leadership position in the 
Indonesian entertainment industry. By capitalizing 
on the opportunities, MSIN is well-positioned to drive 
continued success and value creation for its stakeholders.
