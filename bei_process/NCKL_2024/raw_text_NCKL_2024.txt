View on External Conditions 
Mengutip laporan Global Economic Prospect (GEP) Januari 2024, 
World Bank memproyeksikan pertumbuhan global melambat dari 
2,9% pada tahun 2023 menjadi 2,4% pada tahun 2024. Perkiraan 
ini merefleksikan kekhawatiran bahwa perekonomian global masih 
akan menghadapi tekanan dari kombinasi kebijakan moneter 
yang ketat, ketegangan geopolitik, serta praktik proteksionis 
perdagangan yang semakin memanas.
Dalam perkembangannya, pertumbuhan ekonomi Amerika Serikat 
mengalami perlambatan di tengah suku bunga yang masih tinggi. 
Ekonomi Amerika Serikat tumbuh 2,8%, lebih rendah dari 2,9% 
pada tahun 2023, tetapi lebih baik dari prediksi awal sebesar 2,0%. 
Prediksi sebelumnya bahwa kemungkinan besar ekonomi Amerika 
Serikat akan mengalami resesi, kenyataannya hanya sedikit 
melemah (soft landing). 
Tiongkok yang merupakan negara dengan perekonomian kedua 
terbesar di dunia, mengalami perlambatan ekonomi dari 5,4% di 
tahun 2023 menjadi 5,0% di tahun 2024. Perlambatan ini selain 
disebabkan oleh pelemahan ekonomi global yang berdampak pada 
melemahnya perdagangan luar negeri Tiongkok, juga dampak dari 
masalah di dalam negeri, seperti krisis sektor properti yang masih 
belum teratasi, pertumbuhan sektor infrastruktur yang lemah, serta 
angka pengangguran di kelompok usia muda yang tinggi. 
Krisis properti di Tiongkok dimulai pada tahun 2021, ketika 
pengembang besar properti menghadapi likuidasi dan gagal 
bayar. Pemerintah, berdasarkan regulasi, memberikan tindakan 
keras terhadap pengembang properti yang memiliki utang 
tinggi sehingga beberapa perusahaan properti raksasa Tiongkok 
terancam terlikuidasi. Situasi ini masih berlanjut dan relatif 
belum teratasi hingga tahun 2024. Sementara sektor infrastruktur 
Tiongkok menunjukkan tren peningkatan tetapi tidak signifikan. 
Pembangunan proyek-proyek raksasa di Tiongkok dilaporkan 
masih mengalami hambatan di pertengahan tahun 2024.
Secara keseluruhan, ekonomi global tahun 2024 lebih rendah 
dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya walaupun lebih baik dari proyeksi 
awal. Menurut laporan World Bank terbaru, pertumbuhan global 
tercatat di kisaran 2,6%, lebih baik dari perkiraan sebesar 2,4%, 
tetapi lebih rendah dari 2,9% pada tahun 2023. 
According to the Global Economic Prospects (GEP) report released 
by the World Bank in January 2024, global economic growth 
is projected to slow from 2.9% in 2023 to 2.4% in 2024. This 
projection reflects ongoing concerns that the global economy will 
continue to face pressures stemming from a combination of tight 
monetary policy, escalating geopolitical tensions, and increasingly 
protectionist trade practices.
In terms of recent developments, the United States economy 
experienced a slight slowdown amid persistently high interest rates. 
The United States recorded growth of 2.8% in 2024, down from 
2.9% in 2023 but still outperforming earlier forecasts of 2.0%. While 
previous projections anticipated a potential recession in the US, the 
economy managed a soft landing with only modest weakening.
Meanwhile, China, the world’s second-largest economy, having its 
growth decline from 5.4% in 2023 to 5.0% in 2024. The slowdown 
was driven by both global and domestic factors. Beside due to the 
slow growth of global economic which affected to the deceleration 
China overseas trade, also caused by unresolved issues in the 
property sector, subdued infrastructure growth, and high youth 
unemployment contributed to the economic deceleration.
China’s property crisis began in 2021, when major real estate 
developers faced liquidation and defaults. In response, the China 
Government implemented stringent regulations targeting highly 
indebted developers, which threatened several giants real estate to 
collapse. This crisis has yet to be fully resolved as of 2024. While 
infrastructure development has shown some improvement, but 
insignificant. Large-scale infrastructure projects in China were 
reported to have faced setbacks as of mid 2024.
Overall, global economic performance in 2024 is weaker compared 
to the previous year, though it exceeded initial expectations. 
According to the latest World Bank update, global growth stood at 
approximately 2.6%, higher than the projected 2.4%, but still below 
the 2.9% recorded in 2023.

Tinjauan Industri
Industry Overview
Secara historis, permintaan nikel didominasi oleh industri baja 
nirkarat yang menyerap sekitar 70% konsumsi nikel global. Baja 
nirkarat memiliki ketahanan korosi yang sangat baik dan sudah 
menjadi bagian dari kehidupan modern. Baja nirkarat digunakan 
dalam berbagai aplikasi, meliputi industri berat, konstruksi, 
arsitektur, manufaktur otomotif, peralatan dapur, serta alat bedah 
dan kedokteran gigi. Industri baja nirkarat menggunakan produk 
FeNi sebagai salah satu bahan utama untuk campuran karena 
sifat tahan karatnya. FeNi merupakan hasil olahan bijih nikel kadar 
tinggi (saprolit) yang diekstraksi di smelter dengan teknologi RKEF. 
Berdasarkan data dari International Stainless Steel Forum (ISSF), 
produsen baja nirkarat dunia masih didominasi oleh Tiongkok yang 
menguasai sekitar 60% pangsa pasar global. Produsen terbesar 
berikutnya adalah Eropa (+12%), Indonesia (+10%), India (+8%), 
dan Amerika Serikat (+4%). Total produksi baja nirkarat dunia pada 
tahun 2024 diperkirakan mencapai sekitar 54–55 juta ton, sedikit 
meningkat dari 53 juta ton pada 2023.
Sejak tahun 2005, Tiongkok mengembangkan produk NPI sebagai 
alternatif pengganti FeNi sebagai bahan baku industri baja nirkarat. 
Umumnya, FeNi mengandung lebih dari 20% kandungan nikel, 
sedangkan NPI hanya memiliki sekitar 10%-12% kandungan 
nikel. Industri baja nirkarat terutama di Tiongkok lebih banyak 
menggunakan NPI dibandingkan FeNi karena biaya produksi 
menjadi lebih murah. 
Pasar utama produk FeNi dan NPI dari Indonesia adalah industri 
baja nirkarat Tiongkok. Krisis sektor properti yang berkepanjangan, 
pertumbuhan sektor pembangunan infrastruktur yang lemah, dan 
perlambatan ekonomi Tiongkok secara keseluruhan, membuat 
kinerja industri baja nirkarat Tiongkok melambat dan berdampak 
pada penurunan permintaan produk FeNi dan NPI dari Tiongkok. 
Nikel juga digunakan sebagai bahan baku prekursor untuk baterai 
karena kemampuannya menyimpan energi dengan sangat baik 
dan efisien. Penggunaan nikel pada baterai pertama kali dilakukan 
pada tahun 1980-an melalui baterai nikel cadmium (NiCd) dan 
baterai nikel metal hidrida (NiMH). Baterai berbasis nikel terus 
berkembang dengan produk turunan bijih nikel limonit berupa 
MHP sebagai bahan baku untuk baterai EV.
Historically, nickel demand has been dominated by the stainless 
steel industry, which has accounted for around 70% of global nickel 
consumption. Stainless steel is highly corrosion-resistant and has 
become an integral part of modern life. It is used across various 
applications, including heavy industries, construction, architecture, 
automotive manufacturing, kitchen appliances, as well as surgical 
and dental instruments. The stainless steel industry primarily 
utilizes FeNi as a key alloying material due to its anti-corrosive 
properties. FeNi is produced from high-grade nickel ore (saprolite) 
processed in smelters using RKEF technology.
According to data from the International Stainless Steel Forum 
(ISSF), China continues to dominate global stainless steel 
production, commanding approximately 60% of the market share. 
Other major producers include Europe (+12%), Indonesia (+10%), 
India (+8%), and the United States (+4%). Global stainless steel 
production in 2024 is projected to reach approximately 54–55 
million tons, slightly increased from 53 million tons in 2023.
Since 2005, China has developed NPI as a cost-effective alternative 
to FeNi for use in stainless steel production. While FeNi typically 
contains more than 20% nickel, NPI has a lower nickel content of 
around 10%–12%. Due to the lower production costs, the stainless 
steel industry in China prefers NPI over FeNi.
China stainless steel industry remains the main export destination 
for Indonesia’s FeNi and NPI products. However, the prolonged 
property sector crisis, weak infrastructure development, and 
a broader economic deceleration in China have led to reduced 
activity in the stainless steel industry, subsequently decreasing 
demand for FeNi and NPI from China.
In addition to its use in stainless steel, nickel is also a crucial 
raw material in the production of battery precursors due to its 
excellent and efficient energy storage capabilities. The use of 
nickel in batteries began in the 1980s with nickel-cadmium (NiCd) 
and nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries. Nickel-based batteries 
have continued to evolve, with derivatives of limonite ore as MHP, 
serving as key materials for EV batteries.

Industri baterai diproyeksikan akan mendorong pertumbuhan 
permintaan nikel yang signifikan dalam beberapa tahun 
mendatang. Menurut Mining.com, permintaan nikel untuk aplikasi 
baterai mencapai hampir 17% dari total produksi nikel pada tahun 
2023, meningkat substansial dari hanya 3% pada tahun 2020. 
Sebaliknya, permintaan nikel untuk baja nirkarat, meskipun masih 
mendominasi, turun dari 71% pada tahun 2020 menjadi 64% pada 
tahun 2023. 
Pertumbuhan permintaan nikel untuk industri baterai didorong 
oleh pertumbuhan penjualan EV global. Pada tahun 2024, penjualan 
EV secara global melonjak ke titik tertinggi mencapai 17,1 juta unit, 
tumbuh 25% dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya. Khususnya pada 
bulan Desember, penjualan EV mencapai 1,9 juta unit, naik 26% 
dibandingkan periode yang sama pada tahun 2023. Meskipun 
pertumbuhan di Eropa stabil, pasar Tiongkok tumbuh sebesar 
lebih dari 40% dengan penambahan 11 juta unit EV. Insentif subsidi, 
keringanan pajak, dan standar emisi yang ketat menjadi pendorong 
peningkatan populasi EV pada tahun 2024. Skema tukar tambah 
Tiongkok, strategi pengumpulan emisi Eropa, dan keringanan 
pajak Amerika Utara menunjukkan bahwa kebijakan pemerintah 
terkait EV sangat berpengaruh terhadap perilaku konsumen dan 
produsen EV. 
Meskipun dengan pertumbuhan yang signifikan, industri baterai 
EV juga menghadapi berbagai tantangan. Pada September 2024, 
United States Trade Representative (USTR) mengumumkan 
kenaikan tarif akhir Pasal 301 atas impor produk Tiongkok, 
termasuk EV dan baterai EV yang masing-masing dikenakan tarif 
impor baru sebesar 100% dan 25%. Kenaikan tarif tersebut akan 
meliputi produk-produk Tiongkok yang nilainya mencapai US$18 
miliar. Pada bulan Oktober 2024, Uni Eropa juga menaikkan 
tarif definitif hingga 45% untuk EV dari Tiongkok. Kebijakan-
kebijakan proteksionis ini berpotensi memicu perang dagang dan 
mempengaruhi rantai pasokan industri baterai dan logam. 
Dari sisi teknologi, baterai berbasis nikel, khususnya baterai NMC, 
yang menggunakan nikel sulfat (NiSo4) menghadapi persaingan 
yang semakin ketat dengan baterai LFP. Keduanya memiliki 
keunggulan dan kekurangan masing-masing, dari waktu siklus 
pengisian/pengosongan kapasitas, biaya bahan baku, kepadatan 
energi yang mempengaruhi ukuran fisik baterai, keamanan dalam 
kondisi suhu tinggi, dan sebagainya. Jika baterai LFP semakin 
populer dan penggunaannya semakin luas, ada potensi perubahan 
peta permintaan dan pasokan nikel di masa depan.
Dari sisi pasokan, produksi pertambangan dan smelter nikel 
terutama dari Indonesia mencapai peningkatan yang sangat tinggi 
sejalan dengan pembukaan IUP baru dan ekspansi kapasitas dari 
sejumlah perusahaan smelter. Sebagai produsen nikel terbesar 
di dunia, pada tahun 2024 Indonesia mencatatkan produksi 
tertinggi di dunia sebesar 2,2 juta ton. Meningkatnya produksi nikel 
Indonesia telah menyebabkan kelebihan pasokan yang berdampak 
pada penurunan harga sejak tahun 2023. 
The battery industry is projected to drive significant growth in nickel 
demand in the upcoming years. According to Mining.com, nickel 
demand for battery applications reached nearly 17% of total nickel 
production in 2023, a substantial increase from just 3% in 2020. 
Eventhough, the demand of nickel consumption is still dominant, 
there is declined form 71% in 2020 to 64% in 2023.
The rising demand for nickel in the battery sector is primarily driven 
by the surge in global EV sales. In 2024, global EV sales reached 
the highest record of 17.1 million units, marking a 25% year-on-
year increase. In December, EV sales hit 1.9 million units, increased 
26% compared to the same period in 2023. While growth in Europe 
remained steady, the Chinese market expanded by over 40%, 
adding 11 million EVs. Government incentives such as subsidies, tax 
exemptions, and stricter emission standards significantly boosted 
EV adoption in 2024. China’s vehicle trade-in scheme, Europe’s 
emission pooling strategy, and North America’s tax relief policies 
highlight how government interventions heavily influence both 
consumer behavior and EV manufacturing trends.
Despite the rapid growth, the EV battery industry faces notable 
challenges. In September 2024, the United States Trade 
Representative (USTR) announced final Section 301 tariff increases 
on imports from China, including a 100% import duty on EVs and 
a 25% duty on EV batteries. These tariffs are expected to affect 
Chinese exports valued at approximately US$18 billion. Similarly, in 
October 2024, the European Union imposed definitive tariffs of up 
to 45% on EVs imported from China. These protectionist measures 
may trigger trade tensions and potentially disrupt the battery and 
critical metals supply chain.
From a technological standpoint, nickel-based batteries, specifically 
NMC batteries that use nickel sulphate (NiSO4), are facing increasing 
competition from LFP batteries. Both battery types offer unique 
advantages and disadvantages in terms of charge/discharge cycles, 
raw material costs, energy density which impacts battery size, 
thermal safety, etc. Should LFP batteries continue to gain popularity 
and wider application, the global demand supply landscape for 
nickel may experience a significant shift.
On the supply side, nickel mining and smelting activities, especially 
in Indonesia, have seen substantial increases, driven by the issuance 
of new IUP and capacity expansions by several smelter companies. 
As the world’s largest nickel producer, Indonesia recorded the 
highest global nickel output in 2024 at 2.2 million tons. The surge in 
Indonesian production has led to an oversupply, contributing to a 
decline in nickel prices since 2023.

Operational Review
Perseroan merupakan perusahaan nikel terintegrasi. Produk 
utama Perseroan adalah bijih nikel laterit yang terdiri dari bijih 
limonit dan bijih saprolit. Secara umum, bijih limonit mengandung 
sekitar 1,1%–1,2% kandungan nikel, dan terletak lebih dekat ke 
permukaan bumi. Sedangkan bijih saprolit terletak di bawah zona 
limonit dan memiliki kadar nikel yang lebih tinggi. Hasil produksi 
pertambangan, baik limonit maupun saprolit, diproses di fasilitas 
pengolahan pada Entitas Anak dan Entitas Asosiasi Perseroan 
yang juga berada di Pulau Obi, sehingga memperoleh keunggulan 
efisiensi biaya logistik.
Perseroan melalui Entitas Anak, PT MSP dan PT HJF, 
mengoperasikan smelter dengan teknologi RKEF untuk mengolah 
bijih saprolit menjadi FeNi yang dapat diproses lebih lanjut menjadi 
baja nirkarat.
Sementara Entitas Asosiasi PT HPL, mengoperasikan fasilitas 
pemurnian berteknologi HPAL pertama di Indonesia, untuk 
mengolah bijih limonit menjadi MHP, yang selanjutnya dimurnikan 
menjadi nikel sulfat dan kobalt sulfat. Kedua bahan ini merupakan 
bahan baku katoda untuk baterai lithium-ion yang berbasis nikel.

The Company is an integrated nickel company. Its primary products 
are laterite nickel ores, which consist of limonite and saprolite ores. 
Generally, limonite ore contains between 1.1%–1.2% nickel and is 
found closer to the earth’s surface. In contrast, saprolite ore lies 
beneath the limonite layer and contains a higher nickel grade. 
Both limonite and saprolite ores are processed at the Company’s 
Subsidiaries and Associated Entities production facilities, which are 
also located on Obi Island, thereby offering logistical cost-efficiency 
advantages.
Through its Subsidiaries, PT MSP and PT HJF, the Company 
operates smelters utilizing RKEF technology to process saprolite 
ore into FeNi, which can then be further refined into stainless steel.
Meanwhile, the Associated Entity PT HPL operates Indonesia’s first 
HPAL processing facility, which refines limonite ore into MHP. This 
intermediate product is then further processed into nickel sulfate 
and cobalt sulfate, key raw materials used in the production of 
cathodes for nickel-based lithium-ion batteries.

MINING ACTIVITIES
The Company conducts its nickel ore mining operations using the 
open cast mining method. This method involves surface mining 
activities where extraction takes place at or near the surface of 
the earth, with workers and heavy equipment operating in direct 
contact with the external environment, and minerals are extracted 
from open pits in the ground.
According to the latest JORC data, the Company holds an estimated 
reserve and resource of approximately 309.7 million wmt of nickel 
ore. These resources are derived from 2 active laterite nickel mining 
projects located within the concessions of the Company and one 
of its Subsidiaries, PT GPS, as well as from 2 additional mining 
concessions with nickel mining prospects under PT JMP and 
PT GTS.
PROCESSING ACTIVITIES
The Company operates 2 RKEF smelters through investments in 
PT MSP and PT HJF. PT MSP runs 4 production lines with a total 
annual production capacity of 25,000 tons of nickel content in FeNi 
metal, requiring 2.125 million wmt of saprolite ore as raw material. 
Meanwhile, PT HJF operates 8 production lines with a total 
annual capacity of 95,000 tons of nickel content in FeNi, requiring 
8.075 million wmt of saprolite ore.

PROJECT DEVELOPMENT PROGRESS
In 2024, as part of its plan to enhance processing capacity and 
further downstream operations, the Company initiated the 
development of its third mining prospect through the concession 
of PT GTS, located in the southern part of Obi Island. This initiative 
is aimed at ensuring the long-term readiness of ore supply. The 
preparation phase, which began in the second half of 2024, includes 
the construction of key infrastructure such as mining yards, hauling 
roads, and jetty facilities. Simultaneously, the appointed mining 
contractor commenced preparatory mining activities, with full-
scale mining operations targeted to begin in January 2025. Once 
the jetty is completed, expected by mid-2025 or earlier, PT GTS’s 
mined ore will be transported to the processing facilities located in 
the industrial area of Kawasi Village on Obi Island. 
The Company holds a 35% investment in PT KPS. The first 
development phase includes the construction of 4 production lines 
with a total capacity of 60,000 tons of nickel content in FeNi per 
year. As of the end of 2024, the construction progress had reached 
94.3%, with production expected to commence in the first quarter 
of 2025.
The Company also owns a 20% stake in PT ONC, which is developing 
a second HPAL refining facility on Obi Island, following the first 
HPAL plant operated by PT HPL. PT ONC has 3 MHP production lines 
with an installed capacity of 65,000 tons of nickel content and 7,500 
tons of cobalt content per year. All three lines have been operating 
at full capacity since August 2024.

KINERJA TAHUN 2024
Penurunan harga nikel di pasar global menjadi tantangan utama 
Perseroan untuk menjaga pencapaian kinerja yang positif di 
sepanjang tahun 2024. Pencapaian ini didukung oleh keunggulan 
operasional di semua segment. Perseroan tetap berkomitmen 
terhadap praktik penambangan yang baik, mengedepankan 
keselamatan kerja, dan menjaga posisinya sebagai cost leadership 
untuk memastikan keuntungan yang berkelanjutan. 
Hingga saat ini, berbagai inisiatif operasional Perseroan berhasil 
mengaplikasikan aktivitas tambang dengan cash cost terendah di 
antara pemain industri sejenis. Hal ini dikarenakan lokasi konsesi 
pertambangan dan fasilitas pengolahan yang berdekatan di 
Pulau Obi. 
KINERJA OPERASI PERTAMBANGAN 
Hingga akhir tahun 2024, kegiatan pertambangan bijih nikel dari 
konsesi PT TBP dan PT GPS mencatat volume penjualan bijih nikel 
sebesar 23,75 juta wmt, naik 54,5% dari 15,38 juta wmt pada tahun 
2023. Volume penjualan tersebut terdiri dari penjualan saprolit 
sebesar 9,16 wmt, meningkat 45,5% dari 6,30 juta wmt, dan limonit 
sebesar 14,59 wmt, meningkat 60,7% dari 9,08 juta wmt.
Kenaikan penjualan saprolit didorong oleh kebutuhan pasokan 
smelter PT HJF yang memiliki 8 lini produksi yang telah berproduksi 
dengan kapasitas penuh pada tahun 2024. Sedangkan kenaikan 
penjualan limonit didorong oleh kebutuhan pasokan fasilitas 
pabrik HPAL kedua yaitu PT ONC dengan 3 lini produksi yang telah 
beroperasi penuh sejak bulan Agustus 2024.

Produk FeNi dihasilkan dari 2 smelter yakni PT MSP yang memiliki 
4 lini produksi berkapasitas total 25.000 ton kandungan nikel 
dalam FeNi per tahun dan PT HJF yang memiliki 8 jalur produksi 
berkapasitas total 95.000 ton kandungan nikel dalam FeNi per 
tahun. Pada tahun 2024, volume penjualan FeNi sebesar 126.344 
ton kandungan nikel dalam FeNi, meningkat 25,2% dari 100.891 ton 
kandungan nikel dalam FeNi, dan 5% di atas kapasitas terpasang.
KINERJA OPERASIONAL HPAL
Produk kandungan nikel berupa MHP dan turunannya nikel sulfat 
(NiSO4) dihasilkan dari fasilitas HPAL PT HPL dan PT ONC yang

sebesar 102.054 ton, meningkat 67,7% dari 60.871 ton terdiri dari 
MHP sebesar 63.431 Ni metal ton, meningkat 37,6% dari 46.093 ton 
dan NiSO4 sebesar 38.622 ton, meningkat 161,4% dari 14.778 ton.
2024 PERFORMANCE
Despite facing significant challenges due to declining global 
nickel prices in 2024, the Company managed to maintain positive 
performance. This achievement was supported by operational 
excellence and strict cost control across all segments. The Company 
remained committed to good mining practices, prioritizing 
workplace safety, and upholding its position as a cost leader to 
ensure sustainable profitability. 
As a result of these initiatives, the Company maintained one of the 
lowest cash costs among peers in the industry. This was largely 
due to the strategic advantage of having closely located mining 
concessions and processing facilities on Obi Island.
MINING OPERATIONS PERFORMANCE
By the end of 2024, nickel ore mining activities from the 
concessions of PT TBP and PT GPS recorded in total nickel ore 
sales of 23.75 million wmt. This represents a 54.5% increase from 
15.38 million wmt sold in 2023. The sales included 9.16 million wmt 
of saprolite, up 45.5% from the previous year’s 6.30 million wmt, 
and 14.59 million wmt of limonite, marking a 60.7% increase from 
9.08 million wmt. 
The increase in saprolite sales was primarily driven by the demand 
from PT HJF, which fully operated 8 of its smelter production lines 
throughout 2024. Meanwhile, the growth in limonite sales was 
supported by the operational ramp-up of the second HPAL facility 
operated by PT ONC, which began full-capacity production with 3 
lines starting in August 2024.

which operates 4 production lines with an annual capacity of 25,000 
tons of nickel content in FeNi, and PT HJF, which runs 8 production 
lines with a total capacity of 95,000 tons of nickel content in FeNi of 
annually. In 2024, the total sales volume of FeNi reached 126,344 
tons of nickel content in FeNi, representing 25.2% increase from 
100,891 tons of nickel content in FeNi in 2023. This figure also 
exceeds the installed capacity by approximately 5%.
HPAL OPERATIONAL PERFORMANCE
In the HPAL segment, nickel content products such as MHP and its 
derivative, nickel sulphate (NiSO4), were produced by PT HPL and 
PT ONC, each operating 3 production lines. In 2024, total sales of 
nickel content reached 102,054 tons, a significant increase of 67.7% 
compared to 60,871 tons in the previous year. This total includes 
63,431 Ni metal tons of MHP, which rose by 37.6% from 46,093 tons 
in 2023, and 38,622 tons of nickel sulfate, which surged by 161.4% 
from 14,778 tons.

SEGMENT PROFITABILITY
According to the financial reporting, operations are categorized into 
business units based on products and services, divided into two 
main operating segments i.e nickel mining and nickel processing.

ore collection, and concluding with ore delivering to the processing 
facilities. The nickel processing segment involves the processing 
and refining of nickel, starting with drying to reduce ore moisture, 
followed by smelting, converting, and granulation processes.

Hilirisasi dan industrialisasi merupakan salah satu fokus pemerintah. 
Sebelumnya, hilirisasi adalah amanat Undang-Undang Mineral 
dan Batubara (UU Minerba) yang ditetapkan pada tahun 2009, 
dimana mengamanatkan pentingnya upaya untuk meningkatkan 
nilai tambah dalam negeri atas sumber daya mineral, yang saat 
ini ditujukan untuk produk tambang nikel, tembaga, bauksit, dan 
timah. Hilirisasi sumber daya mineral didorong sangat masif di 
masa pemerintah sebelumnya khususnya di bidang pengolahan 
nikel di wilayah Sulawesi, Maluku, dan Papua Barat.
Harga nikel diperkirakan masih akan berfluktuasi, tetapi pada 
kisaran yang cukup potensial sehingga tetap membuka peluang 
pertumbuhan bagi Perseroan. Mengutip laporan World Bank CMO 
edisi Oktober 2024, pasokan nikel global terus meningkat pesat, 
meskipun operasi beberapa tambang di luar Indonesia dihentikan 
akibat penurunan harga yang berkelanjutan.
Kondisi oversupply telah membuat harga nikel turun sekitar 30% 
sejak 2022. Kenaikan pasokan terutama berasal dari Indonesia yang 
menyumbang lebih dari setengah produksi nikel dunia. Investasi 
besar dari Tiongkok untuk membangun smelter di Indonesia telah 
mendorong kenaikan produk olahan nikel. Sebagian besar investasi 
ini ditujukan untuk memproduksi MHP sebagai bahan baku untuk 
pasar baterai. Permintaan global untuk nikel diperkirakan akan 
terus tumbuh dalam beberapa tahun mendatang, didukung oleh 
permintaan produsen baja nirkarat dan baterai EV. Pada 2025, 
harga nikel diperkirakan sedikit membaik sekitar 3% dari harga 
rata-rata 2024. 
Menghadapi ketidakpastian tahun 2025, Perseroan fokus untuk 
memperkuat fundamental perusahaan dengan terus meningkatkan 
operational excellence dan cost leadership serta memastikan 
penyelesaian proyek-proyek existing tepat waktu. Perseroan 
akan berusaha tetap menjadi the lowest cash cost producer dan 
meningkatkan efisiensi di semua lini produksi untuk menjaga 
tingkat profitabilitas yang optimal.
Downstream and industrialization remains one of the government’s 
core priorities. This agenda, rooted in Indonesia’s 2009 Mining Law 
(Minerba Law), mandates increased domestic value addition for 
mineral resources. The focus has been on developing downstream 
capabilities in nickel, copper, bauxite, and tin. Under the previous 
administration, the downstream policy, particularly in nickel 
processing, was aggressively advanced across Sulawesi, Maluku, 
and West Papua.
Nickel prices are expected to remain volatile, though within a 
potentially favorable range that still presents growth opportunities 
for the Company. According to the World Bank’s CMO from October 
2024, global nickel supply continues to increase sharply. This 
is despite the suspension of several mining operations outside 
Indonesia due to sustained low prices.
Since 2022, oversupply has driven nickel prices down by 
approximately 30%. The increase in supply has been largely 
attributed to Indonesia, which now accounts for over half of global 
nickel production. Significant investment, particularly from China, 
to build smelters in Indonesian, resulting in a surge in processed 
nickel output. A substantial portion of these investments has 
been directed toward producing MHP as feedstock for the battery 
market. Global demand for nickel is expected to continue rising in 
the upcoming years, supported by the stainless steel and EV battery 
industries. In 2025, nickel prices are forecasted to recover modestly, 
with an estimated increase of around 3% from the 2024 average.
In anticipation of continued uncertainty in 2025, the Company is 
focused on strengthening its fundamentals by further enhancing 
operational excellence and cost leadership, while ensuring the 
timely completion of ongoing projects. The Company will remain 
committed to maintaining its position as the lowest cash cost 
producer and will continue to pursue efficiency across all operations 
to sustain optimal profitability.

SOLVENCY AND RECEIVABLES COLLECTIBILITY
Solvency
The Company’s ability to pay off long-term and short-term 
obligations is reflected in its level of liquidity and solvency. 
Therefore, the Company continuously controls the soundness level 
of the Company’s finances by calculating the liquidity and solvency 
ratios.
Liquidity Ratio
The liquidity ratio is used to measure the Company’s capability to 
pay off its short-term obligations that will mature. The Company’s 
liquidity level can be indicated from the Current Ratio and Cash 
Ratio calculation.
The current ratio rose from 1.26x in 2023 to 2.30x, driven by a 37.1% 
decrease in current liabilities and a 14.5% increase in current assets 
compared to the previous year. Similarly, the cash ratio improved 
from 0.42x to 1.11x, as cash and bank balances surged by 64.9% 
compared to previous year.
The increase in both ratios reflect the Company’s strong financial 
position, ensuring sufficient liquidity to meet short-term obligations 
using available current assets and cash reserves.

Capital Structure Policy
The primary objective of the Company’s capital management is to 
ensure that they maintain healthy capital ratios in order to support 
their business and maximize shareholder value.
The Company is required under their respective loan agreements 
to maintain the level of existing share capital. In addition, the 
Company is also required by the Law No. 40 Year 2007 regarding 
Limited Liability Company, to contribute to and maintain a non-
distributable reserve fund until the said reserve reaches 20% of the 
issued and fully paid share capital. This externally imposed capital 
requirements are considered at the GMS.
Capital Structure
The Company manages its capital structure and makes adjustments 
to it, if necessary, in light of changes in economic conditions. To 
maintain or adjust the capital structure, the Company may adjust 
the dividend payment to shareholders, issue new shares or raise 
debt financing.
The Company’s policy is to maintain a healthy capital structure in 
order to secure access to finance at a reasonable cost. The Company 
monitors its capital using gearing ratios, by dividing net debts with 
the net equity. The Company’s policy is to maintain the gearing 
ratio within the range of gearing ratios of the leading companies 
with similar industry in Indonesia. 
The Company includes within net debt, short-term bank loans 
and long-term bank loans less cash on hand and in banks. Capital 
includes share capital, and equity attributable to the majority 
shareholders of the Company.

ASPEK PEMASARAN
Kebijakan Pemasaran
Perseroan, melalui Entitas Anaknya mendistribusikan produk 
atas bisnis pengolahan nikel (feronikel dan/atau MHP) kepada 
pelanggan di negara-negara seperti Tiongkok, Swiss, Hong Kong,

Adapun untuk bijih nikel yang dipasok oleh Perseroan dan Entitas

Strategi Pemasaran dan Strategi Harga
Dalam menghadapi dinamika pasar nikel global yang tengah 
mengalami tekanan akibat oversupply dan volatilitas harga, 
Perseroan menerapkan strategi pemasaran dan harga yang adaptif 
dan tersegmentasi. Dua segmen utama yang menjadi penggerak 
permintaan adalah industri baja nirkarat dan baterai EV.
Untuk pasar baja nirkarat yang menyerap produk FeNi, strategi 
pemasaran difokuskan pada kemitraan jangka panjang dengan 
produsen baja utama, khususnya di Tiongkok dan Asia Tenggara. 
Perseroan memiliki keandalan pasokan dan efisiensi biaya logistik 
karena memiliki integrasi vertikal dari tambang hingga smelter. 
Strategi harga pada segmen ini bersifat kompetitif, dengan fokus 
pada menjaga cost leadership agar tetap menjadi low cost producer 
di tengah penurunan harga global.
Sementara itu, untuk pasar baterai EV, pemasaran produk Perseroan 
berupa MHP maupun nikel sulfat difokuskan pada perluasan pasar 
ekspor ke negara-negara dengan pertumbuhan tinggi dalam 
adopsi kendaraan listrik, seperti Korea Selatan, Jepang, dan Eropa. 
Dalam hal ini, strategi harga lebih fleksibel dan berorientasi pada 
nilai tambah, mengingat produk berbasis nikel untuk baterai 
memiliki margin yang lebih tinggi serta dipengaruhi oleh tren 
Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG). Penekanan pada 
green production dan jejak karbon rendah juga menjadi elemen 
kunci dalam membangun daya saing dan reputasi Perseroan di 
pasar global.
Untuk mendukung strategi tersebut, Perseroan terus mendorong 
integrasi hilir, efisiensi operasional, serta inovasi produk untuk 
menjaga daya saing di tengah fluktuasi harga dan transisi 
energi global.
MARKETING ASPECTS
Marketing Policy
The Company, through its Subsidiaries distributes nickel processing 
business products (ferronickel and/or MHP) to customers in 
countries such as China, Switzerland, Hong Kong, Sweden and 
Singapore with a Free-on-Board (FOB) scheme. As for nickel ore 
supplied by the Company and its Subsidiaries, it is distributed to 
the nickel processing plants of its Subsidiaries and/or Associated 
Entities for further processing.
Marketing and Pricing Strategy
In response to the dynamics of the global nickel market, which is 
currently under pressure due to oversupply and price volatility, the 
Company adopts adaptive and segmented marketing and pricing 
strategies. Two key demand driving segments are the stainless steel 
industry and EV batteries.
For the stainless steel market which absorbs FeNi products, the 
marketing strategy is focused on long-term partnerships with 
major steel producers, particularly in China and Southeast Asia. 
The Company offers reliable supply and cost-efficient logistics due 
to its vertically integrated operations from mining to smelter. The 
pricing strategy in this segment is competitive, emphasizing cost 
leadership to maintain its position as a low cost producer amid 
falling global prices.
Meanwhile, for the EV battery market, the Company markets 
products such as MHP and nickel sulphate with a focus on 
expanding export markets to countries experiencing strong EV 
adoption, such as South Korea, Japan, and Europe. In this case, the 
pricing strategy is more flexible and value-oriented, as nickel-based 
battery products command higher margins and are influenced by 
Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) trends. An emphasis 
on green production and a low carbon footprint also serves as a key 
element in building the Company’s competitiveness and reputation 
in the global market.
To support this strategy, the Company continues to promote 
downstream integration, operational efficiency, and product 
innovation to maintain its competitive edge amid price fluctuations 
and the global energy transition.

IMPLEMENTATION OF CHANGES IN ACCOUNTING 
POLICY
In 2024, the Company implemented the following changes in 
accounting policies:
•	
Financial Accounting Standards Nomenclature
	
The revised nomenclature is rearranged and amended based on 
those as published by DSAK IAI for financial periods beginning 
on and after January 1, 2024.
•	
Amendment of PSAK 201: Non-current Liabilities with 
Covenants
	
The amendment specify the requirements for classifying 
liabilities as current or non-current. The amendment is not 
expected to have an impact on the Company’s consolidated 
financial statements.
•	
Amendment of PSAK 116: Lease Liability in a Sale and 
Leaseback
	
The amendment specifies the requirements that a seller-
lessee uses in measuring the lease liability arising in a sale and 
leaseback transaction, to ensure the seller-lessee does not 
recognise any amount of the gain or loss that relates to the right 
of use it retains. The amendment is not expected to have an 
impact on the Company’s consolidated financial statements.
•	
Amendment of PSAK 207 and PSAK 107: Supplier Financing 
Arrangement
	
The amendment clarifies the characteristics of supplier 
financing arrangements and require additional disclosure 
of such arrangements. The disclosure requirements in 
the amendments are intended to assist users of financial 
statements in understanding the effects of supplier finance 
arrangements on an entity’s liabilities, cash flow and exposure 
to liquidity risk. The amendment is not expected to have an 
impact on the Company’s consolidated financial statements.
