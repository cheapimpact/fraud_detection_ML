Procedure, Capacity, and Development of  
Business Activities
The Company's products and services are divided into 
5 (five) categories based on business activities: Testing, 
Inspection, Certification, Consulting, and Product Sales.
The Company's governance and strategic oversight are 
entrusted to the Board of Commissioners and Directors, 
as well as the Audit Committee and the Nomination and 
Remuneration Committee, together forming the Board 
of Leader Management (BOLM). This collective body 
steers the Company according to its vision, mission, and 
core values, crafting policies and regulations to direct 
operational activities. An essential component of their 
role involves business planning, crucial for establishing 
objectives and devising effective future strategies.
 
With a focus on continuous improvement, the Company 
identifies and capitalizes on business opportunities while 
efficiently managing risks. Prompt corrective action 
is taken when issues arise, and regular audits 
are conducted to maintain compliance with industry 
standards and regulations. The Company is also 
attentive to handling complaints and ensuring customer 
satisfaction.
The Company is dedicated to its core processes focusing 
on business growth and development. This dedication 
is evident in our strategic approach to leveraging 
business intelligence, exploring new opportunities, 
and implementing effective marketing strategies. 
Complementing these efforts, our Sales Department 
plays a crucial role in driving the Company forward. 
It is tasked with managing sales operations, participating 
in tenders, handling contracts, and fostering strong 
customer relationships. Together, these components 
work seamlessly to ensure the Company's success and 
sustainability.
 
The Company offers a wide array of services across 
multiple industries, ensuring quality, availability, and 
efficiency. Our offerings encompass Survey/Inspection, 
Testing, Certification, Consulting Services, and Product 
Sales. We specialize in conducting comprehensive field 
surveys, detailed laboratory tests, thorough audits, 
and evaluations. Additionally, our Consulting Services 
arm our clients with insightful reports and actionable 
recommendations 
to

their

Furthermore, the Company offers a wide range of 
support services designed to streamline business

Information Systems & Technology, and Quality, 
Health, Safety, and Environment (QHSE) Management

organizational development, recruitment, procurement, 
legal affairs, financial oversight, IT management, and 
compliance assurance, the Company ensures efficient 
operations and adherence to strict regulations.

Marketing Strategy
The Company's strategic focus in increasing its 
marketing presence is centered on digital connectivity 
and brand visibility as the ultimate goal. The emphasis 
on brand awareness has increased through a host of 
channels, including social media, conferences, webinars, 
and print media, showing the Company's commitment 
to reaching a wider audience. This proactive approach 
not only facilitates new customer acquisition, but also 
strengthens customer retention through community 
and loyalty initiatives. 
The

sales 
model,

and

with

This approach, which capitalizes on the strength 
of existing customer networks and word-of-mouth 
recommendations, 
underscores 
the 
Company’s 
dedication to cultivating enduring relationships within 
the Testing, Inspection, and Certification (TIC) industry.
To complement its direct sales efforts, the Company 
also harnesses the power of online media and websites 
to disseminate comprehensive information about 
TIC products and services. Its active engagement 
in conferences, webinars, and industry associations, 
combined with a positive reputation cultivated over 
55 years, significantly bolsters the Company's standing 
in the TIC sector.
This multifaceted and proactive marketing strategy 
has not only established the Company as a key service 
provider but also as a front runner in the industry, 
poised for sustainable growth amid Indonesia’s rapidly 
changing business landscape. The Company's ongoing 
commitment to refining its marketing approach is 
instrumental in enhancing its market dominance further.
 
Market Share
The Company has established its presence across multiple 
sectors within Indonesia, catering to a diverse range 
of industries each with unique services and marketing 
strategies. Presently, the TIC industry in Indonesia lacks 
sufficient transparency to provide specific data on 
market share percentages. Despite this, the Company is 
firmly committed to securing a position among the top 
three entities in each sector it operates. This ambition 
is underpinned by a focus on maintaining a stable 
reputation and achieving consistent business growth, 
which are essential for its standing within the industry.

Dividend Policy
In accordance with the legal framework in Indonesia, 
the annual dividend distribution of PT CARSURIN Tbk 
requires support from shareholders at the Annual 
General Meeting (AGM). The Company has the authority 
to announce the annual final dividend, which depends 
on positive earnings with a mandatory allocation of not 
less than 20.00% of the total subscribed capital to the 
reserve fund. If there is no specific determination in 
the AGM, the remaining net profit after deducting the 
reserve allocation can be distributed to shareholders as 
a final dividend.
 
The decision regarding interim dividends lies with 
the Board of Directors and must be approved by 
the Board of Commissioners. The Articles of Association 
comprehensively outline the criteria governing the 
disbursement of interim cash dividends, emphasizing 
equal rights among all shareholders. The priority 
allocation of interim dividends is directed towards 
strengthening financial stability, meeting creditor 
obligations, and ensuring operational continuity. In the 
event of a loss at the end of the fiscal year, shareholders 
are mandated to return interim dividends, with joint 
responsibility given to the Board of Directors and 
Commissioners.
Post the Initial Public Offering, starting from the 
fiscal year 2023, the Company's management plans 
to distribute cash dividends to shareholders, up to 
a maximum of 50.00% of the annual net profit, depending 
on 
business 
performance, 
cash 
flow 
dynamics, 
future prospects, working capital requirements, and 
compliance with existing regulatory constraints and 
obligations. Dividends will be distributed in Indonesian 
Rupiah, with a firm assurance from management 
regarding the absence of restrictions, commonly 
referred to as negative covenants, which could 
potentially impede the rights of public shareholders.

merupakan modal yang dihasilkan Perseroan melalui 
penjualan saham untuk pertama kalinya kepada

penjaminan emisi dan biaya lainnya, sisa dana dapat 
dialokasikan untuk berbagai keperluan strategis. Bagi 
Perseroan, dana IPO digunakan untuk mendorong 
ekspansi bisnis, berinvestasi dalam proyek modal, dan 
meningkatkan modal kerja. Alokasi dana IPO menjadi 
keputusan penting yang mencerminkan strategi dan 
prioritas pertumbuhan Perseroan.

setelah dikurangi biaya emisi efek sebesar 97,92%

Management Plan
The proceeds from the initial public offering (IPO) 
represent the capital generated by the Company 
through the first-time sale of shares to the public. The 
amount of IPO proceeds is determined by the number 
of shares sold and their offering price. After deducting 
issuance-related costs, such as underwriting fees and 
other expenses, the remaining funds can be allocated 
for various strategic purposes. For the Company, IPO 
funds are used to drive business expansion, invest in 
capital projects, and enhance working capital. The 
allocation of IPO funds is a crucial decision reflecting 
the Company's growth strategy and priorities.
 
After deducting securities issuance expenses, 97.92% of 
the proceeds from the Company's initial public offering 
are allocated to capital expenditure to support the 
Company's business activities in the testing segment, 
such as renovating 5 (five) laboratories in Halmahera, 
Jambi, Kendari, Medan, dan Morowali. Upon completion 
of the laboratory renovations, the Company plans to 
purchase laboratory and office equipment. Meanwhile, 
the remaining 2.08% will be used for working 
capital, including vehicle rentals and the purchase of 
consumables for operational activities.
 
In accordance with the regulations outlined in 
POJK No. 30/2015, the Company reaffirms its 
commitment to providing semi-annual reports on 
the utilization of proceeds from IPO judiciously, 
with 
reporting 
intervals 
set 
for 
30 
June 
and 
31 December. These reports will be submitted to the 
Financial Services Authority (FSA) and subsequently 
communicated to shareholders during the GMS as part 
of our commitment to transparency. In the event of any 
suspected deviations from the planned use of funds 
as stated in the Prospectus, the Company is obligated 
to formally notify the FSA and seek approval from 
shareholders in the GMS.
 
In executing transactions intended for the use of 
IPO proceeds, including those classified as material 
transactions under POJK No. 17/2020 and/or involving 
affiliates and conflicts of interest as regulated in 
POJK No. 42/2020, the Company reaffirms its 
commitment to adhere to the provisions of the 
regulations outlined in both POJK guidelines. If the 
funds obtained from the IPO prove to be insufficient 
to meet the outlined plans, the Company is prepared 
to explore various financing alternatives, including 
leveraging internal cash resources. Any remaining 
or unallocated funds from the IPO will be prudently 
invested in safe and highly liquid financial instruments, 
consistent with sound financial management practices.
