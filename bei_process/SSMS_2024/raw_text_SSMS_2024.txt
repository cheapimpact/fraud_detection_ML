Analisis Kondisi Perekonomian Global 
dan Nasional
Sepanjang tahun 2024, ekonomi global menghadapi dinamika 
dengan ketidakpastian yang meningkat. Pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global tercatat sebesar 3,2% pada tahun 2024. Meskipun 
demikian, perekonomian global masih menghadapi tantangan 
seperti ketegangan geopolitik, perubahan iklim, tekanan utang 
di negara berkembang, dan peningkatan proteksionisme yang 
dapat menekan potensi pertumbuhan ekonomi global. ASEAN 
menunjukkan ketahanan yang lebih baik, sementara Eropa 
dan Amerika Serikat menghadapi perlambatan akibat risiko 
politik dan proteksionisme. Tiongkok mengalami tekanan dari 
lemahnya permintaan domestik dan krisis properti, sementara 
India tetap tumbuh meski melambat karena inflasi pangan[¹].
Aktivitas manufaktur global sempat mengalami kontraksi 
selama empat bulan berturut-turut, namun kembali ke ambang 
ekspansi pada level 50 di bulan November 2024. Eropa dan 
Amerika Serikat masih mengalami kontraksi yang dipicu oleh 
lemahnya tingkat pesanan baru akibat permintaan global yang 
rendah. Menjelang akhir tahun, sebagian besar komoditas 
global, kecuali minyak kelapa sawit (crude palm oil/CPO), 
cenderung mengalami pelemahan, baik pada komoditas di 
sektor energi, pangan, maupun tambang[²].
Di tengah berbagai dinamika tersebut, perekonomian Indonesia 
menunjukkan daya tahan dengan pertumbuhan sebesar 5,03% 
pada tahun 2024. Capaian ini didorong oleh permintaan domestik 
yang kuat, stabilitas harga, peningkatan investasi, dan kinerja 
ekspor yang membaik[³]. Kinerja ekspor melanjutkan tren 
peningkatan hingga November 2024, terutama didorong oleh 
kenaikan ekspor migas sebesar 2,7% (year-on-year, FOB, USD) dan 
ekspor nonmigas sebesar 9,5% (year-on-year, FOB, USD) [²].
Peningkatan ekspor nonmigas ditopang oleh beberapa 
komoditas utama, seperti minyak kelapa sawit olahan (refined 
palm oil), besi dan baja (terutama feronikel), nikel beserta 
turunannya, mesin dan perlengkapan elektrik, serta alas kaki[²]. 
Selama tahun 2024, inflasi juga terkendali dalam rentang 
2,5±1%, nilai tukar rupiah terjaga, dan Neraca Pembayaran 
Indonesia (NPI) mencatatkan surplus[¹]. Selain itu, sektor-
sektor utama seperti industri pengolahan, perdagangan, 
konstruksi, dan pariwisata terus berkembang[³].

Analysis of Global and National 
Economic Conditions
Throughout 2024, the global economy faced increased 
uncertainty. Global economic growth was recorded at 3.2% 
in 2024. Nevertheless, the global economy continues to face 
challenges such as geopolitical tensions, climate change, debt 
pressures in developing countries, and rising protectionism, 
which may constrain global economic growth potential. ASEAN 
demonstrated stronger resilience, while Europe and the 
United States experienced slowdowns due to political risks 
and protectionist measures. China faced pressure from weak 
domestic demand and a property crisis, while India continued to 
grow, albeit at a slower pace due to food inflation[¹].
Global manufacturing activity experienced contraction for four 
consecutive months but returned to the expansion threshold at 
a level of 50 in November 2024. Europe and the United States 
remained in contraction, driven by weak new orders due to 
low global demand. Toward the end of the year, most global 
commodity prices, except for crude palm oil (CPO), tended to 
weaken across the energy, food, and mining sectors[²].
Amid these dynamics, Indonesia’s economy showed resilience 
with growth of 5.03% in 2024. This achievement was supported by 
strong domestic demand, stable prices, increased investment, 
and improved export performance[³]. Export performance 
continued its upward trend through November 2024, driven 
primarily by a 2.7% year-on-year increase in oil and gas exports 
(FOB, USD) and a 9.5% year-on-year increase in non-oil and gas 
exports (FOB, USD) [²].
The increase in non-oil and gas exports was supported by 
several key commodities, such as refined palm oil, iron and 
steel (mainly ferronickel), nickel and its derivatives, machinery 
and electrical equipment, as well as footwear[²]. Throughout 
2024, inflation remained under control within the range of 
2.5±1%, the rupiah exchange rate was stable, and Indonesia’s 
Balance of Payments (BoP) recorded a surplus[¹]. In addition, 
key sectors such as manufacturing, trade, construction, and 
tourism continued to grow[³].

Analisis Industri Kelapa Sawit dan Posisi 
Perseroan di Industri
Kelapa sawit tetap menjadi salah satu komoditas ekspor utama 
Indonesia, memainkan peran penting dalam perekonomian 
nasional. Hingga September 2024, kinerja ekspor non-migas 
Indonesia mencapai USD181,14 miliar, dengan ekspor lemak 
dan minyak hewani/nabati, yang didominasi oleh produk 
turunan kelapa sawit, menyumbang USD14,43 miliar[¹].
Pada tahun 2024, harga minyak sawit mentah (CPO) mengalami 
fluktuasi. Hingga Desember 2024, Harga Referensi (HR) CPO 
menunjukkan tren peningkatan, menjauhi ambang batas 
USD680 per metrik ton. Peningkatan HR CPO ini dipengaruhi 
oleh berbagai faktor, termasuk peningkatan permintaan dari 
India, Eropa, dan Amerika Utara, yang tidak diimbangi oleh 
produksi global. Selain itu, penurunan produksi, kebijakan 
larangan sementara ekspor CPO dari beberapa negara 
produsen, peningkatan konsumsi domestik di Malaysia, serta 
pelemahan mata uang ringgit turut berkontribusi terhadap 
kenaikan HR CPO[¹].
Data dari Gabungan Pengusaha Kelapa Sawit Indonesia (GAPKI) 
menunjukkan bahwa pada Oktober 2024, produksi CPO dan 
PKO Indonesia mencapai 4,84 juta ton, meningkat 9,69% 
dibandingkan produksi bulan sebelumnya sebesar 4,42 juta ton. 
Konsumsi dalam negeri juga naik menjadi 2,08 juta ton dari 1,99 
juta ton pada bulan September 2024. Sementara itu, ekspor 
meningkat dari 2,26 juta ton pada September menjadi 2,89 juta 
ton pada Oktober 2024[²].
Perseroan berkomitmen untuk memperkuat perannya dalam 
industri kelapa sawit nasional, meskipun menghadapi tantangan 
ekonomi global dan fluktuasi harga. Strategi utama meliputi 
peningkatan produksi, melakukan efisiensi operasional tanpa 
mengurangi pemeliharaan tanaman, dan pengembangan produk 
turunan sawit untuk memperluas pangsa pasar domestik dan 
internasional. Harmonisasi hilirisasi menjadi kunci keberhasilan 
dan kolaborasi strategis dengan mitra industri juga terus 
ditingkatkan guna memperkokoh posisi Perseroan di pasar global.
Analysis of the Palm Oil Industry and  
the Company’s Position in the Industry
Palm oil remains one of Indonesia’s key export commodities, 
playing a significant role in the national economy. As of September 
2024, Indonesia’s non-oil and gas export performance reached 
USD181.14 billion, with exports of animal/vegetable fats and 
oils—dominated by palm oil derivative products—contributing 
USD14.43 billion[¹].
In 2024, crude palm oil (CPO) prices experienced fluctuations. 
As of December 2024, the CPO Reference Price (HR) showed 
an upward trend, moving away from the threshold of USD680 
per metric ton. This increase in the CPO Reference Price was 
driven by various factors, including rising demand from India, 
Europe, and North America, which was not matched by global 
production. In addition, lower production levels, temporary 
export bans from several producing countries, increased 
domestic consumption in Malaysia, and the weakening of the 
ringgit also contributed to the increase in the CPO Reference 
Price[¹].
Data from the Indonesian Palm Oil Association (GAPKI) indicated 
that in October 2024, Indonesia’s CPO and PKO production 
reached 4.84 million tons, an increase of 9.69% compared to 
the previous month’s production of 4.42 million tons. Domestic 
consumption also rose to 2.08 million tons from 1.99 million 
tons in September 2024. Meanwhile, exports increased from 
2.26 million tons in September to 2.89 million tons in October 
2024[²].
The Company is committed to strengthen its role in the national 
palm oil industry albeit facing the global economic challenge 
and price fluctuation. The main strategies include production 
increase, conducting operational efficiency without lowering 
plants’ maintenance and development of palm oil derivative 
products to expand domestic and international market share. 
Harmonization of the downstream is the key to success and 
we keep improving the strategic collaboration with industrial 
partners to bolster the Company position in the global market.

Palm Oil Industry Prospect for 2025
Indonesia’s palm oil industry in 2025 is projected to face 
several significant challenges and opportunities. Although the 
total plantation area continues to expand, production growth 
remains limited due to low productivity, particularly from aging 
trees that have not yet undergone replanting. Accelerating the 
smallholder palm oil replanting program is key to improving 
productivity and meeting both domestic and export demand. 
The government is expected to expedite replanting efforts and 
downstream industrial development to boost productivity[¹].
The Indonesian government plans to fully implement the B40 
biodiesel program in March 2025, which involves blending 40% 
palm oil-based fuel. This initiative aims to reduce reliance on 
diesel imports and increase domestic palm oil absorption. 
However, its implementation may affect the availability of palm 
oil for export and potentially drive up global CPO prices[²].
Indonesia's palm oil industry must also address challenges 
from global policies, such as the European Union Deforestation 
Regulation (EUDR), which requires traceability and sustainability 
within the palm oil supply chain. This regulation could negatively 
impact Indonesia’s palm oil exports to the European Union, even 
though it is not the main export market. Therefore, enhancing 
sustainability standards and traceability is crucial to maintaining 
access to the European market[³].

increase added value, downstream processing and product 
diversification are strategic priorities. Improving human

Analisis Kinerja Segmen
Minyak Kelapa Sawit
Produk utama Perseroan adalah minyak kelapa sawit (CPO), 
yang diproduksi dari tandan buah segar (TBS) hasil perkebunan 
kelapa sawit Perseroan. Sebagai salah satu produsen CPO swasta 
terbesar di Kalimantan Tengah, Perseroan mengekstraksi CPO 
dari mesocarp buah kelapa sawit di delapan Pabrik Kelapa Sawit 
(PKS) yang berlokasi di dalam area perkebunan.
Pada tahun 2024, produksi CPO mencapai 461.414 metrik ton, 
menurun 9,33% dibandingkan tahun 2023 yang sebesar 518.819 
metrik ton. Oil Extraction Rate (OER) tahun 2024 tercatat sebesar 
21,8%, meningkat dibandingkan OER tahun 2023 yang sebesar 
22,1%.
Inti Sawit
Inti sawit (palm kernel/PK) diperoleh dari biji kelapa sawit yang 
dikumpulkan selama proses pengolahan. Pada tahun 2024, 
produksi PK menurun sebesar 9,33% menjadi 89.833 metrik 
ton, dibandingkan dengan 99.082 metrik ton pada tahun 2023. 
PK umumnya digunakan sebagai bahan baku dalam industri 
makanan dan pakan ternak.
Minyak Inti Sawit
Minyak inti sawit (palm kernel oil/PKO) merupakan produk 
turunan dari kelapa sawit. Perseroan memiliki kapasitas dan 
kemampuan untuk memproduksi PKO melalui ekstraksi biji 
kelapa sawit di pabrik pengolahan inti sawit (kernel crushing 
plant/KCP). Pada tahun 2024, produksi PKO Perseroan menurun 
sebesar 8,68% dari 22.327 metrik ton pada tahun 2023 menjadi 
20.338 metrik ton.
In 2024, the palm oil product with the highest production volume 
was fresh fruit bunches, reaching 1,562,446 metric tons, while 
palm kernel oil recorded the lowest production volume. Details 
of the upstream plantation production are presented in the 
following table. 
Segment Performance Analysis  
Crude Palm Oil 
The Company’s main product is crude palm oil (CPO), produced 
from fresh fruit bunches (FFB) harvested from the Company’s oil 
palm plantations. As one of the largest private CPO producers 
in Central Kalimantan, the Company extracts CPO from the 
mesocarp of oil palm fruit at eight palm oil mills (PKS) located 
within the plantation area.  
In 2024, CPO production reached 461,414 metric tons, 
a decline of 9.33% compared to 518,819 metric tons in 2023. 
The Oil Extraction Rate (OER) in 2024 was recorded at 21.8%, an 
increase compared to the 2023 OER of 22.1%.
Palm Kernel 
Palm kernel (PK) is obtained from the seeds of oil palm fruit 
collected during the processing stage. In 2024, PK production 
decreased by 9.33% to 89,833 metric ton, compared to 99,082 
metrik ton in 2023. PK is generally used as raw material in the 
food and animal feed industries.  
Palm Kernel Oil 
Palm kernel oil (PKO) is a derivative product of oil palm. 
The Company has the capacity and capability to produce PKO 
through the extraction of palm kernels at its kernel crushing 
plant (KCP). In 2024, the Company’s PKO production declined 
by 8.68% from 22,327 metric tons in 2023 to 20,338 metric tons.

Rp11,77 triliun, menurun 0,39% atau Rp46 miliar dibandingkan

dipengaruhi oleh menurunnya aset tidak lancar.
Aset lancar Perseroan meningkat 4,06% atau Rp204 miliar,

saldo uang muka pembelian barang dan jasa dari beberapa 
Entitas Anak.

oleh kenaikan biaya perizinan, termasuk pembayaran provisi 
sumber daya hutan dan dana reboisasi sebesar Rp146 miliar.

1.	 Individual or immediate family member is considered 
related to the Business Group if that person:
a.	 Have control or joint control over the Company;
b.	 Have significant influence over the Company; or
c.	 Are key management personnel of the Company or its 
Parent Entity.
2.	 An entity is considered related to the Company if it meets 
any of the following criteria:
a.	 The entity and the Company are members of the same 
Business Group (i.e., Parent Entity, Subsidiaries, and 
subsequent Subsidiaries are related to other entities).
b.	 One entity is an Associate or Joint Venture of the other 
entity (or an Associate or Joint Venture that is part of 
the same Business Group as the other entity).
c.	 Both entities are Joint Ventures of the same third party.
d.	 One entity is a Joint Venture of a third entity, and the 
other is an Associate of the third entity.
e.	 The entity is a post-employment benefit program for 
the employee benefits of either the reporting entity or 
an entity related to the reporting entity. If the reporting 
entity is the entity that administers the program, 
the sponsor entity is also considered related to the 
reporting entity. If the reporting entity is the organizer of 
the program, then the sponsor entity is also considered 
related to the reporting entity.

The Company implements an affiliate transaction review

stakeholders, in accordance with applicable regulations, 
including PSAK and Financial Services Authority Regulation 
No. 42/POJK.04/2020 concerning Affiliated Transactions and 
Conflict of Interest Transactions (POJK 42). The implementation 
of this policy ensures transparency and fairness in transactions 
with related parties.
Under POJK 42, affiliated transactions are defined as activities 
or transactions carried out by a Public Company with company 
affiliates, or with affiliates of members of the Board of Directors, 
Board of Commissioners, Major Shareholders, or Controlling 
Shareholders. This regulation also governs the procedural 
obligations that must be fulfilled by Public Companies 
when conducting affiliated transactions, including certain 
exemptions. Routine, recurring, and ongoing transactions that 
are directly related to the Company’s business activities for 
revenue generation must be disclosed in the annual Financial 
Statements in accordance with applicable provisions.
