4.1. Tinjauan Ekonomi dan Industri
4.1. Economic and Industry Analysis
Pertumbuhan 
ekonomi 
global 
tahun 
2023 diperkirakan hanya mencapai 2,9%, 
dibandingkan 8,7% di tahun 2022. Katalis 
utama penurunan pertumbuhan ini ada-
lah peperangan dan tensi geopolitik tinggi 
yang terjadi di beberapa negara termasuk 
Timur Tengah dan Asia. Selain itu kenaikan 
harga komoditi yang menyebabkan inflasi 
signifikan di seluruh dunia juga direspon 
dengan kenaikan tingkat suku bunga dan 
persepsi risiko yang meningkat, sehingga 
investasi langsung asing hanya 3% dan ter-
fokus pada ekonomi-ekonomi maju. 
Di Indonesia, pertumbuhan ekonomi ter-
catat 5,05% dengan tingkat inflasi tetap 
terjaga di 2,57%. Bank Indonesia telah me-
naikkan tingkat suku bunga acuan 2 kali 
menjadi 6% sampai dengan akhir tahun. 
Realisasi investasi 2023  mencapai Rp1.418,9 
triliun tumbuh 17,5% dengan total penyera-
pan tenaga kerja 1.823.543 orang. 
Ekspor sektor perikanan Indonesia pada 
tahun ini belum mencapai target. Nilai ek-
spor produk perikanan tahun 2023 hanya 
berada di angka US$5,6 miliar, 83,5% dari 
target US$6,7 miliar. Sampai dengan triwu-
lan ketiga, produksi perikanan Indonesia 
mencapai 24,74 juta ton, dan rasio ekspor 
ikan dari hasil perikanan yang diterima oleh 
negara tujuan ekspor sebesar 99,84% Be-
berapa kendala yang dihadapi sektor peri-
kanan Indonesia adalah tata kelola sumber 
daya yang belum mencapai standar inter-
nasional, seperti implementasi penangka-
pan ikan terukur. 
Global economic growth in 2023 was es-
timated reach 2.9% compared to 8.7% in 
2022. The main catalyst for this decline 
in growth is war and high geopolitical 
tensions occuring in several countries 
including the Middle East and Asia. 
Apart from that the increase in com-
modity prices which caused significant 
inflation throughout the world was also 
responded to by increasing risk percep-
tions, so that foreign direct investment 
was only 3% and focused on advanced 
economies.
In Indonesia, economic growth was re-
corded at 5.05% with the inflation rate re-
maining maintained at 2.57%. Bank Indo-
nesia has raised its benchmark interest 
rate twice to 6% until the end of the year. 
Investment realization in 2023 reached 
Rp1,418.9 trillion growing 17.5% with a total 
employment of 1,823,543 people.
Exports from the Indonesian fisheries 
sector this year have not reached the 
target. The export value of fishery pro-
ducts in 2023 only US$5.6 billion,83.5% 
of the target of US$6.7 billion. As of the 
third quarter, Indonesia’s fisheries pro-
duction reached 24.74 million tons, and 
the ratio of fish exports from fishery 
products received by export destination 
countries was 99.84%. Several obstacles 
faced by the Indonesian fisheries sec-
tor are resource management which 
has not yet reached international stan-
dards, such as the implementation of 
measured fishing.

4.2. Operational Analysis of
Business Segments
Sebagian besar produksi Perusahaan diek-
spor ke Amerika Utara (sekitar 77%), Asia 
Pasifik (sekitar 14%) dan Eropa (sekitar 9%). 
Most of the Company’s production is ex-
ported to North America (about 77%), Asia 
Pacific (about 14%) and Europe (about 9%).
Pada tahun ini, Amerika Serikat melakukan 
pembelian boga bahari yang signifikan dari 
Jepang setelah China melarang impor boga 
bahari dari Jepang. Pelarangan tersebut 
dilakukan sebagai respon atas pelepasan 
air dari pabrik nuklir Fukushima di perairan. 
Pelepasan ini masih menjadi debat dalam 
aspek keamanan terhadap lingkungan hid-
up di sekitar. Pembelian Amerika Serikat 
adalah tanda dukungan terhadap aksi Je-
pang serta untuk memelihara hubungan 
baik dengan Jepang. 
Pasar Eropa mengalami kenaikan harga 
boga bahari yang diiringi dengan penyu-
sutan konsumsi. Tekanan kenaikan harga 
juga diperberat dengan inflasi yang cukup 
tinggi dan berdampak kepada ekspor dan 
daya beli masyarakat Eropa. Pertumbuhan 
Eropa diperkirakan turun ke 1,3% pada tahun 
ini dibandingkan 2,7% pada tahun 2022. 
China’s restriction on Japanese sea-
food imports led to a notable increase 
in seafood purchases by the United 
States from Japan this year. In reaction 
to water leakage into the seas from the 
Fukushima nuclear plant, the ban was 
implemented. Concerns about this re-
lease’s environmental security are still 
up for discussion. By making the pur-
chase, the US sought to retain cordial 
ties with Japan while demonstrating 
support for Japan’s actions.
Prices for seafood increased in the Eu-
ropean market, but consumption fell at 
the same time. The impact of increased 
costs on exports and the purchasing 
power of European society is further 
compounded by relatively high infla-
tion. Growth in Europe is predicted to 
drop from 2.7% in 2022 to 1.3% this year.

used to purchase raw material of crab 
worth. Advances as of December 31, 2023, 
were recorded at Rp14.76 billion, down 
from Rp25.25 billion in 2022.

Rp43.85 billion or 62.56% to Rp113.94 bil-

the Company’s fixed assets, particularly 
in land, buildings and infrastructures. The 
depreciation expenses this year were re-
corded at Rp8.07 billion.

crease by 2.07% or Rp10.39 billion to 
Rp511.30 billion. Sales are still domi-
nated by export in terms of destina-
tion and crab in terms of product. 
While local sales increased to Rp5.21 
billion and tuna sales increased to 
Rp10.73 billion.

2023 Annual Report and Sustainability Report 
Laba Bruto 
Laba bruto tercatat Rp42,64 miliar, 
turun dibandingkan tahun 2022 kare-
na kenaikan harga-harga bahan baku. 
Penurunan laba bruto disebabkan oleh 
belum optimalnya kinerja penjualan 
tuna. Marjin laba kotor menjadi 8,3% 
pada tahun ini.
Beban Operasi
Perusahaan membelanjakan beban operasi 
sebesar Rp34,24 miliar, turun sebesar Rp16,14 
miliar dari tahun 2022. Beban operasi terdiri 
atas beban pemasaran sebesar Rp9,02 mi- 
liar, beban umum dan administrasi Rp28,28 
miliar, beban lain-lain Rp881,54 juta. Perusa-
haan juga mendapatkan penghasilan lain-
lain sebesar Rp3,74 miliar. 
Gross Profit
Gross profit was recorded at Rp42.64 
billion, down from 2022 due to the 
rising prices of raw materials. The 
decrease in gross profit was due to 
the below optimal tuna sales perfor-
mance. This year, gross profit margin 
was 8.3%. 
Operating Expenses
The Company spent operating expens-
es worth Rp34.24 billion, down by Rp16.14 
billion from 2022. Operating expenses 
consists of marketing expenses of Rp9.02 
billion, general and administrative ex-
penses of Rp28.28 billion, other expenses 
of Rp881.54 million. The Company also 
gained other income of Rp3.74 billion.

proved by the shareholders at the Annual 
GMS.

tribution and the Company records posi-

be distributed provided that the compa-

the sum of its issued and paid-up capital 
as well as the mandatory reserves stip-
ulated by the Limited Liability Company
