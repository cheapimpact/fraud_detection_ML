Tinjauan Makro Ekonomi
Macro Economic Review
Pada tahun 2024, lanskap ekonomi global masih berada 
di bawah tekanan, ditandai dengan perlambatan yang 
berkelanjutan. Menurut Dana Moneter Internasional 
(IMF), pertumbuhan global melambat dari 3,3% 
pada tahun 2023 menjadi 3,2% pada tahun 2024. 
Perlambatan ini sebagian besar dipengaruhi oleh 
ketidakpastian global yang masih berlangsung akibat 
ketegangan geopolitik, kebijakan moneter yang lebih 
ketat serta transisi kepemimpinan yang mendorong 
munculnya strategi fiskal baru atau yang sedang 
berkembang di beberapa ekonomi utama.
Di kawasan Asia Timur, pemulihan pasca-COVID masih 
berlangsung secara tidak merata. Di wilayah Asia Timur 
dan Pasifik (Tidak termasuk Tiongkok), PDB tumbuh 
dari 4,3% YoY pada tahun 2023 menjadi 4,7% YoY 
pada tahun 2024. Meskipun demikian, perekonomian 
Indonesia mengalami stagnasi. Berdasarkan data 
dari Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), pertumbuhan PDB 
Indonesia tercatat di angka 5,03% pada tahun 2024, 
lebih rendah dibanding target pemerintah sebesar 
5,2%. Hal ini mencerminkan perlambatan konsumsi 
domestik dan sentimen investasi yang masih lemah.
Volatilitas nilai tukar turut menegaskan tantangan 
dalam kondisi makroekonomi. Meskipun sempat 
menunjukkan penguatan di awal tahun 2024, namun 
nilai tukar Rupiah pernah mengalami depresiasi tajam 
di akhir tahun, ditutup di angka Rp16.218 per USD, 
dipengaruhi oleh meningkatnya aversi risiko global, 
arus keluar modal  dan penguatan Dolar AS.
Ditambah 
dengan 
melambatnya 
pertumbuhan 
sektor digital, kehati - hatian dari para investor serta 
tertundanya 
belanja 
konsumen

seperti kendaraan listrik (EV) akibat ketidakpastian 
kebijakan, kondisi - kondisi ini menciptakan lingkungan 
operasional yang lebih menantang bagi perusahaan 
berbasis teknologi di Indonesia.
In 2024, the global economic landscape remained under 
pressure, marked by a continued slowdown. According 
to the International Monetary Fund (IMF), global growth 
moderated from 3.3% in 2023 to 3.2% in 2024. This 
deceleration was largely influenced by lingering global 
uncertainty from geopolitical tensions, tighter monetary 
policies and leadership transitions that led to new or 
evolving fiscal strategies in several major economies.
Throughout the East Asian region, post COVID recovery is 
still inconsistent. Within the East Asia and Pacific region 
(Excluding China), the GDP increased from 4.3% YoY in 2023 
to 4.7% YoY in 2024. Despite this, Indonesia’s economy 
experienced stagnation. Based on data from  the Central 
Statistics Agency (BPS), Indonesia's GDP growth was 
recorded at 5.03% in 2024, lower than the government's 
target of 5.2%. This reflects slowing domestic consumption 
and still weak investment sentiment.
Exchange rate volatility further highlights the challenges 
within the macroeconomic landscape. Although the 
Rupiah showed some strengthening in early 2024, it 
experienced a sharp depreciation toward the end of the 
year, closing at IDR 16,218 per USD, driven by rising global 
risk aversion, capital outflows and the strengthening of 
the US Dollar.
Combined with softer digital sector growth, a cautious 
investor landscape and delayed consumer spending in 
sectors like EV due to policy uncertainty, these conditions 
created a more difficult operating environment for 
technology-driven companies in Indonesia.

Produk dan Layanan Energi Bersih
Clean Energy Products and Services  
Addressing environmental concerns, the Company is com-
mitted to reducing carbon emissions in the transportation 
sector through the development of eco-friendly electric 
vehicles. Volta, the Company’s electric motorcycle brand, 
represents this commitment by providing cutting-edge, 
IoT-enabled solutions aimed at achieving sustainability 
targets.
The MCASH Group has intensified its electric vehicle 
initiatives through innovative technologies such as the 
Battery Swapping System (SGB), Charging Battery System 
(SCB) and integrated applications. IoT-enabled features 
in Volta motorcycles provide users with critical insights, 
including battery status, optimizing resource utilization. 
Volta plays a key role in Indonesia’s electric vehicle 
industry, aligning with government initiatives such as the 
2024 subsidy program for electric motorcycles.
In this segment, the Company has succeeded in increasing 
the population of its electric motorcycles (EV) to 22 
thousand units by the end of 2024, from 16 thousand units 
by the end of 2023. The Company, through its subsidiary 
PT Energi Selalu Baru (ESB), also continues to improve 
supporting services by increasing the number of SGB 
and SCB stations, from 295 units by the end of 2023 to 
350 by the end of 2024. Strategic partnerships, including 
collaborations with Microsoft for AI integration and 
government initiatives to expand infrastructure, further 
reinforce the Company’s commitment to sustainable 
mobility.
The macro industry conditions, especially due to the 
temporary suspension of the electric motorcycle subsidy 
program from the Government, have had a negative 
impact on the performance of the Company's clean energy 
segment. Segment revenue decreased by 27.1% YoY, from 
IDR 148.2 billion in 2023 to IDR 108.1 billion in 2024. 
Furthermore, the segment's gross profit only reached IDR 
8.1 billion in 2024 or decreased by 51.8% YoY compared to 
2023 of IDR 16.9 billion.

Struktur Modal
Capital Structure
Manajemen Perseroan mengendalikan struktur modal 
dan melakukan penyesuaian berdasarkan perubahan 
kondisi ekonomi. Untuk mempertahankan dan menye-
suaikan struktur modal, Perseroan dapat mengatur 
ulang pembayaran dividen kepada Pemegang Saham 
atau menerbitkan saham baru. Tidak ada perubahan 
yang dibuat dalam tujuan, kebijakan atau proses sela-
ma periode yang disajikan. Kebijakan Perseroan adalah 
untuk mempertahankan rasio modal yang kuat untuk 
mendapatkan pendanaan dengan biaya yang wajar.
Sebagaimana praktik yang berlaku umum, Perseroan 
mengevaluasi struktur permodalan melalui rasio 
pinjaman bersih (Kas) terhadap modal (Gearing ratio), 
yang dihitung melalui pembagian antara pinjaman 
bersih (Kas) dengan modal. Pinjaman bersih adalah 
jumlah liabilitas berbunga (Interest-bearing liabilities) 
sebagaimana disajikan di dalam laporan posisi 
keuangan, dikurangi dengan jumlah kas dan setara 
kas, sedangkan modal meliputi seluruh komponen 
ekuitas dalam laporan posisi keuangan. Per akhir 2024, 
Perseroan mencatatkan posisi kas neto sebesar Rp 
126,6 miliar, sedangkan gearing Perseroan tetap kuat 
dengan rasio pinjaman bersih (Kas) terhadap modal 
sebesar (0,11).
The Group’s management controls its capital structure 
and makes adjustments, based on changes in economic 
conditions. To maintain and adjust the capital structure, 
the Group may reset dividend payments to shareholders or 
issue new shares. No changes were made in the objectives, 
policies or processes during the period presented. The 
Group’s policy is to maintain healthy capital ratios in 
order to secure financing at a reasonable cost.
As a generally accepted practice, the Group evaluates 
its capital structure through Net debt (Cash)-to-equity 
ratio (Gearing ratio), which is calculated as net debt 
(Cash) divided by total capital. Net debt (Cash) is total 
interest-bearing liabilities as presented in the statement 
of financial position, less cash and near-cash, whereas 
total equity encompasses all equity components in the 
statement of financial position. At the end of 2024, the 
Company recorded a net cash position amounting to IDR 
126.6 billion. Meanwhile, the Company’s gearing remains 
strong with net debt (Cash)-to-equity ratio of (0.11).

Aspek Pemasaran
Marketing Aspect
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan memfokuskan upaya 
pemasarannya untuk memperkuat perannya sebagai 
enabler teknologi bagi para peritel dan pelaku 
usaha di seluruh Indonesia. Meskipun Perseroan 
memulai sebagai platform distribusi produk digital 
seperti isi ulang pulsa dan voucher listrik. Ekosistem 
Perseroan telah berkembang menjadi lebih luas 
dengan solusi yang membantu pertumbuhan baik 
bagi Perseroan maupun mitra ritelnya. Pada intinya, 
Perseroan merupakan perusahaan teknologi yang 
memberdayakan bisnis tradisional maupun modern 
dengan alat yang mereka butuhkan untuk berkembang 
dalam ekonomi digital.
Strategi pemasaran Perseroan tahun ini difokuskan 
pada beberapa tujuan utama berikut:
-	
Memperkuat 
merek 
sebagai 
mitra 
teknologi 
terpercaya bagi peritel dan pelaku usaha
-	
Membuat teknologi menjadi mudah dipahami dan 
diadopsi oleh toko - toko tradisional
-	
Mempromosikan produk melalui kampanye digital, 
program musiman dan interaksi langsung
-	
Membangun loyalitas serta hubungan jangka 
panjang dengan mitra bisnis
-	
Meningkatkan kesadaran akan manfaat solusi 
digital dan energi bersih dalam kehidupan sehari - 
hari
Perseroan ingin menunjukkan bahwa transformasi 
digital bukan hanya untuk perusahaan besar, ini juga 
merupakan peluang yang dapat dimanfaatkan oleh 
toko - toko kecil dan pelaku usaha skala kecil.
Perseroan terus mendukung toko - toko kecil dan peritel 
lokal dengan membantu mereka menyediakan produk 
digital bagi pelanggan mereka. Upaya pemasaran 
difokuskan pada edukasi produk, promosi musiman 
dan strategi bundling yang memudahkan para peritel 
untuk menawarkan produk digital kepada konsumen 
akhir. Peritel - peritel ini merupakan inti dari jaringan 
kami dan membantu mereka berkembang merupakan 
kunci bagi keberhasilan berkelanjutan Perseroan.
In 2024, the Company focused its marketing efforts on 
reinforcing its role as a technology enabler for retailers 
and businesses across Indonesia. While the Company 
started as a platform for digital product distribution like 
phone top-ups and electricity vouchers. It has grown into 
a broader ecosystem with solutions that help both the 
Company and its retail partners grow. At its core, our 
Company is a tech company that empowers traditional 
and modern businesses with the tools they need to thrive 
in a digital economy.
The company’s marketing strategy this year focused on a 
few key goals:
-	
Strengthening the brand as a trusted tech partner for 
retailers and businesses
-	
Making technology easy to understand and adopt for 
traditional stores
-	
Promoting its products through digital campaigns, 
seasonal programs and direct engagement
-	
Creating loyalty and long-term relationships with 
business partners
-	
Raising awareness about how digital solutions and 
clean energy can benefit everyday life
The Company wants to show that digital transformation 
isn’t just for big companies, it’s something that small 
shops and businesses can take advantage of too.
The Company continued to support small shops and 
local retailers by helping them offer digital products to 
their customers. Marketing efforts centered on product 
education, seasonal promotions and bundling strategies 
that made it easier for retailers to provide digital products 
to their end customers. These retailers are at the heart 
of our network and helping them grow is key to the 
Company’s continued success.

	 Amendments to PSAK 201, “Presentation of Financial 
Statements”: Classification of Liabilities as Current or 
Non-current
The 
narrow-scope 
amendments 
to 
PSAK 
201, 
“Presentation of Financial Statements” clarify that 
liabilities are classified as either current or non-
current, depending on the rights that exist at the end 
of the reporting period.
Classification is unaffected by the expectations of the 
entity or events after the reporting date (e.g the receipt 
of a waiver or a breach of covenant). The amendments 
also clarify what PSAK 201 means when it refers to the 
‘settlement’ of a liability.
The amendments could affect the classification of 
liabilities, particularly for entities that previously 
considered management’s intentions to determine 
classification and for some liabilities that can be 
converted into equity.
	 Amendments to PSAK 207, “Statement of Cash Flows” 
and PSAK 107, “Financial Instruments - Disclosures”: 
Supplier Finance Arrangements
These amendments clarify the characteristics of 
supplier finance arrangements and require additional 
disclosure of such arrangements. The disclosure 
requirements in the amendments are intended to 
assist users of financial statements in understanding 
the effects of supplier finance arrangements on an 
entity’s liabilities, cash flows and exposure to liquidity 
risk.
