Tinjauan Ekonomi
Economic Landscape
Dinamika geopolitik dunia yang cepat berubah telah 
memengaruhi perkembangan ekonomi global sepanjang tahun 
2024, dan diprakirakan akan terus berlanjut. Pertumbuhan 
ekonomi global pada 2024 diprakirakan melambat menjadi 
3,2% dari 3,3% pada 2023 dan diproyeksikan tetap 3,2% 
pada 2025, serta melambat lebih lanjut menjadi 3,1% pada 
2026. Divergensi pola pertumbuhan antarnegara juga 
berlanjut seiring dengan meningkatnya fragmentasi ekonomi 
dan perdagangan global. Ekonomi AS diprakirakan tetap 
kuat pada 2024 dan 2025 disertai dengan risiko pemanasan 
ekonomi yang berasal dari pembengkakan Defisit Fiskal. 
Sementara itu, pertumbuhan ekonomi Tiongkok dan Kawasan 
Eropa diprakirakan akan melanjutkan perlambatan seiring 
dengan lemahnya permintaan domestik dan dampak dari 
kebijakan tarif dagang yang diterapkan oleh Pemerintah AS. 
Di sisi lain, prospek pertumbuhan ekonomi India, Indonesia, 
serta sejumlah negara Emerging Market Economies (EMEs) 
diprakirakan masih tetap positif, didorong oleh permintaan 
domestik, 
meskipun 
menghadapi 
potensi 
perlambatan 
ekspor akibat penurunan pertumbuhan ekonomi global dan 
fragmentasi perdagangan dunia.
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia di tahun 2024 diperkirakan 
berada dalam kisaran 4,7-5,5%. Dari sisi lapangan usaha, 
pertumbuhan ekonomi ditopang oleh sektor ekonomi yang 
cenderung padat modal, dan karenanya sektor ekonomi 
yang padat tenaga kerja perlu lebih didorong ke depan. 
Perkembangan 
ini 
tercermin 
pada 
sektor-sektor 
yang 
berorientasi ekspor dan sebagai hasil dari hilirisasi Sumber 
Daya Alam seperti Pertambangan dan Penggalian, serta Industri 
Pengolahan yang tumbuh relatif tinggi. Sektor-sektor yang 
mencerminkan pembangunan infrastruktur dan konektivitas 
seperti Konstruksi, serta Transportasi dan Pergudangan juga 
mendukung kinerja pertumbuhan ekonomi Nasional. Selain 
itu, sektor-sektor yang sejalan dengan kenaikan konsumsi 
masyarakat dan digitalisasi seperti Perdagangan Besar dan 
Eceran, 
Akomodasi 
dan 
Makanan-Minuman 
(Akmamin), 
serta Informasi dan Komunikasi, juga makin tumbuh tinggi. 
Demikian pula berbagai sektor jasa seperti Jasa Keuangan dan 
Asuransi, Jasa Perusahaan, serta Jasa Kesehatan dan Kegiatan 
Sosial yang terus menunjukkan peningkatan.
Perkembangan berbagai lapangan usaha ini menunjukkan 
pola pemulihan ekonomi Indonesia Pasca Covid-19 yang 
makin kuat, dari sektor-sektor yang makin pulih dari dampak 
memar (scarring effect) Covid-19 pada periode 2021-2022 
ke sektor-sektor yang berorientasi ekspor pada periode 
2022-2024, dan selanjutnya ke sektor-sektor untuk makin 
mendorong permintaan domestik ke depan.

The world’s fast-changing geopolitical dynamics have affected 
global economic development throughout 2024, and are 
expected to continue doing so. Global economic growth in 2024 
is forecast to slow to 3.2% from 3.3% in 2023 and is projected to 
remain at 3.2% in 2025, and slow further to 3.1% in 2026. The 
divergence of growth patterns between countries also continues 
along with the increasing fragmentation of the global economy 
and trade. The US economy is forecast to remain robust in 2024 
and 2025 coupled with the risk of economic warming stemming 
from the swelling fiscal deficit. Meanwhile, economic growth in 
China and the Euro Area is expected to continue to slow down 
along with weak domestic demand and the impact of trade tariff 
policies implemented by the US Government. On the other hand, 
the economic growth prospects of India, Indonesia, as well as a 
number of Emerging Market Economies (EMEs) are predicted to 
remain positive, driven by domestic demand, despite the potential 
slowdown in exports due to the decline in global economic growth 
and fragmentation of world trade.
Indonesia’s economic growth in 2024 is estimated to be in the 
range of 4.7-5.5%. In terms of business sectors, economic growth is 
underpinned by economic sectors that tend to be capital intensive, 
and therefore labor-intensive economic sectors need to be 
further encouraged going forward. This development is reflected 
in export-oriented sectors and as a result of the downstreaming 
of Natural Resources such as Mining and Quarrying, as well as 
Processing Industry which grew relatively high. Sectors reflecting 
infrastructure development and connectivity such as Construction, 
and Transportation and Warehousing also supported the National 
economic growth performance. In addition, sectors that are in line 
with the increase in public consumption and digitalization such 
as Wholesale and Retail Trade, Accommodation and Food and 
Beverage, and Information and Communication, also grew higher. 
Similarly, various service sectors such as Financial and Insurance 
Services, Corporate Services, and Health and Social Services 
continue to show improvement.
The development of these various business sectors shows a 
stronger pattern of Indonesia’s post-Covid-19 economic recovery, 
from sectors that are increasingly recovering from the scarring 
effect of Covid-19 in the 2021-2022 period to export-oriented 
sectors in the 2022-2024 period, and then to sectors to further 
boost domestic demand going forward.

Growth in the Health and Social Services sector is expected to be 
in the range of 7.7-8.5% in 2024, higher than the 2023 growth 
rate of 4.76%.
In 2024, the Health and Hospitals industry in Indonesia experienced 
significant growth driven by regulatory reforms, increased 
Government spending, and a focus on Digital Transformation.
The enactment of Government Regulation No. 28 of 2024 (PP 
28/2024) signifies a significant change in Indonesia’s healthcare 
landscape. This regulation is part of the Health Transformation 
initiative aimed at establishing a resilient, independent, and 
inclusive health system in the country. With the issuance of this 
regulation, 26 (twentysix) Government Regulations and 5 (five) 
Presidential Regulations are rendered invalid.
According to Indonesia’s 2024 State Budget, the health sector 
budget amounts to Rp187.5 trillion, representing an 8.7% 
increase compared to the 2023 health budget. This budget aims 
to strengthen the health system through various transformations, 
including enhancements to primary and referral services, health 
resilience, health financing, human resources, and technology. 
Additionally, the development of the health sector prioritizes 
efforts to reduce the prevalence of stunting and address 
catastrophic diseases.
In 2024, the Digital Health initiative gained significant 
momentum, with the Ministry of Health implementing the Digital 
Health Transformation Strategy Blueprint. This blueprint is 
designed to foster a Healthy Indonesia in collaboration with all 
stakeholders within the health industry through the Indonesia 
Health Services (IHS) Platform. The IHS Platform serves as a 
digital health ecosystem that provides data connectivity, analysis, 
and services to support and integrate various health applications 
across Indonesia. The implementation of this blueprint is expected 
to guide Indonesia toward a measurable and directed digital 
transformation, fostering the development of an integrated and 
sustainable health service system.

satu  indikator untuk mengukur kinerja Perseroan dalam

bagi Pemegang Saham. Pada tahun 2024, secara umum rasio

dengan tahun sebelumnya. Hal tersebut dipengaruhi oleh 
meningkatnya laba yang  diperoleh Perseroan. 
Net Cash Provided by Operating Activities
Net cash provided by the Company from operating activities 
in 2024 showed an increase of 190.28% to Rp81.80 billion, 
compared to Rp28.18 billion in 2023. This was mainly due to the 
increase in cash receipts  from patients by 22.88%.
Net Cash Used in Investing Activities
In 2024, the Company recorded a decrease in net cash used in 
investing activities to Rp41.61 billion compared to the previous 
year of Rp54.67 billion. This was mainly due to the withdrawal 
of time deposits.
Net Cash Used in Financing Activities
In 2024, the net cash used in financing activities amounted 
to Rp37.23 billion, a decrease of 58.56% compared to 2023, 
which amounted to Rp89.82 billion. The decrease was mostly 
because payment for cash dividends in 2024 were lower than 
that in 2023.
Profitability Ratio
The Company uses profitability ratio as one of the indicators to 
measure the Company’s performance in order to generate profits 
and value accretion for Shareholders. In 2024, in general, the 
Company’s profitability ratio increased against last year. This 
was influenced by the increase in profit earned by the Company.

Business Development Strategy for 2025
Dalam upaya meningkatkan kualitas hidup masyarakat, 
Perseroan berkomitmen untuk menyediakan jasa layanan

Untuk mencapai tujuan tersebut, Perseroan menerapkan 
strategi pemasaran yang efektif dan adaptif  untuk 
meningkatkan jumlah kunjungan pasien ke rumah sakit dan

Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan telah menetapkan beberapa 
strategi pengembangan usaha, yang meliputi:

Pusat penanganan stroke terpadu, baik dalam pencegahan,

pembuluh darah yang didukung oleh para ahli dan peralatan 
medis dengan teknologi terbaru dan fasilitas lengkap.
3.	Acute and Trauma
	
Layanan kesehatan yang disediakan untuk menangani 
pasien  khusus  yang  mengalami  trauma  atau  penyakit 
akibat kecelakaan kerja secara komprehensif yang didukung 
oleh peralatan medis dengan teknologi dan fasilitas terbaru 
dan lengkap.
4.	Robotic Medical Rehabilitation
	
Pusat rehabilitasi Medik menggunakan teknologi Robot 
LEXO® dan DIEGO® (Robotic Assisted Rehabilitation) 
membantu pemulihan pasien pasca stroke yang dinilai lebih 
aman, terukur, dan lebih efektif.

The amendments to PSAK 207 and PSAK 107 clarify the

of Financial Statements in understanding the effects of supplier 
finance and exposure on an entity’s liabilities, cash flows and 
exposure to liquidity risk.
