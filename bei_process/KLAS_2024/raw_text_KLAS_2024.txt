PEREKONOMIAN DAN INDUSTRI
ECONOMIC AND INDUSTRY REVIEW
Pada tahun 2024, perekonomian global masih menghadapi 
tantangan besar akibat ketegangan geopolitik, perlambatan 
perdagangan dunia, serta dampak dari kebijakan moneter ketat yang 
diterapkan oleh bank sentral di berbagai negara. Menurut laporan 
World Economic Outlook (WEO) Januari 2025 dari International 
Monetary Fund (IMF), pertumbuhan ekonomi global pada 2024 
sebesar 3,1%, sedikit lebih tinggi dibandingkan proyeksi awal 2,8% 
tetapi masih di bawah rata-rata pertumbuhan sebelum pandemi.
Selain itu, ketegangan perdagangan antara Amerika Serikat (AS) 
dan Tiongkok tetap menjadi faktor utama yang menghambat 
pertumbuhan volume perdagangan dunia. IMF mencatat bahwa 
pertumbuhan perdagangan global pada 2024 hanya mencapai 
1,3%, lebih rendah dibandingkan tahun 2023 yang mencapai 2,3%. 
Peningkatan kebijakan proteksionisme dan tarif yang diberlakukan 
beberapa negara menyebabkan gangguan rantai pasok, terutama 
pada sektor manufaktur dan teknologi.
Dari sisi inflasi, tekanan harga mulai mereda seiring dengan kebijakan 
suku bunga tinggi yang diterapkan sejak tahun sebelumnya. 
Inflasi global turun dari 6,9% pada 2023 menjadi 5,8% pada 2024, 
meskipun harga energi dan pangan masih mengalami fluktuasi 
akibat krisis geopolitik di beberapa kawasan.
Walaupun di tengah ketidakpastian global, ekonomi Indonesia 
tetap tumbuh stabil. Berdasarkan data Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), 
pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia pada Triwulan IV-2024 mencapai 
5,03% (YoY), sehingga secara keseluruhan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
Indonesia sepanjang tahun 2024 mencapai 5,03%, sedikit lebih 
rendah dibandingkan capaian 5,05% pada 2023.
In 2024, the global economy continued to face significant challenges 
due to geopolitical tensions, a slowdown in global trade, and the 
impact of tight monetary policies implemented by central banks in 
various countries. According to the January 2025 World Economic 
Outlook (WEO) report from the International Monetary Fund (IMF), 
global economic growth in 2024 stood at 3.1%, slightly higher than 
the initial projection of 2.8% but still below the pre-pandemic growth 
average.
Additionally, trade tensions between the United States (US) and 
China remained a major factor hindering global trade volume 
growth. The IMF noted that global trade growth in 2024 reached only 
1.3%, lower than the 2.3% recorded in 2023. The rise of protectionist 
policies and tariffs imposed by several countries disrupted supply 
chains, particularly in the manufacturing and technology sectors.
On the inflation front, price pressures began to ease following the 
high-interest rate policies implemented since the previous year. 
Global inflation fell from 6.9% in 2023 to 5.8% in 2024, although 
energy and food prices continued to fluctuate due to geopolitical 
crises in several regions.
Despite global uncertainty, Indonesia’s economy remained stable. 
According to data from the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), 
Indonesia’s economic growth in Q4-2024 reached 5.03% (YoY), 
bringing the overall economic growth for 2024 to 5.03%, slightly 
lower than the 5.05% recorded in 2023.

MANAGEMENT DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia ditopang oleh peningkatan 
investasi yang tumbuh sebesar 4,61% pada 2024, pertumbuhan 
tertinggi dalam enam tahun terakhir. Selain itu, konsumsi rumah 
tangga tetap menjadi motor utama ekonomi, dengan kontribusi lebih 
dari 53% terhadap total Produk Domestik Bruto (PDB). Pemerintah 
juga mendorong pertumbuhan ekonomi dengan meningkatkan 
belanja sosial melalui program makanan gratis, yang anggarannya 
naik menjadi Rp 171 triliun pada 2025. Program ini diproyeksikan 
dapat menambah 2 poin persentase terhadap pertumbuhan 
ekonomi di tahun mendatang. Dari sisi kebijakan moneter, Bank 
Indonesia (BI) menurunkan suku bunga acuan menjadi 5,75% pada 
Januari 2025 untuk mendukung pertumbuhan ekonomi di tengah 
pelemahan ekspor, konsumsi, dan investasi swasta.
Sementara itu, sektor transportasi dan pergudangan mencatat 
pertumbuhan yang kuat sepanjang tahun 2024, mencerminkan 
peningkatan aktivitas logistik dalam negeri dan ekspor. Menurut data 
BPS, industri transportasi dan pergudangan tumbuh sebesar 14,7% 
pada 2024, menjadikannya salah satu sektor dengan pertumbuhan 
tertinggi dibandingkan sektor Lapangan Usaha lainnya.
Pada semester I 2024, volume angkutan barang domestik melalui 
transportasi laut mengalami peningkatan sebesar 7,5% dibandingkan 
periode yang sama tahun sebelumnya, mencapai 183,2 juta ton. Tren 
pertumbuhan ini sejalan dengan meningkatnya permintaan logistik 
dari sektor manufaktur dan perdagangan yang mulai pulih pasca-
pandemi.
Indonesia’s economic growth was supported by increased 
investment, which grew by 4.61% in 2024, the highest growth 
rate in the past six years. Additionally, household consumption 
remained the main driver of the economy, contributing more than 
53% to the total Gross Domestic Product (GDP). The government 
also promoted economic growth by increasing social spending 
through a free meal program, with its budget rising to IDR 171 trillion 
in 2025. This program is projected to add 2 percentage points to 
economic growth in the coming year. In terms of monetary policy, 
Bank Indonesia (BI) lowered the benchmark interest rate to 5.75% 
in January 2025 to support economic growth amid weakening 
exports, consumption, and private investment.
Meanwhile, the transportation and warehousing sector recorded 
strong growth throughout 2024, reflecting an increase in domestic 
logistics activities and exports. According to BPS data, the 
transportation and warehousing industry grew by 14.7% in 2024, 
making it one of the fastest-growing sectors compared to other 
industries.
In the first half of 2024, domestic freight transportation by sea 
increased by 7.5% compared to the same period the previous year, 
reaching 183.2 million tons. This growth trend aligned with the rising 
logistics demand from the recovering manufacturing and trade 
sectors post-pandemic.

transport and warehousing sector in 2024, including increased 
logistics demand after the elections, sustained export levels, port 
modernization, and rising investment in the transportation and 
warehousing sectors.

to grow in several key sectors, such as mineral commodities, 
coal, and manufactured products. Major export destinations, 
including China, India, and Vietnam, remained potential markets 
for Indonesian products, driving the volume of shipments via sea 
routes. Additionally, government policies aimed at expanding trade 
agreements and reducing export barriers also contributed to the 
growth of the logistics and transportation sectors.

and digitizing logistics systems to improve distribution efficiency.

waiting times and enhance port operational efficiency.
Through these supporting factors, the maritime transport and 
warehousing sector is projected to maintain positive growth in 2025. 
Despite external challenges such as the global economic slowdown 
and protectionist trade policies in some partner countries, this 
sector is expected to remain a key pillar in supporting Indonesia’s 
economic growth.

Menurut proyeksi dari beberapa lembaga keuangan internasional,

tetap stabil di kisaran 5%, sejalan dengan target yang ditetapkan

pertumbuhan ekonomi global yang diperkirakan hanya mencapai 
sekitar 3,0%. Beberapa lembaga telah memperkirakan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi Indonesia untuk tahun 2025, seperti World Bank sebesar 
5,0%, ADB sebesar 5,1%, IMF sebesar 5,0%, dan OECD sebesar 5,2%.
Pemerintah Indonesia menargetkan pertumbuhan ekonomi sebesar 
5,2% pada tahun 2025, dengan fokus pada penguatan investasi, 
peningkatan konsumsi domestik, serta penguatan daya saing industri 
dan perdagangan. Untuk mencapai target tersebut, Indonesia perlu 
menghadapi berbagai tantangan, baik dari faktor eksternal seperti 
perlambatan ekonomi global, ketegangan geopolitik, dan volatilitas 
harga komoditas, maupun faktor internal seperti inflasi domestik, 
defisit transaksi berjalan, serta efisiensi distribusi logistik dan 
infrastruktur.
Sejalan dengan proyeksi pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional, Supply 
Chain Indonesia (SCI) memperkirakan Produk Domestik Bruto 
(PDB) sektor transportasi dan pergudangan akan mencapai sekitar 
Rp1.641 triliun pada tahun 2025, tumbuh sebesar 14,3% dibandingkan 
tahun 2024. Perkiraan ini didasarkan pada pertumbuhan sektor 
transportasi dan pergudangan yang terus mengalami peningkatan 
sepanjang 2024, yang diproyeksikan mencapai 14,7% pada akhir 
tahun. Kontribusi sektor ini terhadap PDB nasional terus meningkat, 
didorong oleh pertumbuhan perdagangan, digitalisasi rantai pasok, 
serta investasi dalam infrastruktur logistik dan transportasi laut.
According to projections from several international financial 
institutions, Indonesia’s economic growth in 2025 is expected to 
remain stable at around 5%, in line with the government’s target. This 
figure is higher than the projected global economic growth, which 
is estimated to reach only about 3.0%. Various institutions have 
forecasted Indonesia’s economic growth for 2025, including the 
World Bank at 5.0%, ADB at 5.1%, IMF at 5.0%, and OECD at 5.2%.  
The Indonesian government has set a growth target of 5.2% for 
2025, focusing on strengthening investment, increasing domestic 
consumption, and enhancing industrial and trade competitiveness. 
To achieve this target, Indonesia must address various challenges, 
including external factors such as the global economic slowdown, 
geopolitical tensions, and commodity price volatility, as well as 
internal factors such as domestic inflation, current account deficits, 
and the efficiency of logistics and infrastructure distribution.  
In line with the national economic growth projections, Supply 
Chain Indonesia (SCI) estimates that the Gross Domestic Product 
(GDP) of the transportation and warehousing sector will reach 
approximately IDR 1,641 trillion in 2025, growing by 14.3% compared 
to 2024. This estimate is based on the sector’s continuous growth 
throughout 2024, projected to reach 14.7% by year-end. The sector’s 
contribution to national GDP continues to increase, driven by trade 
growth, supply chain digitalization, and investment in logistics and 
maritime transport infrastructure.

solid growth amid global economic dynamics.
