uncertain global economic conditions. Data from the 
Central Statistics Agency (BPS) shows that Indonesia's 
economy grew by 5.05% in 2023, although slightly 
slower than the previous year's growth of 5.31%.
Indonesia's economic growth is supported by strong 
domestic demand. Household consumption grew 
by 4.82%, driven by continued mobility, stable 
purchasing power, and high consumer confidence.
Furthermore, Indonesia's economic growth is also 
supported by relatively good export performance, 
albeit lower than the previous year. Indonesia's 
export value in 2023 amounted to USD258.82 billion, 
slightly below the 2022 export achievement of 
USD291.90 billion. This slowdown in export value 
is in line with the moderation in the prices of 
Indonesia's key commodities, such as palm oil and 
coal. Additionally, the economic slowdown in some 
of Indonesia's main trading partner countries also 
contributed to the decline in Indonesia's export value.
Throughout 2023, Indonesia's exports remained 
concentrated in China with a share of 25.66%, the 
United States with a share of 9.57%, and India with 
a share of 8.35%. Meanwhile, Indonesia's exports to 
ASEAN and the European Union had shares of 18.35% 
and 6.78%, respectively, of Indonesia's total exports 
in 2023.
Indonesia's trade balance recorded a surplus of 
USD36.93 billion during 2023. This achievement 
indicates Indonesia's trade performance being 
maintained amidst global economic slowdown.

Analisa Operasi per Segmen Usaha
Business Segment Operational Analysis
Perseroan menjalankan kegiatan usaha di bidang 
jasa pelayaran transportasi sungai dan laut yang 
terbagi atas 2 segmen usaha utama, yaitu pelayaran 
yang dilakukan perusahaan sendiri dan bongkar 
muat yang dilakukan oleh perusahaan entitas anak. 
Pengelompokkan segmen usaha ini telah sesuai 
dengan Pernyataan Standar Akuntansi Keuangan 
(PSAK).
Segmen Pelayaran 
Segmen pelayaran merupakan segmen utama 
bagi Perseroan. Perseroan memberikan layanan 
transportasi pelayaran sungai dan laut untuk 
menunjang transportasi komoditas industri 
kehutanan, batu bara dan CPO. Pelayanan yang 
diberikan dilakukan secara efektif dan efisien, dimulai 
dari pelabuhan loading dan pelabuhan bongkar muat 
yang ada di dalam maupun luar negeri.
Perseroan terus mengembangkan jasa pelayanan 
transportasi dengan menambah armada tug boat dan 
barge untuk memenuhi tuntutan dan kebutuhan pasar 
transportasi, bukan hanya batu bara tetapi komoditas 
lainnya, baik itu domestik maupun internasional yang 
terus berkembang.

previous year-end position of 5.50% to ensure core 
inflation remains controlled.
However, the exchange rate of the Rupiah against 
the US Dollar experienced a depreciation. This is due 
to global economic conditions, resulting in outflows 
from Emerging Markets due to high interest rates in 
advanced economies.
Although export and import values have decreased, 
maritime transportation activities, especially cargo 
transportation, have increased. According to BPS 
data, during January–December 2023, the volume 
of goods transported increased by 9.47% to reach 
351.0 million tons. This is a positive development for 
players in the maritime cargo transportation sector.
The company operates in the river and sea 
transportation services sector, divided into 2 main 
business segments, namely the company's own 
shipping and loading and unloading carried out by 
subsidiary companies. The segmentation of these 
business segments has been in accordance with 
Financial Accounting Standards (PSAK).
Shipping Segment 
The shipping segment is the main segment for the 
Company. The Company provides river and sea 
transportation services to support the transportation 
of forestry, coal, and CPO commodities. The services 
provided are carried out effectively and efficiently, 
starting from loading ports to loading and unloading 
ports both domestically and internationally.
The company continues to develop transportation 
services by adding tugboat and barge fleets to meet 
the demands and needs of the transportation market, 
not only for coal but also for other commodities, both 
domestic and international, which continue to grow.

Di tahun 2023, Perseroan merealisasikan penambahan 
satu armada kapal baru, yaitu satu unit kapal tunda 
(tugboat) dan satu unit tongkang (barge). Dengan 
penambahan armada kapal baru, volume angkut 
Perseroan dapat mencapai 11,17 juta metrik ton (MT) 
atau kurang lebih 1.351 shipment.
Di tahun 2023, segmen pelayaran mencatatkan 
pendapatan neto Rp922,06 miliar, meningkat 20,81% 
dari tahun sebelumnya sebesar Rp763,25 miliar. 
Peningkatan pendapatan neto tersebut sejalan 
peningkatan volume kargo yang diangkut, yaitu dari 
9,65 juta metrik ton (MT) di tahun 2022, menjadi 
11,17 juta MT di tahun 2023, atau meningkat 15,79%.
Seiring dengan peningkatan pendapatan neto, 
laba usaha yang dibukukan Segmen Pelayaran juga 
meningkat 39,54% menjadi Rp169,55 miliar, dari tahun 
sebelumnya sebesar Rp121,51 miliar, sehingga margin 
laba usaha Segmen Pelayaran juga meningkat menjadi 
18,39% dari 15,92% di tahun 2022. Sedangkan laba 
tahun berjalan yang dibukukan Segmen Pelayanan 
tahun 2023 tercatat sebesar Rp157,39 miliar, 
meningkat 35,68% dari tahun sebelumnya sebesar 
Rp116,00 miliar.
Segmen Bongkar Muat 
Segmen Bongkar Muat dijalankan oleh entitas anak 
Perseroan, yaitu PT Hasnur Resources Terminal.
Tahun 2023, Segmen Bongkar Muat membukukan 
peningkatan kinerja yang baik. Volume bongkar 
muat yang dilayani Perseroan mencapai 14 juta MT 
atau meningkat 14,75% dari tahun sebelumnya 
12,2 juta MT, sehingga pendapatan neto yang 
dibukukan Perseroan juga meningkat 8,93% menjadi 
Rp27,00 miliar dari Rp24,79 miliar di tahun 2022. 
Namun demikian, laba tahun berjalan yang dibukukan 
Segmen Bongkar Muat justru mengalami penurunan 
54,19% menjadi Rp2,34 miliar dari Rp4,70 miliar 
di tahun 2022.
In 2023, the Company added one new fleet, namely 
one tugboat and one barge. With the addition of 
new fleet, the Company's transport volume can 
reach 11.17 million metric tons (MT) or approximately 
1,351 shipments.
In 2023, the shipping segment recorded a net revenue 
of Rp922.06 billion, an increase of 20.81% from the 
previous year's Rp763.25 billion. This increase in net 
revenue is in line with the increase in cargo volume 
transported, from 9.65 million metric tons (MT) 
in 2022 to 11.17 million MT in 2023, or an increase 
of 15.79%.
Along with the increase in net revenue, the operating 
profit booked by the Shipping Segment also increased 
by 39.54% to Rp169.55 billion, from Rp121.51 billion 
in the previous year, so that the operating profit 
margin of the Shipping Segment also increased to 
18.39% from 15.92% in 2022. Meanwhile, the current 
year profit booked by the Shipping Segment in 2023 
amounted to Rp157.39 billion, an increase of 35.68% 
from the previous year's Rp116.00 billion.
Loading and Unloading Segment 
The Loading and Unloading Segment is operated 
by the Company's subsidiary, PT Hasnur 
Resources Terminal.
In 2023, the Loading and Unloading Segment 
recorded good performance. The volume of loading 
and unloading services provided by the Company 
reached 14 million MT, an increase of 14.75% from 
the previous year's 12.2 million MT, resulting in a net 
revenue increase of 8.93% to Rp27.00 billion from 
Rp24.79 billion in 2022. However, the current year 
profit booked by the Loading and Unloading Segment 
experienced a decrease of 54.19% to Rp2.34 billion 
from Rp4.70 billion in 2022.

Perseroan berkomitmen untuk memberikan nilai

•	 Dividen pada perseroan bidang sejenis secara

•	 Tidak terdapat negative covenant yang 
menghambat Perseroan untuk membagikan dividen.
Sesuai dengan Undang Undang No. 40 tahun 2007 
tentang Perseroan Terbatas, pembagian dividen

shareholders. Therefore, the company is committed 
to providing returns in the form of dividends to 
shareholders when the company generates profits.
The company has a policy governing the distribution 
of dividends to shareholders, which must consider 
factors such as:
•	 Cash balance, net gearing, return on equity, and 
retained earnings;
•	 Expected financial capacity;
•	 Level of capital expenditures and other projected 
investment plans;
•	 Dividend yield received;
•	 Dividends in similar sector companies globally; and
•	 No negative covenants preventing the company 
from distributing dividends.
In accordance with Law No. 40 of 2007 concerning 
Limited Liability Companies, dividend distribution 
is carried out in accordance with the decision of the 
Annual General Meeting of Shareholders (AGM) or 
Extraordinary General Meeting of Shareholders 
(EGM). Before the end of the financial year, 
interim dividends may be distributed as permitted 
by the Company's Articles of Association. The 
distribution of interim dividends is determined by 
the Board of Directors and approved by the Board 
of Commissioners.
