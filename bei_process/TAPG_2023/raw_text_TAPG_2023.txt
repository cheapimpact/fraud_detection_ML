Di tengah terpaan tantangan ekonomi global, pertumbuhan 
ekonomi Indonesia mampu mencapai 5,05% pada 2023. 
Pertumbuhan ini ditopang oleh komponen pengeluaran 
konsumsi rumah tangga yang tumbuh sebesar 4,82% 
(yoy) serta Pembentukan Modal Tetap Bruto (PMTB) yang 
mencapai 4,40% (yoy). Dari sisi lapangan usaha, sektor 
transportasi dan pergudangan tumbuh signifikan sebesar 
13,96% (yoy). Pada sisi pengeluaran, pertumbuhan tertinggi 
dialami oleh konsumsi lembaga non-profit rumah tangga 
(LNPRT) sebesar 9,83% (yoy).
Pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional pada 2023 mampu 
melampaui beberapa negara mitra seperti Malaysia 
(3,77%) dan Korea Selatan (1,36%), serta lebih tinggi dari 
pertumbuhan ekonomi negara G20 seperti Amerika Serikat 
(2,5%), Perancis (0,9%) maupun Jerman yang mengalami 
kontraksi (-0,3%).
Dari sektor kelapa sawit, Gabungan Pengusaha Kelapa 
Sawit Indonesia (GAPKI) mencatat adanya peningkatan 
konsumsi minyak sawit domestik sebesar 25,4 juta ton 
pada 2023 atau naik 9,08% dari 23,28 juta ton pada 2022. 
Konsumsi biodiesel mendominasi dengan penyerapan 
11,6 juta ton. Di sisi lain, peningkatan konsumsi biodiesel 
juga memengaruhi kinerja ekspor yang kemungkinan 
berkurang sebesar 4,13% atau sekitar 29 juta ton.
Tinjauan Ekonomi
Economy Overview
Amid global economic challenges, Indonesia’s economy 
grew 5.05% in 2023. This growth was bolstered by 
household consumption expenditure, which grew by 4.82% 
(yoy), and Gross Fixed Capital Formation (PMTB), which 
reached 4.40% (yoy). From a business perspective, the 
transportation and warehousing sector grew significantly 
by 13.96% (yoy). On the expenditure side, the highest 
growth was experienced by household non-profit 
institutions (LNPRT) consumption at 9.83% (yoy).
National economic growth in 2023 exceeded several 
peer countries like Malaysia (3.77%) and South Korea 
(1.36%) and was higher than the economic growth of G20 
countries such as the United States (2.5%), France (0.9%), 
or Germany which experienced a contraction (-0.3%).
From the palm oil sector, the Indonesian Palm Oil 
Association (IPOA) recorded an increase in domestic palm 
oil consumption of 25.4 million tons in 2023, up 9.08% 
from 23.28 million tons in 2022. Biodiesel consumption 
dominated, with an uptake of 11.6 million tons. On the 
other hand, the increase in biodiesel consumption also 
affected export performance, which is likely to decrease 
by 4.13% or about 29 million tons.

Management Discussion and Analysis
Beban Pokok Penjualan
Beban pokok penjualan Perseroan berasal dari beban 
pokok penjualan sawit, karet dan turunannya. Seiring 
dengan penurunan laba Perseroan, beban pokok 
penjualan pada 2023 turut menjadi salah satu penyebab 
utama peningkatan sebesar 9% dari Rp5.630 miliar pada 
2022 menjadi Rp6.109 miliar. Hal ini dikarenakan sebagian 
besar oleh peningkatan harga pupuk seiring meningkatnya 
harga energi global.
Laba Bruto
Perseroan membukukan laba bruto sebesar Rp2.217 
miliar pada 2023, menurun dari tahun 2022 yaitu Rp3.716 
miliar, utamanya dikarenakan oleh penyesuaian harga 
komoditas global dan produksi yang mengalami koreksi 
natural seiring iklim yang lebih kering.
Laba Usaha
Laba usaha Perseroan mengalami penurunan sebesar 
55% dari Rp3.032 miliar pada 2022 menjadi Rp1.373 miliar 
pada 2023. Hal ini didorong oleh penurunan penjualan 
dan peningkatan biaya operasional dari perseroan.
Laba Tahun Berjalan
Laba tahun berjalan Perseroan mengalami penurunan 
sebesar 46% dari sebesar Rp3.089 miliar pada 2022 
menjadi Rp1.661 miliar pada 2023 yang disebabkan oleh 
koreksi natural pada produksi dan penyesuaian harga 
secara global.
Laba Komprehensif Tahun Berjalan
Pada 2023, Perseroan membukukan laba komprehensif 
tahun berjalan sebesar Rp1.681 miliar, menurun sebesar 
46% dari Rp3.113 miliar pada 2022. Penurunan ini 
disebabkan oleh peningkatan biaya pokok secara global 
dan penyesuaian harga komoditas serta penurunan 
produksi akibat iklim.
Cost of Sales
The Company’s cost of goods sold came from the cost 
of palm oil, rubber and their derivatives. Along with the 
decrease in the Company’s profit, the cost of goods sold 
in 2023 became one of the leading causes of an increase 
of 9% from Rp5,630 billion in 2022 to Rp6,109 billion. This 
mainly was due to increased fertilizer prices following the 
rising global energy prices.
Gross Profit
The Company recorded a gross profit of Rp2,217 billion 
in 2023, down from Rp3,716 billion in 2022, mainly due 
to the adjustment of global commodity prices and natural 
production corrections in a drier climate.
Operating Profit
The Company’s operating profit decreased by 55% from 
Rp3,032 billion in 2022 to Rp1,373 billion in 2023. This is 
driven by a decrease in sales and increasing operational 
costs for the company.
Profit for the Year
The Company’s profit for the year decreased by 46% 
from Rp3,089 billion in 2022 to Rp1,661 billion in 2023, 
caused by natural corrections in production and global 
price adjustments.
Comprehensive Income for the Year
In 2023, the Company recorded a comprehensive profit 
for the year of Rp1,681 billion, a decrease of 46% from 
Rp3,113 billion in 2022. This decrease was caused by a 
global increase in the cost of goods and commodity price 
adjustments and a reduction in production due to climate.

Saat ini, Indonesia merupakan negara yang mendominasi 
hingga 85% pangsa pasar minyak sawit dunia. Untuk 
itu, pemerintah Indonesia terus berupaya menjaga daya

strategis nasional. Lembaga Oil World (November 2023)

dipengaruhi perubahan iklim global. 
Prospek industri sawit nasional diperkirakan akan lebih 
cerah pada akhir 2024, karena produksi minyak nabati 
global diperkirakan belum akan meningkat signifikan pada

B35 seiring peningkatan jumlah penduduk. Program 
B35 yang diperkirakan akan berjalan dengan baik sejak 
awal tahun diharapkan dapat berkontribusi lebih baik 
dibandingkan 2022. Faktor-faktor di atas diharapkan 
mampu menggerakkan peningkatan konsumsi melalui 
pangan, industri, dan energi dalam negeri.

sebelumnya, namun industri sawit nasional masih memiliki

positif. Estimasi terjadinya fenomena iklim yang netral di

signifikan, yang diharapkan dapat menjaga harga minyak

government continues to strive to maintain the 
competitiveness of the Indonesian palm oil industry by 
strengthening sustainable palm oil production. With the 
global economy still shrouded in uncertainty and projected 
to slow in 2024, Indonesia needs to take strategic steps to 
maintain the performance of the palm oil industry, which 
is one of the nation's strategic industries. The Oil World 
Institute (November 2023) estimates that global vegetable 
oil production growth will slow by 4.2 million tons in 2024, 
influenced by global climate change.
The national palm oil industry prospects are expected 
to brighten by the end of 2024, as global vegetable oil 
production is not likely to increase significantly in 2024, 
which will directly keep CPO prices at a relatively high 
level. CPO and PKO production are expected to grow 
by 5%, reaching 56 million tons. Palm oil consumption is 
also expected to rise by 9% and reach 25 million tons, 
supported by increased consumption of food, industry, 
and rising national energy consumption through the B35 
program as the population grows. The B35 program, 
expected to perform well from the beginning of the year, 
is hoped to contribute better than in 2022. The above 
factors are expected to increase consumption through 
food, industry, and domestic energy.
Although commodity prices have not skyrocketed like 
in previous years, the national palm oil industry still has 
immense potential to show a positive performance. The 
expected occurrence of the neutral climate in 2024 will 
not affect supply significant, which will then cause palm 
oil prices in the global market remains at a relatively 
high level.

Changes of Accounting Principles Implemented by the Company in Fiscal Year
The Company has applied a number of amendments and 
improvements to accounting standards that are relevant 
to its consolidated financial reporting and effective for 
periods beginning on or after January 1, 2023 as follows:
1.	 Amendments to PSAK 1: Presentation of Financial 
Statements Classification of Liabilities as Current 
or Non-current.
The amendments specify the requirements for 
classifying liabilities as current or non-current 
and clarify:
•	
what is meant by a right to defer settlement.
•	
the right to defer must exist at the end of the 
reporting period.
•	
classification is not affected by the likelihood that 
an entity will exercise its deferral right.
•	
only if an embedded derivative in a convertible 
liability is an equity instrument would the terms 
and conditions of a liability will not impact 
its classification.
The amendments had no impact on the Company’s 
consolidated financial statements.
2.	 Amendments to PSAK 1: Presentation of Financial 
Statements - Disclosure of accounting policies.
The amendments provide guidance and examples 
to help entities apply materiality judgements to 
accounting policy disclosures. The amendment aims 
to help entities provide accounting policy disclosures 
that are more useful by replacing the requirement 
for entities to disclose their ‘significant’ accounting 
policies with a requirement to disclose their ‘material’ 
accounting policies and adding guidance on how 
entities apply the concept of materiality in making 
decisions about accounting policy disclosures.
The amendments have had an impact on the Company’s 
disclosures of accounting policies, but not on the 
measurement, recognition or presentation of any items 
in the Company’s consolidated financial statements.

3.	 Amandemen PSAK 16: Aset Tetap - Hasil sebelum 
Penggunaan yang Diintensikan.
Amandemen ini tidak memperbolehkan entitas untuk 
mengurangi suatu hasil penjualan item yang diproduksi 
saat membawa aset tersebut ke lokasi dan kondisi 
yang diperlukan supaya aset dapat beroperasi sesuai 
dengan intensi manajemen dari biaya perolehan 
suatu aset tetap. Sebaliknya, entitas mengakui hasil 
dari penjualan item-item tersebut, dan biaya untuk 
memproduksi item-item tersebut, dalam laba rugi.
Perseroan menerapkan amandemen tersebut secara 
retrospektif hanya untuk etik tetap yang dibuat supaya 
etik siap digunakan pada atau setelah awal periode 
penyajian paling awal etika entitas pertama kali 
menerapkan amandemen tersebut.
Amandemen ini tidak berdampak pada laporan 
keuangan konsolidasian Perseroan karena tidak ada 
penjualan atas item-item yang dihasilkan aset tetap 
yang menjadi tersedia untuk digunakan pada atau 
setelah awal periode sajian paling awal.
4.	 Amandemen PSAK 25: Kebijakan Akuntansi, 
Perubahan Estimasi Akuntansi dan Kesalahan terkait 
Definisi Estimasi Akuntansi.
Amandemen PSAK 25 memperjelas perbedaan antara 
perubahan estimasi akuntansi, perubahan kebijakan 
akuntansi dan koreksi kesalahan. Amandemen juga 
mengklarifikasi bagaimana entitas menggunakan 
teknik pengukuran dan input untuk mengembangkan 
estimasi akuntansi.
Amandemen ini tidak memiliki dampak terhadap 
laporan keuangan konsolidasian Perseroan.
5.	 Amandemen PSAK 46: Pajak Penghasilan tentang 
Pajak Tangguhan Terkait Aset dan Liabilitas Yang 
Timbul Dari Transaksi Tunggal.
Amandemen PSAK 46 Pajak Penghasilan 
mempersempit ruang lingkup pengecualian pengakuan 
awal, sehingga tidak lagi berlaku pada transaksi yang 
menimbulkan perbedaan temporer dapat dikurangkan 
dalam jumlah yang sama dan perbedaan temporer kena 
pajak dan seperti sewa dan liabilitas dekomisioning.
Amandemen ini tidak mempunyai dampak terhadap 
laporan keuangan konsolidasian Perseroan.
3.	 Amendments to PSAK 16: Fixed Assets - Proceeds 
before Intended Use.
The amendments prohibit entities from deducting from 
the cost of an item of fixed assets, any proceeds from 
selling items produced while bringing that asset to the 
location and condition necessary for it to be capable 
of operating in the manner intended by management. 
Instead, an entity recognizes the proceeds from selling 
such items, and the costs of producing those items, 
in the profit or loss.
The Company applies the amendments retrospectively 
only to items of fixed assets made available for use on 
or after the beginning of the earliest period presented 
when the entity first applies the amendment.
These amendments had no impact on the consolidated 
financial statements of the Company as there were no 
sales of such items produced by fixed assets made 
available for use on or after the beginning of the 
earliest period presented.
4.	 Amendments to PSAK 25: Accounting Policies, 
Changes in Accounting Estimates and Errors - 
Definition of Accounting Estimates.
The amendments to PSAK 25 clarify the distinction 
between changes in accounting estimates, changes in 
accounting policies and the correction of errors. They 
also clarify how entities use measurement techniques 
and inputs to develop accounting estimates.
The amendments had no impact on the Company’s 
consolidated financial statements.
5.	 Amendments to PSAK 46: Income Taxes - Deferred 
Tax related to Assets and Liabilities arising from a 
Single Transaction.
The amendments to PSAK 46 Income Taxes narrow 
the scope of the initial recognition exception, so that 
it no longer applies to transactions that give rise to 
equal taxable and deductible temporary differences 
such as leases and decommissioning liabilities.
The amendments had no impact on the Company’s 
consolidated financial statements.
