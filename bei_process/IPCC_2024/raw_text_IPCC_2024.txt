Perekonomian global pada tahun 2024 menghadapi berbagai 
tantangan yang mempengaruhi laju pertumbuhannya. 
Berdasarkan data dari International Monetary Fund (IMF), 
proyeksi pertumbuhan ekonomi global untuk tahun 2024 
diperkirakan mencapai 3,2%, sedikit menurun dibandingkan 
dengan 3,3% pada tahun 2023. Meskipun inflasi global 
menunjukkan tren penurunan, IMF memperingatkan bahwa 
tarif impor yang diusulkan oleh Amerika Serikat dapat menambah 
ketidakpastian ekonomi dan berpotensi memperlambat 
pertumbuhan. 
 Di sisi lain, perekonomian Indonesia pada tahun 2024 mencatat 
pertumbuhan yang positif meskipun sedikit melambat 
dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya. Berdasarkan data dari Badan 
Pusat Statistik (BPS), ekonomi Indonesia tumbuh sebesar 
5,03% secara tahunan (year-on-year/yoy), sedikit menurun 
dibandingkan dengan 5,05% pada tahun 2023. Seluruh lapangan 
usaha menunjukkan pertumbuhan positif, dengan sektor jasa 
lainnya mengalami kenaikan tertinggi sebesar 9,80%. Namun, 
Indonesia menghadapi potensi dampak dari kebijakan tarif impor 
yang diusulkan oleh Amerika Serikat, yang dapat mengurangi 
laju pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia sebesar 0,3 hingga 0,5 
poin persentase.  
Dalam menghadapi dinamika ekonomi global dan domestik, 
pemerintah Indonesia terus berupaya menjaga stabilitas 
ekonomi melalui berbagai kebijakan, termasuk deregulasi, 
pemotongan pajak, dan pelonggaran kebijakan impor untuk 
mengurangi dampak negatif dari kebijakan luar negeri negara 
mitra dagang utama. Upaya ini diharapkan dapat mendukung 
target pertumbuhan ekonomi nasional dan menjaga daya saing 
Indonesia di kancah global.
The global economy in 2024 faced various challenges that affect 
its growth rate. Based on data from the International Monetary 
Fund (IMF), the global economic growth projection for 2024 is 
estimated at 3.2%, slightly lower than 3.3% in 2023. Although 
global inflation showed a downward trend, the IMF warns that 
the import tariffs proposed by the United States could add to 
economic uncertainty and potentially slow growth.
On the other hand, Indonesia's economy in 2024 is recorded 
a positive growth, although slightly slower than the previous 
year. Based on data from the Central Bureau of Statistics (BPS), 
Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.03% year-on-year (yoy), slightly 
decreased from 5.05% in 2023. All business sectors experienced 
a positive growth, with the other services sector experiencing 
the highest increase at 9.80%. However, Indonesia also dealt 
with potential impacts from the import tariff policies proposed 
by the United States, which could reduce Indonesia’s economic 
growth rate by 0.3 to 0.5 percentage points.
In facing the dynamics of the global and domestic economy, the 
Indonesian government continues its efforts to maintain economic 
stability through various policies, including deregulation, tax cuts, 
and easing import regulations to reduce the negative impact 
of foreign policies from key trading partners. These efforts are 
expected to support the national economic growth targets and 
maintain Indonesia’s competitiveness on the global stage.

in demand from household consumer sector, automotive dealers 
and the need for passenger transportation and rental fleets.

from the logistics sector, infrastructure projects, procurement 
of transportation equipment and construction by government 
and private companies.

segment amounted to Rp964.47 billion, a decrease of 6.51% 
compared to the previous year of Rp1,031.62 billion. This 
decrease was mainly due to the optimization of fixed assets as 
well as efficiency efforts in the management of resources carried 
out on an ongoing basis.
Meanwhile, segment liabilities increased by 5.76%, from 
Rp485.39 billion in 2023 to Rp513.37 billion in 2024. This 
increase reflects increased operational activities as well as 
higher short-term commitments to support expansion and 
service improvements. 
This condition shows that despite the decline in assets, the 
segment is still able to maintain a stable financial structure with 
manageable liabilities. Balance sheet strengthening strategy 
continues to be the main focus in supporting sustainable business 
growth amidst the challenges of port industry dynamics.
In 2024, the capital expenditure of Terminal Services and Cargo 
Services segment was recorded at Rp16.66 billion, a significant 
decrease of 39.07% compared to the previous year of Rp27.34 
billion. This decrease reflects the Company's strategy to be more 
selective in capital expenditure allocation, focusing on priority 
projects that provide optimal added value and operational 
efficiency.
In contrast, depreciation and amortization expenses showed 
a slight increase of 1.65%, from Rp105.17 billion in 2023 to 
Rp106.91 billion in 2024. This increase is in line with the addition 
of fixed assets from the previous period which began to undergo 
full depreciation during the current year.

PTOS-C atau Pelindo Terminal Operating System Car merupakan 
sistem digunakan untuk operasi kegiatan di Terminal yang 
dilakukan secara real-time. Mulai dari kegiatan perencanaan, 
booking, realisasi kegiatan, kontrol dan pengawasan sampai 
dengan terbit laporan kinerja dan billing. Cargo atau vehicle 
identification number (VIN) dalam bentuk barcode. Sistem 
ini terintegrasi dengan sistem Car Maker guna mengotomasi 
proses booking dari customer, TPS Online Bea Cukai Ciesa guna 
mengotomasi proses clearance document, mengoptimalkan 
proses kegiatan perencanaan, pelaksanaan dan pemantauan 
kegiatan di terminal. Selain itu, dengan pencatatan secara real-
time pemilik cargo dapat mengetahui pergerakan cargo yang ada 
di dalam terminal dan dapat dikirimkan ke sistem milik pemilik 
barang dalam hal ini car maker melalui sistem yang digunakan 
oleh customer, yaitu sistem IBS.
PTOS-C or Pelindo Terminal Operating System Car is a system 
used for operational activities in the Terminal which is carried 
out in real-time. Starting from planning activities, booking, 
activity realization, control and supervision to the issuance of 
performance reports and billing. Cargo or vehicle identification 
number (VIN) in the form of a barcode. This system is integrated 
with the Car Maker system to automate the booking process 
from customers, TPS Online Customs Ciesa to automate the 
clearance document process, optimize the planning process, 
implementation and monitoring of activities in the terminal. In 
addition, with real-time recording, cargo owners can find out 
the movement of cargo in the terminal and can be sent to the 
system owned by the owner of goods, in this case the car maker, 
through the system used by the customer, namely IBS system.

ini akan menjadi dasar strategi IPCC dalam melakukan ekspansi 
wilayah dan layanan di wilayah tersebut, termasuk penentuan 
tahap awal prioritas wilayah yang paling potensial bagi IPCC

berbasis RORO di wilayah Indonesia Timur, IPCC memiliki 
potensi untuk meningkatkan fasilitasnya pada layanan RORO 
dan Penumpang, konsolidasi cargo RORO, termasuk penyediaan 
layanan pendukung konektivitas dari sumber cargo menuju ke 
terminal maupun sebaliknya. IPCC memiliki kekuatan jejaring

PENDAPATAN OPERASI
Perseroan berhasil membukukan pendapatan operasi tahun 2024 
sebesar Rp824,60 miliar, meningkat 12,16% atau setara dengan 
Rp89,39 miliar dibandingkan tahun 2023 sebesar Rp735,20 
miliar. Kenaikan ini mencerminkan keberhasilan Perseroan dalam 
mengelola pertumbuhan volume layanan serta mengoptimalkan 
peluang bisnis di tengah tren pemulihan ekonomi dan industri 
otomotif nasional.
Peningkatan pendapatan terutama didorong oleh:
1.	 Pertumbuhan Volume Ekspor-Impor Kendaraan
Seiring dengan pulihnya aktivitas manufaktur dan 
distribusi otomotif global, volume pergerakan kendaraan 
melalui terminal internasional meningkat signifikan. Hal ini 
berdampak langsung terhadap peningkatan layanan seperti 
cargodoring, stevedoring, dan pengelolaan lapangan di Car 
Terminal.
2.	 Permintaan Pasar Domestik yang Meningkat
Kenaikan konsumsi domestik serta peluncuran produk baru 
dari beberapa produsen otomotif turut mendorong mobilitas 
distribusi kendaraan di pasar dalam negeri, berkontribusi 
pada pertumbuhan pendapatan dari terminal domestik.
3.	 Optimalisasi Layanan dan Pemanfaatan Aset
Perseroan berhasil meningkatkan produktivitas aset 
operasional melalui pemanfaatan dermaga dan lapangan 
secara lebih efisien, serta implementasi sistem manajemen 
operasional yang lebih terintegrasi. Hal ini mendorong 
peningkatan frekuensi dan nilai transaksi layanan.
4.	 Diversifikasi Sumber Pendapatan Pendukung
Pendapatan dari kerja sama pelabuhan, jasa penempatan 
manajemen, serta pendapatan lain-lain menunjukkan 
pertumbuhan yang stabil dan mendukung keberlanjutan 
kinerja keuangan secara keseluruhan.
Peningkatan pendapatan operasi berkontribusi positif terhadap:
1.	 Pertumbuhan laba bruto, yang naik sebesar 6,44% menjadi 
Rp347,52 miliar.
2.	 Peningkatan laba usaha, meskipun dihadapkan pada tekanan 
beban operasional yang juga meningkat.
OPERATING REVENUE
The Company successfully recorded operating revenue of 
Rp824.60 billion in 2024, an increase of 12.16% or equivalent 
to Rp89.39 billion compared to Rp735.20 billion in 2023. This 
increase reflects the Company's success in managing service 
volume growth and optimizing business opportunities amid the 
recovery trends in the national economy and automotive industry.
The increase in revenue was mainly driven by:
1.	 Growth in Vehicle Export-Import Volume  
With the recovery of global automotive manufacturing and 
distribution activities, the volume of vehicle movements 
through international terminals rose significantly. This had a 
direct impact on the growth of services such as cargodoring, 
stevedoring, and yard management at the Car Terminal.
2.	 Increased Domestic Market Demand  
Higher domestic consumption and the launch of new 
products by several automotive manufacturers boosted 
the distribution mobility of vehicles in the domestic market, 
contributing to revenue growth from domestic terminals.
3.	 Service Optimization and Asset Utilization  
The Company successfully enhanced operational asset 
productivity by utilizing docks and yards more efficiently and 
implementing a more integrated operational management 
system. This led to an increase in service transaction 
frequency and value.
4.	 Diversification of Supporting Revenue Sources  
Revenue from port cooperation, management placement 
services, and other incomes showed stable growth, 
supporting the sustainability of overall financial performance.
The increase in operating revenue contributed positively to:
1.	 Growth in gross profit, which rose by 6.44% to Rp347.52 
billion.
2.	 Increase in operating profit, despite facing rising operational 
cost pressures.

1.	 Rasio Kas
Rasio Kas digunakan untuk mengukur kemampuan Perseroan 
dalam melunasi kewajiban yang sudah jatuh tempo dari kas 
yang dimiliki. Di sepanjang tahun 2024 terjadi kenaikan 
1,12 kali pada posisi Rasio Kas Perseroan, dari sebesar 3,25 
kali di akhir tahun 2023 menjadi sebesar 4,37 kali di akhir 
tahun 2024. Peningkatan ini mengindikasikan penguatan 
kas internal Perusahaan dalam menghadapi kewajiban yang 
telah jatuh tempo. Kenaikan ini didorong oleh arus kas masuk 
yang lebih besar dari aktivitas operasional, terutama dari 
peningkatan pendapatan usaha dan efisiensi biaya, yang 
berkontribusi terhadap saldo kas akhir tahun.
2.	 Rasio Cepat
Rasio Cepat adalah rasio yang digunakan untuk mengukur 
kemampuan Perusahaan dalam membayar utang jangka 
pendeknya dengan aktiva-aktivanya yang paling likuid. 
Pada tahun 2024, nilai Rasio Cepat Perusahaan sebesar 
4,87 kali, meningkat 1,18 kali dibandingkan tahun 2023 yang 
sebesar 3,69  kali. Hal ini menunjukkan bahwa aset lancar 
likuid Perusahaan (seperti kas, setara kas, dan piutang usaha) 
memiliki proporsi yang cukup tinggi terhadap kewajiban 
jangka pendek. Peningkatan ini menjadi indikator penting 
atas kestabilan operasional dan efektivitas manajemen modal 
kerja dalam menjaga likuiditas jangka pendek.

LIQUIDITY RATIO
Liquidity ratio is a ratio used to measure the Company's ability to 
settle its short-term obligations. Measurement of the Company's 
liquidity can be seen from the results of the calculation of Cash 
Ratio, Quick Ratio, and Current Ratio. Higher value of these 
ratios indicates that the Company has the ability to meet its 
obligations that will fall due.
1.	 Cash Ratio
The Cash Ratio is used to measure the Company's ability 
to pay off maturing liabilities from its cash. Throughout 
2024, there was an increase/decrease of 1.12 times in the 
Company's Cash Ratio position, from 3.25 times at the end of 
2023 to 4.37 times at the end of 2024. This increase indicates 
the strengthening of the Company's internal cash in facing 
maturing obligations. This increase was driven by greater 
cash inflows from operational activities, especially from 
increased operating revenues and cost efficiency, which 
contributed to the year-end cash balance.
2.	 Quick Ratio
The Quick Ratio is a ratio used to measure the Company's 
ability to pay its short-term debts with its most liquid assets. 
In 2024, the Company's Quick Ratio value was 4.87 times, 
increasing 1.18 times compared to 2023 of 3.69 times. This 
shows that the Company's liquid current assets (such as cash, 
cash equivalents, and trade receivables) have a fairly high 
proportion of current liabilities. This increase is an important 
indicator of operational stability and the effectiveness of 
working capital management in maintaining short-term 
liquidity.

5.	 Rasio Jumlah Liabilitas terhadap Jumlah Ekuitas, atau Debt

Rasio ini digunakan untuk mengukur bagian modal yang

liabilitas baru selama tahun berjalan.
3.	 Current Ratio
The Company's ability to meet short-term liabilities by relying 
on its Current Assets can be measured from the Current 
Ratio indicator. As of December 31, 2024, the Company 
recorded a Current Ratio value of 4.87 times, increasing 
1.18 times compared to 2023 in the same period of 3.69 
times. The increasing Current Ratio performance further 
emphasizes the Company's ability to manage current 
liabilities with available current asset resources. This reflects 
a healthy financial condition and a prudent asset and liability 
management strategy.
SOLVENCY RATIO 
The Company’s ability to pay all its liabilities, both long-term 
and short-term, can be measured by the solvency ratio, which 
at least consists of the Debt to Total Assets Ratio (DAR) and the 
Debt to Equity Ratio (DER). 
4.	 Debt to Asset Ratio (DAR)
This ratio is used to measure the portion of assets used to 
guarantee all liabilities or debts. In 2024, the DAR value 
was recorded at 30.99%, where this value is 0.63% lower 
compared to the DAR value in 2023 of 31.62%. Higher asset 
growth compared to liability growth reflects healthier and 
more conservative capital structure management.
5.	 Debt to Equity Ratio (DER)
This ratio is used to measure the portion of capital used as 
collateral for all liabilities or debts. The Company's DER 
calculation value in 2024 was obtained at 44.90%, where 
this value is 1.34% lower compared to the DER value in 2023 
of 46.24%. This occurred due to an increase in equity along 
with an increase in retained earnings and minimal increase 
in new liabilities during the year.

TUJUAN PENGELOLAAN PERMODALAN
Tujuan utama pengelolaan modal Perseroan adalah untuk 
memastikan pemeliharaan rasio modal yang sehat untuk 
mendukung usaha dan memaksimalkan imbalan bagi pemegang 
saham.
Perseroan mengelola permodalan untuk menjaga 
kelangsungan usahanya dalam rangka memaksimumkan 
kekayaan para pemegang saham dan manfaat kepada pihak 
lain yang berkepentingan terhadap Perseroan dan berusaha 
mempertahankan keseimbangan antara tingkat pinjaman dan 
posisi ekuitas untuk menjaga struktur optimal permodalan untuk 
mengurangi biaya permodalan.
STRUKTUR MODAL PERUSAHAAN
Hingga akhir tahun 2024, Perseroan tidak memiliki utang berbasis 
bunga. Seluruh pendanaan untuk operasi dan pengembangan 
usaha didapatkan dari ekuitas atau permodalan sendiri.
KEBIJAKAN MANAJEMEN 
ATAS STRUKTUR MODAL
Struktur modal berpengaruh terhadap pengembangan bisnis 
terutama dalam sumber dan penggunaan dana. Hal ini mendorong 
Manajemen Perseroan untuk senantiasa meninjau komposisi 
struktur modal agar mencapai kondisi yang optimal. Perseroan 
lebih memilih Ekuitas untuk membiayai Aset. Meskipun demikian, 
penggunaan Utang di Perusahaan juga mengalami peningkatan 
sehingga dipandang mampu meminimalkan (Weighted Average 
Cost of Capital/WACC) karena biaya utang (cost of debt) lebih 
murah dari biaya modal sendiri (cost of equity). Penggunaan 
utang juga akan mengurangi biaya kena pajak sehingga akan 
terjadi penghematan pajak. Akan tetapi, peningkatan utang 
berdampak terhadap biaya beban bunga yang meningkat 
sehingga mengurangi nilai Perseroan secara keseluruhan. Oleh 
karena itu, penggunaan utang dan modal senantiasa dikelola oleh 
Perseroan sehingga diperoleh kondisi yang optimum. Perusahaan 
juga senantiasa melakukan monitoring terhadap rasio likuiditas 
karena semakin tinggi rasio likuiditas maka struktur modal akan 
optimal.
CAPITAL MANAGEMENT OBJECTIVES 
The main objective of the Company’s capital management is to 
ensure the maintenance of sound capital ratios to support the 
business and maximize shareholders returns. 
The Company manages capital to maintain its business continuity 
in order to maximize shareholder wealth and benefits to other 
parties with an interest in the Company and tries to maintain a 
balance between borrowing rate and equity position to maintain 
an optimal capital structure to reduce capital costs. 
CAPITAL STRUCTURE OF THE COMPANY 
Until the end of 2024, the Company has no interest-based debt. 
All funding for operations and business development is obtained 
from equity or own capital. 
MANAGEMENT POLICY ON 
CAPITAL STRUCTURE 
Capital structure affects business development, especially in the 
source and use of funds. This encourages the Management to 
continually review the composition of capital structure in order 
to achieve optimal conditions. The Company prefers Equity to 
finance Assets. However, the use of debt in the Company has 
also increased, hence it is seen as being able to minimize the 
Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) because the cost of 
debt is cheaper than the cost of its own equity. Using debt will 
also reduce taxable costs, which resulted in tax saving. However, 
increasing debt has an impact on increasing interest costs, 
thereby reducing the overall value of the Company. Therefore, 
the use of debt and capital is always managed by the Company 
so that optimum conditions are obtained. The Company also 
continuously monitors the liquidity ratio because the higher 
the liquidity ratio, the more optimal the capital structure will be.

PEMBAGIAN DIVIDEN
Kebijakan dividen yang diterapkan di Perusahaan mengacu 
pada Undang-undang No. 40 Tahun 2007 tentang Perseroan 
Terbatas, di mana pembagian dividen disetujui dan ditetapkan 
oleh pemegang saham di dalam Rapat Umum Pemegang 
Saham (RUPS). Jumlah dividen yang dibagikan senantiasa 
memperhatikan sejumlah aspek, di antaranya tingkat kesehatan 
keuangan, kecukupan modal, dan kebutuhan pendanaan untuk 
ekspansi bisnis. Manajemen Perseroan mengajukan usulan laba 
bersih setelah pajak untuk dividen kepada Pemegang Saham 
yang akan disetujui dalam RUPS.
Dalam prospektus Penawaran Umum Perdana Saham Perseroan 
yang diterbitkan pada tanggal 2 Juli 2018, disebutkan bahwa 
setelah Penawaran Umum Perdana Saham ini, Direksi berencana 
untuk membagikan dividen tunai kepada pemegang saham 
dengan nilai sekurang-kurangnya 30,0% dari laba bersih tahun 
buku yang bersangkutan, dimulai dari tahun 2019 berdasarkan 
laba bersih tahun buku 2018, dengan memperhatikan keputusan 
para pemegang saham dalam RUPS. Apabila RUPS menyetujui 
adanya pembagian dividen, maka dividen tersebut akan dibagikan 
kepada seluruh pemegang saham yang tercatat pada tanggal 
daftar pemegang saham yang berhak atas dividen, dengan 
memperhitungkan PPh dan pemotongan pajak sesuai ketentuan 
yang berlaku, jika ada. Direksi dapat melakukan perubahan 
kebijakan dividen setiap waktu, dengan tunduk pada persetujuan 
dari pemegang saham melalui RUPS.

BASIC POLICY ON DIVIDEND 
DISTRIBUTION 
The dividend policy implemented in the Company refers to 
Law no. 40 of 2007 concerning Limited Liability Companies, in 
which the distribution of dividends is approved and determined 
by shareholders at the General Meeting of Shareholders 
(GMS). The amount of dividends distributed always consider a 
number of aspects, including the level of financial health, capital 
adequacy and funding needs for business expansion. Company 
Management submits a proposal for net profit after tax for 
dividends to Shareholders which will be approved at the GMS. 
In the Company’s Initial Public Offering prospectus published 
on July 2, 2018, it is stated that after this Initial Public Offering, 
the Board of Directors plans to distribute cash dividends to 
shareholders with a value of at least 30.0% of the net profit 
for the relevant financial year, starting from 2019 based on 
net profit for the 2018 fiscal year, taking into account the 
decisions of shareholders at the GMS. If the GMS approves the 
distribution of dividends, then the dividends will be distributed to 
all shareholders registered on the date of the list of shareholders 
entitled to dividends, considering the income tax and withholding 
taxes in accordance with applicable regulations, if any. The Board 
of Directors can make changes to the dividend policy at any 
time, subject to approval from shareholders through the GMS.

ALASAN DILAKUKANNYA TRANSAKSI
Dalam kegiatan usaha normal, Perseroan  melakukan transaksi 
dengan pihak berelasi, yang dilakukan pada tingkat harga dan 
persyaratan yang disetujui kedua belah pihak.
PENJELASAN MENGENAI 
KEWAJARAN TRANSAKSI
Seluruh transaksi dengan pihak berelasi telah sesuai dengan 
kebijakan dan syarat yang telah disepakati kedua belah pihak, 
sehingga Perseroan menganggap bahwa semua transaksi masih 
dalam batas wajar sesuai dengan PSAK No.7 (Revisi 2010) 
tentang Pengungkapan Pihak-Pihak Berelasi. Semua transaksi 
dengan pihak yang berelasi, dilakukan dengan persyaratan, 
tingkat harga, dan kondisi normal sebagaimana yang dilakukan 
dengan pihak ketiga dan telah diungkapkan dalam dalam Catatan 
Atas Laporan Keuangan (CALK) yang terkait, yaitu CALK No. 24.
PERNYATAAN DIREKSI ATAS 
TRANSAKSI AFILIASI TAHUN 2024
Direksi berpendapat bahwa seluruh transaksi material dengan 
pihak-pihak berelasi yang dilakukan Perseroan selama tahun 
2024 sudah dilaksanakan pada nilai yang wajar (arm’s length) 
sesuai dengan syarat dan ketentuan yang sama apabila transaksi 
serupa dilakukan dengan pihak yang tidak terafiliasi.
PERAN DEWAN KOMISARIS DAN KOMITE 
AUDIT DALAM MENGAWASI JALANNYA 
PROSEDUR TRANSAKSI AFILIASI
Sebagai organ pengawas, Dewan Komisaris dan Komite Audit 
bersikap objektif dan independen dalam mengawal usulan 
maupun realisasi transaksi material antara Perseroan dengan 
pihak terafiliasi. Pengawasan ini mencakup aktivitas audit 
internal maupun audit eksternal untuk menjamin bahwa semua 
transaksi tersebut tidak mengandung konflik kepentingan yang 
menguntungkan salah satu pihak, serta sudah dilakukan secara 
transparan dan dapat dipertanggungjawabkan sesuai dengan 
kaidah dan standar akuntansi yang berlaku.
REASON FOR TRANSACTION 
In normal business activities, the Company carries out 
transactions with affiliated parties, which are executed at the 
same price and terms agreed by both parties. 
EXPLANATION OF 
TRANSACTION FAIRNESS 
All transactions with affiliated parties are in accordance with 
the policies and conditions agreed by both parties, so that 
the Company considers that all transactions are still within 
reasonable limits in accordance with PSAK No.7 (Revised 2010) 
concerning Disclosures of Related Parties. All transactions with 
related parties are carried out with normal terms, price levels 
and conditions as those carried out with third parties and have 
been disclosed in the related Notes to Financial Statements 
(CALK), namely CALK No. 27. 
BOARD OF DIRECTORS’ STATEMENT ON 
AFFILIATED TRANSACTIONS IN 2024
The Board of Directors considers that all material transactions 
with affiliated parties carried out by the Company during 2024 
have been carried out at fair value (arm’s length) in accordance 
with the same terms and conditions as if similar transactions 
were carried out with unaffiliated parties.

COMMISSIONERS AND AUDIT 
COMMITTEE IN SUPERVISING 
THE AFFILIATED TRANSACTION 
PROCEDURES 
As supervisory body, the Board of Commissioners and Audit

Company and affiliated parties. This supervision includes internal 
audit activities and external audits to ensure that all transactions 
do not contain conflicts of interest that benefit either party, and 
have been carried out transparently and can be accounted for 
in accordance with applicable accounting rules and standards.

Kebijakan Perseroan Terkait Mekanisme Review atas Transaksi 
Mekanisme review atas transaksi dengan pihak berelasi melalui 
proses audit baik yang dilakukan oleh auditor internal maupun 
auditor eksternal. Sebagai upaya untuk memastikan tingkat 
kewajaran transaksi dan kesesuaian dengan standar akuntansi 
yang berlaku umum di Indonesia, laporan transaksi afiliasi di atas 
telah diaudit oleh Auditor. Mekanisme ini tertuang dalam PSAK 
7 (revisi 2014) tentang “Pengungkapan Pihak-Pihak Berelasi”.
PEMENUHAN PERATURAN 
DAN KETENTUAN TERKAIT
Pemenuhan peraturan dan ketentuan mengenai transaksi 
dengan pihak-pihak berelasi dipastikan telah memenuhi Standar 
Akuntansi Keuangan (SAK) di Indonesia, yaitu PSAK 7 (revisi 
2014) tentang “Pengungkapan Pihak-Pihak Berelasi”. Uraian 
lengkap akan hal ini juga telah disajikan dalam Laporan Keuangan 
Tahunan Perseroan untuk tahun buku yang berakhir 31 Desember 
2024 pada catatan 24, yang merupakan bagian tidak terpisahkan 
dari Laporan Tahunan dan Laporan Keberlanjutan 2023.
Seluruh pengungkapan informasi di atas juga telah dilaporkan 
kepada regulator sebagai bentuk pemenuhan terhadap Peraturan 
OJK No. 42/POJK.04/2021 tentang Transaksi Afiliasi dan 
Transaksi Benturan Kepentingan.
Dewan Komisaris dan Direksi Perseroan telah memastikan bahwa 
seluruh transaksi yang dilakukan telah memenuhi armslength 
principal. Dewan Komisaris bersama dengan Komite Audit 
bertugas melakukan kajian dan memantau seluruh transaksi 
afiliasi yang dilakukan Perseroan untuk memastikan seluruh 
armslength principal terpenuhi dengan baik. Tidak terdapat 
transaksi afiliasi yang dilakukan Perseroan yang membutuhkan 
persetujuan pemegang saham minoritas.
Company Policy Regarding Review Mechanisms for Transactions 
Mechanisms for reviewing transactions with related parties 
is done through an audit process carried out by both internal 
auditors and external auditors. In an effort to ensure the level of 
fairness of transactions and conformity with generally accepted 
accounting standards in Indonesia, the affiliated transaction 
reports above have been audited by the Auditor. This mechanism 
is contained in PSAK 7 (revised 2014) concerning “Related Party 
Disclosures”. 
COMPLIANCE WITH RELATED 
RULES AND REGULATIONS 
Compliance with the rules and regulations regarding transactions 
with related parties is ensured to comply with Financial 
Accounting Standards (SAK) in Indonesia, namely PSAK 7 
(revised 2014) concerning “Related Party Disclosures”. A 
complete description of this matter has also been presented in 
the Company’s Annual Financial Statements for the fiscal year 
ending December 31, 2024 in note 24, which is an integral part 
of the 2024 Annual Report and Sustainability Report. 
All disclosures of the information above have also been reported 
to the regulator as a form of compliance with POJK No. 42/
POJK.04/2021 concerning Affiliate Transactions and Conflict 
of Interest Transactions. 
The Company’s Board of Commissioners and Board of Directors 
have ensured that all transactions conducted have met the 
armslength principal. The Board of Commissioners together with 
the Audit Committee are tasked with reviewing and monitoring 
all affiliated transactions conducted by the Company to ensure 
that all armslength principals are met properly. There are no 
affiliated transactions conducted by the Company that require 
the approval of minor shareholders.
