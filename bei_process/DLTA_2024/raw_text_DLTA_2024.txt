Indonesia’s Economy in 2024
Pada 
tahun 
2024,

Indonesia

target pemerintah sebesar 5,2%. Perlambatan ini 
terutama disebabkan oleh tekanan terhadap daya 
beli konsumen, ditambah ketegangan perdagangan 
global.
Tren penurunan ini semakin terasa pada kuartal

Untuk

memangkas suku bunga acuan sebesar 25 basis poin 
menjadi 6% pada bulan September di tengah deflasi 
yang berlangsung selama beberapa bulan.
Di sisi eksternal, faktor signifikan yang menghambat 
pertumbuhan adalah perlambatan ekonomi di China, 
mitra dagang terbesar Indonesia. Para ekonom 
memperkirakan bahwa setiap 1% perlambatan PDB 
China akan memangkas pertumbuhan ekonomi

In 2024, Indonesia’s economy experienced its slowest 
growth in over three years, with GDP expanding by 
5.03%, marginally lower than last year and missing 
the government’s 5.2%  target. This slowdown was 
primarily due to pressures on consumer purchasing 
power, coupled with global trade tensions. 
This downward trend became more pronounced 
in the third quarter, when GDP growth eased to 
4.95%, falling short of expectations. In response, the 
government introduced targeted fiscal incentives 
to boost household spending, following a series of 
factory closures and job cuts which undermined 
consumer confidence in the economy. To further 
support the economy, Bank Indonesia (“BI”) cut its 
benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points to 6% in 
September amid some periods of deflation.
Externally, a significant factor restraining growth 
was the economic slowdown in China, Indonesia’s 
largest trading partner. Economists estimate that 
every 1% slowdown in China’s GDP could reduce 
Indonesia’s economic growth by approximately 
0.29%, indicating the country’s vulnerability to 
external shocks.

Pasar Bir Indonesia
Indonesia’s Beer Market
Indonesia 
tetap 
menjadi 
salah 
satu 
negara 
dengan tingkat konsumsi bir per kapita terendah 
di dunia. Meskipun demikian, pasar bir negara ini 
menghadirkan potensi pertumbuhan jangka panjang 
yang signifikan, dengan proyeksi pertumbuhan 
volume rata-rata sebesar 4% selama lima tahun ke 
depan.
Prospek 
ini 
didukung 
peningkatan 
konsumsi 
minuman beralkohol yang relatif stabil berkat 
pertumbuhan 
ekonomi, 
preferensi 
konsumen 
yang terus berkembang, populasi usia muda yang 
besar, urbanisasi pesat, inovasi produk, serta 
perkembangan 
pariwisata. 
Pusat 
perkotaan 
dan tujuan wisata populer, yang dikenal dengan 
kehidupan malamnya yang semarak, tetap menjadi 
pusat utama konsumsi bir. Meskipun secara historis 
angka penjualan baseline tidak terlalu tinggi, prospek 
untuk perluasan pasar di masa mendatang tetap 
menjanjikan.
Namun, 
pasar 
minuman 
beralkohol 
Indonesia 
dibatasi oleh berbagai peraturan dan budaya yang 
kompleks. Pembatasan penjualan alkohol dan cukai 
yang tinggi terus menghadirkan tantangan bagi 
para pelaku industri. Kemampuan beradaptasi 
dengan kendala-kendala ini sangat penting untuk 
memastikan pertumbuhan yang berkelanjutan dan 
kehadiran pasar yang kuat.
Indonesia remains as one of the countries with 
the lowest levels of beer consumption per capita 
globally. Nevertheless, the country’s beer market 
presents significant long-term growth potential, 
with an average projected volume growth of 4% over 
the next five years. 
This outlook is supported by a steady increase in 
overall alcoholic beverage consumption, driven 
by 
economic 
development, 
evolving 
consumer 
preferences, a large and youthful population, rapid 
urbanization, new product innovations as well as a 
thriving tourism sector. Urban centers and popular 
tourist destinations, known for their vibrant nightlife, 
remain key centers of beer consumption, suggesting 
strong prospects for future market expansion 
despite a historically low baseline. 
However, the Indonesian alcoholic beverage market 
continues to operate within a complex regulatory and 
cultural environment.  Restrictions on alcohol sales 
and high excise duties continue to pose challenges 
for industry players. Successfully adapting to these 
constraints is essential to ensure sustained growth 
and strong market presence.

Analisis Bisnis dan Pemasaran
Business and Marketing Analysis
Pada tahun 2024, Perseroan tetap berkomitmen 
pada strategi bisnis intinya dengan mempertahankan 
kehadiran yang kuat di pasar tradisional/Traditional 
Trade (“TT”). Pada saat yang bersamaan, kami 
secara strategis memperluas keberadaan kami 
di pasar modern/Modern Trade (“MT”), yang 
mencakup segmen on-premise dan off-premise, 
untuk menangkap peluang pasar yang sedang 
berkembang.
Pertumbuhan di saluran MT terutama didorong oleh 
portofolio merek premium dan varian bir berperisa 
buah rendah alkohol. Penawaran ini secara khusus 
dikembangkan untuk memenuhi preferensi para 
peminum generasi milenial dan Gen Z, yang lebih 
menyukai pengalaman minum bir yang berorientasi 
pada interaksi sosial dan gaya hidup.
Untuk 
meningkatkan 
efektivitas 
pemasaran, 
kami 
memanfaatkan 
wawasan 
berbasis 
data 
untuk mendapatkan pemahaman yang lebih baik 
tentang perilaku konsumen dan tren pasar. Hal ini 
memungkinkan pengembangan kampanye dengan 
target khusus yang bertujuan untuk meningkatkan 
visibilitas dan kesadaran merek kami. Aktivasi 
pemasaran utama selama tahun ini meliputi Anker 
Sound-On, Anker Stout Rhythm, SML TwiLIGHT 
Cheers, Carlsberg Libeeration, Carlsberg Euro2024 
Live Viewing, dan Cerveza Blanca Date Night Special 
Food Pairing.
Dari bulan Agustus hingga Desember, Perseroan 
mengimplementasikan program perluasan pasar 
yang berhasil mengaktifkan kembali sejumlah 
besar gerai. Upaya ini dilengkapi dengan promosi 
konsumen rutin dan kampanye penjualan dan 
pemasaran terpadu di seluruh platform luring dan 
daring. Eksekusi ini meliputi pemanfaatan papan 
reklame, videotron, materi point-of-sales (POSM), 
branding di dalam toko, media sosial, dan kehadiran 
di pasar daring (e-commerce).
Untuk memperluas jangkauan dan ketersediaan 
pasar, kami terus menjajaki kemitraan saluran 
baru dan jaringan distributor. Pada saat yang 
bersamaan, kami memperkuat hubungan bisnis 
jangka panjang kami dengan para distributor dan 
wholesaler kami melalui peningkatan insentif dan 
program penghargaan. Hal ini didukung oleh inisiatif 
pemasaran yang menarik seperti Anker Funtastic 
dan KP Wheel of Fortune yang dijalankan di gerai-
gerai mitra utama.
In 2024, the Company remained committed to its core 
business strategy by maintaining a strong presence 
in the traditional trade (“TT”) channel. At the same 
time, we strategically expanded our availability in 
the modern trade (“MT”) channel, covering both 
on-premise and off-premise segments, to capture 
emerging market opportunities. 
Growth in the MT channel was primarily driven by 
our portfolio of premium brands and low-alcohol 
fruit-flavored beer variants.  These offerings were 
specially developed to cater to evolving preferences 
of Millennials and Gen Z drinkers, who favor social 
and lifestyle-oriented drinking experience.
To enhance our marketing approach, we leveraged on 
data-driven insights to gain a better understanding 
of consumer behavior and market trends. This 
enabled the development of targeted campaigns 
aimed at increasing our brand visibility and 
awareness.  Key activations during the year included 
Anker Sound-On, Anker Stout Rhythm, SML TwiLIGHT 
Cheers, Carlsberg Libeeration, Carlsberg Euro2024 
Live Viewing, and Cerveza Blanca Date Night Special 
Food Pairing. 
From 
August 
to 
December, 
the 
Company 
implemented trade programs which successfully 
reactivated a significant number of outlets. These 
efforts were complemented by regular consumer 
promotions and integrated sales and marketing 
campaigns across both offline and digital platforms. 
Executions included use of billboards, videotron, 
point-of-sales materials (POSM), in-store branding, 
social media and marketplace presence. 
To expand market reach and availability, we 
continued to explore new channel partnerships and 
distributor networks. Concurrently, we strengthened 
our 
long-standing 
business 
relationships 
with 
our 
wholesalers 
through 
enhanced 
incentives 
and recognition programs. This was supported 
by engaging marketing initiatives such as Anker 
Funtastic and KP Wheel of Fortune implemented at 
key partner outlets.

Cost of Goods Sold 
In line with lower sales volume, Cost of Goods Sold 
stood at IDR197.8 billion, down 11.7% from to last 
year’s IDR224.1 billion. However, it was tempered 
by an increase in production and distribution costs, 
combined with the impact of the depreciation of the 
IDR currency on imported materials. 
Gross Profit 
In line with the decline in sales volume, Gross Profit 
decreased by 12.4% to IDR449.0 billion compared 
to IDR512.8 billion in the previous year. The rise in 
the cost of production inputs, particularly malt, 
adjuncts and select packaging materials, was largely 
mitigated by the February 2024 price increase. As 
a result, the Company maintained a healthy Gross 
Profit Margin of 69% consistent with the prior year. 
Operating Expenses 
Total Operating Expenses this year rose marginally 
by 1.0% reaching IDR295.2 billion from IDR292.2 
billion in 2023. This increase was driven by higher 
expenditures on salaries and wages, third party 
service providers’ employee training programs, 
promotion activities, royalty fees, and business 
travel as part of investments to support strategic 
initiatives and market expansion efforts. 
Income Before Tax and Income for the Year 
Attributable to Owners of the Company 
Income Before Income Tax declined by 27.8% to 
IDR181.4 billion in 2024, from IDR251.1 billion in the 
previous year. Despite the challenges, the Company 
sustained a robust profit margin, bolstered by pricing 
actions.  The decline in pre-tax income was primarily 
due to weaker sales volume, higher production costs 
per volume and increase in operating expenses. In 
parallel, Income for the Year Attributable to Owners 
of the Company decreased by 28.5% totaling 
IDR142.3 billion in 2024 compared to IDR199.1 billion 
in 2023.

new investments, appropriate reserves and working

Meeting of Shareholders.

on May 16, 2024, the shareholders approved the 
distribution of cash dividends of IDR281 per share,

the IDR325 per share or total dividends of IDR260.2

ratio of 113%, which is at the same level declared in 
the previous year. 
In compliance with regulations, the appropriation

Changes in the Regulations and its Impact 
on the Company and Beer Industry
On Excise Taxes
In December 2023, the Ministry of Finance issued 
Regulation No. 160/2023 increasing the excise duty 
on Class A alcohol beverages, including beer by 10% 
from IDR15,000 to IDR16,500 per liter, effective 
January 1, 2024.  
In a bid to curb smuggling, the Directorate General of 
Customs and Excise (DGCE) in May 2019 abolished 
the cigarette and alcohol excise tax exemption in 
the Free-Trade Zone (FTZ) of Batam, Bintan, and 
Karimun. Consequently, the new excise levy will also 
apply in these areas.
Indonesia’s 
excise 
tax 
structure 
on 
alcoholic 
beverages is tiered based on alcohol content and 
product classification, and is imposed on a per-liter 
basis.  The categories are defined as – Class A (up to 
5% alcohol content), Class B (above 5% to 20%) and 
Class C (above 20% to 55%)
The sales and distribution of alcoholic beverages 
in Indonesia remain subject to strict regulatory 
controls.  Key national regulations include:
1.	
Presidential Regulation No. 74/2013, governing 
the production, distribution, and sale of alcoholic 
beverages;
2.	
Trade Ministerial Regulation No. 20/2014, which 
mandates that retailers and distributors obtain 
special linceses for the storage and sale of beer;
3.	
Requirements for off-premise outlets such as 
supermarkets and hypermarkets, to allocate 
dedicated display areas and separate storage 
rooms for alcoholic products.
To further strengthen the regulatory environment, 
Trade Ministerial Regulation No. 6/2015, effective 
since April 2015 prohibits the sale of beer in 
convenience stores and other identified outlets.

Reduction of Corporate Income Tax for 
Domestic Tax Payers
The Indonesian government issued Law No. 2/2020 
which was further elaborated in Government 
Regulation (PP) No. 30/2020 to safeguard the 
economy and ease the burden on domestic companies 
from the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.
One of the main changes introduced by this 
regulation was the reduction in corporate income 
tax from the prevailing 25% to 22% for taxable years 
2020 and 2021, and a further reduction in the tax rate 
to 20% beginning taxable year 2022. Publicly listed 
companies with at least 40% of its shares traded in 
Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX) will be entitled to an 
additional 3% reduction in tax rate.
However, the reduced corporate income tax of 20% 
by 2022 was indefinitely deferred based on Law No. 
7/2021 on Tax Harmonization.
In the case of PT Delta, the Company’s taxable 
profits are currently subject to a reduced income 
tax rate of 22%. However, it is not qualified for the 
additional 3% corporate income tax reduction based 
on the percentage of Company’s shares traded in 
the IDX since it is below 40%.

impact consumer prices including those of alcoholic 
beverages thereby potentially influencing purchasing 
behavior.
However, on December 31, 2024, just hours before 
the 12% VAT rate was set to take effect, President 
Prabowo Subianto, announced a policy revision.

Impact of the Rupiah Depreciation
Throughout 2024, the Indonesia Rupiah remained 
under persistent pressure due to the sustained 
strength of the US dollar. After a brief period of 
appreciation in August and September, the Rupiah 
began to depreciate in October.  This reversal 
was driven by uncertainties over the US Federal 
Reserve’s plans for interest rate cuts and escalating 
geopolitical tensions.
The Rupiah’s decline continued until the end of 
the year, eventually falling to a four-month low of 
IDR16,120 to a dollar on December 18, 2024, ranking 
as one of the weakest-performing currencies in Asia. 
The depreciation of rupiah in 2024 significantly raised 
the Company’s production costs as the prices of 
imported direct materials such as malt and several 
packaging materials became more expensive.
