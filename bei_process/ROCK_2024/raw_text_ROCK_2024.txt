MACROECONOMIC OVERVIEW
In 2024, the global economy exhibited a stable 
trend, with growth projected at 3.2% for the year. 
Global inflation is expected to decline from 6.8% 
in 2023 to 5.9% in 2024 and further to 4.5% in 2025, 
with advanced economies returning to their 
inflation targets faster than emerging markets. 
This decline in inflation has enabled some central 
banks to begin easing their monetary policies. 
Nevertheless, risks such as rising protectionism 
and geopolitical tensions may hinder future 
global economic growth.
According to data from Statistics Indonesia 
(BPS), Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.03% 
(cumulative-to-cumulative) 
in 
2024, 
slightly 
down from 5.05% in 2023. The highest growth 
by sector on the production side was recorded 
in Other Services, which expanded by 9.80%, 
while on the expenditure side, Household Non-
Profit Institutions Serving Households (LNPRT) 
posted the highest growth at 12.48%. In the 
fourth quarter of 2024, the economy grew 5.02% 
year-on-year compared to the same period in the 
previous year. Indonesia’s year-on-year inflation 
rate in December 2024 stood at 1.57%, with the 
Consumer Price Index (CPI) at 106.80. Overall, 
the annual inflation rate declined compared 
to the previous year, reflecting relatively well-
maintained price stability. Going forward, key 
risks include the potential weakening of global 
demand—particularly from China—which may 
impact Indonesia’s exports, as well as challenges 
in maintaining fiscal stability amidst increasing 
public spending needs.

PROPERTY INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
Indonesia’s property industry recorded positive 
growth in 2024, despite facing various structural 
challenges and market dynamics. According 
to data from Statistics Indonesia (BPS), the 
real estate sector grew by 2.50% year-on-year 
(yoy), reflecting an improvement compared 
to the previous year. This growth was largely 
driven by rising housing demand due to 
continued urbanization in major cities such as 
Jakarta, Surabaya, and Medan. The migration 
of populations to urban areas has increased the 
demand for housing, opening up promising 
investment opportunities in the residential 
property sector. Furthermore, according to 
industry surveys, 67% of stakeholders expressed 
confidence that the property sector could serve 
as a key pillar in national economic recovery 
amidst the global challenges of 2024.

sector is undergoing a period of adaptation due 
to shifting business trends in the post-pandemic 
era. Many companies have streamlined operations 
by reducing the use of conventional office spaces 
and shifting toward more flexible options such 
as co-working spaces, while the demand for 
logistics and warehousing facilities has surged in 
line with the growth of e-commerce. Industrial 
zones have also garnered increased investor 
interest, seen as more stable with long-term 
potential. Additionally, investment in property is
