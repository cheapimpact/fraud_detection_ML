Pendapatan penjualan Perseroan berasal dari 
penjualan Filament Yarn, Staple Fibre, Polyester 
Chips dan Performance Fabrics, baik di pasar 
domestik maupun ekspor. Total penjualan pada 
tahun 2023 mengalami penurunan dibanding 
tahun sebelumnya karena sejumlah faktor 
seperti kondisi pasar yang lesu, tren penurunan 
harga rantai polyester, dan perlambatan operasi 
hilir sepanjang tahun. Perekonomian global 
terus menghadapi tantangan berupa inflasi 
yang terus berlanjut dan prospek pertumbuhan 
yang lemah. Pertumbuhan ekonomi global 
melambat menjadi 2,70 pada tahun 2023, 
sedikit lebih kuat dari perkiraan pada awal 
tahun. Pertumbuhan PDB saat ini melambat 
sebagai dampak dari kondisi keuangan yang 
lebih

lemahnya 
pertumbuhan 
perdagangan, 
dan

Di

perlambatan ekonomi global, pertumbuhan 
ekonomi Indonesia tetap tangguh, inflasi berada 
dalam tren menurun, dan volatilitas mata uang 
terkendali.

The sales revenue of the Company is derived 
from the sales of Filament Yarn, Staple Fibre, 
Polyester Chips and performance fabrics, both 
in domestic and export markets.  The total sales 
in 2023 have dropped as compared to the 
previous year due to host of factors such as 
sluggish market conditions, declining trend in 
the polyester chain prices, and slowdown in 
downstream operations during the year.  The 
global economy continues to face challenges 
of persistent inflation and subdued growth 
prospects. The global economic growth slowed 
down to 2.70 in 2023, slightly stronger than 
expected at the start of the year. GDP growth is 
currently moderating as the impact of tighter 
financial conditions, weak trade growth and 
lower business and consumer confidence.  
Amidst 
global 
economic 
slowdown, 
the 
Indonesia’s 
economic 
growth 
remained 
resilient, inflation is on a declining trend, and 
the currency volatility manageable.  
 
 
 
The Country’s economy expanded to 5.05% 
during the year 2023 from the level of 
contraction of 5.31% in the previous year. 
Household consumption, which makes up over 
half of Indonesia's gross domestic product, 
increased 4.82% last year compared to 4.94% 
in 2022. Export growth slowed sharply to 1.32%, 
compared with 2022's expansion of more than 
16%. By value, shipments last year shrank by 
about 11%. 
 
 
 
The price of Brent crude oil averaged USD83 
per barrel (b) in 2023, down from USD101/b in 
2022, a difference around USD19/b. Global 
markets adjusted to new trade dynamics, with 
crude oil from Russia finding destinations 
outside the EU, and global crude oil demand 
fell short of expectations.  
 
 
Bank Indonesia’s benchmark rupiah rate 
(Jakarta 
Interbank 
Spot 
Dollar 
Rate, 
abbreviated JISDOR), which remained weak at 
15,592 as of December 22, continued to 
strengthen and remained relatively stable 
during January to September 2023. The 
currency had weakened in quarter IV and 
closed at 15,439 as of end Dec 2023.

Cost of Goods Sold 
 
Cost of goods sold for the year 2023 was 
USD289.0 million, as compared to USD364.0 
million in 2022, registering a drop of 20.60% 
over the last year, principally due to significant 
reduction in volume and drop in raw material 
costs (which also led to lower sales prices). 
However, conversion cost per unit of product 
increased due to hike in energy costs and lower 
capacity utilization.  
 
 
 
 
Gross Profit / (Loss) 
 
The Company posted a Gross profit of USD 2.21 
million in 2023, as compared to USD32.94 
million in 2022. The significant drop in gross 
profit was attributable to due squeezed 
contribution margins for all products due to 
sharp decline in polyester chain margins and 
lower volumes.  As the demand from both 
domestic and international markets remained 
weak through the year, the plants were 
operated at sub-optimum levels resulting in 
higher production costs, eroding the margins 
further. Performance Fabric Division had also 
experienced reduced performance due to 
sluggish retail market.  
 
 
 
Other Expenses

as compared to USD28.83 million in 2022 in 
line with the reduced volume of production and 
sales.
