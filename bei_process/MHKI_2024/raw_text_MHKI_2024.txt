2024 Macroeconomic Overview
Tahun 2024 menjadi periode yang penuh tantangan 
bagi ekonomi global, dipengaruhi oleh meningkatnya 
ketegangan 
geopolitik, 
kebijakan 
moneter 
yang 
ketat di negara-negara maju, serta fluktuasi harga 
komoditas utama yang berdampak pada rantai pasokan 
internasional. Ketidakpastian yang terus berlangsung, 
baik 
dalam 
aspek 
geopolitik 
maupun 
ekonomi, 
memberikan tekanan terhadap dinamika pertumbuhan 
di berbagai kawasan. Menurut laporan World Economic 
Outlook dari International Monetary Fund (IMF), ekonomi 
global diperkirakan tumbuh sebesar 3,3% pada tahun 
2024, angka yang lebih rendah dibandingkan rata-
rata pertumbuhan periode 2000-2019 sebesar 3,7%. 
Di antara negara-negara utama, Amerika Serikat 
menunjukkan ketahanan yang lebih baik, sementara 
Eropa menghadapi ketidakpastian yang signifikan 
dan perlambatan ekonomi Tiongkok turut memberikan 
tekanan terhadap stabilitas ekonomi global.
2024 has been a challenging period for the global 
economy, influenced by rising geopolitical tensions, 
tight monetary policies in advanced economies, and 
fluctuations in key commodity prices that have affected 
international supply chains. The ongoing uncertainties, 
both geopolitical and economic, have put pressure on 
growth dynamics in various regions. According to the 
World Economic Outlook report from the International 
Monetary Fund (IMF), the global economy is expected 
to grow by 3.3% in 2024, a lower figure compared to the 
average growth rate of 3.7% from 2000 to 2019. Among 
major countries, the United States has shown better 
resilience, while Europe faces significant uncertainty, 
and the economic slowdown in China is putting pressure 
on global economic stability.

Despite global uncertainties, Indonesia’s economy 
has remained relatively resilient. Based on data from 
the Central Statistics Agency (BPS), 2024 Indonesia’s 
economic growth reached 5.03% year-on-year (C-to-C), 
reflecting stability amid external and domestic pressures. 
The services sector was the main driver, with a significant 
increase of 9.80%, while consumption by non-profit 
institutions serving households (PK-LNPRT) showed the 
highest growth at 12.48%, driven by increased social 
spending and government welfare programs. This data 
indicates that domestic demand remains strong despite 
external challenges.
In international trade, Indonesia recorded a surplus of 
USD 31.04 billion in 2024, the lowest in the past four 
years. This decline was due to a 5.31% increase in 
imports of raw materials and capital goods, reflecting 
high domestic industrial demand, and a drop in mining 
exports impacted by global commodity price volatility. 
Although exports still saw a 2.29% increase, the faster 
rise in imports indicates that domestic demand remains 
strong. This situation suggests that domestic purchasing 
power and industrial activity are still robust, despite 
ongoing external pressures.
Inflation in 2024 remained relatively controlled, with 
an annual rate of 1.57%, despite significant variations 
between regions. The main drivers of inflation were 
the rising prices of essential goods, especially food 
and beverages, and a surge in service costs, such as 
personal care, which increased by 7.02%. The varying 
inflation rates across regions led to more flexible 
economic policies, including adjustments to energy 
subsidies in areas with high inflation and strengthened 
social assistance programs to support purchasing power 
for affected communities. The Papua Mountain Province 
recorded the highest inflation at 5.36%, while North 
Sulawesi experienced the lowest inflation at just 0.44%.

Dalam menghadapi tekanan eksternal, kebijakan moneter 
yang diambil oleh Bank Indonesia berperan penting 
dalam menjaga stabilitas ekonomi nasional. Salah satu 
tantangan utama tahun ini adalah fluktuasi nilai tukar 
Rupiah terhadap Dolar AS. Sepanjang tahun, Rupiah 
mengalami pergerakan signifikan, dengan nilai tertinggi 
pada 2 Januari 2024 sebesar Rp15.516 per dolar AS dan 
nilai terendah pada 27 Juni 2024 sebesar Rp16.517 per 
dolar AS. Rata-rata nilai tukar sepanjang tahun berada 
pada Rp16.092 untuk kurs jual dan Rp15.932 untuk kurs 
beli, lebih lemah dibandingkan asumsi dalam RAPBN 
2024 yang memperkirakan rentang Rp14.700–Rp15.300 
per dolar AS. Pergerakan ini dipengaruhi oleh kebijakan 
suku bunga The Fed serta intervensi Bank Indonesia 
dalam menjaga stabilitas pasar valuta asing.
Pelemahan Rupiah mendorong Bank Indonesia untuk 
melakukan berbagai langkah intervensi guna menjaga 
stabilitas makroekonomi, termasuk kebijakan suku 
bunga dan penggunaan cadangan devisa. Depresiasi 
ini juga memberikan tekanan terhadap inflasi, terutama 
untuk barang-barang impor, serta menjadi tantangan 
bagi sektor industri yang bergantung pada bahan baku 
impor. Namun, di sisi lain, pelemahan Rupiah berpotensi 
meningkatkan daya saing ekspor dan menekan 
defisit transaksi berjalan jika sektor manufaktur dapat 
memanfaatkan momentum ini dengan baik. Bank 
Indonesia juga melakukan operasi moneter untuk 
meredam volatilitas nilai tukar, termasuk intervensi di 
pasar valas dan pengelolaan cadangan devisa.
Seiring dengan upaya menjaga stabilitas nilai tukar, 
kebijakan suku bunga yang diterapkan oleh Bank 
Indonesia turut menjadi faktor kunci dalam pengelolaan 
ekonomi makro. Pada awal tahun 2024, Bank Indonesia 
mempertahankan suku bunga acuan BI 7-Day Reverse 
Repo Rate di level 6,00%. Namun, dalam Rapat Dewan 
Gubernur Januari 2025, Bank Indonesia mengejutkan 
pasar dengan menurunkan suku bunga sebesar 25 basis 
poin menjadi 5,75% sebagai langkah untuk mendorong 
pertumbuhan ekonomi. Kebijakan ini diambil di tengah 
tekanan terhadap nilai tukar Rupiah yang sempat 
mengalami volatilitas akibat ketidakpastian global, 
dengan harapan dapat meningkatkan investasi dan 
konsumsi domestik.
In facing external pressures, the monetary policies 
implemented by Bank Indonesia played a crucial role in 
maintaining national economic stability. One of the main 
challenges this year was fluctuating the Rupiah exchange 
rate against the USD. Throughout the year, the Rupiah 
saw significant movement, reaching its highest value on 
January 2nd, 2024, at Rp15,516 per US dollar, and its 
lowest on June 27th, 2024, at Rp16,517 per USD. The 
average exchange rate for the year was Rp16,092 for the 
selling rate and Rp15,932 for the buying rate, which was 
weaker than the assumption in the 2024 State Budget 
Draft, which estimated a range of Rp14,700–Rp15,300 
per USD. This movement was influenced by the Federal 
Reserve’s interest rate policies and Bank Indonesia’s 
interventions to maintain the stability of the foreign 
exchange market.
The depreciation of the Rupiah prompted Bank 
Indonesia to take several intervention measures to 
maintain macroeconomic stability, including adjusting 
interest rates and using foreign exchange reserves. This 
depreciation also put pressure on inflation, particularly 
for imported goods, and posed challenges for industries 
dependent on imported raw materials. However, on the 
positive side, the weaker Rupiah could enhance export 
competitiveness and reduce the current account deficit 
if the manufacturing sector can take advantage of this 
opportunity. Bank Indonesia also conducted monetary 
operations to reduce exchange rate volatility, including 
interventions in the foreign exchange market and 
managing foreign exchange reserves.
To maintain exchange rate stability, the interest rate 
policy implemented by Bank Indonesia has also been 
a key factor in managing the macroeconomy. At the 
beginning of 2024, Bank Indonesia kept its benchmark 
interest rate, the BI 7-Day Reverse Repo Rate, at 6.00%. 
However, during the January 2025 Board of Governors 
meeting, Bank Indonesia surprised the market by 
lowering the interest rate by 25 basis points to 5.75% to 
stimulate economic growth. This policy was introduced 
amid pressure on the Rupiah, which had experienced 
volatility due to global uncertainties, to boost investment 
and domestic consumption.

Data from the Directorate of Hazardous and Toxic Waste 
Management in the official document “Hazardous and 
Non-Hazardous Waste Management Policy for the 
Industrial Sector” by KLHK shows that the total amount 
of hazardous waste generated in Indonesia in 2024 
reached over 67.9 million tons, a decrease from 83.4 
million tons in the previous year. However, the amount 
of waste stored in Temporary Storage Facilities (TPS) 
increased significantly from 3.02 million tons in the 
previous year to 21.8 million tons in 2024. This indicates 
issues with the advanced processing system, leading to 
a rise in the volume of waste that has not been effectively 
managed. Meanwhile, the amount of waste that was 
properly processed decreased from 80.4 million tons in 
the previous year to 46.1 million tons in 2024.

the Central Statistics Agency (BPS) shows that the Water

sector has experienced significant growth. In 2024, the 
sector’s GDP at current prices reached Rp14.3 trillion, 
an increase from Rp13.3 trillion in the previous year. At 
constant 2010 prices, the sector slightly decreased from 
Rp10.7 trillion in the previous year to Rp10.9 trillion in 
2024.

dynamics. In the third quarter of 2024 compared to 
the second quarter, the sector contracted by -0.94%.

compared to the third quarter, with growth of 2.50%. 
On an annual basis, the sector grew by 1.56% in 2024. 
The distribution and contribution of this sector to the 
national GDP remained stable, accounting for 0.06% of 
Indonesia’s total GDP.
Although this sector has experienced positive growth, 
its contribution to the total national GDP remains small. 
The fluctuations in growth between quarters indicate 
instability in the sector, possibly related to factors 
such as regulations, investment, or demand for waste 
management services. Despite its small share, the 
sector’s growth has been relatively consistent compared 
to others, suggesting opportunities for investment and 
industry expansion.

Perseroan menyediakan layanan One Package Service 
sebagai keunggulan kompetitif, mencakup pengangkutan, 
pengumpulan, pengolahan, dan pemanfaatan limbah B3 
dan non-B3 sesuai dengan prinsip 3R (Reuse, Recycle, 
Recovery) serta konsep cradle to grave. Layanan ini 
diberikan kepada berbagai sektor industri dan dirancang 
untuk mengurangi risiko pelanggan, yang dikelompokkan 
menjadi beberapa kategori:
•	
Pengangkutan limbah B3, non-B3, dan limbah medis
•	
Pengumpulan limbah B3, non-B3, dan limbah medis
•	
Pengolahan limbah B3, non-B3, dan limbah medis
•	
Pemanfaatan limbah B3, non-B3, dan limbah medis
The Company operates through two main business 
segments that is services and goods sales. In the 
services segment, activities include integrated waste 
transportation, processing, and utilization, with a strong 
emphasis on environmental sustainability. These two 
segments complement each other, reinforcing the 
Company’s position as an efficient and value-added 
solution provider.
WASTE TRANSPORTATION, PROCESSING, AND 
UTILIZATION SERVICES
The Company offers a One Package Service as its unique 
selling proposition (USP), encompassing transportation, 
collection, processing, and utilization of both hazardous 
(B3) and non-hazardous waste. This approach aligns 
with the 3R principles (Reuse, Recycle, Recovery) and 
the cradle-to-grave concept. Services are provided 
across various industries, with each solution tailored to 
reduce client risk and segmented into several categories:
•	
Transportation of hazardous (B3), non-hazardous, 
and medical waste
•	
Collection of hazardous (B3), non-hazardous, and 
medical waste
•	
Processing of hazardous (B3), non-hazardous, and 
medical waste
•	
Utilization of hazardous (B3), non-hazardous, and 
medical waste.
