In 2024, the global geopolitical landscape remained 
influenced by the ongoing conflict between Russia and 
Ukraine, as well as the tensions in the Middle East between 
Israel and Palestine, which continued to impact global 
economic conditions, particularly due to the rising energy 
prices. Additionally, the re-election of President Donald 
Trump in the United States introduced new dynamics with 
the “America First” policy; thus, potentially exerting major 
impact on the global economic landscape.
The rapidly evolving geopolitical dynamics significantly 
affected economic development and growth patterns 
across nations, causing increased fragmentation of the 
global economy and trade. In 2024, the United States 
economy grew by 2.8%, slightly below the previous year’s 
2.9%, while also facing the risk of increasing inflation

implemented. In contrast, China’s economy grew by 5.0%

government stimulus measures. Meanwhile, the European 
Union experienced a slowdown in economic growth, at a 
rate of 1.0% in 2024, primarily due to weakening exports and 
falling domestic demand.
Overall, global economic growth in 2024 was projected to 
be in the range of 2.6% to 3.2%, based on projections from 
international institutions, where the World Bank estimated 
world economic growth to grow by 2.6%, while the IMF 
projected the growth at 3.2%.
Amid this situation, the Indonesian economy grew quite well 
by 5.03% in 2024, with a controlled inflation rate of 1.57%, 
supported by domestic consumption that remained strong.

Indonesia tercatat mengalami pertumbuhan sebesar 7,57%

mencapai 221,5 juta  pada tahun 2024, dengan tingkat

tantangan utama. Wilayah seperti Maluku dan Papua

gilirannya akan menciptakan layanan telekomunikasi yang 
luas, murah, dan berkualitas.

In 2024, Indonesia’s telecommunications infrastructure 
industry was still affected by the impact of consolidation 
in the cellular industry. However, mobile network operators 
(MNOs) trend to spend capital more efficiently contributed 
to the growing demand for telecommunications supporting 
infrastructure. Throughout 2024, the tower industry 
recorded relatively good growth, along with the cellular 
industry growth. Driven by the increasing demand for 
mobile data services, the telecommunications industry in 
Indonesia recorded a growth of 7.57% compared to that of 
the previous year, based on data from Statistics Indonesia 
(BPS).
Indonesia’s telecommunications industry growth was 
supported by a projected 221.5 million internet users in 
2024, at a penetration rate of 79.5% of the total population. 
However, equalizing internet access was still a major 
challenge. Internet penetration rates at regions such as 
Maluku and Papua were below the average of 69.9%, lower 
than in Java which reached 83.6%. This disparity highlights 
the necessity for further efforts to overcome the digital 
divide, particularly in rural areas.
Furthermore, the industry challenges are not only about 
providing equal access, but also about improving the 
quality and developing integrated infrastructure; thus, 
encouraging the creation of industrial efficiency which 
in turn will create widespread, cost-effective, and high-
quality telecommunications services.

3.	 Power as a Service
	
Power as a Service (PaaS) adalah layanan penyediaan 
pasokan energi listrik untuk menara telekomunikasi 
dengan berbagai solusi hemat biaya dan ramah 
lingkungan. Layanan ini dirancang untuk memastikan 
ketersediaan daya yang andal guna mendukung 
operasional menara, baik di area yang terhubung ke 
jaringan listrik (on-grid) maupun di lokasi yang memiliki 
keterbatasan akses listrik (semi on-grid dan off-grid).
fiber optic route patrols, supervision, security against 
third-party disruptions, preventive maintenance, and 
troubleshooting issues (corrective maintenance).
	
Fiber Optic Transport Managed Service focuses 
on the availability of fiber optic transport (outside 
plant/OSP) and the management of spare parts for 
Telkomsel’s internet service provider (ISP) equipment. 
This service includes patrolling, supervision, and fiber 
optic maintenance, as well as spare parts support with 
a 4-hour response time from the request. Additionally, 
service request support management is available 24/7 
to ensure optimal service performance.
	
Radio IP and IP Long Haul is a Managed Service that 
supports the availability of radio-IP transport for 
Telkomsel’s node-B network owned by Telkom. 
This service includes technician deployment, work 
equipment provision, corrective maintenance, service 
request support management, and additional services 
based on customer requests.
	
CME Managed Service focuses on power supply, 
mechanical, and electrical (CME) maintenance. This 
service includes operational monitoring, preventive 
maintenance of CME equipment, and equipment room 
cleanliness to ensure optimal operations.
	
Fiber Optic Last Mile Managed Service functions as 
24/7 surveillance and patrolling of fiber optic cable 
routes, especially in backbone areas and fiber optic 
access networks. The main goal is to prevent disruptions 
caused by natural factors or third parties that could 
impact network connectivity.
	
The advantages of Mitratel’s Managed Service are as 
follows.

3.	 Power as a Service
	
Power as a Service (PaaS) is a service that provides 
electrical power supply for telecommunications towers 
with various cost-efficient and environmentally friendly 
solutions. This service is designed to ensure reliable 
power availability to support tower operations, whether 
in areas connected to the power grid (on-grid) or in 
locations with limited access to electricity (semi on-grid 
and off-grid).

5.	 Non-Terrestrial Network
	
Non-Terrestrial Network is a connectivity system service 
that operates above the Earth’s surface (up to an 
altitude of 550 km) with a system based on high-altitude 
platform stations (HAPS) and satellites. This service 
functions as a backhaul network, connecting BTS to the 
telecom operator’s control center, particularly in remote 
areas without access to terrestrial networks such as 
fiber optics or microwave radio (MW).

Dividend Policy
The Company’s dividend distribution is determined 
through the Annual General Meeting of Shareholders 
(AGMS) based on the recommendation of the Board of 
Directors, in accordance with the Law on Limited Liability 
Company and the Articles of Association. Dividends can be 
distributed if the Company has a positive retained earnings 
balance, by considering financial performance, cash flows, 
and business prospects.
Furthermore, interim dividends may be distributed before 
the fiscal year ends with the approval of the Board of 
Directors and the Board of Commissioners, provided that 
it does not cause the Company’s net assets to fall below 
the paid-in capital. If the Company incurs losses after the 
fiscal year ends, any interim dividends paid must be returned 
by the Shareholders.
In accordance with the Initial Public Offering Prospectus, 
the Company plans to distribute cash dividends with a 
maximum payout ratio of 70% of net profit after reserves, 
as determined by the GMS resolution. Dividends will be
