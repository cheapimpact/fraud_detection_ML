Global Economic Overview
Kondisi ekonomi global sepanjang tahun 2024 masih dipenuhi 
ketidakpastian. Berlanjutnya ketegangan geopolitik di Eropa 
dan Timur Tengah serta perang dagang antara Amerika 
Serikat dan Tiongkok masih menjadi tantangan utama yang 
berdampak signifikan terhadap kondisi ekonomi dunia. 
Kondisi ini berdampak pada gangguan rantai pasok dan 
komoditas global sehingga berdampak pada melambatnya 
pertumbuhan ekonomi negara-negara di dunia.
International Monetary Fund (IMF) dalam World Economic Outlook 
Update periode Januari 2025 memperkirakan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi dunia hanya mampu tumbuh sebesar 3,2% di 
tahun 2024 atau sedikit melemah dibanding tahun 2023 yang 
sebesar 3,3%. IMF mencatatkan capaian ini dikontribusikan 
dari pertumbuhan negara maju yang diperkirakan hanya 
mampu tumbuh sebesar 1,7% dan pertumbuhan ekonomi 
negara berkembang sebesar 4,2% pada tahun 2024. Menurut 
IMF, perlambatan kondisi ekonomi terutama dirasakan di 
kawasan Asia dan Eropa.
Meski kondisi ekonomi cukup menantang, akan tetapi tingkat 
inflasi secara global menunjukkan tren positif. Hal tersebut 
tercermin dari tingkat inflasi tahun 2024 yang sebesar 
5,8% atau mengalami penurunan dibanding tahun 2023 
yang mencapai 6,7% akibat kebijakan moneter ketat yang 
diterapkan oleh bank sentral di sejumlah negara. Atas hal 
tersebut, The Federal Reserve (The Fed) kembali memangkas Fed 
The global economic conditions throughout 2024 remained 
uncertain. Ongoing geopolitical tensions in Europe and the 
Middle East, along with the trade war between the United States 
and China, continued to pose significant challenges impacting 
the global economy. These conditions led to disruptions in global 
supply chains and commodities, which in turn slowed economic 
growth in several countries. 
The International Monetary Fund (IMF), in its World Economic 
Outlook Update for January 2025, projected that global economic 
growth would reach only 3.2% in 2024, slightly lower than 3.3% 
in 2023. The IMF attributed this to the modest growth of 1.7% 
in advanced economies and 4.2% in emerging markets in 2024. 
According to the IMF, the slowdown in economic conditions was 
mainly felt in the Asia and Europe regions.
Despite the challenging economic conditions, global inflation 
showed a positive trend. This was reflected in the 2024 global 
inflation rate of 5.8%, down from 6.7% in 2023, driven by tight 
monetary policies implemented by central banks in several 
countries. In response, The Federal Reserve (The Fed) cut the

Amid global economic uncertainty in 2024, Indonesia’s economic 
growth demonstrated strong resilience. According to data from 
the Statistics Indonesia (BPS), the national economic growth rate 
in 2024 was recorded at 5.03%. Although slightly lower than the 
5.05% recorded in 2023, achieving a growth rate of 5% was a 
positive outcome, especially considering that 2024 was a political 
year in Indonesia. This situation led investors and consumers 
across various industries to adopt a wait-and-see approach, 
which slowed the pace of national economic activity. 
The Growth Domestic Bruto (GDP) growth by industry was 
primarily driven by the construction sector, followed by trade 
and repair, mining and quarrying, manufacturing, agriculture, 
and other sectors. Meanwhile, GDP growth by expenditure was 
mainly supported by household non-profit institutions serving 
households (LNPRT), followed by exports and imports, government 
consumption, household consumption, and investment or gross 
fixed capital formation (PMTB). 
Bank Indonesia (BI) also reported that foreign exchange reserves 
reached USD155.7 billion in December 2024, the highest level 
compared to previous periods. This was supported by tax 
and service revenues, government foreign loan withdrawals, 
and foreign exchange earnings from the oil and gas sector. BI 
estimated that the Balance of Payments (BOP) in 2024 remained 
stable, supporting external stability, with the current account 
deficit maintained at a low range of 0.1% to 0.9% of GDP.

Industrial Overview
Tahun 2024 menjadi tahun yang penuh optimisme bagi 
industri penerbangan secara global. Tingginya konektivitas 
dan

mobilitas 
masyarakat 
pasca 
pandemi 
menjadi faktor utama yang mendorong pemulihan bagi 
industri penerbangan global. Berdasarkan data dari Asosiasi

sepanjang tahun 2024 yang diukur berdasarkan Revenue 
Passenger Kilometers (RPK) meningkat 9,71% dibanding tahun 
2023, sekaligus lebih tinggi dibanding pra pandemi tahun 2019 
yang sebesar 3,8%.
Di samping itu, kapasitas total yang diukur dalam Available 
Seat Kilometers (ASK) juga meningkat 7,78% di tahun 2024. 
Namun, tingginya RPK dibanding ASK di sejumlah wilayah 
menyebabkan Passenger Load Factor (PLF) ikut meningkat 
hingga mencapai rekor baru sebesar 87%. Hal ini menunjukkan 
bahwa permintaan perjalanan udara terus meningkat, 
meskipun 
terdapat 
tantangan 
seperti 
keterlambatan 
pengiriman pesawat baru dan masalah rantai pasok yang 
mempengaruhi beberapa maskapai.
Secara regional, Asia Pasifik menjadi kontributor utama dalam 
pertumbuhan industri penerbangan global, menyumbang 
lebih dari setengah peningkatan total trafik penumpang 
sepanjang tahun 2024. Wilayah ini diikuti oleh Eropa dan 
Amerika Utara, yang bersama-sama menyumbang sekitar 
sepertiga dari total pertumbuhan RPK global. Pasar domestik 
Tiongkok dan India mengalami ekspansi yang pesat, yang turut 
mendorong peningkatan lalu lintas udara di kawasan Asia 
Pasifik.
Di sisi perjalanan internasional, RPK meningkat sebesar 13,6% 
YoY, mencapai rekor tertinggi sepanjang sejarah industri 
penerbangan. Namun, beberapa faktor eksternal, seperti 
konflik geopolitik dan pembatasan wilayah udara, masih 
berdampak pada pola perjalanan global. Rute penerbangan 
antara Eropa dan Timur Tengah mengalami pertumbuhan 
signifikan, naik 11,9% dibandingkan tingkat pra-pandemi 
2019, sementara lalu lintas antara Timur Tengah dan Asia 
tumbuh 10,1%.
The year 2024 was filled with optimism for the global aviation 
industry. The high level of connectivity and increasing mobility 
needs of people in the post-pandemic era were the primary 
drivers of recovery in the global aviation sector. According to data 
from the International Air Transport Association (IATA), passenger 
traffic in 2024, as measured by Revenue Passenger Kilometers 
(RPK), increased by 9.71% compared to 2023, surpassing the pre-
pandemic level of 3.8% recorded in 2019. 
In addition, total capacity measured in Available Seat Kilometers 
(ASK) also grew by 7.78% in 2024. However, in several regions, the 
higher growth of RPK compared to ASK resulted in an increase in 
the Passenger Load Factor (PLF), reaching a record high of 87%. 
This indicates that air travel demand continued to rise despite 
challenges such as delays in new aircraft deliveries and supply 
chain disruptions affecting some airlines. 
Regionally, Asia-Pacific was the primary contributor to the growth 
of the global aviation industry, accounting for more than half of 
the total increase in passenger traffic in 2024. This region was 
followed by Europe and North America, which together contributed 
around one-third of the total global RPK growth. The domestic 
markets of China and India experienced rapid expansion, further 
driving air traffic growth in the Asia-Pacific region. 
On the international travel front, RPK grew by 13.6% year-on-year, 
reaching the highest level in the history of the aviation industry. 
However, several external factors, such as geopolitical conflicts 
and airspace restrictions, continued to impact global travel 
patterns. Air traffic on routes between Europe and the Middle East 
recorded significant growth, rising by 11.9% compared to pre-
pandemic 2019 levels, while traffic between the Middle East and 
Asia increased by 10.1%.

In the national aviation industry, the government has continued 
implementing the Pioneer Air Transport Program, aimed at 
enhancing connectivity in remote, outermost, underdeveloped, 
and border areas (3TP). In its execution, the program consists 
of 22 Regional Coordinators (Korwil) for Pioneer Air Transport, 
covering a total of 264 passenger routes, 44 pioneer cargo routes, 
and one subsidized air transport route. Through this initiative, 
the government ensures accessibility to air transportation across 
Indonesia, particularly for regions not yet served by other modes 
of transport. 
On the other hand, Indonesia’s aviation sector continues to show 
signs of recovery. According to data from the BPS, the number 
of international air passengers in 2024 reached 19.0 million, an 
increase of 21.46% compared to the previous year’s 15.64 million 
passengers. This growth reflects the recovery of the tourism 
sector and the increasing mobility of people traveling abroad. 
Meanwhile, domestic air passengers totaled 63.7 million, marking 
a 1.76% increase from the previous year’s 62.6 million passengers.

Prospek Usaha
Business Prospect
Pemulihan kondisi yang terjadi pada tahun 2024 diperkirakan 
akan terus berlanjut hingga tahun 2025. IMF dalam laporannya 
memproyeksikan pertumbuhan ekonomi global tetap stabil 
di angka 3,3% pada tahun 2025 dan 2026. Meskipun laju 
pertumbuhan diperkirakan tertahan, akan tetapi kondisi 
ini tetap didukung oleh faktor lain, seperti menurunnya 
tingkat inflasi di angka 4,2% pada 2025 dan 3,5% di 2026. IMF 
mencatat bahwa penurunan tingkat inflasi ini turut didukung 
oleh mulai normalnya kondisi di pasar tenaga kerja serta 
inflasi harga komoditas utama yang terus menurun di bawah 
tren yang terjadi. 
Tren pemulihan kondisi juga diperkirakan dapat dirasakan 
oleh industri penerbangan. IATA memproyeksikan industri 
penerbangan 
global 
mampu 
mencatatkan 
penguatan 
profitabilitas di tengah tantangan dengan membukukan 
pendapatan sebesar USD1,01 triliun atau naik 4,4% dibanding 
tahun 2024, dengan jumlah pengeluaran tumbuh 4,0% 
menjadi USD940 miliar. Hal ini juga menjadi pertama kalinya 
pendapatan industri penerbangan menyentuh angka USD1 
triliun.
Tingginya konektivitas dan mobilitas masyarakat pasca 
pandemi juga menjadi katalis positif bagi pertumbuhan 
industri penerbangan ke depan. Di tahun 2025, jumlah 
penumpang diperkirakan akan mencapai 5,2 miliar atau 
tumbuh 6,7% dari tahun sebelumnya, dengan permintaan 
tertinggi berasal dari kawasan Asia Pasifik. Di samping itu, 
volume kargo juga diperkirakan mampu tumbuh 5,8% menjadi 
72,5 juta ton pada tahun 2024.
Meski demikian, Indonesia National Air Carrier Association 
(INACA) melihat bahwa kondisi industri penerbangan di dalam 
negeri masih dihadapkan oleh sejumlah tantangan, seperti

c.	 Merupakan personel manajemen kunci entitas pelapor 
atau entitas induk dari entitas pelapor.
The Company and its subsidiaries engage in transactions with

conducted based on terms agreed upon by both parties, which 
may differ from transactions carried out with non-related parties.
Parties classified as related parties under PSAK 224 include:
1.	 A person or his/her immediate family member is said to have a
