Pada tahun 2023, ekonomi global tumbuh melambat 
yang dilatarbelakangi adanya eskalasi geopolitik di 
Benua Eropa dan Timur Tengah, belum pulihnya ekonomi 
negara maju, serta berlanjutnya ketidakpastian pasar 
uang, sehingga menimbulkan inflasi. Bank Indonesia 
memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan 
ekonomi 
global 
sebesar 3,0%, dan akan terus melambat di tahun 2024 
dengan proyeksi sebesar 2,8%.
In 2023, global economic growth will slow down against 
the backdrop of geopolitical escalation on the European 
Continent and the Middle East, the economic recovery 
of developed countries, as well as continued financial 
market uncertainty, giving rise to inflation. Bank 
Indonesia projects global economic growth at 3.0%, and 
will continue to slow down in 2024 with a projection of 
2.8%.
Sementara itu, ekonomi Indonesia tumbuh dengan baik 
mencapai level 5,05%. Pertumbuhan ini dilatarbelakangi 
adanya permintaan domestik yang meningkat, investasi, 
serta berlanjutnya proyek strategis nasional (PSN) di 
sepanjang tahun 2023. Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia 
juga dihasilkan dari pertumbuhan produk domestik bruto 
(PDB), baik dari lapangan usaha maupun pengeluaran.
Meanwhile, Indonesia's economy grew well, reaching a 
level of 5.05%. This growth is motivated by increasing 
domestic demand, investment, and the continuation 
of national strategic projects (PSN) throughout 2023. 
Indonesia's economic growth is also resulting from 
growth in gross domestic product (GDP), both from 
business fields and expenditure.
Pada aspek lapangan usaha, pertumbuhan tertinggi 
diraih oleh sektor transportasi dan pergudangan 
sebesar 10,33%; jasa lainnya 10,15%; serta pengadaan 
listrik dan gas sebesar 8,68%. Pada aspek pengeluaran, 
pertumbuhan tertinggi diraih oleh konsumsi lembaga 
non profit yang melayani rumah tangga (LNPRT) sebesar 
18,11%; pembentukan modal tetap bruto (PMTB) sebesar 
5,02%; dan konsumsi rumah tangga sebesar 4,47%.
In the business sector aspect, the highest growth was 
achieved by the transportation and warehousing sector 
at 10.33%; other services 10.15%; as well as electricity 
and gas procurement of 8.68%. In the expenditure 
aspect, the highest growth was achieved by consumption 
by non-profit institutions serving households (LNPRT) at 
18.11%; gross fixed capital formation (PMTB) of 5.02%; 
and household consumption of 4.47%.
Meningkatnya ekonomi Indonesia berasal dari kinerja 
industri barang logam, mesin, alat transportasi dan 
elektronika (ilmate), yang berhasil tumbuh sebesar 
10,33% dan menjadi salah satu sektor dengan kontribusi 
besar pada PDB. Kondisi ini didukung semakin 
kompetitifnya produk elektronika di Indonesia, sehingga 
memberikan peluang dan meningkatkan keyakinan 
investor untuk menanamkan modal. 
The increase in the Indonesian economy came from 
the performance of the metal goods, machinery, 
transportation equipment and electronics (ilmate) 
industry, which managed to grow by 10.33% and became 
one of the sectors with a large contribution to GDP. This 
condition is supported by the increasingly competitive 
electronic products in Indonesia, thus providing 
opportunities and increasing investors' confidence in 
investing capital.
Ke depannya, pemerintah akan melakukan rencana untuk 
pengembangan industri elektronik dengan menyusun 
roadmap elektronik Indonesia, membangun elektronik 
industrial park, mengembangkan sumber daya manusia 
(SDM) dengan keterampilan tinggi, serta kegiatan riset 
dan pengembangan di bidang elektronik.
In the future, the government will plan to develop 
the electronics industry by compiling an Indonesian 
electronics roadmap, building electronics industrial 
parks, developing human resources (HR) with high skills, 
as well as research and development activities in the 
electronics sector.
