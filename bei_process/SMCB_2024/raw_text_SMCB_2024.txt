Due to to hard work, solid synergy, and strategic roles of 
the State Budget (APBN) as an instrument in overseeing 
the economy, Indonesia was able to maintain stable growth. 
The Statistics Indonesia has announced that Indonesia’s 
economic growth in 2024 was 5.03%, slightly below the 2023 
achievement of 5.05% and lower than the 2024 APBN target 
of 5.2%.
Factors affecting economic performance during 2024 included 
a decline in exports in the second half of 2024. Indonesia’s 
exports weakened significantly due to the decline in global 
commodity prices. Conversely, imports showed relatively 
better signs of recovery. Export performance contributed 
22.2% to Indonesia’s GDP, while imports contributed 20.4%.
On the other hand, household consumption remains the main 
driver of domestic economic growth, growing by 4.9%, higher 
than the 2023 growth of only 4.8%. Solid domestic demand, 
supported by fiscal policy, plays a major role in overcoming 
economic pressures and protecting the community, especially 
the poor and vulnerable. Investment or Gross Fixed Capital 
Formation (PMTB) also grew by 4.6%, up from 4.4% in 2023.
Inflation reached its highest position of 2.05% in March 2024, 
influenced cyclically by the Eid al-Fitr celebration. Furthermore, 
the inflation rate continues to move down to 1.57% in 
December 2024. This decrease in inflation was influenced 
by weakening domestic consumption and purchasing power 
which had an impact on the downward trend in domestic 
industry and trade.
At the end of 2024, the manufacturing sector returned 
to positive movement. This was reflected in the results of 
a survey released by S&P Global, which showed that the 
achievement of the Indonesian Manufacturing Purchasing 
Manager’s Index (PMI) in December was in the expansion 
phase at 51.2, a significant increase compared to November 
which experienced a contraction at 49.6. This means that 
the national manufacturing industry has managed to rebound 
after five consecutive months of contraction since July 2024.

SEMEN HIJAU
Perseroan senantiasa melakukan inovasi produk untuk 
memperluas pasar yang disesuaikan dengan kebutuhan 
pelanggan. Perseroan mendorong pemanfaatan produk 
semen non-OPC yang lebih ramah lingkungan dalam berbagai

non-OPC dapat dikategorikan sebagai “semen hijau” karena 
menggunakan material dan proses produksi ramah lingkungan 
dengan kinerja produk yang sesuai peruntukkan konstruksi.

ramah lingkungan dan diproduksi menggunakan teknologi 
terkini yang lebih efisien dalam penggunaan energi dan ramah 
lingkungan. Proses produksi semen hijau menghasilkan emisi 
karbon yang lebih rendah yaitu bervariasi sampai dengan 38% 
dibandingkan semen yang diproduksi secara konvensional. 
Selain itu, semen hijau memiliki Tingkat Komponen Dalam 
Negeri (TKDN) di atas 90%.

aggregates with a size of 0-5 mm and other aggregates.

problems.

TINJAUAN PEMASARAN 
Mengingat persaingan di industri semen semen yang sangat 
kompetitif, perumusan, evaluasi, dan penyesuaian strategi 
pemasaran menjadi sangat penting bagi Perseroan agar dapat 
memenangkan persaingan. 
Sebagai bagian dari SIG, strategi dan kegiatan pemasaran 
Perseroan menginduk pada strategi SIG yang telah 
merumuskan strategi penetrasi pasar domestik yang 
disesuaikan dengan kondisi pasar dan dinilai efektif dalam 
menghadapi kompetisi di industri bahan bangunan. 
 
Strategi Multibrand  
Strategi 
ini 
disusun 
untuk 
mengoptimalkan 
dan 
mempertahankan dominasi pangsa pasar semen tahun 2024 
di tengah permintaan semen yang terkontraksi terutama untuk

kelebihan bahkan penambahan kapasitas pasok.
Perseroan 
menerapkan 
strategi 
multibrand 
dengan

dari sisi jumlah pemain dan perbedaan harga jual. Strategi ini 
dipandang tepat untuk menjaga kondisi industri tetap kondusif 
dengan tidak memicu perang harga. Penerapan strategi ini 
memungkinkan Perseroan untuk dapat menahan peningkatan 
pangsa pasar dari pesaing yang tergolong ke dalam economic 
brand.
 
Strategi Micro Market
Untuk mengoptimalkan pencapaian pangsa pasar dan 
profitabilitas, Perseroan menerapkan startegi micro market 
dimana kebijakan yang berhubungan dengan penjualan dan 
pemasaran didasarkan pada karakteristik pasar untuk setiap 
area yang berbeda. Kebijakan pemasaran dan penjualan 
ditentukan berdasarkan area yang lebih kecil dengan 
mempertimbangkan potensi pasar di area tersebut dan tingkat 
kompetitif SIG.
Skema ini memberikan manfaat untuk penerapan strategi 
yang lebih tepat sasaran sehingga optimalisasi penjualan 
dapat tercapai baik dari sisi volume penjualan dan dari sisi 
pencapaian pendapatan.
 
Strategi Direct Selling 
Perseroan juga mengembangkan strategi direct selling untuk 
melayani pelanggan strategis secara langsung. Strategi ini 
digunakan untuk memperkuat hubungan dengan pelanggan 
dan memastikan produk diterima langsung oleh konsumen 
dengan efektif dan efisien. 
 
Dengan kombinasi strategi kanal distribusi melalui distributor 
dan direct selling, perseroan dapat memastikan ketersediaan 
produk secara merata di seluruh Indonesia, mendukung 
pertumbuhan bisnis, dan meningkatkan kepuasan pelanggan 
secara berkelanjutan. 
 
MARKETING OVERVIEW
Considering the highly competitive competition in the cement 
industry, the formulation, evaluation and adjustment of 
marketing strategies are very important for the Company to 
win the competition.
As part of SIG, the Company’s marketing strategies and 
activities are based on SIG’s strategy which has formulated 
a domestic market penetration strategy that is adjusted 
to market conditions and is considered effective in facing 
competition in the building materials industry.
Multibrand Strategy
This strategy is designed to optimize and maintain the 
dominance of the cement market share in 2024 amidst 
contracted cement demand, especially for the bag cement 
segment, while the industry is still experiencing excess or 
even additional supply capacity.
The Company implements a multibrand strategy by optimizing 
product marketing with the premium SIG brand in each 
major market, as well as using certain brands selectively in 
market areas with high competition in terms of the number of 
players and selling price disparity. This strategy is considered 
appropriate to maintain conducive industrial conditions by not 
triggering a price war. The implementation of this strategy 
allows the Company to withstand the increase in market share 
from competitors classified as economic brands.
Micro Market Strategy
To optimize market share and profitability, the Company 
implements a micro market strategy where policies related 
to sales and marketing are based on market characteristics 
for each different area. Marketing and sales policies are 
determined based on smaller areas by considering market 
potential in the area and the competitive level of SIG.
This scheme provides benefits for implementing more targeted 
strategies so that sales optimization can be achieved both in 
terms of sales volume and in terms of revenue achievement.
Direct Selling Strategy
The Company also develops a direct selling strategy to 
serve strategic customers directly. This strategy is used to 
strengthen relationships with customers and ensure that 
products are received directly by consumers effectively and 
efficiently.
With a combination of distribution channel strategies through 
distributors and direct selling, the Company can ensure product 
availability evenly throughout Indonesia, support business 
growth, and increase customer satisfaction sustainably.

POLICY ON CAPITAL STRUCTURE
Capital Structure
The Company’s capital structure consists of funding sourced 
from interest-bearing loans, lease liabilities and equity. At the 
end of 2024, the largest composition of the Company’s capital 
structure was from equity of 85.8%, and loans of 12.9%. 
Analyzing from its composition, a decrease of the loan portion 
resulted from repayment of debt in 2024. Equity portion 
increased due to the addition of retained earnings.
Management Policy on Capital Structure
The Company manages capital to ensure that it maintains 
healthy capital ratios to support its business and maximise 
shareholder value.
The Company is required by Corporate Law 2007 to 
maintain appropriately retained earnings amounting to 20% 
of the issued and fully paid share capital. The Company has 
established appropriated retained earnings amounting to 
Rp901.9 billion as of December 31, 2024, and December 31, 
2023, respectively. 
The Company’s policy is to maintain leverage within an optimal 
range by comparing it with similar companies in Indonesia. 
This is being done to ensure a good financial condition of 
the company and ensure access to competitive sources of 
funding.
The Company maintains a sound level of its capital structure 
by monitoring the net gearing ratio, which is calculated by 
dividing total loans with interest bearing liabilities net of cash 
and cash equivalents by total equity attributable to owners of 
the parent entity.

PROSPEK USAHA 
Tahun 2025, dunia diperkirakan masih menghadapi sejumlah

pelemahan ekonomi China dan AS, ketegangan geopolitik 
yang berlanjut, dampak perubahan iklim, serta ketidakpastian 
suku bunga global.
Indonesia berhasil melalui tahun 2024 dengan performa yang 
kuat pada pertumbuhan ekonomi didukung oleh keberlanjutan 
reformasi struktural dan stabilitas sektor keuangan. Memasuki 
tahun 2025, World Bank memproyeksikan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi Indonesia dapat mencapai 5,1%, meningkat dari 
5,03% pada tahun 2024. Potensi keuangan Indonesia 
diperkirakan tetap menjanjikan, didorong oleh reformasi 
kebijakan ekonomi, adopsi digitalisasi, serta investasi pada 
sektor-sektor strategis. 
Risiko global yang dapat memengaruhi kondisi ekonomi 
domestik di antaranya terkait dengan trade war 2.0, yakni 
potensi perang dagang akibat kebijakan tarif impor yang 
akan dikenakan oleh Pemerintah AS yang baru. Selain itu, 
ketegangan geopolitik, khususnya di Timur Tengah, China-
Taiwan, serta hubungan AS dengan negara-negara BRICS, 
turut meningkatkan ketidakpastian di pasar keuangan global. 
Peluang
Industri semen diperkirakan akan tumbuh didukung oleh 
normalisasi aktivitas manufaktur pasca-pemilu dan berjalannya 
pemerintahan baru. Komitmen Presiden Prabowo Subianto 
terhadap kelanjutan pembangunan IKN juga menumbuhkan 
optimisme bagi pelaku industri. Selain itu, penurunan suku 
bunga acuan memberikan katalis positif bagi industri semen, 
karena berpotensi menurunkan suku bunga KPR dan 
meningkatkan aktivitas infrastruktur dan properti. Dukungan 
pemerintah baru terhadap sektor perumahan rakyat dan 
infrastruktur diproyeksikan dapat meningkatkan volume 
penjualan semen dan mendukung stabilisasi harga semen.
Pemerintah mencanangkan program pembangunan tiga juta 
rumah untuk masyarakat berpenghasilan rendah. Jika bisa 
direalisasikan lebih awal, maka akan menjadi pendorong

BUSINESS PROSPECTS
In 2025, the world is expected to face several challenges that 
could hinder global economic recovery. Key risks affecting 
global conditions include the economic slowdown in China and 
the US, ongoing geopolitical tensions, the impact of climate 
change and uncertainty surrounding global interest rates.
Indonesia successfully navigated 2024 with strong economic 
growth, supported by continued structural reforms and 
financial sector stability. Entering 2025, the World Bank 
projects Indonesia’s economic growth to reach 5.1%, up 
from 5.03% in 2024. Indonesia’s financial prospects remain 
promising, driven by economic policy reforms, digital adoption 
and investments in strategic sectors.
Global risks that may impact the domestic economy among 
others related to Trade War 2.0, referring to possible trade 
conflicts arising from new US government-imposed import 
tariffs. Additionally, geopolitical tensions, particularly in the 
Middle East, China-Taiwan and US relations with BRICS 
nations, further contribute to uncertainty in global financial 
markets.
Opportunities
The cement industry is expected to grow supported by 
the normalization of manufacturing activities following the 
elections and the establishment of the new government. 
President Prabowo Subianto’s commitment to continuing 
development of the IKN has also fostered optimism among 
industry players. Additionally, the decline in benchmark interest 
rates serves as a positive catalyst for the cement industry, as 
it could lower mortgage rates and stimulate infrastructure 
and property activities. The new government’s support for 
the housing and infrastructure sectors is projected to drive 
cement sales volume and to help stabilize cement prices.
The government announced a program to build three million 
houses for low-income communities. If thid program is realized 
earlier, it will be a driver of cement industry growth in 2025. 
This program is estimated to increase cement consumption 
from SIG by 15 million tons per year. In addition, innovations 
in non-cement products such as precision interlocking bricks, 
decorative concrete, and porous paving blocks will also be 
needed in this project.
The government’s decision to extend the value-added tax 
incentive covered by the state (PPN DTP) for the transfer 
of landed houses and apartment units for fiscal year 2025 
effective from February 4, 2025, also has the potential to 
increase demand for bagged cement sold through retail 
channels for new housing developments by property 
developers.

Challenges
In January 2025, President Prabowo Subianto issued 
Presidential Instruction (Inpres) No. 1 of 2025 concerning 
Spending Efficiency in the Implementation of the State Budget 
(APBN) and Regional Budget (APBD) for 2025 Fiscal Year. This 
directive aims to achieve APBN savings of Rp306.6 trillion and 
to reduce regional transfer funds (TKD) by Rp50.5 trillion.
In addition to potentially suppressing consumption and 
reducing household purchasing power, the budget cuts will 
also impact infrastructure projects funded by the APBN and 
APBD. The budget ceiling for the Ministry of Public Works (PU) 
for 2025 fiscal year, initially set at Rp110.95 trillion, has been 
reduced by Rp81.38 trillion, leaving a remaining budget of 
Rp29.57 trillion.
This policy is expected to affect the continuation of projects, 
such as road construction and rehabilitation, toll roads, as well 
as reservoirs and dams for irrigation purposes. The budget 
reduction will also have repercussions across the construction 
supply chain, including workers in factories or manufacturers 
supplying materials for infrastructure projects. If construction 
projects are delayed or halted, the distribution of building 
materials will also be disrupted.
Nevertheless, the Minister of Public Works has assured that 
its infrastructure development programs for 2025 fiscal year 
have been carefully planned. Therefore, budget efficiency 
measures will not disrupt, let alone halt, ongoing infrastructure 
projects.

Dividend Policy	
The Company is committed to optimizing value for shareholders 
through the distribution of dividends depending on the 
Company’s financial condition. The decision to distribute the 
Company’s dividends is based on, among other things, the net 
profit earned in the fiscal year and the Company’s obligation 
to allocate reserve funds in accordance with applicable 
regulations and the Company’s financial condition.

rates and expansion plans in dividend distribution decisions.

the COVID-19 pandemic.

to pay cash dividends to entitled shareholders no later than 30 
days after the announcement of the summary of the minutes 
of the GMS which decides the distribution of cash dividends.
