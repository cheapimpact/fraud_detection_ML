MINING AND PROCESSING
Production
Between October 2022 and April 2023, the Batu Hijau 
mine experienced approximately 4,100 mm of rainfall, 
nearly double the historical annual average, and as a result, 
collected a large volume of rainwater at the bottom of 
the pit. As water levels in the Santong-3 holding pond had 
already reached critical levels we were unable to pump water 
from the pit to the Santong-3 pond at a rate fast enough to 
resume normal mining operations, as the additional discharge 
of water from the pond into the nearby river system could 
have exacerbated flooding downstream. As a result, we were 
unable to access to Phase 7 fresh ore at the bottom of the pit 
until July 2023.
To optimize operations, we focused on Phase 8 waste 
removal, which will allow accelerated access to Phase 8 
fresh ore in 2024-2025. As we transitioned into the dry 
season, we accelerated pit dewatering to in order to resume 
access to high-grade Phase 7 ore. However, once the pit was 
dewatered, substansial residual mud impeded ore mining. 
Significant time and costs were incurred in handling the mud.
We drew on our stockpiles to continue copper processing at 
full capacity while fresh ore extraction was delayed. However, 
as the stockpiled ore is of a lower grade than the fresh ore, 
copper concentrate production was lower than anticipated 
for the year. Copper concentrate production increased 
significantly from September 2023 as we resumed fresh ore 
production. By the end of the year we were achieving record 
productivity levels.
Although ex-pit tonnes mined in 2023 increased compared to 
2022 due to accelerated production in the second half, total 
production of copper concentrate was lower than in 2022 
due to the processing of lower grade ore stockpiles over a 
period several months.

DEVELOPMENT AND OPERATION OF SUPPORT 
FACILITIES
Smelter and PMR
The smelter and PMR are currently under construction at a 
location 18 km from the Batu Hijau mine and approximately 
1.5 km from Benete Port. The smelter’s input capacity of 
900,000 dry metric tonnes per annum (“tpa”) of copper 
concentrate and is designed for concentrates from the Batu 
Hijau ore and the future Elang ore. When fully operational, 
the smelter is expected to produce up to 220 ktpa of copper 
cathode with a purity of 99.99% as well as 820 ktpa of 
industrial grade sulfuric acid with concentration of more than 
98.0%.
The PMR will process 970 tpa of anode slime from the smelter 
to produce approximately 18 tpa of gold bullions and 55 
tpa of silver bullions with a purity of 99.99% and 99.95%, 
respectively. It will also produce 77 tpa of selenium with a 
purity of 99.90%.
Both facilities will be equipped with state-of-the-art 
environmentally friendly technology. Our smelter has a slag 
concentrator which recovers additional copper and gold in 
the discard slag and returns valuable material to the primary 
smelter.  Furthermore, slag tailings is also looped back to 
the primary floatation circuit, in order to enhance metals 
recovery and tailings quality.  
To expedite the delivery of the project and to ensure strict 
quality assurance in a more controlled environment, we are 
managing much of the fabrication offshore, using independent 
quality surveyors to supervise and manage QA/QC plans during 
fabrication and pre-dispatch approvals. As of 31 December 
2023, we had completed 76.1% of the smelter, or 105.1% of 
the scheduled year-end target of 72.4%, according to an 
independently verified assessment. Progress on the PMR had 
reached 72.7%, or 100.7% of the scheduled year-end target 
of 72.2%. We expect the smelter construction to progress as 
scheduled, with physical completion by the end of May 2024 as 
required by the Government. Following the physical completion 
of the smelter, we will focus on commissioning process and 
the production of the first copper cathode in the latter part of 
2024.
The development of the smelter and PMR reflect 
the commitment of the Group to the Government’s 
downstreaming strategy of growing domestic mineral refining 
capacity in order to move up the commodity value chain. Both 
facilities are considered to be national strategic projects 
and as such are monitored by the Coordinating Ministry for 
Economic Affairs of the Republic of Indonesia. It is expected 
that, when fully operational, these facilities will stimulate 
local and national economic growth.

QUALITY CONTROL
AMNT’s processing plant also houses processing QA/QC and 
an environmental laboratories. The laboratories analyze key 
parameters at various stages of the ore extraction process – 
from the ore mining to the process plant and to concentrate 
shipping.
 
The QA/QC laboratory is used for grade control during 
ore mining, monitor processing recoveries through the 
process plant and accurately measure the copper, gold and 
silver content of concentrates before shipping. The QA/
QC laboratory also regularly tests tailings samples to ensure 
compliance with the quality standards set by the Government. 
The environmental laboratory, operated by the Environmental 
Affairs Department, tests wastewater and environmental 
conditions to ensure compliance with government quality 
standards. The environmental laboratory has been certified 
and accredited for SNI ISO/IEC 17025:2017 (testing and 
calibration laboratories) by the National Accreditation 
Committee of Indonesia.

PROSPEK BISNIS  
Business Prospects
The shift from high carbon fuel sources towards 
electrification and renewable energy, as well as increasing 
urbanization and industrialisation in developing economies, 
are expected to sustain demand for copper over the short 
and medium term. Globally, total copper consumption is 
projected to grow at a Compound Annual Growth Rate 
(“CAGR”) of 2.7% between 2023 and 2033, while refined 
copper consumption is expected to show a CAGR of 2.2% 
over the same period.
Copper price is projected to increase to an annual average 
of US$8,725 per tonne in 2024 on the back of rising demand, 
stimulated by a potential lowering of interest rates as 
inflation eases over the year. Higher prices are also likely to 
be supported by a tightening of copper supplies on the back 
of an expected acceleration of economic activity in China, 
an upturn in the global economic recovery, and the slowing 
pace of mine growth, as well as the suspension of operations 
at one of the world’s largest mines in Panama.2 Nevertheless, 
the ongoing geopolitical tensions continue to pose the risk of 
trade disruptions or recession, which could negatively impact 
prices.

ASPEK PEMASARAN  
Marketing Aspects
Group produces high grade copper and gold in concentrate 
form, which is also extremely clean with minimal impurities 
of arsenic, mercury, lead, fluorine, antimony, and bismuth 
meeting most stringent industry standards. Due to these low 
impurity levels, copper concentrates produced by the Group 
are highly sought after by smelters within Asia.
For the last two decades we have sold our products 
principally to copper smelters in Japan, South Korea, the 
Philippines, Indonesia, and India. In recent years we have 
expanded our market, through global trading partners, to 
smelters in China, where our copper concentrate is used as 
sweetener to blend with higher impurity materials either at 
Chinese ports or at customer premises.
Our marketing strategy is to have a diversified sales portfolio 
across established counterparties within two major segments: 
1) directly to copper smelters and 2) distribute through global 
trade houses. 
We have a dedicated sales and marketing team that manages 
our long-term relationships with existing and potential end 
customers, international traders, market participants, analysts 
and other commodities and trading stakeholders.
Grup memproduksi tembaga dan emas berkadar tinggi dalam

seperti arsenik, merkuri, timbal, fluorin, antimon, dan bismut 
sehingga memenuhi standar industri yang paling ketat. 
Karena kandungan kotoran yang minimal tersebut, konsentrat 
tembaga yang diproduksi oleh Grup senantiasa diminati oleh 
smelter di Asia.
Selama dua dekade terakhir kami telah menjual produk kami,

Strategi pemasaran kami ditekankan pada upaya untuk

global.

KEBIJAKAN DIVIDEN  
Dividend Policy
Under Indonesian law and our Articles of Association, 
the decision on the distribution of dividends is made by 
a resolution of shareholders at the annual GMS upon the 
recommendation of the Board of Directors. The Company 
may declare dividends (in US dollars or any other currencies 
pursuant to the prevailing laws and regulations) in any year if 
the Company have positive retained earnings. The declaration, 
amount and future payment of cash dividends, is determined 
by Board of Directors and Board of Commissioners of the 
Company, and subject to approval at GMS of the Company. 
The amount of dividends will be subject to various factors, 
including, but not limited to: 
i.	
the retained earnings, operating performance, cash flows, 
future business prospects and financial condition of the 
Company, and 
ii.	 other factors deemed relevant by the shareholders of the 
Company. 
Based on our Articles of Association, prior to the end of 
a financial year, an interim dividend may be distributed, 
provided that the interim dividend may be distributed if it 
does not result in net assets of the Company becoming less 
than the total issued and paid-up capital and the statutory 
reserves. Such distributions are determined by Board of 
Directors of the Company after first being approved by 
Board of Commissioners of the Company. If, after the end 
of the relevant financial year, the Company incur losses, 
the distributed interim dividend must be returned by the 
shareholders to the Company, and the Board of Directors and 
Board of Commissioners of the Company will be jointly and 
severally responsible if the interim dividend is not returned. 
With due observance to Indonesian law and Articles of 
Association of the Company, the Company intend to declare 
dividends to be approved by the shareholders after the 
Company achieve positive retained earnings. To the extent the 
Company decide to declare dividends, dividends will be paid 
in Rupiah. Holders of the Company's shares on the applicable 
record dates will be entitled to the full amount of dividends 
approved, subject to any Indonesian withholding tax imposed. 
Dividends received by a non-Indonesian holder of shares will 
be subject to 20% Indonesian withholding tax. 
Company recorded a positive retained earnings balance as 
of 31 December 2021 and has not paid any dividends since 
then. At this time, the Company does not intend to pay any 
dividends until we complete our project expansion programs. 
The amount of dividends and ability of the Company to pay 
dividends in the future shall depend on cash flows, retained 
earnings, financial conditions, working capital needs, and 
investment plans of the Company. in the future, as well as the 
limitations specified by any applicable laws, regulations or any 
other requirements.
Berdasarkan hukum yang berlaku di Indonesia dan Anggaran 
Dasar kami, keputusan tentang pembagian dividen dibuat 
berdasarkan keputusan pemegang saham pada RUPS tahunan 
atas rekomendasi dari Direksi. Perseroan akan melakukan 
pembayaran dividen (dalam dolar AS atau mata uang lainnya 
sesuai dengan peraturan perundang-undangan yang berlaku) 
setiap tahun hanya jika Perseroan memiliki saldo laba positif. 
Pengumuman, jumlah dan pembayaran dividen tunai yang 
akan datang, ditentukan oleh Direksi dan Dewan Komisaris 
Perseroan, dan tunduk pada persetujuan RUPS Perseroan. 
Jumlah dividen ditentukan berdasarkan pada berbagai faktor, 
termasuk, namun tidak terbatas pada: 
i.	
saldo laba, kinerja usaha, arus kas, prospek usaha di masa 
depan dan kondisi keuangan Perseroan, dan 
ii.	 faktor-faktor lain yang dianggap relevan oleh pemegang 
saham Perseroan. 
Berdasarkan Anggaran Dasar kami, sebelum akhir tahun buku, 
dividen interim dapat dibagikan, dengan ketentuan bahwa 
pembayaran dividen dilakukan jika tidak mengurangi aset 
bersih Perseroan terhadap total modal ditempatkan dan 
disetor serta cadangan wajib. Pembagian tersebut ditentukan 
oleh Direksi Perseroan setelah mendapat persetujuan 
terlebih dahulu oleh Dewan Komisaris Perseroan. Jika, setelah 
akhir tahun buku yang bersangkutan, Perseroan mengalami 
kerugian, dividen interim yang dibagikan harus dikembalikan 
oleh pemegang saham kepada Perseroan, dan Direksi serta 
Dewan Komisaris Perseroan akan bertanggung jawab secara 
tanggung renteng maupun secara sendiri-sendiri jika dividen 
interim tidak dikembalikan.
 
Dengan memperhatikan hukum yang berlaku di Indonesia dan 
Anggaran Dasar Perseroan, maka Perseroan berencana untuk 
membagikan dividen yang telah mendapat persetujuan dari 
pemegang saham setelah Perseroan membukukan saldo laba 
positif. Dalam hal Perseroan memutuskan untuk membayarkan 
dividen, maka dividen akan dibayarkan dalam mata uang Rupiah. 
Pemegang saham yang tercatat pada tanggal pencatatan 
yang ditetapkan berhak atas jumlah dividen yang disetujui, 
namun akan dikenakan pajak sesuai peraturan perpajakan yang 
berlaku di Indonesia. Dividen yang diterima oleh pemegang 
saham di luar Indonesia akan dikenakan pajak sesuai peraturan 
perpajakan yang berlaku di Indonesia yaitu sebesar 20%. 
Perseroan mencatatkan saldo laba positif per 31 Desember 
2021 dan belum membayar dividen sejak saat itu. Namun 
pada saat ini, Perseroan tidak berencana untuk membayar 
dividen hingga kami merampungkan seluruh program ekspansi 
modal kami. Jumlah dividen dan kemampuan Perseroan untuk 
membayar dividen di masa depan akan tergantung pada arus 
kas, saldo laba, kondisi keuangan, kebutuhan modal kerja, dan 
rencana investasi Perseroan di masa depan, serta batasan yang 
ditentukan oleh undang-undang, peraturan, atau persyaratan 
lain yang berlaku.
