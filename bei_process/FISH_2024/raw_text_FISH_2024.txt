Economy and Industry
ECONOMIC REVIEW  
According to data from the IMF World Economic Outlook 
Update (January 2025), the global economy in 2024 
is projected to grow by 3.2%, down from 3.3% in 2023. 
Global inflation in 2024 is also projected to decrease to 
5.7% from 6.7% in the previous year.  
The global economy in 2024 maintained stable growth, 
supported by controlled inflation, sustained private 
consumption, and early monetary policy easing in major 
markets. However, economic performance remained 
constrained due to increasing geopolitical tensions and 
policy uncertainty, resulting in lower economic growth 
compared to the previous year.  
Amid global challenges, Indonesia’s economy grew by 
5.03% throughout 2024 (c-on-c). Although slightly 
below the 5.05% (c-on-c) growth recorded in 2023, this 
performance highlights the country’s stable economic 
growth above 5% in recent years. Indonesia’s economic 
growth in this political year is driven by increased 
investment and manufacturing activities, as well as still 
significant domestic demand, even though it has slightly 
weakened compared to the previous year. The growth of 
several sectors, such as the logistics sector related to food 
and feed, has even outperformed the overall economic 
growth.

Trading and Distribution
The Company’s trading and distribution activities are 
distributing food and feed ingredient through various 
distribution channel at national level. Food ingredients 
mean staple foods and fast-moving consumer goods for 
cooking, additional ingredients in the kitchen, and snacks. 
Those products are distributed through B2B and B2C 
channels. 
Throughout 2024, the Company faced challenges due 
to the weakening purchasing power of the public, which 
also affected demand from the company’s clients, both 
B2B and B2C. To address this situation, the company has 
implemented several initiatives, including:
1.	 More Flexible Pricing
	
The company continues to adjust product’s price 
based on market analysis and consumer purchasing 
power to remain competitive. The company is also 
trying to provide attractive discounts and promotions 
for customers.
2.	 Strengthening and Enhancing Relationships with 
Customers
	
The Company continues to develop loyalty programs 
to reward loyal customers. Additionally, the company 
has created a customer satisfaction survey process 
to gather feedback from customers, allowing for 
continuous improvement in service.
3.	 Improving Distribution and Accessibility
	
The company optimizes the supply chain to ensure 
products are always available at the right distribution 
points, making it easy for consumers to obtain the 
products they need.
4.	 Regular Monitoring and Evaluation
	
The company regularly evaluates sales performance 
and the impact of each applied strategy to identify 
potential areas for improvement. If necessary, the 
Company will make adjustments to marketing and 
distribution strategies can be made to be more 
responsive to changes in market purchasing power.

Assets
Total Assets
As of 31 December 2024, the Company’s total assets 
increased by 6.4% to USD522.2 million from USD490.8 
million on 31 December 2023. This increase was primarily 
due to increase in current assets amounting to USD23.3 
million or 6.5% compared to prior year.
Current Assets
The net revenue received by the Company from trading 
and distribution activities in 2024 was recorded at USD1.26 
billion. There was a decrease of 8.3% compared to the 
previous year’s revenue of USD1.37 billion. This difference 
occurred primarily due to a general decline in commodity 
prices compared to 2023. Although sales volume did not 
reach the expected growth target, it remained within the 
same range compared to 2023.
Logistics
The Company’s logistics services cover port services, 
warehousing, and transportation services. The revenues 
came from stevedoring and port handling services, 
warehousing (warehouse lease and management), and 
transportation services in Cilegon and Surabaya.
In 2024,  the revenue received by the Company from 
logistics activities was recorded at USD35.8 million. This 
represents a 7.2% increase compared to the previous 
year’s revenue of USD33.4 million. The increase in revenue 
was due to the operation of additional conveyor systems 
with higher service prices and adjustments in service rates 
in several areas.
Manufacturing
The Company’s manufacturing activities involve the 
production of various processed fish products, including 
fish oil and fish meal. While this activity is the cornerstone 
of the business, it is not the primary focus of the Company 
at present. The revenue derived from processed fish 
products is highly dependent on the availability of fish.
The Company’s net revenue from manufacturing activities 
in 2024 was USD6.6 million, experiencing an increase of 
171.4% compared to 2023.
