Laporan Tahunan & Laporan Keberlanjutan 2023
Pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia tumbuh resilien sebesar 
5,05% pada 2023, melambat dari tahun 2022 yaitu 5,31%. 
Pertumbuhan ini utamanya dikontribusikan oleh komponen 
pengeluaran konsumsi rumah tangga yang tumbuh sebesar 
4,82% (yoy) serta Pembentukan Modal Tetap Bruto (PMTB) 
yang mencapai 4,40% (yoy). Dari sisi lapangan usaha, 
sektor transportasi dan pergudangan tumbuh signifikan 
sebesar 13,96% (yoy). Pada sisi pengeluaran, pertumbuhan 
tertinggi dialami oleh konsumsi lembaga non-profit rumah 
tangga (LNPRT) sebesar 9,83% (yoy).
Pada 2023, produksi minyak sawit yang mencakup CPO dan 
PKO tercatat mencapai 54,84 juta ton atau tumbuh sekitar 
7% dibandingkan produksi pada 2022 yaitu sebanyak 51,24 
juta ton. Kenaikan ini juga disebabkan adanya tambahan 
tanaman menghasilkan (TM) yang ditanam pada 2017-2020. 
GAPKI mencatat ekspor minyak sawit pada 2023 mencapai 
32,21 juta ton atau menurun sebesar 2,8% dibandingkan 
tahun 2022 yang tercatat sebesar 33,15 juta ton. Kegiatan 
ekspor produk sawit Indonesia mengalami peningkatan 
ke beberapa negara importir seperti China, India, Afrika, 
Amerika Serikat, dan Bangladesh, namun menurun ke Uni 
Eropa dan Pakistan.
Pada 2023, konsumsi minyak sawit domestik mengalami 
kenaikan 8,9% (year-on-year/yoy) dari 21,24 juta ton pada 
2022 menjadi 23,13 juta ton pada 2023. Penerapan kebijakan 
mandatori biodiesel 35% atau B35 yang secara efektif 
dimulai pada Juli 2022 diakui telah meningkatkan konsumsi 
minyak sawit sebesar 17,68%, yakni dari 9,04 juta ton pada 
2022 menjadi 10,65 juta ton pada 2023. Peningkatan ini 
tentunya berimbas pada volume ekspor.
Indonesia’s economy resiliently grew by 5.05% in 2023, 
slightly slower than the 5.31% recorded in 2022. The 
growth was mostly due to higher household consumption 
expenditure, which increased by 4.82% (yoy), and Gross 
Fixed Capital Formation (PMTB), which reached 4.40% 
(yoy). The transportation and warehousing sector also 
grew significantly, by 13.96% (yoy). Among the expenditure 
components, the highest growth was seen in consumption 
by household non-profit institutions (LNPRT), which 
increased by 9.83% (yoy).
In 2023, palm oil production (CPO and PKO) reached 
54.84 million tons, which was about 7% higher than the 
production in 2022, 51.24 million tons. This increase was 
due to additional productive crops (TM) planted between 
2017 and 2020. However, palm oil exports were recorded 
at 32.21 million tonnes, a decrease of 2.8% compared to 
33.15 million tonnes in 2022. Although palm oil exports 
have increased to countries such as China, India, Africa, 
the United States, and Bangladesh, they have decreased 
to the European Union and Pakistan.
In 2023, domestic palm oil consumption is expected to 
increase by 8.9% (yoy), from 21.24 million tonnes in 2022 
to 23.13 million tonnes in 2023. This increase is due to the 
effective implementation of the mandatory 35% biodiesel 
or B35 policy, which has been started in July 2022. It has 
increased palm oil consumption by 17.68%, from 9.04 
million tonnes in 2022 to 10.65 million tonnes in 2023. This 
increase in domestic consumption significantly impacted 
the export volume.

Tinjauan Operasi Per Segmen Usaha
Operational Review by Business Segments
As of December 31, 2023, the Company’s only business 
segment is palm oil plantations, which produce Fresh Fruit 
Bunches (FFB), Crude Palm Oil (CPO), and Palm Kernel 
(PK).
Production Flow
The Company and its subsidiaries follow all necessary 
regulations and apply for complete permits before 
cultivating any land. The seeding process is carried out in 
four stages, which are: 
1.	 Acquiring Plant Seedlings/Seeds;
2.	 Pre-Nursery;
3.	 Main Nursery;
4.	 Planting on Site.
Once the seeding process is complete, the land is prepared 
for planting, which includes designing the block or layout, 
clearing the land, building roads, bridges, and water 
channels, and finally planting the palm oil. During the 
maintenance and fertilization process, the Company carries 
out soil and leaf health testing, fertilization, weed spraying, 
and pest control to ensure healthy growth and production.

Solvency
Liquidity
The level of liquidity reflects the ability of the Company and 
its Subsidiary to meet current liabilities using their current 
assets. The level of liquidity is measured by the current ratio, 
which is the ratio of current assets to current liabilities at 
a particular time and represents an indicator of the ability 
of the Company and its Subsidiaries to meet all current 
liabilities using their current assets. 
Solvency
Solvency is the ability of the Company and its Subsidiaries 
to meet all liabilities by using all assets or equity. The 
Solvency Ratio can be calculated using 2 (two) approaches, 
as follows:
1.	 Total liabilities divided by total equity (Equity Solvency);
2.	 Total liabilities divided by total assets (Asset Solvency).
Return-on-Assets 
Return-on-Assets is the ability of the Company and its 
Subsidiaries to generate profit for the current period/year 
from assets owned, which can be calculated from the 
comparison between the current period/year’s profit and 
total assets. 
Return-on-Equity
Return-on-equity is the ability of the Company and its 
Subsidiaries to generate profits for the current period/
year from their equity, which can be calculated from the 
comparison between the current period/year’s profit and 
total equity.

tambahan kargo kepada pelanggan lain.
Aspek Pemasaran
Marketing Aspect
The Company sells CPO products to palm oil management 
companies, which are then processed into other products. 
In marketing its products, subsidiaries sell directly without 
involving third parties.
The Company markets its products to customer 
representatives in Jakarta and sends products to Gresik 
and Marunda. The delivery provisions carried out by the 
Company are:
1.	 Free-on-Board (FOB), where delivery of goods is carried 
out on board the buyer’s ship for CPO commodities.
2.	 Franco, where the goods are delivered by the seller to the 
buyer’s warehouse using a truck for PK commodities.
The Company sells its products through two systems:
1.	 Long-term: The process involves calculating cargo per 
2,000 tons for the next month, and then submitting the 
calculation data to the buyer.
2.	 On-the-spot: This refers to a situation where there is 
additional cargo beyond what has been determined by 
the long-term system. In such cases, the Company will 
offer additional cargo to other customers.

to the provisions contained in the Company’s Articles of

Company records a positive net profit.
The Company’s Articles of Association allow the distribution 
of interim dividends provided that such distribution does not 
cause the Company’s net worth to be less than the issued 
and paid-up capital plus statutory reserves. The distribution

with the Company’s activities.

Amendment of PSAK 1: Presentation of Financial

This amendment provides guidance to help entities apply 
materiality judgments to accounting policy disclosures. 
The amendments aim to help entities provide accounting 
policy disclosures that are more useful by replacing the 
requirement for entities to disclose their ‘significant’ 
accounting policies with a requirement to disclose their 
‘material’ accounting policies and adding guidance on how 
entities apply the concept of materiality in making decisions 
about accounting policy disclosures.

disclosures of accounting policies, but not on the 
measurement, recognition or presentation of any items in 
the Group’s financial statements.
Amendment of PSAK 16: Fixed Assets – Proceeds 
before Intended Use
The amendments prohibit entities from deducting from the 
cost of an item of fixed assets, any proceeds from selling 
items produced while bringing that asset to the location 
and condition necessary for it to be capable of operating 
in the manner intended by management. Instead, an entity 
recognizes the proceeds from selling such items, and the 
costs of producing those items, in the profit or loss.
The Group applies the amendments retrospectively only to 
items of fixed assets made available for use on or after the 
beginning of the earliest period presented when the entity 
first applies the amendment.
The amendments had no impact on the consolidated 
financial statements of the Group as there were no sales of 
such items produced by fixed assets made available for use 
on or after the beginning of the earliest period presented.

Penerapan praktik GCG yang kuat merupakan kunci 
Perseroan untuk mempertahankan kelangsungan usaha 
dan mendorong pertumbuhan secara jangka panjang. 
Perseroan senantiasa melaksanakan aktivitas bisnisnya 
dengan hati-hati serta berfokus pada pemenuhan 
kepentingan Perseroan. Penerapan ini juga diperkuat 
dengan komitmen Perseroan dalam mematuhi seluruh 
peraturan yang berlaku.
Tujuan Praktik GCG
Tujuan penerapan GCG Perseroan sebagai berikut:
1.	 Mengatur dan mengendalikan hubungan antar 
pemangku kepentingan.
2.	 Menjalankan usaha yang transparan, patuh pada 
peraturan, dan beretika bisnis yang baik.
3.	 Peningkatan manajemen risiko.
4.	 Peningkatan daya saing dan kemampuan Perseroan 
dalam menghadapi perubahan industri yang 
sangat dinamis.
5.	 Mencegah terjadinya penyimpangan dalam 
pengelolaan perusahaan.
The Company’s breakthrough in becoming a public company 
serves as a manifestation of the Company’s commitment to 
sustainable growth. The Company understands that it can 
take on larger and broader responsibilities at the moment. 
Therefore, the Company is committed to implementing 
its corporate governance based on the Good Corporate 
Governance (GCG) principles. 
A solid implementation of the GCG principles is the 
Company’s key to maintaining business continuity 
and achieving long-term growth. The Company always 
prudently conducts its business activities while focusing 
on achieving the Company’s interests. In addition, the GCG 
implementation is supported by the Company’s commitment 
to complying with the applicable laws and regulations.
Objectives of GCG Practice
The following are the objectives of implementing the 
Company’s GCG:
1.	 Regulate and control the relationship 
between stakeholders.
2.	 Conducting a transparent business that complies with 
regulations and adheres to good business ethics.
3.	 Improving risk management.
4.	 Increasing the Company’s competitiveness and 
adaptability to extremely dynamic industrial changes.
5.	 Prevent the occurrence of irregularities in the 
Company’s management.
