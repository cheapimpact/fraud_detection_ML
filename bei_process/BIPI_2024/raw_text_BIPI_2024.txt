TINJAUAN PEREKONOMIAN
Pertumbuhan ekonomi global mengalami perlambatan 
pada tahun 2023. International Monetary Fund (IMF) 
mencatat per Januari 2024, pertumbuhan ekonomi global 
diperkirakan hanya mencapai 3,1%, atau menurun sebesar 
0,4% dibandingkan pertumbuhan yang terjadi pada tahun 
2022 yang mencapai 3,5%. Menurunnya pertumbuhan 
ekonomi global ini disebabkan oleh laju pemulihan 
ekonomi pasca pandemi COVID-19 yang mulai melemah 
ditambah berkurangnya dukungan fiskal, dan faktor-
faktor lainnya seperti fragmentasi geoekonomi dan cuaca 
ekstrem yang terjadi di berbagai belahan dunia.
Sejalan dengan lambatnya pertumbuhan ekonomi global 
yang terjadi, inflasi global juga mengalami penurunan 
yang cukup positif. IMF mencatat inflasi global di tahun 
2023 hanya mencapai 6,8%, meskipun masih berada di 
angka yang cukup tinggi, namun mencerminkan adanya 
pemulihan sebesar 1,9% jika dibandingkan dengan tahun 
2022 yang mana jumlah inflasi global mencapai 8,7%.
ECONOMIC OVERVIEW
The global economic growth slowed down in 2024. The 
International Monetary Fund (IMF) stated that as of January 
2024, global economic growth is expected to reach only 
3.1%, a decrease of 0.4% from the 3.5% growth in 2022. 
The decline in global economic growth was due to the 
weakening pace of economic recovery after the COVID-19 
pandemic, reduced fiscal support, and other factors such 
as geoeconomic fragmentation and extreme weather 
events in various parts of the world.
In line with the slow global economic growth, global 
inflation has also experienced a significant decline. The 
IMF predicted that global inflation in 2023 would reach 
6.8%, representing a 1.9% improvement from 2022 when 
global inflation reached 8.7%.

also plays a role in this growth.
MINING INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
Positive national economic growth is closely linked to the 
mining sector, which contributed 7.46% to the growth, 
marking an increase of 0.51% from the previous year’s 
6.95%. This is evident in the rising coal production and 
sales in 2023, with coal remaining Indonesia’s primary 
export commodity.
In 2023, the MODI data management application, owned 
by the Directorate General of Minerals and Coal, reported 
that coal production reached 770.82 tons, showing an 
increase of 85.02 tons compared to 2022 at 685.80 tons. 
Additionally, the amount of coal exports also increased 
by 38.73 tons, totaling 406.67 tons compared to that of 
previous year at 367.94 tons.
