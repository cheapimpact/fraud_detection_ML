Bank notes that the global economy is showing a trend of 
stable, yet still unremarkable, growth. The global economy is 
projected to grow by 2.7% in 2025 and 2026, a figure similar

NATIONAL ECONOMIC ANALYSIS
Indonesia’s Economic Analysis for 2024: Resilient Amidst 
Global Slowdown
Amid the global economic slowdown in 2024, Indonesia’s 
economy has remained resilient, demonstrating strong and 
stable performance—although slightly lower compared to 
2023. In 2024, the Indonesian economy grew by 5.03%, a slight 
deceleration compared to the 5.05% growth in 2023 (c-to-c). 
From the production side, the highest growth was recorded 
in the “Other Services Activities” sector, which expanded by 
9.80%. Meanwhile, from the expenditure side, the highest 
growth came from the “Non-Profit Institutions Serving 
Households” (NPISHs) consumption component, which grew 
by 12.48%. Economic and political stability, supported by 
effective fiscal and monetary policies, successfully enhanced 
investor confidence and encouraged investment realization 
across various sectors.
Key Growth-Driving Sectors
Economic growth in 2024 was driven by several key sectors: (1) 
Manufacturing Industry: As the largest contributor, this sector 
grew by 4.5%, supported by increased manufacturing output 
and exports of processed products. (2) Wholesale and Retail 
Trade: This sector recorded 4.8% growth, benefitting from 
rising domestic consumption and stronger purchasing power. 
(3) Information and Communication: With the highest growth 
at 9.3%, this sector continues to expand due to accelerating 
digitalization and broader internet penetration. (4) Household 
Consumption and Expenditure.
On the demand side, household consumption remained 
the main driver, growing by 4.9%. Investment, measured 
through Gross Fixed Capital Formation (GFCF), also showed 
improvement with a 4.6% increase, reflecting investor 
confidence in Indonesia’s economic prospects.
Meanwhile, the mining sector experienced moderate growth of 
3.95% in the fourth quarter, or 4.90% for the entire year of 2024. 
The moderation in commodity prices was a contributing factor 
to the sector’s slower growth. Regionally, all areas recorded 
positive growth throughout 2024.

oleh peningkatan konsumsi masyarakat dan pertumbuhan

Economic Outlook 2025
Several international institutions, in their latest releases, 
forecast that Indonesia’s economic growth will remain stable 
at around 5%. The World Bank, in its East Asia and the Pacific 
Economic Update released on October 8, 2024, projected 
Indonesia’s economy to grow by 5.0% in 2024 and 5.1% in 2025. 
These projections are slightly higher than previous estimates of 
4.9% for 2024 and 5.0% for 2025.
The World Bank expressed confidence in Indonesia’s economic 
resilience amid challenges faced by countries across the 
East Asia and Pacific region. The main drivers of Indonesia’s 
economic growth moving forward include increased 
household consumption, investment, and government 
spending.
However, the World Bank also cautioned about the declining 
trend in household consumption in East Asia and the Pacific—
including Indonesia—as reflected in the slower growth 
of retail sales and imports compared to pre-pandemic 
levels. Additionally, the World Bank noted that Indonesia’s 
manufacturing exports still contribute relatively little to 
the overall economy, and private investment needs further 
improvement.
Meanwhile, the International Monetary Fund (IMF) in its 
Country Report No. 24/270 published in August 2024, asserted 
that Indonesia’s economy remains robust despite a challenging 
global environment. Inflation is expected to remain under 
control, staying near the midpoint of the government’s target 
range.
Real GDP growth in 2024 is projected at 5.0%, supported 
by strong household consumption and investment growth, 
which offset net export constraints due to external pressures—
including declining commodity prices and weaker growth 
among trade partners.
In its August 7, 2024 report, the IMF emphasized that Indonesia’s 
growth remains strong despite external headwinds. Inflation 
remains low and well-contained, the financial sector is 
resilient, and policy decisions have generally been prudent and 
supportive. The IMF expects real sector exports to slow, while 
imports are likely to recover in line with sustained domestic 
demand. Furthermore, the Organization for Economic 
Cooperation and Development (OECD) in its Economic 
Outlook Interim Report (September 2024) estimated 
Indonesia’s economic growth at 5.1% in 2024 and 5.2% in 2025.

GOLD COMMODITY
The World Gold Council (WGC), in its report titled “Gold 
Demand Trends: Full Year 2024” (published on February 5, 
2025), stated that the gold market in 2024 showed solid growth 
in both demand and supply, with investment and jewelry being 
the main drivers.
In 2024, global gold investment reached its highest level in 
four years, totaling 1,180 tonnes valued at US$90 billion. This 
increase was primarily driven by strong demand for gold 
ETFs, especially in the second half of 2024, fueled by declining 
interest rates, geopolitical uncertainty, and strong gold price 
performance, which attracted investors.
Meanwhile, demand for gold bars and coins remained stable 
at 1,186 tonnes, but investment value surged by 23%, reaching a 
record US$91 billion.
Four Key Points of the Global Gold Performance Report
a.	 Record Gold Prices: The LBMA gold price (PM) set 40 
new all-time highs throughout 2024. The average price 
in the fourth quarter reached a record of US$2,663 per 
ounce, resulting in a full-year average price of US$2,386 
per ounce (surging by 23%).
b.	 Highest Demand Value: The value of gold demand 
reached an unprecedented level. A combination of 
record-setting gold prices and volume resulted in a 
fourth-quarter value of US$111 billion. This pushed the 
2024 annual value to an all-time high of US$382 billion.

Timah (Tin) adalah logam berwarna perak yang lunak, mudah 
dibentuk, dan memiliki daya tahan tinggi terhadap korosi. 
Karakteristik ini menjadikannya salah satu logam penting 
dalam berbagai aplikasi industri, terutama sebagai bahan 
utama dalam produksi solder, serta sebagai pelapis logam lain 
untuk mencegah karat dan degradasi.
Dalam era transisi energi dan teknologi ramah lingkungan, 
peran timah semakin menonjol. Timah digunakan secara 
luas dalam panel surya (fotovoltaik), kendaraan listrik, dan 
perangkat elektronik modern, menjadikannya komoditas 
strategis dalam industri lingkungan dan keberlanjutan.
Secara global, negara-negara utama penghasil timah meliputi

Myanmar. Indonesia sendiri termasuk dalam tiga besar 
produsen timah dunia, berkat cadangan alam yang melimpah 
dan sejarah panjang dalam industri pertimahan.

serta menyediakan transparansi dan likuiditas bagi pelaku 
industri dan investor.

lingkungan dan elektronik, prospek pasar timah diperkirakan 
tetap kuat, menjadikannya komoditas yang penting untuk 
diawasi dalam dinamika ekonomi global.

LEAD COMMODITY
A Strategic Metal for Modern and Sustainable Industries
Tin is a silver-colored metal that is soft, malleable, and highly 
resistant to corrosion. These properties make it a key material 
in various industrial applications, particularly as the main 
component in solder production, and as a coating for other 
metals to prevent rust and degradation.
In the era of energy transition and environmentally friendly 
technologies, tin plays an increasingly important role. It is 
widely used in photovoltaic (solar panel) installations, electric 
vehicles, and modern electronic devices, making it a strategic 
commodity in environmental and sustainable industries.
Globally, the major tin-producing countries include China, 
Malaysia, Indonesia, Peru, Thailand, Bolivia, and Myanmar. 
Indonesia ranks among the top three tin producers in the 
world, supported by abundant natural reserves and a long-
standing history in the tin industry.
In the global market, Tin Futures are traded on the London 
Metal Exchange (LME), with a standard contract size of 5 tons. 
The LME serves as the international benchmark for tin pricing 
and provides transparency and liquidity for industry players 
and investors.
With growing demand from green technologies and electronics, 
the market outlook for tin remains strong, positioning it as a 
commodity to watch in global economic developments.

Likuiditas dan Solvabilitas
Rasio Solvabilitas adalah kemampuan perusahaan untuk 
memenuhi kewajibannya, baik jangka pendek maupun 
jangka panjang. Rasio lancar, yang dihitung dengan membagi 
aset lancar dengan kewajiban lancar selama periode yang 
bersangkutan, adalah rasio yang mengukur kemampuan 
perusahaan untuk memenuhi kewajiban jangka pendek. 
Rasio utang, di sisi lain, adalah rasio yang mengukur 
kemampuan perusahaan untuk memenuhi seluruh kewajiban 
dengan menggunakan seluruh aset atau ekuitasnya. Rasio ini 
dihitung dengan membandingkan total kewajiban dengan 
total aset perusahaan selama periode yang bersangkutan.
Imbal Hasil Ekuitas dan Imbal Hasil Aset
Kemampuan Perseroan menghasilkan laba dari Total Aset 
dan Ekuitas masing-masing dan terhitung selama satu tahun 
dapat diukur masing-masing dengan Rasio Imbal Hasil Ekuitas 
(Return on Equity/ROE) dan Rasio Imbal Hasil Aset (Return on 
Asset/ROA). ROE merupakan hasil perbandingan antara laba 
bersih dengan total modal sendiri Perseroan. Sedangkan, ROA 
merupakan hasil perbandingan antara laba bersih dengan total 
Aset Perseroan.

ATAS STRUKTUR MODAL
Kebijakan Struktur Modal
Perseroan mengelola permodalan dengan tujuan untuk

memaksimalkan keuntungan bagi pemegang saham dan

Liquidity and Solvency
The solvency ratio reflects the Company’s ability to meet 
its obligations, both short-term and long-term. The current 
ratio, which is calculated by dividing current assets by current 
liabilities during the relevant period, measures the Company’s 
ability to meet its short-term obligations.
The debt ratio, on the other hand, measures the Company’s 
ability to meet all of its obligations using its total assets or 
equity. This ratio is calculated by comparing total liabilities to 
total assets during the relevant period.
Return on Equity and Return on Assets
The Company’s ability to generate profit from its Total Assets 
and Equity over the course of one year can be measured by 
the Return on Equity (ROE) and the Return on Assets (ROA) 
ratios, respectively. ROE is the ratio of net income to total 
shareholders’ equity, while ROA is the ratio of net income to 
total assets of the Company.

The mining industry is a highly competitive sector, both 
nationally and globally. Business competition in this industry is 
influenced by various factors such as global commodity prices, 
availability of mineral reserves, operational costs, technology 
used, and the ability to manage the environment and comply 
with regulations.
In Indonesia, mining industry players face competition from 
large-scale companies with strong financial and technological 
capabilities, as well as medium-sized companies that are 
aggressive in seeking exploration opportunities and developing 
new projects. Competition also occurs in terms of concession 
area acquisition, licensing, access to infrastructure, and skilled 
labor.
The Company continues to maintain its competitiveness 
through operational efficiency, implementation of appropriate 
technology, human resource development, and compliance 
with environmental standards and good corporate governance 
(GCG). In addition, a sustainable project development strategy 
and optimal asset utilization are key to maintaining the 
Company’s position amid the dynamics of competition in the 
mining industry.
