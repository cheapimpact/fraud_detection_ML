TINJAUAN MAKROEKONOMI 
MACROECONOMIC OVERVIEW
Kondisi perekonomian global masih dipenuhi oleh 
berbagai ketidakpastian. Meskipun pandemi COVID-19 
berangsur memasuki masa pemulihan, konflik antara 
Rusia dan Ukraina serta serangan Israel terhadap 
Palestina 
telah 
memberikan 
dampak 
terhadap 
pertumbuhan ekonomi. Selain itu, krisis iklim juga 
menjadi salah satu faktor utama yang memperlambat 
pertumbuhan 
ekonomi. 
Menurut 
laporan 
World 
Economic Outlook yang diterbitkan pada Januari 2024, 
IMF memperkirakan bahwa pertumbuhan ekonomi 
global tahun 2023 dan 2024 akan mencapai 3,1% dan 
3,2% secara berturut-turut. Hal ini disebabkan oleh 
membaiknya kinerja ekonomi beberapa negara besar 
sejak akhir 2022 serta perkiraan penurunan inflasi 
secara perlahan pada tahun 2024.
 
Di sisi lain, perekonomian Indonesia pada tahun 2023 
berhasil mengatasi tekanan yang ada dan tumbuh 
sebesar 5,05%, meskipun angka ini sedikit lebih rendah 
dibandingkan dengan pertumbuhan pada tahun 2022 
yang mencapai 5,31%. PDB per kapita Indonesia pada 
tahun 2023 mencapai Rp78,1 juta, yang lebih tinggi 
dibandingkan dengan PDB per kapita pada tahun 2022 
sebesar Rp75,9 juta.
Pada tahun 2023, kinerja sektor properti di Indonesia 
masih 
belum 
menunjukkan 
pertumbuhan 
yang 
signifikan, sebagaimana tercermin dari pertumbuhan 
sektor ekonomi terkait seperti sektor konstruksi dan 
real estate. Faktor-faktor seperti kondisi perekonomian 
domestik dan campur tangan kebijakan pemerintah 
masih mempengaruhi perkembangan properti di 
Indonesia. Karena sektor properti memiliki dampak 
multiplier 
yang 
besar 
terhadap 
perekonomian, 
pemerintah dan otoritas terkait termasuk Bank 
Indonesia berusaha mendorong pertumbuhan sektor 
properti melalui insentif fiskal.
TINJAUAN INDUSTRI PROPERTI 
PROPERTY INDUSTRY OVERVIEW
The global economic conditions remain fraught 
with various uncertainties. Although the COVID-19 
pandemic is gradually entering a recovery phase, 
conflicts such as the Russia-Ukraine conflict and 
Israel’s attacks on Palestine have impacted economic 
growth. Additionally, the climate crisis is also a key factor 
hindering economic growth. According to the World 
Economic Outlook report published in January 2024, 
the IMF estimates that global economic growth in 2023 
and 2024 will reach 3.1% and 3.2% respectively. This 
is attributed to the improved economic performance 
of some major economies since late 2022 and the 
projected gradual decrease in inflation in 2024.
 
On the other hand, Indonesia’s economy in 2023 
managed to withstand existing pressures and grew by 
5.05%, although this figure is slightly lower than the 
growth rate in 2022, which reached 5.31%. Indonesia’s 
GDP per capita in 2023 reached Rp78.1 million, higher 
than the GDP per capita in 2022, which was Rp75.9 
million.
In 2023, the performance of the property sector in 
Indonesia has yet to demonstrate significant growth, 
as reflected in the growth of related sectors such 
as construction and real estate. Factors such as 
domestic 
economic 
conditions 
and 
government 
policy interventions continue to influence property 
development in Indonesia. Given the substantial 
multiplier effect of the property sector on the economy, 
the government and relevant authorities, including 
Bank Indonesia, are striving to stimulate sector growth 
through fiscal incentives.

Asset 
The Company recorded total current assets amounting

decrease in investment property. 
Overall, as of December 31, 2023, the Company 
recorded total assets of Rp979,580.35 million, 
experiencing a decrease of Rp56,706.49 million or 
5.47% compared to Rp1,036,286.84 million in 2022.

decrease in cash and cash equivalents and investment 
property.
Liabilities  
The total short-term liabilities of the Company 
amounted to Rp46,373.95 million as of December 
31, 2023, experiencing an increase of Rp7,825.48 
million or 20.30% compared to Rp38,548.47 million in 
2022. The increase in short-term liabilities is primarily 
caused by increase in trade payables and  increase  in 
unearned income.
The total long-term liabilities decreased by Rp88,205.55 
million or 38.47% from Rp229,307.85 million in 2022 to 
Rp141,102.30 million in 2023. The decrease in long-
term liabilities is mainly attributed to decrease in bank 
loan and decrease in other payable.
Overall, the Company recorded total liabilities 
of Rp187,476.25 million in 2023, experiencing a 
decrease of Rp80,380.06 million or 30% compared to 
Rp267,856.32 million in 2022. The decrease in total 
liabilities in 2023 is caused by decrease in the amount 
of long-term liabilities.
