Perekonomian Global
Peningkatan inflasi, tingginya suku bunga acuan dan 
penyaluran kredit yang terbatas merupakan fenomena 
ekonomi dunia sepanjang tahun 2023. Inflasi yang tinggi

premi jangka panjang.
Dampak lanjutannya adalah terjadi pembalikan arus 
modal dari negara-negara berkembang menuju negara 
maju dan aset yang lebih likuid. Akibatnya, dolar AS 
menguat terhadap mata uang banyak negara, termasuk 
rupiah. Hal ini semakin meningkatkan ketidakpastian di 
pasar keuangan global.
Global Economy
Rising inflation, high benchmark interest rates and limited 
lending are global economic phenomena throughout 
2023. High inflation is attributable to rising commodity 
and energy prices due to the escalation of geopolitical 
tensions such as the Russia and Ukraine war as well as 
in the Middle East region. 
To control the inflation rate, central banks in many 
countries maintain high benchmark interest rates as 
done by the Central Bank of the United States (US), 
The Federal Reserve. The high benchmark interest rate 
was followed by an increase in US bond yields to meet 
government debt financing needs and an increase in 
long-term premiums.
A further impact is a reversal of capital flows from 
developing countries towards developed countries and 
more liquid assets. As a result, the US dollar strengthened 
against the currencies of many countries, including the 
rupiah. This has further increased uncertainty in global 
financial markets.

Economic Overview
Kenaikan suku bunga telah melemahkan daya beli 
konsumen di AS, Eropa dan Asia. Akibatnya, arus 
perdagangan barang dunia mengalami perlambatan dan 
meningkatkan fragmentasi ekonomi global. Organisasi 
Perdagangan Dunia (WTO) telah menurunkan separuh 
perkiraan pertumbuhan perdagangan global pada tahun 
2023 menjadi 0,8% dari sebelumnya sebesar 1,7%.
Perlambatan perdagangan terjadi pada berbagai jenis 
barang, khususnya besi dan baja, peralatan kantor dan 
telekomunikasi, tekstil dan pakaian. Namun demikian, 
untuk permintaan mobil tetap tinggi sebagai kompensasi 
atas pembatasan pengiriman selama masa pandemi. 
Penurunan juga terjadi pada perdagangan jasa komersial. 
Peningkatan risiko ketidakpastian ekonomi dan pasar 
keuangan yang disebabkan beragam faktor tersebut 
menjadikan ekonomi global melambat. World Bank 
dalam laporan berjudul Global Economic Prospects yang 
dirilis Juni 2023, memperkirakan ekonomi dunia hanya 
akan tumbuh 2,1%, lebih rendah dari pencapaian tahun 
sebelumnya sebesar 3,1%.
Perekonomian Indonesia
Dalam perekonomian yang semakin terkoneksi, situasi 
global berdampak terhadap perekonomian Indonesia. 
Mengacu laporan Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), ekonomi 
Indonesia tumbuh sebesar 5,05% secara tahunan di 
tahun 2023, dengan nominal berdasarkan harga yang 
berlaku sebesar Rp20.892,4 triliun.  
Dari sisi pengeluaran, pertumbuhan tertinggi berasal dari 
konsumsi swasta yang diikuti konsumsi dan investasi. 
Sementara secara distribusi, konsumsi rumah tangga 
masih menjadi kontributor terbesar dalam pertumbuhan 
ekonomi nasional. Investasi yang tumbuh sejalan 
dengan penyelesaian Program Strategis Nasional. 
Namun untuk sektor perdagangan terdampak dari 
pelemahan global. Ekspor tumbuh tipis sebesar 1,32% 
dan impor terkontraksi sebesar 1,65% secara tahunan di 
tahun 2023. Terbatasnya permintaan barang dari negara 
Rising interest rates have weakened the purchasing 
power of consumers in the U.S., Europe and Asia. As a 
result, world trade flows of goods have slowed down and 
increased the fragmentation of the global economy. The 
World Trade Organization (WTO) has halved its forecast 
for global trade growth in 2023 to 0.8% from 1.7%.
The trade slowdown occurred in various types of 
goods, especially iron and steel, office equipment and 
telecommunications, textiles and clothing. Nevertheless, 
demand for cars remains high as compensation for 
shipping restrictions during the pandemic. The decline 
also occurred in commercial trade in services.  
The increased risk of economic and financial market 
uncertainty caused by various factors has caused the 
global economy to weaken. The World Bank in its report 
Global Economic Prospects released in June 2023, 
estimates that the world economy will only grow 2.1%, 
lower than the previous year’s achievement of 3.1%.
Indonesian Economy
In an increasingly connected economy, the global 
situation has an impact on the Indonesian economy. 
Referring to the Central Statistics Agency (BPS) report, 
Indonesia’s economy grew by 5.05% on an annual basis 
in 2023 with a nominal value based on current prices of 
Rp20,892.4 trillion. 
In terms of expenditure, the highest growth derived 
from private consumption, followed by consumption 
and investment. Meanwhile, in terms of distribution, 
household consumption remains the largest contributor 
to national economic growth. Investment that grows 
in line with the completion of the National Strategic 
Program.

downturn. Exports grew modestly by 1.32% and imports

Perekonomian global masih menghadapi sejumlah

Economic Projections for 2024
In 2024, the global economy continues to confront a 
multitude of challenges. The real estate crisis in China 
is one of the factors that could trigger a global economic 
slowdown. The continued escalation of geopolitical 
tensions and climate change has the potential to increase 
energy and commodity prices. 
Rising commodity prices keep inflation high and further 
burden heavily indebted countries. This will widen 
geoeconomics fragmentation between regions and 
complicate accelerating global economic growth.
Align with high inflation, the benchmark interest rate is 
also predicted to remain high, resulting in tighter credit. 
As a result, global investment and trade will weaken. 
Based on these factors, the World Bank predicts global 
economic growth at the level of 2.4%. 
The increasing risk of global economic and financial 
market uncertainty in 2024 will have an impact on 
the Indonesian economy. The goods export sector is 
expected to experience pressure in line with moderation 
in prices of mainstay commodities such as coal and 
CPO as well as weakening demand from major export 
destination countries.
 Consumption and investment will remain the main pillars 
of national economic growth. In the 2024 State Budget, 
the government targets economic growth of 5.2% and 
inflation of 2.8% in 2024. The rupiah exchange rate in 
macro assumptions is set at Rp15,000 per US dollar.

2.	 The appointment of a Public Accountant (AP) and/or Public 
Accounting Firm (KAP) who will provide audit services on 
annual historical financial information is decided by the 
general meeting of shareholders convened by the Party taking 
into account the proposal of the board of commissioners, 
supervisory board, or parties who perform supervisory 
functions as carried out by the board of commissioners.
3.	 Proposals for the appointment of AP and/or KAP submitted 
by the board of commissioners, supervisory board, or parties 
performing supervisory functions as carried out by the board

Committee shall consider:
a)	 independence of AP, KAP, and KAP insiders;
b)	 scope of audit;
c)	 audit service fees;
d)	 expertise and experience of AP, KAP, and audit team from

e)	 methodologies, techniques, and audit tools used by KAP;
f)	 the benefits of a new perspective that will be obtained 
through the replacement of AP, KAP, and audit teams from 
KAP;
g)	 potential risks for the use of audit services by the same 
public accountant consecutively for a long period of time; 
and
h)	 the results of an evaluation of the implementation of audit 
services on annual historical financial information by AP 
and KAP in the previous period, if any.
4.	 The Audit Committee evaluates the implementation of audit 
services on annual historical financial information by AP and/
or KAP, which at least includes:
a)	 conformity of audit implementation by AP and/or KAP with 
applicable audit standards;
b)	 adequacy of time for field work;
c)	 review of the scope of services rendered and the adequacy
