Tinjauan Makro Ekonomi
Macro Economic Overview
Berbagai risiko ketidakpastian ekonomi global masih 
membayangi situasi perekonomian tahun 2024. Tensi 
geopolitik global terpantau meningkat sejalan dengan 
tingginya dinamika politik Amerika Serikat (AS) menjelang 
pemilihan presiden pada November 2024, serta potensi 
instabilitas Timur Tengah dan Rusia - Ukraina. Harga 
komoditas juga menghadapi tekanan sejalan dengan 
turunnya permintaan global. Selain itu, meskipun inflasi 
nasional tercatat menurun, inflasi global masih di level yang 
tinggi. Tekanan inflasi di AS, diperkirakan semakin mendekati 
sasaran inflasi jangka menengah sebesar 2% di tengah 
melambatnya pertumbuhan ekonomi dan meningkatnya 
angka pengangguran. Perkembangan ini mendorong prospek 
penurunan Federal Funds Rate (FFR) yang lebih cepat dan 
lebih besar dari perkiraan semula. Meski demikian, ketatnya 
pasar tenaga kerja dan masih tingginya inflasi di beberapa 
negara maju lainnya mendorong berbagai bank sentral untuk 
tetap menjaga tingkat suku bunga ‘higher for-longer’. Kondisi 
ini memengaruhi kebijakan moneter global menggerus arus 
modal keluar dari negara-negara berkembang, termasuk 
Indonesia.
Di dalam negeri, selain perkembangan moneter global, 
kondisi makro ekonomi tahun 2024 juga dipengaruhi iklim 
politik. Periode Pemilihan Umum (Pemilu) diperkirakan 
memiliki dampak pada pertumbuhan dan berbagai indikator 
makro ekonomi di tahun ini serta tahun mendatang. Indonesia 
Various global economic uncertainties continue to weigh 
on the outlook for 2024. Geopolitical tensions are rising, 
driven by heightened political dynamics in the United States 
ahead of the November 2024 presidential election, as well 
as potential instability in the Middle East and the ongoing 
Russia-Ukraine conflict. Commodity prices are also under 
pressure due to weakening global demand. While Indonesia’s 
domestic inflation has shown signs of easing, global inflation 
remains elevated. In the United States, inflation is gradually 
approaching the medium-term target of 2%, amid a slowdown 
in economic growth and rising unemployment. These 
developments are fueling expectations of a faster and deeper 
cut to the Federal Funds Rate (FFR) than initially projected. 
Nevertheless, tight labor markets and persistent inflation in 
several other advanced economies have led central banks to 
maintain a ‘higher-for-longer’ interest rate stance. This global 
monetary environment has triggered capital outflows from 
emerging markets, including Indonesia.
Domestically, in addition to global monetary developments, 
Indonesia’s macroeconomic conditions in 2024 are also 
shaped by the political climate. The General Election period 
is expected to influence economic growth and various 
macroeconomic indicators both this year and into the next.

This condition has impacted the multifinance industry, 
with overall financing receivables of financing companies 
experiencing slower growth. According to the Financial 
Services Authority (OJK), financing company receivables 
reached Rp503.43 trillion in December 2024, growing by

The growth of financing receivables in 2024 was primarily 
driven by investment financing and working capital, which 
grew by 10.47% (yoy) and 9.56% (yoy), respectively. On the

growth.
The risk profile of financing companies remains relatively well-
maintained, with a net Non-Performing Financing (NPF) ratio 
of 0.75%, while the gross NPF reached 2.70% in December 
2024. The gearing ratio of financing companies was recorded 
at 2.31 times in December 2024, which remains well below 
the regulator’s maximum limit of 10 times. The total financing 
disbursements for 2024 was Rp530.47 trillion, reflecting 
a growth of 6.82% compared to 2023. This financing 
disbursement growth was also lower than the growth in 2023, 
which reached 12.61%.

receivables is considered crucial for the Company’s business 
continuity.
The Company is also committed to maintaining strong 
relationships with customers, creditors, and business 
partners. In order to enhance customer satisfaction, the 
Company strives to accelerate financing disbursement by 
strengthening infrastructure, advancing technology, and 
improving the capacity of Human Resources (HR). Another 
strategy implemented is enhancing retention rates and 
providing appreciation programs for dealers and partners.

memastikan arah kebijakan baru.

FINANCIAL LEASE

segment was recorded at Rp533.27 billion, reflecting an 
32.77% decrease compared to the Rp793.15 billion recorded 
in 2023. The mining sector remains the largest contributor to 
the finance lease portfolio, which was 38.54% in 2024, slightly 
down from 42.89% in 2023. The service sector follows at 
29.02%, an increase from 26.91% in the previous year.
The decline in new financing disbursement was influenced by 
the correction in coal commodity prices, which weakened the 
performance of mining companies during the first quarter of 
2024. Additionally, the 2024 Mining Work Plan and Budget 
(RKAB) was mostly approved in May 2024, delaying production 
in the mining sector. This situation was further complicated 
by the political climate, where the election results led to a 
change in leadership, prompting many business owners to 
adopt a “wait-and-see” approach to assess the direction of 
new policies.

peningkatan

beban keuangan sebesar 33,92%, dimana beban ini 
merupakan komponen terbesar, dengan porsi 47,54% dari 
jumlah keseluruhan beban. Komponen terbesar lainnya dari

Finance Lease Income
The Company generated Rp121.24 billion in income from the 
finance lease segment in 2024, marking a decrease of 16.13%, 
or Rp23.31 billion, from Rp144.56 billion in 2023. This income 
contributed 13.86% of the Company’s total income in 2024.
Consumer Financing Income
Consumer financing income increased by 33.79%, or Rp154.80 
billion, reaching Rp612.90 billion in 2024, compared to 
Rp458.10 billion in 2023. The growth in consumer financing 
income is also aligned with the increase in consumer financing 
receivables, which grew by Rp1.07 trillion (net after allowance 
for impairment losses), from Rp4.22 trillion in 2023 to Rp5.29 
trillion in 2024. The contribution of consumer financing 
income to the Company’s total income reached 70.05%.
Other Income
Other income consists of three main components: factoring 
income, interest income and penalty, and other income. 
Interest income and penalty represents interest income from 
time deposits, current accounts and penalties for late payment 
of finance receivables. Other income includes administration 
income, recoveries from receivables previously written-off, 
and gains on sale of fixed assets.
In 2024, other income increased by 14.35%, or Rp17.67 
billion, reaching Rp140.79 billion, compared to Rp123.12 
billion in 2023. This growth was primarily driven by an 
increase in administration income, which amounted to 
Rp78.60 billion, an increase of 36.21%, or Rp20.89 billion, 
compared to Rp57.71 billion in 2023. This achievement aligns 
with the growth in new financing disbursements, particularly 
within the consumer financing segment. Meanwhile, factoring 
income also increased by Rp4.60 billion or 178.37%, from 
Rp2.58 billion in 2023 to Rp7.18 billion in 2024.
TOTAL EXPENSES
The Company’s total expenses in 2024 amounted to 
Rp791.70 billion, an increase of 33.36%, or Rp198.06 billion, 
from Rp593.64 billion in 2023. The largest portion of this 
increase was due to a rise in financing costs, which increased 
by 33.92% and comprised 47.54% of total expenses. 
Other major expense components included general and 
administration expenses, and provision for impairment losses, 
which accounted for 31.87% and 17.46% of total expenses, 
respectively.

miliar dari posisi tahun 2023 sebesar Rp1,55 triliun menjadi

POLICY ON CAPITAL STRUCTURE
The Company manages its capital in accordance with OJK 
Regulation No. 47/POJK.05/2020, issued on November

Regulation for Financing Companies and OJK Regulation No. 
35/POJK.05/2018, effective December 27, 2018 concerning 
the Conduct of Financing Company Business. These regulation 
stipulate that financing companies maintain a capital ratio of 
at least 10%. This ratio represents the proportion of adjusted 
capital to adjusted assets. In 2024 and 2023, the Company’s 
capital ratio was recorded at 28.67% and 34.91%, respectively.
OJK 
Regulation 
No.

also

provisions such as:
•	
A minimum paid-up capital requirement of Rp100 billion,
•	
A minimum equity of 50% of paid-up capital, and
•	
A maximum debt to equity ratio, including subordinated 
loans minus participation, of 10 times, applicable to both 
domestic and foreign loans.
