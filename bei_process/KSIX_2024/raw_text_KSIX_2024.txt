Tinjauan Ekonomi Global dan Nasional
Global and National Economic Overview
Sepanjang tahun 2024, ekonomi global tetap stabil meskipun 
pertumbuhannya 
cenderung 
melambat. 
International 
Monetary 
Fund 
(IMF) 
dalam 
laporan 
World 
Economic 
Outlook yang dirilis pada Januari 2025 memproyeksikan 
pertumbuhan ekonomi global sebesar 3,2%, sedikit lebih 
rendah dibandingkan dengan 3,3% pada tahun 2023. Angka 
ini mencerminkan pemulihan yang masih berlangsung, tetapi 
dengan tingkat pertumbuhan yang moderat.
Perekonomian 
di 
negara-negara 
maju 
menunjukkan 
pertumbuhan yang stagnan, dengan tingkat ekspansi 1,8% 
pada tahun 2024, sementara negara-negara berkembang 
diperkirakan mengalami pertumbuhan 4,2%. Pertumbuhan 
di Asia berkembang, yang merupakan salah satu pendorong 
utama ekonomi global, diproyeksikan mencapai 5,3%, dengan 
India dan Tiongkok sebagai kontributor terbesar. Namun, 
Tiongkok menghadapi tantangan akibat lemahnya sektor 
properti dan rendahnya kepercayaan konsumen, meskipun 
ekspor yang lebih baik dari perkiraan membantu menjaga 
stabilitas pertumbuhan.
Throughout 2024, the global economy remains stable although 
growth is likely to slow down. The International Monetary Fund 
(IMF) in its World Economic Outlook report released in January 
2025 projected global economic growth of 3.2%, slightly lower 
than the 3.3% in 2023. This figure reflects the ongoing recovery, 
but with a moderate growth rate.
Economies in advanced economies show stagnant growth, with 
an expansion rate of 1.8% in 2024, while emerging economies are 
expected to experience growth of 4.2%. Growth in emerging Asia, 
which is one of the main drivers of the global economy, is projected 
to reach 5.3%, with India and China as the largest contributors. 
However, China faces challenges from a weak property sector and 
low consumer confidence, although better-than-expected exports 
helped keep growth stable.

tahun sebelumnya, tekanan harga masih menjadi tantangan 
bagi banyak negara. IMF memperkirakan inflasi global akan 
turun dari 6,7% pada 2023 menjadi 5,8% pada 2024, dengan 
tingkat inflasi di negara maju diproyeksikan sebesar 2,6% 
dan di negara berkembang sebesar 7,9%. Seiring dengan 
perlambatan ekonomi dan kebijakan moneter yang lebih 
ketat, beberapa negara menghadapi risiko meningkatnya 
volatilitas pasar keuangan dan ketidakpastian geopolitik yang 
dapat berdampak pada investasi global.
Dalam menghadapi ketidakpastian ekonomi global, Indonesia 
tetap

5,0% pada tahun 2024 dan meningkat menjadi 5,1% pada 
tahun 2025. Konsumsi rumah tangga menjadi pendorong 
utama, didukung oleh inflasi yang terkendali serta ekspansi 
sektor manufaktur dan investasi. Pemerintah juga terus 
memperkuat

peningkatan kemudahan berbisnis diharapkan dapat menarik 
lebih banyak investasi dan mendukung pertumbuhan ekonomi 
jangka panjang.
Sektor konstruksi dan properti tetap menjadi bagian penting 
dalam perekonomian nasional. Pembangunan infrastruktur 
yang berkelanjutan, termasuk pengembangan konektivitas 
transportasi, kawasan industri, serta proyek-proyek strategis 
lainnya, memberikan dampak positif terhadap sektor ini. 
Permintaan terhadap kawasan yang terintegrasi dengan 
fasilitas modern terus meningkat, mencerminkan pergeseran 
preferensi pasar yang semakin mengutamakan efisiensi 
dan aksesibilitas. Selain itu, kebijakan pemerintah dalam 
menjaga stabilitas suku bunga dan memberikan insentif bagi 
pengembang turut mendorong pertumbuhan sektor properti 
di tengah dinamika pasar yang terus berkembang.
Dengan prospek ekonomi yang tetap kondusif, sektor properti 
diharapkan 
terus 
berkontribusi 
terhadap 
pertumbuhan 
nasional, baik melalui investasi baru maupun ekspansi proyek 
yang sudah berjalan. Di tengah tantangan eksternal, kebijakan 
ekonomi yang proaktif dan penguatan sektor riil menjadi 
faktor utama dalam menjaga momentum pertumbuhan yang 
berkelanjutan dan inklusif.
Although global inflation started to decline compared to the 
previous year, price pressures are still a challenge for many 
countries. The IMF expects global inflation to fall from 6.7% in 
2023 to 5.8% in 2024, with inflation rates in developed countries 
projected at 2.6% and in developing countries at 7.9%. Along with 
the economic slowdown and tighter monetary policies, some 
countries face the risk of increased financial market volatility and 
geopolitical uncertainty that could impact global investment.
In the face of global economic uncertainty, Indonesia continues 
to show resilience with steady growth. Based on the Ministry of 
Finance’s projection, national economic growth is expected to 
reach 5.0% in 2024 and increase to 5.1% in 2025. Household 
consumption is the main driver, supported by manageable 
inflation and the expansion of the manufacturing and investment 
sectors. The government also continues to strengthen economic 
transformation through downstream industries, electric vehicle 
(EV) ecosystem development, and accelerated digitalization to 
improve national competitiveness. In addition, structural reforms 
focusing on improving the ease of doing business are expected to 
attract more investment and support long-term economic growth.
The construction and property sectors remain an important part 
of the national economy. Continued infrastructure development, 
including the development of transportation connectivity, 
industrial estates, as well as other strategic projects, has had a 
positive impact on the sector. Demand for integrated areas with 
modern facilities continues to increase, reflecting a shift in market 
preference that increasingly prioritizes efficiency and accessibility. 
In addition, the government’s policy of maintaining interest rate 
stability and providing incentives for developers also boosted the 
growth of the property sector amidst evolving market dynamics.
With a favorable economic outlook, the property sector is expected 
to continue contributing to national growth, both through new 
investments and expansion of existing projects. Amidst external 
challenges, proactive economic policies and the strengthening 
of the real sector are key factors in maintaining sustainable and 
inclusive growth momentum.
Tinjauan Industri
Industry Overview
Industri properti di Indonesia pada tahun 2024 terus 
berkembang di tengah dinamika ekonomi global dan 
domestik. Berbagai faktor seperti urbanisasi, pertumbuhan 
kelas menengah, serta meningkatnya kebutuhan akan hunian 
dan ruang komersial mendorong permintaan terhadap sektor 
ini. Selain itu, pemerintah terus mengambil langkah-langkah 
strategis untuk mendukung industri properti melalui insentif 
fiskal, kemudahan regulasi, serta percepatan pembangunan 
infrastruktur. Dengan adanya konektivitas yang lebih baik 
dan perkembangan kawasan perkotaan, sektor properti 
diharapkan tetap menjadi salah satu pendorong pertumbuhan 
investasi di Indonesia.
The property industry in Indonesia in 2024 continues to grow 
amidst global and domestic economic dynamics. Various factors 
such as urbanization, the growth of the middle class, and the 
increasing need for residential and commercial space are driving 
demand for the sector. In addition, the government continues 
to take strategic steps to support the property industry through 
fiscal incentives, ease of regulation, and accelerated infrastructure 
development. With better connectivity and the development of 
urban areas, the property sector is expected to remain one of the 
drivers of investment growth in Indonesia.

Penjelasan Segmen Usaha
Business Segment Explanation
Perseroan mengklasifikasikan kegiatan usahanya ke dalam 2 
(dua) segmen utama, yaitu real estat dan wahana air. Adapun 
penjelasan singkat masing-masing segmen usaha sebagai

Segmen Real Estat
Perseroan merupakan pengembang properti terkemuka di 
Indonesia yang telah membangun berbagai proyek real estat 
bersama entitas anak, mencakup hunian bersubsidi hingga 
perumahan untuk segmen pasar menengah ke atas dan 
ke bawah. 
The Company classifies its business activities into 2 (two) main 
segments such as real estate and waterpark. A brief explanation 
of each business segment is as follows: 
Real Estate Segment 
The Company is a leading property developer in Indonesia that 
has built various real estate projects with its subsidiaries, ranging 
from subsidized housing to residential properties targeting the 
upper, middle, and lower market segments. 
Industri properti di Indonesia pada tahun 2024 mengalami

Secara keseluruhan, pertumbuhan penjualan terkontraksi

untuk memperluas portofolio investasinya. Dengan kebijakan 
yang tepat dan responsif terhadap dinamika pasar, industri

The property industry in Indonesia in 2024 experienced slower 
growth compared to the previous year, in line with various 
economic challenges affecting consumer purchasing power. 
Based on Bank Indonesia’s Survey of Residential Property Prices 
(SHPR), the Residential Property Price Index (IHPR) grew by 1.39% 
(yoy) in the fourth quarter 2024, lower than the 1.46% (yoy) in 
the third quarter 2024. This decline in price growth reflected the 
pressure on the property sector due to high mortgage interest 
rates and global economic uncertainty that impacted consumer 
purchasing power.
Overall, sales growth contracted by 15.09%. In terms of financing, 
consumers still relied on mortgages as the primary source for 
property purchases, accounting for 72.54%, while developers 
depended on internal funds by 74.38% to finance their projects. 
This limited developers’ flexibility in expanding new projects 
and increased consumer dependence on bank credit facilities. 
Additionally, the decline in sales growth also reflected challenges 
in consumer purchasing power and cautiousness in property 
investment decisions.
With the industry continuing to grow but facing structural 
challenges, the Company has the opportunity to capitalize on 
market trends with a more adaptive strategy. The development 
of areas with integrated concepts that offer high added value 
can be an attraction for consumers amidst more selective market 
conditions. In addition, the growing leisure property sector also 
provides opportunities for the Company to expand its investment 
portfolio. With the right policies and responsiveness to market 
dynamics, the property industry still has positive long-term 
prospects in supporting national economic growth.

Real Estate Segment
Perseroan 
menjalankan 
kegiatan 
usaha 
di 
bidang 
pengembangan kawasan perumahan (real estat), yang

pembelian,

Perseroan berfokus pada pengembangan kawasan strategis 
yang didukung oleh akses transportasi yang mudah serta 
fasilitas umum yang lengkap. Dengan pengalaman lebih 
dari empat dekade di industri properti, Perseroan telah 
membangun berbagai proyek perumahan, mulai dari hunian 
bersubsidi hingga hunian untuk segmen pasar menengah.
Adapun beberapa properti yang telah Perseroan bangun, 
antara lain meliputi Grand Nusa Indah, Adhigana @ Grand 
Nusa Indah, dan Permata Nusa Indah Situsari di kawasan 
Cileungsi; Taman Krakatau di kawasan Cilegon serta Vila Bogor 
Indah dan Gaura @ Vila Bogor Indah di kawasan Bogor. Grand 
Nusa Indah merupakan kawasan perumahan dengan total 
5.679 unit hunian, menawarkan berbagai tipe rumah dengan 
kisaran harga antara Rp400 juta hingga Rp1,5 miliar. Adhigana 
@ Grand Nusa Indah dikembangkan dengan 1.359 unit 
rumah, menyediakan hunian dengan harga mulai dari Rp400 
juta hingga Rp1 miliar. Permata Nusa Indah Situsari, dengan 
1.300 unit rumah, menghadirkan pilihan hunian dengan harga 
berkisar antara Rp400 juta hingga Rp700 juta. 
Sementara itu, Taman Krakatau dengan unit sebanyak 6.205, 
dan kisaran harga Rp200 - Rp600 juta. Vila Bogor Indah dengan 
jumlah unit sebanyak 744 rumah dengan kisaran harga Rp1 - 
2 miliar. Sedangkan, Gaura sebanyak 424 unit rumah dengan 
kisaran harga Rp1 - 2 miliar. Seluruh proyek ini dirancang untuk 
menghadirkan hunian yang nyaman, aman, dan strategis, 
dilengkapi dengan fasilitas pendukung seperti keamanan 24 
jam, mini market, serta akses mudah ke transportasi umum 
dan pusat kegiatan masyarakat.
The Company conducts business activities in the field of residential 
area development (real estate), which includes the purchase, sale, 
rental, and management of properties, both owned and leased. 
The properties developed include residential and playgrounds.
The Company focuses on developing strategic areas supported 
by easy transportation access and complete public facilities. With 
more than four decades of experience in the property industry, 
Kentanix has built various housing projects, ranging from 
subsidized housing to housing for the middle market segment.
Several properties developed by the Company include Grand 
Nusa Indah, Adhigana @ Grand Nusa Indah, and Permata Nusa 
Indah Situsari in the Cileungsi area; Taman Krakatau in the 
Cilegon area; as well as Vila Bogor Indah and Gaura @ Vila Bogor 
Indah in the Bogor area. Grand Nusa Indah is a residential area 
comprising a total of 5,679 housing units, offering various types 
of houses with prices ranging from Rp400 million to Rp1.5 billion. 
Adhigana @ Grand Nusa Indah was developed with 1,359 housing 
units, providing homes priced from Rp400 million to Rp1 billion. 
Permata Nusa Indah Situsari, with 1,300 housing units, offers 
residential options priced between Rp400 million and Rp700 
million.
Meanwhile, Taman Krakatau consists of 6,205 units, with prices 
ranging from Rp200–600 million. Vila Bogor Indah comprises 744 
housing units with prices ranging from Rp1–2 billion. Gaura @ Vila 
Bogor Indah consists of 424 housing units, also priced between 
Rp1–2 billion. All of these projects are designed to provide 
comfortable, safe, and strategically located housing, equipped 
with supporting facilities such as 24 hour security, mini markets, 
and easy access to public transportation and community activity 
centers.

Discussion and Analysis is based on the Audited Financial 
Statements of PT Kentanix Supra International Tbk for the years 
ended December 31, 2024 and 2023. The Financial Statements 
have been audited by Teramihardja, Pradhono & Chandra Public 
Accounting Firm, with Tedy Chandra, S.E., Ak., CPA in charge. The 
audit report, published on March 25, 2025, provides a fair opinion 
in all material respects.

accordance with Financial Accounting Standards (SAK) applicable 
in Indonesia, which include Statements of Financial Accounting 
Standards (PSAK) and Interpretations of Financial Accounting 
Standards (ISAK) issued by the Financial Accounting Standards 
Board of the Indonesian Institute of Accountants.
In 2024, the Company’s assets reached Rp1,209 billion, a decrease

at Rp1,249 billion. This decline was primarily due to a decrease 
in cash and cash equivalents as a result of dividend distribution 
amounting to Rp59 billion and advance payments for land 
acquisition for project expansion.

Perseroan mengukur kemampuan dalam melunasi kewajiban

aset lancar dan jumlah liabilitas jangka pendek atau rasio lancar. 
Dengan manajemen kas yang efektif, Perseroan memastikan 
bahwa pembayaran utang jangka pendek, termasuk pinjaman 
yang jatuh tempo dan kewajiban operasional, dapat dilakukan 
tepat waktu. Selain itu, strategi optimalisasi modal kerja 
diterapkan guna menjaga keseimbangan antara penerimaan 
dan pengeluaran kas, sehingga risiko ketidakseimbangan 
likuiditas dapat diminimalkan.
Untuk memenuhi kewajiban jangka panjang, Perseroan 
mengandalkan rasio solvabilitas yang tercermin dari rasio 
liabilitas terhadap ekuitas dan rasio liabilitas terhadap aset. 
Perseroan menerapkan strategi pengelolaan utang yang hati-
hati, termasuk pemantauan terhadap struktur permodalan 
dan proyeksi arus kas jangka panjang. Selain itu, disiplin dalam 
pembayaran pokok pinjaman dan bunga dilakukan untuk 
menjaga hubungan baik dengan lembaga keuangan serta 
memastikan keberlanjutan akses terhadap pendanaan di

tahun 2024 mengalami penurunan yang cukup besar sebesar 
Rp39 miliar, sehingga total rasio lancar Perseroan mengalami 
peningkatan.
The Company measures its ability to meet short-term obligations 
through liquidity ratios, reflected in the total current assets and 
total short-term liabilities or the current ratio. With effective 
cash management, the Company ensures that short-term debt 
payments, including maturing loans and operational obligations, 
are made on time. Additionally, a working capital optimization 
strategy is implemented to maintain a balance between cash 
inflows and outflows, thereby minimizing the risk of liquidity 
imbalance.
To meet long-term obligations, the Company relies on solvency 
ratios, as reflected in the debt-to-equity ratio and the debt-to-
assets ratio. The Company applies a prudent debt management 
strategy, including monitoring capital structure and long-term 
cash flow projections. Additionally, discipline in principal and 
interest payments is maintained to foster good relationships with 
financial institutions and ensure continued access to funding in 
the future.
In 2024, the current ratio was recorded at 41.37 times, reflecting 
an increase compared to the previous year’s ratio of 13.35 
times. This indicates that the Company’s short-term debt in 2024 
experienced a significant decrease of Rp39 billion, resulting in an 
increase in the Company’s current ratio.

Kebijakan pembagian dividen Perseroan didasarkan pada 
Undang-Undang No.40 Tahun 2007 tentang Perseroan 
Terbatas dan Anggaran Dasar Perseroan, ketentuan dalam 
anggaran dasar serta keputusan Rapat Umum Pemegang 
Saham 
(RUPS), 
yang 
mempertimbangkan 
rekomendasi 
dari Direksi. Dalam memberikan rekomendasi, Direksi 
mempertimbangkan 
berbagai 
faktor, 
termasuk 
tingkat 
pendapatan, kondisi operasional dan keuangan, likuiditas, 
rencana belanja modal, peluang akuisisi serta prospek bisnis 
di masa depan. 
Sebagian dari laba bersih Perseroan, sebagaimana ditentukan 
oleh RUPS harus dialokasikan ke dana cadangan. Sampai 
jumlah dana cadangan tersebut mencapai paling sedikit 20% 
dari jumlah modal disetor, maka dana cadangan hanya boleh 
dipergunakan untuk menutup kerugian yang tidak dapat 
dipenuhi oleh cadangan lain, meskipun tidak ada jangka waktu 
yang ditentukan untuk mencapai tingkat dana cadangan ini.
Anggaran Dasar juga memperbolehkan pembagian dividen 
interim dengan syarat tidak mengurangi kekayaan bersih 
di bawah modal ditempatkan dan cadangan wajib. Jika 
terjadi kerugian di akhir tahun, pemegang saham wajib 
mengembalikan dividen interim. Pembagian atas dividen 
interim ditetapkan berdasarkan keputusan Direksi setelah 
mendapatkan persetujuan dari Dewan Komisaris. 
The Company’s dividend distribution policy is based on Law No. 40 
of 2007 on Limited Liability Companies and the Company’s Articles 
of Association, as well as resolutions of the General Meeting of 
Shareholders (GMS), taking into account recommendations from 
the Board of Directors. In providing recommendations, the Board 
of Directors takes into account various factors, including income 
levels, operational and financial conditions, liquidity, capital 
expenditure plans, acquisition opportunities, and future business 
prospects. 
A portion of the Company’s net profit, as determined by the GMS, 
must be allocated to a reserve fund. Until the reserve fund reaches 
at least 20% of the total paid-up capital, it may only be used to 
cover losses that cannot be covered by other reserves, although no 
specific timeframe is set for achieving this reserve level. 
The Articles of Association also allow for the distribution of interim 
dividends, provided that it does not reduce the net assets below 
the issued capital and mandatory reserves. If a loss occurs at the 
end of the year, shareholders are required to return the interim 
dividends. The distribution of interim dividends is determined by 
a resolution of the Board of Directors upon approval from the 
Board of Commissioners.

Changes in Accounting Policy and Impact on the Company
Selama tahun 2024, Perseroan telah menyusun kebijakan 
akuntansi sesuai dengan Standar Akuntansi Keuangan 
(SAK) yang telah diterbitkan oleh Dewan Standar Akuntansi 
Keuangan-Ikatan Akuntan Indonesia (DSAK-IAI) yang berlaku 
efektif untuk tahun buku yang dimulai pada 1 Januari hingga 
31 Desember 2024. Adapun perubahan-perubahan kebijakan 
akuntansi dan dampaknya terhadap Perseroan adalah sebagai 
berikut:
1.	 PSAK 201 (Amendemen), “Penyajian Laporan Keuangan”: 
Klasifikasi Liabilitas sebagai Jangka Pendek atau Jangka 
Panjang.
2.	 PSAK 201 (Amendemen), “Penyajian Laporan Keuangan”: 
Liabilitas Jangka Panjang dengan Kovenan.
3.	 PSAK 207 (Amendemen), “Laporan Arus Kas” dan PSAK 107 
(Amendemen), “Instrumen Keuangan: Pengungkapan”: 
Pengaturan Pembiayaan Pemasok.
4.	 PSAK 116 (Amendemen), “Sewa”: Liabilitas Sewa dalam Jual 
dan Sewa-Balik.
Meskipun perubahan standar tersebut mulai diterapkan, 
implementasinya tidak menimbulkan dampak yang signifikan 
terhadap laporan keuangan Perseroan.
Throughout 2024, the Company has formulated its accounting 
policies in accordance with the Financial Accounting Standards 
(SAK) issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board of the 
Indonesian Institute of Accountants (DSAK-IAI), which became 
effective for the fiscal year beginning January 1 and ending 
December 31, 2024. The changes in accounting policies and their 
impact on the Company are as follows: 
1.	 PSAK 201 (Amendment), “Presentation of Financial Statements”: 
Classification of Liabilities as Current or Non-current.
2.	 PSAK 201 (Amendment), “Presentation of Financial Statements”: 
Non-current Liabilities with Covenants.
3.	 PSAK 207 (Amendment), “Statement of Cash Flows” and PSAK 
107 
(Amendment), 
“Financial 
Instruments: 
Disclosures”: 
Supplier Finance Arrangements.
4.	 PSAK 116 (Amendment), “Leases”: Lease Liabilities in a Sale and 
Leaseback Transaction.
Although these revised standards have been adopted, their 
implementation has not had a significant impact on the 
Company’s financial statements.

Perseroan terus memperkuat penerapan prinsip-prinsip Tata

dalam menjaga integritas dan daya saing usaha. Melalui

The Company continues to strengthen the implementation of Good 
Corporate Governance (GCG) principles as a crucial foundation for 
maintaining business integrity and competitiveness. Through periodic 
evaluations, policy refinements, and a balanced, professional 
governance structure, the Company is committed to creating long-
term value for shareholders and all stakeholders.
