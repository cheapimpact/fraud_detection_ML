ANALISIS PEREKONOMIAN GLOBAL

GLOBAL ECONOMIC ANALYSIS
Throughout 2024, the global economy faced significant 
challenges that led to a slowdown in growth across various 
regions. The World Bank reported that global economic growth 
is projected to slow for the third consecutive year, from 2.6% 
in the previous year to 2.4% in 2024, nearly three-quarters of 
a percentage point below the average of the 2010s decade. 
Developing countries are expected to grow by only 3.9%, 
more than one percentage point below the previous decade’s 
average. Factors such as the geopolitical conflicts such as the 
war in Ukraine, and the increasingly evident effects of climate 
change have further hindered global economic recovery.

ANALISIS PEREKONOMIAN NASIONAL
Sepanjang tahun 2024, perekonomian Indonesia mencatat 
pertumbuhan sebesar 5,03%, sedikit melambat dibandingkan 
tahun sebelumnya yang mencapai 5,05%. Produk Domestik 
Bruto (PDB) atas dasar harga berlaku mencapai Rp22.139 
triliun, dengan PDB per kapita sebesar Rp78,6 juta atau setara 
USD4.960,3. Meskipun angka pertumbuhan ini menunjukkan 
stabilitas, terdapat indikasi bahwa daya beli masyarakat masih 
menghadapi tantangan.
Despite this, there are several optimistic scenarios that could 
drive global economic growth. For instance, the easing of 
tariff policies by the United States has the potential to boost 
consumer confidence and global growth. Additionally, 
increased investment and defense spending in Europe, as 
well as advancements in artificial intelligence in China, could 
provide a positive stimulus to the global economy. However, 
risks such as rising protectionism, geopolitical tensions, 
and structural challenges like an aging population and low 
productivity remain major concerns that could hinder future 
economic growth.
ANALYSIS OF THE NATIONAL ECONOMY
Throughout 2024, the Indonesian economy recorded a growth 
of 5.03%, slightly slower than the previous year which reached 
5.05%. Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at current prices reached 
IDR22,139 trillion, with GDP per capita of IDR78.6 million or 
equivalent to USD4,960.3. Although these growth figures show 
stability, there are indications that people’s purchasing power 
is still facing challenges.

PROSPEK EKONOMI TAHUN 2025
​Memasuki tahun 2025, perekonomian global dan nasional 
akan dihadapkan pada berbagai tantangan dan peluang yang 
mempengaruhi prospek pertumbuhan.​
Organisasi Kerjasama dan Pembangunan Ekonomi (OECD) 
memproyeksikan pertumbuhan ekonomi global pada tahun 
2025 sebesar 3,1%, sedikit menurun dari perkiraan sebelumnya 
sebesar 3,2%. Penurunan ini disebabkan oleh hambatan 
perdagangan di beberapa negara anggota G20. ​
Di 
tingkat 
nasional, 
berbagai 
lembaga 
internasional 
memproyeksikan pertumbuhan ekonomi Indonesia pada 
tahun 2025 berada di kisaran 5%. Bank Dunia memperkirakan 
pertumbuhan 
sebesar 
5,1%, 
didukung 
oleh 
ekspansi 
fiskal dan peningkatan konsumsi domestik. Namun, OECD 
menurunkan proyeksi pertumbuhan Indonesia menjadi 4,9%, 
mengindikasikan potensi perlambatan akibat faktor eksternal 
dan internal. ​
Tantangan utama yang dihadapi meliputi ketidakpastian global, 
meningkatnya proteksionisme dan volatilitas pasar keuangan. 
Pemerintah Indonesia telah menyiapkan berbagai strategi 
untuk menghadapi tantangan ini, termasuk optimalisasi 
sektor-sektor potensial dan pelaksanaan program prioritas 
nasional. Selain itu, Pemerintah menargetkan pertumbuhan 
ekonomi mencapai 8% dengan mengoptimalkan berbagai 
sektor potensial. ​
Secara keseluruhan, meskipun terdapat tantangan yang 
signifikan, prospek ekonomi Indonesia pada tahun 2025 tetap 
menunjukkan potensi pertumbuhan yang positif, didukung 
oleh kebijakan fiskal yang ekspansif dan upaya Pemerintah 
dalam mengatasi hambatan ekonomi.
TINJAUAN INDUSTRI MIGAS TAHUN 2024
​Sepanjang tahun 2024, industri minyak dan gas bumi (Migas) 
Indonesia menghadapi berbagai tantangan dalam upaya 
meningkatkan produksi dan mencapai target nasional. Satuan 
Kerja Khusus Pelaksana Kegiatan Usaha Hulu Minyak dan Gas 
Bumi (SKK Migas) menetapkan target lifting minyak sebesar 
635.000 barel per hari (BOPD), namun hingga Maret 2024, 
realisasi lifting minyak bumi baru mencapai 567.000 BOPD atau 
89,4% dari target APBN. Demikian pula, lifting gas bumi dari 
target 1,33 juta barel setara minyak per hari (BOEPD) hanya 
terealisasi 885.460 BOEPD pada periode yang sama. Kondisi 
ini mendorong Pemerintah dan Kontraktor Kontrak Kerja Sama 
(KKKS) untuk memperkuat kolaborasi dan mengambil langkah-
langkah strategis guna mengatasi hambatan produksi serta 
mencapai target yang telah ditetapkan. ​
ECONOMIC OUTLOOK IN 2025
Entering 2025, both global and national economies will face 
various challenges and opportunities influencing growth 
prospects.
The Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development 
(OECD) projects global economic growth at 3.1% in 2025, 
slightly down from the previous estimate of 3.2%. This decline 
is attributed to trade barriers in some G20 member countries.
At the national level, various international institutions project 
Indonesia’s economic growth in 2025 to be around 5%. The 
World Bank forecasts a 5.1% growth rate, supported by fiscal 
expansion and increased domestic consumption. However, 
the OECD has lowered its projection for Indonesia to 4.9%, 
indicating potential slowdowns due to external and internal 
factors.
Key 
challenges 
include 
global 
uncertainties, 
rising 
protectionism, and financial market volatility. The Indonesian 
Government has prepared various strategies to address these 
challenges, including optimizing high-potential sectors and 
implementing national priority programs. Additionally, the 
Government aims for an 8% economic growth target by 
maximizing key sectors.
Overall, despite significant challenges, Indonesia’s economic 
outlook for 2025 remains positive, supported by expansionary 
fiscal policies and Government efforts to overcome economic 
obstacles.
OIL AND GAS INDUSTRY OVERVIEW IN 2024
Throughout 2024, Indonesia’s oil and gas industry faced 
multiple challenges in increasing production and achieving 
national targets. The Special Task Force for Upstream Oil and 
Gas Business Activities (SKK Migas) set an oil lifting target of 
635,000 barrels per day (BOPD). However, as of March 2024, 
actual oil lifting reached only 567,000 BOPD, or 89.4% of 
the state budget target. Similarly, gas lifting, with a target of 
1.33 million barrels of oil equivalent per day (BOEPD), only 
achieved 885,460 BOEPD in the same period. This situation has 
prompted the Government and Production Sharing Contract 
(PSC) contractors to strengthen collaboration and take 
strategic measures to overcome production constraints and 
meet targets.

HARGA MINYAK MENTAH INDONESIA TAHUN 2024
Sepanjang tahun 2024, harga minyak mentah Indonesia 
(Indonesian 
Crude 
Price/ICP) 
mengalami 
fluktuasi 
yang 
dipengaruhi oleh berbagai faktor global dan regional. Pada awal 
kuartal-III, ICP sempat turun dari USD78,51 per barel di Agustus 
menjadi USD72,54 per barel di September akibat melemahnya 
permintaan dari Tiongkok. Namun, di Oktober, harga kembali naik 
menjadi USD73,53 per barel, didorong oleh ketegangan geopolitik 
di Timur Tengah, terutama akibat serangan Iran ke Israel serta 
sanksi baru Amerika Serikat terhadap Iran. Kenaikan ini bersifat 
sementara, karena pada November ICP kembali turun ke USD71,83 
per barel seiring dengan meredanya ketegangan di Timur Tengah 
dan meningkatnya produksi OPEC+. Tren penurunan berlanjut di 
Desember, dengan ICP turun sedikit menjadi USD71,61 per barel 
akibat pelemahan ekonomi global dan penguatan Dolar AS.
Fluktuasi harga minyak mentah Indonesia sepanjang tahun 2024 
mencerminkan sensitivitas pasar terhadap dinamika geopolitik, 
perubahan permintaan global, serta keseimbangan pasokan minyak 
dunia. Faktor-faktor seperti kebijakan OPEC+, ketegangan di kawasan 
penghasil minyak, serta kondisi ekonomi negara-negara besar, 
terutama Tiongkok dan Amerika Serikat, memainkan peran utama 
dalam menentukan harga. Dengan volatilitas yang terjadi sepanjang 
tahun, pelaku industri energi di Indonesia perlu terus memantau 
perkembangan global untuk mengantisipasi dampak terhadap 
produksi, ekspor, serta kebijakan energi nasional.
Meanwhile, exploration and development activities have been 
intensified to support production goals. As of August 31, 2024, 
SKK Migas recorded the drilling of 545 development wells, with 
an estimated total investment of USD1.27 billion. Additionally, 
25 exploration wells were drilled with a total investment of 
approximately USD469 million. Seismic surveys also continued 
to identify potential reserves. These extensive drilling and 
survey efforts by the Government and PSC contractors aim 
to discover new oil and gas reserves and increase national 
production in line with the targets.
INDONESIAN CRUDE PRICE IN 2024
Throughout 2024, the Indonesian Crude Price (ICP) experienced 
fluctuations influenced by various global and regional factors. 
In early Q3, the ICP dropped from USD78.51 per barrel in 
August to USD72.54 per barrel in September due to weakened 
demand from China. However, in October, prices rebounded 
to USD73.53 per barrel, driven by geopolitical tensions in the 
Middle East, particularly Iran’s attack on Israel and new U.S. 
sanctions against Iran. This increase was temporary, as ICP fell 
again to USD71.83 per barrel in November following eased 
tensions in the Middle East and increased OPEC+ production. 
The downward trend continued in December, with ICP slightly 
declining to USD71.61 per barrel due to global economic 
weakness and a stronger US Dollar.
The fluctuations in Indonesia’s crude oil prices throughout 2024 
highlight the market’s sensitivity to geopolitical dynamics, 
changes in global demand, and the balance of oil supply. Factors 
such as OPEC+ policies, tensions in oil-producing regions, and 
the economic conditions of major countries, particularly China 
and the United States, played a crucial role in determining 
prices. Given this volatility, energy industry players in Indonesia 
must continuously monitor global developments to anticipate 
impacts on production, exports, and national energy policies.

TINJAUAN OPERASI PER SEGMEN OPERASI
PT Energi Mega Persada Tbk (EMP) adalah perusahaan hulu 
minyak dan gas bumi yang didirikan pada 16 Oktober 2001 dan 
melantai di Bursa Efek Jakarta (sekarang Bursa Efek Indonesia) 
pada Juni 2004 dengan kode saham ENRG. EMP fokus pada 
eksplorasi, pengembangan dan produksi minyak mentah serta 
gas bumi.
Wilayah operasi EMP tersebar di berbagai lokasi di Indonesia 
dan Mozambik. Di Indonesia, EMP mengelola 12 blok dari 
Sumatra, Jawa Timur dan Sulawesi Selatan, antara lain:​ 
Bireun Sigli, ‘B’, Gebang, Tonga, Siak, Kampar, Bentu, Korinci 
Baru, Malacca Strait, South CPP, Kangean dan Sengkang. Di 
Mozambik, EMP mengelola 1 blok yakni Buzi.
Selain itu, EMP memiliki anak perusahaan PT EMP Daya 
Nusantara, yang menandakan diversifikasi usaha ke sektor 
infrastruktur dan energi. Dengan portofolio aset yang luas, 
EMP berperan signifikan dalam industri energi Indonesia dan 
berkontribusi pada pemenuhan kebutuhan energi domestik 
maupun internasional. ​
PRODUKSI MIGAS EMP
EMP berhasil meningkatkan produksi minyak gross hariannya 
sebesar 22% menjadi 8.097 barel per hari (bph) pada tahun 
2024, dibandingkan produksi minyak harian di tahun 2023 
yang sebesar 6.644 (bph). Manajemen Perseroan menjelaskan 
produksi minyak EMP terus meningkat disebabkan oleh 
keberhasilan kami melakukan akuisisi 2 blok aset minyak yaitu 
blok Siak dan blok Kampar, sedangkan produksi gas masih 
cukup konsisten dai tahun ini. Harga minyak dunia mengalami 
penurunan dan di lain pihak, harga jual gas perusahaan 
mengalami peningkatan.
Selanjutnya, EMP menunjukan kinerja keuangan yang semakin 
membaik pada tahun 2024 bila dibandingkan dengan tahun 
sebelumnya. Kinerja Penjualan, EBITDA dan Laba Bersih 
Perusahaan mengalami kenaikan masing-masing sebesar 11%, 
5% dan 10% di tahun 2024 dibandingkan tahun sebelumnya.
HARGA JUAL MIGAS EMP
Pada tahun 2024, EMP mencatat kenaikan harga jual rata-rata 
gas sebesar 6% menjadi USD6,59 per MCF, dibandingkan dengan 
USD6,22 per MCF pada tahun 2023. Sementara itu, harga jual 
minyak mentah mengalami sedikit penurunan, dengan rata-rata 
USD79,4 per barel pada 2024 dibandingkan USD80,75 per barel 
pada 2023. Kenaikan harga jual gas ini turut mendukung kinerja 
keuangan EMP meskipun terdapat fluktuasi harga minyak mentah 
di pasar global.
OPERATIONAL REVIEW BY SEGMENT
PT Energi Mega Persada Tbk (EMP) is an upstream oil and 
gas company established on October 16, 2001, and listed on 
the Jakarta Stock Exchange (now known as Indonesia Stock 
Exchange) in June 2004 under the ticker symbol ENRG. EMP 
focuses on the exploration, development, and production of 
crude oil and natural gas.
EMP operates across multiple locations in Indonesia and 
Mozambique. In Indonesia, EMP manages 12 blocks across 
Sumatra, East Java, and South Sulawesi, including Bireun Sigli, 
‘B’, Gebang, Tonga, Siak, Kampar, Bentu, Korinci Baru, Malacca 
Strait, South CPP, Kangean, and Sengkang. In Mozambique, 
EMP operates one block, Buzi.
Additionally, EMP owns a subsidiary, PT EMP Daya Nusantara, 
marking a diversification into the infrastructure and energy 
sectors. With an extensive asset portfolio, EMP plays a 
significant role in Indonesia’s energy industry, contributing to 
both domestic and international energy needs.
EMP OIL AND GAS PRODUCTION
EMP successfully increased its daily gross oil production by 
22% to 8,097 barrels per day (BOPD) in 2024, compared to 
6,644 BOPD in 2023. The company attributed this growth to the 
successful acquisition of two oil blocks, Siak and Kampar, while 
gas production remained relatively stable. Despite the decline 
in global oil prices, EMP benefited from an increase in its gas 
selling price.
Furthermore, EMP showed improved financial performance in 
2024 compared to the previous year. The company’s revenue, 
EBITDA, and net profit increased by 11%, 5%, and 10%, 
respectively, compared to the previous year.
EMP OIL AND GAS SELLING PRICE
In 2024, EMP recorded a 6% increase in its average gas selling 
price to USD6.59 per MCF, up from USD6.22 per MCF in 2023. 
Meanwhile, the crude oil selling price saw a slight decrease, 
averaging USD79.4 per barrel in 2024 compared to USD80.75 
per barrel in 2023. The rise in gas selling prices contributed 
to EMP’s financial performance despite fluctuations in global 
crude oil prices.

ASET
ASET LANCAR
Total aset lancar Perseroan dan anak Perusahaan pada tanggal 
31 Desember 2024 adalah sebesar USD255,64 juta, mengalami 
kenaikan sebesar 5,48% dibandingkan dengan total aset lancar 
Perseroan pada tanggal 31  Desember 2023 yang tercatat 
sebesar USD242,37 juta. Kenaikan ini terutama disebabkan 
oleh kenaikan piutang usaha Perseroan dan anak Perusahaan 
sehubungan bertambahnya anak Perusahaan Perseroan. 
ASET TIDAK LANCAR
Total aset tidak lancar Perseroan dan anak Perusahaan yang 
berakhir pada tanggal 31 Desember 2024 adalah sebesar 
USD1.327,61 juta, yang mengalami kenaikan sebesar 17,86% 
dibandingkan dengan total aset tidak lancar Perseroan pada 
tanggal 31 Desember 2023 yang mencapai USD1.126,38 juta. 
Kenaikan ini terutama disebabkan oleh kenaikan aset minyak dan 
gas bumi yang timbul dari investasi pengeluaran barang modal 
(capital expenditures) pada KKS Malacca Strait dan KKS Bentu serta 
akuisisi aset minyak dan gas bumi baru sepanjang tahun 2024. 
TOTAL ASET
Jumlah total aset Perseroan dan anak Perusahaan pada 
tanggal 31 Desember 2024 adalah sebesar USD1.583,26 juta, 
mengalami kenaikan sebesar 15,67%  dibandingkan dengan 
total aset Perseroan pada tanggal 31 Desember 2023 yang 
tercatat sebesar USD1.368,76 juta. Kenaikan ini terutama 
disebabkan oleh kenaikan aset minyak dan gas bumi yang 
timbul dari investasi pengeluaran barang modal (capital 
expenditures) pada KKS Malacca Strait dan KKS Bentu serta 
akuisisi aset minyak dan gas bumi baru sepanjang tahun 2024.
ASSETS
CURRENT ASSETS
The total current assets of the Company and its subsidiaries 
as of December 31, 2024, amounted to USD255.64 million, an 
increase of 5.48% compared to the total current assets of the 
Company as of December 31, 2023, which stood at USD242.37 
million. This increase was primarily due to the rise in trade 
receivables of the Company and its subsidiaries, related to the 
addition of the Company’s Subsidiaries.
NON-CURRENT ASSETS
The total non-current assets of the Company and its subsidiaries 
as of December 31, 2024, amounted to USD1,327.61 million, an 
increase of 17.86% compared to the total non-current assets 
of the Company as of December 31, 2023, which amounted 
to USD1,126.38 million. This increase was primarily due to the 
rise in oil and gas assets resulting from capital expenditure 
investments in the Malacca Strait PSC and Bentu PSC, as well 
as the acquisition of new oil and gas assets throughout 2024.
TOTAL ASSETS
The total assets of the Company and its subsidiaries as of 
December 31, 2024, amounted to USD1,583.26 million, an 
increase of 15.67% compared to the total assets of the Company 
as of December 31, 2023, which stood at USD1,368.76 million. 
This increase was mainly driven by the growth in oil and gas 
assets due to capital expenditure investments in the Malacca 
Strait PSC and Bentu PSC, as well as the acquisition of new oil 
and gas assets in 2024.

liabilities with total assets for the given period.

seluruh perusahaan eksplorasi dan produksi, dengan tujuan 
mengoptimalkan pendapatan negara, menjaga kestabilan 
pasokan energi, serta mendorong investasi di sektor Migas. 
Proses pemasaran minyak dan gas bumi melibatkan berbagai 
pemangku kepentingan, termasuk Pemerintah, badan usaha 
dan pihak swasta, yang harus mematuhi ketentuan terkait 
kontrak, harga, distribusi, serta pemanfaatan sumber daya 
secara adil dan berkelanjutan.

ESDM Republik Indonesia Nomor 29 Tahun 2021 tentang Tata 
Cara Penetapan Metodologi, Formula, Harga Minyak Bumi 
Indonesia tertanggal 22 Oktober 2021 serta Menteri ESDM 
Republik Indonesia pada setiap bulannya mengeluarkan 
Keputusan Menteri tentang Formulasi Harga Minyak Bumi 
Indonesia.

The governance of oil and gas marketing encompasses a 
set of policies, regulations, and mechanisms established 
by the Government to ensure transparency, efficiency, 
and sustainability in the energy industry. In Indonesia, this 
governance is regulated through laws that bind all exploration 
and production companies, aiming to optimize state revenue, 
maintain energy supply stability, and encourage investment in 
the oil and gas sector. The marketing process involves various 
stakeholders, including the Government, business entities, 
and private sectors, all of whom must comply with regulations 
related to contracts, pricing, distribution, and the fair and 
sustainable utilization of resources.

the Minister of Energy and Mineral Resources of the Republic 
of Indonesia issues a Ministerial Decree every month regarding 
the Formulation of Indonesian Crude Oil Prices.

Regulation of the Minister of Energy and Mineral Resources of

Government of the Republic of Indonesia through the 
Regulation of the Minister of EMR of the Republic of Indonesia 
No. 06 of 2016 concerning Provisions and Procedures for 
Determining the Allocation and Utilization and Prices of 
Natural Gas. In this regulation, the priority order of natural gas 
utilization has been determined as follows:
•	 Supporting the Government’s program for the supply 
of Natural Gas for transportation, household and small 
customers;
•	 Increasing National Oil and Gas production;
•	 Fertilizer industry;
•	 Natural Gas-based Industry;
•	 Supply of electric power;  
•	 Industries that use Natural Gas as fuel.
The regulation states that in the event that all priorities for 
domestic Natural Gas Allocation and Utilization as described 
above have been fulfilled, the seller of the State’s share of 
Natural Gas or the PSC contractor may conduct an auction of 
unabsorbed Natural Gas. The winner of the auction will submit 
an application for the allocation and price of Natural Gas by SKK 
Migas to the Minister of EMR.
The regulation also governs the potential buyers of natural 
gas that are allowed to be directly appointed for each of the 
above purposes, the procedures for determining the allocation 
and utilization of natural gas and also the procedures for 
determining the price of natural gas. The existence of several 
alternative prospective buyers makes the domestic gas market 
more competitive because gas allocation applications can 
be submitted by PSC Contractor through SKK Migas and/

accelerate the process of Natural Gas allocation approval.

MANAJEMEN RISIKO KEUANGAN
Perseroan dipengaruhi oleh berbagai risiko keuangan, seperti 
risiko mata uang asing, risiko tingkat bunga, risiko kredit dan 
risiko likuiditas. Tujuan manajemen risiko Perseroan secara 
keseluruhan adalah untuk secara efektif mengendalikan risiko-
risiko ini dan meminimalisasi pengaruh merugikan yang dapat 
terjadi terhadap kinerja keuangan mereka.
RISIKO MATA UANG ASING 
Risiko mata uang asing adalah risiko dimana nilai wajar arus 
kas masa depan dari aset atau liabilitas Perseroan dalam mata 
uang asing dapat berfluktuasi karena perubahan nilai tukar 
mata uang asing. Perseroan terekspos risiko perubahan nilai 
tukar mata uang asing terutama dari aset dan liabilitas moneter 
yang diakui dalam mata uang selain dari mata uang fungsional 
Perseroan (Catatan 46 atas Laporan Keuangan Perseroan).
Sebagian besar pendapatan  Perseroan adalah dengan harga, 
ditagih dan dibayar dalam Dolar Amerika Serikat (USD). 
Sebagian besar beban pokok penjualan, beban usaha dan 
belanja modal adalah dalam USD. Namun demikian, terdapat 
beberapa biaya dan beban dalam mata uang Rupiah seperti 
gaji, upah dan pajak.
Untuk mengelola risiko,  Perseroan melakukan penyepadanan 
liabilitas dalam mata uang asing dengan aset keuangan 
dalam mata uang asing terkait dan melakukan pembelian 
dan penjualan mata uang asing saat diperlukan. Manajemen 
memandang belum perlu melakukan lindung nilai mata 
uang asing karena aset moneter dalam mata uang asing yang 
tersedia cukup untuk melunasi liabilitas dalam mata uang 
asing terkait.
FINANCIAL RISK MANAGEMENT
The Company is exposed to various financial risks, including 
foreign currency risk, interest rate risk, credit risk, and liquidity 
risk. The overall objective of the Company’s risk management 
is to effectively control these risks and minimize any adverse 
impact on its financial performance.
FOREIGN CURRENCY RISK
Foreign currency risk arises when the fair value of future cash 
flows of the Company’s assets or liabilities in foreign currencies 
fluctuates due to exchange rate changes. The Company 
is exposed to exchange rate fluctuations primarily from 
monetary assets and liabilities recognized in currencies other 
than its functional currency (Refer to Note 46 of the Company’s 
Financial Statements).
Most of the Company’s revenue is priced, billed, and paid in 
U.S. Dollars (USD). Likewise, most of its cost of sales, operating 
expenses, and capital expenditures are in USD. However, some 
expenses, such as salaries, wages, and taxes, are in Indonesian 
Rupiah (IDR).
To manage this risk, the Company matches its foreign currency 
liabilities with corresponding foreign currency assets and 
conducts foreign currency transactions when necessary. 
Management currently does not deem it necessary to 
implement foreign exchange hedging, as the available foreign 
currency monetary assets are sufficient to cover its related 
foreign currency liabilities.

IMBALAN PASCA KERJA
Perseroan menentukan liabilitas imbalan pascakerja sesuai

35/2021) yang melaksanakan ketentuan Pasal 81 dan Pasal 
185(b) Undang-Undang No. 11/2020 mengenai Cipta Kerja. 
PSAK No. 24 mensyaratkan entitas menggunakan metode 
“Projected Unit Credit” untuk menentukan nilai kini kewajiban 
imbalan pasti, biaya jasa kini terkait dan biaya jasa lalu.
Ketika entitas memiliki surplus dalam program imbalan pasti, 
maka entitas mengukur aset imbalan pasti pada jumlah yang 
lebih rendah antara surplus program imbalan pasti dan batas 
atas aset yang ditentukan dengan menggunakan tingkat 
diskonto.
Entitas mengakui komponen biaya imbalan pasti, kecuali SAK 
mensyaratkan atau mengizinkan biaya tersebut sebagai biaya 
perolehan aset, sebagai berikut:
1.	 biaya jasa dalam laba rugi;
2.	 bunga neto atas liabilitas (aset) imbalan pasti neto 
dalam laba rugi; dan
3.	 pengukuran kembali liabilitas (aset) imbalan pasti neto 
dalam penghasilan komprehensif lain.
Pengukuran kembali atas liabilitas (aset) imbalan pasti neto 
yang diakui dalam penghasilan komprehensif lain tidak 
direklasifikasi ke laba rugi pada periode berikutnya. Akan 
tetapi, entitas dapat mengalihkan jumlah yang diakui sebagai 
penghasilan komprehensif lain tersebut pada pos lain dalam
ekuitas.
