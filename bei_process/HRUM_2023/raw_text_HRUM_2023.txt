Tinjauan Ekonomi
 Economic Overview
The global economy grew modestly in 2023, reaching 3.0%, 
following a previous robust growth of 3.5% in 2022. Factors that 
contributed to the slowdown in global economic growth included 
supply constraints due to the scarring effect on corporations and 
immigration restrictions in some developed countries. There was 
also increased geopolitical-economic fragmentation due to the 
Russia-Ukraine war, which led to supply constraints and global 
energy and food prices. Declining exports-imports and world 
trade volumes due to trade tensions between the United States 
and China, as well as tensions in the Middle East between Israel 
and Palestine, further contributed to global geopolitical tensions. 
Additionally, inflationary pressures rose due to energy and food 
prices, and there was tightness in the labor markets in some 
developed countries.
Amidst the challenges and turbulence in the global economy, 
Indonesia’s economy remained resilient and grew properly in 2023, 
supported by strong domestic demand. Indonesia’s economic 
growth in 2023 was recorded at 5.1%. From the business sector 
perspective, Indonesia’s economy was primarily driven by the 
performance of transportation and warehousing sector, which grew 
by 14.0%, followed by other services at 10.5%, and accommodation 
and food services at 10.0%. Meanwhile, from the expenditure 
side, the highest growth occurred in PK-LNPRT (expenditure 
on consumption by Non-Profit Institutions Serving Households) 
component at 9.8%, followed by PK-RT (Household Consumption 
Expenditure/PK-RT) component at 4.8%, and PMTB (Gross Fixed 
Capital Formation) component at 4.4%.

besar berasal dari estimasi beban kekurangan kewajiban pasar

di tahun 2022.

In 2023, the cost of revenues and direct costs was recorded at 
USD543.1 million, an increase of 49.6% compared to the previous

due to an increase in production costs in line with the increase 
in coal production volume, particularly overburden costs and 
ferronickel production costs in 2023.
Gross Profit

from USD541.5 million in 2022. Thus, the Company’s gross profit 
margin also reflected a decline to 41.3% compared to 59.9% in 
2022.
Selling Expenses
The Company’s selling expenses in 2023 were recorded 
at USD43.5 million, a decrease of 33.6% compared to 
USD65.4 million in 2022. The decrease in selling expenses mostly 
came from the estimation of domestic market obligation shortfall 
to USD1.5 million in 2023, which decreased from USD36.5 million 
in 2022. This was in line with the increasing coal sales volume 
to the domestic market. There was an increase in transportation 
costs in 2023 to USD36.7 million from USD22.4 million, in line with 
the increase in the quantity of coal sales compared to that of the 
previous year. 
General and Administrative Expenses
The Company’s general and administrative expenses in 2023 were 
recorded at USD52.1 million. This amount increased by 45.1% 
from USD35.9 million in 2022. This increase was mostly due to an

from USD8.0 million in 2022.

kenaikan pasokan, permintaan nikel diprediksi akan berkurang

menguat menjadi USD20.500 per ton pada tahun 2025, apabila ada 
peningkatan permintaan baterai mobil listrik.
Business Outlook
Entering 2024, global economic growth is forecasted to weaken to 
2.8% with widening divergence in growth among countries. The risk 
of a correction in global economic growth could occur if the high 
fragmentation in geopolitics and geoeconomics persists, including 
its impact on the resurgence of energy and food prices, as well 
as prolonged monetary policy tightening, especially in developed 
countries to bring inflation back to their respective targets. 
Meanwhile, Bank Indonesia analyzes Indonesia’s economic growth 
in 2024 to be in the range of 4.7%-5.5%, and inflation is expected 
to remain under control within the target range of 2.5±1%. Key 
indicators driving Indonesia’s economic growth include domestic 
demand aligned with the increase in civil servant salaries, the 
general elections, and the development of Nusantara Capital City 
(IKN).
As for opportunities in coal industry, the Company sees vast market 
potential due to the government’s ambitious coal demand targets. 
In 2024, the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources will prioritize 
coal needs to fulfill domestic interests with a Domestic Market 
Obligation (DMO) target of 181.3 million tons from a total production 
of 710 million tons. The increasing trend in coal demand is driven 
by electricity demand and additional new coal-fired power plant 
projects from the 35,000 MW program.
For nickel industry outlook, the World Bank projects that global 
nickel prices will decline again in 2024, continuing the weakening 
trend observed throughout 2023. The World Bank makes this 
projection based on the increase in nickel supply from major 
producing countries such as China, Indonesia, and the Philippines. 
While supply is increasing, nickel demand is predicted to decrease 
with the emergence of lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery 
technology for electric vehicles as a substitute for nickel. However, 
the World Bank expects nickel prices to slightly strengthen to 
USD20,500 per ton in 2025 if there is an increase in demand for 
electric vehicle batteries.
